This was a good expos section especially if you like the environment / environmental justice. If not, it might be a little difficult for you to feel super engaged and interested with the readings. There were readings due for every class but as long as you stay on top of them, they are very manageable (there were only a few that I found difficult to get through or too long). Overall the class is manageable as the only assignments involved prep / writing for the 2 essays and the Capstone project which felt well spaced out.
This is a great class! Environmental justice is such a fascinating and important topic, and Dr. Lane is an amazing, sweet, patient, and understanding teacher! We have a lot of discussions so make sure you at least skim the readings before class. Also, make sure to read Dr. Lane's feedback before going to your one–on–one conference so that you can discuss actionable steps you can take to improve your paper. I've read so many Q reports saying that Expos 20 is a dreadful requirement, but I have come to really enjoy it, grow close to my class, and you should take "I Love That Dirty Water"!
TAKE THIS EXPOS!!! It's so interesting to learn about environmental justice and how it intertwines with society.
Highly recommend this class, especially if you're interested in environmental issues and/or social justice issues! Required readings are not too lengthy, and I found most of them pretty interesting. Some readings (typically the scholarly articles) were a bit dense, but the other less dense readings (including watching the hunger games lol) balanced those out. I also think it can be a little intimidating being in such a small class your first year at Harvard, but Professor Lane along with my fellow classmates really cultivated a safe environment where I truly felt comfortable to share my perspectives/opinions. Another major plus is we only write two papers plus a capstone project (creating a zine), whereas some other expos courses write three papers.
This is definitly one of the best expos sections you can place into. The workload is very manageable and Prof Lane is absolutely wonderful. You get what you put into it. You'll get very comprehensive feedback on essays that, if you heed, will certainly shape you into a more effective and nuanced writer. The topic (despite the funny name of the course) is incredibly relevant and thought–provoking. If you aren't very well–versed in ideas concerning environmental justice and their applications, it'll shift how you view things in your day–to–day life and the world around you. I really recommend this section!
A great EXPOS for anyone interested in environmental justice and environmental issues. This isn't a particular interest of mine, but I still found the class pretty interesting!
This is one of the easier expos classes as Ive heard, however, it still does require a decent amount of reading, writing, and discussion
This is a great course in terms of the topic, the people interested in the climate change discussion, and the readings. The workload is extremely balanced, with the readings being pretty short and effective. The one thing I would change is the feedback, which is not very clear in its grading rubric.
SUCH A GOOD COURSE!! You don't even have to be interested in environmental science to love this class, there's so many intersections with other topics (social justice aspects like racial equity, prisons, even feminism) that you can find a way to draw connections that will make you interested.
