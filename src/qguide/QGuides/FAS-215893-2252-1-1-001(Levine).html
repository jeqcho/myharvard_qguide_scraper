this class has felt a bit disorganized and disconnected from student needs. First off, there’s no class–wide feedback form, which kind of gives the impression that student input isn’t valued. On top of that, we have a scheduled final during reading period, which adds unnecessary stress during an already packed time. The quiz format is also stressful. Sometimes we get 5 minutes to answer two really tough free response questions that require multiple sentences each. Add to that the weekly 80–100 pages of reading, a 2–hour film, and acting assignments (which, to be honest, no one seems particularly qualified to teach), and sometimes the weekly assignment is not even updated until the weekend. It just feels like a lot for a gened.

Workshop is fun I guess but honestly did not learn much at all. A lot of people perform their subjects as a caricature of their identity (gym bros, athletes etc.) which could be funny but I feel like is not what this course wanted us to do.

The quizzes have been especially frustrating because they're based almost entirely on whether you understood really dense, difficult readings, and not much room is given for grappling with the material. My TF originally graded based on whether she could tell if you’d done the reading, but then was told she had to grade purely on accuracy, which seems inconsistent and unclear. It also feels like the Head TF doesn't really engage with or support the rest of us, which doesn’t help.

The lectures themselves are confusing. The professor spends time asking rhetorical questions or talking in circles instead of clearly explaining the readings. And based on the quiz questions, it’s clear they expect us to basically teach ourselves everything from the readings. So… why are we even going to lecture?
