100% TAKE THIS COURSE. I know that a lot of people put that on Q reports, but this is an absolutely amazing course that you should take. I came in knowing very little about Rome or Classics, and have left with a wealth of knowledge. This course is really approachable, and I know people who are Classics concentrators who have also learned things from the course and enjoyed it. Not only is the work is incredibly manageable, but it's also super interesting. I expected to kind of superficially like the course content, but it ended up being so much more fascinating and engaging than I expected. Also, Dr. Love is the best professor I've had so far. I haven't written a research essay before this course, and she was so helpful. She took the time to meet with me multiple times to go over my topic and just generally how to write a research paper. So, yes, my honest review is to absolutely take this course!
Take this course! You'll definitely get out what you put in. If you want to be quiet in class and coast through the semester, you can do that, but this is a course where the conversations can be incredibly rich and relevant if you commit to thinking about the material. The requirements are pretty minimal, and you get tons of scaffolding for all the assignments, so it's very manageable for how rewarding it is. Prof. Love is a fantastic instructor and just a great person to know, and she's an extremely personable and supportive discussion leader.
Great class! Take it
Take this class! It is one of the most enriching intellectual experiences you will have. Rachel Love is a brilliant and inspiring professor—her lectures are rigorous, engaging, and full of passion. You’ll learn how ancient history continues to shape the world we live in, and you’ll leave the class with new questions and perspectives.
I really enjoyed this class! While it is a pretty niche topic, I find myself coming out of it with a lot of interesting knowledge about the Roman Empire and its system of exemplarity! The class is pretty low–stress, with a few assignments sprinkled throughout the course (two presentations and two short response papers) which are super manageable, and I really enjoyed getting to listen to everyone's presentations as they were genuinely super interesting!
Great course if you have interest in Rome or classical studies that does not require any prior knowledge.
