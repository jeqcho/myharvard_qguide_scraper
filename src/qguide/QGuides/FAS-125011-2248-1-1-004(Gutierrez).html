What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Spanish 20 is what you make of it. While it's not an "easy" class, it's not as difficult as I assumed it would be after reading the q reports. Try to get Gaby as your TF and do office hours; she is invaluable in clarifying anything you are confused about and helping workshop your compositions. My grade (A) in the class is entirely due to the office hours we scheduled. The readings can be confusing, so discuss them with your peers before class.
This level of Spanish is extremely grammar–heavy and can feel fast–paced. As someone with a much better background in conversational Spanish, I struggled during some parts of this class. It can also take some getting used to only speaking in Spanish in class if you're like me and didn't have that experience before. Overall you will definitely learn a lot about Latin–American peoples and cultures, and certainly about different verb tenses. I enjoyed this class once I fully adjusted to the change from previous experiences.
Don't be intimidated; if you're on the fence between Spanish 11 and 20, go for 20. I came into this with two years of lax high school Spanish and some daily practice on Duolingo. Experience will vary between sections, but this section was engaging and challenged students to listen attentively. Compositions and exams take preparation but are doable. If she teaches again, I definitely recommend Gaby.
I really liked the unit topics in Spanish 20 and felt like I learned about the history and culture of Latin America as well as gaining language skills. Take the time to do the homework for each class session and you will be successful in this class (plus you can re–do homework three times and keep the average of your scores). The compositions allow you to build off of the writing you do on exams, which I loved. Expect a more accelerated version of a high school language class –– in my opinion, this pace is preferable. If you get Gaby as your instructor, you're in luck, she is incredibly helpful and genuinely wants every student to succeed.
Engage with the language and the material as much as you can outside of class. Go to tutoring and office hours if you need, but make as much time as possible to practice because engagement and repetition is how the material sticks. If you do this, the class will be great.
