Dr. Zwang (Ted) is a great discussion leader! He will ask you thought–provoking questions, that, at times, made my heart race because they do not have a black–and–white answer and really make you think! NEURO 101AA was great!
101AA is a great course that provides insights into modern views on Alzheimer's Disease, certain topics about modern innovation were interesting, and also reading about differing opinions about where the area of research should be focused was interesting as well. Although I did feel that toward the latter half of the course a lot of topics were intersecting and the discussion wasn't as fruitful since everyone kept bouncing around the same topics of conversation from previous weeks. However, Ted did provide great questions to think about so maybe if he had a list of questions to potentially answer during the discussion would inspire new topics of conversation and more fruitful conversations.
This is an excellent course if you have any interest in learning more about Alzheimer's. In discussion we dig deeper than just disease hallmarks, focusing more on how different discoveries might impact patient outcomes which I really enjoyed exploring.
TED IS SO GREAT. So knowledgeable, the subject is interesting, being able to hear his experiences and advice about research was invaluable. You don't need to know everything about neuroscience for this course, just have an interest and you will learn the rest in class! The presentations by a student each week was also great because it allows you to dive deep one week that interests you, while also getting to learn from your classmates.
I would highly recommend taking it if you're interested in AD or other neurodegenerative diseases.
This class is a must–take for anyone interested in neuroscience, medicine, or aging and memory. It does such a good job of combining scientific inquiry with a deep sense of empathy and ethical reflection. The readings are so interesting and often challenging, but the classroom environment makes it easy to ask questions and explore ideas and grow. Ted is an outstanding instructor. He is thoughtful, kind, and it is clear that he is deeply invested in his students’ learning. He does an incredible job of encouraging inclusive discussions where every student’s perspective is valued. I came away from this course not only with a better understanding of Alzheimer’s disease, but also with a clearer sense of why this field matters and how I might contribute to it in the future.
