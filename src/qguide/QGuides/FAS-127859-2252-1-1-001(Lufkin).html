What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is SO fun but also a lot of work (lowkey my most difficult class this semester). There is a LOT of reading and you need to actually read all of it because Felicity is locked in on the content, she literally knows everything it is so impressive. If you're willing to do the reading and participate in class, it's a super rewarding class and you can get a lot out of it. Also the final project is a lot of fun and you'll get to take away a complete quilt that you made! Would definitely recommend the course, just be warned that it is not easy.
Everybody should talk FolkMyth 172 if you get the chance to! This is a rigorous academic treatment of quilt history, and I have learned *so much* about quiltmaking and its significance throughout the course. Felicity has a *staggering* knowledge of quilt history, and it is so wonderful to hear her speak during lecture. The section part of the course is also *incredible*—I had absolutely *no* background in *any* form of needlework, yet I was still able to be successful in lab, even making a full quilt as a final project. This class is no joke, and there is a *lot* of reading and writing to do each week, but I found this class to be so rewarding, and I highly recommend it to anybody with an interest in learning a new skill and a lot of *fascinating* art and American history! Felicity puts so much care into the class, and you will be successful if you put in your best effort.
Be present in the class and don’t be afraid to ask questions ! You’re not expected to know everything, so go for it! Start your final project sooner rather than later. The time will sneak up on you and it’s much more investment needed than you might think.
YOU SHOULD TAKE THIS CLASS!! I knew nothing about quilting before this semester, and now I am so glad that I took this course! Quilts are deeply ingrained into our history as a nation, and it was so fun to learn about the peoples and cultures that have shaped the world of quilt making. Professor Lufkin was an amazing instructor during discussions and the quilt lab! I had no experience with sewing and needlework, but Professor Lufkin was patient with us all and helped everyone through the process of quilting! It was so cool to have submitted my own quilt for the final project! Regardless of your academic interests, from science to philosophy, everyone should take this class!
This is such a fun class! Weekly readings provide a comprehensive overview of each topic, and classroom discussions are very open to conversation. The final project is a great way to pursue a sewing project. While time consuming, you can tailor it to your own interests and have a lot of fun with it.
