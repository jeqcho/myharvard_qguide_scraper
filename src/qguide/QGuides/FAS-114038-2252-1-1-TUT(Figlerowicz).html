What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course offers a deep introduction to what comparative literature is and the kinds of questions and methods the field invites. Be prepared to engage with challenging theoretical texts and to work on synthesizing complex ideas across readings. The course can really help develop your reading and writing skills, especially in terms of close analysis, building arguments, and working more efficiently with difficult material. At the same time, it can be demanding if you're not already used to working with dense theory or writing in this style. Coming in with an open mind and a willingness to ask for clarification or support when needed can make the experience much more manageable and rewarding.
I loved the sophomore tutorial, and it has been one of my favorite classes at Harvard so far. The class gives an excellent introduction to comparative literature, equipping students with the basic understanding to dive deeper into topics of their own interest. The weekly assignments may feel pretty frequent, but they really make for good practice.
This is a requirement for both the concentration and the secondary, and it can definitely be a lot at times. However, the weekly responses do build you up to the final paper, so take those seriously, and if you get the chance to work with Matylda and need any accommodations, know that she will have you covered and will go out of her way to make sure you have what you need.
This was such a fantastic class!! If you’re not sure about comparative literature as a field or where your interests will lie within the field, this class will get you up to speed. You get experience with reading an incredible variety of genres and time periods, and learn how to read literary criticism with an informed eye. Matylda facilitates in–class discussions beautifully, and is particularly adept at highlighting both the strengths of your responses and places to develop your ideas further. I always walked away feeling both supported and challenged. Christine gives fantastic, very thorough feedback on your weekly assignments. Even though I felt as though I had a lot of experience with academic writing and thinking about literature, she always found new ways to challenge me and push me to grow as a scholar.
