I highly recommend this course, especially if you are a pre–medical student. Medical practitioners will benefit significantly from understanding how the drugs they prescribe are developed and produced, and how the pharmaceutical industry performs its role.
amazing class, anyone interested in biotech (especially upperclassmen) should take this
Great class if you are interested in biotech VC, biotech HF, etc. as it gives you a great overview. Taught MBA style.
Must take class if you're interested in the biotech or pharma industry in the future. Covers many topics within the field with excellent and very relevant guest speakers. In addition, Stan and Peter are incredible sources of knowledge who are each experts in their own right and provide incredible insight into course material. Very little work in this class outside of the two projects, which you should make sure to start early.
The course focuses a lot less on the science behind drug development and more so on the business/economic/regulatory end of it, which would make it great for anyone looking to get into biotech or industry in general. It's definitely one of those classes where you get out of it what you put in!
I would recommend this class. It's got a very light workload, and most of the lectures are interesting. Stan is kind of an NPC but Peter is funny and a big personality. Some of the guest lecturers rock, like the IP attorneys, but not everyone hits a home run. Overall you'll learn a lot about drug development and it's a fairly different course from what you'd typically get in a science course.
It's a great course for background on biotech industry.
I would not personally recommend this course. I found lectures to be really dry and didn't think it was worth using precious Harvard class time on such lectures. Every assignment was group based and so completing my assignments (which made up my entire grade) were DREADFUL. Also, you don't really get rewarded for going to lectures so I was one of the only ones there which didn't make it any more engaging...
