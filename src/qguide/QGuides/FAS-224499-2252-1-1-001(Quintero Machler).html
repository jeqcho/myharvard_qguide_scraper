What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an excellent course that has helped me investigate and process perspectives different from my own. It facilitates building a comprehensive understanding of a vast and essential tradition in Latin American political and sociological thought. For a Spanish learner like me, it was a very interesting way to deeply engage with the language and critically read a specific genre of literature.
This is an excellent course that provides a deep overview of the Latin American conservative tradition. It covers themes ranging from religion to gender to fascism, and the class discussions are always interesting and rooted in the fundamental philosophical texts that we read in the first week of the course. It also does a great job of providing some interesting historical background surrounding the regions and events that we discussed.
You need to take this course!
Take this class! Super interesting and Prof. Quintero Machler is fantastic!
Alejandro is amazing! He is super nice and plans each session very thoroughly so that most of the time, we still have stuff to talk about before the two hours are over. The readings are very challenging for people whose Spanish is relatively weak, but you will get better, I think, if you try to read and understand even portions of them thoroughly. It helps to be interested in the topic of Latin American conservative thought in the 18th and 19th centuries, but if you take it to get better at Spanish then it's also great practice with tough literature.
