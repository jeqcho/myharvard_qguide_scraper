This course is amazing! Not only will you get exposure to many different thinkers, disciplines, and methods, you will also get a solid understanding of the key debates surrounding climate justice. Jon is super encouraging and class discussions always feel rich. He comes with a deep understanding of political theory and its always interesting to see its connections to fields, even if they are technically outside his field.
This is an amazing 98! Even if you are someone familiar with climate change, the way that the class works through different political theories feels like a new perspective on tackling the issue. The readings are really engaging, with a mix of more journalistic/case study–type readings, theory, and methdological readings to prepare for the self–directed final research project. Jon is an amazing teacher, can't recommend this enough
This is a wonderful class! Dr. Masin–Peters does a great job facilitating discussion, the readings are interesting and enjoyable, and weekly written assignments are useful in fleshing out your thoughts ahead of class. If you are looking to gain a better understanding of the current academic conversation around political theory and climate change, especially if this is something you are considering writing your thesis about, I highly recommend this class!
I would 100% recommend taking this course and think it is such a valuable and insightful class. That being said, if you have no interest in climate or the environment, it is probably not the right course. Also, I would say not to be afraid to speak up in class it makes the conversations that much more stimulating, and Professor Masin–Peters really appreciates it!
