This is a Sociology requirement so you have to take it! Have no fear; this is a great class!!! You learn a lot about the sociological canon, but also important sociologists that are often overlooked in favor of the canon. The discussion posts are fun.
This class is very reading–intensive but SO MUCH FUN! The lectures were very engaging (we mostly watched movies or went to a museum). The section was so insightful, even though long. Jane (TF) and Shai (instructor) are fantastic!
I liked Soc97! It's a requirement for anything related to sociology, most who take it are sophomores concentrating in sociology (a couple students taking it for fun). Because of this, it's a pretty small class size for a concentration requirement, but I like that aspect.

I really enjoyed the balance between section and lecture, where section is more understanding theory and section is more like applying it

There is a bunch of readings involved which can be hard sometimes, but section does a good job at filling the gaps whenever necessary (but you can't come to section empty brained with no reading bc there is cold–calling).

Many of the lecture times were spent at the library, or art museum, or meeting with TF's about papers so for many weeks we only met once a week.
Wish there was less emphasis on movies/TV – felt like a film class at times. Shai's lectures outside of the media were a little disengaging. Appreciated the diversity of the readings – those were excellent.
'
Jane was a FANTASTIC TF (accessible, helpful feedback, knowledgable, engaging, nice, etc.). Make sure to get her as your TF if you can.

Also, would've appreciated more creative midterm/final exam prompts – basically the same essay but different lengths.
Good class, make sure to do most of the readings. Sections are super helpful, and don't wait last minute to start the essays.
I would take this class whether it's a requirement or not. I learned so much about society, about racial and class dilemmas and interactions, and more. I felt seen in this class by both my peers and the teaching staff. I recommend you fully engage with the material––you'll learn a lot from it.
This is a required course and you can't really get out of it, but it can definitely still be enjoyable. The discussion posts are nice to reflect and test your understanding about theory. I wish that I went to more office hours with Emma to talk about the parts of text theory I was confused about. Would highly recommend office hours to go over the writing prompt (midterm & final). The reading guides are super useful so use those!!
This course was a great overview of sociological theory, I now feel much more confident in my ability to read and understand complex texts. Section and lecture brought a lot of variety to the course.
you will learn so much about sociology
You have to take this course if you're a Sociology concentrator—if you're not, there are better, more applicable/relevant electives to take.

To be frank, this course was a LOT of work and stress. The readings often felt inaccessible, and the Discussion Posts just felt unnecessary. However, the course staff are great! Shai is a fun instructor, and Emma is a PHENOMENAL TF! I think the staff is what makes this course even remotely bearable. Don't be afraid to meet with them if you're struggling at all!

Ultimately, my advice is to not go into this course expecting much. While learning the theory was very interesting, it also felt pointless and was very taxing. I will note that I hate doing dense, hard–to–understand readings, which this course had a lot of, so my feelings are very biased and personal. I think this course is a necessary evil, and I will never blame the teaching staff for the ills of theory and how concentrations are designed here.
Easily the best (and most rigorous) undergrad course in the sociology department. A wonderful environment to meet and discuss people who are genuinely interested in sociology! Course structure and materials are well chosen and teaching staff is great!
Shai is a wonderful professor (I've had him twice!!) and Emma is an amazing TF! The readings are dense, but I loved the class.
