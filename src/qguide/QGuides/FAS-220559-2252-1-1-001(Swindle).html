What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is so helpful in developing the skills necessary for writing a thoroughly thought out research paper. Professor Swindle is very detailed with his instructions in the class assignments and in presenting his own research. He always tries to make himself available to help his students.
if Professor Swindle is teaching this again in the future, I highly recommend taking it. This class is especially impactful if you are a social science, concentrator or humanities concentrator looking to explore how to work with quantitative data. I had a minimal background in coding in R. I took (STAT 100) before this course. I think taking (STAT 100) was very helpful preparation. If you don’t have any experience in coding in our or any coding language, you will find this course quite difficult. Even with my preparation, I still found this course to be difficult due to the rigor of creating your own research project. This course requires a lot of independence, although Jeffrey is very accommodating flexible and a great mentor. You would want to take this course with a lighter courseload.
Dr. Swindle is approachable and I encourage you to go to office hours, and take advantage of the extra support to address your coding struggles. You get as much as you put in, so try to understand the why and how and challenge yourself. Make yourself a manual to remember your notes and troubleshooting.
Some knowledge of R is definitely helpful for this class, which either way is amazing and I highly recommend for anyone interested in quantitative work or learning more about it!
Jeff is great!! He is very accommodating and is willing to meet outside of class. The classroom atmosphere was positive and conducive to learning. That said, I would recommend this class to students with at least some coding background. The pacing of this course is challenging if you do not have any coding experience. Personally, I found this class useful because I learned a new coding skill – how to analyze survey data in R. However, I think the course would benefit from focusing on either beginners or those with prior coding experience, rather than trying to accommodate both.
Swindle is an amazing professor. He was always ready to answer any questions we had in class. Moreover, he gave very good, tangible feedback to make our projects better.
