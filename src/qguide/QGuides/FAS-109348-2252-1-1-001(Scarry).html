TAKE THIS CLASS!! Scarry is incredible and will encourage you to love the Brontës. Very eye opening.
This is a great class! Professor Scarry runs such a productive and warm section environment, and you will dig into every text from multiple angles. People share their thoughts every week, based on their writing assignments, so there was never any pause, and a lot of variety in what we talked about. Warning (it goes without saying) there is a mountain of reading you must do. The novels are long and there are many of them. I would recommend reading them over the break. Coming in with Wuthering Heights, Jane Eyre, Agnes Grey in my back pocket was clutch. I wish I had read either Villette or Shirley, too. Especially since I was writing my thesis.
A great class, but be prepared for lots of reading!
One of my favorite seminars taken within the English department. Professor Scarry is so welcoming, engaging, and attentive to her students, and the overall class engagement felt very genuine and exciting. It is, of course, on the heavier side with reading — but it's run almost as a book club, in the sense that it encourages you to read genuinely interesting literature every week, and sets a great motive for reading consistently throughout the semester. The professor leaves detailed feedback on all of the reading responses, and encourages you to follow your own niche areas of interest; it's overall a very encouraging class, could not recommend enough.
Elaine is INCREDIBLE. Seminar with her felt incredibly oriented to the text and the experience of reading in an open spirit of joint collaboration and adventure, rather than trying to be "smart" for the sake of saying smart things. In that sense, even though I feel like I really intimately experienced these books, it never felt forced or stuffy in the way that I can sometimes find literature classes (as a non–concentrator). The reading load is pretty hefty, but it was almost all seniors this year, most of whom were writing theses (myself included) and I think it was a nice distraction from other work. Some of the tangential parts of the syllabus (eg the books not written by brontes) sometimes felt extraneous but all in all an incredible experience that has made me leave college excited to read more, even though the particular work of the Brontes era is not necessarily my favorite type of literature –– Elaine's love of reading and literature is just so inspiring and worth spending time around.
You read A LOT for this class. Pace yourself and stay on top of it (1 book a week, essentially). But makes discussions worthwhile. Prof. Scarry is absolutely incredible. So wise and always has fascinating comments.
Take this class! Prof. Scarry is truly one of the most wonderful professors out there — she is kind, witty, energetic. From the very first class I was enchanted. Everything she says is gold, and all her feedback is impeccable. She truly gives the most feedback out of all my English professors, down to line edits. She cares!! And she is so happy to meet with students and chat with them outside of class. This is the definition of a person who should teach!!
Professor Scarry is such a thoughtful, kind, and encouraging teacher. She provides handwritten comments on each of your short essays and remembers the details in class, which is amazing. She facilitates a warm and encouraging classroom atmosphere.
Take this course!!! Professor Scarry is incredible and the readings are phenomenal. You will not regret it!
ugh i am obsessed with elaine scarry and simply learned so much. easily one of the top courses i've taken during my time here. if u get in, please please accept.
The readings are long and not the easiest to get into so make sure you have time in your schedule to actually complete them. Other than that the assignments during the semester are not the hardest, but the final paper is big and counts for a major portion of the grade.
This course was an absolute delight. Professor Scarry is an amazing teacher and has so much knowledge about the Brontës and the technical aspects of how literature works. Our discussions were engaging and it felt like everyone was invested in the novels. The short papers give you a chance to express your opinions throughout the semester without being too much work/stress, and Professor Scarry gives thoughtful feedback on each one. The reading is heavy but worth it because these novels are so important and well–known. I would definitely take this course — it was one of my favorite courses I've taken at Harvard.
