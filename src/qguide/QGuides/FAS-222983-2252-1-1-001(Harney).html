What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
An interesting course to take, but definitely not a gem. The topics covered were interesting, and you would probably enjoy this class if you're really into human population genetics or ancient population genetics. The article discussions were interesting and there weren't too many assignments throughout the semester although the final paper was very long. The professor was clearly very enthusiastic about the subject and easy to reach outside of class.

Given all this, lectures were very dry – the lecture format was the professor reading off very dense slides, which were almost word–for–word taken from the textbook, for the entirety of the class period. Tests did not feel representative of the material covered at all, and there was no guidance given for studying – essentially, the only way to do well was to memorize the hundreds of lecture slides, since some questions asked things covered by one or two lines in one lecture slide. The grading wasn't harsh but it felt like the professor always took points off of every assignment and wasn't very specific about how to improve. Not a bad class to take but not very engaging.
You learn quite a bit, but there aren't many assignments so doing poorly on one will tank you. Professor Eadaoin is super open to talking things through with you, and her insights into pop gen are so valuable. Literature responses were tedious but doable. Tests are moderate, first was definitely better than the second, but be sure to pick a topic you like for final project otherwise...
I highly recommend this class to anyone interested in population genetics. The first half of the class is more models/calculations than I expected but it's all skill building that is really useful and relevant to the topic of the class, which is really interesting. Prof Harney is also an incredibly effective instructor, breaking down complex topics in ways that give you a really deep understanding of them. She has so much knowledge about the field and is the most engaged and engaging instructor I've had at Harvard–– taking a class with her is a huge privilege. The class has no busywork –– every test, article review, pset, etc is relevant and useful. If you're interested in HEB, whether as a concentrator, or for an elective, I'd really recommend you take the class.
This course provides such a great intro to the world of population genetics, without the complexity of statistics and math! The workload also isn't too much, as long as you keep up with the content. Having such a small sample size is a big plus as well, because you get to know your peers very well.
