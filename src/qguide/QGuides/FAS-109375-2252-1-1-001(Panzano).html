What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
High workload, high payoff. As an HAA Architecture Studies concentrator, I needed to take this class, but I am glad I took it regardless. It totally took over my semester and was often very frustrating with late nights in the studio and early mornings scrambling, but I loved it and became friends with pretty much everyone in the class. Truly think I came to a new level of understanding of architecture, design, and the creative process as a whole, with every little consideration that goes into it. Was very cool to feel that happen in real time, which hadn't happened to me before.
Know that you're not bad at architecture just because the only kind of feedback you get is negative critique, that is just the structure of the GSD so that's how the course operates.
Also, be prepared to work a lot.

Personally I didn't have much of a life outside of this class this semester and had to pull a few all–nighters (as someone who had never done an all–nighter before). It felt like an unsupportive introduction class because it didn’t feel like an environment where you could make mistakes, ask questions, or not be knowledgeable. There was some active eye–rolling when I asked questions even though it was an intro class and so of course you would have questions. They also don’t teach how to use of the software in a very clear way which makes the assignments stressful and hard to figure out. For reference, a metaphor someone else in the class came up with was that this class is like having a paper due before every single class (meets Mondays and Wednesdays) but each time it’s in a new language and instead of being taught the grammar of any of those languages you’re just told everything you did wrong. It’s quite a stressful process but you will become very capable even though there's quite a learning curve. I wouldn't recommend this class unless you have to take it but you will feel proud of yourself afterwards.
Some weeks/projects require a significant amount of time. It can be frustrating, but it is rewarding if you stick with it.
This class can be quite time consuming, but you're allocated enough time to complete projects comfortably. A fun experience to try a class that is very hands on, I learned a lot about architectural thinking and was always prompted to explore new ideas. I'd definitely recommend if you think this would something you'd be interested in, especially if you're considered pursuing architecture and want to see what a studio is like.
This class will suck up ALL of your time and live in your head rent free. But in the end it will feel worth it, with your final projects in front of you and a whole new skillset of 3d and physical fabrication skills behind you. This class will give you a little taste of architecture school as it is experienced in the real world. The first half of the semester WILL be difficult, so please do not load yourself with too much work outside of this class. The second half feels much more relaxed as you have gotten past the steep learning curves for the most part and just have to focus on designing a structure for a given site. I also bonded with my studiomates quite a bit over the hard work and long nights spent at the studio. Be prepared for a LOT.
this is by far the most rewarding class I have taken at harvard. given that I am on the architetcure track, the insane time commitment and learning curve were beyond worth it to me. I feel like I've learned skills and vocabulary that I couldn't have learned in any other class at harvard. and getting to produce large scale projects that aren't essays is so cool! it is a huge time suck, though, so definitely not recommended if you aren't very determined to learn design as a specialized skill.
