Comments
This was a really great FYSEM!! Professor Sampson is clearly very knowledgable and passionate about the area of study and facilitates some very engaging and captivating discussions throughout the course. The readings and assignments (only 2: one final paper about your hometown and some memos throughout the semester) were light and interesting, especially compared to other seminar classes with loads of reading. Some really fun components to the course as well, like guest speakers (honestly one of my favourite parts of the course) and a visit to the Harvard Museum! As long as you truly have an interest in crime and justice and want to learn more about the theories, patterns and solutions that have been proposed over the years, this course will be perfect.
TAKE THIS SEMINAR! Professor Sampson is truly one of the best in the field of criminology and social analysis, and he helps make the classroom a place where you learn from so many people with different beliefs and experiences, including your classmates! After taking this course, you will better understand crime in your city, the US, and in general, and will become a more open and curious person. A decent amount of reading per week, but super low pressure, and the only formal assignment (final paper) is very flexible and allows you to explore any topic and place of your choice! You will meet a bunch of guest speakers too, all of which are very knowledgeable and moving!
Do the readings on time. Take adequate notes. Participate as much as possible in class. You’ll thank yourself later.
If you have any interest in crime, criminal and/or social justice, and even African American Studies take this class! There's so much to be explored. This course will definitely expand your perspective on crime and justice in America.
Thought–provoking discussion and the readings are VERY interesting –– doing all the readings in–depth isn't necessary to be able to participate in discussions but I would recommend doing at least some of them. In totality, they offer a really comprehensive view on such an important/pertinent subject. The readings are also not very dense and easy to read quickly/understand. Sampson's book as a lot of graphs/numbers/stats that can be intimidating at first but he clarifies and explains them in more detail in class.
This was a very interesting seminar if you like crime trends and looking at the deeper reasons for crime and disorder within our cities. Professor Sampson is very knowledgeable and the class readings are interesting and relevant to the subject matter. The guests for class are always very interesting and the final paper was enjoyable to write since you had a lot of freedom to choose a city that interested you.
