Solid Expos choice. Moderate difficulty. Coursework met expectations and the teaching (Professor Taleen) exceeded expectations.
I didn't think I'd like Expos as much as I did. Expos is often seen as a chore that most Harvard students need to get done and over with, and the more I hear about the experiences of other students, the more grateful I am for ending up in a section like this. Taleen is fantastic, including giving constructive and thoughtful comments, leading engaging and balanced class discussions, and also just being a great person to talk to and catch up with. The course description is a little confusing, but you do end up learning how to think about language in addition to learning how to write about it: the class is more–or–less how language affects individual people and then how language affects society. We do touch up on some interesting linguistic theories from Walter Benjamin (but which is not in any way the standard linguistics one might cover in Ling 83), but ultimately we read a lot on how language permeates every aspect of our lives in different ways, which was very fascinating. Ultimately, of the four classes I took this semester, I'd probably have this as my #1. Take this section if you can!
I strongly encourage you to take this EXPOS 20 class.
This class is one that you certainly need to have as your top choice! This class helped me grow so much as a writer, and the feedback that is provided definitely helped me improve. I think that this class makes you wonder why so many people dislike expos since the class felt like it improved my writing, and made me a more analytical reader. If you want to really improve your writing, you should take this expos.
This was hands down the best writing course I have ever taken. The course transformed how I write completely: from initial drafting, to revising, to analyzing material. What you will learn in this class will not only help with writing, but also understanding more deeply language itself.
If you are wondering which EXPOS section to rank, RANK THIS ONE HIGH!!! Taleen is absolutely amazing and one of the most chill preceptors, from what I have heard from all of my friends. This class allows you to explore so many different types of topics within the scope of language, and the conferences with Taleen will be amazing in helping you narrow down your focus and truly understand your arguments. The class also has VERY, VERY fair grading, much better than from what I have heard. As long as you have all of the components on the rubric she gives you guys, you are almost guaranteed to get a good grade. Please rank this one high, Taleen is amazing and you will not regret it!
Take this course! Given the material and course work, you'll have a great time learning.
I would definitely recommend this expos section because Taleen the preceptor is absolutely great, very supportive and understanding. I also think that the course draws a very wide range of perspectives due to it being about language, which allowed me to meet some really interesting peers. The research essay is very wide open, and as far as expos sections go I would definitely take this one!
TAKE THIS CLASS! Taleen is an amazing preceptor who truly cares about her students. It may get tough at times, but go to office hours and the writing centers and you will push through. This class really got me to see how much of an impact language has throughout our society and really expanded my point of view. HIGHLY RECOMMEND.
I LOVED this course! I fully did not expect Expos to be my favorite class this semester, but I was proven wrong. Taleen is a wonderful instructor who makes class engaging, provides good feedback, and is very reasonable about the amount of work she assigns. The readings were genuinely interesting, I enjoyed our class discussions, and essay conferences were really helpful. Overall, the course was well–organized and did not feel stressful.
