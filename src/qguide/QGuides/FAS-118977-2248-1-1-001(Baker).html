What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The few Friday sessions we had were not always the most productive; I wish they were more frequent and (peer) workshop–style for accountability. Would have also appreciated soft, internal deadlines for accountability. The thesising experience is largely dependent on individual advisors.
This course does not add any stress to the thesis experience – it mainly is the informational class component to supplement your weekly meetings with your advisor. Don't be afraid of the Friday class meeting – you only meet a few times each semester.
Make sure you like your thesis topic. The process will be long and tedious at times, but if you are passionate about your research questions it makes it more bearable.
The thesis tutorial is not a particularly strenuous part of the thesis process, which is very helpful! The courses function as a time to get together and go over logistics or talk through general questions. The peer review workshop also happens during class at the end of the semester.
I have found some trouble communicating with my thesis advisor.
The required class meetings for HS99A are not the most useful. Mostly, it feels like a regurgitation of the syllabus that leaves you wondering why you had to spend an hour and a half of your time in the Science Center. I am thankful that we don't have required meetings every week (like in the Junior Tutorial) because that would have been frustrating, when the time is better spent on actually researching and writing. This said, I think the required class meetings for HS99 could really use some reform to be more useful.
