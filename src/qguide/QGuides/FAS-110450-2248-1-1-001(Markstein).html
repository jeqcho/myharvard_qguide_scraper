Overall, I am glad I took this course but I have some comments. First of all, the class is quite hard. I thought it was harder than Chem17. There is an absolute avalanche of content that you have to constantly memorize through sheer force of will.

The main gripe I had with this course was the teaching format. Before each lecture, you have to watch a series of informational videos on a website. These videos are FILLED with information, usually around 20–40 minutes. Professor Viel dumps a ton of information at you in these videos and you have to memorize a lot of the specifics of the enzymes and pathways mentioned, so watching them while taking notes usually took me around 2 hours before each lecture. (Exams were usually around 5+ additional hours of studying , and they happen every other week).

Professor Viel was very nice, knowledgable, and he was a warm presence in the classroom. With that said, I found the in–person lectures really unengaging. So so so unengaging. Most of the information seemed to be repeated from the online videos that you had to watch before lecture. So your brain kind of shuts off because you’ve heard it already. HOWEVER, sometimes new information is presented and this new information is important cause it appears on the exams. If you miss it cause you were zoning out then you need to spend a lot of time learning it on your own (lecture is not recorded). Overall, the in–person lectures sometimes left me wondering why I showed up in the first place, or spent so long watching the videos, due to the redundancy.

I have to give a special shout out to my section TF, whom I will not name. This was the worst TF I have ever encountered. He seemed unenthusiastic about TFing the course — during section, he did not seem to know any of the material we were learning at all (his research was in other areas). Every time we asked a question, he would either Ctrl + F the slides he was given, or search the question on Google. Most of the time, our questions were left unanswered. With that said he definitely seems like a cool and nice person, but the TFing I was like often like what ?????

Also worth mentioning is the oppressively frigid class comaraderie. The energy in the room seemed to get sucked out of the air every time lecture started. I think most people were online shopping or playing games on their computer or whatever. The vibes were so so so dead, Professor Viel would ask a question and you could hear crickets.

I have not taken the MCAT yet, but it seems like everything you learn in this course is on the MCAT, and the professor tries to help people prepare for it as well. There is no final.
