The grading is ridiculous. I had to take this as a very senior CS undergrad because I needed one more requirement. Any advanced programming technique is strongly discourages (I would lose points). Similarly, the checks for understanding are graded so arbitrarily.
Be prepared for super long hours on a couple weeks. I think taking it in the spring is totally fine, but also be prepared to not have as much fun as your fall peers due to lack of events, and to potentially have less access to office hours due to the smaller class size. On the whole though, TAs are super amazing and communicative, and you'll be totally fine.
Take this with an open mind and only if you can devote 12–16 hours per week.
CS50 is a great introduction to computer science and exposes you to a little bit of many different languages. It's a little bit of a time commitment so be prepared to work. As for the final project, start earlier rather than later if at all possible and do it with a group.
