Wes's lectures are the best. Definitely one of the most close–knit classes. The class goes over single variable calculus (and a little bit of topology), then multivariable calculus and analysis of L2 function space. PSETS do take time; maybe start early.
Take Math25b with Wes Cain! If you are inclined to studying math and willing to take the challenge, by the end of the semester you will grow for sure. You will be more than prepared to take other upper level math courses coming out of it.
Take it! You learn a lot + get a lot of exercise with proofs. The course is very well structured, Wes is amazing, and his lectures are clear, engaging, and time–efficient. The psets are challenging but rewarding, and most important, the community is a guaranteed benefit of taking this class. Idk why you should take this course over 22 or 55, but I have 0.00 regret, and I can tell you I absolutely loved math 25.
For me, Math 25B was significantly harder than 25A, especially conceptually. For context, I have no background in multi/real analysis, and I think having seen multi before would make the second half of the course more manageable. I don't have many comments on the material itself, and it almost exactly follows the syllabus.

This year, Wes taught both 25A and B, so all seven of us knew what we were signing up for. Just like 25A, the lectures were fantastic, and "caring yet inflexible" sums everything up. The CAs/TF were great, and I felt supported throughout the class.

In terms of academic benefits, doing more psets/taking more tests did improve my math background, and I now have a strong foundation in real analysis/multi. However, tests were brutal, and I wouldn't go into this class expecting to get an A.

Personally, I found the 25B community much stronger than 25A, most likely due to a much smaller class size and everyone struggling more. I fully expect to stay in contact with everyone throughout the my next three years, and I think it's one of the biggest benefits.

Since Wes isn't teaching next year, it's hard to say exactly what will happen to the course, as a large reason that I took the course is Wes's lectures and incredible organization for the class.

Overall, I have no regrets taking 25B, and while Math 25 is incredibly intense and isn't for everyone, it was definitely the highlight of my freshman year.
As an important disclaimer, this course will be very different if not taught by Wes. Wes is an incredible lecturer who is incredible at creating an overarching narrative for his math courses. However, his exams are extremely punishing, and some of the psets for this course SHOULD NOT be tackled without help (you can lose many hours thinking through a single problem and make absolutely no progress until you go to office hours).

I can't say how much of this was due to my own personal first year spring slump, but this course ended up being much more punishing and much less rewarding than 25A. You will need to put in significantly more effort to stay at the same level of understanding or grades. This class could be a good test of whether theoretical math is for you, since it includes a moderate dose of topology, some solid real analysis, and this year there was some functional analysis. However, as Wes himself said, this intensive format can hurt more than it helps and make math less appealing. Unless you love Wes' teaching from 25A and really care about this material, I would caution against committing to this course.
Wes is a fantastic lecturer, and he might be the best you have here. His lectures are clear, effective, and well–paced. The problem sets, while time–consuming, are very good and help solidify both course material and advanced proof–writing skills; the CAs are all very helpful when you are working through problem sets or struggling with real analysis concepts. The course is definitely difficult: I spent a lot of time on it, struggled on the exams, and didn't finish with what I would generally consider a strong grade. However, I feel that I learned far more about real analysis than I could have from any other course in a way that was extremely rigorous, with much of the credit going to Wes' instruction. Absolutely take it, especially if the GOAT Wes is teaching it. Class community was also elite.
Wes is THE GOAT. He is a phenomenal lecturer who is capable of explaining complicated material in a very accessible way. His enthusiasm and devotion to his class and the students in it are very inspiring. That being said, 25b is a hard class, definitely harder than 25a and one that is likely to kill your GPA, but you come of it with increased mathematical maturity and independence as well as proof stamina. Personally, I preferred the content of 25a, but I am more of an algebra guy, so take this as you will. If you're on the fence between 25b and 55b, I would definitely shop both especially given that next year's 25b will be taught by a different instructor. But personally, I feel like I gained a better understanding of real analysis in 25b than I would have in 55b. All in all, this was a great class with a fantastic community and an incredible instructor.
