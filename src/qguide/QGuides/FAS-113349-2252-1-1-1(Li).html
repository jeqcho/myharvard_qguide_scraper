What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
As is typical with graduate–level courses, I would recommend this class to students who already have some sense of their research interests in economics. That said, if you are interested in economic theory, this course is a must. It equips you with essential tools such as fixed point theorems, monotone comparative statics, and envelope theorems.

Shengwu is an excellent teacher who presents the material with both formal rigor and clear intuition. Importantly, he also devotes time to offering practical and thoughtful advice on how to approach research in economic theory, which adds significant value beyond the technical content.

This course is especially well–suited to students who enjoy working with abstract models and are looking to deepen their theoretical toolkit.
Would highly recommend! I was always looking forward to class! Probably one of my favorite classes throughout my four years here. Shengwu is very clear and the topics and techniques are at the frontiers of research. A must–take!
This course is fantastic—I think it's the best course I've taken at Harvard. I think you should take this course if you're interested in exploring a discipline where the distance between mathematics and the world is small: i.e., if you're interested in questions about strategy, behavior, or the world, but find yourself interested in math, this is exactly the right course.

I came into this course with significant mathematical background, but little economics background (only 1011a). It was approachable for me, but I was also very excited about the material, and I found myself thinking about the ideas all the time. If you're an undergraduate with some good math background (i.e. you've taken some upper level math courses), then this course will likely be accessible to you. For reference, I found the problem sets in this course harder than the problem sets than the ones in upper level
undergraduate math courses (i.e. they are not relatively routine exercises), and closer on par to the graduate ones.

It is really inspiring stuff, and Shengwu is a great expositor of the material, as well as a lovely person to talk to about all things economics.
