What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Please do the readings.
The material and the fun topic. I indicated the class for my friends.
Definitely rank this class in your Expos lottery, especially if you have any interest in humor. The readings for this class are very interesting, and most of them are fairly short. Professor Turley also does a great job facilitating conversation and getting you to think critically. He is very thorough in his feedback as well. Take advantage of the full drafts you'll be asked to write: that is where you'll get feedback before you submit your final revision, and you want to get the most accurate and reflective feedback as possible. Overall, a great class!
PICK A TOPIC THAT IS INTERESTING TO YOU (not only for Expos class selection but also essay topic selections)
I enjoyed this class. The professor is great and very supportive of his students. The readings can get confusing at times, but in class discussions typically clear them up. The papers are also quite difficult... Don't procrastinate!
I went into Expos thinking that it was going to be my most boring class, but it actually turned out to be one of my favorites! And this is coming from someone whose favorite subject is not English or Writing. Professor Turley creates an environment where everyone feels like their voice can be heard, and although it started out a little stiff, we had some great laughs by the end of our class. This class requires a good amount of work, but it is so satisfying to see your hard work pay off in the end!
Pretty solid expos class. 1 comedic performance essay, 1 research paper essay, and 1 comedic review (so only two essays while other classes had 3). I somewhat enjoyed the subject, but it eventually did get a bit boring talking about all these details of humor. The professor is really great and understanding though! In the beginning, you have 2 readings that are like 30–40 pages, but the rest are not bad. Just start assignments ahead of time and be prepared to have to change your entire thesis!
This class is not filled with the laughter and jokes that you are probably expecting from the name, but it is still interesting and allows you a lot of freedom in choosing topics for the essays.
