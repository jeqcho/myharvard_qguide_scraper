What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
TAKE THIS CLASS. Super chill, really cool experience to be able to compose your own piece and have it be performed by a professional string quartet. Honestly one of the most uplifting musical experiences that I've had at Harvard.
This class was really cool! But it was not really what I expected. Chaya is a very modern composer (she has music online you can listen to for a sense); super atonal, and she is all about getting you out of the box. People in our class had really different music backgrounds, and it was so fun seeing what that meant for everyone. This isn't a class that will refine your understanding of super technical aspects of composition, (harmony, voicing, whatever), it's much more about the broad, artistic aspects of what you're writing. Very little direction is given because Chaya and Ali want you to just write what you're going too write, which can be scary or it can be super fun.
Anyway take it if you want to just write a lot and take a pretty chill class and hear what other people are writing
This course is truly amazing. I had never composed before taking the class, and I've learned so so much because of it. I ended up spending a lot of time on the composition assignments for this class, because it made me want to. Chaya is amazing and will help you unlock your full potential you never knew you had. At the end of this class you will have a totally new perspective on music.
This class opened my mind to what I was capable of as a musician. I've only ever worked within conventional frameworks. I feel kind of like this class removed my skull, and now I have infinite musical options available to me.
