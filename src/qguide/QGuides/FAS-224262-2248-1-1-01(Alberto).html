Professor Alberto is an enthusiastic, dedicated, and caring educator and discussion leader. Any student would benefit from taking a class with her, regardless of the subject—but with a topic as rich as the Black press and race in Latin America, it'd be a shame to miss this course.
It is a very interesting and engaging class, but be prepared to read and write a lot
This work requires a lot of work and preparation before class time. The work you spend doing outside of the classroom is much more important than what is expected of you to get done during class time. Laura is very understanding and a phenomenal instructor, and the lessons and conversations covered are far from 'simple.' This course demands considerable effort and preparation well ahead of class sessions. It's clear that the time invested outside the classroom overshadows the relatively limited expectations during class. While Paulina is undoubtedly an understanding and exceptional instructor, one cannot overlook that the material presented is anything but 'simple.' The depth and complexity of the lessons and discussions require a serious commitment from students, often leaving them feeling overwhelmed rather than adequately prepared for the challenges ahead.
You should take this course!!! Prior, I'd never taken a history course and was intimidated by it being a seminar with mostly grad students, but I throughly enjoyed this class! The readings are reasonable for a seminar and feed quite nicely into each other; the weekly timeline posts (ie. discussion posts) are great prep for class and graded on completion; the assignments are spaced out nicely and are useful for the final project; and the feedback on assignments is fantastic and timely. I think all of the students in the class are either from Latin America or study Latin America, but I did not have that background and didn't feel I was particularly disadvantaged. I would also add the grad students were SUPER interesting and lovely and would add components of their research to the class which enriched our conversations. Would definitely recommend this course for anyone looking for a reasonable class looking to dive deeply into the histories of Latin American countries (namely: Cuba, Brazil, Uruguay, Argentina).
Anyone interested in the history of Black populations in Latin America should take this course! It is a really unique opportunity to learn about historical Black newspapers and publications in Cuba, Uruguay, Argentina, and Brazil, as well as the general historical context that informed what these Afro–descendant authors were writing about. The assignments are very carefully designed to give students a glimpse into how historians work with the Latin American Black press, which was also another unique component of the course.
It is an excellent class—mind–blowing and very thought–provoking. The instructor is well prepared, and all the topics are well selected.
