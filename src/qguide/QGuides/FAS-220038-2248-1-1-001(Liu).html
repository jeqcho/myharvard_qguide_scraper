What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is great! It complements the Classical Chinese Ethical and Political Theory very well. The vocab quizzes every week are very straightforward, and studying the vocab is enjoyable and doesn't take long. The teaching staff is 10/10 and supports you in many ways; they are very understanding and encouraging. The final project is so open–ended, and there were so many creative and fun ones that I was really impressed by. I took this to finish my language requirement, but it really made me appreciate classical Chinese a lot more. Also, no midterm and final exams was awesome. Definitely recommend!
This increased my interest in Chinese History, Culture, Literature, and Philosophy. It was an amazing introduction to Chinese culture and its study, and it also made me more confident in my ability to think and express myself independently in Chinese. If you've taken previous Chinese courses, this one is very different because it emphasizes content and original thinking more, which was very welcome after having to always make sentences in a very pre–determine style. The material is interesting and the professors/TAs are very passionate, which allows you to explore the material in whatever direction you find most interesting. Workload is not low but they're very flexible.
It's a great content–based Chinese course with minimal work. The homework is fairly do–able, but you will have to teach yourself the Chinese part; Class will be for going over the ideas of each philosopher/some discussion.
This class is not less work than the standard Chinese classes, in fact, I think it is more work. The readings and classical Chinese grammar patterns are much more difficult. The weekly homeworks are also longer. Also, the class is really long and there are less opportunities to practice speaking. However, you do get the opportunity to speak with other students with a range of Chinese skills, which can challenge you and be fun. The content is also really interesting and pairs well with the gened. It does a good job combining Chinese philosophy with teaching the grammar and vocab of 130, and you also get a bunch of random vocab about philosophy/history. Liu lao shi is super sweet, accommodating, and a fun lecturer. The TFs also really want to support you. The final projects were all really impressive and enjoyable to do. If you have a genuine interest in classical Chinese or being able to talk about philosophy in Chinese or want the experience of learning in depth about something in Chinese (rather than using short topics to learn Chinese), I'd take the course, but be ready to put in at least some time. If you want to improve your speaking skills and/or have less time and patience for classical Chinese, I'd probably stick with the gened and a normal Chinese class.
Great teaching team, well structured course, nice alternative to daily class meetings of regular Chinese classes. I took it after completing 120b, amd it was very challenging, esp the reading. The homework took up the better part of my weekends, mainly because the reading took so long!! I would still 100% recommend though, I learned a lot and had so much fun on the final project. Just be willing to put in the time!
I took 130XA/B last year, and overall I'm really happy that I took this course instead of 140XA to round out the end of my language citation. Great, engaging lectures with a good course organization that I really appreciated. Homework can sometimes be a bit long and repetitive, and there is some pretty major overlap in vocab for the first half of the class, but overall it was a really valuable learning experience for me and deepened my appreciation for the language. I would recommend taking it alongside the Gened–it's a great way to knock out two requirements and I think makes the content more manageable (but you'll probably be fine without it too).
