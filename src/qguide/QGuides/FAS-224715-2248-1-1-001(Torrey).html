For future students, I highly suggest taking this course. It is an excellent introduction to the immigration system and an interactive experience that can help you understand how advocacy can influence the world around us in big or small ways. The community you will find in this course will be a passionate and welcoming one that will love to get to know you and the immigration system alongside you. With the added benefit that the course is quite laid back and can be a great mode booster if the semester ever feels like a drag.
super fun class and the professors are super nice and the CA is super helpful as well
I am a Mexican–American student, with Immigrant parents. The topic of immigration was not always welcomed in my household therefore I was not super aware of the process my parents were forced to go through. This class allowed me to gain the knowledge and courage to start that conversation with my parents. This class allowed me to feel more in tune with my background and family.
Take this course if you are interested in immigration at all!! I cannot recommend this course enough. Professor Ardalan, Professor Torrey, and Helen are absolutely amazing. They are caring and very knowledgeable about the immigration system. They bring in their own professional experience from the immigration clinic at Harvard Law School. We were able to hear from a wide range of incredible speakers from immigration attorneys to doctors to mental health professionals. We also got to hear from a panel of Harvard Law students, which was helpful as someone who was considering pursuing law. You will not regret taking this course!

Take it!!!!! I could not recommend it more it was such an incredible class and I feel lucky to have had the opportunity to take it and learn from the wonderful professors.
Oh my god. Where do I start?! I LOVED this section and truly hope that it returns next year for the Class of 2029. Our professors were genuinely the sweetest educators I have ever met. They showered us with so much love and attention despite the fact that we were significantly less knowledgable or mature than their typical demographic (law school students) and perfectly tailored the coursework to fit our level of understanding. There was very little "dumbing down" of material and I learned a lot! The highlight of the class was our trip to the JFK Federal Building to witness immigration law in action, which was a unique experience that I don't think I could have gotten from any other seminar or course offering here at the undergraduate level. I cannot help but rave about our professors ESPECIALLY. Phil and Sabi are the most incredible, down–to–earth, lovely people I have had the pleasure to learn from.
TAKE THIS SEMINAR! The professors are incredibly kind and genuinely care about your success. The conversations are very interesting, as is the course material. Come with an open mind and you will be so grateful you did!
100% recommend this course to anyone interested in law, and most importantly, the immigration system. Sabi and Phil are incredible law school professors who try their best to imitate the way they teach law students onto first–years in a way that makes sense. Because of the class size, you build an incredible community to fall back on. The guests we have biweekly are also a blast to hear from, as they come from different backgrounds and work experiences. Our CA, Helen, was also super great and it was awesome receiving feedback from her for our essays.
