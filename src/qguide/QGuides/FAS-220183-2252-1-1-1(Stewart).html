I think the way I described this course most to my friends was: weird. But I mean that in a good way. We talked about a broad range of topics and artists which was cool and challenging, and usually quite enjoyable. A lot of the material is performance or visual art like photographs, so that made the assigned content fun, but there was also a lot of dry reading which you should probably spend some time with (although I definitely didn't comprehend a lot of it). There's enough range in the subject matter that you can still participate in class without having a solid grip on the readings. You don't need any German background to take this course, although it can be nice if you want to connect more deeply with the German artists. The class also attracts a wide range of concentrations, so it's cool to take a small course with such a diverse group of scholars. Professor Stewart is extremely passionate about the subject matter and does a good job of facilitating discussion and encouraging everyone's ideas.
Really really cool readings and visual materials, and Prof. Stewart is fabulous! This course made me enjoy going to art museums. Super relevant and thought–provoking in today's world of images, internet, and AI. Everyone else in the class was enthusiastic, invested, and amazing. Chill (meets once a week & 2 medium–length papers) but packed with interesting content & intense discussions, highly recommend :)
This was one of the best courses I've taken at Harvard. It gives students a glimpse into media and tech studies that is hard to find anywhere else at this college. The material is far–ranging and truly interdisciplinary (a word that gets thrown around a lot but is actually applicable here). If you are at all interested in the relationship between technology, art, humans, apparatus and body, algorithm, feminism, war, film, performance art, photography, reality, German culture and thought, take this class – you will be a much better and more well rounded thinker because of it. And you'll get to feel like you're "in the know" on a lot of cool stuff :) This has been one of the only courses I've been a part of where every discussion post and conversation in class truly prompted me to think differently and more expansively. That is rare!
Take this course if you are at all interested in media theory, art history, or AI/technology in society. I had no background in German art and this course felt approachable while going into depth in certain art movements as well as broader questions about human existence and the role of technology.
take this course!!! i feel incredibly fortunate to have experienced it. some classes i remember jotting stuff down verbatim that would span multiple pages because every turn of phrase was so pithy and every idea was amazing brilliant etc

the course title might be a bit ambiguous — this is a course about media theory. "ai" comes up very briefly, but the bulk of the class focuses on performance art, photography, film & their associated technologies. this may not sound as interesting but WS approaches everything with so much gusto that it became interesting regardless of prior buy–in (e.g. isn’t it weird that the inventor of composite photography was also the founder of modern eugenics?)

some of the texts are lowkey challenging but i feel like this course really rewards deep reading as WS will give you a lot of feedback on your writing & incorporate a lot of discussion post material into class. Transformative! 5 Stars
HIGHLY HIGHLY RECOMMEND! Professor Stewart is such a dedicated professor that really puts into a lot of thinking for his course design. The rich set of materials spanning dance, performance arts, photography and moving films that ultimately weave back into dialogue with each other will blow your mind. The class goes to dif venues including the art study center, film archive and offsite storage spaces that give you an immersive experience. The diverse composition of the class also helps fructify the discussions that happen in and beyond the classroom ––– applicable to all concentrators.
