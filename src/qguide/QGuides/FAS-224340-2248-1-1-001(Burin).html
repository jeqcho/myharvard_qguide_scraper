The class was great overall, would highly recommend. Staff is helpful, great at communicating, and great at bringing enthusiasm for the topics.
It's awesome but requires a time committment, especially if you aren't great at art!
Super great if you want to get your a Gen–Ed requirement out of the way. The class is very flexible and allows you to explore art in ways that you choose to.
There is no concrete grading system and interpretation of each piece of art created by the student is very subjective. The structure of each project is vague and the professor says that you can do what you want and explore different possibilities, but there is a specific good and bad that she has according to what she personally likes (which she does not tell). So despite exploring the areas of art, taking a project in my own direction, and putting effort and meaning into a piece, in the end I dont receive the grade that I think that I deserve and I dont get feedback on why I was graded that way. Also students dont receive feedback/grades on any of the projects until after classes are over and the transcript comes out.
This course is not necessarily difficult, but it is time consuming. Assignments are somewhat random and instructions are unclear. I was excited to learn about all sorts of sculpture, but the assignments are somewhat restricting and like I said, random. If you are like me and need specific instructions and expectations about assignments, and want to get things done efficiently without going on tangents this is probably not a great class. Instructors are extremely knowledgable, but you need to reach out to them for help if you need any, the teaching style is definitely sporadic. This did not make me excited about art–– however if you make the most of it and bounce around ideas with instructors (they seriously are so helpful if you use them) and are genuinely interested in sculpture, this could be a very great class for you!

Some time is spent in lecture talking about upcoming assignments and also random unrelated works of art, talking about the Carpenter building, etc. Lectures can feel very long, sometimes it is the whole 2 hr 45 minutes. Other time is spent in the studio working on assignments, and section time is also spent working on assignments.
This is a great course to fulfill the gened A&C if you enjoy art and are willing to spend time in the studio. Lectures can be a bit boring but studio time is great. I definitely spent more time on it than the average gened but I found it to be a great reprieve from other work. Its fun if you take it with friends because you can go in the studio together. That being said, I went in with a lot of acquaintances and met new people. I learned a lot about different types of art and how to use the studio. Woodshop was particularly fire. If you put in time outside of class and listen to feedback in class, you can get a lot out of the class and have fun while doing it.
This class can be quite a bit of time, and quite disorganized, but overall I did enjoy it. We got to make a chair and other projects and it filled my desire to take an art class at Harvard lol
Definitely stressful if you are not an art student because assignments and grades seem very subjective. That being said, it is a great class to develop artistic skills. Also very amazing to get access to the wood shop. I've been able to make some really amazing objects.
Very good class, would highly recommend.

Great opportunity to dedicate time to art, I really enjoyed it. Not a ton of instruction in techniques, which was unfortunate, but even so was fun to have some time to do art! Did require a significant amount of time, but found it well worth it!
This class is awesome and you should definitely take it. The instructors are great and the projects are fun and engaging. It definitely has some kinks to work out, but that will come with time since this is the first year this class is being offered as a GenEd. If you are engineer and are tired of PSets and technical engineering, this is a great creative outlet class that will make you think different about materials that you may be super familiar with already. Also, take the time to talk to the instructors and TF's about their own work and experience. They all are a wealth of knowledge and can really open up what you can take away from this class.
Fun if you take it with some friends, and you'll definitely pick up some life skills. Worked with a lot of different materials and got in the woodshop a lot – could definitely see myself using some of these learnings later on in my life
TAKE THIS CLASS. It was for sure my favorite class this semester! You get to experiment with all kinds of materials and Katarina really pushes you to explore them in depth. It is cool to enter the studio every week and see what crazy cool things your peers have come up with. I truly truly enjoyed that class.
This art gened will take some time. It's a bit hard to gauge how much work you should put in to get an A. However because art is so subjective it is nice that what you produce doesn't have to be perfect, just make sure to update your section leaders on your progress for feedback.
Take this class. I absolutely loved it. Professor Burin is amazing and super enjoyable to work with. If you have any interest in art, sculpting, working with wood you will love this class.
Very challenging and time consuming course. Recommend only for highly skilled sculptors as other forms of artwork are unacceptable. Your work will be constantly critiqued and you cannot construct anything that looks like a tangible item, i.e., animals or people.
TAKE THIS GENED. You get out what you put in, and you will be well–served by buying into the processes of making good work. You do not need any sculpting or art background to take this course, just an interest in the materials you are working with and a willingness to explore your own creativity.
This is a fun course!! It takes thinking, but is mostly just a great way to work with your hands.
