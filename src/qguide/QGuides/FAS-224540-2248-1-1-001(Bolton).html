What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Professor Bolton is lovely and this class in general is great, so if you're into reading (and especially analyzing everything you read) this is a great course for you. The novella selection is very good and I barely found novellas that were boring/I didn't like, and although at the very beginning everyone was a bit awkward about talking about the books in class, in the end the conversations became very engaging. I hadn't realised how much you can learn about the book by talking to others about it, and I was able to get so much more from each book by listening to my classmates and their interpretations and things they picked up on.

I do want to add a disclaimer for the class, though: it is a heavy amount of work for a first–year seminar. Apart from writing a two–page entry every week for the novellas (which, at least for me, takes a long time), you have four more complete papers for the class. They aren't very long, but I felt like it was unfair to have them assigned simultaneously to the normal readings and writings, since some of the tasks literally involve rereading a book, meaning one week you will essentially read and write about two books. These extra papers detracted from the experience, since their due date usually coincided with exams, and demotivated me from reading.
Solid class, pretty manageable workload, novellas can be hit or miss, however discussion can get pretty dry/boring pretty often, recommended for those who want an easy class and are interested in reading/novellas
Workload is not heavy, enjoyable readings
Professor Bolton is a brilliant instructor. It's clear that he has put a lot of thought and intentionality into this course. Although I did not enjoy every novella, I am glad I read them all. As a writer, I was exposed to so many new ideas, conventions, styles, and ideas, that I otherwise would have never known about (especially since a lot of these novellas are translated works!) As a reader, I'm thankful I got the opportunity to practice incorporating reading time into my weekly schedule (I hope to continue my reading schedule next semester too). The course *can* feel stressful if you put off your reading until the last minute, but generally, Prof. Bolton is very understanding and kind. He genuinely wants to make this course an enjoyable experience for his students and does not want to make your life more stressful than it already is. You can always feel free to reach out about anything if need be. I should also mention that he is a very thoughtful person in general; as the course went on, I felt like he genuinely got to know me and he was always telling me about different events or books about topics I had previously told him I was interested in. If the course interests you, I would definitely recommend that you take it!
