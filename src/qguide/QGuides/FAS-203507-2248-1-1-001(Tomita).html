Please avoid taking Nozomi Tomita's section. She is a very nice individual and seems excited about being at Harvard and conversing with others in ASL.

However, she is the most difficult person on the planet to get in contact with. I emailed her several times over the course of the semester regarding seeking help, missing class due to illness, issues regarding the online homework software, or questions regarding assignments: ALL of which went unanswered. She does not hold office hours and you have to email her to meet (so good luck getting help outside of class). Her assignments often are unclear on expectations, you rely heavily on the online software to learn, she will not post her materials for the quizzes until 7:30 am day of exams.... the list could go on.

I highly suggest looking to the other instructors to ensure you are actually learning and enjoying such an interesting language, without the stress of an unorganized instructor/course.
ASL 1 is such a great class and the ASL faculty on campus are super supportive, welcoming, and kind! I highly recommend everyone to take this course, regardless of your background or experience with ASL.
Be engaged during class, if you don't pay attention you will definitely miss something important.
TAKE THIS COURSE! It is absolutely amazing and will open your eyes to the world of Deaf culture and signing. It's so different than any other course I have ever taken before, but I learned so much and fully intend to keep learning ASL. I cannot recommend highly enough! It is incredibly immersive and challenging, but Professor Tomita is wonderful and will support you in your ASL learning journey.
If you are considering taking ASL for your language requirement I would 100% recommend it! The class is taught in an engaging and interactive format which makes the class something to look forward to!
I learned a lot, Professor Tomita was AMAZING, she is so kind and an incredible teacher.
Take this course!!! This is one of the best classes I have taken at Harvard. Learning a new language is challenging, and the professor is not always the most responsive to emails, but she provides the materials necessary for you to do well on exams.
Amazing class! Prof Tomita is the best~
Of all languages, TAKE ASL!!!! Firstly, it will likely be a bit easier to learn if English is your first language. But more importantly, ASL is SO MUCH FUN, engaging, intellectual, emotional, and lively!! Its culture is so vibrant and being able to connect with others through signing is a completely different experience from talking with someone new. I was in Professor Tomita's section and she was an INCREDIBLE teacher. She made class so engaging and fun and really challenged us when it came to quizzes or assignments. Because of her, I already feel so comfortable with signing. With that being said, you will always get more from a class if you put more excitement and energy into it, so I suggest doing that as well! Practice in your free time, watch ASL videos or shows in ASL, read articles, etc. Go out of your way to find signers to practice with. Definitely top 10 experiences at Harvard or classes I've taken.
TAKE ASL! It is such a wonderful language to learn at Harvard, where the support for it is wide and deep. It is a little uncomfortable at first to be plunged into a no–speaking environment, but you get the hang of it quickly. You are also warmly invited into the ASL community at Harvard and greater Cambridge, which makes for genuinely amazing events and new connections. Professor Tomita is also an incredible professor who really cares about her students. One of my favorite classes this semester!
