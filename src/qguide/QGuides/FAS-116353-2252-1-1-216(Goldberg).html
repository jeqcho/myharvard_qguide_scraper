Breaking the Norm is by far one of the better Expos Courses! Dr. Goldberg is a fantastic professor: he fosters an open class environment, welcomes everyone to speak their minds, and spends a lot time dedicated to the art of writing. The material in this course is super interesting: we started the year with a series of short stories like the Yellow Wallpaper and then transitioned to films like Paris Is Burning and social theory. This class has changed my writing for the better and gave students the space to find their own unique voices as writers. The class discussion are some the best I have had in a while. Would strongly recommend this course!
To approach the more difficult readings slowly and to hand in the best first draft possible. Ultimately, I am of the mind that as long as the literature, difficult as it may be, is in a known language, if you read through it enough times, take sufficient time in between to break down what message is being shared, and talk about it with peers, that you can almost fully comprehend the material.
As for the first drafts, that’s just a pro tip really as it will make your life easier in the long run since the marginal return to effort before the draft is submitted is significantly greater than after receiving feedback on the draft
This is probably one of the best Expos classes out there, definitely would recommend doing it! Expos is slightly draining by the fact that it is mandatory, but this class made me enjoy my experience through it a lot. The class gives you a lot of freedom when deciding what to write about, and Dr. Goldberg is super chill and helpful. You can tell he puts effort into his Expos, and it pays off a lot. The section is lots of fun and if, like me, English is not your first language, provides lots of information on how essays are structured. You're never pushed to conform to a universal style, everything is more of a suggestion of what works best, but Dr. Goldberg always makes sure to include exceptions that work in order to show how anything can be effective if you know how to write it. We were given lots of resources, and the literary sources we analyzed and talked about were not only cool but also were brimming with possible discussions that could lead to different theses, so they were good places to practice your writing on. I don't know if any of that makes any sense (I am very burnt out at the moment), but the class does a wonderful job at what it's supposed to do and is also super pleasant and enjoyable. Definitely rank it high when you sign up to the lottery!!
This class is great and Dr.Goldberg is super nice!
I highly recommend this EXPOS class! Dr. Goldberg brings great vibes to the class, which breeds a great class dynamic. The course material is so interesting, and therefore, class discussions and essays are actually fun to be a part of and do! This is a great Expos section! Dr. Goldberg is really funny and very friendly, so class meetings were rarely boring and usually quite enjoyable. The class is not too focused on Gender and Sexuality; it is only covered in the second unit and you can, but do no not have to, write about it in the third unit. As someone who is not incredibly interested in this field, the class is still very interesting due to the variety of texts we read. Of course, if you do really want to learn about gender and sexuality norms, then unit 2 will be particularly interesting. The readings can be quite long, but are usually manageable as you get away skimming some of them if you have a very busy week. Dr Goldberg's grading is fair, but not easy and you have to put in quite a lot of work to get an A on an essay, though the overall grading is a bit more lenient. His feedback is very detailed and helpful and I do feel like I improved a lot as a writer.
Please take this class! Dr. Goldberg is THE GOAT. He is an incredible instructor and a wonderful human being. The topic of the class –– societal norms and how humans and the works of art that they create break established rules and conventions –– is extremely fascinating especially if you're into sociology, political science, cultural theory or the like. I think this section is the closest you can get to an Expos g3m. The workload (at least for me) was very chill, and I ended up with good grades on my essays, so if you have a busy schedule and are looking for a light (but at the same time, fun and interesting) Expos section to fulfill your writing requirement, then Breaking the Norm is for you!
