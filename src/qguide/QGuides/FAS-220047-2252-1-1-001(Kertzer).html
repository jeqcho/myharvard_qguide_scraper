This course is very difficult. The final exam itself covers the whole semester and the amount of concepts and readings you have to remember is just insane. start studying for the final early. The two papers are graded not too bad though. Do the readings, revisit past lectures, attend office hours, participate in section and all the basic stuff. the key is doing everything that is required of you – this is not the course you get away with doing everything in the last minute. readings are heavier and more complex than other courses.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Kertzer genuinely cares about all his students and his lectures are both interesting and fun. He's one of the best professors I've had at this school. The class is super tight–knit and it's a great dynamic to be a part of. Mitsuru is an incredible TF and a really fair grader. He's willing to work with you on all the writing and really helps you level up your analytical skills.
Kertzer is an amazing professor with very engaging lectures! You will learn a lot about both IR and psychology –– but this was very different from the structure of 1790 if that's what you expect. The small lecture size was great and it felt like we had a nice community at the end. Quite a few readings, but not often discussed at length in class and the lectures were enough for me in weeks where I fell behind.
This is an AMAZING class. Professor Kertzer is such a great lecturer and really keeps all the students attentive. I will say, it is definitely challenging. Both the readings and the concepts can take time to understand, and the final exam is definitely on the harder side, but if you are certain in your interest with this topic, you absolutely should take this class.
This is such a great class! If you loved 1790 and loved Kertzer, then you will love this. All of the assignments are very interesting and manageable and the readings were some of the most interesting that I've read for a class. This class is so fun and juicy!
This course is excellent, especially for those who liked and did well in Gov 1790. I loved the material. It was substantive, engaging, and delivered well. The reading and difficulty level are about the same at 1790, but expect more participation due to the small class size. The smaller space means that the class is more engaged and interactive, and you will have to keep up with readings and the possibility of being cold–called. I personally enjoyed it a lot. If you haven't taken 1790, I'd recommend doing a brief preparation in the standard IR theories as having that baseline helps you appreciate how the psychology aspect is different and complementary. This is a class that you should take with the intention of putting effort in.

Professor Kertzer is a gem. One of the best professors I've had in my four years, and the only one who makes the effort to remember the names of all his students no matter if it's a small seminar–style class or a large lecture class. He brings a sense of liveliness, humor, and juice to his lectures. He's very sharp and informative when it comes to answering questions. If you're interested in government, you'll be missing out if you don't take a class with Kertzer at least once before you graduate.
If given the opportunity you should absolutely take this class. Psychology in international relations is such a fascinating field, especially given the complicated IR scene currently, and I found myself able to formulate different theories for why leaders behave the way they do, every time a new current event comes up. This class is the gift that keeps on giving — enjoy Kertzer's amazing references, apple spittle, and incredible knowledge.
