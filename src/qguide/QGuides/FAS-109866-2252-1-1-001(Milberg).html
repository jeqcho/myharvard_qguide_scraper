TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! Professor Milberg is GENIUS! He is truly knowledgeable on all things neuroanatomy and it was incredible to learn from someone with so much knowledge and experience in the field. It is so cool to learn about his in–person interactions with landmark neuroscience patients like HM and leading professionals in things like Alzheimers and prosopagnosia. If you are at all interested in learning more about landmark cases like Broca, Phineas Gage, and HM, or you are interested in neuroanatomy, take this class!! Very relaxed in terms of workload, truly just a place to learn from experts in cool neuroscience cases.
This course is super chill, yet interesting! There's only a major presentation (that you do with a partner) and final paper, and there are a few readings at the beginning of the course. Professor Milberg is very kind, patient, and supportive– he's always ready to answer any questions you have, and you can tell that he's passionate about the material. Getting to touch a real human brain was also very cool!
This was one of the most interesting courses I’ve taken at Harvard! Dr. Milberg is a true expert in neuroscience, and his personal experience with some of the patients discussed in class makes the lectures especially engaging. The second half of the course (where students present on landmark cases) is particularly fascinating. You gain a much deeper understanding of patients you may have only briefly encountered in introductory psychology courses.

That said, the first portion of the course could benefit from a bit more structure and clarity. I understand it’s challenging to condense an entire neuroscience curriculum into just a few weeks, but it might help to focus primarily on the most essential concepts (such as brain areas and their functions) that are necessary for understanding the cases. Providing more visual materials (maybe through slides or supplemental readings) would also be helpful, especially since students come into this course with varying levels of background. Lastly, a short 5–10 minute break during the two–hour lectures would go a long way since maintaining focus through dense material can be quite tough.

Overall, it’s an excellent, low–workload class led by a fantastic professor!
This was a very unique course –– you focus on neuroanatomy during the first half of the semester, and then deep dive into historical case studies and their implications on our understanding of cognition (think HM and phineas gage). I found the first half of the semester very fast–paced and a bit hard to follow, but the second half was very interesting and it was great to learn from student presentations. The only assignments are a midterm, a presentation, and a short final paper (no weekly readings). Definitely ask Dr. Milberg for guidance/feedback on the presentations, it'll make your life a whole lot easier than guessing what to include and focus on. Overall recommend!
Take this class for sure if you’re thinking about doing a MBB secondary (or concentration). It’s definitely a gem. Professor Milberg is great, full of knowledge, and super chill. Loved it!
Take this class! Professor Milberg is amazing! The content is very interesting, we got to see and work with a real human brain, and the workload is very manageable. I don't have anything negative to say about this class.
