Fantastic class, definitely difficult. You will need to dedicate some time to this class, so be cautious about picking a heavy course load with it (will be a problem in midterms). 10/10 class except for the AI assignments, which are terrible. Either find a PSET group or go to MQC and office hours, professors/TFs are super helpful and friendly.
I highly recommend this class — the grading was fair, the small–class sections were engaging and useful, and there are a lot of support resources available!
Background: Freshman who took 22a in the fall, and decided to take 21a in the spring. In HS, did AP Calc BC in Junior Year & Multi/Linear Algebra in Senior Year.

Thoughts: Very solid class. Excellent teaching staff and environment. You learn a decent amount but it's super reasonable. PSETs aren't too hard. [ALSO] I made the decision to take 22a in the fall because it was 1 pset and was more challenging than some classes I thought were a little easy. 22a was taught poorly so I made the switch to 21a. The 3 psets aren't bad. In fact, I spent more time in 22a doing a pset than maybe like 4–5 psets in 21a.
I would definitely recommend taking this class. There is a lot of content and if you just want an A in the class it is possible without too much extra studying as the PSETS are essentially homework assignments that get you to understand the content from each section. However, if you want to truly and deeply understand it requires studying on your own time. The exams are also easier than the practice exams, so do not be intimidated. Overall, if you like critical thinking and you like math, this class is perfect for you.
Take it! Its very challenging but very rewarding!
