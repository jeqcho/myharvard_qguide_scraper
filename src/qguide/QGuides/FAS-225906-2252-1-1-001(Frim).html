Take this course. Prof. Frim is great and provides excellent feedback. The work is engaged but not overbearing. While it may be more beneficial to take the class if you have an interest in continuing to study folklore and mythology, anyone can take it and have a good experience, as well as learn a lot about culture, oral history and narratives.
THIS WAS SUCH AN INCREDIBLE CLASS!!! This was definitely the highlight of my three years at Harvard. The topic and material is extremely exciting, the structure of the class made it both a rigorous and an intensive introduction, and also very low–stress. The essays we had to write could virtually be on any topic we found interesting. The readings for each week were a balanced mix of fascinating and mysterious tales and secondary sources that explain many things one would not see on their own. DANIEL IS AN AMAZING PROFESSOR!!! He is extremely knowledgeable, passionate, friendly, approachable, and understanding. He facilitated amazing discussion during class while also including mini–lectures and sharing his experience. He always found new and fun ways for us to engage with the topics, ranging from in–class close–reading activities, to coming up with fun questions that make you think, for example, what part of indigenous culture would we want to explore if we had access to a time machine, or in–class exercises where we try to make the best argument to back up a thesis proposed by certain author. I feel I learned so much not only about the culture of the Northwest Coast, but also about literature and academic writing and research, too. The class also included guest lectures and a visit to the Peabody Museum. I just can't express how extremely happy I am that I took this class. And I am not even a humanities concentrator. I took this class as an elective, and this is by far my best decision at Harvard! DEFINITELY TAKE THIS CLASS!!!
This class was absolutely one of my favorites at the college so far. I throughly enjoyed getting to engage with myths and narratives in this way as I had never done so before. Frim is an absolute pleasure to have as a professor and does his best to make you feel welcome and heard. He always welcomes any questions and doesn't stray from being honest with you about any knowledge he has on the subject or things that may still be unclear as we discover new things everyday. As long as you come in willing to learn and excited about the material you'll enjoy it and get a lot out of this course!
I would highly recommend it. Dr. Frim is awesome and it's very interesting.
