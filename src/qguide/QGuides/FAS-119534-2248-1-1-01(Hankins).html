Take this class! The reading list is unlike almost any other course, and Prof. Hankins does a wonderful job contextualizing the readings, something you can't get by reading these works on your own. You will 100% leave with a better understanding of the modern way of thinking!
This is a great class to take if you're looking for a crash course in the intellectual history of theology, philosophy, and science. Major thinkers are covered and broad connections between ideas and movements are made explicitly clear.
Hankins is an amazing professor. He is so knowledgeable in this field, and you cannot find an equivalent of this course outside of Harvard. Moreover, he is also very approachable outside of class and generates enthusiasm for the topic. I really loved the material as well. You get to read the great authors and philosophical texts of the 12th through 17th centuries, and these continue to have a profound influence on the world. I took this course as a CS concentrator who had never taken history before at Harvard, and I found it very doable and very interesting. Take take take (if it's Hankins)!
Coming from a math concentrator: Readings are worthwhile do them if you can, I unfortunately missed some due to other psets taking forever but this really should be priority. Hankins' lectures can be tiring given the way he presents, just low energy and monotone, but the actual content is mostly quite interesting. As far as humanities courses go, this is probably among the best each semester as you can actually learn something.
Professor Hankins is amazing and should be cherished.
Very good class, Hankins is a brilliant professor and this course is designed to give a good arc of Western Intellectual History. Surprising to learn about how intellectual developments occurred and good readings.
This is a rewarding course with challenging readings and very engaging lectures and overall instruction. I would recommend it to any history concentrator to develop a deeper understanding of the broader impact of Western intellectual history on whatever area of history you are interested in.
this was a great class! If you write a lot of papers, its a good way to offset a humanities class with the exams (which are mostly essays not too difficult if you revives the PowerPoints). It was rlly great to have a macro history class, and I now feel like I have a far better handle on how epochs fit together
This is a really great class for those who want to know more about Western intellectual history or political philosophy. While the course does require a good amount of close reading, Hankins' lectures are excellent and you will learn a lot.
Take this course! This was my first class in the History department and I was pleasantly surprised by how effectively centuries of history were presented in just one semester. By taking a chronological approach, Prof. Hankins was able to clearly make a historical argument that is easy to follow while managing to foster engagement throughout the semester. I feel that this class equipped me to tackle debates and conversations confidently and gave me the context for my studies in contemporary history (of science) and philosophy.
This course is a must–take for anyone interested in the Western tradition. Professor Hankins is incredibly knowledgable and he's truly fair to all legitimate viewpoints.
I highly recommend this course. The reading list for this course is excellent while also being a manageable amount each week. The reading is a perfect compliment to the topics covered in lecture. Professor Hankins is great and does an incredible job providing a narrative of the intellectual trends throughout the periods covered in the course. I highly recommend anyone seeking to become well–read take this course.
