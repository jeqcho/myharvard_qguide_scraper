Professor Deloria is wonderful a strong, engaging lecturer, and extremely supportive of his students. If you're looking for an introductory history course, this is as good as any just know that historical writing skills aren't emphasized, so you should have a fairly good sense of how to structure a longer (at least 10–12 page) historical argument. If you're a STEM concentrator, this could be a particularly good course for you environmental history integrates aspects of the life and earth sciences (biology, geology) and you can use sources pertaining to those disciplines in your final project.

Also know that this is not a course on the history of environmentalism it's environmental history, meaning that you get to ask "How did the land get to be this way? Who shaped it, and why?" You get into a lot of interesting political and cultural history (e.g. history of bird hats, feminism, consumerism!) even though the focus is on the land itself. It's perhaps not your typical history course, but worth taking nonetheless.
