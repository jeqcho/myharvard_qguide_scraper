If you are anywhere on the range of not knowing anything about MENA to thinking that you know everything about MENA, you will get something out of this course. Coming into the class I knew the region's geography a bit from previous study, but did not understand the complicated politics of each country there. You should absolutely take this class to learn about the region in depth. However only take it if you can commit yourself to doing each reading– simply because the readings are expertly assigned to build better discussions.
Dr. Sener is one of the most wonderful instructors I have had the privilege of learning from at Harvard thus far. Great class overall—readings are super interesting and sections are incredibly engaging. Would strongly recommend it for any Gov concentrators, particularly those who are interested in the MENA.
This seminar was a really great survey of the MENA region. Her lectures are very interesting and she is very intentional about answering questions. She gives thoughtful feedback on assignments and creates a welcoming class environment. Definitely recommend!
TAKE THIS CLASS WITHOUT HESITATION! Content wise, this class is extremely interesting even if you're not familiar with the region at all – it discussed the region's history yet mainly focused on applying and explaining many political concepts and how they apply to the region. Ipek is such an amazing professor, genuinely the best I've had so far at Harvard. Clearly passionate about the subject. Structure/load wise, the class's discussion flows well and the assignments are not at all bad, they are very clearly described and it's only a total of three papers and a presentation for the semester. I really really enjoyed being in this class and I highly recommend it if you need any sort of Gov class.
This class is a great class! The reading load isn't too overwhelming at all, with about 50 pages or so per week. There are two response papers that allow you to engage with the material more deeply and a final paper that you have a lot of creative liberty with. It's a great class if you're looking to learn more about the Middle East, even if you're coming in with little to no knowledge about the region (like me). You'll learn a lot, and this isn't a super discussion–based traditional seminar. Instead, class time was spent learning and engaging with the material through lessons taught by the professor.
Really good class!
