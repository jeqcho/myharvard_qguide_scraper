What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
PLEASE take it! Professor Gough has so much to say about modernist art, architecture of the grid, and just the lens of women historical role in weaving, bauhaus, fine art, modernism. The readings are amazing too, and each class discussion will make you reevaluate even your own connection to the clothes you wear.
If you ever get a chance to take a class with Prof. Gough, take it! She is such a knowledgable lecturer you will learn so much from her, and so interested in facilitating learning between students that you will learn so much from your peers as well.
Such a fun class! This obviously depends on your classmates to some extent, but Professor Gough and my peers made the class conversations so lively and engaging. I also appreciated the structure of every class, which often (not always) started with a quick presentation by Professor Gough that provided an overview of the topic as well as can supplement any gaps in understanding the denser readings. This is then followed by looking at objects and art in the study center. We had a trip to the MoMA in April which is a great experience to speak to curators and other Harvard faculty.
This class was extremely well–structured and the material we were engaging with is important. Professor Gough's work on highlighting the importance of women's contributions to modernism through textile and thread is necessary.
This course provides a one–of–a–kind perspective on modernity, abstraction and materiality; one that is at the current frontier of art historical thinking. It is quite exciting and rare to have the opportunity to do so much original research and discuss so many new ideas given the slow but steady study of textile works in the fine arts today. The course also ties these ideas to pressing matters today, such as societal perceptions of science and rationalization, political struggles, and––of course––the role of women in contemporary society. It is a privilege to navigate the art history world with such a fresh and visionary perspective!
As the youngest student, and rather under–experienced (sophomore undergrad), this course was tough but incredibly interesting. Professor Gough keeps you engaged with the course material and you learn so much about textiles and artists of that time. It is reading heavy so be prepared to dedicate most of your week(end) to the readings.
