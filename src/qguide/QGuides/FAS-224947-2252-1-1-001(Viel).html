This class is definitely what you make of it. It can be extremely interesting and enjoyable if you come into the class with an idea you are passionate about and if you are really interested in the intersection between biology, technology, and business. However, if this is not really your cup of tea, I do not recommend it as you will likely sit back and watch everyone around you feel more engaged.
This class is great exposure to the world of start ups, especially for life sciences students who may not have thought that they could do something like this. I enjoyed hearing all of the speakers and getting advice from them on how to build our hypothetical project.
This is a great class if you have a start–up idea in mind. Lectures are interesting and professor is sweet. Some of the assignments are a little busy work and the feedback isn't very helpful.
*** This class is a unique opportunity for students interested in entrepreneurship within the life sciences. Professor Alain Viel does an outstanding job curating guest speakers and creating opportunities for students and their startups to thrive. His experience in the field is evident, and he offers thoughtful, practical advice—both during class and in his conveniently timed office hours right after. He’s genuinely supportive and always willing to listen.
That said, the class is very much what you make of it. If you're serious about advancing your startup, be prepared to dedicate significant time outside of class. The more you invest, the more you'll get out of the experience.
