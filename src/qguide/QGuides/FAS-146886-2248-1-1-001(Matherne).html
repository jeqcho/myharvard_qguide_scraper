One of the best courses I've taken at Harvard. If you are interested in philosophy or art (or both!) then I would highly recommend – I had no philosophy background before this class and it was very doable (and I improved so much throughout the semester at being able to ask good questions/have more rigorous philosophical discussions). Prof. Matherne is amazing – her lectures are the best Harvard has to offer and her office hours are awesome to discuss course content. The assignments are also super cool – one of the first times I really looked forward to doing an assignment because the philaesthetic experiences were really enjoyable (you pick an "aesthetic experience" like listening to an album, looking at a piece of art, etc. to revisit 3 times throughout the semester and think about through the perspective of one of our philosophers) and the essay topics are all interesting. Could not recommend more – a course super rich in content and has tons of opportunities to actively engage with it more, which made the class super worthwhile and led me to make multiple new friends too.

Professor Matherne is an amazing professor, and she has put together an amazing class! I highly recommend it for anyone interested in taking a philosophy class regardless of background (I'm a STEM concentrator). Aesthetics is not the typical political philosophy you might think of, but we read a lot of classic philosophers and tackled interesting questions about art, beauty, and the good life (which are fun things to think about in an elective class). Professor Matherne gives very engaging lectures, and I often left awestruck and with a new view on life. This class seriously did change the way I see the world.
Assignments wise, the readings were pretty manageable week over week and the three phil–aesthetic assignments were fun and pretty easy to do, and they were essentially completion based. In typical philosophy fashion, there's no easy As on the midterm papers, so you might consider going Pass/Fail if this is an elective for you to not stress about small differences in grades. There was a lot of freedom on the final paper, and I liked writing it.
Overall, I highly recommend this course, and encourage you to shop it if you're at all interested!

This was the first philosophy class I took as a non–philosophy senior. I'm really glad I took this class as the first and probably last philosophy class I'll take. Matherne is a great lecturer who brings both excitement and sense to these philosophers. She structures her lecture with an outline so it's easy to follow and take notes.

As a non–philosophy person, I gave up on the readings and read summaries most of the time as Matherne is really good at explaining these philosophers better than they can. At times, the arguments can seem quite dry and construed, slightly sleep–inducing when after lunch. Besides that, it's obviously harder to do well on the assignments writing philosophy papers with no experience, but I got slightly better throughout the semester. Not a big deal to me, but keep in mind if you really want an A and have no experience, you'll have to be on top of learning the style and expectations of these papers.
As a senior, this was probably my favorite class I've taken at Harvard. A professor like Matherne is rare to find nowadays – she's incredibly passionate about the course material, clearly prepares thoroughly for lectures, and is very enthusiastic about teaching and getting to know students in general. By the end of the course, she knows most students' names. The workload is low – Matherne puts a lot of intention into selecting readings, only picking relevant and interesting parts. Overall, the course content is highly relevant for your practical life (which isn't always the case in philosophy), and the survey style of the course means you get exposed to many different thinkers. My only critique is that many philosophers we covered have very complex metaphysical frameworks that need to be understood before you can understand their aesthetics arguments, so majority of class is always spent setting up the metaphysics first. That aside, highly highly recommend!
