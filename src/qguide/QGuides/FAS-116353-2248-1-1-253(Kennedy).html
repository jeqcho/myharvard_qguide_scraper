TAKE THIS COURSE
If you're looking for which Expos sections to rank, I'd say this is a good choice. Dr. Kennedy is wonderful and gives helpful feedback. I don't think she grades too harshly, but some of the paper prompts feel unclear. Be prepared: the workload for this class varies widely depending on the week. If you have a paper due, expect to spend a lot more time on this class. If you're looking for more direct instruction on writing, I would consider taking a different section. We read a case for each unit, and the cases were interesting, but the paper topics were not always as applicable to the case as they could be.
I strongly recommend this course. It is one of the most interesting and thought–provoking Expos courses in my opinion. The in–class simulations are a lot of fun and the whole class is not just about writing but also about the skills of negotiating and examining such in the real–world.
Take this class!!!!
So fun, so engaging, and lovely professor who genuinely wants to help with her students. Go to office hours! A lot of people don't take advantage of office hours, and if your like me with limited writing knowledge go to the writing center, super helpful community.
I would really recommend this class. It is light in term of reading overload and it provides you a comprehensive and interesting perspective on negotiations and fairness. The professor is very supportive and creates a pleasant and safe space. In terms of writing, it offers less theory, following a more trail–and–error approach. As an international students, I had no prior experience with academic papers in English and I managed with the course and its assignments. I would highly recommend if you are interested in social sciences/government/international relations.
Dr. Kennedy is a great professor and super understanding, take this class if you're interested in government and politics.
I want to stress that while Dr. Kennedy is a wonderful teacher who cares deeply about her students and their well–being, this is NOT a writing class. It is a negotiation class with emphasis on writing put to the side. This class did not teach me how to be a better writer directly, instead I had to learn how to become a better writer because you are graded on certain things like structure that are never explicitly taught. I learned a lot about how to negotiate with people, but do not take this class if you want to focus on really improving your writing.
This class has a heavy focus on simulation based negotiations and not a huge focus on writing. If you really want to learn how to write this is not the class for you. That said, professor Kennedy is wonderful and really cares about each of her students and takes the time to get to know everyone and is always available to give feedback on papers and writing style.
Be excited and take advantage of the course! You get to negotiate and engage in relevant research!
Very good class depending on what you are looking for. Professor Kennedy is sweet and obviously cares about her students but is tougher on late work. You have to do the readings to participate in discussions but there is not too much assigned. You will learn less about how to write and more about how to negotiate which is a slight negative if you are looking for a foundational writing course. Negotiation simulations were interesting and added a nice hands–on element. Most of the essay topics were only moderately challenging. Knowledge from this class can be applied to a variety of fields including business and international relations.
Take this course if you want to explore negotiation, or take a negotiation course. As a writing course, this will not be the formative course that shapes you as a writer, but that's okay, it's just Expos. I don't know if other Expos end up feeling the same way, or it's a consequence of the students, but I don't think Expos helped me a ton to improve my writing. We covered some grammar fundamentals, some structure ideas, but I don't think I have the intuitive grasp of a writer.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Dr.Kennedy is so fun, understanding, and nice! She makes the classroom atmosphere very engaging and comfortable. You can ask her about anything in the course and also anything totally unrelated to class. She is a very kind teacher who is interested in getting to know her students. The course content is very interesting and you can learn about three very different negotiation situations which will prompt you to think more outside of the box. They will lead you to consider different interests people have, many ways deals can be designed, and how different negotiations can play out. In in–person conferences, I was able to bounce off ideas I had in mind and it was helpful in figuring out which parts I could improve. Overall, I really enjoyed the class and am glad that I was able to take it as one of my first courses at Harvard.
Professor Kennedy is extremely kind and understand and honestly made my experience in this course so awesome. A lot of people hated their expos classes but I genuinely enjoyed it. There is a good amount of reading but it's not that bad and for the most part, the readings are interesting. When picking an expos, either pick something you're interested in or pick an easy course based on what others say.
