Math 113 was a wonderful class, and a must–take for anyone considering studying math further. As is normal with math courses here at Harvard, it is somewhat dependent on the instructor, and Prof. Harris is possibly the best professor for the course. Having said that, the material you learn in this course is definitely very important, and I highly recommend you to take this course.
Complex analysis is very cool class, at least the first half of it was cool. As we got toward the end of the semester, it lowkey became a real analysis class—we talked about infinite series and products for weeks, and I didn’t find that very fun. We could’ve learned so many more cool theorems in complex analysis than spending too much time on things we could’ve learned in real analysis. But what this meant was that the PSets were very easy in the second half of the semester. As long as you get through the pretty tricky proof questions as the course starts, things get significantly more straightforward at the end. The professor was also very relaxed with giving assignments. We had a lot of breathing time—sometimes we wouldn’t have PSets for a week, sometimes we would have an extremely short PSet. I think people should take this class for sure because it still gives you the mathematical vocabulary that you wouldn’t otherwise know. Complex analysis is pretty thought–provoking and I hope in the future, it could use time better to teach more interesting results in the complex world.
Golden rule of the Harvard math department – take anything that Joe Harris teaches. It was a pleasure learning from him in both 122 and 113, and I hope you will take up the chance to do the same.
When Joe teaches this class, it is generally well run in terms of the lectures and problem sets.

I think the performance by the course assistants was lackluster and frankly unacceptable. Grades were extremely delayed. Some CA's were unprepared for office hours. Joe should exercise greater supervision over the course assistants and ensure they are doing what they need to do. They need to be held accountable. The only CA that seemed to really truly care and be putting in the effort was Tyler ––– he was consistently prepared for office hours and took his job seriously. There were others that were varying degrees of helpful and prepared, but nobody consistently was able to help with the entire problem set, which really should be the standard and expectation. And even Tyler could do better ––– he was part of the problem with returning grades in a timely manner as the coordinating CA.
I do wish we could have covered some more of the insightful and cool things in complex. I took this because my friends said complex analysis was some of the most beautiful math they had worked with, and I was excited as well but we barely got to weierstrass P functions by the end, and we only really dealt with things that felt like extensions of real analysis. I'm tempted to take 213 just to see the wonderful math that lives in complex analysis, but I'm disappointed about how little we covered.

Besides that, the content we did cover was well taught and I'm glad I have the background in these things now, but that's my rational point of view. It is still a bit overshadowed by disappointment.
Joe Harris is a genius and one of the most enthusiastic professors at Harvard. He knows how to explain concepts multiple ways and was always able to answer any questions during lecture. I do wish homework assignments were graded more consistently given how much of our overall grade it is.

I'm happy I took it this year with Joe though we may have not learned as much as usual. Broadly speaking, this is a good class – you will learn everything you should in an introductory course to complex analysis. Professor Harris and the teaching staff were great and provided good lectures and feedback on assignments. If you have any previous exposure to complex analysis (or even if you have more exposure to formal mathematics, especially analysis previously), this course could feel somewhat slow paced and repetitive at times.

I would highly recommend this class! The material is fascinating and beautiful, and really helpful for thinking about the complex numbers and what functions look like in that realm.
