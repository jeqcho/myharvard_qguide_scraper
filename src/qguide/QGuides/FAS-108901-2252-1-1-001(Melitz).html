This class is great!! Melitz is an amazing lecturer, and he is genuinely interested in teaching. He is always available to answer questions, and he makes a point to stay after lecture and talk to anybody that wants to (there is often a line, as he gives attentive responses). The class is supposedly a lot better off cycle, and I would agree. There are not as many study groups or class resources, but they post good information to use for studying and the textbook (written by the prof) is a great way to clear anything up. If you read the textbook and do the assignments honestly, you will learn the material without much outside work on your end. An A is a little tough, but can be done with attentiveness and studying for the exams. Would recommend!

It wasn’t that bad!! You are probably either an ec concentrator or an ec secondary thinking about which 1010 to take and whether it will be on– or off–cycle. I think 1010a off–cycle was a very good class. Only meets twice a week, sections are option and recorded to be posted online. So, week–to–week the class demands were not that much. However, I think this comes at the cost of learning the material, which makes preparing for exams more difficult.

It was very nice that the problem sets had unlimited tries online, and the accompanying weekly quizzes were not too bad. However, it was really frustrating that a lot of this felt self–taught. We did not do any practice in lectures, and I also found it frustrating when we wouldn’t explicitly go through the limited practice exercises that were included in the slides. The textbook was also not helpful at all.

The lectures were a bit dry, but Professor Melitz is really nice and accessible. On the first midterm, when we reached time, nobody got up to hand it in lol, so Professor Melitz gave us a few more minutes. But that also shows that people didn’t really feel confident with the material. Overall, I think 1010a was a solid class, and since you have to do it anyway, it’s really not that bad. It also can be interesting at times! Though often it’s theory that feels disconnected from anything applicable. It builds a bit off of what you learned in 10a (or other intro micro), adding a bit of derivatives.
This class is very managable, and the smaller environment helps you get more help than you would in the larger ec1010a. Melitz is a great professor and very open to answering questions whenever.
I would HIGHLY RECOMMNED taking this class off cycle, although there are a few things to keep in mind before making that decision. This course meets only 2 days a week, which is one less than the on cycle course in the fall. This means that the course is on a more accelerated track, and also makes it so exams must be taken outside of normal class hours (usually on Monday nights). This also increases the speed of lectures, and makes it somewhat difficult to walk away from class with a full understanding of the concepts discussed that day. That being said, I do not think this poses a major problem for the majority of people. The lectures are organized by chapters of the textbook, which is very good, so if there are any gaps in your understanding it is very easy to cross reference the textbook and fill them in. Additionally, there are ample resources available, including Professor, TF, and CA office hours, and because of the small class size it is very easy to take advantage of and significantly benefit from these resources. The professor, Mark, is wonderful, and is always making an effort to encourage students to give him feedback so he can make the class experience better for all of us. Attendance to lectures is "mandatory", but students are allowed to miss 1/3 of total lectures so this isn't that bad. Additionally, you have infinite tries on the psets, which are online via the textbook, and as such are essentially guaranteed a hundred on the pset grade.

TLDR; Take this class! Lots of support available, great professor, and great course structure. Workload is very manageable!
