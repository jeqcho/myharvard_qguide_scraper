Overall, this class was great! I took this class to fulfill my pre–med stats requirement, and I really think this was the best option (especially considering the downfall of stat 100 this year). In PSY1900, you will both learn the basics of stats (t–test, anova, chi–squared, regression, etc.) and will learn the basics of performing statistical analysis in R. I came in with zero (and I mean zero) coding background, but the course is structured in a way where, if you are willing to put in a bit of time to watch videos the course staff provides about coding and then you are willing to practice what was covered in the videos, you will learn to code just fine. I also appreciated that no one in the class new how to code; this wasn't a CS50–type class where everyone says they don't know how to code but really people already do.

Don't get me wrong, however, PSY1900 wasn't easy all the time. There are three 3–part exams (multiple choice, written/coding, and oral), and there is a final project, all on top of weekly asynchronous hour long videos and PSETS. You will definitely have to put in some time, but once you get into the routine, it will be totally manageable. It is also nice that the exams (with the exception of the oral part) are open note and you can use AI, which is more representative of the real world.

From a grading perspective, don't worry! There is SO MUCH extra credit! At times, it was kind of ridiculous. If you put in effort and try to get stuff right, you will be fine. By the end of the semester, there was so much extra credit that I started not to do the extra credit, which I have never really done before lol.

The main flaw with this class is the amount of busy work there is. The lab videos can get tedious, and the 3 part exams that spanned over 2 weeks were really annoying, especially because the next exam usually started less than 5 days after the previous one ended. The office hours for this class were also very limited. A 200 person class should offer more office hours than Tuesday and Thursday from 1–3:30 pm, especially since many classes meet during these times.

Sadly, Grace (the main instructor) is leaving Harvard after this semester, so I am not sure how this course will change in the future, so maybe just confirm that the syllabus is the same before committing to taking it. I hope that this class doesn't go downhill like stat 100 did.
