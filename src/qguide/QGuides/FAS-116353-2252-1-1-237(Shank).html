This class is interesting, insightful and worth taking. However, it is NOT EASY and you should be prepared to be challenged. No matter how good of a writer you think you are, Ian is sure to leave very detailed, extensive comments on many aspects of your work. My best advice is to listen very carefully to everything he says and try to adopt that in your essay. He teaches very specific models and frameworks in class that he wants you to follow, and to get a good grade you should expect to follow it. It's not an easy A but you will learn something new and concrete. Ian is also a fantastic professor and has really funny attendance questions that help you bond with your class. Overall, I'm glad I took it!
Take this Expos! Topic is interesting and Ian facilitates a strong class community. Workload is overall very manageable and you will become a better writer by the end :)
Just write. You won't get away in this class if you treat it as a side quest or something. Ian is a great professor, but you genuinely get what you put in. Lean on the resources he gives you and you will do fine. Get ready to learn a ton!
Ian is amazing!! Participate actively in class and pay attention to his feedback!
The class topic was interesting and I learned a lot about becoming a better writer. Ian is a fantastic teacher and makes class a lot of fun. Additionally the workload isn't that bad.
You cannot get away with submitting a first draft; it will be obvious and you will be graded accordingly. You will learn how to write, and it'll be hard depending on where you left off in high school. But it's not impossible, and it's going to be one of the most helpful classes you'll take.
I really appreciated how structured this class was, and felt like I learned new ways of writing that weren't taught to me in high school. I also had a great time getting to know the other students in my section, and always had fun discussing the readings and content.
Take this class 100%. The readings are engaging and digestible, making it easier to focus more on improving your writing. The attendance questions build a great community in class, and Ian makes the class incredibly entertaining. Although feedback may seem harsh at points, none of it seems totally unfair, and your writing will benefit from the process Ian outlines. Your writing will undoubtedly change for the better.
The grading isn't easy, but it is worth taking for the atmosphere and ultimate take aways.
Very good class! Ian is great, he makes a very fun class environment You'll learn a lot and become a much better writer. The only thing is the grading is a little harsh, I've heard harsher than other expos classes
This class is indeed difficult. Ian can be a tough, but fair grader. However, he is not looking to fail students rather teach and improve their writing. He fosters a welcoming culture where students feel comfortable sharing their points, opinions, critiques, and questions. Thus, while students have two free absences, it is rare for anyone to utilize that grace. Moreover, the topics themselves are interesting. Covering real and fictional con artists, the course prompts students to analyze the base characteristics of society and people that allow for the prevalence of scams. All of this occurs while truly growing as writers. That being said, time is necessary for papers. Regardless of the skill of the writer, multiple drafts are necessary to have a good final product.
