Great class, especially if you like systems/Eddie! You definitely need CS61 and probably 161 and 263 to fully appreciate this course. Not a crazy workload and Eddie made an effort to find papers that people were excited about reading. The assignments are fun and very open ended
Eddie is amazing as usual! They make the classroom environment so so much fun. I learnt a lot and this was by far the most motivated I have felt to show up to a 9:45 am. Would definitely recommend if you're looking to get into systems research. One of my favorite classes yet
Just take any class with Eddie. It's really that worth it. I think Eddie is that professor that makes me find a love for computer science in every class. Eddie made me love computer science in 61, and every class after that. My advice is, don't take advantage that Eddie is nice – really put in the work, participate in class and on projects. I wish I could have worked even harder than I did in this class – It is one of those you get what you put into it classes. I think I feel more comfortable with research, and I really found out that parts of papers which I am overly critical sometimes (evaluation) are so hard to get right. If you love CS, take this. If you want to love CS, take this. I think the biggest benefit for me is that after such a exhausting semester with many project failures(ish), there was so much warmth in this class that I feel that even if my project did not turn up how I wanted, I was happy with how the semester went and what I learned. Also, go talk to Eddie if you take this class!! Ask questions! Have opinions in class! Talk to your classmates!
This is a very enjoyable paper reading class. You will read many influential systems papers from the past few decades before diving into more modern and wacky research papers. The course is student–driven; if there are specific parts of systems research that excite you, you can likely get Eddie to spend a week on it. The projects require substantial work; they are all open–ended, so there is significantly more planning and scoping that needs to be done on your part. I think this course is a great way to dip your toe into systems research and should not be intimidating at all.
