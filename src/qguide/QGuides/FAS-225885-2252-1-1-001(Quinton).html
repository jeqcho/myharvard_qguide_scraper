Love Professor Quniton! She is not only an amazing professor, she is also a wonderful person, and I absolutely love her style of teaching and the way she works with students and helps us rigorously engage with the course material!
I HIGHLY recommend this class. I study economics and had never taken a TDM class or danced before and I found this class to be so interesting and engaging. The class is very welcoming for anyone to share their perspective, and we spend most of the class just talking as a group and you get very close to your classmates since its a small class size. The readigns were a good size, the papers were short and fun to write, and we had class excursions to the ballet and other performances which were so fun!!
PLEASE take this course!!! It was one of my favorite classes at Harvard, and I really enjoyed the thought–provoking discussions about dance and its relation to society as a whole. Professor Quinton was the absolute best.
This class is one of my favorites I have ever taken at Harvard. Dr. Quinton is a phenomenal professor and is truly so knowledgeable and passionate about this topic. You will learn so much about dance history, understand how to be a dance critic, and look at dance through a very analytical lens. The wide array of people and companies that this course studies ensures that there will be something of interest for each person who takes this class–– do not be turned away if you are not a dancer! There is no background in dance needed to both enjoy and excel in this course. I highly recommend.
Take this class! It opens you up to a whole new side of dance, and Dr. Quinton is extremely helpful, encouraging and knowledgeable!
This class is incredible! Laura is a fantastic teacher who is incredibly knowledgeable about the field (and so accomplished with her own research!) Each week, you will learn about a different choreographic lineage and the class covers a multitude of different dance styles and genres. The workload is incredibly manageable with weekly readings/videos and the assignments are super fun–you get to attend free live performances and write about what you see. I would definitely recommend this course to both TDM concentrators and anyone with an interest in dance–it is an incredible opportunity to learn about how to study dance as an academic discipline.
This is a wonderful class!! This course gives you a true overview of dance history and the ways in which different choreographers relate to one another over time and place. You'll also have the chance to see live dance performances, which is an awesome chance to really use the dance analysis skills from the course. The final project provides a great chance to do research on someone who is interesting to you. Whether you have lots of dance experience or none at all, the class discussions each week are totally accessible and nuanced. The readings each week can take a bit of time, but they're all really interesting and it never feels like you're doing busy work.
Laura is such an amazing professor! I had such a wonderful time learning more about dance history!!
