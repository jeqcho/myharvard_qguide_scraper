This class is not easy, but it is very interesting! I highly recommend taking the course if you have an interest in modeling very complex systems/ want to learn beyond linear algebra etc. Professor Seigal is a fantastic lecturer and is very supportive of her students.
I cannot speak enough about how amazing Prof. Seigal is. During the mid–course feedback, I probably wrote 5 paragraphs about how unique and incredible she is. The course is very interesting, and I could only see this class improving significantly after the first year of its running. Math 22 or equivalent should be good prep. Again, Prof. Seigal is amazing, as are the current staff, and the course is one of the best in the AM department. Reasonable amounts of work, but not the easiest by any means
It is difficult material conceptually but the work itself is manageable. 1 pset every other week, quizzes every other week, no midterms
Professor Seigal is a great lecturer and super passionate about the course, which always makes the course so much better. I recommend you review topics from 21b and Math101/any proof and theory class you've taken because a large component of the course is theorems and proving them and applying them. I liked that this class was a smaller size, so it felt more comfortable to ask questions during lecture.
This class is excellent. It is rather proof heavy for an AM class, but this is definitely a great strength of the course. The concepts and tools we spend the semester building come together in an intensely satisfying and very beautiful way by the end, and your computational skills improve dramatically along the way. As long as you have a good understanding of linear algebra and at least a little exposure to / interest in proofs, you will be more than prepared for this class.
Outside of the content we learn, Professor Seigal is truly amazing. She memorized everyone's names by the second day of class, and did an excellent job making the class interactive and fun. She does her best to welcome and answer questions both in class and at office hours. It is clear she really cares about her students.
Even being the first iteration of this class, it was an incredible experience and will surely be even better next time around. Definitely take this!
The professor is a really good lecturer and is very kind. The course starts out slow and ties itself together towards the end in a pretty interesting manner. The TFs are nice. Office hours are really helpful but infrequent due to the small size of the class. I think it being a small class makes it easier to learn during lectures though. The psets are fairly interesting and don't take too long to complete. There are lecture notes for every lecture which helps, and there's a supplementary textbook. Quizzes are not on course material but rather on random linear algebra I feel. The final exam was difficult and worth 40%. I'd recommend the course for those who love linear algebra and are comfortable with proofs (I fit neither category).
You should know basics of linear algebra and multivariable calculus. The class covers multiple topics, mainly linalg (regression, PCA), Lagrange multipliers, ideals, varieties, and algebraic descriptions of models. Seigal's lecturing style is to provide many examples, but her explanation for the motivation behind techniques, especially in the linalg/multivariable part of the class at the start, were sometimes unclear.

There is a large Julia coding component on psets, which I found pretty tough. Definitely go to OH for help on them!

I found the latter part of the class on ideals, varieties, and models great as an introduction to the algebraic geometry way of thinking. It's interesting to think about how the parameterization of models can be thought of as a curve or surface in space.
I think this class works if you have strong foundations in linear algebra and are willing to spend time outside of class learning the proofs and the concepts. There weren’t many practice materials available for quizzes.
