Peter Gordon is an extraordinarily thoughtful professor (Michael Mango is also an excellent TF!) and the reading list was really wonderful. However, I think the format of the class sessions wasn’t the best. This class was one of those weird middle–grounds where it was meant to be a seminar but then they didn’t want to put a cap on it so it was too big for a normal seminar and became like a pseudo–lecture with a big emphasis on class discussion. I thought this format was a little bit “worst of both worlds” because you don’t get the classic, Gordon lecture which people rave about nor did you get the benefits of a small seminar where everyone’s coming really prepared to talk about the material. That said, I’m still glad I took the course and particularly enjoyed the thoughtful conversations we had about Trump/everything going on on campus this semester.
Great class! Prof Gordon is an absolute legend–– super smart and very compassionate. I always looked forward to our weekly discussions. Great class to get an overview of foundational texts relating to fascism.
This is a really great class! Prof. Gordon is so brilliant and fosters such an incredible conversation. He always makes time to discuss current events and their relationship to the course, which is perhaps the part I most appreciated. The final paper is totally open–ended, so you get to pursue whatever you want which is quite liberating and can be very intellectually fulfilling. The reading is also interesting — and Prof. Gordon is one of the most thoughtful professors around.
There are many ways to try to isolate the slippery object of fascism. Enjoy the activity of finding the method that works for you and what you care about!
Professor Gordon is a fantastic lecturer, and this is a super interesting course. The readings are well selected, and introduced me to modern Western thinkers who I had not previously encountered. Highly recommend!
TAKE THIS COURSE!!!! Professor Gordon is a phenomenal lecturer, and the readings are intriguing. Not sure how the course will be structured in the future, but there was plenty of time built into the course for open discussion, and we were free to choose any topic we wanted for the final paper. This was a low–stress, high–reward class to which I looked forward every week. Cannot recommend this course enough!
I recommend this course with some reservations. Because the course meets once a week (and is more of a lecture than a seminar), it is easy to feel disconnected from the course unless you make an active effort to participate in class and attend office hours. The readings are incredibly interesting, but they are focused on interwar Europe (especially Germany) and 20th–century political theory; though I enjoyed completing the readings, I sometimes wish that our readings had covered a wider geographical range.
Take this course and any course with Prof Gordon! You will learn so much! It's generally a super chill, engaging, interesting, and fun environment
Take it. This was the best lecture course I have taken in my entire Harvard career.
Professor Gordon is simply the ideal of what an excellent professor looks like. He is so learned, brilliant, and yet so accessible and interested in what the class has to say. More than anything, he is a very skilled discussion leader –– he made room for controversy and opinion, allowing an exchange of ideas that is rare at Harvard. If all professors were like him, this institution would be better.
