This is a great course about the fundamental math associated with robotics. It is a TON of work but rewarding. It will take over your life the semester you take it.
if you are into robotics, this class is a lot of fun and very hands–on! granted it is quite a lot of work, as the problem sets are non–trivial and the labs take quite a lot of time, especially if you aren't comfortable with coding in matlab, but it pays off as you get to do many cool things with a 6–jointed robot arm
Definitely one of the better engineering classes I've taken. The lectures are not great, but the book is good and the Northwestern videos animating the textbook are phenomenal. There's a blitzkrieg of derivations at the beginning of the course. Don't sweat every line of math, but do take time to really think about screw axes, the Jacobian, and change of basis.
It's a very interesting class that does cover the theory of robotics well. It takes an ENORMOUS amount of time, be prepared to live in OHs and walk to the SEC on the regular. You can actively feel yourself learning though and there is a lot of freedom to take the course in the direction you're most passionate.
Take this class if you are fine with linear algebra. Very interesting class and improved my understanding of robotics.
It is very math heavy and (a bit too much) theoretical at times. Labs were great for learning.
