This course is great and the teaching staff was amazing at teaching. The readings vary in length and depth for each work but it is moderate.
Take this class like seriously. It's not an overwhelming work load at all, and will give you a plethora of knowledge on healthcare within the United States. This is NOT a copy and paste of global health and sociology classes where they shove the same concepts into you. Although concepts which you may have already heard of will be reviewed, a lot of new things will be learned. Healthcare is really broken down in this class ranging from obvious and commonly spoken about topics such as racism, to not as dove into concepts like the economics of healthcare or the historical policies created influencing it. As a whole, for pre–meds I think this class is a necessity. It really provides a holistic view of healthcare while just breaking it's nature down into the hard cold facts. The course itself also isn't a burden, there are easy weekly assignments which can be completed in less than 3 hours, meaning that really you don't have much work to do aside from just coming to class and taking notes (you can get an easy A if you just put in the work if that is your concern). George and David are great teachers and make the class fun, they aren't so serious but make sure they provide you with the tools to learn. You really get what you want out of the class, so it depends on you. For the low workload and amount of knowledge you can get from it, I'd suggest it completely.
If you have any interest in public health or health equity, YOU HAVE TO TAKE THIS CLASS! This is one of my favorite courses I've ever taken at Harvard. David and George are so sweet and an amazing teaching team, and the course content is filled with genuinely interesting and applicable insights into health as a social phenomenon. Whether you come from a primarily social science or medical science background (or both), you will definitely come away from this class with critical new perspectives. The workload is not too much at all, but you will still get so much out of the class and discussions.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! I would not call this course a gem, as you need to put work into doing the readings and studying for the exams. However, the class is graded very fairly, if there are any concerns about that.
The topics we cover in this class are, of course, very interesting but also incredibly important to be aware of for anyone entering the medical field. This one of the few courses I've taken where the takeaways extended beyond academic and intellectual aspects, and I think everyone should take it (especially premeds, for the sake of the future).
David is an awesome professor, and they are clearly knowledgeable, and their passion is evident. I enjoyed how understanding they were and open to student feedback, and they constantly maintained a comfortable environment.
George is an awesome TF! Sections felt very lowkey and never stressful, and participation felt especially voluntary. He did things called "medical minutes," which were nice opportunities for us to learn more about the medical context of our readings and topics. He was also receptive to student feedback.
I would recommend this course with extreme enthusiasm! Professor Showalter and George are wonderful teachers who make learning fun and exciting. I always looked forward to attending this course, as the class environment was so great. Also, George runs the best sections I've had at Harvard! I was so impressed it was his first time teaching, because he seems to have a true calling. Overall, I could not say enough positive things about this class!!
This class is such an important class for all pre–meds to take–––it teaches about the social factors and interactions that influence healthcare, something that all people aspiring to be physicians should understand.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! ESPECIALLY IF YOU ARE PREMED. Actually one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard.. You get an absolutely eye–opening tour through how our healthcare system reproduces societal inequality, how we interface with it, why it is the way it is, and what people have done to try and change it. The course content is straightforward yet thought–provoking; some of it will stay with you after class and probably for the rest of your life. David is an amazing, compassionate, fair, and very supportive professor. Their office hours are always open to talk about anything.
I LOVED SOCIOL 1146!!! It's my first course in the Soc department, but it was such a great introduction to the department. The course material was so interesting and class and section were always so engaging. We had really meaningful discussions, and I felt like I learned SO much this semester. David and George are SO awesome. They're very passionate about the course topics, and the course is structured in a really nice way. They're also very open to receiving feedback (about the midterm for example) and genuinely care about our learning. I especially loved section because George (who's an MD–PhD in Sociology) presented sociology scenarios in medicine with case scenarios and medical minutes. We had really interesting discussions, and I really enjoyed class and section. I'm confident that everything I learned this semester will help me in the future. This course has already expanded my worldview, and it will continue to shape how I view the healthcare system and how to address systemic issues in medicine. I highly recommend!!!
This is easily the best sociology course I've taken so far. Professor Showalter and George are incredibly knowledgeable in the field and do an amazing job explaining these concepts in a manner that's easy to digest. If you are (or maybe aren't) considering a career in healthcare, you MUST take this class— it's extremely interesting material and it forces you to think beyond the conventional biology of healthcare.
