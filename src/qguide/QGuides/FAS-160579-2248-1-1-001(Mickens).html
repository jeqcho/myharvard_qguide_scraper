This class is super rewarding for anyone interested in security. Make sure to start psets early, but definitely take this class! Mickens is also a great lecturer.
awesome course mickens is MAH BOI everything he teaches is a must take!!

this course is titled systems security but actually covers quite a broad scope since understanding SOTA attacks and defenses requires having a deep knowledge of systems in general. There is a mix of more practical lectures which cover networking basics + some fun ones thrown in – have you ever wondered how Bitcoin works under the hood (and why its all a big sham)? Is it possible to launch targeted attacks against Musk's Starlink satellite fleet? and are you really safe buying contraband on that free vpn you downloaded on Harvard WiFi?
This is a really interesting class if you're even remotely interested in cybersecurity, or just want to know about the important technology in safety and privacy (or discussion on current events). James is a fantastic lecturer with great slides and he always holds hour–long yap sessions– I mean office hours after class to discuss lecture material or how it applies to events happening in the real world (like the various Starlink attacks). That being said, expect to get assignment feedback and grades back two months later, and make sure you take this class with a good group of people because your final project group members will have a big effect on how much you enjoy this class at the end.
I am now a l33t h4ck3r thanks to Professor James "when I worked at Microsoft" Mickens!
If you're interested in systems security, this class is definitely for you! Mickens is a wonderful lecturer, the 3 projects were very fun to do, they're challenging enough to be interesting, but at the same time definitely doable (I didn't feel the need to go to office hours at all). The late policy is definitely harsh, but you're given at least 2 weeks for every project (and in my experience, a project took no more than 2 days to finish, but this definitely depends on programming experience). The readings were consistently interesting to do. The class was still lecture–style so you don't really discuss the papers, but you still submit some observation/questions you have about the reading assignment before every class. Regarding exams: Mickens has a very distinct exam style that focuses on more conceptual questions (which is very different from the usual exam style in CS 61). On this end, I just wished there would be practice exams, but there weren't any that I could find. For exam preparation, I recommend rewatching lectures and ensuring you feel confident on the topics. There's a final project component that you do within groups of 2–3 people, and the difficulty of the project really depends on the topic you select. Overall, I highly recommend this class, but definitely be mentally prepared – it's very non trivial.
This is truly an exciting, educational, and wonderful class. If you entered CS inspired by hackers from movies, then this class is for you. If you enjoy understanding how the things around you – from VPNs, to Bitcoin, to even your own computer – work (or don't work), then this class is for you. If you enjoy feeling the thrill of applying in–class knowledge into real projects, then this class is for you.
This course teaches you so much, and I might even say too much. Professor Mickens is clearly an extremely great lecturer, and his lectures are always packed with information. Moreover, the course covers so many topics; it really is an overview course on security in all the different areas of systems (for instance, we spent one lecture learning about how satellites and satellite security works). Though there are only a couple of psets, it did feel like the assignments were somewhat burdensome, especially towards the end of the semester when most students are trying to study for the final exam and work on the final project. But after taking this course, one will for sure be very well–versed and knowledgeable in security and systems as a whole.
