This course is a fantastic choice for anyone interested in understanding the strategic models that shape politics, and I think its only going to get better. The material is engaging, with every class introducing fresh concepts that are exciting to apply to real–world political scenarios. The "choose your own adventure" style of the papers is another highlight, giving students the freedom to explore topics theyre passionate about in creative and meaningful ways. While the current version is already solid, I truly believe the next iteration in 2025 will be incredible, as the course will have had more time to refine itself and fully adapt to its place within the concentration. If youre considering this course, its definitely worth takingits both intellectually stimulating and rewarding!
This is a useful course on game theory, seems very accessible to those who haven't done it in the past. Lectures are well–structured and clear. Every concept is paired with a connection to political ideas which was nice and something you won't get in other game theory courses. If you've taken a substantial game theory class already or have a math background, the class will be fairly easy but still worth taking for the political application and as a light course.
Take it if you want to build an intuition in modeling in politics. fun, accessible, and interesting. Nothing revolutionary, however
Prof. Hummel is great!
If you hope to go into international relations, investor relations, political activism or any other sort of negotiation–based field, this course is a great addition to your coursework. The course was an exceptional introduction to the logic and intuition behind game theory. Having a specific topic to approach allowed a much easier time of understanding the concepts and applying them in context.
Take this class if you want to enhance your ability to think critically about the world, especially in politics. Prof. Hummel is great and this is probably one of the best intros to game theory you can find at Harvard, not too technical but just technical enough to make the thinking rigorous and intuitive. The discussion is the best part so you do not have to implement these models without understanding their strengths and limitations.
It's not too much work weekly but the papers take some of your time as you need to draw and mathematically explain your logic behind your model and paper.
Fantastic course, another Sarah Hummel masterclass. Lectures were incredibly well organized and clear, wonderful blend of politics and mathematical modeling –– a true political science class. Absolutely loved it and learned so much!
Absolutely recommend this class if you are someone into strategic decisions and particularly how these choices play out in politics. The assignments can feel quite long and like a constant cycle, but no midterm or final exam makes it totally worth it. Professor Hummel is also great and the small class makes for almost a seminar feel.
Professor Hummel is a great professor! However, this is a harder course in the government department. If you don't have experience with game theory or economics, then I wouldn't expect for this to be a walk in the park. Even so, I enjoyed the course and the assignments were reasonably easy! She gives great feedback at office hours and it's easy to succeed in this course.
Professor Hummel is a phenomenal lecturer. The content can be a bit intimidating if you do not pay attention in class, so this is not really a class that I would recommend skipping even if it is not a whole lot of work outside class time.
This class is great, professor hummel is super helpful. The work load is not super heavy most of the time, but the assignments can take you a handful of hours if you put them off until the due date
Lectures were very informative and the assignments were a reasonable workload while still being very helpful towards understanding the key concepts from each module. This class was a great introduction to game theory applied to political models and would highly recommend overall! The workload is reasonable but you still take a lot away from the class!
