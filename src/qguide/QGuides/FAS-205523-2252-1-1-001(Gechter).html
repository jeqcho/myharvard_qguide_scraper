What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This iteration of ECON 2115 was very easy, low workload, and focused very heavily on few topics. It's helpful for thinking about causal inference through potential outcomes (more theoretically), but not recommended if you're trying to learn a lot of different techniques. only 4 psets.
Not too difficult, good introductory course in Econometrics (this felt like the first course in the series versus the second one)
A useful addition to your econometric toolbox, grounded in classic causal inference methods, with some exposure to frontier developments. Not too stressful if you've taken any econometrics in the past. More focused on application than theory. I say this positively — and don't worry, theoretical motivation is still provided and you will run through some of the standard potential–outcomes derivations. A useful opportunity to code a bit and think through the many assumptions incumbent with various approaches to inference. You'll walk away with a better understanding of the bread–and–butter of economic analysis!
For students without prior exposure in causal inference techniques, I think this is a really good course. For PhD students who have taken some causal inference coursework before, this course can introduce you to some tools and literature you may not be aware of, but if you really want to push your skills/understanding I would perhaps recommend taking a course in the statistics or econ department in lieu of or in addition to this course
