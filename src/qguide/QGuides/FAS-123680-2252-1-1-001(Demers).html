For context on what I'm about to say, I had taken AB Calculus in high school before this class. Matt was a great, super effective instructor. PSETs were harder at first but got easier as the semester went on. Class is structured well toward learning, but it felt a bit fast at times. There are a lot of PSETs which took me about 3 hours each doing them in one sitting (so ~9 hours a week) which can feel like a lot to balance on top of other classes, though the PSETs themselves were not super hard and definitely helpful in solidifying your understanding of the material. But tbh I preferred this over 1 PSET due at the end of the week as someone who would've definitely procrastinated until then. Sometimes PSETs took forever to be graded—like I'd notice that maybe a couple days before a midterm I'd have the last 6 PSETs ungraded (which was not horrible because the solutions are always posted 1 day after the due date). All of the CAs were super nice and helpful. Having 9am 3x a week was not great, and got hard towards the end of the semester, though Matt is super energetic and all the math turns ur brain on. All in all a good class that is more time intensive than it is hard imo.
If you are considering whether to take this off–cycle or not, TAKE IT OFF CYCLE. Trust me, I was in the same boat and I don't regret my decision. But I will say I took Calc BC in high school but I did very poorly in the class then so taking the Calc 1 portion here again was very useful in terms of seeing the material again and having it explained way better. The course is VERY fast paced so if this is your first time with calculus, you will probably struggle and all the people I knew that were in that boat, did struggle. In terms, of the professor, Demers, I don't know if he's going to be teaching this class again but I HOPE HE DOES because he is literally the sweetest person ever and is SUPER approachable and I never hesitated to ask any questions or participate in class ever and I'm generally an introvert. I was highly considering dropping this class because I was taking 5 already but he was the only reason why I didn't drop it. Genuinely made me love calculus as someone who used to despise it.

TAKE THIS CLASS!! If you can take it with Matt– TAKE IT!! This class is so incredibly well structured and yes it's a lot of work but they teach you so well. You'll be doing so much active practice that when midterms come around, you'll have been studying already. Do all the psets immediately upon getting them, go to office hours and MQC, and go to all the quiz sessions to make the most of this class. You'll learn so much and get to know the teaching staff and other students.
Even with a background in calculus AB, this class was pretty challenging just because it changed my way of thinking and learning math. They love to throw natural logs, rationals, logs, and trig in everything so as long as you have a solid background in pre calc you should be fine. The exams are almost identical to what you are tested on in calc AB but the psets are pretty difficult and challenging because it's mainly word problems.
Matt Demers (the course head) was excellent. I had not met a better math teacher in my life. On the other hand, the Math department needs to do a better job at screening undergraduate course assistants for this class. The course assistants were often unable to explain concepts covered in class and would regularly make mistakes in grading problems sets. I understand the sheer volume of grading load imposed upon them (3 psets / week) can be tough, but some of the mistakes were truly appalling.
Make sure to go to MQC and quiz sessions and put some effort into studying for the exams.
