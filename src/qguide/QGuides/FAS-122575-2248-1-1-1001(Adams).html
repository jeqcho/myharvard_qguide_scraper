The professors in this course are extremely well–intentioned, but PS2 is in need of some serious reform in order to be a good physics class. First, the positives: the course staff is, overall, very kind and good–natured. Steve and Greg both responded to emails very quickly and clearly wanted students to do well. This is, fortunately, not one of those pre–med classes where the professors revel in it being students' worst nightmare. Additionally, they did their best to accommodate the varying preferences of students. For the midterms, you take the exam once and then do it again in a group. The professors gave the option to do the group exam in person or at home. Additionally, since the final was on December 20, they offered the option to take it from home, which I was very thankful for. Additionally, the midterms and final are representative of the course material and there are no real surprises. This said, the course has a lot of drawbacks. First, the pacing is terrible – both professors struggled to cover all of the material in class (it would consistently take 40 minutes to make it through the first page of notes), meaning we often didn't cover important material in class. The professors try to include a lot of demonstrations, but this often takes out time from the needed content. Overall, I think it would be possible to shorten some of the activities and still get through all of the content. It was obvious that students started to get really bored in those first 40 minutes (because things weren't moving very quickly), which I think contributed to the low attendance (which I understand was really demoralizing for the professors). Second, the pre–activity system is very flawed. I think that PS2 Pal is an excellent supplemental tool, but as a required activity it falls flat. It doesn't cover all the material that is needed (based on the pre–activity handout) and if you have had no exposure to that topic before, it is really hard to get a handle on things, even conceptually. I recommend releasing the pre–activity handout well before class and leaving PS2 Pal as an optional activity, or something you do after you've read through the material once. Third, the problem sets are often quite difficult, considering how much we've learned in class. Beginning with the problem set on rotational motion and for several weeks thereafter, I couldn't do the problems without going to office hours (and I don't know anyone who could). Overall, this class is quite poorly organized and is in need of a much better structure. Steve and Greg were both very kind and approachable professors and I know they had the very best of intentions. I hope that they will implement some of this feedback to improve future iterations of the course!
