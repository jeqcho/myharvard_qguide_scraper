This was my first 2hr+ class but Les does a great job of filling up the time with engaging activities and interesting subject matter. The Perusall element of the course was welcome because it held me accountable to do the readings. All of the assignments are very fair and you learn a lot of content while flexing your creative skills.
Professor Robinson is extremely open–minded (and also a fun teacher to be leading a seminar). The assignments are manageable and well–structured, particularly the scaffolded assignments leading up to the final paper. There is a lot of feedback you get before turning in your final draft. The content of the course is also a great way to explore the topic (as a complete beginner or someone already knowledgeable on war psychology). I would keep in mind that the class oftentimes has group activities (break–out discussions or casual small group presentations) and is not always a fully open discussion, like other seminars may be.
This is an amazing class. Les is an incredible instructor who is very personable, engaging, and helpful when it comes to suggestions on work. The readings can be dense but needing to leave comments on these readings made them less difficult to follow. As you read, you can see your classmates thoughts/comments as you read, which can also be helpful in understanding. The subject material is interesting and will change your perspective of any previous conceptions you had of warfare, violence in human nature, and developments in psychology. You will enjoy this class if you are interested in psychology and/or history––this course feeds those intertwined interests.
I highly recommend this class. The work is interesting and not too much, and Dr. Robinson knows his stuff and runs an engaging class conversation. There is a lot of reading but it is worth it, and the actual essays/projects are not very much work and can be taken pretty much wherever you want. Take this class and any others taught by Dr. Robinson! He is a gem.
Take it!!
Les is an incredible professor, and even beyond that, a mentor that I'm so glad I met. You can tell how much he loves what he teaches, and that energy is infectious. This course changed my mind on many issues within psychology and also the history of American war. The material is super interesting, and though the readings can be long, the workload is manageable. We would often watch movies and other forms of media as well. The final exam gave so much freedom with what I could explore and how I explored itand he always encouraged taking risks. He gave great feedback and I believe I became a better writer after this course. For the HistSci department, I SUPER recommend taking this class :)
TAKE THIS COURSE. Les is amazing and the content is fascinating. You will be exposed to a new type of content and approach to learning relative to most other courses at Harvard.
