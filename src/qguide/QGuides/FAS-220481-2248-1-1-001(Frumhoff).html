What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I very much enjoyed this course. The class expanded my knowledge of current events related to climate and how the responsibilities of fossil fuel companies, individuals, and nations is determined. The class is very collaborative as it is taught in a 50/50 lecture and discussion format. Given this, the grades were very subjective. Assignments, specifically the midterm, lacked structured rubrics leading to added subjectivity within grading.
This is a fantastic course to give you a global, multidimensional view of climate responsibility and climate action. This course will give you the skills to critically evaluate corporate climate claims and pledges, as well as the efficacy of climate activism. Take this course if you are interested in making change in the climate sphere.
If you are interested in climate action (regardless of whether or not you have a background in this field), please take this class! This course is not only extremely organized and manageable in terms of workload, but it is also an amazing opportunity to learn about how climate action is informed by complicated interactions between lots of different stakeholders with differing sets of climate responsibilities. The guest speakers that come to the discussions are also super interesting and provide insights that you don't get to learn from anywhere else. Because this course covers so many different aspects of climate responsibility, there is something in the course for everyone of any background (i.e. it's a great chance to continue learning about climate change through a familiar framework while also learning from perspectives entirely different from your own!!).
I would highly recommend this course to students interested in climate and the environment. It is an excellent way to learn about important concepts and developments in climate change and to debate and think deeply about responsibility and steps to address the climate crisis.
I loved this course so much. Professor Frumhoff is so accomplished yet down to earth, kind, and engaging. The different topics we discussed were so interesting and changed my perspectives.
*** Overall pretty solid class. Each week you have to do a few readings and write a 1–2 page response on them. These don't take too long and the readings are somewhat interesting. The class finishes with an ~20 minute presentation and a 15 page paper, both of which are not too taxing. The teaching staff will give you a lot of advice on these. Content wise, this class was definitely not what I expected. It very much focuses on exactly who should be responsible for climate change without spending too much time on solutions or the standard technical discussions.

Classes can feel a bit long, but that is to be expected in an almost 3 hour class (with a 10 min break in the middle). Overall, the TF, Viktoria was amazing, and this is definitely a class worth taking.
Wonderful course. Prof. Frumhoff has lots of unique experiences to share, as do the guest discussants and teaching fellow (Viktoria). Most students are really interested in this area and invested into discussions, despite coming from diverse backgrounds with respect to climate. Very reasonable workload too the readings each week were fun, and it was also great to work on a more detailed project throughout the second half of the semester. Take ESPP 90P if you can!
