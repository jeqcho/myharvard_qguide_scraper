Do the psets w/ classmates/help available to be more efficient, study w/ practice tests!!
Math 1b is a great foundational class for single–variable calculus.
Do the practice tests. They are the best prep and understanding them is key to getting good grades.
Even if you have taken Calc 2/Calc BC, this course is very difficult and time consuming.
Its important to take advantage of extra help and start psets as soon as possible while the content is still fresh on your mind.
Take this class only if it is a requirement, thoroughly enjoy math, or have a background in calculus (preferably Calc BC). The three p–sets a week are rigorous, as well as the three major exams. I spent a lot of time on assignments that could often feel like busy work, although I learned how to approach math content at Harvard and work with peers from a variety of math backgrounds.
Pay attention in section and use notes to help with the pset. Either check psets with friends of at MQC. Midterms and exams are much easier than practice exams.
It's a well run course, but honestly is both not very fun and not very engaging. As someone who did the highest level of math in highschool and somehow still got recommended for Math1B it was genuinely quite easy. Sometimes a few things will be new or different to what everyone's learnt in highschool but it still is quite basic. It's necessary for a lot of concentrations but I wouldn't highly recommend anybody to take this. Jonier is fun but individual sections can be very hit or miss. Having 3 Psets in a week also blows. At least all the class materials, including the worksheets are well structured and there is plenty of flexibility for PSet drops, etc.The CAs often aren't particularly good either (or have huge variation across sections) so be careful about that as well. Still though it's a well run course and if you don't feel ready for anything in the 20s it's still a good option.
honestly it was just a class I had to take. if I didn't I wouldn't take it. I hope you enjoy it more than I do
Even though 3 psets a week can look scary, I promise that Math 1b is a course worth taking, especially if you don't have a very strong background in math. For example, I came to Harvard that I hadn't done integrals in school, and so I needed a course which would make me go back to the basics and do a lot of math, and 1b definitely gives you that: psets are long but not too difficult, and are super useful to learn the content from sections. Small sections are great, you get to actually do Math in class and even to know your classmates. Exams are super fair: they prepare you with much more difficult practice tests, which is good (heard of the opposite for many other math courses!). Jonier is a great teacher and his exams, as I said, are super fair. All in all, great course either if you need to take it for pre–med/concentration requirement or if you want to start a career in math!
This course is not as scary as it seems. The problem sets are a grind because there are three a week, but please dont believe the horror stories you hear about the course. It is totally possibly to do just fine so long as you put in the work. The exams and grading are all very fair, and youll make connections with people in your section because so much of it is based on group work.
