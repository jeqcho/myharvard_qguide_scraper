This course was fun to take. I hadn't taken a French class in 4 years, I was rusty at first but I feel confident in my French now. The virtual reality component is cool.
You get out as much as you put in! My grammar, which is what I wanted to improve the most, got much better by the end of the course. Get comfortable with making mistakes. No one is judging you on how you sound or what you say, just try your very best. Do study before the unit quizzes! They are very fair as long as you put some effort into reviewing beforehand.
this class is good! personally, i am significantly better at reading/writing/grammar + wanted to improve my conversational skills, and i think this is a good space to do that. i knew most of the grammar points beforehand so that was nice, but i could see a lot of the grammar coverage being confusing or lacking. some of it goes quickly or is integrated into the material quite awkwardly, so coming in already knowing a lot of that was really, again, pretty helpful. the class is overall pretty funnily structured –– you create a character and everyone in the class is 'living' together in the same apartment building in paris. people had a lot of fun with that, and that definitely made parts of the class really fun. you'll write 'memoirs' about what your character has been doing lately, their interactions with other people in the building, etc. this part was really enjoyable! however, i will say that the rest of the homework assignments (reading a $50 online textbook, completing worksheet problems) were really annoying and more tedious than anything else. the quizzes were great –– if you study, you'll definitely do well on them! and lucie, our TF, was lovely. i think it was sometimes hard because the TFs have to adhere to the main course head and the main course materials, and even she rightfully thought/recognized that some of the course materials were annoying, we had to get through them nonetheless. oh well.

tl;dr –– good way to get through the language requirement, but definitely some tedious/annoying assignments and stuff! thank you lucie!! you're the best!
Good class – not hard to jump from French 10 to this one – I even had a summer in between those two classes and still felt well prepared. Course content is interesting – more about French culture and even politics which was interesting. VR component isn't super extensive, just kind of a side activity.
