This class is very intellectual stimulating but requires a considerable degree of effort. This was the hardest class I have taken in Harvard but it pushed me to be a more careful and intentional thinker resulting in a strong basis of international law. Before taking this class I had no experience in international but I dedicate myself to doing the readings, listening intensively in lecture, and participating in section to ensure I understood the concepts. There is also cold–calling but I call it a cold interrogation because it's actually multiple questions in a row. I would recommend you take this class only if you are reading to engage thoughtfully in a law class and have taken previous history and/or government classes. I throughly enjoyed this class.
This message is directed towards undergrads taking this... I'm not saying don't do it, because I don't necessarily regret doing it, but make sure you know what you're getting into. This is not an easy class and it's not really a history class in the way that you might be used to. He cold calls, you'll be expected to learn legal rules and apply them to cases real and imagined, and you'll be learning a lot of legal terminology. You will learn a LOT but it is rigorous and even the 3Ls in the class were saying it's challenging. I just wish I'd known how hard it'd be because it did impact my semesterly course load with lots of my energy being devoted to just keeping my head above water in this class. Difficulty aside, Chris is a wonderful and helpful TF and Fofana is brilliant –– definitely go to his office hours! They're very supportive and understanding that a lot of this is new to undergraduates.
This course is extremely challenging. It is essentially a law school course except undergrads are required to complete an extra three response papers and are required to attend section for an hour a week whereas law school students do not have to. The final exam was an 8 hour exam (that most students didn’t finish bc it was so difficult) and the readings are lengthy and dense. This course will teach you a bunch and get you used to law school coursework and international case law but it is not for the lighthearted and I wish I knew that before going into it.
Great class, but be prepared. Be prepared for about 200 pages of reading per week. The Monday and Tuesday back–to–back lecture format also makes getting these readings done a greater challenge because if you've only completed the Monday's readings during the weekend, then you will find yourself with anywhere between 50 and 100 pages to do Monday night before class on Tuesday.

Also, the final exam was HARD. The final was the most difficult exam I've ever taken in my Harvard tenure (and I'm a senior). You are allotted 8 hours to complete the exam, and despite the professor saying it should take you 4–5 hours, it took me and all the other undergrads I know the full 8 hours to complete the exam. And I did not even complete the exam.

With that being said, however, this is a really great course that is intellectually stimulating. If you're looking for a challenge, then take the course.
I would really recommend this class if you have an interest in international law and history, especially if you are interested in learning about this from a non–Western and critical viewpoint. I also think this is a great class to do as a college student if you want to gain of flavour of law school electives and interact with law students. Professor Fofana is incredibly passionate and knowledgeable about this area of law and the TFs offer lots of support for college stundents.
Interesting topic, readings can be dense, but worth it. Great experience to take
This is a truly wonderful class. If you are at all interested in law or international relations, you should definitely take it. It's also a great class if you are interested in multilateral institutions. The readings are fascinating and the course is rigorous in a refreshing way (there are not enough classes at the college that actually force you to think). I couldn't recommend prof Fofana more.
This is definitely a very hard class, especially if you take the exam–route and decide to do the final exam which is 8 hours long. There's a lot of readings and the professor, though super supportive, may not be the most effective communicator of everything he hopes to deliver in class. So be prepared for a lot of self–study in that regard. However, if you managed to get through this, it is definitely a super transformative experience, mentally and intellectually.
Really loved this course, readings are sometimes complex and you spend the first part in the legal weeds of it all – but a still really awesome class!
