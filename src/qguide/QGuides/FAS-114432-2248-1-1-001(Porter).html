I learned the most from this class than any other class I took this semester, and I thoroughly enjoyed it overall. Professor Porter is a joy to learn from and the teaching team (namely David) were great. That said, the lectures in this course are not useful as far as course content is concerned – most of what is needed for papers and exams comes through the readings. Professor Porter is filled with great stories and anecdotes, but the tradeoff is that only a small portion of lecture is actually dedicated to covering the topic at hand. Additionally, assignment deadlines sneak up fast and were quite close together in the case of the second paper and the final. Overall, I am very glad I took this course and it's great to see how passionate Professor Porter still is teaching it even after 30+ years.
Grades can be arbitrary, prompts for the exams and papers are unclear. David is a great TF but he is leaving the course so not sure how this class might look next year. Be warned that the exams are basically short response papers and truly are super unclear and graded randomly, but if you want to learn about Professor Porter's personal experiences (which are plentiful and fun) then this is a good class.
The experience of taking a class with Professor Porter was incredible – his insights are unmatched!! Some readings can feel reminiscent of what you already know if you have taken U.S. government in the American education system, but the conversations (especially during an election year) make it so worthwhile. (Slightly harsh grading on assignments) DEFINITELY take this course!!!
This class was super interesting, and I highly recommend it for anyone wanting to pursue a career in politics! Professor Porter shares extremely unique stories about his time serving under various presidential administrations, and his knowledge is unparalleled when it comes to understanding how the office of the presidency works and functions.
Take this class if you want to learn a lot more about the government. Great professor who has so much enthusiasm for the topic on top of very bought in students to make for a fascinating and productive time spent in lectures.
Great course. Professor Porter is one of the wisest professors on campus and you can't find the stories he tells anywhere else. David is an excellent section leader who solidifies content from lecture and the readings.
I recommend this course with reservations. Highlights of the course: 1) Professor Porter is an admirable man. You can tell he loves what he does. His stories from his time in government are fascinating. 2) You will learn a fair amount. I feel like I have a much better understanding of how the executive functions and how presidents can be successful.
Some negatives: 1) The course is largely graduate students at the Kennedy School. They are insufferable. They ask pointless questions that derail lecture. They overwhelm the sign–up for the professor's office hours. If you can imagine IOP kids but mid–way through their careers, that is a fairly accurate picture. 2) The lectures can occasionally lack focus. We often do not thoroughly cover the material from the readings (which you are assessed on!) 3) The grading is harsh and idiosyncratic. You must be very carefully to answer each and every part of the prompt. Even so, it is difficult to tell what constitutes a satisfactory response when writing.
So, this course is not a walk in the park. You will have to devote a considerable amount of time to the assignments. They are all essentially essays. (Two papers 5–7 pages. "Exams" are made up of two take–home essays of 3–pages each).
Take this class. Do not be intimidated by the Kennedy School.
Great class, but graded strictly in my opinion. Your grade is almost entirely decided by four written assignments from the semester, and the restrictive page limit on each makes it difficult to pack the content needed in each paper. The feedback also isn't the best because it's not very specific on what you need to do to write at an A level
Take this course! There are so many fascinating stories about what goes on within the White House that only Professor Porter can tell!
