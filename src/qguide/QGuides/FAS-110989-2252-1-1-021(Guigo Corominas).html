Picture this: it's Thursday, and all your friends have made plans to hang out later that night. However, you had not started your 21b pset, so you started it an hour or two before you were supposed to meet up with your friends, because you thought it would take two hours at most, like 21a. Wrong. Instead, you are stuck staring at a confusing textbook for 3–4 hours, still not understanding what the questions are even asking. You miss the hang–out, and all of your friends hate you. But at least you have 21b.
I enjoyed this class far more than 21a (I did not like 21a at all), but my grade will likely be worse. I put in lots of time to the psets – easily 4–6+ hours each – and spent lots of time studying for the exams. The material was incredibly interesting but I found that, like many math courses here, they want you to 'implicitly' learn from the worksheets. This made it very challenging for me, as there was little clear summarization for topics. Overall, the class was incredibly interesting and I feel very capable of applying linear algebra to future courses although this will likely be one of my lowest grades here (I am likely in a high C to low B range).
I had no experience in linear algebra whatsoever and came into this class pretty confident since I did very well in 21a. I was wrong to be confident. This was the first class to push my mathematical understanding since it is a heavily conceptual class. And still I would easily recommend this class. TAKE IT! If you are not an AM or math major this is probably the most useful and practical math class you will take. My main suggestion is to build a strong network of people in the class that you can ask for help and build ideas off of. Personally, I am not a big fan of office hours or MQC since I just sit there stuck until hopefully one of the CAs make eye contact. Even without this support the psets were the perfect level of difficulty and I could usually reason them out on my own. Sometimes they get long so start early.
After reading the Q guide from past years, I was absolutely terrified to take Math 21b. The material is challenging and abstract (and it gets harder as the semester goes on) and it takes time to get used to so I would say be patient with yourself. Roderic was a wonderful TF, and I loved my CAs too. Overall I think that 21b is doable and not too painful!I found the class doable because my other classes were relatively easy, so I could put in 3+ hours for each Pset to really understand them. I wouldn't say that I am super good at math or anything but I did well on both midterms by just doing all the practice exams and trying to not fall too far behind when I didn't understand something. I also found peer tutoring from the ARC to be helpful. Just don't take it alongside Stat 110 or another super hard class otherwise you will truly not have a life outside of your classes. You will be just fine!
After taking 4 intro math courses at Harvard (1a, 1b, 21a, 21b), I can certainly say this is one was the hardest. It was the second most enjoyable after 21a and definitely built off of a bit of 21a, so I'd just recommend starting with 21a. The PSETs for this class are long and having three of them a week is annoying but it really does make you learn the content. Overall, like every intro math class, very solid but again highly dependent on your TF (I had Roderic and he was awesome!!), so don't be afraid of jumping around sections in the beginning to find the right TF for you. My only pet peeve with this class is the T or F, I understand that it allows you to cover more content in a very short format but I feel like there have got to be better ways of doing that than testing corner cases of theory. I don't think students will remember a specific counter example for something about the kernel in an nth dimension vs what a fourier series was and how it is applicable. I just think that isn't very conducive to learning, especially if it can come down to just a 50/50 chance and you not even having to explain your train of thought. Idk. I think if you get them right without knowing you got free points, but at the same time it's very difficult to study and be prepared for those corner cases. Also, perhaps changing the format of the weekly quiz sessions again so that maybe 4 quiz session attendances (so a month) is equal to one pset drop with a max of two extra pset drops or something because I feel like late hours for psets really isn't useful because there is already immediately another one breathing down your neck. The exams were relatively easier than the practices but you learn so much from the practices and they really humble you so you go in prepared for the exams. Overall, great course, content is super interesting but difficult, same old of doing the psets with a pset group, paying a lot of attention in class, and grinding a lot before exams by doing all the practice exams.
Honestly more interesting than 21a but I will say that the tests are slightly more difficult. First exam in this class is much worse than the second so don’t be scared if you don’t do well initially.
This is a good course to learn linear algebra with. It is a lot of work, the psets sometimes turn repetitive and unnecessarily time–consuming, so be aware of that. Overall, it's a solid way to learn the material, it delivers exactly what was promised.
