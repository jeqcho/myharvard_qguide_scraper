This topic was super interesting, it just felt like the section and some of the assignments weren't the most organized. I always really enjoyed the readings and felt like the class discussion was really great, I just wish the instructor was a bit more prepared and could've given stricter guidelines for some of the assignments.
I think the course content was really interesting to engage with, but Highsmith is probably one of the worst course leads I've had here. He's talkative but still manages to never have answers for our questions, and took forever to give necessary feedback. It's a requirement so it's hard to avoid, but I wouldn't recommend taking it with him. Course was also pretty reading heavy, but you didn't have to do them all to thrive (especially if your final paper wasn't about them). His understanding of the final product required from the course did give us a lot of freedom in what we could do, but what was expected of us in his view was still generally unclear until around the end of the course. It's clear he's passionate about the content (which is awesome!), but maybe his passion does not translate into skill.
Great class! I really enjoyed Professor Highsmith as a professor. He's super flexible with the class syllabus based on student demands and he cares a lot about the subject matter, making it a very interesting course. The only reservation I had is that every week by the end of the readings, they became way too repetitive and redundant.
