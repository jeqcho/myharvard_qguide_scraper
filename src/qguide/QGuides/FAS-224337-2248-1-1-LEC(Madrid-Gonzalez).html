This is one of those courses that makes Harvard
Pros:
– Dr Madrid is (or appears to be) THE expert in this field. He knows a lot, and the course is based off of his research, which is so cool. He is also very chill, kinda like dad vibes
– Diverse sonic journey through the course
– Eye opening about what Mexico actually is
– Opportunity to learn a lot about the actual music theory if you understand it
– You will probably acquire a ridiculous amount of niche knowledge that very few people in the world besides professor madrid have, so if you need a course to acquire things to talk about this works well

Cons:
– Expensive book
– Not a lot of grading done before the end of semester, so if you are the type of person to become anxious due to this be aware. However, I don;t think we were graded very harshly anyway.


Don't take this if you aren't enthusiastic about the subject matter though. Dr Madrid deserves much better

Easily one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard. PSAlthough it is under the Music department you do not need to know anything about music nor how to play an instrument. It's also only offered every 2 years.
