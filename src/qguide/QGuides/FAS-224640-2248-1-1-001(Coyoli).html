I strongly encourage anyone with an interest in education and politics to take this course. It's a really great opportunity to engage in meaningful discussions with other students from a wide variety of educational backgrounds and collectively gain a better understanding of how education and political systems are interconnected. Dr. Coyoli was an amazing instructor and helped facilitate in–class discussions and provide valuable feedback to prepare for the final paper. Be sure to stay committed to completing course readings and intermediate assignments as they are really crucial to being able to contribute to class discussions and doing well on the final paper.
This course is great for teaching you about writing for social science research and Professor Coyoli is awesome. You definitely learn a ton about education around the globe but it honestly was not as much as I was expecting– expect to leave with more knowledge in writing skills rather than content.
If you are interested in education politics, DEFINITELY take this class!! The final paper is a lot but Julia is amazing and there to support you along the way and there are a lot of smaller assignments throughout the semester building up to it. Readings are a little dense but very interesting and expand your perspective especially if you are someone focused on U.S. politics.
Take this, especially if you are a gov major. It provides you with the foundation to be a good gov student
This course is hands down one of my favorites that I have taken at Harvard. Dr. Coyoli is absolutely amazing and she teaches you so much about how to understand a source and the context behind it just from one lecture. Additionally, I felt I learned so much about education on the global stage, and how different countries differ from each other not only in their education systems but how politics plays into them as well. I genuinely learned so. freaking. much. I would without a doubt recommend this, and would love to take another class with Dr. Coyoli.
If you're lucky enough to the accepted into this class, take it you will not regret it Julia is a wonderful professor and the content is so interesting. It really makes you think about the impact that education policy has on communities students and teachers. It opened my perspectives to learn more about aspects of Education I hadn't considered.
This is a great class for anyone who wants to learn more about education or is pursuing a secondary. Julia is an amazing seminar leader who knows exactly how to direct the conversation to understand the material better, how to encourage students to talk about their perspectives, and how to stray from US–centric evidence to support an argument.
I loved this course! I really enjoyed the readings and the discussions we had in class were incredibly engaging and complex.
