The assignments are very reasonable and though sometimes time–consuming, honestly pretty fun; the section component is also fun and ties in well with course concepts. This is the rare Gened that actually ended up being one of my favorite classes I've ever taken in college.
If you're at all interested in film, Japanese or not, I believe you would enjoy this course

Professor Zahlten makes taking the course a must. But you shouldn't take this class if your schedule looks roughat least for this semester, we had to go to a 3–hour lecture, 1–hour section, and 2–hour screening. Combine that with the time–consuming video essays, and you have a time commitment that could cause problems. However, the readings were highly engaging, and the course gave me much more to think about than just "global Japanese cinema" (check the topics in the syllabus if you're interested). Overall, I'd recommend this course to almost anybody. Just put in the work and you'll have a great time!
This class is split into 3 core topics:
– Cinematography Techniques
– Broad Japanese History and Nationalism
– Japanese Film Role in Society

To me, the most interesting part of the class was the intersection of the last 2. Unfortunately for me, the focus was on the first. One thing that I didn't know about the course going in was that almost the entire grade for the class (the 3 video essays+final paper) would be focused almost entirely on analyzing the cinematic techniques used in certain scenes of films. There is a bit of a dissonance between this graded portion and the bulk of class time, which is focused more on the history of Japan and Japanese cinema, and feedback given to improve video essays were too general to implement effectively.

Additionally, the course lecture was held on 9am, and the lecture flow was dry with little chance for student engagement. One of the best parts of the course were guest lecturers (producers, filmmakers, scholars, and even performers), but without these class felt very long and disengaging. Teaching assistants are very unforgiving when it comes to attendance and feedback, and aren't extremely helpful when it comes to completing assignments and understanding material.

Overall, take this course if you are very interested in Japanese Cinema or Arts History or some other related field. Otherwise, steer clear and pick something less specialized, otherwise you will feel unprepared and out of your depth.
