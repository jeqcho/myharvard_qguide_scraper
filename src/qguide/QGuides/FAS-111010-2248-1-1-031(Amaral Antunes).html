What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Math 1b is a great class and the material is incredibly engaging. I personally really enjoyed everything we learned, and as nothing is incredible theoretical it was fun to just do problems. I will say a lot of your experience in this class is dictated by your section TF. And also do PSETs early, if you really actually want to understand what you’re learning – a last minute rush will not help. And also work with other students on PSETs, collaboration is essential.
Try to pay attention as much as possible in section. Ask questions whenever you're confused. Don't start the PSETs late and try to stay on top of them because 3 a week is a lot; go to MQC almost daily! For tests, try to start studying at least a week before and make an effort to do all the practice exams they give, they help a lot. The practice exams are purposefully harder than the actual tests, so don't get too discouraged by bad scores on practice tests. Try to form a study group where you guys work on the PSETs together and go over your answers; this will save you if you're not able to make it to MQC one day. It's not difficult to get an A in the class as long as you're genuinely willing to seek help. Also, I wouldn't recommend taking 2 more pset courses along with this course, it might take you out haha.
This is not an easy class and will be a lot of work. As someone who is not the best at math, it was a struggle the whole way and did not get easier for me. I literally cried every week over the topics and the number of psets. However as painful as the class was, it is also extremely rewarding knowing I finished it and challenged myself.
Only take this class if you must. The workload is heavy, as there are three sections a week with three corresponding PSETs. The flow of work is literally constant, and the PSETs cannot usually be completed on autopilot. However, the materials to help understanding in the course are wonderful, especially for the exams.
I am the last to praise a math class, but I genuinely enjoyed this class. You need to find a group within the class asap, and then you can work on psets together anytime. Give the curriculum a chance, even if it is different from how your high school taught it. I think the way they teach calc concepts actually sticks with you. Also, as the course went along our teacher explicitly connected concepts, and talked about big ideas that are super helpful outside of math class.
