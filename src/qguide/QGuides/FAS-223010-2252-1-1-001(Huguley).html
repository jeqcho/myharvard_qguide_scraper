What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you have any interest in understanding war and why states fight, take this course. You'll come away a better thinker having engaged with the content and surveyed the readings critically.
This is an incredibly interesting course that has a few lengthy assignments but if you read, you will learn! Jack is very nice and makes class enjoyable. It is clear that IR is his wheelhouse.
There are better Gov94s and better IR courses. Unless you have a very specific interest in International Security, this course is not worth it. Even if you do have an interest in security studies, I would encourage you to take a second look at the QGuide and see if you could take something more applied. The flow of this course is circular and you generate the intuition through independent reading, rather than assignment feedback or quality discussion
Super relevant course given the current geopolitical climate! I had some background on IR going into the class, which I found helpful to fully understand the course material. While Jack makes an effort to make basic IR theory/foundations accessible to everyone, there's definitely a learning curve if you haven't previously taken an IR class. Expect 60+ pages of reading/week and three writing assignments leading up to a 20–page final paper; sometimes we had to write the next assignment without receiving feedback for the previous one, and readings were pretty theory–heavy. Overall, would recommend — especially if you're interested in learning more about the themes on the syllabus!
Jack is one of the best discussion leaders at Harvard. He is truly one of a kind professor. Make sure to maximize your time with him and even go to office hours to talk about whatever IR and security questions you have. The assignments were challenging but manageable. Definitely a rewarding class.
