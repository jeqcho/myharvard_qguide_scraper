i loved this class with doug!! hes very good with feedback, and willing to work with you to get your essay to a place youd like it. i found the topixs to be really interesting, but i would make sure youre really interested in this anthropological perspective since a lot of the readings focused on the same topics in the beginning. overall, i loved it!
I really enjoyed this class! Professor Bafford is really kind and enthusiastic, and he makes classes and group discussions fun and engaging. He also provides a ton of useful feedback on first drafts, so make sure to read everything he leaves and start thinking about changes you will make before revising or meeting with him one–on–one. I feel like his comments really helped me improve my writing from where it was at in high school, so I think this is a great expos class to take.
This is definitely not one of the easier Exposes, so don't take it if you want to put no work into your Expos. That said, it's definitely set up for everyone to succeed if they're willing to put the work in. Doug gives amazing feedback, and he's super willing to meet you outside of class or at office hours and read/comment additional drafts. The readings are long, but interesting, especially if you put in the work to read them closely or annotate them. But to be honest, once Unit II ends, you can get away with just skimming the readings very briefly. This course also has a generous late policy!! Don't be afraid to use your late days!

Also make sure to ask Doug about his mugs, because he has a different themed mug for each month :)
I loved this section of Expos. I just loved it. Professor is an amazing person who is literally always there for you and is SUPER perceptive, super engaged, and ready to help. Assignment feedback is super helpful and detailed (even with jokes and notes) and if you have any questions you are more than welcomed during office hours. The class starts with a narrow out of pocket question, like "What is Love?" or "What is the next animal that humans should domesticate?" to engage and make sure that everyone is present. The professor is very friendly (honestly, it is a very, very, very good experience) and really wants to make this course as interesting as possible. There are a lot of discussions, very little boring readings, and it just so pleasant to take a part in. Please choose this expos if you can an amazing experience.
Doug is amazing, so considerate and understanding, and he is a great resource for those who seek out his help. He is always available for feedback sessions (and even offered to look over a second draft of my essay and provide feedback even though he had already given me written feedback). In terms of receiving feedback and having a great professor, this course is great. I will say the readings are a bit long, but otherwise it's a fairly lenient course.
