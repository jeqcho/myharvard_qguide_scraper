TAKE THIS COURSE! Professor Hummel is super knowledgeable about protest movements and structures the course super well! The workload is super manageable and you really get to learn about a lot of different protest movements! The materials are well thought out and truly a great mixture of course materials: memoirs (a few chapters which are super enjoyable), research papers (2 articles a week)! Assignments are reasonable, there's a lot of flexibility in what assignment prompt you can do! Section is amazing and the abstract theories become applicable! Grading is fair. Lectures are very interactive and would recommend getting to know Hummel! This is by far one of my favorite courses that I have taken here!!

Learned very interesting things about protest movements, though!

TAKE THIS CLASS! Professor Hummel is great and is always willing to help students.
This is such an engaging and interesting course! Professor Hummel and Shady are absolutely fantastic: they created a welcoming environment with vibrant discussions and pushed students to question and defend their positions. Readings could sometimes be dense but lectures and section were a great, clear, and concise review of the material. There weren't too many assignments, just four reading responses, a few preliminary final paper assignments to make sure students were on the right tract, and a 12–15 page final paper. The final paper was difficult especially because it was open ended (what are the metrics for determining the success and failure of a protest movement) and relied heavily on social theory, but manageable and enjoyable. Would most definitely recommend this course!
