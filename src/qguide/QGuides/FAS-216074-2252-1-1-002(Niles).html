What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Take this class! It is very doable, and you learn a lot of really cool theory.
I'm someone who likes analyzing texts a lot more than I like theory, so I didn't think I would really like this class, or that it would necessarily be a good use of my time. In the end, I felt it was worthwhile. Even though sometimes the theory felt theoretical to the point of being slightly ridiculous, this course gave me a really good overview of the types of topics that are being studied in English today and made me much more confident in reading scholarly articles. I gained a much better understanding of literary scholarship, and feel well equipped to continue my English studies. This course introduced me to some really influential thinkers and their basic ideas, like Foucault, and to some really interesting areas and modes of literary study that I hadn't known about before, like archives. We also got introduced to fields of theory that are relevant not only in English but in all of the humanities (for instance, feminist theory, queer theory, postcolonial theory) so if you would like to gain a better understanding of what those phrases actually mean this class will help with that. We also got to go see old rare books from the Houghton, which was cool. While I don't think that I would take another class with so much theory, I think that being forced to take this one class on it was good for me after all.
Alan made this class such a treat!!! He's incredibly genuine + approachable, and he led very well–structured discussions which centered a good selection of theory/primary source readings we'd do for that week; in all of the assignments, Alan + Celine also gave really good feedback! The environment of this class was really collaborative + friendly. Will say, although some of the theory readings are quite dense (I didn't love cyborg manifesto...), I found myself really digging theory as I went on in the class. Finally, you also get a lot of guidance + support in how to approach the final paper.
You take it because you have to. If you love breadth and learning a little bit about a lot of things, this class is for you. If you like to dive more deeply into individual subjects, you might not like it as much. The assignments are fine, but a lot of them feel like teaching you how to do things you already learned in expos, which is slightly hand–holdy for second semester sophomore English concentrators. Readings really varied in length, check ahead so you don't get blindsided by a lot on a given week. TONS of freedom for the final paper. The class is what it is, basically. Also, two hours and forty–five minutes for a discussion class of less than ten people is a crime.
