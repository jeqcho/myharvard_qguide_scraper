+s:
– Very little weekly homework (your grade is comprised of 5 short post–lab assignments, 3 lab practicals, 2 midterms + 1 final)
– Really interesting assessment formats (the practical exams are great practice for med school)
– Excellent introduction to basic anatomy/physiology. Each class goes over a broad topic (e.g. immunology, nutrition, locomotion, respiration) in a good level of detail

–s:
– Exams are crunch time. The amount of material you have to memorize before the written exams borders on absurd (~11 60–slide presentations), and the lab practicals will require a good amount of time memorizing bone structures/muscles/organs.
– The exams can be really long (midterm 2 was 30 pages) and quite difficult (avg on midterm 2 was ~75). Go through the exams quickly without spending too long on any one question, and you should be alright. Don't expect to have time to check your answers at the end.
