What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This workshop was essential for any student hoping to enter the nonfiction writing world, whether through journalism, academia, digital media, or other routes. I highly recommend this course for people interested in essay, memoir, or science communication in particular. It is an adjustment from typical academic writing, but provides much more benefits to your academic writing than you may think.
This semester was taught by Maggie, not Darcy, so I'm not sure how that will factor into the Q Guide (?) but Maggie was a wonderful instructor—always super kind, welcoming, and enthusiastic. I will say that this semester at least was very focused on personal essays as opposed to reporting or criticism when it came to the readings, so if that's your thing, definitely consider taking this course!
100% take this class. Everyone should take a writing workshop at some point IMO, and this should be the one you take. I may have gotten lucky with prof/classmates, but every discussion was a delight, and every member of the class really took the readings and workshopping super seriously. Tons of diversity of background and perspective – lots of grad students.
I think what was hard about the class this year is that it was Darcy's syllabus but Maggie was teaching. By no fault of her own, this meant the course was a little wonky.
If you're an international student I would recommend caution and get advice about the diversity of perspectives and experiences of participants in the workshop before signing up. It's a great course but you may be made to feel some kind of way about your writing and ideas if they're not wholesale relatable to American experiences (particularly undergraduates).
