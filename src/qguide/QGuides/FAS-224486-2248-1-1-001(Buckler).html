Comments
Take this class!! I really enjoyed the combination of short stories, plays, and their film adaptations. The films selected by Professor Buckler are incredibly enriching to Chekhov's works. Our discussions are a good blend of the historical context of Russia with traditional literary analysis, making it a very engaging and interesting class.
Enjoy the class! It is one of those classes that help engage a lot with your creativity especially with readings that may have an inconclusive ending. This class is one of the most exciting classes I took and I truly love the time I had with my other classmates.
Whether you've never read Russian literature before, or it's your favorite thing, it doesn't matter; this course is for anyone with an open mind and a full heart.
I loved this class! Getting to spend time with Professor Buckler is a treat and even better if you can catch her after class/during breaks. Her course is a good intro to Russian Lit without too much demand. Students who are actually interesting in the literature and want to get familiar with Chekhov will get a lot out of this course. Awesome first year seminar!
Go into the class with an open–mind. The assignments are very doable. The max number of pages you are required to read a week is about 70ish. I strongly recommend this course for all majors (as a bio major). It will help you discover lessons and really think deeply about how society has changed and how society is the way it is.
I would not recommend taking this. I think if you absolutely love Anton Chekhov, you’ll find the class more rewarding, but the in–class experience wasn’t great.
