Another amazing class in the Chinese department! This class feels far more more digestible than 123XB because although the grammar structures/topics/vocabulary is harder, there is less to memorize every week — and so I really appreciated the slower pace. The teachers are also very flexible on assignments, so although there are presentations and projects, they space out the deadlines so you never feel overworked.
If you enjoyed 123xb, you will like this class even more! I felt alot more community in this class than in 123xb, and also the decrease from 80 terms/week to 50 terms/week was a really nice surprise.
TAKE IT. Harvard's Chinese department is not only excellent in terms of teaching, but you'll find a community here. Challenging at times but worth it
THE MOST WONDERFUL, FANTASTIC, BRILLIANT, WELCOMING CLASS EVER!!!!!!!!!!

I came into the class determined to improve my Chinese, but even if I didn't already have that goal, the people in the course definitely would've made it worthwhile anyway. The small class size is PHENOMENAL. You get such a nice little community and so much personal attention... You get to practice Chinese a LOT.

Also, maybe this was just our class, but literally EVERYONE, Yang Laoshi and Lu Laoshi included, was SO FUNNY. So hilarious. I think I laughed the most in this class than anywhere else this semester. Life–changing. Can't go wrong with this Chinese class. Everyone is so kind and loving. Wow. Stunning. Chefs kiss.
Yang Laoshi and Lu Laoshi are great teachers. The class is fun and you learn a lot about different social issues in China. There's weekly homework, and how long it takes just depends on how much effort you want to put in. I feel like the teachers are also very understanding about workload, and they're always willing to help & chat.
