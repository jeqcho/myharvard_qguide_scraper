This class is great! Really improves your skill in reading scientific papers and engaging with a variety of topics. Mark and Doug are fantastic, and Alex is the best section leader. Love that the class is so independent.
This class is an incredible opportunity to learn directly from some of the most accomplished individuals in the field of biomedical research. Each lecture feels like a privilege, as you get to hear about cutting–edge advancements and ask questions that dive into their thought processes, how they achieved their breakthroughs, and their perspectives on novel and even experimental ideas. It’s an amazing chance to sit at the forefront of the future of medicine and therapeutics and to really understand the challenges and possibilities that lie ahead.
This is the best class I've taken at Harvard. Doug and Mark are world–class. The guest speakers are the best in the industry. This course teaches you how to begin thinking about curing diseases and is taught by people who actively cure diseases. Absolutely love. Definitely take it.
You learn a lot in this class in a very interesting way. The professors are in the top of their field and are very knowledgeable. They always brought in really cool guest speakers.
This class is awesome! Super interesting with the MD MBA students there, contributing to the discussion. I never thought about the moral and ethical problems behind drug discovery and the business side of things. This will also be great practice reading scientific papers, if that is something you want. Weekly work is just reading a paper and answering a couple questions on it before section. There is one partnered presentation where you explain a paper related to a lecture concept during section. Then there are 2 papers where you break down and critique a paper of your choice (majority of your grade). Everything else is just participation. **
Every lecturer had an incredibly interesting perspective and story to tell. The course gives a great survey of different domains of biotech and current advancements in drug development and therapeutics. Section is structured so as to develop true critical thinking skills and reading/statistical comprehension as it pertains to scientific papers. I feel like I have not only gained knowledge, but soft skills in science. Assignments are not going to destroy your life – it is actually pretty chill for an upper level biology. Alex was great and tried to make section as interesting as possible, but there were some weeks where the section material unfortunately felt a bit repetitive. There is also a decent amount of redundancy between presenters which feels like an inefficient usage of time – perhaps if presenters were told more about the course before they presented it would lead to a better experience. Papers are mostly interesting, slides are helpful. A good foundation in biological sciences is undeniably helpful. I also felt that lab experience was a huge plus – I found it hard to evaluate the methods of such a wide variety of papers with only limited experience. I really do recommend taking this class!
Amazing class with great speakers.
I think this class can be as much or as little as you want it to be. You can thoroughly prepare for the lectures and be ready to ask questions or you can simply do the two written pieces and get by in the class. Either way, you will learn a lot about this relevant and upcoming field and I would highly recommend taking it.
Great class! Take it – espically if you're interested in learning about therapeutics and medical research.
This course is not a gem in terms of workload, but is a gem in terms of content. The best and most interesting course I have taken at Harvard. You get what you put into the class. The lectures are interesting (guest each class who is an expert in their field), medium workload each week, and two written papers. However, this is one of the first classes where I actually learned something that I will keep in my memory forever, not just meaningless facts. Take this class!
The lectures were fascinating. Each lecturer was at the top of their field and covered an interesting topic.
SCRB197 is a great class if you're interested in translational medicine. I took the class because I'm interested in medical research but wanted to figure out if I'm leaning towards basic or applied science, and I feel like this class does enough of an introduction to landmark biomed papers and clinical studies that you walk out with a sense of the main challenges that a benchside scientist faces versus those of a clinical–trial scientist. I took this concurrently with MCB60 and loved the overlap between the two classes. It's unnerving to not receive a lot of feedback for the two main written pieces but overall the rubrics are fair and the assignments are very doable. I really recommend SCRB197! I wish I had taken more advantage of the connections that the class presented every lecture.
start early and go to office hours for your written assignments. wish I did that for my first one — but i did it too late and it was def reflected in the quality of my work. but my TF was super helpful and supportive and helping me with feedback and also brainstorming for my 2nd written assignment
TAKE THIS CLASS! One of the best SCRB classes to take. The lectures are given by guest speakers who come and speak about their research/experiences in medicine. We had one of the founders of Moderna come to class! The workload is super manageable, and you get to dive into your research interests through the essays you write — you can choose the scientific papers for your essays (which are a major part of your grade). There are no exams, so you can focus on actually learning instead of stressing about grades. What you get out of it really depends on how much you put in!
It is a great class for anyone interested in the translation of basic science into medicines. You get to hear from incredible speakers, and are pushed to go beyond the science to consider broader societal implications.
One of my favorite courses I've taken at Harvard. You will hear from the world's top experts on all sorts of interesting topics in therapeutics and regenerative medicine. You will walk away knowing how to dissect a scientific paper with ease. The incredible syllabus lineup also speaks for itself. Profs Fishman and Melton are brilliant and clearly care a lot about sharing their experience with undergraduates.
Truly a class you can only take at Harvard. The speakers invited were at the forefront of their fields in therapeutic research and the course materials really helped us develop a strong translational framework with which to evaluate current research. I think most of the bio classes are very theoretical, but this course did an amazing job at incorporating student feedback, helping us engage with the material, and understand how the research being conducted can go on to make real changes in people's livelihoods. Alex, the TF, is definitely a standout — highly recommend trying to get her if you can. This is definitely a survey class, but a really effective one. There are MS/MBAs, but not that much interaction between undergrads and MBAs.

Assignments are weekly Canvas discussion posts, 1 paper presentation, and 2 papers. Really well–spaced out and the papers and presentation in particular really help you develop a great framework to challenge the experimental design, conclusions drawn from experimental results, and understand what are the therapeutic implications of the research presented — all things I haven't found in other courses at Harvard. Grading is fair and if you put in the work, you will definitely do well.
This is an incredible class that offers a unique opportunity to interact with the big heads in the research/pharma field. The lectures each have impressive accomplishments ot share and are successful in their fields, providing a unique perspective on these areas of science. The assignments for the class are extremely easy to manage. It's definitely one of the best courses you could take to fulfill the bio requirement for HDRB or other bio concentrations.
