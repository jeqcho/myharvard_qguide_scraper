What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You don't necessarily have to read the full reading that you are assigned per week. What I recommend is skimming the readings and trying to find something that interests you. A puzzle, perhaps, that has you questioning why political actors act in a certain manner given a certain context. You will be trained a bit on how to identify this yourself and don't expect the section leader to give you everything. The thing about poli sci is you need to train yourself in doing this and being curious is the answer to finding a research topic.

I took the climate change section, but it wasn't what I initially wanted. I recommend that you take a section topic that you are greatly interested in because (1) it will make your life easier, (2) it will be more interesting and geared towards something your passionate about, and (3) it can serve as a potential senior thesis. The assignments will prep you on your lit review and research design. Together it helps you streamline your final paper. So don't worry about this class being that time consuming. Just be prepared for the first paper and prepare early.
Gov 97 is required of every gov concentrator, so there's no escaping it, but please choose a topic that actually interests you! I found the writing process to be much easier on a topic I was passionate about, along with the discussions we'd in class. Brandon is an excellent seminar leader and is highly accessible outside of class. Sometimes writing papers can feel a little frustrating, but if you are thinking of thesizing, this class gives you great insight into what it's like to thesis (sets the tone, lol).
The course itself feels like a Gov version of expos, but I love Brandon!! Great classmates too, good community bonding. Learned a lot about policymaking!
Don't take this section if you don't deeply care about climate policy –– as in climate policy isn't near the top of your list of social issues. I was miserable for most of the semester and dreaded coming to class because I literally could not care less about the topic. The TF is great and you can learn a lot from him and the other students, but it's not worth the mental struggle, in my opinion.
Gov 97 is what you make of it, stay on top of readings, make sure you meet with your TF before assignments, follow the grading rubric religiously
