Definitely take this class! Joey is an amazing instructor and the class discussions were always so interesting. I really enjoyed the reading materials, they were all super different and mixed a lot of different methods / perspectives. It's also not too much work or reading at all which is great. (Be sure to do the readings though, the class is all discussion so it'd be pretty obvious). Highly recommend this course, no sociology background necessary!
Joey is a great course leader, and this is a great class to take, especially if you are interested in the topic of homelessness. I loved that conversations continued among people in the class even after the 2 hours were over, as people actually cared about the topic and wanted to learn and think about how to both approach homelessness from academic and pragmatic lenses. Every student brought an interesting angle to discussions that was valued in the class.
This is a great class especially for folks who volunteer at HSHS/Y2Y or have done housing/homelessness related work. Joey is a fantastic discussion leader and does a good job of curating interesting readings on a wide variety of topics within the field. The class has a very reasonable amount of work. Make sure to start the final project early in order to do the interviews and research that you need for a good sociological paper.
