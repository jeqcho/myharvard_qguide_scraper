What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a newer course. We were the first year so I am sure it will be improved. Museum and archive visits were a cool component of the class which we all enjoyed! We spent half of the semester reading about quilts and applying craft theories to quilts which was not terrible but not too interesting either. Our 2nd essay, we were allowed to choose a specific object to analyze and were basically told to write a 8–10 page paper about our analysis with no direction. I am sure later years will have more access to reference materials but it was just difficult to write an essay from scratch with little to no instruction, yet with expectations on its outcome. Feedback was amazing and very very useful. Stay on top of readings and participate in class or you with struggle with essays.
I would say do the readings or else writing your essay will be a pain! There aren't assignments for each reading, but the discussions of the readings in the class will only be helpful if you do the readings ahead of time. Don't give yourself a hard time by saving the readings for when you have to work on the essay!
Only take this class if you find arts and crafts interesting. The workload is pretty light but I wasn't interested in it so I found it hard to read. If you are looking for a fairly easy expos course this is good compared to what ive heard about other sections, but the only downside was that it was boring in my opinion.
You probably won't enjoy this class as much if you aren't at all interested in the subject, but if you are, definitely rank it high!! Prof. Zecca is so incredibly kind and supportive (reading her comments and feedback can literally make me feel better about myself as a person). Workload for the class is pretty light––the only thing I'd say is that the final paper for each unit can be a tight deadline, but there aren't too many writing assignments (no discussion posts!) and the reading is definitely manageable (especially in Unit 2, you can definitely get away with just skimming some of the articles if you're really busy with other stuff). Having only 2 papers and a capstone (fun and pretty easy!) is a huge plus as well. Overall, def recommend!
Try to read the first couple thick readings in depth, those you will have to base your arguments around in the future. Also, it might get a little dry in class, but try to participate–– it makes things more enjoyable for everyone. DO NOT EXPECT TO DO ANY CRAFT (may change next year)
Professor Zecca gives great feedback and is very understanding. This is a great expos craft for people who enjoy craft, and there are only two essays and a craft–based capstone instead of three essays!
