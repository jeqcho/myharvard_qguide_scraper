If you're interested at all in learning about children (whether you want to be a parent or whether this topic just fascinates you), this is a must–take. Caitlin is a wonderful lecturer and you really learn so much about children's social worlds. It's a great class for anyone new to sociology or even for the sociologically–adept researcher. Lots of fascinating topics and readings for you to explore throughout the course. You will care a lot more about the ways in which our society treats children and you will recognize how we weaponize childhood for our own adult aims. TAKE!
This course is very interesting and I recommend it. The readings are easy to digest and extremely doable. I spent only like two hours on the readings for each class meeting. Do now that they grade extremely strict on papers. The final exam wasn’t bad but definitely study. Most of the course seems like common sense but what it does give you is the why behind it. Overall, I loved this course and Professor Daniel was an amazing instructor.
IF YOU HAVE EVER BEEN A CHILD YOU SHOULD TAKE THIS CLASS!!!! Professor Daniel is a fantastic professor and the course material is so interesting and engaging because we have all (presumably) been children before so you can inherently relate to the content. This class opens your eyes to the ways in which children shape our lives and how we shape children's lives, and after it ends, you won't see the world the same. The workload is super manageable with just two fairly short midterm papers and a final exam. The readings are incredibly interesting so they never feel like much work and class discussions are always fun because Professor Daniel encourages students to share their expertise as former children through anecdotes and childhood memories. All around one of the most worthwhile and impactful classes you can take at Harvard right now. You will leave class every day with something new to think and talk about. Take this class, you won't regret it!!!

This was a very interesting class that got me to learn more about the sociology of children and think about childhood in ways I hadn't before. Professor Danial is a great lecturer and great at leading class discussions. I felt engaged in the class and got to meet other people.
I LOVED this course! It's one of many sociology courses I've taken, and stands out. Caitlin Daniel knows her stuff. She is sharp, reasonable, and clearly an expert in the topic. She is able to command the room and keep all students engaged – lecture often did not feel like we were being talked *at*, but engaged *with*. Assignments were reasonable (2 essays and a final 2 hour sit–down exam), and the content was interesting enough to organize / keep track of. Readings were interesting and a very reasonable length (shorter than some sociology classes I've taken, longer than others – quite average reading amount). Importantly, the course content shaped the way that I think about children, children's agency, and our social constructions of children! It drives home the idea that everything is a social construction. Highly recommend this class!

FYRE CO CHAIR CERTIFIED: This class is extremely interesting! There is so much opportunity to relate to what the course is teaching because you yourself were once a kid too! This course is not difficult, but its not unchallenging either. You usually have to read 60 pages a week, which is so fair, and the readings are always super relevant to what is taught in the course. You get a really good survey of the sociology of childhood and how its changed throughout history. The written assignments are fun and 4–5.5 pages which means you're not slapping on words to a google doc trying to get to the page limit. The final exam is also super fair, although you did need to study to do well. It was very much like an AP exam.

I think the most challenging part of this course to me was being able to identify social constructions of children while reading or consuming media. This definitely came with practice for me. To me its analogous to trying to correct an essay but your brain keeps autocorrecting any errors you come across.

Overall such a wholesome course! I was never bored, and I was always excited to come to class.
