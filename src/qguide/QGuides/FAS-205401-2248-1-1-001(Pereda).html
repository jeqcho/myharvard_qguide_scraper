My feelings towards this class are very mixed. On the one hand, I genuinely found both lecture and section very engaging, and I can confidently say that I know more about Spanish golden Age painting now than I did before I took the class. But, as a relatively type–A person, the lack of organization definitely stressed me out more than the actually course content. By the end of the semester, the canvas had not been updated since the last time this course was taught, in 2020. There was no actual syllabus, no rubrics for anything, and communication between the professor and teaching staff appeared to be nearly non–existent. I'm sure for most students this posed no real issue, but I felt like I spent half the semester seeking out basic information that should have been readily available to me. Also, the grading is lowkey strict lol
I highly recommend this course to anyone and everyone! I came into this class with pretty much no prior knowledge of art history, and I absolutely loved it. I took away so much from the course, not only about Spanish Golden Age art, but also on life more broadly. Professor Pereda is an incredible lecturer and is so knowledgeable and passionate about what he teaches, while also maintaining a remarkable humility given all that he knows. He always fostered a very welcoming class environment that invited all of us, regardless of previous background knowledge or experience, to contribute to the discussion and share our own interpretations. In addition, the class projects always felt very meaningful, and I actually found it quite exciting to formulate my own analyses using my personal experiences and the knowledge that the course equipped me with. This has been one of my favorite courses in my nearly 4 years at Harvard so far, and I am so grateful to have taken itI hope you have the opportunity to do so also!
