AMAZING CLASS! This was the first anthropology class I've ever taken, but it made me much more interested in the subject and all of the course material was super interesting. Although you don't need any prerequisites for the course, the class will be much more enjoyable if you are already interested in families, relationships, reproductive technologies, or cultures. Having an interest in the subject made the essays actually fun to write, and the class discussions entertaining. The instructor has lived in six continents and has really interesting lore and research that enriches the subject matter regarding kinship. She will question your pre–existing beliefs and encourages everyone to discuss their perspective. The classroom environment is academic yet informal, and students feel comfortable voicing their opinions on the content, which makes the seminars fun to attend. Of course, the class is still rigorous and requires attentive reading and participation. Overall, I loved this Expos! Highly recommend.

Take this course—especially if kimcraig is teaching it. I learned so much not just from the incredible materials she curated, but from the depth of her lived experiences, which she shared with care. It often felt like I was being given a window into her soul, and I’m forever marked by that. She put her whole heart into this course, and because of that, it became so much more than just a writing class. This class became a space for real transformation. I’ll always be grateful for it!
