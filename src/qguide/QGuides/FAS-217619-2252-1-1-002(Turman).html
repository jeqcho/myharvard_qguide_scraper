What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I really loved the class overall and learned a lot about French history and culture while also learning more grammar and solidifying my previous knowledge. I think that I mainly improved my speaking skills a lot and felt more comfortable conversing by the end. If you feel like you have a solid basis but don't think you are at a "proficient" level yet, this is the course to take. Dr. Turman is AMAZING and very kind and fair, and is always willing to answer questions and let you ramble on in French even when what you are saying isn't perfect. I think she has a lot of passion and enthusiasm for the class which definitely translated to good conversation and fun vibes. There is A LOT of homework which is mainly writing answers to a bunch of questions in response to a reading or a video. This class will push you in the best ways!
Je peux parler français avec plus de confiance. That was my goal in taking this class, and Dr Turman for sure helped me achieve that goal. When the class is in the 8–10 hour/week, we really mean this is intense. And this is the average, expect more hours during the staggered week with the midterm project and essay, as well as the essay and final project. We were a group with very few people < 10, and so it was easy to get caught comparing yourself, however, that level of work ethic and interest in the course pushed me to dive deeper into the french than to do simpler do the minimum. Id say its an effective learning environment if you treat it as a way to consume as much french as possible for your own betterment, as opposed to a metric stick to get over 94% and get the A.
