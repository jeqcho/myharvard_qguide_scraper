take this class if you want a fun way to learn about earth science and the crazy animals that have lived in and on the earth! the instructors are great.
Both professors are good lecturers and are engaged with the class. If you're interested in OEB, it's probably a good introductory class to the history of life. I had literally zero OEB knowledge going in which made some of the content basically roll off me because I had no background/frame of reference for it. The class isn't too difficult or time consuming—there's no pset, just lab assignments that are sometimes basically a pset. Labs were hit or miss. Some involved looking at cool fossils, and some were mostly googling what the differences between two random extinct plants are. I discovered about halfway through the class that I am not a history of plants and animals enjoyer, which unfortunately led to me going "why do we care?" about a lot of the later content (which is mostly that) despite the lectures being good. So if you're considering taking this to fulfill an EPS sub–discipline requirement, I'd recommend thoughtfully assessing how much the evolution of life actually interests you.
Great class. It gives you a great overview of earth history, and you learn a lot about how the earth evolved into what it is today, both physically and biologically. Nadia, Javier, and the TF's are very kind.
This class is interesting, and it is a pretty decent balance between biology and EPS. The work load is fair and the teaching staff is supportive, but make sure you stay up to date on the lecture notes and actually attend class. If you don't, thdings can start becoming a lot because such a large time span is covered. While the midterm and final are fair, they can become difficult if you do not spend enough time reviewing. Overall, this is a good course that teaches you a lot about how the Earth has evolved and changed over time. It is not super easy or super hard, but it requires consistent attention and effort to thrive.
