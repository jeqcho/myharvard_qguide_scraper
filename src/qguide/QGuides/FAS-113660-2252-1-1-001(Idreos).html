Take this course! Professor Stratos and the entire teaching staff is awesome.
Fantastic class, truly learned a lot. Stratos is a great professor and his lectures are engaging and interesting. Would recommend taking it!
Reading the recent research papers and presenting to the class was initially intimidating, but it was a fantastic experience. The first half of the class is basically just looking at what is happening at the DASlab, their research, and an introduction to the systems project, which was so amazing that I wanted to implement both of them. The second half is where students present research papers; each group is responsible for presenting papers. Choosing a good group, prep for it. This class is a no–brainer; take it. I loved this class. Probably one of the best CS classes I've taken at Harvard.
Take this class if you want to research with DASlab. The class is generally interesting (especially Prof Idreos's lectures in the first half of the course), and comes with a substantial research project towards the end. The workload is very reasonable, though the grading is unknown (nothing in the semester has been graded...). If you liked the content of CS 1650, you should take this class. To be clear, this class is more geared towards ML–sys, but the fundamental technical principles are the same.
The class changed a lot over the course of the semester. The first part was a rehash of CS 165, but then the second part had more new research ideas in the realm of Big Data Systems. I can't express how important the core principles of this class are. You will learn about the ins–and–outs of Model Parallelism, bottlenecks in RAG architectures, multi–model training, and other flashy topics. But this is not the AI slopaganda you get online: I really learned the core challenges when designing robust and performant systems. Why does model training not scale: overhead of replicating parameters across GPU nodes fills out VRAM. Why is GPU training slow: communication is not fully overlapped with computation. Honestly, I think the secret code to life is just data movement, prove me wrong.

The project experience depends on which track you picked: Systems project it will be similar to CS 165 but more related to the class material, i.e. second hardest undegrad CS class, Research Project, it will be less clearly defined and its what you make of it –– some are really time intensive while others will be like a CS pset essentially.

Beyond the assignments, the DASLAB series (165, 265) has honestly been great. The lab takes an underrated approach in integrating the lab members with the class enrollees. Its essentially one big community, and often extends beyond the limits of a semester, e.g. some research projects run throughout the summer and the following semester, so great course if you are looking for publishing with a research lab.

Also shoutout Stratos, Qitong, Utku and Sanket. Absolute GOATS
