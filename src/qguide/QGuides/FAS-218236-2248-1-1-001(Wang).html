The course provided an in–depth introduction to popular culture in modern China, with a particular focus on Republican era Shanghai and the socialist period. The readings are varied and cover a wide variety of mediums, from literature to film, from music to games, from comics to animation. The professor is extremely knowledgeable and an honor to learn. The section leaders are also very approachable and kind. The course covers some history context, but overall emphasizes the personal appreciation and understanding of the themes in the popular media. Sometimes the slides are sparse so you have to take good notes to capture the professor’s content. Given that China’s modern history is not that widely taught, the history background for the course can be somewhat high. Nevertheless, I personally found the readings interesting and enjoyed the class immensely.
