What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Write a thesis!
The actual presentation of your thesis is as important as the research you are doing. If a thesis reader can't grasp exactly what it is that you've done in a quick glance, then you're setting yourself up for failure. In a perfect world, all thesis readers would take the time to read every page—but this is not the reality, especially since we have no control over who reads our work. In order to have time to work on the actual structure and writing of your argument, CONTINUE WORKING OVER WINTER BREAK! I did this, which provided a lot more wiggle room to inevitably make mistakes.

Something that is little talked about is the honors exam. It creeps up quickly—unlike what you may expect, it doesn't happening during finals period. If you take spring break off to decompress from thesis writing, then you only have two weeks to study. That's simply not enough time. If you can, try to start studying earlier. That being said, this a big critique of mine of the Economics Department, and I hope that this will change soon.

Good luck to all thesis writers! As long as you stay diligent, you'll make it out on the other side!
