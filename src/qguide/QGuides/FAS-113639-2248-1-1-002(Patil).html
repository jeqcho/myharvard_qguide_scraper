I think the professor gets unnecessary hate in the Qreports. She is really really good. I learned a ton, and she is super nice. TAKE THIS CLASS
I strongly recommend this class! As a freshman, it was nice getting close to a group of people and seeing them around campus. We would ask questions to the person we sat next to in Hindi which helped us get to know each other while learning. I have tried to self–learn Hindi multiple times and could never figure it out but this class made it possible. It can be difficult at first because you are learning a new alphabet and new language but after you get through the first two weeks, it gets so much easier. There are daily assignments that are due every three weeks and I recommend staying on top of these assignments because they could pile up (which did happen to me but it was still manageable). The weekly assignments take less than an hour and truly put your knowledge to the test. We went over the questions before we turned in the assignment so you can correct your work before you turn them in and do well on the assignments. I genuinely loved this class and looked forward to it every day!
If you want to learn Hindi–Urdu, then take this class. The language is not particularly difficult, but will take some time if you have no background in the language. The workload is easily manageable, and the final exam is not particularly difficult as long as you spend some time grappling with the grammar throughout the year. Really try to immerse yourself during class, speak in Hindi, write in Hindi, ask questions in Hindi. If you do, you'll quickly grasp it. Both Hajnalka Ji and Richard Ji are good teachers that will explain the grammar of the Hindi language, as well as important historical and cultural information necessary to truly grasp a language.
Listen, TAKE THIS COURSE!! It will change your life. I came into this class disconnected from my culture, mainly because of my language, but now I feel like I've embraced it and feel so much more in tune with myself. Besides that, ITS A GEM!!! Especially for heritage speakers, a lot of it will be things you are familiar with, and though non–heritage speakers may have to put in like 1.25–1.5x the effort, it's still really easy to pick up. The work is graded fairly and you learn a lot of new information in a low–stress environment. The class is really fun, people joke around a lot and it feels more like I'm with a group of small friends than a class. Sections are small, so maximum teacing time. About sections, Hajnalka Kovacs is actually great. Reviews last year dragged her into the mud, so much so that I was scared to get her, but she's awesome. She really knows her stuff (as our awesome TA Tulaib said) and is very chill with how much attention people pay. With minimal instruction, she got me to understand Hindi's grammar system form zero knowledge. You would think that a grammar–focused approach would be boring, but she makes it suprisingly effective and intuitive, even if class can be dry sometimes.
