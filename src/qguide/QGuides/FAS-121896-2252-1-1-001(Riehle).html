What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Highly recommend for people who already mastered the essential knowledge about Ancient Greek, and are interested in Byzantine history and culture. Also I believe this course should be a “should–better” course for people who work on modern Greek language and literature, to feel how vernacular was formed step by step.
Absolutely fascinating class! I highly recommend! This course was incredibly well thought–out: all components, from translation assignments to our Houghton visit to exploration of Greek grammars and dictionaries really complemented one another. In particular, the Greek and English materials were fascinating, informative, and adequately challenging. I am leaving this class with a strong grasp of the evolution of medieval Greek and the historical and cultural forces that influenced/accompanied this development.
Also, Alex is a wonderful professor––very very knowledgeable but simultaneously down–to–earth, friendly, responsive, and encouraging.
