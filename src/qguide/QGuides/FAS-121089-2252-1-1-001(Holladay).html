This class is great. You learn about syntax domains in the first weeks, but then the course shifts to be defense sessions where you complete problems and then have to defend your answers to Kaden and Aljoša. They’re really nice about it and will ask questions, letting you come to the answer through what they ask you, which makes learning syntax fun. Make friends with people, as the problems are easier in groups and you need to collaborate with people who are experts in other domains for the last problem, which you complete over reading period.
This was my first linguistics class at Harvard. As a mechanical engineering major, I did not have so much time for electives nor did I have past experience with linguistics outside of a vague interest. From a friend's suggestion, I took this class as my first free elective at Harvard. (I'm a senior now). It was easily the best class decisions I have made. I had so much fun in this class. It was taught in a way that is accessible to beginners. Kaden's energy and excitement about syntax was really infectious and I found myself happy doing the homework. The class was not terribly difficult, but for those with no linguistics experience, it can be tricky to think about syntax in the way that the problems require, but you catch on pretty quickly. Had I taken this class earlier, I may have pursued a linguistics secondary. This was easily one of the best classes I have taken.
This class is a very approachable introduction to the field of syntax. That being said, a lot of said introduction is self–led. Assignments are graded based on your ability to "defend" problems of escalating difficulty related to four different specialities within the larger discipline of syntax. The assignments are difficult enough to feel challenging, but not so hard that they cannot be tackled within the allotted time, especially when working with a larger study group.

The course falters just a tad in its flipped classroom format, as Kaden leads with lectures that eventually fizzle out into student–led "open sessions" in which individuals can work on ongoing assignments and, when ready, "defend" in front of either the TF or the professor. Although the professor provides extremely constructive and specific feedback, you are still expected to master difficult syntax concepts solely from 10–15 page essays provided at the beginning of each assignments which vary in instructional clarity. I would say that the class is good and I would recommend it to those interested in syntax or linguistics as a whole, with the caveat that you will likely be teaching yourself (with plentiful support!) for the vast majority of this course. It's certainly not impossible to succeed –– in fact, Kaden clearly invests a lot of effort into ensuring each student progresses from problem to problem on track throughout the course –– but the course ultimately relies on you and your self–motivation.

Another important note is that this course divides syntax into four specialties, which each student is encouraged to adopt "expert status" in by completing three problems: wh–movement, argument movement, head movement, and binding theory. Because of this format, most students will complete the novice level problem for each of the aforementioned categories and then focus the remainder of their efforts towards more complex problems in just one of the disciplines. This results in students leaving with a somewhat lopsided knowledge of syntax as they are mostly knowledgeable about one specific niche. I think this is actually a strength of the course because it enables students to feel like actual experts in at least one subfield of linguistics by the course's conclusion, but it could be a less advantageous attribute if you're looking to sample every subfield evenly.

However, once we got to the free work session part of the class, it ended up being more fun in my opinion because we get to choose which assignments to complete, and you have to defend your answers to one of the course staff –– while initially daunting, getting one on one time to talk about my answers really reinforced my understanding and they were always nice enough to help us through it or give us hints to keep working. Felt sorta like a game at time since in order to get the next harder assignment in a specific topic, you had to complete the earlier assignments first.

overall really enjoyed this class, and you should consider taking this class even if you aren't a ling concentrator
