This class is great! I enjoyed it more than Spanish 30 since the materials we work with are far more interesting. While the movies sound like a daunting amount of time, you can usually watch on 2x speed if you need to. I was also taking Chem 17 and Neuro 80 this semester, and this fit well into that schedule and didn't take away much from those classes. The final project was to make a short film which was really fun, especially to see everyone else's! One of the themes the movies focus on is immigration, which was interesting to talk about it class. Overall, you get out of this class what you put in!
This course is structured for students who have a mastery of Spanish grammar and a large Spanish vocabulary. The course focuses on immersing students in the language and forcing them to use Spanish in informal, conversational situations––which is valuable––but it only covers grammar and vocabulary in a rapid, cursory review. The movies and readings for this course are excellent, and the assignments are moderate and graded fairly.
LOVED this spanish class! I think spanish 40 is definitely an engaged class and everyone is pretty fluent. The films are super interesting and I loved the final project to all make a movie. I highly recommend taking this class and its not too hard, but requires consistent work.
Spanish 40 is so much fun!!! It's basically a movie club conducted in Spanish – each week you read a short story and watch a movie for homework and then you come into class to talk about it, and all of the materials are amazing and thought provoking and really show a broad spectrum of cultures/experiences
Aina was an incredible professor – she facilitated class discussions and left very detailed feedback on all of our assignments
In terms of assessments, the class is very chill – the only challenge was the final short film assignment which can come at a busy part of the semester (also it really helps if you have someone with imovie experience in your group)
Overall I would highly recommend this class; it was so much fun and insightful and also chill!
This class is amazing! Highlight of my semester! Teaching staff is so welcoming and helpful; movies are entertaining and relevant; assignments are straightforward and good practice. You will genuinely get better at Spanish, I promise.
You'll watch lots of dark movies about gang violence, rape, dictatorship, etc. If that bothers you, I would look at the course materials ahead of time and choose wisely. Otherwise, it's a pretty chill class. I enjoyed the insightful discussions about very intense matters. The grammar review felt kind of lacking, and I definitely struggled on exams and essays because of that. But I didn't have a good Spanish education in high school– you definitely need 4 years of GOOD preparation to get the most out of this class, because I was often hesitant to voice my thoughts because my Spanish wasn't good enough to communicate them. That being said, I did alright, and I've heard that Spanish 10–30 have a heavier workload, and I do plan to take Spanish 50 after spending winter break working on my skills, so you might be fine anyways.
