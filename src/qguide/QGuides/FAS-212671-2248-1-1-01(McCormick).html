Professor McCormick and Reed are extremely knowledgeable and passionate about history! I'm not a history concentrator, but this class was fun to take. The workload is on the lighter side, but you should take this class seriously and keep up with content. Professor McCormick and Reed are super approachable and open to questions even beyond course content.
This might be the best course I have ever taken in my life! The course staff truly cared for us as human beings and were so passionate and dedicated to the subject! The course was very inclusive and welcoming to students of all backgrounds and we learned things across an incredibly broad spectrum always taught in the most engaging way. This course fostered intellectual curiosity in all the best ways! It covered such a large variety of topics and taught everything in such an accessible manner so that you didn't require any background! The field trips, guest lectures, and guest lunches were an outstanding way to extend our learnings and further explore the field. The teaching staff were incredibly kind, and generous, and really went out of their way to get to know us and to care for us. This class is an incredible gem and journey.
This class gives a great overview of the Science of the Human Past. Professor McCormick is a passionate lecturer who breaks down materials to make them approachable to students of all backgrounds. The content is extremely interesting and I appreciate the interdisciplinary approach. It makes you think in a good way.
This is a great choice for a fifth class.
This class is really fun! Prof. McCormick supported a friendly and constructive learning environment, and we had a lot of opportunities to explore the subject hands–on with museum trips. As a biology major, this also class expanded my view of my field as to how it can be applied to global issues, such as understanding the history of disease. Additionally, the presentation assignments were good public speaking practice. This class is a fun and relatively easy way to broaden your knowledge and skill set in almost any field.
Prof. McCormick breathes life into a course that deals with a lot of dead people. Over the course of 10 weeks, I learned about ancient DNA, ice cores, coins, pots, etc. Going to the Arboretum or making clay pots at the Ceramics studio are experiences that I will never ever forget. I saw the concepts and methods that we learned through scientific papers come to life. I cannot overstate how much passion Prof. McCormick and Reed have for the class and how much love they give to the class – it's infectious and it has gotten me so excited about the ways the ancient world permeates how own existence. I can only hope that one day I will be able to teach or pursue a topic or field of study with as much love and curiosity as Prof. McCormick and Reed.
Definitely take this course! Professor McCormick is one of the most enthusiastic people I've ever been taught by, and he wants everyone to enjoy the subject as much as he does.
This class is truly one of a kind. Getting to learn about history from scientific evidence like tree cores and coins has been so enlightening. Professor McCormick cares so deeply about his students and the research he does and it has been a true pleasure hearing about his work
This course is incredible! I learned so much in a low stakes environment. The field trips and final project are so engaging, and both Reed and Professor McCormick are amazing—very kind and engaging. Highly recommend!
This class if very interesting. Take it if you need to fill a divisional distribution requirement. It is also pretty easy, there are no exams and no essays.
Take this class.
10/10 course. This is one of the best courses I've taken in my time at Harvard. Professor McCormick is like a golden retriever, and you can talk to him about literally anything. Reed is one of the best TFs ever and is super approachable. They are Batman and Robin. The course has so many interesting facets, you learn about ancient DNA, ancient Rome, ice cores and weather, pottery, and coins. You get to go to the arboretum and the ceramic studio, and making pottery in the ceramic studio is one of my favorite activities in any course, ever. This course truly is unlike any other and I cannot recommend it enough. The one minor (very minor) downside is that section is mildly pointless, since you talk about articles already discussed in class but that's such a small price to pay. The readings are optional and you don't really need to get the books that are "required." There's like two assignments all semester so it's super easy. Also, make sure to go to the leadership lunches – you get to meet really cool scientists and get free lunch so it's a two–for–one deal.
This is an excellent class. It is a rare gem where you will also learn a ton about the new discoveries we have made at the intersection of history and science (ancient DNA to understand ancient human ancestors/pathogens, methods to evaluate the state of climate throughout the earth’s history, etc.). There are no exams in this class, and the major exams are a scientific poster, speaking assignments (the course instructors give awesome feedback on public speaking that I had never received before), & participation/attendance. You also go on 2 field trips.
