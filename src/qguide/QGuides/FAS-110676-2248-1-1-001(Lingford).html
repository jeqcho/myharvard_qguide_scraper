What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

STEM concentrator here, was taking the course out of interest and to fulfill the divisional distribution requirement. I had a love–hate relationship with the class. On one hand, I really liked learning about animation, and creating a concrete project that taught me many technical skills. On the other hand, the professor was very unhelpful, and made the course often unnecessarily stressful. Here is a list of pros and cons:

Pros:
+ A beginner–friendly creative space where you can have fun and learn several new tools/programs that are applicable to other fields (video editing, game design, etc.)
+ The material is really easy, and you won't be "stuck" on an assignment (unless .. see cons)
+ No writing: As someone who doesn't like writing papers, this was a perfect class to balance my schedule with my pset courses without having to write! I really enjoyed editing audio, animating clay models, making puppets and learning Blender.

Cons:
– The professor dislikes digital animation (Blender), and offers zero help to those who choose to use it for their final project. There's a helpful Extension School lecturer who might visit once during the semester, and that's literally all the help/teaching that you will get.
– Can be incredibly time–consuming (8–10 hours per week on average, more than that while working on the final project). The work could be truly fun though, and you'll get out of it as much as you put in.
– Once you get to the final project, the professor will only have criticism to offer. The TF, Michael, is nice and tries to help but Lingford will always have generic criticism (that doesn't actually tell you how to improve) and little advice.

Overall, I recommend this course with caution. Know what you want to get out of the course (learn Blender/After Effects/Adobe Premier Pro/etc., get the requirement out of the way, have fun) and decide how you're going to use your time accordingly. Start thinking about your final project as early as possible, and be very careful in assessing whether you'll have the time/skills to implement your ideas. Have a backup plan in case you get stuck, because there's no one to help you, and don't let the professor discourage you.
This course is rewarding if you give it your time. It's also better if you let yourself experiment.
Good class to take if you are interested in animation, but does take a significant time commitment towards the end for the final project. However, the teachers and TFs are always willing to help and give advice, and it's really satisfying to see it all come together.
I wholeheartedly recommend this class to anyone with an interest in animation. As someone with a STEM background that doesn't consider themselves very artistic, I took the class on a whim and it ended up being one of my favorite classes at Harvard. You get a great introduction into a lot of different mediums of animation (drawing, stop–motion, digital) and then have the opportunity to choose your favoriteor do something entirely newfor your final project. It definitely might take more time out of your schedule than you bargain for, but as with most artistic endeavors that require a lot of time and effort, it's 100% worth it.
If you are at all interested in animation, I
HIGHLY recommend you take this class! Its a great introduction to the basic types of animation and Ruth is really great at giving feedback while also letting you stick to your own vision of what you want to do.
