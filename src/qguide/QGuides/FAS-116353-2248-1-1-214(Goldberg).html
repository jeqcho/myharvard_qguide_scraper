What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
What you put in this course is what you will get out of it. Doing the readings and coming to class ready to discuss will make writing the essays easier. Don't be afraid to ask Questions to Dr. Goldberg or discuss things that you find difficult because his answer will likely improve your essay. Go to the writing center!
I think this class does a really good job of deconstructing the 5 paragraph essay style taught in high school. I came out of the class feeling more confident about my writing abilities for future classes. The professor is very helpful and willing to talk through any questions and concerns, and his feedback on essay drafts are very constructive, positive, and helpful. The readings are really interesting, but do get longer as the course goes on. The selection of texts and films to write about are also very diverse and you can write about almost any topic that matters to you. It does take a decent amount of time and effort to get an A on a paper, but the professor is very understanding and willing to help.
Be prepared to read!
I think this is one of the better Expos options out there. The content is pretty interesting, you have a lot of freedom on what to write about, and the readings are not as long as what I've heard about other Expos classes. In addition, Dr. Goldberg is a really nice preceptor. He really wants all of his students to succeed and puts in a lot of effort towards that end. As a STEM concentrator coming from a mediocre writing background (hadn't done much beyond the 5 paragraph essay in high school), I found this class difficult, especially at the beginning, but I grew in my writing a lot through that initial struggle and confusion. The biggest piece of advice I have for future students is: GO TO THE WRITING CENTER. I went before I started writing my first essay to get help with outlining it and that 1 hour session really helped me understand what was expected of me in this class and how to meet those expectations in my essays. This made the second and third essays a bit easier. Dr. Goldberg's feedback, his Office Hours , and librarian hours at the library are also super helpful so definitely take advantage of all these resources here to help you!
There are icebreakers at the beginning of each class that are fun, but waste some time. You don't have to do the readings thoroughly– just skim them very lightly to get the general gist.
Using professor Goldberg's office hours are very helpful!
I would highly recommend this class to anyone interested in the course material and willing to improve their writing skills. I benefited greatly from the instructor and my writing is stronger because of it. The material is no cakewalk, but it is all manageable, fascinating, and relevant to our world today. The process of writing for this course is helpful, starting with a draft and then getting meaningful feedback and conferences with the instructor. Then, you turn in a revision a week after receiving the draft back. The grading rubric is very fair, and I was never disappointed with the feedback on any of my essays. It only helped me grow. This is an amazing writing course with an amazing teacher and interesting readings!
I would tell them to take it because it is a very interesting course that helps students prepare for future writing. It is probably one of the most interesting expos classes. The essays were not long, the readings are not heavy at all. Definetly take this expos because it taught me a lot about interesting things.
The course itself is extremely useful for developing new techniques in writing. The feedbacks given by Perceptor Goldberg were always very thorough and well thought out. He always makes himself available for questions even outside of office hours so he does a very well job trying to support students in their writing process. My only complaint would be the short turn–around time between the previous essay and the next essay because the time we receive the previous essay we typically only have a few days to finish the second one. Though, I would say is arguably understandable because the feedback provided was extremely thorough and useful for the next essay.
I would tell future students this class talks about some perhaps difficult sexual and race topics that can be hard to talk about.
Expos is not going to be easy, regardless of the class you choose. If you embrace the challenges, however, then you'll soon find that writing is an incredibly rewarding process, and if you take this class, I can assure you that you will not regret it. :)
