What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
1) Go to as many office hours and MQC as you can and need for this class. And if you need extra help, like ARC peer tutoring or the math coaching program, join it! There is no medal for suffering your way through the class (which I did in the beginning for sure) without understanding what you're doing. It's not shameful to ask for help, so get as much help as you can! I will personally recommend the math coaching program so so highly.

2) If you take the 9am section like me, make sure you set several alarms. And make sure that you know exactly how long it takes you to get out of bed, get ready to whatever level you intend to (whether that's a shower, getting dressed, brushing your teeth, etc.), and get to the class from your dorm. Wake up with enough time to do that and give yourself 5 extra minutes for any possible delays. No need to get up earlier than that though – try to sleep as much as you can!

3) Participate in all the sections at the board. For one thing, it affects your participation grade, which you can try to get as close to 100% on since that is an easy thing to help boost your grade in the class. If you're sick, get a doctor's note or they will absolutely mark down your participation even if they see that you're sick. Aside from that though, your really won't get much out of the class if you don't actively participate and contribute to the group work we do at the boards – it's really where you learn in the section!
Yes it's 3 psets a week and somewhat of a time commitment, but all in all, it's a great course. As long as you go to MQC and do your psets on a consistent schedule, it will be fine. There is also a good community in MQC and grading is super fair.
I would tell them to ask without hesitation anytime when they are confused
Overall, I really enjoyed Math 1b. There are a lot of problem sets. The best way to manage the high volume of homework is to do the problem sets the day they are assigned because the lecture will be fresh in your mind and MQC is far less busy when a problem set isn't due the next day.
I would tell them to work hard and do the psets seriously.
If you are deciding between this and a higher level math course, take the higher level math course. The grading is harsh and there is no curve.
