Just make sure you understand that your term paper affects your grade a LOT
Professor Losick was a great lecturer and is very clearly passionate about what he does. It's also super interesting since he was alive for a lot of the major breakthroughs in biology and knew quite a few influential people, so it was fun hearing about the gossip. The quizzes can be tough sometimes since the historic papers can be dense and hard to understand. Reading the ebook helped. The term paper is fine as long as you start early, but it can be frustrating at times when the only guidance you have is that it should be about the life and science of an influential biologist (and not much other guidance). But the TFs and Professor Losick are very approachable so feel free to reach out to them if you have questions.
Amazing course! Great professors! Great and fun lectures
TAKE THIS CLASS. It is such an interesting dive into the field of molecular biology, and I come away from it knowing much more about the contributions of many scientists, the politics behind publications and awards, and the way the research method has changed over the years. Professor Losick is my favorite professor I've had at Harvard (and I am a senior) –– he cares so much about each student, and his passion for the subject is evident. The class has a light workload (1–2 scientific articles and a very short quiz before each class) and culminates in a 10–20 page term paper. The term paper was a very engaging way for me to dive into a scientist and discovery we hadn't talked about, and I am glad I started early.
This course will expose you to some of the most important discoveries in the history of molecular biology, perhaps in the history of science in general, and Prof. Losick is a wonderful storyteller. The class and section discussions were stimulating, and the final term paper was actually a lot of fun to write, which is something that I do not often say about a class. The quizzes before each class were challenging enough to ensure that we understood the papers that we were reading, but not challenging to the point of being overwhelming, which I appreciated. Overall, MCB 149 was an excellent course, and I would recommend it in a heartbeat.
