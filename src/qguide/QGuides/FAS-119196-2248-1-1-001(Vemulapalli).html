Take advantage of the MQC and all the office hours offered. They are incredibly helpful resources, and all of the CA's in the class are more than willing to help you a understand a concept.

The class does present a lot of work, but it is completely manageable if you stay on top of learning the course content outside of class. Prepare in advance for exams.

I recommend keeping a notebook with the key takeaways from each lecture that will help you review for exams, or review after the end of each week so you don't have to extensively review each lecture before midterms or the final.
I was often confused during section, but my course CA's were always helpful and provided useful clarification. Going to MQC is a great resource for succeeding in this course
Make sure you have enough time to devote to this class. It teaches you a lot about multi and you get a really good understanding about it, but it really is a time consumer. You have 3 psets a week and each one requires a considerable amount of time and effort. So, just make sure with your other classes that you're able to handle it. Also, go to MQC (math question center) for your psets and general questions! It's super helpful.
This course will make you spend a lot of time on psets every week but it is a very easy course since you barely need any extra learning beyond that and it really helps you really understand all concepts of Multi over time, through the non–stop exposure to. Resources for this class are very good, and you will have fun if you enjoy calculus. Recommend the course! Absolutely essential for all STEM majors in my opinion.
DO YOUR PROBLEM SETS!!! Most of the people who walk out of the exam hall feeling terrible are also the people who do not complete their homeworks diligently. The psets will give you everything you need to know, put in the necessary work there.
took as freshman, not too bad, know yourself and whether you are going to wake up for 9am math class 3 times a week, 3 psets per week nearly killed me and it became my entire life but you have a lot of opportunities to gain pset drops, going to mqc helped a lot whenever I had time but ended up just coping towards the end of the year, final was insane
Great class! definitely recommend this over 22 series if you are not comfortable with proofs – this is a direct application of math, and definitely gets more comfortable as the course progresses
This class becomes very manageable if you are willing to make an honest effort to spend time on the material. MQC and office hours are wonderful resources to review concepts, answer questions, and work on problem sets with classmates. Problem sets and exams are fair, and the class is well–balanced.
You will learn a lot in this class, and you will be a better mathematician because of it. There are a lot of problem sets, and each one takes some time, but if you dedicate the time, the problem sets are very valuable for preparing you for exams in this class. The teaching staff is great too!
I felt like I wanted to take this class just to take the next "level" of math after AP Calc BC. Unless you are doing something quantitative that requires this course, the 3 psets a week will take up SO much of your free time, so I would not recommend this course to anyone who is just taking the "next" level of math just to take it. Although I learned a TON through this course, the section component is largely disorganized. I am someone who learns best through lecture format; every single day we would learn the concept (15 ish minutes) and then we would be given a packet to figure out ourselves. The CA to student ratio was honestly very difficult to facilitate learning; if it werent for my table group, I for sure would have not done well on the psets. The first midterm is easier than the practice exams, the second midterm is harder, and the final exam is honestly a toss up– I studied for 3–4 days prior and felt like I knew how to do everything, but only because I studied. For all of the exams, look at the solutions from the in–class worksheets to prepare yourself. MQC is CRAZY (people will literally be lined up for CA help and then hog the CA to get answers out of them) the day before a pset is due, so always go earlier. Take 21a over 22b if you are not doing anything theoretical concentration–wise.
Do not take this course if you don't already have a pretty good understanding of calculus and preferably some on multivariable calculus as well. This is a very fast–paced course and the professors are not the best at teaching. be prepared to spend lots of time on the 3 psets a week and also GO TO MQC!!! Literally MQC saved my life, use it well.
