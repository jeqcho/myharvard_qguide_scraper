If you are interested in learning about the Hebrew Bible, this is a great introduction. Professor Teeter focuses a lot on the structure of the books (which some people might find to not be the most engaging), but I found it to be a really great introduction to the Book's contents.
If you are at all interested in the Hebrew Bible / Old Testament, please take this class. Prof. Teeter is so knowledgeable and is so skilled at drawing out the rich tapestry or literary art and meaning in these texts. He is a pioneer in his approach to reading the Hebrew Bible, and it is a privilege to learn from him. You should consider taking this course even if you have already learned the Old Testament in a personal religious context. As an evangelical, I grew in my faith and appreciation for God's Word through this text. You may not agree with everything Prof. Teeter or your classmates say (you will agree with a lot of it though!), and it will be time well spent learning about and engaging with these ideas.
Take this class!! Prof. Teeter is amazing and the content is fantastic. Best course at Harvard
Prof. Teeter will help you understand and appreciate the crazy complexities of the Hebrew Bible. Everything has so many more layers than you would have thought, and the intertextual and intratextual connections, literary allusions, literary techniques are mindblowing. Even if you are not Jewish or Christian, you will come away with the class with, at the very least, an appreciation of the Hebrew literary tradition and the moral depth of the texts. You can see history being played out in the texts as writers wrestle with the social and political problems of their time, and how they were related to religious understandings.
The lectures do sometimes beat a dead horse though; there is a lot of discussion in respect to how the text is written and how it is inviting you to think about themes, but this often becomes redundant, as texts often employ the same technique again and again. I wished we could spend more time analyzing specific verses or chapters or actually discussing the intended moral and political meanings of the text.
It is a bit frustrating that the lectures do not engage with the secondary readings, but those readings are the core material for the exams. The TF does an excellent job in the optional review sessions (attend those!) in ensuring you are prepared for the exams.
Take the class if interested in Hebrew Bible beyond source criticism.
