What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
230 has some really interesting material and could be enjoyed by somebody who puts the time in. Lectures are three hours long on fridays and are entirely chalk talks with some pretty in depth derivations. Problem sets are very bashy but sometimes interesting and are always quite long. Final projects are fun.
The goal of active matter is to find emergent behavior in systems with external energy input, kind of like temperature as an emergent property of quantum mechanical systems with lots of particles. The interesting part is how you handle external energy inputs, which takes the problem away from traditional stat mech and leads to interesting SDEs, fluid mechanics, and high (4th, often) order nonlinear PDEs. The problem sets are essentially linear stability analysis over and over on different biologically–relevant systems, which can be tedious but worth doing if your research is on these higher order equations. You get out of this course what you put in; you might learn some rote mathematics if you only do problem sets, but the lectures are necessary to really understand why the problems matter.
