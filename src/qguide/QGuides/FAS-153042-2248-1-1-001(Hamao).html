This course is really fun if you like to play chamber music. The coaching I had was positive and helpful. The course atmosphere is friendly.

I feel like the average ability level in this course is extremely high, but coaches adjust their expectations based on where you start, so don't be intimidated.

Word of warning to pianists, be realistic with yourself about how much time you can dedicate to this course. Look urself dead in the eyes in the mirror at the beginning of the semeseter and have a heart to heart. I overestimated my abilities and I spent so so so much time practicing this semester. I spent more time practicing than studying for any other class I took, organic chemistry included.
If you are musician/instrumentalist passionate about chamber music, create a group with your friends and take this course! The Parker Quartet and Dr. Chung are truly amazing instructors, and you will learn a lot about ensemble playing!
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Harvard is incredibly lucky to have the Parkers on faculty. Make use of them!!! They can meet everyone where they are at.
Even if you are not coming into this class with a typical string ensemble, take this course! The coaches really meet you where you are and this is one of a kind.
Take it! It is an awesome class that is more of a light extracurricular than a class. If you can take it for a letter grade you should 100% do it because it is a super fun GPA booster. Also, definitely take it as a 5th or 6th class, it's only about 2 hours a week. Freshman can take it as a 5th first semester, you just have to petition.
This is an excellent course to take if you enjoy music making and playing chamber with your friends! Level of music making is honestly as good as you can make it, even matching Juilliard levels!
The best course at Harvard! The Parker Quartet and Dr. Chung are fantastic instructors, and having an opportunity to work with them while playing chamber music with your peers is truly special!
TAKE THIS CLASS!
Kee is a fantastic coach. I love the new format of the class where we perform 3 times in workshops. Mia is a FANTASTIC chamber piano coach.
The coaches are great at working with the group as a whole as opposed to isolating one person's particular flaws, which I think is really helpful. Can be a difficult experience if you don't get along well with your group–mates, so put some thought into selection.
Classical musicians looking to play great rep in a formal setting, especially if you want to play with friendstake this class! Such a great opportunity to be mentored by amazing musicians (Parker Quartet is wonderful, Mia Chung is actually just so great), and it's really fun to do music seriously with other people who will take it just as seriously as you. It's certainly a time investment (esp. if you're a piano player), but I think super worth it. There's a reason why people repeatedly do 189R.
Incredible class. The Parker Quartet is amazing and it is so cool that we have the opportunity to study with them. The group matters so much, so choose carefully.
Music 189R is fantastic. Simply put, it was the most I've grown as an instrumental musician in a long time. Dr. Chung is the most wonderful coach we could have imagined. Make sure that you are clear about how much effort you want to put in and communicate that with your group. If you choose a really hard piece, it is going to require a LOT of work and practice. But that's really rewarding! Just make sure you're conscious of how you plan your semester. Highly recommend this class.
This class, as always, is a really fun to take; essentially, it is a way to play chamber music with friends and receive letter graded credit for it. Keep in mind, however, that, compared to prior years, the Parker Quartet has tried to make the course a bit more time–consuming. Masterclasses are now three times a semester, and there are also some other mandatory evening lectures scattered throughout. Though this may not sound like a lot, it adds up, and it definitely requires attention as they are easy to forget about. However, this is a small detail, and, overall, I highly recommend this class.
