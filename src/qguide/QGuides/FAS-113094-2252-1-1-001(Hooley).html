The person teaching my section, Jada, was an absolutely wonderful TF and a great person in general. I absolutely appreciated all of the diversity in the materials she included in the syllabus, as well as how she centered some discussions around her own work. The work she does on stigma is also very related to my interests and I will actually be working with her this summer!
I mean if you're taking this course you kinda have to. Your tutorial leader will definitely make or break your experience but it is very manageable with other course work as you spend the whole semester writing the paper and there is a ton of support

Lexi was great! Each week consisted of reading 3 articles with discussions and activities. We wrote the final paper in chunks over the semester, which was helpful. Very interesting topics and well taught!
A great tutorial for sophomores interested in the concentration! You will read a bunch of papers and engage in discussions about them–– pretty relaxed. I would say taking PSY 1901 before this one makes it MUCH easier to handle, as you have already written a real research paper before and are familiar with methods, making critiquing papers and dealing with the final assignment in 971 much easier.
The difficulty of this class completely depends on who you get for your tutor. I got a very nice very chill very sweet tutor who gave really good feedback on assignments and also graded very nicely so I got an A without a tremendous amount of effort. However, some people definitely have a harder time with it depending on their tutor. And you lowkey don't get to decide your tutor, so – good luck.
I loved the small class size, it really made me feel like I had an intimate connection with all my classmates as well as my instructor
Jordan is the best!!! Do not worry about the large final paper, you have a bunch of time and support leading up to it. Do the readings each week so you can have a productive conversation. Get to know the people in your section. For a required class, I loved this one (especially due to the small group format).
This class was a grind but it was very rewarding. If you asked me midway through the semester I would’ve probably not given super positive feedback, but having reached the end of this course I would say it was super beneficial overall and worth the time commitment.

My instructor (Jordan) was amazing which helped too. I really think my writing developed so much from this class.
