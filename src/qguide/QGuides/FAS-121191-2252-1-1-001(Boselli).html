For PIEs, I would suggest doing the following: read the lecture notes. They are wonderful. Don't waste your time rewatching the lecture videos –– the lecture notes contain nicely written out details of all you need to know. Once you've read the lecture notes, read any following tutorials. Then try a practice PIE without any notes. It doesn't have to be time–limited necessarily –– just force yourself to stare at the problem until you can figure it out. The struggle is worth your brain learning how to solve the problem. Then watch the review session video (I always skipped that class but if you went it's ok). The most important thing is to hammer out the practice problems. They are AMAZING. I didn't realize how similar they are to PIE questions until it was too late. If you don't have time to do them without looking at the key, it helps to just download a PDF of the key and annotate the solutions, making sure you know how to do them. Once you feel confident, take the second practice PIE timed, no materials. Then after grading your PIE, consolidate what you NEED to have on a sheet and make your sheet last. Ideally you don't need the sheet –– it's a safety mechanism because Ls1b is not TOO concerned with little random facts/details.

I think if you have the outlined study regiment as listed above, you'll be fine. Of course, attend section. Attending lecture is not super necessary in my opinion as long as you read the lecture notes. Definitely try to solve the PSETs on your own. This class is definitely doable.
