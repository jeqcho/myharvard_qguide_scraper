I would highly recommend this course, Professor Halford is amazing and such a kind professor. This is a really fun course that gets you to understand why people believe in conspiracy theories and how these communities function. Professor Halford and Nefara had a clear organization for this course and every lecture felt like something new was being learned. I didn't know much about conspiracy theories but I feel like I learned so much from this class! It really got me thinking about how society thinks about conspiracy theories and people who believe in conspiracy theories. There are only three unit papers which are pretty manageable and graded fairly. Take this class!!! Dr. Halford is a sweetheart and is excellent at teaching the subject matter. I came away from this course with so much new knowledge, and I'd recommend it to anyone. The readings are manageable (because they're interesting!!) and the Unit Papers are actually engaging.
This is an amazing class that teaches you a lot about how conspiracy theories form, why we are where we are today, and some of the things we need to pay attention to as we think about the future. Especially given the political climate we were in while this course was taking place, I felt like we had so many real world examples to draw from and critically think about. This class pushed me to think harder, not be so quick to dismiss the people even if I dismiss the conspiracy theories, and allowed me to learn a lot about how to employ the sociology of culture. You don't need any sort of a background in sociology to learn a ton in this class, and professor Halford does an amazing job at ensuring that it is accessible to everyone. She is kind, supportive, flexible, incredibly knowledgeable about the course content, and it is obvious she loves what she does. If you need any accommodations of any kind, you won't have to worry about them not being met, because professor Halford pays attention to the little things, and works hard to stay on top of anything that needs to be done to ensure everyone is successful in the course. you simply won't get as much out of the course without doing the readings each week, and there are short weekly responses that are required. However, the reading load is completely doable and interesting. There are also three unit papers, all of which are 4–6 pages, and those are due about a month apart each, with plenty of support from the teaching staff if you reach out for the help. There is also a final exam, which serves as a brief overview for the course, but is more than doable, especially if you have been paying attention in class and have kept up with the readings. The lectures are engaging, and professor Halford uses a mix of teaching methods that keeps everyone engaged. Definitely take the course if you have any interest in learning about conspiracy theories, especially with our current political climate!
