I think it's a great class. By virtue of its requirement, expos, it can be very annoying at times. You will have a lot of random work, e.g. reading the AI module, but this is again typical of an expos class. The one thing I do want to warn about this class is that you do not actually learn that much about the theories. You learn more about their applications and spend most of your class trying to apply them, but you pretty much only vaguely learn about persona. Professor Strauss is a very nice instructor, and she is very accommodating to our schedules. The only thing is that sometimes she changes her mind between reads, so it can be hard to know what the problem with an essay is. You will also have one week in the semester where you watch 2 2–hour films, which can be a lot (this is true of Spring, I'm not sure about fall). Overall, I think this is not one of the easiest expos, but it can be interesting if you want to take a try at exploring films.
This class is a wonderful way to develop as writer. It will change you and the way you view film and writing for the better. All assignments are manageable and designed to help you.
Professor Strauss is wonderful! She gives great feedback on essays and is very kind/understanding. Coming from someone who hated writing in high school, I actually enjoyed Expos and found most of the course material interesting. Take this expos section!!
TAKE THIS CLASS! It is incredibly interesting, professor strauss is so kind and empathetic. She really emphasizes clarity of writing, which is something that students will learn and grow on throughout the course. The capstone was really fun to do and decreases the amount of workload of 3 essays, enabling more creativity through different mediums.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Gem, and much less busy work compared to other Expos.
Strauss is a lovely human and great teacher. Overall, this is a nice expos course, and you will learn how to analyze movies and connect them with other topics and mediums. She grades very fairly, and gives you alot of good comments after your first draft. If you take her comments seriously, you will be rewarded with a good grade. I also really did enjoy the final capstone video, which felt way more exciting and less intensive than a third essay
