Most definitely take this course! Sweeney is an amazing professor, you will learn tons, and the workload is entirely reasonable!
Assignments were a bit lengthy and somewhat repetitive, but they tied back to activities/ lessons the Professor teaches.
If you have the space in your schedule and will engage with the course, just take it. I have been around Harvard on and off for around 6 years and this course is easily in my top five experiences.
The content is somewhat interesting but not life–changing in anyway. Professor Sweeney is very passionate about the topic so it does make each class very engaging. As the course goes on there seems to be a lot of busy work given to us, especially with the in–class activities so it often feels like a high school class. For example, I remember crafting a harmonizer statement at least 3 times in class to the point where it felt annoying, repetitive, and pointless. But I will say the mock Senate hearing and one of the students presenting their startUp technology were very interesting and I heard the grad school version of the class facilitates better discussions. So TLDR: while the course load may have some busy work here and there it is manageable and Prof Sweeney is such a sweetheart and is a literal genius in the tech world so it'll make for some interesting convos.
***I enjoyed this course a lot. Prof. Sweeney is an absolute pioneer in computer science and in fields like privacy, algorithmic fairness, etc. She has really cool stories involving all sorts of technology, policy, and societal decisions that really grounds the discussion and are quite interesting. You don't need to be a technical / CS student to take and contribute to this course, and it really values everyday experiences and frustrations with technology as starting points for issues.

On GOV 1433 vs. 1430: this course is a prequel to the spring one. It builds up our knowledge and understanding of tech–society clashes, culminating in an experimental proposal (that you don't). However, in the spring you actually implement experiments, so if you're more hands–on, you might appreciate that more — I haven't taken it yet though but will.
professor Sweeney is a really experienced and knowledgeable professor who has great stories to share with the class about her experiences in the field. However, sometimes the course feels a bit repetitive in terms of the content and activities. There are many in–class activities so it is nice to have friends in the class
Professor Sweeney is excellent and really cares about each one of her students. I enjoyed the class discussions and learned a lot about how cookies and SSNs work. However, the grading in this class is randomly extremely difficult and unclear.
I recommend taking this course!!! I really enjoyed learning from Professor Sweeney and she has a lot of life experience that could not be more relevant to the course. It is not a lot of work, there are primarily 4 busy weeks. There are 4 units and within each of those, there are a few days that things are do. Outside of those few days, the course is chill. No prior background knowledge on anything is needed – I took it for my Gov concentration, but also because I love talking about technology. It is extremely timely (we talked a lot about issues with AI) and I definitely recommend this course!
The content of this class is wonderful, but be prepared for many busy work in class activities that waste your time. Additionally, most students ChatGPT all of their assignments, so be aware of this as well.
