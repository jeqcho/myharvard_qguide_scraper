Cookies. Chinese takeaways. Prof. Strominger. Kip Thorne. This seminar is an awesome opportunity and I would highly recommend for anyone interested in Physics and Black Holes. Courseload each week is light and all physics concepts are fascinating. Its an incredible opportunity to be taught by Prof. Strominger and I'm very grateful I took this course.
Definitely take it. It's a class that is super forgiving.
This was by far my favorite class of freshman year. If astrophysics is something you are at all interested in, I would absolutely encourage you to take this class. The readings were super interesting and the lectures were fantastic (plus there's insomnia cookies!). Prof. Strominger does an amazing job introducing the basics of astrophysics. You definitely don't need any experience coming into the class. I will say that the more you put into the class, the more you will get out of it. The readings are a really great resource and I would recommend doing them if you want to leave the class with a good grasp of the material!
Learn about the lecturers early on so that you spend your time around them wisely.
Professor Strominger is so kind, lovely, highly intelligent, and still makes time to bring us Insomnia cookies every class, or go out to dinner for classroom to table. He really works to create a community and an interactive course with trips to the Black Hole Initiative or Interstellar screenings. That being said, I did not have a strong basic understanding of theoretical physics and I often felt like many of the course concepts completely flew over my head. He's such an intelligent, highly–advanced physicist, that sometimes he may forget the limited knowledge of us measly first–years. Thus, take this class if you're really interested in theoretical physics and plan to be a physics major –– and you'll get a fantastic mentor out of it.
Professor Strominger is one of the most brilliant scientists in theoretical physics today, and his willingness to explain, answer questions about, and share his love for physics, black holes, and studying the universe makes this seminar a truly unique opportunity. Every week, you’ll get to discuss various aspects of the universe, modern physics, and astrophysics over cookies and milk. Professor Strominger’s experience and renown also means that he gets very cool guest speakers to come in (including a Nobel Prize winner this year) and that he’ll share anecdotes of working with people like Stephen Hawking! Though the readings are long and sometimes dense, try to at least skim them before class and always be thinking of questions to ask. This seminar is not limited to those interested in pursuing physics– regardless of your intended concentration, don’t rule it out! I highly recommend taking this seminar for an insight into what it’s like to pursue theoretical physics or just a glimpse into the incredibly fascinating mysteries of our universe.
This class has taught me so much about black holes, theories of the universe (string theory included!), and physics overall. Lectures and discussions in class are captivating and make time fly by. Classes with Professor Strominger are highly engaging and wholesome, and the movies we watch and their corresponding speakers add so much to the class. Although the readings are lengthy and by no means easy, they are rewarding and build such a neat foundation for the field. Likewise, the texts teach you a lot about the history of physics and lead into important problems and theories of today. This class is great for anyone with an interest in the universe and a will to take on the challenge of understanding various theories and conflicts in the field. (Note: although I am not a physics major, I came in with a good foundation in math and STEM as a whole.)
Please take this class. There are a lot of readings to get through but in overall you will come out of the class well knowledgable. Professor Strominger is also the best. We also got to meet and talk with other renowned scientist including Nobel prize winner, Kip Thorne.
