What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is a must–take for anybody who will be reliant on crystallography for their current or future research. That being said, it is not a pleasant class and requires substantially more work than seems necessary to learn the important material. It is possible to learn a lot and pretty much be prepared to become a DIY user of the x–ray facility, however Shao is extremely harsh and frequently openly criticizes individual students, which makes for an unpleasant learning environment. Many assignments must be completed on a weekly basis with little guidance, though very little of it contributes to your grade and assignment of numerical grades seems very subjective or arbitrary.
I don't recommend taking this class until an extreme overhaul is done. The content is valuable but the quality of the teaching is frustrating and honestly subpar. If you are interested in learning about crystallography, you would be better off reading the textbooks assigned on the syllabus (because that's honestly the only place I learned anything anyway) or waiting until another instructor picks this course up/until you can take it at another institution.
