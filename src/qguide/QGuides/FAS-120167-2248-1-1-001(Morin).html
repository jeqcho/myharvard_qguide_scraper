Take this class if you're a mech E looking for an advanced science course. Prof. Morin is a great lecturer and his enthusiasm for the material really comes through when he teaches. Some of the material is a bit dry, but luckily the topics change every lecture so it's not too painful for too long. PSets were straightforward, especially if you go to section. We got to learn about some cool topics! I really liked this class.
TLDR; If you need an easier physics course to satisfy your physics requirements, take it (it's better than, say, Physics 181). Otherwise, I'm not sure I'd recommend. It's a cool course; you get to cover so many topics, but as Morin says himself, you don't go in–depth into any of them, which can be a bit frustrating. I felt like I was always trying to play catch–up, and as soon as I understood the material and got excited to go deeper, we moved on. Also, I was NOT a fan of the exams; they basically force you to memorize all these little tricks to solve specific types of problems, rather than really focusing on physics. And there's a TON of actual numbers, which many physicists (myself included) are not a fan of. It's still and easy and interesting course, though. So like I said, I'd take it if you need a Physics requirement filled. But if you are looking for a class to learn cool theory, look elsewhere.
This is an Engineer's physics class. Problems are applicable to real life scenarios and are very interesting. I took this for my Mechanical Engineering advanced science requirement and was very happy that I did.
If you need another science or physics class, you should take this one! It was so interesting, and the teaching staff is so wonderful. The topics were interesting and diverse, and the exams are very doable if you study. I missed multiple weeks of class at the beginning of the semester, and the teaching staff was so helpful and understanding.
I love David Morin!!! Super cool content, reasonable exams, reasonable psets. I wish there were some demos bc there were points I think in lecture it felt a bit less motivated but overall good course.
While I'd overall recommend the class for being interesting and low–pressure, I do have two reservations. First, I think the grading, especially on the exams, was unusually harsh. Second, it often felt like we were learning Professor Morin's trick for solving a very specific physics problem instead of actually studying a topic. If you're a jaded senior who just needs to finish their requirements, I'd take it; we learned some genuinely cool stuff. But, if you'd actually like to learn useful physics, maybe hold back.
This is a great class! It is very interesting and not overly difficult. Psets and exams are fair. The only difficult part is that we go over a lot of topics in relatively little depth just by the nature of the course, so remembering everything for the exam can be a bit of work.
Fantastic class, as expected of Morin. The topics are all pretty interesting and relevant, and dont take an incredible amount of physics background to grasp (relativity was probably the only part of the class that required background beyond AP physics). Psets are interesting, exams are easy to study for because they are very similar to the homeworks, and the project we work on at the end is pretty fun and not very stressful. Highly recommend this class!

However, please note this will probably take just a little less time than 15A, and still requires a substantial amount of time each week; block out a few days of just studying the homework problems for the exams. Grading is similar to Prof Morin's other classes. Overall, great class!
A great look into a mix of interesting physics phenomena!

For MechE, good advanced science class to take. It's not easy, but definitely more interesting and possibly applicable than other courses in that requirement.

This class is amazing and you should definitely take it. Professor Morin is great and does a great job of making this class fun and interesting. The class covers a WIDE range of topics, but each one is covered in just enough detail to where you can tell your friends about it and they'll think you're a genius. The problem sets aren't very difficult and each problem feels relevant and almost always has some kind of interesting "aha!" moment where everything comes together into a pretty elegant answer. Every engineer should take this course. Physics 125 is probably the best course of my STEM career. No matter what discipline you are, at some point in your career, you will work on an interdisciplinary team, and having a high–level understanding of the wide world of physics is so incredibly helpful. This course will put all of your previous physics work into perspective such that you may effectively capitalize on all the material you have already learned before. If anything, simply having the course's typed lecture notes is so helpful that whenever you need to look into a new topic vaguely related to physics, you will already have a primer on the material to start from.
