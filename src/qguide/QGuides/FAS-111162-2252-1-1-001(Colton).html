This is just not a good course to be taking. The lectures are three hours straight during lunch time, with only a very short break. The professor quite literally reads the entire time. I remember him saying once: "This is obviously very bad for Russia." Then, he looked down at his notes. Looked back up. "Sorry I said here, this is extremely bad for Russia." Not only does he not put any effort into the delivery of the class, the content itself is not designed for an undergraduate class. Sometimes, he would put the photographs of 10 influential people in XYZ affair and then describe all their lives in excrutiating detail. In my notes, somewhere buried, I know where the mom of some business tycoon grew up. This is the kind of information that makes up this class – mostly trivial. And even when it wasn't trivial, but quite interesting in fact, there was often no thesis/point the professor wanted to make. So the class was essentially a three–hour long amalgamation of details.

Now, I need to also acknowledge that the readings for this class were AMAZING and I learned so much about Russia through them, and section. I really feel better equipped to discuss Russian politics. So, if you want to learn everything there is to know about Russia, do most of the work piecing things together yourself and do not mind boring lectures, this is the class for you. If you do all the readings (of which there are an insane amount), I guarantee this will be a positive experience. But you could be taking so many other classes at Harvard that you could be absolutely loving instead of this one.
This class is great, super informative, and honestly really interesting if you spend the time engaging in lecture and the reading. There aren't that many opportunities to prove your knowledge outside of section and 2 exams though, so keep that in mind. Prof. Colton is a fantastic lecturer, however, lectures are 2h45min and pretty dense, so be prepared for that.
The readings can be overwhelming (there are so many more than might be considered reasonable for many weekly assignments). However, my experience with tests was that they did not completely hinge on doing the readings, although that depends on who is the Section Head that writes your tests. The content is very interesting though.
Take this course if you have some background knowledge of Russia (both political and historical), this course claims to be beginner–friendly friendly but you should have some working knowledge (the course goes quite in–depth!). Also, consider the reading list and how this fits into the rest of your coursework. Professor Colton and Ms. Boriana are amazing individuals with a lot of knowledge, but check the syllabus and make sure you have the time to commit to this class.
This is a class with so many readings and so much to learn that it almost needs a full year of back–to–back classes. As much as I loved the content, I was not a fan of changing the midterm format the week before the midterm was given, when the syllabus said something completely different, and I wish I had taken this class later in my Harvard journey. I don't do well with in–class timed essays (Just not my favorite), and they switched to that after the syllabus said that wouldn't be the midterm.
I would recommend taking this class, even if you have little or minimal interest in Russia. I only took this class to fulfill my secondary requirement (and had 0 knowledge of Russia going in) and was able to learn a lot and genuinely engage with the course material. The biggest issue is the amount of readings — it can be impossible to do all of them some weeks.
Take it, genuinely the best gov class I have taken at Harvard. Prof Colton has an insane amount of knowledge on the subject matter.
I would tell future students that this is a great class to take if they are very interested in learning a lot about the region in a short time frame.
you'll get out the amount of effort that you put in – to truly understand the topic, engage with readings throughout the semester
Unless you have a true passion for Russian history and have a strong background knowledge in Russian politics, I would not recommend this course.
Professor Colton is a good lecturer, and genuinely cares about the subject material and his students. Even though the lectures were nearly three hours long, I never felt like I was anxious for them to end (though the five–minute break probably helped). They were full of information, some of it useful, some of it more like trivia (e.g., there were a few slides where Colton would show a bunch of people's pictures and tell you who they were, most of whom never came up again). Boriana was a solid TF; she often relied on students to lead discussion, which sometimes led to little being said when people had neglected the readings. She always sent out an email saying which readings were to be focused on in that week's section, which was very helpful. There were no assignments besides the midterm and final (both of which were open–internet), which was nice, but also made it hard to determine my master of the course content.
Be prepared for long lectures and a lot of reading. Professor Colton and Boriana are very nice, you get what you put into this class. Two assessments so not crazy on exam workload but it is important to keep in mind that your grade depends on these two (with participation).
