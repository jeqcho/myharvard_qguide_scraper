What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a very nice way to get your Gov political theory requirement. Jordan is a very down–to–Earth, thoughtful, and kind presence in the classroom. I think he strikes a good balance between going into more detailed explanations and just letting the conversation flow. Jordan is also very accessible outside of class and good person to get to know. The readings are all very reasonable and mostly quite interesting. It's hard for me to say much more because I am just not a political theory person, but I very much enjoyed the course!
It is a good opportunity to practice your writing skills. I consider it a great first preparation for a senior thesis because the writing assignments allow you to practice the formulation of strong arguments while learning how to interpret authors like Paine, Burke, and Smith. This political theory course has a historical approach, which gives it an interdisciplinary focus. With the Gov94 seminar, I recommend you take one that you are particularly interested in because you are going to spend a lot of time with the reading materials, and you are going to use them for your papers. Therefore, prioritize your preferences and choose one that you like.
Would recommend this course! Jordan is fantastic and really knowledgeable about what he teaches. The list of readings gives a great overview of political thought about the American and French Revolutions (as well as adjacent topics). A solid political theory class — there's only 3 papers and the prompts are all quite broad.
Great class if you are looking to get into foundational political theory authors such as Thomas Paine, Edmund Burke, and Karl Marx, but want a more laid–back vibe than some other classes offered in the Government department. Jordan takes complex texts and distills them succinctly while also maintaining their richness. The other students in the class always had interesting things to say and Jordan did a great job of guiding the discussion. Assignments and grading were also chill. Would recommend for anyone looking to fill their political theory or Gov94 credit!
If you're interested in taking this class, then you probably should! The reading and writing workload is fair, the topics are interesting and quite applicable to modern–day issues, and Jordan knows how to keep conversations on track and engaging. He's also quite available outside of class if you have any questions related to the material or even just slightly adjacent to it.
I highly recommend that students who are interested in political theory take this course. The broader theory landscape within the government department is a wasteland. This course does what a political theory course should do, which is engage directly with the primary texts and authors themselves. You will read some of the most important texts of the time period covered in this course (Paine, Burke, Publius, Maistre, even Marx). Professor Ecker does a great job putting these thinkers in conservation with one another. Do not take this course if you are not interested in political theory, you will ruin the experience for your fellow students. The writing assignments are reasonable in length and wide in scope, giving students the freedom to engage with the course material as they wish.
