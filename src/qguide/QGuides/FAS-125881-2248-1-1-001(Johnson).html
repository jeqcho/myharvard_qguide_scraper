I love this class, best class ever!
Definitely take this course if you're an astro concentrator. The light workload will be a nice break from all your hard physics class and Johnson is a super engaging and passionate lecturer. Much of the class is spent working through worksheets, which might not be the ideal learning style for everyone but I really liked it. The worksheets are really fun and well done and do a good job teaching the material.
very good class! Know how to code though.....
This class is really fun! I'd never taken an astro course before and it's been a couple years since I took 15a, so I felt a bit lost at the beginning, but the course is structured so that you can catch up quickly and painlessly. Most of the course meetings are spent doing worksheets on the board in a group, so you've got to come to class ready to engage and collaborate. We had one sort of heavy python assignment, but other than there wasn't too much work out of class.
I love Professor Johnson's approach to learning! It's all centered around board work, collaboration, and practice. You don't think that you've taken away a lot, but then when you do the midterm or write up a homework assignment, you realize you actually have learned so many concepts about exoplanets. While there were some organizational issues with homeworks and some of them were slightly too difficult, the TFs were amazing and helpful for any questions I had about the material. Overall, you take away learning a lot about the mathematical formulations behind exoplanets (beyond just thinking it's "cool"), and you should have no worries about your grade at all. Go in with the mentality that you're here to learn and it'll be a great experience.
Astro110 is an AWESOME class. Professor Johnson's teaching style is great because after learning the basics, you get split into groups and work on problems together. For someone who is a learn–by–doing type, this was a great way to actually feel solid in the concepts, and also made every class fun and different from the last, not just constant lectures. There is homework on a regular basis, but I honestly found it enjoyable for the most part (except 1 pset that was very very hard). The homework generally was challenging in a good way and never stressed me out at all, never was unreasonable, and never took way too long like some other classes. The midterm consisted of having to teach someone who knows nothing about astrophysics what we were learning in this class. This was the best midterm I have ever had. It was not only really fun, but it actually genuinely helped solidify the concepts, while not having to be stressed out about a sit–down midterm. Having experience in Python will be helpful, but you can learn it on the fly if you need to; just be prepared for the fact that it will be there. Similarly, Astro 16 can be helpful background but def not a requirement, I was fine without it. If you're interested in exoplanets or astrophysics in general, take this class!!
