I absolutely loved my time in this course! My only regret is that I didn't take it sooner. Be sure to budget enough time to read the required readings (and some of the optional readings) in detail, rather than just skimming them; while we review required literature in class, the opportunity to read (and slowly understand) archaeological literature is one of the most enriching components of the course.
Take this course! Professor Warinner is a great lecturer and a great person. I genuinely learned so much from this class. Every lecture was very interesting, and I feel that I walked away from each class with a better understanding of the scientific method, archaeology, and the relationship between biology, chemistry, history, and anthropology. The midterm and the final (where they would give you an example of an archaeological site and you would have to write about methods to analyze it) were also very fair and honestly kind of fun to complete. Also, shoutout to Percy for being a such a kind and knowledgeable TF
This class requires dedication and focus, and students who are willing to engage with the material through discussion will get the most out of it.
