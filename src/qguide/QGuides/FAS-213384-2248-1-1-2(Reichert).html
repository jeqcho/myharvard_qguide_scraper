This course is very easy to disengage from and skate by without doing work, because there isn't a ton of accountability with the readings or to engage in class. If you already know what you want to do for your thesis and are actively planning how to approach your research, this will probably be much more enjoyable and you will appreciate learning methods more.

Otherwise, it can become pretty repetitive just brainstorming hypothetical scenarios of how you would research a given topic ("I would go to X archives" "I would do a statistical analysis using X and Y variables and collect data from Z online publication.") That said, the readings are interesting and the chance to discuss them (and not just their methods) can make it enjoyable too.
Ultimately, this is a required class. You get out of it what you put into it. Go to office hours, discuss ideas with friends, engage with the material and in class. If you are stuck or confused about where your thesis will go, use this class as an exploratory stage to develop some real concrete ideas.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Would recommend doing it in the fall, as the earlier you start thinking about your thesis, the better, especially for research funding deadlines in January and February. Matt is so knowledgeable and has been so helpful this entire process. He tailors material and activities to relate to the interests of the class, so the effort he has put in is very evident. It truly was a joy to take this class.
Go into this class with an open mind and a willingness to test ideas as you work toward your thesis. Take advantages of chances to explore your thoughts and ideas through assignments, class discussions, exploratory workshops, and office hours!
This course is so helpful as you start thinking about the research process ahead of senior theses. Matt is an incredible resource and goes above and beyond to provide support and feedback. He really emphasizes putting thoughts to paper, which provides a helpful low–commitment way to think through what a thesis project could look like in greater detail. Workshops were a useful way of trying out methods on a smaller scale.

This course will give you practice in refining a research question to an empirical plan, which is helpful, though I'd recommend coming into the course with a decent idea of what you might want to do for your thesis. Matt is really a fantastic member of the Social Studies department, it's worth it to take the class just to get to chat with him about research.
