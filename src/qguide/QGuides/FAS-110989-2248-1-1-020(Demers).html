Take this class! Do not be afraid. The material is enjoyable and very applicable to real life situations across all disciplines (sports teams rankings, nature conservation efforts, loss of energy in a system, etc).

The three sections a week + three p–sets are very helpful for solidifying the material. P–sets (and the class in general) were easier to complete than the p–sets in math 21a, and if you haven’t taken math 21a there is no need before taking this class. My peers who had not taken math 21a performed just as well as those of us who had.

Exams are an entire different format than class problems or p–sets, so completing the practice exams is absolutely necessary. Studying for this class was manageable because you remember most of the material and it is not that hard to relearn material you struggled with.

Good luck! At the very least you’ll make cool friends, at the most you’ll come out this class with an entirely different perspective on how math models so many things in the world around us.
