The experience of this course is totally dependent on your section leader. It is a pity that the gov department does not have its tutorials with professors rather than TFs. In my case, my section was perhaps the worst experience I've had so far at Harvard, with seminars that were taught more like lectures, horrible and inconsistent feedback on assignment, a general lack of enthusiasm, and no encouragement of discussions. However, do not let this discourage you completely as you might be lucky and get a great section leader.
content is cool and you can learn about non–WEIRD societies. not a lot of fun, tho. despite being a seminar, it sometimes was more of a lecture than dicussion–based, though that shifted a bit in the latter part of the course. you can experiment with ideas for a possible thesis in the final paper, which is nice. however, the final paper feels like little: the final paper is an introduction. an iNtRoDuCtIoN. sure, it's an 8–9 paper introduction, writing a proper introduction is quite some work, and it sets the ground for the rest of the paper. but still, it's an introduction. it would be more interesting to have the opportunity to develop a mini–study. if you've already written a Government research paper before, the course might feel a bit redundant but you can take the opportunity to consolidate the skills needed to write a good research paper and do the readings for fun (because they can be quite interesting). the class can be a sort of a breath of fresh air in the Govenrment department because it doesn't focus on the US – we don't need to discuss the Congress or the President of the US ever. take it as a chance to learn about other cultures and not the typical DC–obsessed Gov kid. assignments are doable and not a lot of work. Julio is great TF and allows us to be flexible in choosing the topic for the final paper – he guides us in pursuing a topic of our own choice, that is fitted to our interests.
Take this class! The assignments are fair and interesting, while the topics discussed were intriguing and easy to follow along.
This class was pleasant and was a good introductory step to experience with the gov program.
This was a deeply rewarding course, made better by Julio. He's just really chill and VERY approachable. I think initially coming into this course I wasn't super sure how to do assignments & did badly on the first assignment, but he definitely worked with me to keep up and do better, despite a number of personal challenges I faced throughout the semester. Learned so much, and really appreciated all the conversations we had.
