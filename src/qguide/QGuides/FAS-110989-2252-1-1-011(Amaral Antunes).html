Very cool and eye opening class. Would only take if you needed it though. Quite a lot of knowledge condensed into a relatively short course so be ready to work.
Listen, if you're taking this class you probably have to so it doesn't make a difference what the feedback is. BUT. This class is very difficult so do not pack your schedule. The second half of this class is brutal, the instructors do not grant extensions even with serious illnessess, the tests are often very difficult even with a lot of preparation, and this class might make you regret coming to this school. That being said, you can find a community of people who are also struggling and all study together which makes it slightly better. If you at all can avoid taking this class I would.
Spend 20 minutes before each class reviewing the reading materials because it will allow you to better absorb information in class and you'll feel more confident approaching the worksheets. After class, finish the worksheets in OH or on your own to prepare for the Psets. Space out the Psets across two days each so you have time to ask questions in OH and aren't overwhelmed by them. Do the extra steps like warmups and extra credit problems, you'll thank yourself later because they further your understanding of material that you otherwise wouldn't get. Conceptual understanding is INCREDIBLY important in this class––I cannot stress enough how memorizing formulas/rules will take you like 30% of the way there but you need to develop a flexible understanding of things beyond that. Space out studying with practice exams, it took me two days per exam to first take it then review it deeply, redo questions, and come to OH. If you don't do well on a question lean into that concept––review textbook, handouts, videos, write down even the most minute questions you have about it to make sure you cover your bases. The weekly quizzes are relatively helpful but the free late days from them are definitely worth it. If you are historically not confident in your math ability, like me, try to come into the class with a positive mindset and take advantage of as many of the resources as you can and I truly think you'll be okay. 21B was a wonderful course for me and I'm so glad I took it, I also found it a lot more approachable than 21A (my calc background is pretty weak).
Especially if you take Math 21a right before this, taking Math 21b WILL feel exhausting. 3 p–sets a week will catch up to you no doubt whether you like it or not. With that said, Linear Algebra is required for so many concentrations that if you are taking this course in the first place, it is likely that you have to take it. So, how can you make the experience a bit better? Get a p–set group ASAP! Struggling through the coursework on your own is so hard, and honestly just not worth it. Do your p–sets with each other and bounce concepts off of one another before exams.

I will say, the fact that there are no workshops in Math 21b is a life–saver. That was my LEAST favorite part of Math 21a. Instead, there are these super awesome optional quiz sessions which I would go to on the weeks I didn't understand the material as well. It really helped solidify those concepts.

Lastly, you will never be able to look at a true/false question without trauma after taking this class. 20 true or false questions on every exam. My best advice for these problems: do the true false at the VERY end of the exam. bubble in all the ones you know. For the ones you have no clue on, take your pencil and drop it onto the paper. If the pencil falls right, bubble true. If it falls left bubble false. (Basically, simulate probability so you have a 50–50 chance of getting the question right or wrong)

This might be my favorite course at Harvard so far. If you enjoy math and problem solving I highly recommend this course. It is definitely the most difficult of the intro series (1a/b, 21a/b), but it is very rewarding. In addition, the 20 question T/F section that all the exams begin with force you to have a very concrete understanding of the material, so the exams really push you to learn.
This course is a lot. Do your PSETs right after class while it is still fresh. Go to office hours to get feedback and guidance. Use MQC to check your PSETs and GO TO QUIZ SESSIONS. Quiz sessions are good practice for exams.
While I don't recommend any of the Harvard intro math courses, 21b is BY FAR the worst of the offerings. By taking this course, you will be thrown into a world that basically throws out everything you've learned up to this point but still moves at an insanely rapid pace. None of my classmates had any idea what was happening for the course. Major portions of exams are true/false questions with NO partial credit, and they are so challenging even the COURSE HEAD couldn't answer one during a review session for a midterm. Someone needs to make major changes to this class, but so far it remains bad. If you have to take it, grit your teeth and find a good study buddy. But honestly this is my least favorite class I've taken at Harvard.
Keep up with problem sets and class attendance throughout the semester; very difficult to catch up otherwise.
I think the Math 21 series is probably the closest thing you can get to a high school class in college. The environment is very conducive to collaborative problem–solving. There is a ton of structured support for the course with MQC running basically every night to help with psets, individual TF office hours to address conceptual questions, and practice/review sessions before the midterms. 21b definitely felt like more of a time commitment than 21a (the psets were longer and required more critical thinking). If you spend 10 minutes after each class summarizing the key ideas of the pset and worksheet, you should be well–prepared for the exams. Often the practice exams were much harder than the actual exam.
This class is difficult at times, but it is very rewarding as long as you put the effort into completing the assignments and understanding the material. As some of the TFs say, this is a definition heavy class, so understanding theorems is very helpful for psets and exams.
