What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If this class is offered again at Harvard, take it! Elisa is one of the best instructors I have had throughout my undergrad education here. If you are interested in ethnography, cities, social theory, economics, philosophy, architecture, planning—any of these, really—you will (1) be able to contribute to the intellectual diversity that makes the seminar engaging and (2) appreciate the readings Elisa has selected, which span a wide geography.
Read everything you're asked to read!
Please take this course– you won’t regret it. Professor Elisa is one of the most supportive professors I’ve ever met. Her warmth, patience, and genuine care for her students create an environment where you feel seen, heard, and encouraged to grow. This class isn’t just about learning, it’s about being inspired.
I really recommend this class to students who are interested in urban studies or anthropology. This class was many of my peers' first anthropology class, and it was a really good introduction to what the department offers. It is clear that Professor Tamburo has a passion for teaching anthropology and has mastered the art of leading productive discussions. I would recommend any class by Professor Tamburo, especially those new to the field.
For those who are interested in urban studies or ethnography, definitely take this course.
