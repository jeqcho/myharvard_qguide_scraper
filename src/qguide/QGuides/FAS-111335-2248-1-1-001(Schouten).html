Take this course!! It might seem intimidating, but everyone is genuinely invested in your success. Pedagogy is a priority in this course.
This class was great. The professor is beyond phenomenal. There are few classes at Harvard that will hold you accountable for your contributions and engagement, which is the only way you can develop your critical thinking and discussion skills, and this is one of them. The syllabus is well–crafted so you will gain a sense for nuance while learning to approach political questions philosophically, or philosophical questions politically.
The course covers crucial topics and pushed me to think about what justice might look like for oppressed communities. It will challenge you to think beyond yourself and think critically about difficult and pressing questions. I recommend this course without reservations!
Definitely recommend this course! Schouten has the best pedagogy of any teacher I've had at Harvard. Her class has the perfect balance of lectures vs. discussions, and she does a great job facilitating engaged conversations between students. The assignment structure is very focused on helping you improve throughout the semester, which I found helpful, and deadlines are spaced out so the class isn't too much work all at once. This is essentially an entire course on Rawls and interpretations/applications/critiques of Rawls, so if you don't like Rawls, it isn't the course for you. But otherwise I highly recommend :)
This class is fantastic. Gina is an amazing teacher, and the course content will get you thinking hard.
Excellent reading list with an excellent professor! If you are remotely interested in political philosophy and have not taken a course with Professor Schouten, you are doing something wrong. Also, the main assignment for this course was structured in a pretty unique way: theres a paper due halfway through the course (not a rough draft but a real paper), you present on your paper in small groups with either the professor of the TF, and then the final paper is a revised version of the initial paper based on the feedback you received. Plenty of classes do scaffolding for final papers that is a bit similar, but this was the first time at this school where it felt like I really had the opportunity to receive meaningful feedback and actually get the chance to think about it and apply it. That was a huge perk of this course.
This will be one of your best seminar experiences at Harvard. Professor Schouten is an incredible teacher – don't miss the chance to learn from her. You'll become a better writer and thinker.
