You should take it, but just know you really do have to read every single reading. This professor won't let you slide by without having read, in my opinion.
This course was AMAZING!!! We went through so many interesting topics, and I truly gained a new insight into how I think about food, famines, agriculture, and so much more. The readings can be a bit dense at times, and section is solely meant for participation (one in–depth, ongoing conversation for the entirety of the seminar slot) but if you start a couple of days before class and pace yourself, it's not terrible at all. I truly enjoyed the readings and our class discussions, and I wish I could take more classes with Professor Soto Laveaga. You also get to go on a food tour in the North End to explore Italian cuisine, and it's EXQUISITE!
This is a great course, I would just be prepared to do a good amount of reading. The professor is amazing and genuinely excited to teach students about class material.
This class was super interesting and engaging. The material we cover not only teaches you new things but teaches you to think about power and systems which govern us today from a completely different perspective. The seminars can be a bit long and hard to sit through but if there are enough readings itll work out well. Readings take time and if youre like me and aren't good at managing reading on the college level it may be difficult.
We had the best conversations in this class!! Professor Soto Laveaga is amazing and the best at opening the floor for discussion; I personally learned so much about the food systems that are so ingrained in our lives (ex: how food has been used to reinforce narratives of hunger, how food has been used as a political and economic tool, etc.).
This was an incredibly interesting class and I highly recommend taking it, especially if you are interested in food. This class transformed the way I think about commonly accepted scientific concepts (like the calorie) and helped me think about hunger through a variety of lenses.
This is a fascinating class–Professor Soto Laveaga is one of the best professors I've learned from while here, and she does a great job of encouraging discussion and designing an interesting syllabus. The class has great flow. You'll always be making connections with prior weeks as you move throughout the semester, and it's a wonderful learning experience.
