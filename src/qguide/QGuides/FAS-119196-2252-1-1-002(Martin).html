This course is intense. By the end of the semester, you'll eat, drink, and speak math. PSets and worksheets keep you busy daily, as do AI assignments.

The quantity of material covered is so much that it might seem (and it is true) that at times the material is not being learnt too efficiently, but that is okay once you get to exams. Exams (4 of them throughout the semester) are a way for you to go over all the material you covered in class, so by doing the practice tests (i.e. doing them in a timed environment, checking answers, reattempting them) you'll learn everything. It always strikes me that unfortunately, at Harvard, course support is decreasing in difficulty of the course itself. That is to say, this course has a ton of support material, MQC, office hours, fun TFs that are very good at explaining, so you'll never run out of options to consider when doing work for the class. But this is jus because this class is introductory. Overall, the course is well–structured, intense, and prepares you well for courses in other concentrations that you might approach in your time at Harvard, so I recommend this (especially over 22B. I took 22A and it was just the worst experience I've had at Harvard).
