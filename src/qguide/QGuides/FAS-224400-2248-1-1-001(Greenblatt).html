Professor Greenblatt is a fascinating lecturer. His approach is not particularly conventional––you may have mixed feeling about this. Slides would be populated with a mixture of quotes from the text, and odd curiosities here and there––reviews of the play, Bruegel paintings, early illustrations and the like. For every brilliant lecture (and they are really really brilliant) there are two or three that will leave you wondering whether you had read the same text, or whether that would have mattered. If you feel like you already have a reasonable handle on the Shakespeare AND on criticism, and want to know what Professor Greenblatt is thinking about in relation to these texts, this is a great course. If you are looking for an "Introduction to Shakespeare" class––you should perhaps look elsewhere (online, or another course). I had a great time––and would happily take this course again. You just need to be aware of the limitations.

It was incredible to see some of Harvard’s Shakespeare collections, watching Romeo and Juliet live and the assignments were super – not repetitive and creative. One of the highlights for me was section as everyone came to class excited to talk and the teaching fellow, Victoria was phenomenal about explaining the play and broader concepts. Sections felt fun, energetic and genuinely interesting.
