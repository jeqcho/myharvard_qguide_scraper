What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you are not secure with your Spanish knowledge from High School, do not start with this course. Start with Spanish 11. This is not a beginner course. The readings are long and you need an hour minimum to complete them.
Spanish 20 introduces you to a range of useful grammar structures, from more beginner ones to some new. Your experience depends very much on your section teacher. There's a lot of studying you should do for exams that is not obvious in the homework. I also got less speaking experience in class than I had wanted, but it was a good class for learning about more than just the grammar structures. Most of classes were actually discussing the literature that we read in our homework, so that was a more challenging way of learning and engaging with Spanish for the better.
1) There's no substitute for allocating sufficient time to meaningfully grapple with the texts and assigned course materials – it will take longer than you think if you're earnestly trying to unpack some of the meatier content.
2) Adopt a consistent approach to tracking vocabulary and grammar rules throughout the semester; whatever strategy you opt for, be disciplined about keeping your notes up to date.
3) Find little ways to incorporate practice into your daily routine – I'd chat with one of my classmates in Spanish for ~2 hours after class ended as we went about our days.
IF YOU HAVE JOSE DE LEON, DO NOT HESITATE TO SWITCH SECTIONS. On my first day of class, my class could immediately tell that he was going to be very rude and mean. The next day, over half the class switched to a different section. However, I wanted to give him the benefit of the doubt and I stuck it out. Worst decision of my life. Not only is he an incredibly harsh grader, but he also makes the classroom a terrifying environment in which to speak another language. And I am someone who genuinely wanted to learn Spanish before this and was considering concentrating in it or doing a citation, but he has stamped all of the love for learning another language out of me. I am also not someone who typically feels this strongly about teachers or dislikes people easily. He is truly awful. I also didn't even learn much. However, I absolutely loved Spanish 11 and I think I would have loved Spanish 20 as well if I was smart enough to switch sections when I had the chance. I've heard great things about the other teachers, so I would definitely say to still take the course if you're interested in learning some Spanish.
This class does not go into a ton of depth to the grammar in class.
Do not take this class with Jose De Leon. Especially if you are just taking Spanish to fulfill the requirement. His grading is ridiculous and takes away from enjoying the class.
This class is definitely a lot of work, but it is truly rewarding. By the end of it, I was so much more comfortable speaking on a variety of important topics. My grammar and reading also really improved. It takes some adjusting, but by the end, you learn a lot and you feel truly prepared to have a conversation with a native speaker.
