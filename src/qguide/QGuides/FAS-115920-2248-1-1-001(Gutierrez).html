I skipped from 30 to 50 and am very happy I did so. If you want to do the same thing and did well on the oral exams in 30, you'll be totally fine. This is an advanced class for sure, but that made it so much more interesting than lower level Spanish classes. While students still, by and large, are unable to express ideas with quite the level of nuance that would be available in their native language, the discussions we can have about course materials in this class were so much deeper.
this course is a lot of busy–work and the exams being memorized plays presented to the course are lowk more work than is worth it. at this level, I regret not having taken 59, 49, or 70.
EXAMENS ARE FUN
This class is great! You get pretty close with your classmates, and you will have a lot of fun in class with them. However, heads up, you do need to memorize the plays that you perform as your exams, which does require some time spent out of class practicing with your group and memorizing your lines on your own. However, with the right group, even that is fun!
I wouldn't recommend taking it unless you are really interested in Spanish theatre and performing yourself. Personally, I do not believe that this course works as a logical conclusion in the Spanish 10 through 50 sequence.
This class was fun and quite immersive. But there was also a lot of work and it was graded with more scrutiny than most other spanish classes. If you take the course just make sure to put your best into every assignment to get the grade you want!
This class is a lot of fun! It is different to Spanish classes of other levels because it focusses on theatre – both reading and writing plays. There is little to no review of grammar, so much sure you know the different tenses before taking the class.
I did not enjoy this class as much as I hoped I would. The exams can be fun (they're just skits you have to write and perform), but I did not look forward to going to class each day. Readings were not very interesting and speaking up in class was intimidating. I wouldn't really recommend taking it, but if you want the secondary it's an okay course to count toward it.
