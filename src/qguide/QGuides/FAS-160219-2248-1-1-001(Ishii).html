Professor Ishii is amazing, super fun, and genuinely cares about everyone's learning process. Also, lots of candy! TAKE THIS CLASS!!! This is without a doubt one of the best first year seminars out there. I will give a warning that it was a little bit more of a time commitment than I had been expecting, but this is mostly just due to the longer class time (2h45), and movie watching (2–3h). Homework typically could be done in around an hour, and this is without office hours as they conflicted with one of my classes. The community is also amazing and everyone is super supportive as it is a pass or fail class. The movies are also super entertaining! I often went back on YouTube to replay some of the memorable scenes :)

This was definitley a super fun seminar. I'd say it is a bit more of a time commitment than I originally expected because your spendings sometimes two hours on a weekday night watching a movie, but I also thought it was nice to just get a break from normal schoolwork in a way thats still productive since you're still doing something for a class. Dr. Ishii is also just so smart and knowledgeable, and it was such a cool experience to learn from her, plus she has a lot of really fun games to play in class.
Take good notes while watching the movies and while Professor Ishii is working through problems during class. The content there will be important for the homeworks.
This class is great. While it's a bit of a time commitment, with a three–hour class and two–hour movie each week, the material is interesting and group activities are fun. Dr. Ishii is awesome – do the readings and take notes on the movies to have the best chances of receiving candy! The assignments are relatively easy, however the calculations component of this course should not be shadowed by the movie–watching component – they are often difficult and will require you to seek help. However, collaboration with peers is allowed, which I didn't do enough of but would encourage. Take this class if you're interested in natural disasters, love movies, and can manage physics calculations!
