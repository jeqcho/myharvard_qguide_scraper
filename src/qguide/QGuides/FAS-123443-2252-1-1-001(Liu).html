This class is excellent. Professor Liu is the best; her lectures are insightful and engaging. This course covers some of the most important techniques in causal inference and data analysis—I think you get a ton out of it even if you have seen the topics in other settings before. I cannot recommend taking this class enough!
The first few weeks may feel overwhelming, but it really gets better. It is a challenging course if you don't have a strong quantitative background, but there is a lot of support available. I came out of the course with stronger quantitative skills, I highly recommend it if this is something you want to work on.
This course is more difficult in terms of math and coding than GOV50, but definitely manageable. Professor Liu and Sima are very good at breaking down complicated statistical concepts and guiding students in the learning process. Also very helpful during office hours, exam review, and final project.
TAKE THIS CLASS! The first half of the course leading up to the midterm is math–heavy and focuses on your conceptual understanding, but the midterm exam is open book, so as long as you pay attention in lectures and sections and understand the materials, you should be fine. The second half covers different advanced topics but is designed for you to focus on your final project, which you should definitely get started on right after your midterm, because you don't get much time to work on it. If you are interested in causal inference, regression, text analysis, machine learning, and network analysis with a focus on social science, this is the right class to take!
Naijia is a great professor, super supportive and accessible. Pranav is also a very eloquent section leader and really knows how to help us better understand the concepts in class – also just very dedicated in his extended office hours. The best thing about this class is that its underlying focus is not to need you know how to code everything by hand, but really the design–thinking skillset behind using code to solve problems, hence it also allows the use of ChatGPT –– which i think is a really forward–thinking and experimental notion.
The information in this course is extremely useful for social science research, but this course is a little tricky for someone without a statistic background.
Personally did not enjoy this class. PSETs and midterms were centered around coding but lectures and sections were quite conceptual so there was a disconnect which made working on the PSET quite difficult. My TF was not helpful when it came to questions (related to lecture material) and whenever I asked I usually got a roundabout response that did not directly answer the question and I had to figure it out on my own. I began going to other TFs for help because I felt unsupported in my section. The feedback process throughout this course is very weak. We spent a month on a final project with four check–in points but only received feedback on the first draft (which was due a week before the final and consisted of a sentence). The grade on my final project came with no feedback whatsoever. It's not the hardest material to get a handle of but there was not a lot of support. Love Pranav though he is super helpful.
