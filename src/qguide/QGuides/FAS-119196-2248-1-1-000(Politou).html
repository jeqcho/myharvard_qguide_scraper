Go to office hours if you need it, they're better than MQC in my opinion.
if you do not love math in the sense of looking at numbers all day long then you should be vary of taking this course. Most TFs will just ask you to figure stuff out on your own and someone like me prefers a lecture–based situation where I am taught the concept before and then I apply it. So, if you are like me then you should not take this course, or if you need to take it know that you will be studying mostly by yourself.
You're probably not taking this class as an elective, so you'll need to deal with it anyways. Best to give yourself lots of time to do PSets (they take 2h, maybe 2.5h on average and there are three each week + a workshop roughly every two weeks) and exams (mini–exam, two midterms, a hard final). Find yourself a good PSet group, go to OH, and MQC, and put in the effort –– you should be fine.
Also, I took this as a senior to do econ honors: what a bad choice. Get your math out of the way as soon as you can if you're an econ concentrator.
As long as you have a pretty strong single–variable calculus background, it is a very fair class and you should do fine in the course.
Take this class! If you are worried about not having the appropriate background, I would say go for it. Your peers will be from a variety of grades and backgrounds, but they give you all the proper resources to succeed.
I really enjoyed this class. The discussion format is really helpful, and although the three psets per week is admittedly time–consuming, they're rarely so hard that you can't make any progress on them, and they each usually took me about three hours. I would definitely recommend going to MQC or getting a study group to check your answers on each one, because you're bound to lose lots of easy points otherwise. I learned a lot from this class, and I think the same is true for most people who take it.
The structure of this course it great! Material progresses in a logical fashion, building off concepts that you learned earlier in the course. Workshops can be tedious. Midterms and exam are very fair.
You cover a lot of material in this class. Would recommend if you enjoy math but just know that you need to put a lot of time into this class but in return you gain a lot of knowledge
