What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a good course if you are looking for a rigorous, theoretical tutorial where people really do the readings each week and where the seminar itself is two hours of sometimes intense very focused dialogue with the group (so this is definitely more work than your average soc–std junior tutorial). Bo–Mi is a very experienced social studies prof who can explain complex theoretical ideas deftly. As you can imagine the slant of the course is largely skeptical of technology but it is not anti–tech altogether –– the idea is to sort of unpack the false promise of technologies like AI, or "pull back the curtain" on them.
Take this class!!! Bo–Mi Choi is amazing, likely one of the wisest professors you’ll have at Harvard. Discussions are amazing if people do the readings and reading load is fairly manageable. I highly recommend.
This is a great junior tutorial to take. Bo–Mi has been teaching it for many years, so the syllabus and her pedagogy are quite refined. Our class had very good theoretical discussions that Bo–Mi also did her best to orient toward praxis, which I really appreciated, and she also led every week with a meditation. Support for the term paper was well structured, and discussions with her were always generative. I only wish that there was more guidance about methodology specifically, because "doing" critical theory can be quite slippery.
I loved the readings of this class and Bo–Mi grounds everyone in the class with a meditation at the start. However, I would note that we received no feedback before the final paper. I wish we received grades throughout because no one knows where they actually stand in the class. Also, everything is via email rather than canvas which is slightly annoying and feels disorganized. I will say though, Bo–Mi is very intelligent, has amazing perspectives, and is very understanding.
