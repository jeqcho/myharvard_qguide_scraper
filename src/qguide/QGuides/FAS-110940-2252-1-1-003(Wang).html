What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It is hard class no doubt. If you don't keep up with the coursework, it is very overwhelming as the material continuously builds up on each other and there are very frequent assessments and homework. However, it was also a very rewarding course. My Chinese improved by so much from the beginning of the semester to now that it's truly incredible. All the instructors are incredible teachers and just fun people to be around and the Chinese Department is such a great community. If you want to learn the language, stick with the Chinese Program here––if you just want a fun language class, I'd recommend against taking this course, it requires serious commitment/buy in.
This is a great class to gain more speaking vocabulary. The teachers are super friendly and welcoming. It can be intimidating to be cold–called, but the teachers are always there to help you.
Chinese at Harvard is incredible. All the teachers are so kind and so smart and the best people I have ever met in my entire life.
Take this class! While the workload is comparable to Chinese 120a in the fall, I found the topics/subject matter more interesting as a whole (e.g. learning about Inner Mongolia and prejudices). As someone who has been to Taiwan and am visiting Mainland China, I feel like learning about some cultural customs has also been quite rewarding. It’s a lot of work, but you certainly will not regret it!!!
The teaching staff puts in so much effort to make the class fun and engaging. Learned more in this class than I did from years of high school Mandarin. Lots of speaking practice opportunities.
