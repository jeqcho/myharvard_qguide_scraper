I LOVEEEE THE ASL COMMUNITY HERE!!! The professors are so engaging, supporting, caring, and enthusiastic about ASL and sharing about Deaf Culture. I especially love the community hours aspects (we have to get 10+ community hours each semester) and the lovely events that are thrown –– they are wonderful! I love the structure of the class being more hands–on and engaging. You can tell the professors would rather have actual conversations than just lecture, so most of the time we're playing games for review and learning or practicing through our own conversations.

If you're doing this to fulfill the second part of your language requirement then you'll love it but I'd suggest for anyone to take ASL here!

Please take this course if you can!! Because the ASL class sizes are small, you really build a community with the students in your section. It is so rewarding to go to the ASL events and feel your conversations becoming more complex as you use what you've learned in class in a relaxed environment.
Prof. Frances and Prof. Nozomi have done an incredible job building a strong ASL community at Harvard this year. There were so many great events, coffee chats, and guest lectures. They also kept us informed about external opportunities, which helped us stay connected to the broader ASL and Deaf communities. The class itself was engaging, and both professors were very responsive to student feedback and concerns. It was clear that they genuinely wanted students to succeed, which can be rare at Harvard. The SignPal Pals program was an amazing addition to the course and made the experience more immersive and collaborative.

My main concerns were with the Signing Naturally platform and the consistency across course sections. We did not find out until several weeks into the semester that there was a way to complete the assignments without paying. This should have been clarified before the course began, ideally during winter break. Because of this delay, some students paid while others did not, and there was a lot of confusion about which assignments were required. These expectations were never clearly communicated, as there were some discrepancies between the two professors, and Canvas was not updated to reflect what they were saying. Additionally, there were four different course sections with different lecturers, and there was a noticeable lack of alignment. Some sections fell behind or covered different material, and the assignments and assessments varied across sections. This created an uneven learning experience. The issue was apparent from the start of the semester, as students entered ASL 2 with very different levels of prior knowledge depending on their section in ASL 1. It would be helpful if the course could be more standardized across sections to ensure that all students are receiving the same quality of instruction and opportunities to succeed.
