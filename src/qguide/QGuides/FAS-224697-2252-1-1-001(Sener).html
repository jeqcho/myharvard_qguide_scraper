This class is very interesting and covers a wide variety of issues in the region. I highly recommend reading all the readings and attending class, not to prepare for the exams, but to truly learn the content. Ipek is so very kind and is a great lecturer!
Straightforward government class. A good sampling of many issues in Middle Eastern politics. The main assignments are the two exams and a group presentation, so not that much work. The one thing that could have been improved is more integration of readings into class discussion and/or lecture. Also, a lot of the reading felt redundant since it was background rather than research papers or something of the like. Also, REALLY cool guest speakers came in, like the Former Prime Minister of Jordan.
This course was amazing! As someone who knew very little about the modern history of the Middle East before this class, I cannot recommend it enough! It was comprehensive, informative, and has left me feeling more confident approaching difficult conversations about issues pertinent to the Middle East.
Take this class if you want to learn more about the Middle East! No background necessary; I certainly did not know anything coming in. Ipek is a great professor and brings in such great guests, including former heads of state and government in the Middle East!
Great intro to the region and its political history that I think will improve anyone’s understanding of the Middle East, which is of course very timely. I felt freer to discuss controversial topics in this class than almost any other at Harvard, and we were given the tools to do so.
Please sign up. Honestly was a really great experience. Great teacher welcomes everyone's perspective. Great TF Khaled. Not a heavy workload. Midterm and final are open book.
This class is a strong introduction to the history and politics of the region. Highly recommend purchasing the textbook to supplement readings and prep for the in–class exams.
This was a very good course. The topics were great and I truly feel like I learned a lot about the politics of the middle east. The teaching staff was amazing and very knowledgeable, both prof. Sener and Khaled. Thank you for an amazing semester!
This class is great if you want an overall introduction to politics and governance structures in the Middle East; however, there is so much information taught in it that it's difficult to retain what you learn. As this class goes super quickly through various topics, I was a little frustrated by how much content was somewhat glossed over during each lecture. That said, you do get a basic overview of each country in the region and the historical backgrounds that inform their current development, and I feel like I have a solid foundation to work with now as I look for future classes.
The professor is clearly very knowledgeable and cares a lot about her students! The history we learned about was fascinating. However, though this course was framed as a beginner course, most students had thorough knowledge of the materials going in, so I found it a bit difficult to keep up as a beginner. I was a bit confused about what exactly I was supposed to be taking away or remembering from lectures. Going into office hours definitely helped.
This was a wonderful class!! I always looked forward to it and the instructors were so kind and sweet and understanding. I appreciated that the course didn't have busy work along the way, and all readings felt very useful and weren't too hard. I often found participation a little tough and intimidating because a lot of students in the class were either from the Middle East or had lived there, so it was of course often easier for them to speak up and I often didn't in case I got something wrong (but that's more of my problem than the class's problem)! I would definitely take this class again and I left with a broad understanding of the history and politics of the Middle East, and learned so much more than I knew before. I recommend it!
Section is amazing. Lecture lacks engagement most of the time. Exams are open–book/ open–note and what you would expect. Happy I took the course but honestly it wasn't too engaging
