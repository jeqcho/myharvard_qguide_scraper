The content's interesting, not super excessive but definitely more work than the average expos. The classroom environment was very fun and discussions engaging and comfortable, I have few complaints about the course content. The only problem really is the writing expectations and grading, I truly didn't improve my writing at all, nor did I properly learn how to cite, which feels like the point of expos, but besides that the class was ok. Rob's a cool dude, sometimes a little rude and offputting, and he grades like a hater, but that's just his vibe. He's kind, chill, and approachable, and really cares about his job and students.
This is a fantastic expos class! As long as you are interested in somewhat abstract philosophy, the content is super interesting, and Rob does a great job teaching it. My sense is that this is definitely one of the harder expos classes, but I also think it was a lot more rewarding. It is clear that Rob really cares about his students (even if he can be grumpy at times), and he is extremely supportive and gives great feedback.
Pretty dense/boring readings and the topics can feel abstract/complicated at times, but Rob is great and the class has a very good vibe. Grading is hard but not unfair and an A is possible if you work hard and write well. Rob gives helpful feedback on essays and I feel like I've become much better at writing and argumentation thanks to this course.
Expos with Rob is definitely really hard. He doesn't really focus too much on "writing skills" and focuses a lot more on the content that he wants to teach. The content is really interesting, but he doesn't really grade with a rubric like other Expos professors, so if you get a grade on a paper that you don't really agree with, it's a little bit difficult to challenge that grade if there is no rubric to back you up. The readings can sometimes be intense or a lot so be sure to spread your work out accordingly.

The office hours with him are really helpful in guiding you in a direction for your papers, listen to that feedback and really apply it.

Rob is not very good with deadlines for himself. He doesn't really upload grades when he says he is, but it's fine. He is very lenient with turning things in a little bit late and such. Be sure to communicate if you need an extensions.

PARTICIPATE IN CLASS. He's really lovely and just wants to hear your thoughts and chat with you about them.

This might be the hardest expos class (and all of them are really hard) but I don't regret my decision at all. Just be mentally prepared to do all the work and thinking required for an advanced phil class.
I honestly didn't love this class. If you're really into philosophy, then go for it. But I wasn't as interested in the content as some other kids were. The readings can be difficult, especially at the beginning of the semester. But I don't feel like the class helped my writing at all, and it was mostly centered around talking about different philosophies. So, if you're not into that, then I would go for a different expos.
You should take this class. Most expos classes are very specific and kind of random, but this one is pretty legit. If you are at all interesting in taking a philosophy course at Harvard, you might as well bang it out along with the expos requirement in one fell swoop. This is a great course. We first talk about the meaning in language (how is it that words can transmit meaning, and what is that meaning/how is it determined), literature (what role does an author play in the meaning of a text? How important are language rules for meaning?), and then life (is life meaningful or absurd? How can we find meaning in our lives?). The readings were, albeit long and dense at times, overall super interesting. We read David Foster Wallace, Frankl, Barthes, Wittgenstein, Hegel, Grice, and more. Rob is very knowledgable and smart; this means that he is not very lenient on essays and will both clock as well as comment on any inaccuracies in your writing. I appreciate that in a teacher – he doesn't take bs. Still, Rob is very kind. He wants us all to feel good in class and he likes to joke around with us. He's funny and he is not mean at all – he is just smart and wants us to understand the concepts well. Class can be tedious at times but is generally super interesting with a fair amount of discussion, group work, and class chats. Rob takes the time to explain any confusing readings to us in a very detailed and helpful way. There is usually a fair amount of time left for people to comment on readings and give different takes. Rob is organized in terms of class content, but disorganized in Canvas. Comments take a while sometimes but they are thorough. All in all this was a great class and I learned so so so much. I am very glad and grateful to have taken it.
