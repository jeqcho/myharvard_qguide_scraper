This course is great; if you are interested in the topic, definitely take this class, it requires a lot from you but at the same time offers a chance to greatly improve.
My writing came a long way from this class. Dr. Monaghan is a great instructor. Her feedback is blunt and concise. I appreciate that. The grading is fair, but you will do very well if you can humble yourself and apply the concepts of the class.
Definitely one of the more difficult of the Expos, hard to adjust to from school. However, IF you are interested in war history and manage your time well, you will learn a lot about college writing from this class. With that said, you will also likely learn just as much in the other expos classes.
Participate in class, come to conferences with many specific questions, dont save your papers u tik the last minute.
The class is not an easy Expos class by any mean, there are a lot of assignments and the deadlines will sneak up on you if you are not on top of it. I would not recommend this class if you are not interested in military history, but even if you do, then the class can be more theoretical than what you would expect (remember that this is a writing class and not a history class). The professor is supportive and will encourage you to try out new writing techniques to get you to grow. This class is not generous with extension at all, so be prepared to start working early on or cram until 4 am the night before (I personally have reached 6 am once). I would not recommend it if you have a lot of pset classes.

I still like this class a lot. You will grow as a writer and see a new perspective on military history, but be prepared to spend serious time for it.
Dr. M provides extensive opportunities to ask questions and attend office hours throughout the course. She is fair, and provides clear communication on the expectations for the course assignments. Take advantage of in–class opportunities to ask questions and discuss readings with peers.
As far as Expos classes go, I would recommend this one. If you have any interest in war in general, this class gives you a lot of freedom at the end to research whatever aspect of war you would like. I feel like this class probably has the same amount of work as most other Expos sections.
Take this course if you want to become a better writer, as that is something that will definitely happen. Another thing that would be good to know is that in this course when you hand in a draft it needs to be as perfect as a revision, which makes the revision process so much easier compared to other expos courses. Also, the research paper is cool because it is literally about anything to do with war, literally anything.
