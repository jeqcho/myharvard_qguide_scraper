This class is legitimately fantastic, and totally changed my perspective on human behavior, especially as it relates to how genetic evolution and culture intersect. So long as you do the readings and go to class and pay attention you will be totally fine, and it's well worth the kind of annoying strict reading requirements on Perusall. The course staff is also super helpful. One note of caution, though, is that the grade cutoff is a 94 for an A. Since I haven't seen my grade yet it's hard to say what this will mean but it may be harder to ensure an A than a normal gened as a result. No matter what, this class is really fantastic and the course staff is generally very kind and knowledgable. If you're looking for a science gened, this seems to be as good as it gets!
Take this class! If you are at all interested in humans and our ancestors, this is a fantastic and interesting way to knock out the science GENED. Prof. Henrich and Dr. Curtin are great teachers. They each have a ton of experience doing field work in Fiji and Mexico that they weave into lectures. You do need to study for the exams, but as long as you review your notes and have been paying attention in class they are totally manageable. I would not say this is a gem, but I still highly recommend it because of the subject matter and professors. I'm a senior and this is the best GENED I've taken.
