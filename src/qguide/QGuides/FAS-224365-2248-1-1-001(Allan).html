You should take this class! Angela is absolutely wonderful and has an obvious, radiating passion for the course material that becomes infectious. The readings are super manageable, and the net semesterly writing workload is even palatable for STEM students! Seriously whether you consider yourself to be super culturally literate or you don't have social media or don't have an American cultural background, take this class and you'll learn just as much from your fellow students as you will from the readings.
i would definitely recommend this course if you're looking for an interesting seminar on pop culture. the different weekly subjects were super interesting, and covered a wide range of topics. class discussion was always fascinating, it was a great class!
I absolutely love this class; it was one of my favorite courses I have taken at Harvard. It opens your mind to so many new ways of thinking and helps to inform the way that you consume pop culture. As pop culture is something that is pretty inescapable, it is so interesting to look at it from a historical lens and see the ways it has changed over the last century. Each week I was excited to read the course materials and engage with my classmates in discussion. Angela is amazing and is great at fostering a welcoming environment where you can become friends with your classmates, learn, ask questions, and improve as a writer and student. I am very sad to be done with this course. I highly recommend it to anyone who is interested.
This class is super interesting! All of the course materials were incredibly interesting when looking through different mediums and aspects of pop culture throughout history and in modern time. Angela may seem intimidating at first as the Head of the HistLit department and her tendency to grade more on the difficult side, but she is always there to help and provides really helpful feedback!
Take this class!!! Angela is super sweet and supportive, and all of the content is very interesting.
Super fun and interesting material and I loved engaging with the texts every week and learning about aspects of popular culture. The grading could be a bit harsh at times and I feel like even when I went to office hours, I still didn't have a clear sense of what to improve on/advice (though the feedback on the papers were very great and meticulous). It was probably one of my favorite classes in terms of content but I also felt that Angela wasn't super helpful during the office hours appointment.
