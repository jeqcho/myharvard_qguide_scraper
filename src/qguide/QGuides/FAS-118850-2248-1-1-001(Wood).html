EXCELLENT class! One of my favorite classes ever. So grateful to have gotten the chance to take this my senior year. James Wood is a wonderful professor, the class follows a wonderful reading list, and the seminar style discussions were always wonderful. What a joy! It was all passion, never stress. Everyone wanted to be there to talk about these fantastic books. This is what a Harvard English class should be.
Professor Wood is awesome –– really engaging in class, and very approachable outside of class. And the books on the reading list are great. Good for an English concentration elective. Took a lot away from this class.
Because of the number of students, this is not a true seminar experience and you don't get to bond with your classmates. I wished there was more structure to this class, as Professor Wood is a rambler, albeit with beautiful ideas. I would've appreciated hearing his organized thoughts rather than what felt like aimless ramblings with random student participation at times. I didn't enjoy this course as much as I thought I would based on prior years' Q–guides and my personal interest in the syllabus, even though we read some excellent texts. However, Professor Wood is very kind.
This semester, 90LV was simply too disorganized. I know I'm likely one of the only people to accurately call this out but I found the professor, who is the only point of contact in this class with no TFs to be super inaccessible, therefore would not recommend to anyone who isn't taking it as a senior purely looking for good reads. Also, the entire grade is based on 2 papers, with no to limited instruction/help throughout to grow from the first. I found the class super isolating as a starting out English concentrator who was genuinely looking to improve for an A.
This readings for this class are amazing and Professor Wood always has insightful things to say about them. I really enjoyed doing them every week. Essays are not too long and pretty chill, though they should be well executed. This class is not a tradition seminar because most of the time Professor Wood speaks and people aren't really responding to each other with their comments, but I think it works well for the course (because what Professor Wood says is more interesting than what my classmates would likely say).
Professor Wood has such great insights on the readings! Also, the syllabus is fantastic – each one of the novels were amazing.
while there is a bit more lecturing than a typical seminar, the methodical way wood unpacks realism will be really helpful to all of your readings in the future! the syllabus may seem eclectic but you get a really interesting survey of different literature eg russian, german etc. wood is also just brilliant so if he does offer it then worth taking
If you're interested in ever reading the books on this syllabus, take this class! I got so much more out of reading these authors in this class than I ever would have just reading on my own. Prof. Wood is so knowledgeable, engaging, and facilitates discussion so well.
A semester spent in the company of great books, the greatest of which is To the Lighthouse, a book to change your life. And with that, the guidance of Prof Wood and his devotion to a practice of reading. Endlessly worth taking
This is an absolutely wonderful class! If you haven't taken a class with Professor Wood, whether the Essay or this class, get on it now! He is such a knowledgeable, passionate, kind and inspiring professor. You can tell he loves the works he's teaching, and the readings he provides on each are thought–provoking and encourage you to focus on little, surprising, stunning details of each book. Everyone in the class was captivated, and for good reason.
Professor Wood puts forward a model of the seminar that is rare but exciting a kind of intimate stage. Students participate, but don't necessarily hold the reins, and we're all better for it. The books are all incredible and he has a good sense for whether or not the majority of the class has done the reading and still makes the two hours worthwhile. Incredibly engaging lecturer and kind teacher. Charmingly self aware of the "Professor Wood's book club" view but in my mind, who wouldn't want to be in this book club? Take this class for a history of literary realism and the "age of the novel" (particularly the adultery novel) over a class about literary consciousness (though that will certainly come up too).
I highly recommend this class! The texts don't need any introduction, they truly are *transformative*, and Professor Wood is incredibly smart and knowledgeable (as are the people taking the class with you). Reading these incredible books alongside others and with such insightful guidance was exactly the kind of experience I hoped for when coming to Harvard. However, I found the format of the course a bit challengingit's a mix between a lecture and a seminar, which didnt really work for me. For a seminar, the class size was quite large, so contributing often felt intimidatinglike giving a speech with 20 people around the table. I would have preferred a more traditional lecture format or a smaller seminar setting. Additionally, while it was wonderful to hear everyones scattered thoughts, the discussions tended to feel unstructured. If youre hoping for a focused exploration of consciousness or a clear thematic thread, you might find it harder to extract cohesive ideas, as our discussions often revolved around whatever we found interesting in the books. Additionally, the grading relies on two essays without rubrics or structured promptsyou have the freedom to write on any topic, but this can feel risky if youre concerned about your GPA. Despite these structural challenges, I am so glad I took this class. I learned a lot, and the experience of engaging with these texts in such an enthusiastic, intelligent environment was so rewarding!
