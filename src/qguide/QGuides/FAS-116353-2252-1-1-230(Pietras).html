This class is super chill, but I feel like it didn't talk as much about queer coming of age stories as much as I thought it would. I thought there were going to be a lot more open discussions about a lot more literature than what we actually covered. A majority of the classes go over the elements of an academic argument, so there's not a heavy emphasis on in–class discussions about queer coming of age stories I feel.
Dr. Pietras is the best! He is very passionate about the topic at hand and will guide you very well on how to write your papers. The one thing that may be difficult is the end–of–semester video essay, it just takes a while to edit! I found the course very insightful and if you are looking for a great environment in your EXPOS class, I would opt for this one.
Take this class with Dr.Pietras!! He spends a lotttt of time covering the foundations of writing and it's really really helpful! The class is such a non–judgmental environment, and Dr.Pietras makes sure to help it feel like a supportive community. It's also super cool to be able to write about my favorite queer coming of age movies!!
Dr. Pietras is a great professor with deep interests in queer studies (sounds quite generic but what I mean is he has great expertise and insights in this topic and can be helpful if you have the same interests). I would not say this is a gem, as I gradually got brought into genuine interests as this class proceeds, so I feel passionate about doing my work (hence, easier for me). However, even if you do not care about the topic, Dr. Pietras still gives out very very generous grades and provides REALLY great feedback. All I want to say that I literally love this class so much and you can also meet the queer fellows of your life. (TAKE IT)
