The class is a fun one, especially if the group of students is humorful. On the whole, the class does a pretty good job of making technical information accessible to people from all backgrounds, so this is an ideal class for ticking off the science distributional req.
this course can feel a bit repetitive as we approach the same material from different directions (reports, quiz, class discussions, and the final project feel like we are saying the same thing in different fonts) but it is interesting and fun to have discussions about the material and can help you change the way you approach science writing to make it more approachable (could also help you reflect on your own science writing)
This course covers a wide variety of topics relating to vertebrate evolution with a heavy emphasis on discussion.
To take this class you must be prepared to read and critique bad writing. Neil Shubin will test your patience every week. Nonetheless you will have many great opportunities to think critically about science communication!
This class is really interesting, and the TF + Professor Pierce are super passionate which shows through the class discussions, curated labs, and interactive website. I'd recommend – it's fun!
