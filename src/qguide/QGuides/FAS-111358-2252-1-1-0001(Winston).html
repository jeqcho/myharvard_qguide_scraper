What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Prepare to spend a significant amount of time reading and understanding the papers each week. It can be a lot of work, but so worth it. I learned so much about so many things.
This was probably my favorite class that I took in graduate school! Fred, Steve, and Scott are fantastic professors and super experienced discussion leaders. The format of the course is simple and the expectations are clear, which lets students decide week to week how in depth they want to go in both reading and discussing papers. Reading all the papers in depth is time consuming, but it's absolutely worth it for the pace of discussion that the class can take when everyone is plugged in. Overall, participation teaches you about the literal content in the papers as well as how to think *about* the papers (their logic, rigor, experimental design) which is an essential skill for a PhD level scientist.
Easily the best course in genetics at HMS.
This course is really amazing – the professors are very passionate about subjects taught and encourage alot of great discussion during the class. If you aim to join a genetics lab, this course will definitely be very useful for you. Additionally, students are required to present one paper at the end of the course, after which professors provide very detailed and constructive feedback. That being said, students do have to put in quite a bit of effort to read the papers every week to truly engage in the discussions.
This was everything I'd hoped grad school classes would be.
This was a class that I would take again if I could. I absolutely loved the instructors—and that was what made the class amazing. I hope that one day, I could be a scientist and an educator like they are.
I would definitely take this class if you want to broaden and deepen your genetics knowledge, and learn practical skills regarding reading and discussing primary literature, participating in and hosting journal clubs, and interpersonal and scientific communication along the way. Fred, Scott and Steve are fantastic teachers and mentors and I could feel the (professional) love, care and effort they put into the course and their students. They thrive in the classroom and want to see you thrive too.
If you choose to take this class, commit a significant portion of your time and effort to get the most out of it. I would recommend planning ahead to lessen your workload in research activities and other classes during the semester. I can strongly attest that the intellectual investment you make in this class will be incredibly rewarding.
