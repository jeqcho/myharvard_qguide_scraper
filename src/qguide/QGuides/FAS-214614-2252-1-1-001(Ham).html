Great class! Interesting material and super reasonable assignments that reinforced lectures.
This is by far the best class I took at Harvard. All thanks to the clarity and the enthusiasm of the professor.
If Donhee teaches this class again, take it! Donhee is one of the best teachers I've had at Harvard. Having taken physics classes where profs cram in tons of content at the expense of clarity and students' quality of life, Donhee's clear and slower teaching style made me feel like I was actually learning without having to stress a lot. Donhee's notes are very clear and pedagogical, even though they're handwritten. His assignments also basically just ask you to solve problems with answers in the lectures, which allowed me to actually study the material without stressing about whether I was doing it right or wrong. Even though I had taken graduate solid state physics before (P295A), I feel like I actually understood those concepts in Donhee's class. This class is also more focused on topology, which is great if you want to do research in that direction. Overall, could not recommend this class more.
As with all physics classes, start the homework early and engage with it, the problem sets were my most effective way to learn
Great course, you have to work hard to understand the material but the professor is very helpful and willing to answer questions.
Great course. Prof. Ham is a great lecturer, it's clear he cares a lot, puts a lot of effort into his presentation of the material. Very relevant to modern condensed matter, covers material at a very comfortable pace –– not too slow or too fast. Was a lot of fun, highly recommend.
Donhee is a great professor! Many interesting topics were covered in this class (a great extension of solid–state principles). Assignments were fair and relatively easy to complete by attending office hours.
Fantastic class! Donhee is a great lecturer and his notes are very comprehensive. Material is extremely interesting if you want to learn more about fundamentals of condensed matter systems.
I would highly recommend this course to anyone interested in flat materials or even just condensed matter. It's a fabulous introduction. Professor Ham is incredibly kind, puts in the effort to learn people's names, and gives great feedback. The course structure makes very difficult concepts manageable thanks to the homeworks structure.
