What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a gem course to take for any major!
You don't have to be pre–law, gov or even humanities in general to take this course! Professor Hochschild is a brilliant woman that makes this class so enjoyable! You have class once a week for two hours were you discuss the readings for the week. Warning, though, the readings are long, plentiful, and complicated. Start the readings a week in advance! The class discussion is a great way to dissect the readings and gain a better understanding of what it was about while also going beyond that and applying it to real life.
Professor Hochschild is incredibly kind & will make herself available to you when you need her!
This class is also cool because it's with graduate students (you can learn a lot from them!)
You will get out of this class what you put in. You will also get a fairly straightforward/inflexible picture of "American society," related to traditional institutions and highly–scrutinized populations
Hands down, this is one of the best courses I have taken at Harvard. Dr. Hochschild is an incredible professor and extremely knowledgable about the field of power in politics. I learned so much about different exercises of power within our political and social worlds, including power exercised by individuals, institutions, time, and random chance. It was also great to take the course alongside a diverse mix of undergrads, masters students, and PhD students, all of whom brought different perspectives and areas of focus to the course. If you have the chance, I would strongly recommend you take this course!
