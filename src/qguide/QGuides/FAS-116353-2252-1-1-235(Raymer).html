This class is quite the Expos. Dr. Raymer is incredibly helpful and sweet as a preceptor, exactly the person you’d want running an expos. She gave me very detailed feedback on my essays, and, for the final essay, looked again at my thesis the day before the deadline to see if it was in good shape, which was a really kind gesture. She did teach me a ton about writing and tips: make sure you have a sound, defendable thesis and link everything in your paragraphs back to it.

Now, the readings. There’s… a lot. Sometimes 200 pages for 2 days. You have to get good at dissecting the main points and bringing a potential discussion idea for each reading to the next class. Dr. Raymer very much cares about participation in class discussions.

It is not an easy expos content wise. The material is abstract and you really have to think. There is a lot of reading as well. But I feel if you manage your time well and enjoy reading about scientific communication, this class could be for you!
