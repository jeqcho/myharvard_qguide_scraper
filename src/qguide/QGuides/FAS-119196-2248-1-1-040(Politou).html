Take it! If you're hesitant, just know that this class requires a lot of work, especially if your math background isn't great. It'll feel too hard sometimes and you may be filled with regret every now and then, but by the end of the class, you'll be happy you took the class and definitely feel the improvement in your mathematics, and overall studying abilities.
Math 21A is a big time commitment, but the concepts are relatively quick to learn. The three psets a week are quite annoying to do at times and the workshops are so easy to forget to do (which can screw up your grade, so don't forget to do them). However, my section leader (Kenneth) is super nice and accessible for help, and the classes went by relatively quick (when they didn't go over time lol). The mini exam and two midterm structure was actually quite helpful to prepare for the final exam, so you were essentially forced to break up your studying over the entire semester –– which was a good commitment device and definitely saved some pain for the final exam.

I did think the final exam was significantly harder than any of the prior exams though.
To ensure success in this course, make sure you do all the worksheets and go to a section with a GOOD section teacher. The sections are so variable and getting a meh TF will change your experience so much in this course.

However, if you have a meh TF, just do the worksheets and self–teach yourself. The worksheets are very well designed. 3 psets a week is a pain and do not take more challenging classes if you are already taking Math 21A. I took this with Stat 110 and it nearly broke me this semester.
