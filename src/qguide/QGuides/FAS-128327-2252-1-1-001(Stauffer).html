Highly recommend!

This is a good class, and highly informative. It makes you incredibly aware of the persistent influence of slavery on America and American culture, even after its abolition. The class has a lottt of readings. You read at least four books (that I can remember off the top of my head), along with excerpts of countless other books and speeches. However, you get exactly as much out of this class as you are willing to put in. You can learn so much from this class as a student and as a person if you sit down and do the readings, or you can just pass the class by skimming if that's your goal. One thing to note: the class (obviously) covers a lot of graphic content, including photos, videos, and vivid descriptions. There are no trigger warnings. So consider this your trigger warning, and be prepared.
This course provides an excellent introduction to the Civil War, and I highly recommend taking this course. It provided me with a strong basis for the lead up to and aftermath of the Civil War, and I greatly appreciate its implementation of primary source materials and literature from the time period, such as Uncle Tom's Cabin and The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn. The vast majority of the content of this course was taught in an excellent manner, and it changed my interpretation of various texts from the time period, now that I see the historical precedents for influential works of literature.
