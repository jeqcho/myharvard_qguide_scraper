Comments
I was fairly skeptical about contemporary art going into this class, and I feel like I have a much better understanding and appreciation of contemporary art now! Through this course, I got to connect with so many pieces that I absolutely love and would not have known about otherwise. My one reservation to this course is that I was a bit frustrated with the disorganization of a few of the exams and assignments, but I would still recommend it to anyone with an appreciation for art and a desire to learn more!
I enjoyed this course. I really enjoyed spending time at the Harvard Art Museum every week. There are a lot of assignments and 2 exams, though I think the workload is reasonable. I feel like I left knowing the different questions to ask when examining contemporary artworks. I wish I learned more about the trajectory of contemporary art though.
If you want to learn more about art and you don't know all too much – please take this course! I learned a lot, and remember a lot of what I learned, so I'm grateful I took this course.
Take this class! You don't need to know anything about art going in. Before this course, my knowledge of contemporary art summed up to the Art Basel banana. This class showed me that contemporary art can speak to meaningful issues, and showcased a wide range of artists and works from around the world that I've developed a deep appreciation for. This class will push you out of your comfort zone; it doesn't shy away from difficult pieces and difficult to talk about issues. And Professor Lambert–Beatty is an incredible lecturer, every lecture tells a story and I truly looked forward to showing up to class each day.
Having students from various disciplines enriches the class discussion. I would encourage students regardless of major/program to take the class.
This is a really cool class with a lot of cool art to look at! Do be prepared, though, to be expected to understand high level concepts in media theory / philosophy / social theory to successfully engage with the class. Some of the issues that frame each lecture can be disorienting without proper explanation / prior understanding.
In this course there seems to be a right answer, which is tough when there seem to be many different ways to interpret art. This is also graded pretty tough, especially if you haven't taken an art class in the past.
