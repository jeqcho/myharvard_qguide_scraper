Make sure that you frequently ask what your standing is in the class, because the section leaders are not going to go out of there way to tell you where you stand and what you must improve on.
This class is a fun course. Rehearsing plays in Spanish is a very fun way to practice Spanish. You probably don't want to take this class with Jos as your section leader. He is never on time to class, doesn't print out the class materials for his students, never responds to his emails, is very strict, picks on particular students, and rarely gives As on assignments. That being said, he is also probably one of the best teachers in the spanish department and has improved my spanish so much over the semester.
I want to start by saying that the acting portion of this class was a cool experience. You have the creative freedom to act out whatever script you've made, granted that it follows the exam prompt. It was nice using props and acting out different types of scenes! Other than that, for TFs, try not to get Jose. If you do get him, try to switch your section time. He's a very harsh grader, considering that this is a language learning class. If you make a few mistakes, he takes so many points off, and if you hand in an assignment with no/barely any mistakes (only because you reviewed your work multiple times to avoid a harsh grade), he makes you rewrite your composition. Also, it's like he hands out grades randomly since he gave people the same grades for exams, even though classmates did so much better in one exam than the other. If you make a mistake when speaking Spanish, most times he corrects you, which is nice, but other times he would talk for a while about your mistake in a way that makes you not want to participate in class anymore.
