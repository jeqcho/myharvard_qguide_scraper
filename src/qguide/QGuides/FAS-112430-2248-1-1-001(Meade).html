12/10 for a gened, please take it!

Great class to fulfill GENED requirement!
It's a great class and I learned so much from it!! Great professor and TFs are also great. It can be a little hard to study for exams because so much of it draws from lecture material that often isn't in the slideshows, so be sure to attend lecture and take copious notes. Definitely recommend!
While I personally loved the course content and Brendan's lectures, it does require more memorization and an understanding of geography to the point that simply attending lectures and lab may not be enough to do well on the exams. Learning GIS and getting experience using these skills to make maps with actual real–world applications was an absolutely amazing aspect of the course that I enjoyed immensely. A two–hour lab once a week may sound like a lot for a GENED, but I really did get a lot out of it. If you don't want to go to lecture, take notes, or study for course exams, I wouldn't take this course because I think those are all necessary to doing well if you aren't familiar with the field. But if you are interested in the course content and actually want to learn some really useful and valuable information about natural disasters, please do! Just be prepared to study for the exams and get familiar with some maps :)
Really good gened, although it is more challenging than most. Even with a heavy workload, this class is very doable. You can get through your labs in class (mostly) and the GIS is a great skill to have. I would take this class again if I could.
This course largely leaves understanding the course material up to you and doesnt comprehensively guide you through understanding every piece of information. You have no graded assignments other than 2 exams and a handful of admittedly creative labs, meaning that most of the time you will be giving yourself feedback on how well you understand the course material via self–studying. Although the exams are pretty high stakes, they are manageable and can be conquered by starting to study early most importantly, showing up to lecture. Even though attendance is not required, I think you should really show up to all lectures if you want to do well in the course, as the slides dont always cover all content that you are expected to know. Nonetheless, a pretty engaging course, especially if youre into STEM.
The class will teach you something about natural disasters, which is interesting if you know nothing about them otherwise. That said, it goes into very little depth on the science and I don't feel I learned much that common sense or a youtube video wouldn't teach me. The labs were interesting but often tedious. Overall a decent course that you'll learn something in but not fantastic.

Most people don't go to lecture, exams are difficult, little to know homework, sort of interesting material, very nice professor.
Great course for a GENED with minimal out–of–class work, especially if you put in effort during the section times. The slideshows aren't very detailed/can be hard to follow along if you don't go to the lecture, so try going as much as possible and take detailed notes. Ask the TFs for help if you don't understand any concepts. The exams are weighted pretty heavily but the study guides cover almost all of the topics that will be on them, so as long as you prep in advance you should be ok.

I hate getting up and lectures were optional, but I never missed a lecture because Brendan was just so passionate and happy to see students interested, present, and engaging. No work outside of lab assignments and very interesting material. As long as you attend lecture you will do well on the exams. Take this class and please actually go as well!!!!

It is a very interesting class and you learn a lot of cool things. Make sure to study for your midterm as it is 30% of your grade.
I enjoyed this course. Lectures are fascinating, and Professor Meade is an excellent lecturer whose deep interest in and knowledge about what he teaches make every class fun, informative, and memorable. I found lab frustrating at times though. QGIS could be very finicky, but we never quite spent enough time on it for me to get a better feel of how to problem shoot, leaving me confused and less self–sufficient with the software than I would've hoped to get. It also felt to me that labs were often not run as efficiently as they could be. That said, labs were generally interesting and informative, if imperfect, and coupled with the fantastic lectures I found this to be a very worthwhile class to take.
