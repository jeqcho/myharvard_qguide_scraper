What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class does involve a lot of reading, so definitely be prepared for that.
It’s a good class, you’ll learn alot
1. Clearer prompts. For essays worth such a large portion of the grade, the prompts are impressively ambiguous. Going to office hours just to figure out what they're asking for is a must, or else you're just cooked. I went to writing center once and my tutor read the prompt and literally asked me "do you know what this is saying?" and I'm like "If I knew I wouldn't be here."
2. The structure of the class just doesn't make sense, the first essay is due way before any kind of writing activities are done in class, so if you've never written a scientific paper before you're on your own. Research only helps so much. Same for the writing center. A majority of the tutors are philosophy/English/hist&lit etc. so they most likely have never encountered much scientific writing either. There's a lot I learned about scientific writing in this class (which is great, because this is a seminar meant to teach scientific writing!). Unfortunately, it was at the cost of my grade, as there is no other guidance aside from just the feedback and grade on your paper after you already turn it in.
3. I love my preceptor, don't get me wrong, she is so sweet but the course setback is just not that ideal.
If you go in with the right mindset, you can learn some very valuable academic reading and writing skills that will serve you well in your future endeavors.
