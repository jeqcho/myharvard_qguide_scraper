The professor is great
Overall: ES 255 combines people from an electronic circuit design background (Team E) and from a photonic circuit design background (Team P) to create electro–optic transceivers, modulators, and devices. You do not need to know both domains! The point of the class is to expose you to both domains and how they can work together to create electronic–photonic circuits that leverage the strengths of both electronics and photonics (Team PE/EP). The course is largely structured around components and trade–offs of an electro–optical transceiver for communication in data centers.

Professor: The same Gage we’ve known and loved from ES 152 and CS 1480/2480 <3

Textbooks: N/A

Topics covered: electro–photonic integrated circuit transceiver overview (memory and sequential electronic logic, VCSEL and driver, optical coupling, optical fiber channel, photo–detector array, bandwidth/data rate/BER, optical receiver); optical waveguide model; electro–optic phase shifters, interferometers, and modulators (plasma dispersion effect, Pockel’s effect, digital/analog/linearized MZM, intensity/quadrature); transmission line impedance matching; directional couplers and micro–ring resonators; analog/digital/mixed signal simulation and design (a la CS 1480/2480) and photonics PDK; power/performance/bandwidth estimation/simulation of FETs, drivers, and receivers; VCSEL vs. MZM modulator comparison; optical network switches, active optical metamaterials, optical computing.

Course structure notes: Two psets plus a final presentation and project deep–dive on a topic of your choice. Though a lot can (and will) change since the course is brand–new my year.
It's a good course thebfirst iteration was a bit rough but I think it will get better overtime
This was the first year this course was taught, and I won't lie – this class was really poorly structured. I did not learn anything from this class. No homework, no labs, no office hours, no Slack channel, no nothing. We did have a final project and a report, but even then, I'm surprised we were able to submit something, given that everyone I talked to had no idea what they were going to do.

The professor, Gage Hills, is a very passionate and ambitious man when it comes to EE. His accomplishments and knowledge about computers are very, very admirable. However, this passion often comes at a cost. We often didn't have class because Gage was at numerous conferences for his research. We did not have any homework assignments because they were not prepared. No labs either. I was looking forward to taking this class, but I was met with disappointment. Not because of the lack of homework or labs, quite frankly. I was disappointed because this class felt like a second priority. Perhaps this is a greater, systemic issue at a research institution like Harvard, where research takes precedence over teaching.

All in all, if you want to take this course when it's offered again in Spring 2027, go for it. By then, Gage will have prepared some homework assignments, so class will be more normal. I just can't say I enjoyed it this year, nor can I wholeheartedly recommend ES255 in its current state.
