Stay on top of the class, always check that the autograder score is what you expect before going to bed, and know that grading is pretty inflexible, so make sure you have a fairly good idea of how you're doing and adjust accordingly as early as possible.
CS50 is CS50. Overall, it is a very well designed, well practiced course that gives a good introduction to coding (though not necessarily to computer science, per se). Unlike other introductory courses, it actually explains the (very, very basics) of how computers work, which is extremely important to know.

Spring CS50 has upsides and downsides. One of the upsides is being able to watch lectures at your own pace, whenever you want. I personally watched the lectures at .95 speed (David Malan is a fast lecturer)regardless, you really do need to watch the lectures.

Not having ability separated sections (which fall CS50 has) sucked. I entered with a good amount of programing background, so I got literally nothing out of sectionit was focused on things that I already knew. I don't blame the CA'smost of the students in the section were beginnersbut it was a big downside.

Spring CS50 also just felt very isolating. I couldn't find anybody else at my ability level, so I simply... worked on all the PSets alone the entire semester. Possibly this could be avoided if you're a social bee or you plan ahead to take the course with friends, but otherwise it's a big downside of the spring version.
If you don't need to take it for a letter grade, just take it Pass/Fail. Also, go to office hours every week.
This is not an intro CS class. If you have never coded before be prepared to put in a lot of work
This is a really great class for non–STEM and STEM majors alike. You learn a wide breadth of computer science concepts, and what is actually happening behind the scenes when you click on a software or use an application. Problem sets can be stressful but there are many opportunities to succeed and do well on them.
Take it
A necessary class to take. You will be immersed in a great community, get to know an amazing lecturer and learn a good amount about computer science.
start the psets a little early and your life will be a whole lot easier
Pretty light lift when taken in the spring, only thing you really have to do is go to section and complete a pretty easy weekly PSet
This class is time consuming, but it is incredibly fulfilling and worth it. It's perfectly reasonable to take it with no programming experience – they do a really good job of teaching everything from the bottom up.
