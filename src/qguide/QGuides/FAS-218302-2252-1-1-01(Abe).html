Professor Abe is amazing. Great lecturer, good sense of humor, passionate about the material, and very chill. Very understanding of circumstances if you may have any. I would highly recommend this course, as you can write about anything that interests you. I did my final paper on Atsumori–esque "haunted warrior" archetypes in modern media (Vinland Saga, Vagabond, Blue Eye Samurai) and how they embody Buddhist ideals and Noh theater aesthetics, and I had a blast writing it.
THIS IS SUCH A GREAT CLASS!!! Professor Abe is super patient and teaches with a lot of enthusiasm! He really makes the content accessible for people with no background in Buddhist theory, but you will still learn a lot even if this is your field of study. Super chill assignments that allow you to be creative and lectures are very interesting! Highly recommend!!
I took this for my Arts & Humanities divisional distributions requirement, but it ended up being my favorite class this semester!!! Professor Abe is incredibly knowledgeable, funny, and insightful, and he really cares about not stressing students out. There is SO much to learn from this class — it honestly changed my outlook on life and how I approach art/movies/books. HIGHLY recommend
Such a chill class. I especially loved the final project at the end because project specifications are open–ended (so you can really make or do anything)!
This course is an amazing and relatively chill course to learn a lot about Buddhism and a bit about Japanese arts and culture (specifically theatre arts like Noh dramas, though there is a bit about writing and a bit about Japanese visual art at the end). It does focus more on the Buddhism than art, but I still found learning about all the different concepts from all the different schools really interesting, even if it wasn't why I originally chose the course. The course was also super accessible, understandable, and interesting even for a CS/Stat concentrator like me with basically no experience in Buddhism or arts analysis at all. Also Professor Abe is just super sweet, understanding, and passionate about the topic, which was always a delight to listen to. The final project is super flexible and you can basically write either an essay connecting something to Buddhism or a creative piece. Would definitely recommend if you're looking for a beginner level course or elective on this topic.
Take this class! I love love love professor Abe. He's sooooo sweet and understanding and the class is genuinely super interesting and not difficult or draining at all. I took this class among some difficult STEM classes and it was such a good balance to reduce a little bit of my stress. The TFs are also so nice. Assignments are not hard and they are fun and enjoyable to complete. Take it!
