Comments
I enjoyed the tutorial–like format of the class: weekly readings and the opportunity to articulate our thoughts on the topic in a brief response paper preceded the class meetings. The class itself was engaged, and conversation was always lively: students were ready to engage in discussion, listen to the perspectives of others, and defend their views. I found the synthesis of economic frameworks with normative questions (often approached from a lens of public policy or political science) present in this class unique—it offered more possibilities for how we can approach politics. Professor Hart was a delight: he genuinely listened to the ideas of students—supporting or challenging them when necessary. He provided a good balance of explaining new concepts with free discussion. He also has great humor.
DO WHATEVER YOU CAN TO TAKE THIS CLAS!!! ITS AMAZING!! One thing is it is more work than any other semnar I have heard of, but it is so worth it. It takes a group of diverse students from Harvard's first year class and brings them together to debate relevant topics. Getting to meet and hear different people's perspectives was extremely beneficial for my semester and my learning overall. This course is the best example of academic vitality that I have experienced while at Harvard. I felt comfortable expressing my views and I think others did as well. Furthermore, I felt like I was surrounded by the nation's best and brightest and I learned as much from them as I learned from the course material. I think this course also did a great job of fostering interpersonal relationships between students, and I feel like I gained several friends from taking this course. Honestly, I think university officials and professors across the university should look to this course as the epitome of what a Harvard course should be. Professor Hart is so funny and is a great facilitator of discussions. To be honest, I think I was not passionate about some of the topics, and some of the topics felt a little less relevant to me. For example, I didn't love the weeks about government nudges or citizens assemblies, but overall the topics were so intersting. Professor Hart is actually the best he is just so wholesome and has an English accent which makes the class really fun to listen to.
I highly recommend! The discussions are super fun, and the readings are illuminative and challenge your own opinions of a subject (like proposing legalization of all drugs or open borders). Prof. Hart also has a big heart (pun intended) and often cracks jokes during class. Probably my favorite class freshman year.
Take this course, especially if you're a conservative. You will become sharper and certainly lead the dissent. The course atmosphere is great for debate. Do it!
THIS IS THE BEST CLASS I HAVE TAKEN AT HARVARD.
This class was fantastic! Professor Hart really cares about all of his students and offers such meaningful insights during the class. As a warning, this first year seminar is more work than many of the others (the readings can be 100+ pages a week on the heaviest weeks, plus weekly 3–4 page essays), but it's absolutely worth it. We had great conversations (with true variation in opinions along the conservative–liberal spectrum) during this class. Also – if you're thinking about concentrating in Econ, this counts as an elective (and it's pass/fail), which is awesome! Would highly highly recommend, favorite class I've taken at Harvard thus far.
I would recommend this class. Although readings can be long, this class is a very easy way to gain comfort with argumentative writing and shaping your opinions by listening and interacting with others. Professor Hart often supplements are readings with an economic concept to ground our discussions in, but we often veer into psychological and moral arguments during class. This class can be difficult and contentious at times, but this class is truly reflective of Harvard–style thinking and a great introduction to having dialogues about topics that matter. By the end of class, we could usually find one or two things to laugh about, even during a particularly strenuous debate. Would recommend for anyone looking to use economics to solve social problems.
