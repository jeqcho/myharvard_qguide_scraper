What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is a great introduction to computer science. Computer science isn't only about knowing how to code, and this course gives you all the fundamental knowledge.
Make sure you have the space in your schedule and mental capacity for the demands of this course. If you already have a full plate or you are not in a good space mentally, this may not be the course for you.
I would encourage you to take this or CS32, I think CS32 is a little bit easier, but CS50 is also very broad and fairly easy to succeed in.
Highly recommend taking CS50 off cycle! Being able to watch the lectures on my own time (and on 2x speed) was great, and still having the section component was very helpful. I had no coding experience prior to this course, and while I did struggle at times, it was very worth it. I had a great time working through the psets and doing my final project.
Don't stress if you don't catch everything in lecture. Things go too quickly to catch it all! During lectures, focus on the overarching programming concepts, not the syntax. You will have plenty of resources, both CS50–provided and otherwise, to pick up the syntax later. You will learn the syntax best by doing. Start the problem sets on your own and work for awhile, until you get quite stuck, before asking for help. You will be able to get through psets quickly and easily if you ask for help very often, but will not learn the languages and may struggle when it comes time to do your final project.
CS50 is a great class overall. You get to learn hands–on about a lot of computer science and programming. It definitely helps if you have learned some computer science prior to CS50. Regardless if you take it fall or spring, you will learn a lot. Especially if you take it in the fall, go to the events!
GREAT COURSE. It is difficult and honestly hard to follow along sometimes, but 100% worth taking.
If you’re looking to get the most out of the class you should really try to challenge yourself on the more creative independent projects/psets (scratch/website/final project).
