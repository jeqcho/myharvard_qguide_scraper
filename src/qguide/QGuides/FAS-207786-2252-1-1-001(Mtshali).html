Dr. Mtshali is an excellent professor and very knowledgeable about the subject matter. I would take another class with her, although I wouldn't recommend this one in its current form. I felt that a lot of the readings were unchallenging – for example, any of us (classmates) could have predicted that poverty caused poor health outcomes. Not that crazy of an idea. I think it would've been a lot more interesting if we mixed these readings with older interpretations of poverty that are clearly wrong, to show how we've gotten where we've gotten. Right now, this class is pretty superficial and the weekly news articles are very much like busy–work.
It's a great class! It's not the most intellectually demanding, but honestly, that's not always the most imporatant thing. This class really does some important work
This is a great class. Dr. Mtshali is very talented and knowledgeable about the root causes of poverty and how it intersects across different groups. The concepts you will learn in this class apply to so much of what you see in the news everyday and made me a much better consumer of information.
