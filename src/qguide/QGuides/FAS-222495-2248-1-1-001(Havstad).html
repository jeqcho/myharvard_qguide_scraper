This class was great overall. I loved learning about different ways to conduct oral histories and getting to do one myself. Sometimes the class felt a bit disorganized and it was not clear to me and many of my classmates when exactly certain assignment were due and how to complete them. However, I still enjoyed the opportunity I had in this class to be very hands–on with the final project.
THIS CLASS IS PHENOMENAL. The subject matter is fascinating, but Lilly is truly such an incredible teacher. The best I have had at Harvard. She is a gem – I have enrolled in this course because I took her class freshman year and just love being in her class that much! I would HIGHLY recommend. This class challenged my understanding and beliefs, and felt relevant and important in my life. It was challenging, but she is so supportive and equips students with the tools they need to success. 10/10!
Oral Histories is a must–take! The course load is on the lighter side, but don't let that predicate how you engage with the materials. The legacy and use of oral histories is always fluctuating, and this seminar expects the same flexibility of you. This method course encourages you to consider irresolution and personal agency in history; it encourages you to accept that judgment values aren't always what they seem. If Lilly is teaching the course, there's even more reason for you to hop in. It's a generously accommodating space filled with passionate students. Highly, highly recommend!
