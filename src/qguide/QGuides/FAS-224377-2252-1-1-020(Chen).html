The content this class covers is not itself very difficult. The exams don't focus on questions that are particularly difficult either. Just make sure to be careful with your numbers and be thoughtful. The time they give you is generous, so being methodical goes a long way here. The psets can all be completed with great marks without going to office hours or consulting any external resource. However, all of this depends on how confident you are with the core concepts.
This is a fabulous course that I would recommend to any students interested– it is a particular highlight for students in the HDRB or MCB concentration as it satisfies that Math 1B requirement. Prof. Chen is a wonderful, enthusiastic, detail–oriented and organized instructor who cares about her students and presents every course material with a purpose. While my math experience prior sometimes felt like what I was learning was not relevant to what I wanted to study, Math LS is totally different. In every lesson and pset, there is a direct connection in the problems to public health issues and students are also asked open–ended questions while reading brief article excerpts etc. With that said, the biological connections made in the course content with mathematics never feel forced. While the exams are no walk in the park, the course grading is fair in the exams (additionally the practice exams are exactly like the real exams). Most of all, the Math LS team are extremely supportive and the small sections with high TF/CA/prof to student ratio makes the class engaging. This was the first class– even excluding the first year seminars I took– where I felt I'd found a community, which especially being premed, can be a difficult thing. I would advise students though who are considering Math LS to be sensitive and self–aware of other classes they are taking as I also took this with PS11 and LS1B and it is not a class with a light workload or easily digestible content. The class as aforementioned is taught well and by all means, extremely rewarding and interesting but it is not easy and many of the concepts are based on modeling that students have not seen before in other math or biology classes. I would caution prospective students to not be afraid to take this class after their first year if that means they can dedicate more time to it because it is a class that deserves that and one that I will remember fondly.
If you take this class, go to office hours and start the PSet the day of. They are not excruciatingly hard, but they will require some help from office hours. It also just helps to have less pressure and makes it more likely that you will actually submit the assignment on time or at all. Things can build up quickly in this course, but Janet and the TFs are very kind and reasonable.
This class is amazing! Both Janet and the TFs genuinely want you to succeed, and everyone is so nice in helping you. I highly recommend going to the class's MQC every night there's a pset because it's so helpful working with other students and the TFs make sure that you're on the right track.
Take Math LS! It was by far the best way of completing my Math requirement, and a really excellent course that rekindled my love for math while showing its value in biological systems. The course is fair and incredibly well–structured, and Janet (the instructor) is one of the most talented instructors and kind individuals. The course work teaches you the fundamental of calculus (mostly differential equations, eigenvalues/vectors) that will give you the toolset to analyze single and multiple variable systems.
Take this class! I really enjoyed learning about most of the material. I do not yet know how it'll apply to the principles of PS2 (since I took this class for that reason) but I did learn a great ton. It is a very manageable class and the whole Math LS team wants you to succeed. Great course overall
I really loved this class and think it was so helpful in understanding biology (and calculus) better. It can be challenging, but doable if you're in some other hard courses. There are three psets a week, a mini exam, two exams and a final. I'm really happy I took this course over Math MB and feel as if I could self study and continue on to Math 1B if I have change my mind about my math pathway. The real star of this course was Janet (and the rest of the teaching). She is an incredible teacher and explains things in just a clear, concise way. You can tell she cares about the success of her students. I would rate this class 10/10.
