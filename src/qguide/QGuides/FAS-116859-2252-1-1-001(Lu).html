Wonderful class and highly recommended! Yue is a wonderful teacher and I thought the lectures were incredibly helpful. Workload is super light for a STEM class (I took this alongside CS 161, so you can probably trust me on this). 1 homework assignment per week that was fun, not too long and helped you understand the topics and didn't take more than 2–3 hours (unless you insist on LaTeXing all your assignments the way I do, in which case expect to spend slightly more time :D). 1 lab every two weeks that also takes ~1–2 hours to complete (don't worry about knowing Python, as the labs require very basic command of the language and there's an ungraded lab 0 that will help you learn the basics you need). Exams were reasonable and weren't too easy/difficult, and there's always a ton of practice material. Smaller class–size (compared to the massive Stat 110). I never needed to go to OH for this class, but even in the case you do it's probably a relief to know you won't have to wait in a long OH queue :) Overall, loved this class and I'm very glad it's offered as another option for the CS probability requirement. Highly recommended!

For the EE concentrators reading this, ES 150 is probably the easiest of the core classes we have to take. That being said, it is absolutely true that, if you have no prior experience with probability besides maybe vague memories from middle school math, it can be very unintuitive. For example, the entire "counting" section (combinatorics) was deeply unintuitive to me. Other than that, the class is genuinely great; of the professors who teach the core EE classes, Yue is probably the most effective lecturer of them all (some might disagree with me on this, but I definitely think he's a strong contender). The problem sets are useful for understanding concepts (probability is one of those concepts where you really just have to practice a ton to get the hang of it), the labs were more or less useless, and the exams were fair.

Overall, if you are an EE concentrator, don't fear this course. Otherwise, it is a much easier alternative to Stat 110 (at least from what I've heard; I won't touch that class with a ten–foot pole personally).
