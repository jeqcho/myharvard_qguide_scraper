What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is more focused on skills development than teaching overall virology. Be aware that you will not leave having learned more about viruses in general, but will be better able to read papers and ask questions.
This class isn't the most helpful when it comes to learning basic virology and some of the lecturers will treat it more as a research seminar than a class. This might be helpful especially since you're probably looking for labs to rotate in during your first semester but the lack of basic virology concepts might leave you wanting more. However, the paper reading component is very helpful and a great, low–stakes way to start developing the skills to approach literature review with a critical eye. The paper discussions are also a great way to start learning about techniques used in the field.
Overall it is a useful class that helps you develop scientific skills needed for outside of wet lab like critical thinking and writing. I would not say its the best class if you are only interested in learning about virology and different virus families.
