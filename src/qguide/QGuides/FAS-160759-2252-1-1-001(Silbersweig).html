Comments
a great class that surveys lots of topics from discussions of the intersection between neuroscience and psychiatry to the implications of understanding the brain on questions of consciousness and free will. The highlight was an opportunity to hear from a patient, which has helped me better understand how psychiatrists help their patients in addition to rethink of formulations of my own mental health. Leans broad rather than deep and discussions can be extremely unstructured but quite chill with reading/summarizing two papers a week.
This class is so amazing and so enjoyable. Be sure to do the readings!
This is such a good class!! Professor Silbersweig is one of a kind and is so knowledgeable about a lot of things
Dr. Silbersweig is highly knowledgeable and well–regarded in his field but you can tell he cares about his students despite his busy schedule. We had the opportunity to observe a clinical interview which was really eye–opening and meaningful. It was a sweet class, not only vibes wise but work wise too! ****
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! You will learn SOOO much about neuropsychiatry, no matter what your level is. If you have no knowledge on the field, you'll walk away having opened up a whole new world. If you have quite a bit of knowledge, you'll walk away more enriched and knowing about specific niche topics you probably hadn't explored before. Readings can be long and technical sometimes, but as long as you can understand something from it and become curious about it, you'll be fine!!
Take this class!! So interesting, and the instructor cares so much about the students.
Amazing class with great discussions! You will learn about what neuropsychiatry itself is, and the current advances in the field as well as the history of how this field emerged. Be prepared to share your thoughts on the readings.
This class is so cool! It's an in–depth look into the world of neuropsychiatry which is essential in joining neuroscience and psychiatry together to gain a more holistic view of the brain–mind interface and ensure that patients are treated from both sides of a coin – the physical and the mental. All of the readings, discussions, visit to BWH for a psychiatric interview, and final paper helped me learn more about this interesting part of neuroscience and through a neuroimaging lens – what could be cooler than that?! take this course!
Would recommend! The subject matter is interesting and Dr. Silbersweig is incredibly knowledgeable and passionate about the topic. Some of the sections felt a little disorganized, but overall a decent class.
