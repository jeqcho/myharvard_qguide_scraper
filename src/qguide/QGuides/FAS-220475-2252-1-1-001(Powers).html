Take the class! Super interesting unique and fun!
This is an excellent and super interesting course! Course content and material is the strength of this class, and Professor Davis's enthusiasm makes every class engaging.
I really loved this course. I think it fills a huge gap in the psychology department, which is psych's intersection with the law. I cannot recommend it enough. It is definitely a manageable workload, and I looked forward to the readings each week.
This class was a great introduction into how the criminal justice system affects people differently, as well as allowed for a lot of discussion on the course materials. Dr. Davis has a lot of experience, which really enriches our lessons.
I was not satisfied with this class. Dr. Davis has certain views, which she imposes upon the class. Her lectures are long tirades about material that is irrelevant to the readings, giving the appearance that she is often unprepared for class and is just "freestyling" it. She often complains about how much other stuff she had to do as an excuse for her inability to return assignments on–time. She is often three to four assignments behind in terms of grading, meaning students don't receive the feedback they need to improve on their future work. The readings are unnecessarily long compared to the amount of time she spends engaging with them. The mock trial is a cool concept but isn't very successfully implemented. That said, it's not a ton of work as a class.
Take this class!! It's a great class and you learn so much about how psychology and criminal law intersects.
This is definitely one of the more interesting psy courses to take! You get to learn a lot about the criminal justice system and how psychology plays a role throughout. Case studies and readings were very interesting, and the professor has a lot of experience in the field. With that being said, my favorite part of the class is the mock trial! My team bonded over the course of the semester, and we worked hard to find research supporting our side even though the jurors in the mock trial (and the case itself and the professor) were not really in our favor. The final paper felt slightly random (since most of the semester was spent on the mock trial) but was definitely doable. Overall I would recommend!
