This course is like 4 or 5 mini–courses on various topics related to quantum mechanics (e.g., QI, time evolution, symmetries, scattering).
This was a great class. Vishwanath is a fantastic lecturer and very willing to answer questions. I found the pacing a lot calmer than 251a, but the material we covered (especially quantum information) was still very fascinating. The lecture notes were a real highlight of the course for me; they were very organized and worked through the material thoroughly. Would definitely recommend taking this class!
Great class, very interesting and good lectures. The midterm is laughably easy, but the takehome final is quite challenging. Overall definitely would recommend.
The first unit on quantum computing was neat. After that the class became rather dull as we covered various methods in perturbation theory and scattering. The problem sets are straightforward, the in class midterm was very easy, and the take home final was more annoying than difficult. The lecture notes are very detailed, which is nice. Overall, 251b is a lot of nuts and bolts stuff every physicist should see at least once, but it's not the most exciting material in the world.
251b is very light and much easier than 251a. Ashvin is also a great lecturer and the lecture notes are fantastic. Psets are very light and biweekly. Only caveat is I don't think we learned as much as in 251a.
Start psets early and go to OH if you’re confused. Ana is really good at explaining things.
1. First off, this course is advanced quantum mechanics. However, 1 month is spent solely on basic quantum computing, with things like "what's an X gate?" that we did in Physics 143A. This should not be covered in this course, and left for an actual quantum computing course. This takes away valuable time better spent on advanced quantum mechanics topics such as identical particles or relativistic quantum mechanics.

2. Next, lectures are very dry, slow, and extremely boring. It is a struggle to get through lectures.

3. Covered way too much basic things, like what the Heisenberg picture is, what a group is, what a representation is, etc. This left only 2 topics I have not studied before, and were completely new to me: perturbation theory and scattering. We were also supposed to cover relativistic quantum mechanics, but ran out of time (fortunately I learned it in my quantum field theory class).

4. Professor had to miss or adjust 5 out of the 24 lectures, putting us really far behind.

Overall, I was very disappointed with this course and wished it would have covered more advanced topics. Fortunately it counts for a physics degree + masters, and is easy enough to add on as a 6th class without too much extra worry.
This was my first quantum mechanics class, and I really enjoyed it. However, I do think that the first half of the course is quite belabored. I wish there was more coverage of the latter topics, and the second half of the course ramps up during perturbation theory. Using Sakurai to supplement is a good idea
