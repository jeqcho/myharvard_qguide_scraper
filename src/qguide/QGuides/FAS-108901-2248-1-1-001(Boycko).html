Ok, here's the deal: Ec 1010A is awesome. I loved this class. If you are an Econ concentrator, you have to take either this class or 1011A to graduate. I don't know about you but I'm all for the path of least resistance, so I'd take 1010A over 1011A any day. Here are my suggestions:

1) Go to lecture and take notes. This may seem obvious, but if you're distracted during lecture, the class will leave you behind. Two missed lectures are enough to be lost.
2) Try out different sections in your first week! The teaching styles and approaches of section leaders make a real difference; figure out what works for you and stick with it.
3) GET THE TEXTBOOK AND READ IT BEFORE LECTURE. Or, at the very least, read it before the exams. Lectures are derived from the textbook, as are weekly MCQs. Familiarizing yourself with this will work wonders for your understanding.
4) Understand the Problem Sets. Ec 1010A encourages study group formation, and while this is a great part of the class, it can also be to your detriment. If you are relying on your study group for the weekly LPs and MCQs, exams will be tough. And exams are 70% of your grade :)

Overall, I loved Ec 1010A, and you will too! Have a good attitude about it: you have to take this class anyway, and you'll find it far more taxing if you're fighting a mental battle about going to class three times a week.
