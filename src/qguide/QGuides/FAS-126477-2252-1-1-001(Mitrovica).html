What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
TAKE THIS COURSE! I would definitely recommend having at least college–level math/physics under your belt before taking this course, as well as an introductory EPS course; otherwise most of the concepts will fly over your head. Jerry is such an engaging and kind instructor and I couldn't recommend taking this course with him enough.
I enjoyed this class. The content is very interesting and provides insight into both earth science/physics of sea level change as well as its paleo and modern human implications. I appreciated our discussions of archaeology and the chance to look at sea level implications for archaeological sites in our projects. Professor Mitrovica (Jerry) is an engaging lecture and is very approachable and willing to answer questions and provide further clarification. Natasha is also fantastic – she helped guide me through understanding how the concepts from class are applied in sea level codes.
This class covers a broad span of sea–level behavior, from the last interglacial to the modern day and even future predictions. Prof. Mitrovica is engaging and easy to follow, and he always finds another way to explain a thing if it is unclear. This course also brings together the human aspect of sea–level with its prediction models through the class projects, that involve thinking about how actual sites on Earth will be affected by sea–level change.
If it's still being offered with Jerry, take it! You'll learn a lot, laugh a lot, and will long for more.
