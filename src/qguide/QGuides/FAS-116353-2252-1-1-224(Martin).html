BEST!!! EXPOS!!! I think this class is great for people that are interested in learning more about constitutional law and US history. The class reads important SCOTUS cases throughout history (like Dred Scott, Plessy, Korematsu, Brown, Buck, Loving, Roe) while also reading modern cases about abortion and affirmative action. The course starts out fast as we immediately begin analyzing Dred Scott and brainstorming and writing our first essay. You should have a decent writing foundation from high school as this Expos class does not really give explicit instruction on essay writing skills and you begin immediately with drafting your first essay. Most of the writing skills are taught through discussion of the text and feedback you receive when drafting essays. Really read and annotate the SCOTUS cases you read carefully so you can participate in class and so it is easier to find evidence when writing your essays. The class is very discussion heavy and fast paced.

While the class may be on the harder side of Expos, Ross is a really great person and cares deeply about his students. He gives a lot of feedback on your essay drafts, and he is always available in–class and through email if you want help when drafting your essay. He teaches students to advocate for ourselves, so if you are struggling in the class or just want feedback on the work, he strongly encourages you to reach out to him and he will be there to help you. He also is willing to grant extensions on essay drafts and assignments.
