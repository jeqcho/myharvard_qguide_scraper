The course is great in teaching you calc I. It teaches you the how and the why. But it felt fast–paced to me, so I would recommend having taken at least AP Calc AB before in high school before enrolling in this course.
This class is very dependent on your class TF.... If your TF isn't a great instructor and you have never taken an intro calc course this will be a pretty frustrating class. This being said, I feel like people complained about this class more than it was warranted, especially with PSets. Yes, PSets can take hours. If you use your resources well (OH, MQC) they aren't bad for the most part. My advice is to go to Office hours, get to know your/other TF's and take advantage of those relationships. Get to know multiple TFs and find one who makes learning fun and digestable and go to them for questions.
Be prepared to spend a lot of time at MQC or during Office Hours trying to understand the PSETs. Office Hours are more helpful than MQC, but MQC is a great place to check answers and work and get less complex questions answered. Math 1A moves very fast, and make sure you have an adequate pre–calculus background before taking it. The examinations were hard in the sense that you really had to understand the concepts well to do well on certain problems. 3 PSETs a week is manageable. I would definitely study a week or even two weeks ina advance before each exam.
Prepare to learn by yourself
Having a strong foundation in calculus is really helpful for performing well in this class. My background in AP Calculus BC enabled me to grasp new concepts a lot easier than if I had learned them for the first time in this class. The class is fast–paced and the p–sets are pretty hard (so make sure to take advantage of office hours and MQCs)!! One thing to note – the exams were reflective of the content of the course (so no curveballs, thankfully!)
Attempt to place out. Higher level math classes are way more enthusiastic. three PSets is brutal.
