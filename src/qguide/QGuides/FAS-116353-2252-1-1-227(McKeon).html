Take this class. Dr. Keating is one of the best and nicest teachers I’ve ever had, and you will not regret taking his class.
BEST EXPOS EVERR. This was my top choice, but I initially didn't get it, so I filled out the section change form, and luckily, was placed into this Expos. Keating is such an amazing teacher and genuinely made the class interesting and fun. I really enjoyed the course topic(s), which is why it was my first choice. We touched on the intersection of race, ethics, and politics in sports, and I enjoyed writing every essay. Do your best to get this Expos, you won't regret it! This was quite literally the most memorable class I took this semester, and Keating is the reason why.
I came into this class expecting it to be a dumbed down Expos option, recommended by teammates as light on work. But it turned out to be the best class I could have taken, especially as an athlete passionate about sports. Dr. McKeon is truly one of the orators of our time. He is the most articulate and thoughtful instructor I’ve met of his age and has a PhD in classics and philology and a bachelors degree from Columbia: could be considered overqualified to some. McKeon brings a depth of knowledge that makes every class intellectually rich as he often ties our discussions back to that of the ancient Greeks. He’s also one of the kindest and most reasonable professors I’ve encountered—fair with deadlines, generous and thoughtful with feedback, and deeply committed to helping students grow as writers to benefit them after they leave the walls of Sever. The discussions are lively and welcoming, and he makes it easy to participate through open–ended questions and genuine enthusiasm. While the class, like many at Harvard, leans progressive, it remains a space where respectful debate and personal voice are encouraged. I will miss this class greatly and cannot recommend this section more if you have any interest in sports and their ties to the real–world.
