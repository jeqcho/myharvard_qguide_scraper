TAKE ANOTHER LANGUAGE. French is one of the hardest languages, and your absences are ridiculous and will be counted, whether you have a doctor's note or not. You learn a decade's worth of French in one semester and at a pace that will have you wheeling. Do not be quirky and take French because it sounds cool, just take Spanish.
The content is interesting, but sometimes meeting 3x/week seems excessive. pace could be quicker sometimes and focus more on consolidating grammar. Overall, it's a good experience and you can learn french in this class.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! It was so fun and engaging with the Virtual Reality aspect of it. You get to use VR headsets and go into a Visualization Lab. The class also tends to be small, which makes it great for engaging with peers and getting practice for speaking French. The instructor was GREATTT (although he won't be teaching it next year again).
If you’re considering taking this French class, I would say go for it—even if you’re a bit nervous or didn’t take French recently. This course is a great beginner–level introduction that offers a lot of accessible resources. One of the best parts was how it combined language learning with immersive cultural experiences. We explored Parisian life through VR and AI, which allowed us to virtually visit places like the Louvre, the Eiffel Tower, Musée d’Orsay, and Centre Pompidou. We also learned about Paris’s history and its innovation zones, which made the language feel alive and connected to real people and places. The mix of YouTube videos and interactive content made the class engaging and dynamic. Overall, the course offers a holistic approach to French—covering vocabulary, grammar, slang (like Verlan), and deeper cultural understanding. It’s more than just language; it’s about fully immersing yourself in French culture, which I think is essential to learning any language well.
Straightforward class that I learned a lot from. A pretty direction transition from French 10. You get exposure to some new grammar and tenses but a lot of energy is dedicated to informal conversations. I particularly enjoyed the conversations with Atim in each unit.
This is a great class! It's a bit less "introductory" than French 10, as you start to dive into French culture, specifically Parisian culture. There's a large focus at the end on the Gilets Jaunes movement which is something to be aware of (pay attention when it is first mentioned in class). Additionally, the last few weeks of class go really fast – we learn three new tenses at once basically, so it'll take a little bit of work to keep them distinct from each other in your mind (their forms are a little similar). With this class, you put in what you get out! I took French 10 and 11 as a senior and it was really refreshing, fun, and engaging! These classes left me wishing I could learn more French at Harvard!
