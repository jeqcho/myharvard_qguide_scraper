What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Incredibly worth it!!!
Stay on top of your work and set internal deadlines for yourself
I think writing a thesis is an incredibly informative and inspiring process. It made me understand myself in an academic context so much better, and even through my thesis isn't the best thing I have ever written, I am happy to have had this opportunity. That being said, it is incredibly hard! My biggest frustration is the lack of standardization between advisors.
The senior thesis–ing journey and fulfillment of independent undergraduate research (as the sole author of the research project) is unmatched and an incredibly rewarding hallmark of the undergraduate experience in the History of Science Department.
Writing a thesis is a rewarding process. You get to explore any topic and become an expert in it. Start your thesis EARLY. My best advice is to finish two chapters before winter break, rather than just one –– you will be grateful during break and in the second semester. Set firm deadlines and tasks for yourself to make sure you stay on track and don't procrastinate until the end (for example, I wrote 500 words, 4 days per week –– this meant each chapter was drafted in ~3 weeks, giving me ample time to edit and continue research, and I didn't feel the need to work on my thesis every single day. Do what is manageable and makes sense to you!) Seek extra feedback in the beginning as you craft the outline and proposal so that you have a strong foundation as you start writing.
