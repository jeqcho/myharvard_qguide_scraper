Everything you've heard about SCRB10 is true, both the good and the not so good. This class is ENTIRELY based around learning different experimental methods and knowing when to apply them. It is super important to gain a good understanding of all of these methods early on or you will get really lost. While this is a difficult course, it is super rewarding and the content covered in lecture is really interesting. There is a really good mix of lecture content and the experiments covered in class are really cool. There is also an interesting ethical component which encourages students to think critically about the ethics behind these experiments. If you take SCRB10, please, please, please master the experimental methods and do NOT focus on memorizing the specific pathways.
scrb10 is genuinely a pretty great class. It is not super difficult and you learn A TON. I found lectures to be super interesting and they were basically a survey of biological mechanisms in different systems of the body. Super interesting stuff about things I didn't know before. I kind of felt weird being one of the few non–premeds in that class. This was one of my most premed–y classes and it definitely gave those vibes. I feel like the class atmosphere was kind of dead and I never heard most people talk, but that's mostly a function of who takes the class and not the class itself.

The exams and psets were more on if you could understand how to create proper experiments to test hypotheses and critically judge if an experiment is demonstrating what you think it's demonstrating. Because of this, scrb 10 is more of a skills based class than a memorization based class. You won't need to study a ton, but you'll need to practice doing a bunch of problems and pay attention during lecture so you're building that skill over time. I think a lot of people didn't do this which is why the midterm grades were pretty poor even though the exams honestly were pretty chill.

Bill's a great lecturer, too. He's a pretty interesting guy with a ton of knowledge in many different areas of biology. Definitely find a time to chat with him.

I would recommend for anyone interested in the course or HDRB.

If you look at previous Qreports for this class, you will be extremely intimidated. Going into this class, I was. Some students said there was an extremely toxic culture, which is completely inaccurate. I actually found that most people were nice and very willing to discuss psets and writing assignments. While I was not one of the students in Chem 17, a lot of the students bonded over taking both classes. Other students say that it is too difficult. I also disagree with this. There are so many office hours every week. It also isn't a memorization class; you are not expected to remember most of the concepts brought up in class. Instead, we learn to apply them. This means that the psets are often more specific/difficult than the actual exams. If you can answer the pset questions on your own, you can easily do well on the tests. This is not to say this class is easy either. It is definitely taught in a way that most other classes are not. Also, this class is not a high time commitment on most weeks. The exams, psets, and writing assignments are paced out very well. The only work you have on some weeks may be outside studying if you feel like that's needed for you.

If you have no interest in research, this class will be more boring and likely more difficult for you. As a student wanting to pursue a PhD in stem cell biology or tissue engineering, I loved this class.
On top of everything else, the teaching staff is amazing. I absolutely loved my TF; she was super sweet and always willing to answer questions. Bill is a wonderful teacher, and Lauren is so so helpful when you're confused. I strongly recommend both Bill's and Lauren's office hours. Every pset week I tried to go to both, and they helped me figure out why certain answers were better than others.
Ultimately, this class solidified my desire to concentrate in HDRB. I would recommend it to anyone. Please take this class if you have absolutely any interest in stem cells or research around them.

The genetic tools are really important to know well to be able to solve problems and design experiments quickly.
