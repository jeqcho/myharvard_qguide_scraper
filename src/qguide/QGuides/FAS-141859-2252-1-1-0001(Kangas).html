What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
It is super helpful to have an excellent grasp on scientific literature prior to beginning this course. There are a lot of assigned readings and they can get lengthy and complex, so having a general understanding of the layout and rhythm of scientific papers beforehand will allow for more efficient learning. Also, pay attention to what the professor highlights about each reading in the weekly email. The themes he emphasizes often appear on the quizzes, worth a large portion of your final grade. That being said, as long as you read each reading in full and understand their implications, you will do well on the quizzes. There are no tricks.
If you're interested in psychology, drug development, and behavioral pharmacology using animal models, I highly recommend taking this class. The quizzes aren’t tricky, they're closely tied to the assigned readings, so as long as you read the papers carefully, you'll be well prepared. Make sure to come to class with questions and stay engaged; the discussions really help deepen your understanding. The readings can be a bit time–consuming, but by the end of the semester, you’ll likely be surprised by how much you’ve learned from Dr. Kangas and the classmates around you.
