What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Professor Remoquillo is WONDERFUL. She cares about her students, is so clearly knowledgeable about Asian American studies, and gives great feedback on assignments. She fosters a welcoming, inclusive, and open classroom environment, striking the right balance between lecturing/guiding discussion, vs. letting the students speak. Beyond just Professor Remoquillo herself, I also thought the course was well–structured. We only met once a week, and each week had a new theme. The readings consisted of theoretical work + films/books, and as someone who is not a Humanities concentrator, I felt like the readings challenged me, but not in a way that was unapproachable or daunting. I thought this course was a great introduction to Asian American studies, and I really wish there was a department at Harvard!!
Please take this course!! If you are interested in this topic, just take it. The readings can be a lot, especially for people who have not taken a HIST–LIT course before, because sometimes we are asked to read a whole book in one week. However, the extremely engaging and fruitful discussions we have during that two–hour block of our class totally make up for it. Professor Remoquillo is so good at striking a balance between allowing students to take over the discussion and still ensuring that all voices are heard. She has a wealth of knowledge and has a clear direction of where she wants the class to go over those two hours, but I really enjoyed this teaching style. I came into class every week feeling really excited because I knew I would walk away from each class having learned so much.
Prof. Remoquillo is an excellent teacher. She is incredibly smart and able to clarify difficult concepts to her students. She always strives to be helpful to her students and I've appreciated how open she is to our class' wide ranging questions, thoughts and comments. I've spoken highly of her many peers and would recommend everyone to take her classes.
You won't regret taking her course. I pinky promise.
This class is absolutely amazing. It is a very challenging course, not because the assignments are necessarily difficult, but that you will be challenged to engage deeply, personally, and academically with Asian American history and subsequently interrogating what is Asian American culture? If such a thing exists. Be ready to participate. But this doesn't mean knowing how to express yourself eloquently (which is important), but reading the texts closely and deeply, as well as being able to listen to your fellow classmates' contributions. You will learn a lot, you will be challenged on your thinking, your writing, and your own sense of self/understanding in many great ways. I feel that this class was extremely necessary for my personal development and what I learned from both Professor Remoquillo and my fellow classmates will stick with me for a very long time. I really love this class.
