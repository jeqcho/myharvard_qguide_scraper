The professor is great and she is very attentive to the needs and ideas of students. I liked some parts of the course more than others purely based on previous experience with certain topics.
Tracy is very dedicated to the course and prepares very detailed lecture slides –– there's potential to get a lot of this course. I particularly enjoyed the biological and social network applications. The final project is very open–ended, but Tracy meets twice with every group, which was very helpful in putting us on the right track.
This is a good course to take for your concurrent master's in statistics. The grade mainly comes from the final project, which you have plenty of flexibility over. Low workload, decent lectures and good material. Recommend!
An amazing class, especially for people passionate about text/network analysis. Tracy is such a good lecturer, funny and engaging, and extremely passionate and knowledgeable about the topics we discuss in class. It's a very rewarding experience!
You get out of this course what you put into it. It contains many advanced statistics concepts, so make sure you have the appropriate background but there is no pressure if you don't.
This is a really chill class with an awesome professor. You learn a lot of new topics, but your learning experience depends on how much you put into it. There are no formal homework assignments but only a final project at the end. Tracy is a great professor and is always willing to help!
This class covers a wide variety of very interesting topics, but it is easy to not engage that deeply with the material due to the lack of assignments besides the final project. The final project is very interesting and Tracy is very knowledgeable, so overall I would say that you get what you put into this class!
Great class, covers a lot of interesting topics. I found the network analysis section especially interesting. Would recommend if taking for the Stat concurrent masters.
This is a great class. Tracy knows this content intuitively and she communicates the difficult concepts in a way that is easy to comprehend. The final project is a great opportunity to apply some of the content in the class to a real–world problem, and I learned a lot from it. There are no exams, PSets, quizzes, etc. for this class, so staying on top of the content requires the discipline to study on your own time, but I do feel that I took a lot of value out of the class.
This is a great course if you want to learn about various topics within statistics. Professor Ke is a very kind and engaged professor. There is minimal workload throughout the semester, so you can really focus on listening in the lectures and creating an interesting project.
Tracy designed this course to be a low workload course, and it is. The lecture material is quite technical so it can be a bit difficult to follow the details of the material week to week but since there aren't any exams or homeworks, this isn't a big problem for your time in the course. I think the biggest benefit of this course is that you get space to work on a project. Text modeling and network community detection are both interesting methodologies with interesting outputs. If you've got a project in this domain that you want space to work on I highly recommend taking this course.
Very cool work of Pf. Ke's research, she is also very nice and funny. Need to be comfortable with lin alg, stat inference and ML to get the most out of lectures but not fully necessary to do well, if you choose to focus on only the units that interest you for the project. Really great
There aren't really any expectations in this class beyond the final project, so in that sense it is easy. However, because you're not being assessed on the material it is easy to check out and not really learn anything.
Great class! Dr. Ke gives great lectures on topics that are very relevant for data scientists from across many fields (I'm a neuroscientist and found the topics very applicable for my work). There isn't any busy work or exams, just one final project so you have the space to focus on learning the material without stressing about a heavy workload. The light workload is also great for PhD students who have to spend a lot of time on their research outside of classes.
This was a great course! Prof. Ke is a good lecturer and very helpful with feedback on the project
