If you've taken math at Harvard before I don't think this class is that bad. Better than 1B in my opinion. If you go to section and actually pay attention I feel like you can do pretty well. Study early for exams though, you need more than 2 days to full review all the concepts because there's a good amount of memorization involved.
This class is awful. It's just one of the things you need to get through to move on to higher math.
Make sure you remember the theorems as this course progresses, before the final.
Take this course! It is challenging, but very doable if you come in with a strong foundation of Calc I and II. It was my first math course I took at Harvard, coming in having taken AP Calculus BC, and I had a positive experience. The small–section setting makes it much easier to learn than some of the other large–lecture math courses, and there are lots of opportunities for support when needed.
Math 21a has been one of the most satisfying courses I have taken so far. It is structured really well, with each topic building on the previous ones, so that by the time you reach the end, you really feel as though you've learned something and understood the scope of multivariable calculus a bit more. Amadeus is a great section leader––absolutely try to get him as a teacher if you can. My high school math classes were often poorly constructed and taught, so this was a refreshing break to that pattern in that although the course was not easy for me, I felt that through listening and participating in section, thoughtfully doing the homework, and doing the practice materials provided before assessments, I could get a lot out of the course. The expectations set were reasonable and rigorous. Definitely utilize the MQC––I both made friends there and got a lot out of the collaborative component on PSETs. The course feels as though it is designed to be done with others, so having a group of study partners is great. I also appreciate how widely applicable the skills in this course can be––if you're not sure what you will be concentrating in but might need math, this is a good course to take.
This class is a very useful and practical course in multivariable calculus. We emphasized both the understanding as well as some of the key theorems that are necessary to effectively apply these topics to real–world applications.
Definitely a difficult class but the natural progression from IB Math AA HL or BC Calc. I thought this class was so much fun and which I had spent more time struggling with the PSETs and grasping the content. Everything you learn in this class is so useful and you really begin to see the applications of it in other fields. There is a lot of material to learn but do not fall down the slippery slope of relying on MQC or other people to help you finish the PSETs, make sure you are actually learning the content and just comparing answers to make sure you understood things. Also, don't fall down the slippery slope of skipping class, it's so bad when you do that because everything builds on each other and the PSETs really challenge you and make sure you learned everything in class. I def recommend this class and wish I could take it again without skipping any class and studying every day the cool stuff that you learn. One of the most important classes I've taken here and that's despite not being sure I got a good grade overall. The amount of things you learn is insane. ALSO, don't be afraid to jump around sections until you find a TF that matches your learning style. I absolutely hated the teaching style of my first 2 TFs and wasn't learning anything. Only when I switched to my third TF that I noticed that the problem wasn't me LMAO people just stick with the bad sections because of scheduling conflict or they're too afraid of switching, but trust me if you don't like the TF, you'll learn so much more and things will be SO much more fun with a different TF. They def make or break your experience in Harvard math classes in general (this is coming from someone who took Math1a, Math1b, Math21a, and will now do Math21b).
This is a difficult class that takes a lot of work and time. I think it's important to attend class and don't fall behind because it is very difficult to catch up once you do, because it's such a fast–paced class. The material is difficult and it usually takes some time to fully understand the concepts. I suggest studying as much as possible for the midterms and obviously the final, but putting in a lot of effort on the midterms is a good investment both because if you do well on them they can count for a large portion of your grade but also once the final comes around it is nice to feel comfortable about the material covered before the midterms.
If you took BC calculus in high school, you should be fine in this class. No need to take 1a or 1b
