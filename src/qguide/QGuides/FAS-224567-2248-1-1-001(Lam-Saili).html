Do the readings if you want to be able to engage. Even if you can only skim them, make sure you have a solid basis of understanding before coming to class. This isnt a class where you can wing a discussion. Kiran is also a hugely helpful prof when it comes to writing feedback. Although her grading might seem harsh in comparison to other humanities classes you may have taken, her grading isnt outside of the norm for hist and lit classes. If you embrace her feedback and critique as room to grow, you can flourish as a writer. Take this class if you are genuinely interested in feminist theory. It is not a gem and is a class that deserves your time and thoughtfulness, so make sure you only take it if you feel you have the capacity. That being said, the workload itself is manageable and Kiran is very flexible about deadlines.
I would tell them to ensure that they budget enough time for this class each week, as some readings will take you longer than you expect. This is extremely worth it though, as the readings expand your thinking so much further! It will also obviously help you be much more prepared for discussion.
This course has so much potential to be a space where WOC identities can truly be represented, discussed, and celebrated. As her first year teaching, Professor Lam–Saili is a skilled and passionate educator who cares deeply about uplifting this powerful history.
