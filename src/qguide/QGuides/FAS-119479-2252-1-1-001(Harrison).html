Take this course for QRD, Prof Harrison will match your effort and ensure you are sound going into the final project.
It's a great class for humanities students who would like to fulfill their QRD.
It is a great class if you plan to develop a full survey for your thesis or for other reasons. Loved it!*
Take this class! Professor Chase Harrison is a great professor who is enthusiastic about survey design and methodology. If you haven't taken a QRD class, this is a great intro course. You are not expected to know statistics or R programming, but it would be useful if you have prior experience with those tools. Also, the workload is very manageable if you have 4 other classes.

Note that you will work with a group of individuals for the entire semester to plan out a final project survey research design. It sounds like a lot, but it is feasible. My only caveat is that the class may be dull at boring at some points. It isn't Professor Harrison's fault, but learning about survey design can be very boring. Doing the actual hands–on stuff is where the fun begins.
Take this class for QRD if you're not super comfortable with math. Assignments largely lead up to your final project, making it pretty easy, though the readings can be kinda hard to understand at times. Professor Harrison is very knowledgeable and passionate about the course topic.
A good way to finish the QRD for people who are scared of math/numbers. There is also a lot of flexibility with the final project if you have other research interests/ideas.
professor harrison himself has a great amount of enthusiam for the subject and i respect that but somehow not an ounce of it rubbed off on me throughout the entire semester. the only interesting component is the survey project so make sure to pick a topic that you are actually passionate about.
This course is great if you are looking to fulfill your QRD requirement and are afraid of numbers. There is minimal computation. This is also a great GOV elective. This class does teach you a lot about survey methodology and you will leave the class with a complete survey and have analyzed the data. The TF and Professor will help you throughout the process.
If you want a chill class, this is for you. If you want something rigorous, especially from a methodological and statistical perspective, this is not the best use of your time. I found this class really frustrating because I didn't learn much, I especially didn't learn much in the field of government and statistics, and yet there is plenty of busywork.
I highly recommend this class to those looking for a non stressful class to complete their QRD! No math background necessary at all! I also highly recommend if you have a thesis/other research project that you want to design a survey for, because you can spend a lot of time refining an already existing researching question.
Professor Harrison is the best! He is extremely enthusiastic and caring, creating a low pressure learning environment. Highly reccommend especially if you are looking to fill your schedule with lower stakes courses.
take it, you won't regret it. especially as a QRD requirement.
