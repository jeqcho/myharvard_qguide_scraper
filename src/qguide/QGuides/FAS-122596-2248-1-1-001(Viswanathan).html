This course really helps you begin to understand some of the major past and current topics in science that capture attention. We hear from such great experts every week and the intimate experience with such a small class is very valuable. With a mix of students from various disciplines who present papers every week, it also provides an insightful experience.
Take this class but only if you really want to learn. You get what you take out. Also, make sure to be paired with a good presenter. A couple are very bad to work with so reach out before.
Excellent paper–reading course (good exposure to various biomedical topics – primarily cancer as of 2024 Fall) + low weekly workload (~2–3 hrs max) with nearly entirety of grade coming from a paper presentation you make
You get out of it what you put in. If you are looking for a low workload course then you can coast through the semester picking up key learnings here and there. But if you are willing to read the papers and engage in the lectures then you can learn a lot. Either way it is a great class!
Excellent reading course. Workload is minimal: 2–3 papers/week with one presentation during the semester. The format of bringing in faculty experts to present their field and work is engaging and super informative from a science and technical perspective.
You won't be disappointed taking this course! I learned so much new knowledge and was constantly inspired by novel research questions that still need to be answered :)
This course is great! Pretty light workload, but a lot of scientific paper reading. Make sure to have a strong background in developmental genetics and cellular biology. There is a lot of talk on cancer and neurobiology, so courses like SCRB 50, MCB 60, and Neuro 80 will probably be super helpful.
