Gage Martin is a very nice person and was lovely to get to know, but I would not recommend this class if she is teaching it. For reference, I have not ever learned any kind of proofs. She used all of the materials from the previous professor(Dr. Chen) and there are things on the syllabus that are not offered like "problem sessions". She posted Dr. Chen's syllabus, but never her own. Even worse, the homeworks reference worksheets sometimes in problems. I had to skip over a problem because you needed the worksheet reference to do it. Gage used these worksheets to structure her classes, but never handed them out to us or even posted them on canvas. I just do not understand why. Having something to look at and follow along with keeps me engaged in the class and it also gives me another thing to go over when studying and doing assignments. When I asked about the worksheets, I was told that she could send me the ones that I asked for instead of offering to just post them. Overall, I do not think that the teaching style was for me. It was very disorganized and all over the place. You don't really have to go to class. I tried to because I was a little lost, but it did not help at all.
Go to classes and take notes. Do homework. Review notes and homework solutions before exams. You'll make it.
Math 121 is an excellent introduction to pure mathematics while also concerning itself with topics that are useful in many other areas of math and science, both theoretical and practical. Before this course, I had never written a proof (though I had taken a computational linear algebra course, as the syllabus recommends). I felt that the problem sets, lectures, and textbook (Axler's Linear Algebra Done Right) were highly instructive without being overwhelming. I'm now considering taking another pure math class or two. Give this course a shot!
This course is great! And Gage is an amazing lecturer.
