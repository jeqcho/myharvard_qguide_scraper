What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This feels very much like a graduate–level class which focuses on many individual researchers and their frameworks. It really pushes you to consider the methodologies involved in understanding pre–modern historical and theological concepts. Really ambitious in scope, tries to cover a lot in terms of time periods and readings can be difficult. However, the workload is not too bad, and the class being once a week is great! Individually driven, as most reading outside class, and then class is for debate, discussion, and to add nuance. It is not only a historical overview but also an advanced level of legal, philosophical, and theological analysis. People come from diverse religious and cultural backgrounds, adding many different perspectives. It also broadened my understanding of what Islam is and what it has looked like. Challenged my ideas of Islamic 'orthodoxy' even as a Muslim.
I loved this class and would highly recommend taking it –– you learn so much and the course materials are so engaging!
Do not pass up this opportunity to learn, even if you think you don't have the necessary background. It is worth working towards to succeed within the course! Your mind will be blown, over and over again.
Take it! Professor Boylston has such a fun teaching style that makes historical/religious/cultural concepts seem much nearer and more relevant to our context. Also Professor Boylston puts incredibly fascinating ideas from the past/re religious diversity and Islam in conversation with broader issues dominating public discourse today, making the course feel especially relevant!
This class demands a lot of time outside of class, but was worth it for my academic goals (but based on conversations with other classmates, that might be a choice based on how much you want to/can put in). I would highly recommend to those interested in history, even if you think you are not interested in studying religion. You will get consistent feedback that will help you grow as a thinker/scholar.
The professor is very thorough and rigorous in his approach to the class, in that we approached the topic from many different angles giving students a much more robust and nuanced understanding of the topic. Professor is incredible – very passionate about making us great scholars and just more informed people on a very important topic. And he is obviously incredibly expert. Fundamentally changed how I understand Islam in all honesty. We talked about its origins as a religious reform movement as opposed to a discrete new faith, which fundamentally changes my understanding the religion and the following course materials. Students from all backgrounds religious cultural and intellectual should take this class. Really an incredible exemplar of what the humanities can do in the classroom.
Take it, especially if you want to understand more about how to think about challenges to dominant understandings of Islam.
