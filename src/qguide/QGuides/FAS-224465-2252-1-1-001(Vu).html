This course has many small assignments that are not challenging but require diligent work. The course material is interesting; as the course is group and small–group discussion focused, the success of the course is largely dependent on your peers (I had a positive experience with my peers).
It is mostly student based teachings which can sometimes be slow and not as exciting however the discussions on the topics are fun
TAKE THIS CLASS. Honestly feels like we just get to talk about things and share life experience all the time. The workload isn't bad because Prof. Vu gives you feedback on the essays before submitting and the rest is just making sure you do the discussion posts. As long as you keep up with it you'll be fine. There are also 2 extra credit opportunities as well. Prof. Vu is also really sweet and cares about the topic. Honestly, as long as you show up prepared and ready to talk you're gonna have a lot of fun in this class.
I loved this class! I took this as an elective and I highly recommend to anyone who is interested in the topic! I really enjoyed the weekly presentations because this gave every student the opportunity to learn the material and present/teach it in a way that would facilitate understanding for other peers in the class. I also really enjoyed the reflection papers, particularly the ones where we could analyze our own lives and relate it to the research we were learning in class. I thought this was a creative way to not only learn the research, but to apply it to my own life. I was able to identity some of my negative psychological patterns and look at the research to identify how I could adopt new strategies to relieve stress caused by identity threats and to further develop personally.
Great class to learn more about identity––where it comes from, who/what shapes it, and how to deal with threat. The course name/syllabus is a little ambiguous but class discussions were actually really interesting and always tied back to the greater sociopolitical climate. Papers were not difficult, grading was fair, and the pre/post class responses were completion–based. It felt like a 2 hour yap session based on psychological/clinical research. Dr. Vu is also so kind and willing to answer questions!
This is an excellent class for those who want to explore psychology and reflect on their own lives. Great class for senior spring especially to balance workload while thesising since the small assignments throughout the semester are manageable and there is only one big final paper. Come into class ready to share and be open or you won't get as much out of it
I definitely think this course is worth taking. You get what you give. It feels a little theoretical/big picture, but the more you put in, the more you learn.
I HIGHLY recommend taking this course! The assignments are essentially writing about yourself, which is such a fun introspective exercise, and classes are centered around student presentations of the readings for the week and discussion questions about how the readings relate to your life. I have learned so much about how people operate and about myself in this course, so if you're even remotely interested in thinking more deeply about how you view yourself, TAKE THIS COURSE!
While the course material is interesting, the course itself has many different areas where it could improve. Lots of coursework for not as much learning/growth as I have gotten from every other course I have taken so far in the psychology department.
Overall, really good course that had amazing discussion. Sheds light on a lot of the identity threats people and groups experience on a day to day. It helped me to understand human behaviors and reactions better. Stay on top of the short essays because they can sneak up on you if you don't plan in advance.
