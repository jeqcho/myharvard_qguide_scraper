If this class is offered again, TAKE IT. PLEASE. This feels like essential learning, like everyone should take this class. You will be challenged and pushed in so many ways, you will be emotionally moved, you will learn so much about yourself and this world we live in. Every component of this course is so brilliantly designed and executed. You will question and think in ways you never have before. You should gain more compassion toward women and their complex, diverse experiences. You will explore different types of writing, perspectives, and ways of analyses. Whether you're more compelled by science, journalism, narratives, or theory, it's ALL here, covering SO many different topics across feminism and the lives of women. You will learn so much. I cannot praise this class enough, truly one of the best if not THE best class I've taken here. The faculty was phenomenal; Professor Thompson was absolutely fantastic teaching this course and she will be missed so much. I am so happy I took this course. take it take it take it.
When the past Qguides said everyone should take this class, they were absolutely right. Unfortunately, I don't think you'll be able to do that anymore, because I think this is the last year it's being taught. That being said, this course dramatically transformed the way that I view the intersection of misogyny, gender, capitalism, class, racism, and more. I have begun to see the signs everywhere, baked into our daily lives and routines. This course should be mandatory as part of a liberal arts education. There were weekly reading discussion posts that were very fun to create and read from others. There were a lot of readings to base it on and a lot was expected from us when writing them, but they did a good job at pushing us to think about the bigger picture/outside the box. The assignments were also very fun and reasonable. There were 2 visual and language analysis papers and a final creative project or policy reform paper. Dr. Thompson and Vanessa are some of the best thinkers I've met at this school, and having a conversation with them will leave you feeling so fulfilled but also craving even more knowledge and justice. They have left such an impact on me, and I will be forever grateful I took this course.
If this class was being offered again, I would wish everyone to take it. It's so deeply engaging, relevant, and thought provoking. Prof. Thompson is so so incredible and you learn so much about the world AND yourself.
It is a great shame that this course is ending. Professor Thompson dedicated 10+ years to this and her hard work is evident in the quality of this course. She taught and discussed each topic with an educated background and knowledge, yet always approached each topic with an open mind. And, she taught us to do the same. I leave this as a more educated feminist and individual , who is more informed of feminist theory and issues. Additionally, I discovered a lot about my own personal beliefs and opinion, as well as how to properly express them. Overall, it was satisfying to see myself become more aware and openminded about feminist issues in this course. I will miss this course deeply. It was a honor to have be taken this course under Professor Thompson.
This is fantastic class. If you are at all interested in feminism, you should take it. You WILL NOT agree with all of the readings, or all of the Professor's takes, however this course is just as foundational to your education as an intro to Econ class or an Expos class: this isn't just information gathering, rather this is paying time to a whole new perspective through which you can view the world, and other areas of your educational experience. I really recommend it from a personal development perspective, especially for young men.
Solid introductory class if you're curious about WGS in any way. Sometimes it felt like the topics were a bit obvious or on the nose, but for those who are new to classes like this I thought it made it very accessible. And also, if you're looking for deeper engagement in the ideas talked about in lecture, the readings provide that in a very generative way in my opinion. You can do a little or a lot of the readings and do just as well in the course, but you will get the most out of the course by doing as much of the readings as you can, they are really interesting and represent a variety of perspectives (academic, journalism, op–ed, etc). Wish this class was sticking around, Professor Thompson is great and will be missed.
It is a REAL shame this is not being offered again. It is very upsetting.
Take this class. I think everyone should take this class no matter where your interests lie, because it truly changed the way I view societal structures and how women fit in them. I was a little concerned about the diversity of students enrolled in the class, and it isn't perfect, but it is definitely getting there.
This was an incredible class. Every lecture was fascinating and engaging, and difficult, and I felt like I grew so much not just as a student but as a thinker and writer. Phyllis is absolutely rigorous and Vanessa hammers it home in section. Can't say enough good things about this class, but it is what you make of it, so do the readings and come ready to engage.
This is an exceptional course and it would be a terrible mistake to pass up the opportunity to take this course. Prof. Thompson is the most brilliant professor I've met at Harvard and she is an exceptional instructor and lecturer. This course will change the way you think and how you see the world. Unfortunately, a glowing anonymous review can't do justice to how fantastic this course was but I would just say that if you have 1 percent of interest in taking this class, you should do it. And if you have the same experience as I did, you'll find that it will become the best academic decision you'll have made in college. TAKE THIS CLASS!
