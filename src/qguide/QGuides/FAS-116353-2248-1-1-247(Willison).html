This is a high–quality Expos class that you should take if you are interested in philosophy of language, literary theory, and philosophy regarding the meaning in and of life. If you are looking for a "gem," this class is not for you.

From the perspective of a humanities student who enjoys philosophy, this class made me learn something, both in terms of content and writing skills, which made it worth the time for me. However, don't expect this to be a philosophy seminar where you dive very deep into each of the readings. A lot of people in your class also might not be that passionate about the material, since this is a required class and not a self–selected group of students.

Given the fact that this is a writing class and not a philosophy seminar, Rob is faithful to the content component of the course. He wants you to understand the readings, which include difficult material from Frege, Wittgenstein, Grice, Putnam, Barthes, Camus, Nagel, Wolf, and so on. I think you should take this class if you are going to treat the work seriously: it would not be a waste of your time, there is little busy work, and you get to actually grapple with philosophical writing in a way that feels both challenging and rewarding.

Rob is a great teacher who is funny, understanding, and also knowledgeable. He is very likable and will also seek to foster a sense of community within the classroom, something I appreciate a lot. He has high expectations for his students in terms of the level of clarity with which they express themselves in writing. The way he talks and thinks is meticulous and rigorous, consistent with his expectations for his students.

All in all, this was a great class, but only take it if you are willing to put in more work than your peers are for their Expos courses.
