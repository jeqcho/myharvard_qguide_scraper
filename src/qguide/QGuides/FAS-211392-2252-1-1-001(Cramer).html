I highly recommend taking this course, even if you are not that familiar with sports. Professor Cramer does an excellent job at explaining everything, and for those with a passion for sports, lectures will be some of the most entertaining classes you will have in your Harvard experience. As long as you put the work in and attend lecture, you will do well in this course. The problem sets, exams, and final project are all very manageable and there are tons of office hours and other resources to help you do well. If you are even the slightest bit interested in sports, I cannot recommend this course enough. It was one of my favorite classes I have taken as you get to talk and think about sports in a completely different way.
Take this course.

If you have ever gone to baseballreference.com, or hockey or football or other sport equivalent, this course will be the most impactful you have ever taken and probably very intuitive. I have told just about all of my hometown friends how I am rethinking my approach to certain sports and strategies. It truly has made me rethink the sports landscape in general. Lots of interesting nuggets and Judd is the man – could probably rattle off the starting rotation and lineup for most World Series winning teams in the 21st century.

For those who may not be into sports, I am unsure how this course will be. The learning curve may be slightly more steep if you are unaware what ERA or a batting average may be, but absolutely approachable and should not turn you away. Plus, it is all about sports – there are a million resources for you to learn if you find yourself struggling.
