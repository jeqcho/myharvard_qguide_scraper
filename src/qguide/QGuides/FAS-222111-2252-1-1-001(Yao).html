Take this freshman seminar! Professor Yao is an incredible lecturer as well as researcher, and he does a really great job getting to know his students and taking over pizza. Definitely a highlight of my freshman year, I learned a lot of quantum information theory that will serve well in future classes. I must warn you this is a real PSET class and it's quite a lot of work, but if you enjoy physics and have the time it's well worth it.
This class if fun. However, the psets are real psets and do take time, and you will learn more if you actually try on the psets. Some of the content on the psets are not taught in class, which makes them more challenging, but in the end I could figure things out. Professor Yao is a great lecturer, and his choice of topics were very interesting. This class is good if you want to learn more about quantum computing and to have a different perspective on quantum mechanics.
If you have any interest at all in quantum computing and quantum information, take this class! Norman is an incredible teacher, and this class is a wonderful experience with relatively little work.
Professor Yao is amazing and so passionate about this topic. We covered a lot of very interesting concepts in this class. It's also more work than most freshman seminars, with short weekly psets and readings. Depending on your background, you will probably be able to get more out of different aspects of the course. I have more of a background in math and CS and so could appreciate more the theoretical foundations of quantum computing and information theory, while other people could appreciate more what was going on physically in various implementations of a quantum computer.
This is a great class – Norman Yao is a great professor, it is probably more difficult than a typical first year seminar, but chances are there will be people you can work with to make sure the psets get done. Yes, there are psets, but we also got pizza in class every week and then talked about quantum stuff. Overall a very fun course, not harshly graded at all, would totally recommend. Going into the class I had very little knowledge of anything quantum–related, but I came out having a very good general understanding of how quantum computation and quantum sensing works.
If you have any interest in quantum computing TAKE THIS COURSE!! I came in as a student with a slightly less advanced physics/math background (I'm planning to do engineering, not pure physics) and I had a great time. Prof Yao is very accommodating for students of different backgrounds, and I found all the lectures to be very interesting and exciting. This first–year seminar is challenging in the sense that it opens your mind up to new concepts and ideas, but it wasn't stressful to complete the assignments and do well. Prof Yao does a great job at creating an environment where you can focus on learning new topics and trying to understand new things without a ton of pressure to fully master everything that is covered. If you're interested in quantum and want to learn about it in a way that is engaging (without being academically stressful) and fun (without having too shallow of a syllabus), this is the class for you!! Prof Yao is a fantastic, understanding teacher who's always ready with great jokes, helpful explanations, and tasty free pizza.
