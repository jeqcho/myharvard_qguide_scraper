Light readings per week, 3 quizzes that you should study for but not super high stress(very similar to SCRB 10 experimental design but just regeneration content), make sure to know specific examples from lecture/readings, write your synopses early to get them done early. Grading is very fair and flexible. Jessica is very open to student feedback. Lectures a little boring because she just dives into papers and not much content, but pretty interesting to learn about overall, just gets repetitive over the weeks. Think about the final video early on and make sure to practice it with your group. TFs might be different next year, but ours was pretty chill and helpful. You'll notice the course is a little unorganized and sometimes she'll move things around randomly. She also doesn't post the lecture notes before class and will need reminders to post them after class. She didn't post practice quizzes until a few days before as well, so you just need to remind her or she'll forget. Pay attention ish in lecture, definitely read/skim the papers and know the main claim and a critique of them. But, this class is one of the most chill SCRB courses and I highly recommend.

Nothing too complex and sticks to the normal toolbox of developmental biology skills you learned from SCRB10 and SCRB50. Most of what I enjoyed in this class was outside the class, i.e. reading the papers every week and working on the final SciCom project. Day–to–day the class was just normal, lectures were pretty standard and the student paper presentations were fine as well. The assessments were also pretty low–stress as long as you read the papers and had half a mind of SCRB concepts. The TFs were pretty nice and Jessica is also very sweet and clearly talented in her field. The lecturing style takes a second to get used to, but she definitely is passionate about the material and good at answering any curiosity questions that you might have which is really sweet.

This class is great! It basically functions like a journal club. Jessica and the TFs are wonderful and generate so much enthusiasm for the content. You can definitely take this class without having taken SCRB 10/50 but maybe not if you aren't a MCB/HDRB/related concentrator. My only complaint was that the class seemed pretty disorganized at times with exam practice material or grading rubrics not being posted, grades taking an extremely long time to come back, and assignment details not being released much before the due date. Class is straightforward, and you get what you put in. If you treat the exams like SCRB10/SCRB50 style assessments, plan your paper synopses, and prep for the final project, you will succeed.
Looking back, SCRB 111 is one of the best courses that I have taken! The topic itself was very interesting, and the professor was also really engaging and passionate about the course. Compared to SCRB 10 and SCRB 50, this is a significant step up and is a great course for fulfilling upper–level requirements. The setup of the class is very relaxed, with there being no PSETs or exams. Instead, your grade is based off of weekly primary literature review summaries, two in–class presentations, three quizzes, and a final project. All of these aspects allowed students to become more engaged with the content, and complemented the course very well.

My only criticism of this course would be in terms of the grading and feedback by the TFs. As mentioned above, students were asked to submit weekly primary literature review summaries, with only 4 counting towards our final grade and a minimum of 6 required throughout the semester. However, feedback and grades for these summaries were often not returned until over a month past the initial submission date, leading to many students submitting far past the minimum required amount due to being unsure if they had scored well enough to get full credit for the 4 that count. The same feedback applies for the quizzes, which were often not returned until at least two weeks after initial examination. However, this only represents a minor logistics issue, and should not take away from the rest of the class.

Overall, SCRB 111 is an outstanding course that I would highly recommend for anyone looking for upper level STEM or SCRB requirements!
