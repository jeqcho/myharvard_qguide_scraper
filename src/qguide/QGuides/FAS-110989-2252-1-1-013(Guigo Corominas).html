Only take if required to. 21A was so much better IMO. I feel like I only learned the material for the exams and nothing more. The course instruction lacked real world applications and frankly even after taking it, I don’t think I could explain what some of the basic terms and ideas were. The exams are reflective of in–class worksheets but there are often difficult questions that really make you think—and the answers aren’t obvious whatsoever. The exams also contain many T/F questions that don’t give any partial credit for explanation which is an unfair assessment of your knowledge of the content. Also when you’re studying, the practice tests are never reflective of the real exam—they’re usually way harder. The homework is also so long and takes forever. The CAs aren’t helpful. My original TF was not good, had a heavy accent and never answered our questions (“it’s easy, right” no DV it’s not…), and switching out of his section was extremely difficult.
This class is hard, stay on top of the workload. Psets are much longer than 21a psets. Material is confusing at times and there are a ton of definitions you have to memorize. Its manageable but requires a time commitment, and DO NOT leave your psets to do morning of if you don't want to suffer.
If you're not required to take this course, don't take it. It sucks. The psets take way to much time for how little they actually matter in the grading scheme and literally will drain the life out of your semester as well as any interest you had in math in the first place. Half the class is redundant and a quarter of the class is so "hand–wavey" that there's no way what they're saying has any real bearing.
This is a good class to take if you don't want the proof–based approach to linear algebra. The three psets a week honestly aren't that bad (I would recommend doing them right after your class if you can!) Exams were pretty fair, just make sure to take the practice tests before and hone your conceptual understanding (the true and false questions could sometimes be annoying). Overall, I feel like the pacing of this class was good, the teaching staff was amazing, and the concepts we covered were really interesting and SO applicable to a lot of the more advanced science/math classes that you will take at Harvard.
This course isn't as hard as some people make it seem. 3 psets a week is rough, expect to spend 2–3 hours on each one, maybe 4 at the most for like the 3 hardest/most tedious PSETS. You'll be surprised by how everything ties together in the end, and this is great for mathematical thinking and developing logical reasoning. A lot funner than 21A, and although it might take more time, IMO it's actually a little easier. Don't be intimidated! Lots of support. Just make sure to spend a LOT of time on how symmetric/invertible/orthogonal matrices tie together!
This class is hard. As someone who studied A Level Maths and did well in Stat 110, this class turned out to be a lot more difficult than anticipated. There are three problem sets a week, and while the problem sets are useful in the sense that they help supplement the teaching of the class, it feels like a non–stop amount of work for the entirety of the semester. Three sets a week and they're only worth 20% of the grade... The class isn't curved, and while the exams are reasonably difficult, it's really hard to get in the 90–100 range that you need for an A. Your experience also varies greatly with the TF you have: mine wasn't great, and so I spent a lot of time learning the content myself which isn't really the most ideal. Most people considering 21b have to take this class for concentration requirements, so here's my advice: study hard for the exams, do all the psets to the best of your ability, go to MQC, and don't underestimate this class.
