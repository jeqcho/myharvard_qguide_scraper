HRO is an amazing class. It is unparalleled at Harvard.
Just take it. If you plan on being in HRO, taking it as a class will keep you accountable to go to rehearsals and involve yourself as much as possible. The assignments are very reasonable and fair, and fed gives excellent feedback. The readings themselves are also extreemely though–provoking and make you think about the music you are playing/have played at a much deeper level; I learned a ton about composers i had never heard of before and learned a substancial amount of info about ones that I thought I new about, but lacked depth
As someone who doesn't have much classical music knowledge, I surprisingly enjoyed the readings. I thought they provided interesting and important context about Shostakovich and Ustvolskaya's life that helped me think about their music.
This is one of the most rewarding classes you can take at Harvard. Being able to play music and explore musical history with your peers under the direction of Fed is an incredible opportunity.
Take it!! This course always is extremely informative because you are able to interact with the course material and history behind the pieces you are playing. I think it adds an academic perspective to the music that you are playing in HRO, which allows for you to better perform the music. There definitely is a good amount of time needed when you are given an assignment, but they are spaced out super well so just do the readings early. I like how it is very freeform and a good way to interact more in depth with the music you are playing.
Music 107 is amazing! Requires a good amount of work to read through the material and synthesize the material but you learn a lot about Soviet music and the plight of Russians during the 1900s that really supports your interpretation of the piece
This is a difficult class and must be taken seriously. We are held to a very high standard of musicality and that excellence must also carry through into our written work. There is a lot of material to read and absorb, but it relates quite well to the pieces we perform. Thank you for a wonderful semester!
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! HRO is a major time commitment but it is so worth it. Fed has a really deep appreciation for the music we play and its significance, and it's really interesting to get to know more of his thoughts and ideas about why the music is so important, and why he feels that it is worth the amount of time we spend to play any given piece and engage with it intellectually. Taking Music 107 really adds another layer of engagement and understanding to the HRO experience, and getting the letter graded credit means you can prioritize it as a class (I took 3 classes one semester and used Music 107 as my fourth class) and really give it the time it deserves instead of relegating it to the bottom of the priority pile alongside other extracurriculars. As someone who hasn't taken other music classes, the readings can be hard to understand at times, and make me aware of my lack of background knowledge, but keeping with it and wading through it really leads to me learning a lot. Be prepared to engage with music that is hard, technically, musically, in terms of musical and historical significance, because if you do you will come away with a much better understanding of that music, as well as music history and regular history overall. I would 100% recommend.
HRO is a big commitment, but is totally worth it.

The actual assignments of the course are not super difficult, but they are very interesting. I think it is very helpful to be able to read and learn more about the composers/works that we play and it adds a level of depth to the music making that would not otherwise be found.
