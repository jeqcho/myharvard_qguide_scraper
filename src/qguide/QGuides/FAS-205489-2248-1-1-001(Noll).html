What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Dr. Noll is an amazing teacher! If you have any concerns about not being WGS or psych, she will give background in class to help explain and help you understand the course material. The readings can be a lot sometimes, in length and in content (it gets pretty dense) but discussions in class can help you better understand. She also holds office hours, which are incredibly useful if you have questions on the material or the final project!
I would recommend the course, but take it knowing that it might be more work than expected. Prof. Noll is great at facilitating discussion, and this is an important class to have at Harvard. That said, the class itself is pretty long (3 hours), which wouldn't have been a bother if it seemed that we used all three hours usefully – some days the course really dragged on. Nonetheless, absolutely recommend because Prof. Noll is great and you'll have amazing discussions and the assignments are creative and unique!
This is the best class I have taken at Harvard when I balance my enjoyment of it and what I come away having learned. It is not a walk in the park because there are weekly graded response papers to the readings, but the readings are interesting and important. Dr Noll is the best teacher I have ever had. She is genuine, understanding, brilliant, and so incredibly invested in her students. If you have any interest in gender theory or the psychology of the body you should most definitely take this class. I felt like I bonded with my fellow students and developed a real understanding of the material from multiple perspectives. I loved this course, and I feel prepared to use the information in other classes. I can not describe what an excellent learning environment this was. Dr. Noll allows you to learn and participate in your own way, as yourself, and I feel as though she truly knows me as a student and a person now. I believe she will have been the teacher who most impacts my view on what it truly means to teach.
I would highly recommend this class for anyone interested in the intersection between gender studies and psychology. It is a very low stress learning environment that encourages curiosity and collaboration. The class is very discussion based and you learn a lot from your peers, which I really appreciated. Dr. Noll is one of the most supportive professors I've had here at Harvard and I really enjoyed the chance to take a class with her.
Recommend this class if you are interested in science/psychology as well as the WGS department! There are a decent number of research paper readings in this class, which I found not too bad to read due to my background in biology/science. The theory heavy readings were difficult for me, but Dr. Noll is super helpful with explaining these theories and giving examples in class (and in feedback). I found the readings interesting overall because they investigate the subtle interactions (that we don't usually think twice about) between gender, the body, and how we are socialized.
Love Dr. Noll, would highly recommend this course. Interesting and engaging course work, not just typical assignments. Not too difficult either, and still felt like I learned a lot.
