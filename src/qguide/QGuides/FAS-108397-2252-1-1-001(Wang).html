This class is AMAZING! Without feeling too much pressure to perform, we got through tons of really interesting literature and learned how to discuss it in Chinese. All of the teachers are brilliant, and it feels fruitful just to hear them talk (especially because they make such nuanced issues digestible in Chinese for those, like myself, who are a little lackluster on the language skills). Super duper great!!! Take it take it! It will change the way you see language and literature!
The assignments for this class are time–consuming. Be wary of the workload: reading the texts, writing the homework, and the bi–weekly essay components.
The course provided a wonderfully rich and immersive experience for learning the Chinese language (lots of practice for reading and writing, and weekly opportunities to engage in one–on–one conversation). The top highlight of the class, besides the lovely professors and TF, is the unique opportunity to study real–world texts and engage in academic study using the original language. The courses provided an in–depth introduction to modern Chinese literature, from Lu Xun to Hao Jingfang. Each week had a keyword theme, which I find super helpful to orient myself in a new unfamiliar context, and the authors built upon each other so you could feel the origins and energies that inspired the next work. It is definitely a rigorous and challenging class, but it is structured in a way that supports you even if you didn’t understand the text the first time. The class itself attracts a relatively wide range of language levels so it didn’t feel too daunting and I made strong friendships through the class. The course materials are outstandingly well–made. Extensive vocabulary lists are provided for each week’s reading. However much you put into the class, you will get out tenfold. Beyond the readings, the assignments are also not just busywork; they are designed to get you to think about the text, check your understanding, and further your understanding. Sometimes when studying a language it can feel difficult to know just how much you have learned. But through the semester–long research paper, it feels as though I have accomplished something very tangibly significant that I can be proud about. This was the best language learning experience I’ve ever had!
Definitely a bit of a challenge going from standard language courses to a literature one, but so worth it. Professor Wang and Liu are great, generate a lot of enthusiasm for the subject, and make learning about very difficult topics super worthwhile. Strongly recommend.
Take this class! You will have the opportunity to read a lot of classic Chinese literature that spans Chinese history. Professor Wang's lectures are so insightful and he can be very funny.
It might be very hard depending on your language background, but it is worth it!
This class gives a wonderful overview of Modern Chinese Literature that spans different countries and diasporas. I appreciated the breadth of material and the wonderful professors. However, be warned that this class is a lot of work. Readings could go up to 50 pages and in addition to all weekly homework, final essay, oral presentation, and discussion posts, can be very overwhelming depending on your proficiency.
This class opened my eyes to the great works of modern Chinese literature, and I felt so proud to be able to read these texts in their original. I've been studying Chinese for the entirety of my college career (and basically started with nothing), so it's a major testament to the strength of the Chinese language department that I've been able to get to this level at all. Also, unlike typical language classes, you spend most of your time engaging with the texts and focusing on your reading ability which I really appreciated after being a little burnt out from 3 years of daily Chinese class. Also, the 1–on–1 sessions are so enjoyable and helpful for improving your conversational ability. Couldn't recommend this course enough honestly!
