So, you have to take Math 21a and are looking at the Q to find out how bad it'll be?

Honestly, it's fine. It's not the best thing and it's not the worst. It's certainly a grind – 3 psets / week is no joke – but it's also manageable. On weeks with exams, you'll probably study a lot (I am telling you to study a lot. please. it helps. a lot). There's times where the material may be incredibly confusing, but if you study, you will figure it out. If you have to take this course, just do it. It'll be annoying and a bit of a time sink, but it's not awful.
This course is very difficult and there was unfortunately not as much support as I would have hoped for and needed this semester. The exams, in my opinion, were not difficult than the practice exams. In workshop, I wish that we spent more time doing comprehensive building on the course content, not applying the skills to real world application in ways that did not build understanding. However, I loved the content. I am Applied Math.
Theres a lot of support for the class (MQC, Office Hours) so use it! A lot of Math 21A depends on your TF and CAs, some are great and some are not so much, so good luck if you get a poor TF. Collaborate with friends in the class to get psets done. Workshops pretty useless, if you want to save time just do the AI one. Tests are fair, and much easier than the practice ones they post. Do all the extra work they offer for pset drops because youre almost guaranteed to have bad psets out of the 36 total. Stay on top of work and youll be alright.
Look back at your graded homework to understand both your mistakes and your CAs grading style.

Also 2/3 of section time is wasted because nobody is able to solve the worksheet problems unless the TF explains it first (obviously). Coming to class felt like a big waste of time, I could have taught myself everything using chat gpt and the worksheets in half the time. That being said you can't get around this course, so just try to use the sections as time to do other work and find yourself someone who has the answers to all the psets or just use chat gpt. Also the final exam was beyond unfair so prepare to get screwed over by the course staff at the end of the class.
This is not going to be easy but for most people there is no way around it. The exams are honestly are fair bit easier than the practice exams (so make sure to do them!) but were often confusingly worded. 3 psets a week is terrible so make sure to start earlier, go to office hours, and get a good study group (you can;t do it by yourself). It is not the worst, and if you have good study habits a good grade is in reach
