TAKE TAKE TAKE I cannot recommend this class enough. The only work you are responsible for is the prep for your speeches (4 / semester) and each one allows you to reflect on something from your own life. You can always write about what you want to. This class helped me build confidence and get closer to my peers – such a valuable source of positivity in my week. Professor Coburn–Palo is so supportive and starts the semester by telling you that as long as you show up each day and try, you will be on track for an A. If you are not, he will pull you aside to discuss those reasons 1–1. This openness to all speech abilities lowered all stress for this class and allowed me to lean in and try my hardest. Such a great class!! Thank you Professor Coburn–Palo!
TAKE THIS CLASS. Nichols Coburn–Palo is GOATED. This was one of my favorite classes at Harvard.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Expos 40 has been the most rewarding class that I have taken at Harvard. Your speaking skills and confidence will grow tremendously.
Take it! Great experience
Amazing class! Prof. Coburn–Palo is great and incredibly dedicated to teaching. He had pneumonia this semester and still made sure to co to lecture when he wasn't feeling 100%. He's really available and does care a lot about you and what you're working on.
I highly recommend this class to any and all Harvard students. It is one of my favorite courses I've ever taken, and teaches practical skills that everyone needs to know. Prof. Coburn–Palo is extremely kind and creates a classroom environment where public speaking is fun and low–stress. Can't recommend this course enough, especially with this professor.
Pros:
– This was a really fun class. I loved hearing other people's speeches! It was such a cool way to get to know other students.
– I got to practice my public speaking. This was a really supportive space to practice.
– I learned from hearing other people's speeches what I liked and what I didn't and that helped with my own speeches.
– I appreciated the feedback I got from other students.
– Super easy.

Cons:
– Nick cancelled so many classes. They were for valid reasons, but he always did it at like 11pm the night before class, and it was always the night before a speech day, so the people who were meant to give a speech that day didn't know when they were meant to give their speech instead, and it was all just very confusing. It would have been nice to have more notice. The class was so disorganized.
– I actually didn't find Nick's feedback helpful. He would tell us to do things like work on our footwork, but he never explained what good footwork looked like. I also often disagreed with what he told my classmates about their speeches, so I didn't necessarily put so much stock in his comments.
– I didn't find the exercises we did in between our speeches (like the improv speeches) useful. The topics we did were super random (talk about your favorite meal), and I didn't like the structure we used. I think we could have done much more useful things (answer the question "tell me about yourself" in a job interview, elevator pitch a project you're working on...)

My recommendation: take expos 40 with a different preceptor
