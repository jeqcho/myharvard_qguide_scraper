What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Enroll it and you will learn a lot
Great methods class with a focus on applying MLM's (as opposed to really going in depth on math proofs/under the hood of the method). The class has a decent amount of out of class work, but you will exit the course with real skills in how to implement what you learn. Homework is done with groups, teaching team is very available and helpful, the final project resulted in the start of a project my team is continuing to work on. This was an ideal methods course for me as a social research student because it balanced knowing the math enough but focused on application.
I highly, highly recommend you take this class! You will learn so much about multilevel and longitudinal methods, that very soon, you will see them everywhere in the literature! The class moves quickly and is a lot of work, but you will feel very supported with Luke always being open to chat, the TFs being incredibly generous with their time, and problem set teammates ready to collaborate. You also get to work on a project towards the end of the semester, which is an incredibly rewarding opportunity to implement the learning from the class in a real–world setting. Don't be shy to take this class –– you won't regret it!
Take this course if you are interested in multilevel/longitudinal modelling. The pedagogy is outstanding and you'll gain access to resources that will serve well you for a lifetime. The teaching team is also incredibly committed and helpful. Never felt bored during a lecture because the material is well organized and the instructor has got a great sense of humor.
It's really a great class! Try to have fun in it even though some concepts are hard to grasp!
This course is challenging! There are a lot of moving parts and it can be hard to stay on top of the work. That said, the work builds towards something really tangible and useful, and it's worth it. Also –– there's a lot of group work in this course. Choose your group members thoughtfully!
