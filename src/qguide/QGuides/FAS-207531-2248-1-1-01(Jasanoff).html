Comments
Professor Jasanoff is a brilliant mind and a caring and thoughtful instructor, and this was among the best courses I've taken across my four years at Harvard. The readings are excellently curated and always enjoyable. Prof. Jasanoff also creates a classroom culture that is emphatically collaborative and always produces helpful feedback. Of course, the most helpful feedback comes from Prof. Jasanoff herself, who always has incisive and constructive critiques of both the class readings and student writing. Taking this course was a real joy.
Wonderful class! Definitely take it, you get what you put into it. Use this class as a directed way to improve your writing, and if you go to office hours, you will definitely improve. Professor Jasanoff is one of the best!
IF YOU ARE A HISTORY–THESIS–WRITING SENIOR, TAKE THIS COURSE! You learn so much, and I know that what I learned from the course will be so, so helpful in making my thesis more compelling and a better read. Prof. Jasanoff is an absolute delight and one of the most all–around brilliant and wonderful professors here at Harvard, and she creates a really excellent classroom environment. Learning how to write from one of the best and most successful writers in the history department is a one–of–a–kind opportunity that I strongly encourage any student, especially a senior writing a history thesis, to make the most of. I could not recommend this course more!
You should be so lucky to get a spot in this course. Professor Jasanoff is an incredible instructor who puts so much care into fostering better writers. The classroom environment was superb, and the readings were genuinely so much fun. I had always wanted to take a class that would help me become a better history writer, and this was definitely it. There are many excellent offerings for creative writing in the English department, but this class is unique in that the standard of research is high and the work you produce must be strictly factual. It's a delicious challenge. Plus, rather than bogging you down with busy work, this class gives you the breathing room to really work on your final paper, which, if you're writing a thesis, can be related to one of your chapters. This meant that in this class I was a) interested in what was going on, b) not stressed because I had plentiful time to put together my final paper, and c) I knew I was going to get thoughtful, serious feedback on my writing and improve in the process. I don't know about you, but that definitely checks all my boxes!
This is a great course to explore narrative writing in history. You will explore a variety of narrative historical writing from G.M. Trevelyan to Sadiya Hartmann to Taya Miles while experimenting with your own writing. The class discussion is fun and enriching. I would recommend this class to everyone interested in history and/or writing, and especially to seniors and PhDs who are in the writing phase.
TAKE THIS CLASS IF YOU CAN! Professor Jasanoff is an incredible teacher who takes the time to give incredibly specific line notes to each student's work, and you will learn so much about writing (and the world in general) from her. In addition, the peer group is excellent –– great mix of undergrads and grad students who are all very passionate about the craft of writing. This course is the epitome of what a liberal arts education should be!
This is a wonderful class! Please take it.
