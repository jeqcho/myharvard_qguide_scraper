What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you are interested in theater and love to read plays, TAKE THIS CLASS! I have struggled in the past with finding TDM courses not dividing into the beauty of plays and the theory behind how theater gets made, and this class absolutely did. I often felt like I was voluntarily heading to a play reading group every week, rather than a class. You will leave this course with a beautiful introduction to russian drama even if you have no prior experience reading russian literature!
I loved this course! Dr. Khitrova is a great facilitator. She chose amazing pieces for us to read that I really enjoyed. Each class was very enjoyable. We read one piece a week and got to discuss our favorite parts with our classmates and the professor. The professor created a constructive atmosphere that allowed for open conversation. It was fun to hear everyone's different points of view and learn about the history of the pieces and the authors. The assignments were also great. There was one written midterm that allowed us to dig deep into one of our favorite pieces, and there was a creative option for the final assignment that was very exciting to work on. I highly recommend this course to all!
Beyond the fascinating plays assigned for this survey of Russian drama, enlightening presentations and lively discussions in class make this course one of my favorites that I've taken at Harvard. The course is perfectly balanced between analyzing the literature itself and providing the historical context of the plays by evaluating the development of Russian drama. Professor Khitrova's passion for the material is contagious. You should definitely take this course!
