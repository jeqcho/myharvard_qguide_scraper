Mixed feelings about this class. I really enjoyed the material from the first half (graph theory) but found the second half (combinatorics) super dull. The class definitely leans towards being pure math rather than applied, as many of the problems are proof based, especially in the first half. For the most part, problem sets were straightforward and graded very generously. And both exams were fair (midterm average was ~65%). The real problem is that the course is extremely disorganized and there's very little support outside of lectures and office hours. There was no clear topic list at any point, Professor Valiant lectures off of handwritten notes that are not posted afterwards, and there are no TF notes. We were given no problem set solutions, no practice exam solutions, and no exam solutions. So it was extremely hard to work out tough topics or get clarity on solutions without going to office hours (only offered like 3x per week), which is really tedious for a couple problems of a pset.

This class covers some interesting subject material including graph theory, probability, combinatorics, and recursive functions. Importantly, this class teaches essentially no CS, and it gives few chances to apply concepts more broadly. It is very much a theory class, involving many proofs. And little introduction to constructing a proof is taught; students are largely expected to be able to do this already. Professor Valiant does not seem particularly invested in this class; lectures were recorded and sparsely attended in person. He also did not publish any course material this semester, including notes, problem set solutions, midterm solutions, etc., making it quite hard to learn independently. All this being said, this class only has weekly problem sets and two midterms—no final exams or projects. This workload is very reasonable and not too taxing IF you attend office hours; the TF's were incredibly helpful. Learning the content and solving problems will be extremely time consuming otherwise.
Only take if you need to or are seriously interested in graph theory, otherwise it will be brutal
