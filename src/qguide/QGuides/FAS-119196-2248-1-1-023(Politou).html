21A was my lifestyle as a first–semester freshman. I ate, slept, and breathed psets and basically lived at the MQC. My closest friends are in 21A. You cannot escape it. If you are in Natalie's section, switch out right away. She has bad time management and sometimes we were lucky to get through 2 worksheet questions in 75 minutes and the way she explains things is very unclear and she admitted to us that she did not take a traditional multi course in college. The only reason I stayed is because the CAs in the section (Jeremy and Ben) were phenomenal and taught me everything. I had no experience with multi before 21A so sometimes it was intimidating being in the class with people who had the opportunity to do that and more in high school. However, I found it very manageable because the course is structured very intuitively and all concepts build on each other and are constantly reused. The quality of TFs in 21A is very inconsistent so make sure you have a good section. In hindsight, I regret not at least trying 22A but the reason I chose 21 was because of the supports in the class like the MQC and constant office hours and small class sizes which did not disappoint. If you are considering 21A, be prepared to put in the time and make sure that you have somewhere to get help outside of class. For midterms and the final, do and redo ALL of the practice exam questions and make sure you start early enough to understand every single one. Also if you can, go to Eva's office hours. She explains things so clearly and saved my life for the final.
Choose a section that works for you otherwise you will do twice the work
This class is hard! I say this because my TF could not articulate and explain the concepts cleanly, and I know that was a lot of my peer's experience with their section leaders as well. Because this course is only taught in sections (no lecture), that is a key determinate. If you have a good TF, you will be okay, but if you have a bad one (which is the majority of the students), you will find yourself putting in hours of hard work because the psets are relentless. Take this class if you LOVE math, but if you are just filling a requirement, think twice.
Start early with understanding concepts and it can be helpful to get a tutor to meet with weekly through the ARC but make sure you have specific conceptual questions in mind before your meetings to be the most productive! Try to get a morning (9am is best) section time to increase your chances of getting a great section TF and to maximize your time in office hours.
This class is very easy; the psets are simple and should each take you 0.5~1 hour but there are 3 psets a week so it's sometime painful to realize that you have a pset to do on Thursday before you go to sleep. I skipped all the sections after the first midterm and still managed to get a decent grade on the following exams; you can basically teach yourself everything by simply going through the worksheets.
If you took AP Calc BC (or something equivalent) in high school and are looking for somewhere to start your math journey at Harvard, TAKE THIS CLASS. Three psets a week may seem like a lot, but it truly helps you retain the material and keeps you on top of your work, and as time goes on you'll realize it actually does not take up that much time. As long as you take the time to really understand the homework while you are completing it, TAKE THE PRACTICE EXAMS, and actively participate in class, you will succeed.

For me, this course started out pretty easy, and then got more difficult towards the middle of the semester when you get to triple integration/vector problem solving, but then it leveled out again. I would HIGHLY recommend going through the solutions for the worksheets after class/whenever you start the pset to internalize the concepts you learned in class––this becomes very useful during exam time.

I had a slightly more inexperienced TF (absolutely amazing CAs though), but in a way this motivated me to go through the material on my own time, and I was able to do well both on the psets and on the exams.

All in all, I am so glad I took this class, and it was one of the highlights of my semester.
