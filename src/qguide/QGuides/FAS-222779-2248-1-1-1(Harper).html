What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
If you're not that committed to photography/art and are looking to take an easy elective, take intro or something else because it's pretty hard to bs your way through. The professor and TA are people who are professionals and have many years of experience — they can tell when someone isn't being genuine and will push you to put in more effort.

But if you happen to be like me and find yourself obsessed with photo, take this class. Coming from a street photography background, it was quite different from what I'm used to, but changes of pace and unpredictability are what foster creativity so I very much appreciated it.
TAKE THIS COURSE!!! It is a great opportunity to learn photography as an ever–evolving and experimental medium! You get to rent a camera for free from the AFVS department and also request to order supplies you need for your work! You get to read about and interview some innovative photographers! You get a lot of practice with lens–based mediums, scanning, Inkjet printing, Photoshop, InDesign, and recursive processes! This class reframed the way I view art as an ever–evolving process!!
This class will definitely push you to think critically about your own photographic process, and will force you to take your work outside of your comfort zone! I highly reccommend it!!
Definitely take this course if you want to expand your photography practice into new and experimental directions! We learned camera–less ways of making, collage, other techniques, and discussed various photographers and their methodologies. Each student ended up finding their own voice and direction to grow in, and this class provides the flexibility to choose your own ways of experimental making. The intimate class size provides a safe space to explore and take risks with the work. Sharon is amazing, and very adaptive to the needs of the students and the flow of the class. She encourages focusing on process, change, and growth, rather than an end goal or fully finished product. You will learn a lot about yourself as an artist and your relationship to photography and creative practice!
If you enjoy photography and experimenting with your art, take this class! Prof. Harper and Claude (TA) are fantastic instructors, and this class will really push you to grow as an artist
