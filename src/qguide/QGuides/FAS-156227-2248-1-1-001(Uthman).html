Comments
Without a doubt, this is the best class I've taken at Harvard. You will develop skills in analyzing literary texts, engaging in meaningful discussions with peers, and writing about works in ways that reflect their impact on political and historical narratives. The poems, short stories, and books that we read were unforgettable, and while it is difficult to choose a favorite, the final weeks in which we delved into the genre of Arabic prison literature were (while heavy) incredibly important and changed my perspective on the human political realities in the Middle East. This course revived my love for reading and was an incredible introduction to Arabic literature, which I hope to explore further beyond this class. If there is one class you should take at Harvard, it's this one!!
Take this class!! Professor Uthman is a fantastic professor –– he genuinely cares about students and is a brilliant thinker. You'll learn so much. I cannot encourage enough !!
I absolutely loved this course and wish Harvard had more classes like this. Professor Uthman is extremely passionate about the topic and is very skilled at tailoring the class time to go in deep depth on the week's text and broader course themes. The course is not that terrible of a time commitment and is also very enjoyable.
Take it !!!! You will learn so much, even if you've previously thought about displacement/Arabic literature. Truly a one of a kind class.
Regardless of major/program, I would encourage students to take this class. In fact, diversity in disciplinary backgrounds enriches the class discussions.
Take this class! It was one of the most rewarding classes I've taken at Harvard. I grew in so many aspects and appreciated all the texts we engaged in. The structure of the class encourages discussion and it feels more like a conversation than section. Also Prof. Nader is amazing and always makes class exciting and engaging. I really recommend you take this course with him!
Please please take this class. Ustaaz Nader is literally my favorite professor and this class was my favorite in the semester. The readings are very interesting, the discussion posts are very engaging and there is a lot of learning that you will do in this class with regards to understanding displacement and exile in the Arab world. You'll also get a chance to read a generous amount of prison literature. I LOVED this class and recommend it with so much enthusiasm!
