I really enjoyed this course. My only complaint is that there was not much of a debate that occured during each class. Everyone thought as each other did.
I would highly, highly recommend this class if you're at all interested in mainland France's history and/or French colonial history. Professor Lewis is a talented teacher with vast knowledge and the small class size allows for a lot of student to student and student to professor interaction. The class strikes a balance between being a lecture and a section discussion well, and the readings and assignments are manageable and fairly graded. I truly wish more people would take this class and benefit from Professor Lewis' teaching!
Professor Lewis is one of my favorite professors that I have taken a class with while at Harvard. She is SO smart and SO invested in her students' learning and enrichment through the course. I met with her on multiple occasions outside of class, and I always found our conversations to be wonderfully enriching. I cannot recommend taking a course with her enough. This class itself is a great intro to French history. I came in with not a lot of outside knowledge, and I found it accessible and interesting. I really feel that I learned a lot through the course of this class.
I would highly recommend taking this class if you're interested in France, French history, or just looking to learn a lot. Prof. Lewis is an incredible teacher, and I honestly did not want class to end after the 75 minutes. The workload is pretty light. The biggest item by far is the final forgotten chapter, which is a cool and rewarding alternative to a traditional term paper. Some weeks the readings can get long, but they are overall pretty interesting and support the class material.
