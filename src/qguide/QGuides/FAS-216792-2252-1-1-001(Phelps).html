great class! Prof Phelps is wonderful and very kind
I would tell them that though it can be taken for psych credit it definitely is heavy neuroscience––you don't necessarily need to be able to speak in neuro terms to engage in class but the readings rely on in depth neuroscience so be aware of that!
Take take take! This course topic is so interesting, the assignments were all fair, and Dr. Phelps is a wonderful, accommodating teacher. There are four essays, each about 2–3 pages, and they can be revised based on Dr. Phelps' feedback. The final project is creative (I made a video, lots of people made podcasts or presentations), which is super fun and made me even more engaged in my topic. You read papers for each class, and then get to hear from the first authors as guest speakers during class, which was a super cool opportunity. Highly recommend!
I LOVED this course! Such an interesting and engaging way to fill one of the advanced neuroscience requirements. Professor Phelps is awesome and always super willing to answer questions. I would absolutely take this course again!
This course was super great! The seminar style with 2 hour classes can be a bit draining, but I appreciated that Professor Phelps provides a 10 minute break halfway through. The subject matter is really interesting and you learn SO much about affective neuroscience with the opportunity to learn from many knowledgeable scientists. Professor Phelps is a leading expert in the field with many connections to others in the field, which is awesome.
If you need an advanced neuroscience course, take this one! It's a great introduction to cognitive neuroscience and is a completely doable class. Professor Phelps is amazing and you also get to hear from guest speakers who are the authors of the papers you read. You write an essay biweekly with opportunities to re–do if you don't do well on them and there is a fun creative project at the end. Highly recommend!
this was a very enjoyable course. you can learn a lot about a range of interesting topics. doing the readings and paying attention to the presentations in class sets you up to feel confident in answering the paper prompts. final project can be related to anything that interests you, which was nice
