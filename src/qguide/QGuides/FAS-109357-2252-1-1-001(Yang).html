Access to unique resources – a course you could only take at Harvard
Take it! Super chill and really cool material. The cleanroom lab block is a massive chunk of time, but reports are group–based so very little time commitment outside of that. Massive amount of freedom to take final project in the direction that interests you. The profs and TFs are really passionate and knowledgeable, super open to questions.
I loved this course. The teaching team is kind, well–organized, and makes sure everything is prepared so students can focus on learning the most important parts.
Take this course! You will learn so much about microfabrication and semiconductors; it's been such a unique experience to work in the cleanroom and there are not many places that offer this kind of opportunity, so you should definitely take advantage of it. Kiyoul is amazing and is always very approachable when you don't understand something.
This class is challenging to understand in entirely, but easy to get a good grade. The 4 hour labs are exhausting, but not mentally difficult. I would recommend to seniors interested in microfab, but who don't want a big weekly time commitment.
ES177 is a great course. Throughout the semester, you have 4 labs (each ~4 hours), 4 lab reports, and 1 final project review (or final project if you take ES277). The four–hour lab can sound intimidating, but it was honestly a good time. A lot of the time, we finished early. If we went past dinner, the class had a budget and got food afterwards as a group.

This course is a great way to get ISO–5 cleanroom experience, especially with microfabrication. Getting microfab cleanroom experience is typically not offered for undergrads, so this is a great opportunity to try something new. I recommend the course!
Take the class to learn about how tiny semiconductor chips are actually made. Labs can be long.
You learn how TRANSISTORS and other devices are fabricated.
This is such a unique course at Harvard that I'd recommend anyone (undergraduate and graduate alike) to take this course if you get the chance. Professor Kiyoul Yang is great and the teaching staff is wonderful and the actual subject matter is fascinating. If you ever wondered "how the pie was made" so to speak, take this course.
