If you're interested in a Gov94, you should absolutely take this class! Jordan is a wonderful instructor––he's funny, attentive, and leads the class discussions in really interesting and meaningful ways. It's a great way to get a survey of foundational texts in the history of American political thought, and it pushes you as a political theory student and as a thinker without being too stressful.
This is a great class if you enjoy a survey course where you read a solid canon! I enjoyed it. Assignments were very reasonable, somewhat open ended so a it was lot of fun writing them. Jordan is a great guy, who knows a lot about the subject and makes the conversations engaging. I would recommend this to fellow political theory/philosophy lovers, it is a good gov94.
This is a great class! Professor Ecker is a super nice and chill guy. You can tell he has a strong passion for this topic and is great at leading discussions. The readings can be a little dense or difficult to understand at times but overall I found them really enjoyable and interesting. Make sure that you give yourself enough time for the readings each week since it is usually about 150–200 pages of reading and our discussions go pretty in depth so you can't really just skim them.
I really enjoyed taking this course. Jordan does such an amazing job of creating enthusiasm for the content. I was really worried about checking off the theory requirement, but Jordan made every reading interesting and worth dissecting further, even the ones I had the most trouble getting through. The time flies and I really cannot recommend it enough.
I would highly recommend this course to anyone interested in political theory. The readings assigned are all primary sources and are excellent. The assignments are reasonable (one short reflection paper, midterm paper, final research paper). Jordan is very accessible in office hours to discuss topics for these. I felt like I got a lot out of taking this course despite having encountered some of the materially previously.
An excellent way to fulfill your gov theory requirement. The reading is reasonable and Ecker does a great job facilitating discussion.
Take it 1000000%. Jordan is a great professor. If you want to learn about American history and the foundational thinkers of America, this is the class for you.
