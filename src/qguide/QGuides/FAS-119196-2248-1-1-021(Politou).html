They should definitely being going to office hours to do their problem sets and go over material from class that was confusing. If you are willing to put a lot of time into this course, then I would recommend taking it just because you really do learn a lot of math.

I recommend staying on top of the work and starting studying for midterms early. The TFs and CAs are all super helpful so reach out if you are confused or studying! I loved 21a; the material was really enjoyable and I think the teaching staff is really amazing. Eva is the nicest person ever and always willing to help. Mini exam is nothing to stress over, midterm 1 is easy, midterm 2 lowkey hard, and the final was lowkey bad but praying for a curve… hope my A is still there after it but we’ll see in ten days!! lots of extra credit opportunities too so fingers crossed. I do personally recommend taking; this course has really helped me decide to concentrate in math.
If you've done basic research about this course, yes, it's as hard as you think it is. To be fully honest, 3 psets a week sucks. But as someone who is not a math whiz and entered the course having only taken AP Calc BC as my highest math, I can say that it's a very doable course if you stay on top of things and TRY to do well. For the Econ majors out there, if you are between 18a and 21a like I was, take 21a without a doubt.
Take it! Much better taught than Math 22 in my opinion, and I genuinely had fun doing PSETS. Definitely to MQC, it was a lifesaver for getting full points on PSETs. For midterms, practice tests are definitely more difficult than the actual exam itself. The final was harder than any of the previous exams, but everything was definitely manageable.
If you're an Ec concentrator taking this for Honors you should probably do 18 instead. I personally find math to be pretty difficult. The grading scheme of this class is pretty generous but a lot of weight is placed onto the tests. The psets take a really long time and I spent a lot of time at MQC – but it's important to do them and understand them in full. My main thing about this class is that it moves incredibly quick and tries to pack in so much information in what I feel like was too short of a time period. The CAs are pretty great and so are the TFs, who are really accessible and personable. It'd be helpful to review basic concepts of multi like R3 prior to starting the course. I also think that the tests are sometimes not super fair (harder than expected).
I learned very much. It moves extremely fast and dives extremely deep into multivariable, more so than most of colleges across the nation, so having a background in it could make life easier. Overall, very satisfied with my performance and the overall learning that took place.
