If you're a NELC concentrator, this is required. The assignments and workload is reasonable. But, if you are taking this because you are not already set on NELC and this is your general introduction, please take other classes! This is just too broad for you to really take away much. Some of the guest lecturers were phenomenal and others were just fine. It was fine, you'll be fine.
This is an excellent course that I’d recommend to NELC or non NELC concentrators. You get a great sense of the topics available within NELC and the workload is reasonable. Dr. Shahvand is also amazing and the nicest guy, and his personal story is super interesting as well. Great course!!
One of my favorite classes this semester. If you are a prospective NELC concentrator, you should look forward to taking this course, and take it EARLY (ideally your sophomore year).

The greatest strength of the course is its structure – each Tuesday is a new topic taught by a new professor within the NELC department. Then, on Thursdays, you meet and discuss the readings + lecture in a session run by a student, usually with a specific interest in the topic. It is a great way to get an introduction to the array of topics within the domain of NELC, and get inspired for courses to take next.

Do the readings, they are generally not too long but very worthwhile!
