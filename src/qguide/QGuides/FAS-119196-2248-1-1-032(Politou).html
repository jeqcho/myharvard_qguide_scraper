What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
You have to put in the time to do well in this course. The exams are very challenging — the pinnacle of the most difficult questions you'll encounter throughout the course, and often harder than all the problems you've practiced with. The course is well organized and there's enough support, but for me it didn't come at MQC as it was always overcrowded. I just really resonated with my TF and tried to ask him as many questions as possible, which is how I clarified my misunderstandings. This course requires a certain mathematical aptitude and creativity/ability to problem solve, so keep that in mind as not everyone can develop it over one semester. But if you do find a way to perform well on the midterms and final, grades won't be an issue.
Stay on top of your work and the psets, try your best to understand content all the time because this class is cumulative.
This class is hard, but manageable if you have a strong work–ethic. None of the material is by itself overly complicated, there is just a lot to juggle at once, and it builds quickly. Staying organized, being attentive in class, and studying smart are key parts of being successful in this course. A few tips... 1) Attempt the whole pset independently before using a help resource, or else you can become too reliant on them. 2) When you have time, watch the videos for the next lesson before class, so you are not so confused at the beginning. 3) To study, do a practice test, grade it, then complete all the extra worksheet problems of the concepts you need help on. Repeat.
need to stay on top with psets, go to office hours with questions
DO YOU PSETS ALONE. Also, figure out what works for you whether that's MQC, or OH, or study groups. I found it really helpful to do my own psets and then check with a 5 person study group on the right answers so we all got full points on the assignments. Also, take all the practice tests, they're really helpful and they're harder than the actual test because they're all the rejected test questions.
Be ready to work, get the Psets done early after class, and understand the workload you are undertaking before you enroll.
I would like to tell future students that this class is hard but definitely do–able. Don't let yourself fall behind, use resources like MQC, Office Hours, and ARC. Don't be afraid to ask your CA's/TF's for help. Paul's Online Math notes is also a good supporting resource for a lot of the content (to get practice questions), even though 21A teaches some stuff differently than the website.
this class is worth it. stay on top of worksheets, psets, and concepts. do extra things for pset drops, you'll thank yourself by the end of the semester.
