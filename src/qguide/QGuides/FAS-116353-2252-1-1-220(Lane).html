What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It is one of the best expos courses you can take. Isabel is amazing!!! She genuinely cares for her students, and she is literally the best expos teacher I could have. If you are interested in social activism and justice, this course might be for you. You also only have 2 papers, and the final project is a creative one(you make a zine about your Paper 2(research paper), which is like a booklet). You also get to talk and write about Hunger Games for Paper 1. However, there are times when reading felt a bit repetitive but I feel like that's natural. The readings are also not hard to digest.
This is definitely one of the better Expos's in term of courseload and content. If you like environmental studies or advocacy, then this is DEFINITELY the expos for you! Dr. Lane is so down to earth and approachable, and is just generally the best! Ask her about her work with the course material–– it's pretty interesting.
This is a solid Expos with a chill environment and interesting material that is relevant. Learning about environmental issues through a justice approach was new but an effective way of thinking about current problems. I enjoyed the capstone final project, since we got to make zines based off of our research papers. It was a new method of communicating ideas that was refreshing and enjoyable. Dr. Lane is supportive and available, and she leads class discussions in a way that isn't high–stakes but more of a conversation.
