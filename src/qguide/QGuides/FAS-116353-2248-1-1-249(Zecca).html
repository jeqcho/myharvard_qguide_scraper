This course is very interesting. There is definitely a lot of reading at the beginning, but it is ultimately manageable. We have great class discussions, and you will learn a lot about the theories of craft.
You need to really like crafts to take this section of Expos. (And by crafts I mean craftsmanship and the theory of craft and quilts not fun arts and crafts.) She also assigns a LOT of reading– there was over 200 pages of reading assigned on the first day of class due in 3 days and that was really overwhelming. I liked Dr. Zecca and she gave good feedback and was pretty helpful and understanding overall though. Her class on horror sounds like kit would've been a lot more interesting than this one. Another big thing that is a plus for this section: you only write 2 papers and make a zine for the final. Other classes have to write three papers, they're just shorter.
Don't take this course if you aren't passionate about learning about craft.
This course will be interesting if you like to talk and learn about the distinction between art and craft. The first unit is highly theoretical where you just read sources about what it means to be a craftsperson and how is intellectually different from art. Which is interesting but a little boring sometimes. Then the second is a research project about a craft object from one of the Harvard museums. This unit is definitely better in my opinion because you get to chose what you write about and it isn't theoretical. Its not that this class is incredibly hard but like if you don't art and craft it might not be the course for you.
Dr. Zecca is passionate about what she is teaching and kind to students. I do think this class was graded somewhat difficultly. You definitly have to put in the work to get a good grade. She doesnt force every student to participate every day, but encourages conversation always (some is required but in workshops). Also really nice that th capstone is just a zine. I had fun with making it! I would often get bored in class, but could be because I am just not very interested in craft at all. Dr. Zecca helped make it better though!
RANK THIS EXPOS! dr. zecca is INCREDIBLE as a lecturer –– she's so kind, understanding, and facilitates dialogue extremely well in the class. she makes it feel like your voice is truly valued in the discussion, and the class atmosphere is very chill. the sources you'll read are super interesting and easy to understand, and i guarantee that you won't look at craft the same way. there is no busy work in this class –– everything builds upon each other, especially for your final essays. dr. zecca gives a litany of feedback for all of the assignments and they're genuinely so helpful –– they're conceptual and provide lots of interesting ideas that are relevant to your paper, so they encourage you to really engage with your curiosity on the subject matter. in–class field trips were a refreshing break from traditional lecture–style classes. this class is a choose–your–own–adventure, making it highly personalizable.
