What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This class is a ton of work and is only incredibly useful if you do put in that work. I think it was absolutely totally worth it and a blast. It's an amazing opportunity to get to know students in the concentration well, and to really get to pick the minds of brilliant microbiologists in the department.
I would tell future students that this class is invaluable in building paper reading skills and understanding of microbiology concepts and experiments. The course design and the professors make this class a clear example of why the Harvard microbiology department is so well–regarded and well loved by the students that are in it. I am so, so grateful that I got the chance to take this class. Simultaneously, this class is a lot of work. I wish I had understood a little more how much time this course would take and perhaps different strategies for reading the papers and preparing to present figures in class.
This is a challenging class to balance with other classes + rotation work. It is a lot of literature reading, especially if you are taking another class that is structured as a literature class (I was, and it was a bit exhausting). Nevertheless, this is a great way to get up to speed on current topics and history of microbiology research, and you get to connect with other microbiology students and faculty in the Longwood area.
Micro 201 is a great introduction to bacteriology and to research in microbiology. It's challenging but rewarding and I'd strongly recommend someone with interest in micro take the class. Most fun I've had in a class
