What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
The lectures and course set–up themselves were good, and the course provides a wide overview of topics in modern causal inference. However, the topics and subject matter itself often felt incomplete: it was often unclear why we were pursuing a particular mathematical strategy, and we seemed to start some topics but not actually finish them before moving on to new topics.
Think carefully if you truly want to take this course. The homework, journal club and presentation is a bunch of workload.
I highly recommend this class to any student with an interest in causal inference, so long as one has the appropriate background (e.g., the first–year Biostatistics PhD core courses). I found that many of the concepts in this class were directly relevant to improving my own research. Overall, a fantastic way to get acquainted to the field to a surprisingly deep level within the span of a semester. As it is a newer course, some content is likely subject to change from year to year, but the core content of the course is extremely valuable.
The topics of this course are appropriate and useful. Instruction can be slow and because of this less interesting.
