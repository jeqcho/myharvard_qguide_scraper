If you are interested in learning more about metaphysics, this is a fantastic course. The readings are super interesting, and Professor Pham does a great job explaining and extending them in lecture. The lectures are very engaging, and she is super helpful and available outside of class. The readings are intellectually challenging but not necessarily super difficult or long; all of the assignments are quite interesting and very fair. By the end of the course, you will have a very good working sense of the field of metaphysics and its major debates, and also have improved philosophical soft skills (reading, thinking, writing, etc.). By the nature of the subject matter, this course probably isnt for you if you arent interested in abstract philosophy, but as long as you at least slightly are, it is extremely interesting and enjoyable.
I really enjoyed this course! I felt like I learned a lot and I always looked forward to class. The assignments were extremely reasonable in scope and helped me productively engage with the material definitely not busy work. The midterms were very fair and (dare I say) genuinely kind of fun!

Dr. Pham was obviously extremely knowledgeable about the material and responded to everyones questions (from the basic questions to the more complex) with clarity, deft, and (often) humor. Relatedly, Dr. Pham was a skilled facilitator, and did a good job of encouraging participation without ever resulting to cold–calling (which I really appreciated as someone who is shier in class). She was a great hire by the philosophy department and Im disappointed I wont be around to take more or her classes!

One thing to note (for better or for worse depending on what youre looking for) is that rigor–wise, Id place this course at very much an intro level. Certainly everyone can get a ton out of the readings (which can be very complex), and it is an intro to metaphysics after all, but more than just being an intro to the specific content it also felt like the bar for our work was relatively low considering it was a 100s–level course. This was a downside to the extent that I am left wondering a little over how much further I couldve gone if we were pushed a bit harder, butttt I was definitely more helped than hurt by the fact this course enabled me to learn without inflicting life–shortening amounts of stress!

Overall, highly recommend!
Take this class! Its my favorite class that Ive taken so far, and Im so glad I chose to take it. Some of the readings may seem intimidating, but Professor Pham is incredible at explaining the concepts in a way that really makes them accessible. Professor Pham is an incredible teacher, definitely go to her office hoursthat was my favorite aspect of the course.
It's a great class to start with in philosophy, as Professor Pham is very interested in educating not just about the topics, but about how contemporary philosophy operates as a whole. She doesn't dumb anything down, but gives you a strong understanding of the fundamentals.
Professor Pham is wonderful and the class will push you to think and develop positions on topics you may not have found relevant to your life at first. The readings and lectures are great. I especially recommend this class to philosophy students, but anyone can participate.
Professor Pham was wonderfully accessible, engaging, and prepared. She graded exams quickly and generously, and was very adamant about making use of her office hours if we had any doubts at all about how to complete assignments. She understood the material well and had great responses to questions beyond the scope of the reading that are a hallmark of someone who enjoys what they study.

The class itself discusses existence, from possible worlds to holes to time, and goes through a multitude of thought experiments like the Ship of Theseus that are fascinating. This course is a fun course for these thought puzzles but also provides useful theories and frameworks to think about the world, from space–time worms to four–dimensional rotations. Having an interest in rigor and mathematical logic can help parse some of the readings, which can be long or dense or both, but they are always at least broken down in lecture very well and have a lot of extra information to rigorously derive their arguments.

Overall, I would recommend this course to anyone who's interested. It's not a free course but its readings are genuinely very interesting, even if you only skim them or don't fully understand them, and the exams do not need preparation if you do the readings. The responses and essays can be difficult to tackle but much of the work is coming up with ideas about the readings or your own writing and explicating them, instead of the drudgery of having to find sources or add tons of context. This is a pretty standard 100s level Philosophy course taught very well and is very engaging!
If you're interested in philosophy and logic, take this class! It does what it wants to do (give an introductory overview to different topics in metaphysics) VERY well. It was my first philosophy class, and I left it with a decent understanding of how to read/write analytic philosophy.

However, if you're not interested in philosophy, I don't think this class is worth it (or maybe worth to just shop it). The topics covered can feel VERY abstract and detached from everyday life–––I don't think that it changed who I am in any sense, or changed the way I view anything in the world. It felt fun and useful to me as a purely intellectual exercise. But you'll get a sense of this after the first two classes or so, so definitely worth shopping.
Take it before you graduate, even if you have no interest in philosophy.
This was a wonderful class! I am so glad that I took it. Professor Pham is a brilliantly clear and engaging lecturer. The readings are very interesting. The assignments and exams are straightforward. You will gain a nuanced and practical understanding of metaphysics and how it applies to everyday life. You will go deeper than the basic question of "what exists?", zooming in on various theories of time, space, and identity. I highly recommend.
Don't forget to speak up in class – you will really enjoy being a part of the discussions.
