This class is very little work and also a good deal of fun. If you have a lot of background in math outside of school then you probably already know a very big chunk of the material.
Fine class
Take this class! The material is super interesting and the format is super chill. Final project is optional.
This class is super chill and fun, but that might depend a bit on who is teaching it. You can really get as much out of it as you want. There are opportunities to think really deeply about some of the topics, but if you're really uninterested in something you can get by without too much trouble. Also, after the first couple weeks, there was a LOT of number theory, which got a bit annoying, but it eventually ended.
Good course for exploring some niche mathematical topics in broader contexts.
PSETs are sort of difficult but worth 100% of your grade. It won't be the most rewarding academic experience but a nice class to take senior spring and finish off requirements with.
The course is essentially quant interview prep: number theory that's essentially logic puzzles, probability in the style of Stat 110, combinatorics. There was a weirdly large amount of information theory. The concepts don't take a lot of effort to understand, and most of them will be familiar if you're a mathie. Some of the weekly problem sets can take a while... the later ones took me upwards of 12 hours.
This is a good senior spring class if you wanna hang out with friends and think through some interesting problems with minimal workload otherwise. The problem sets are non–trivial though (at least for me as a non–math concentrator), and the problems get significantly less interesting later in the semester compared to the first couple weeks.
take the class!! so fun and gemmy. an easy way to fulfill your extra math classes requirement.
If you want a chill math class this is a pretty good one. You still have psets so it is harder than many humanities but if you know math they are all easy. If you are looking for a serious course obviously you wouldn't be on the page so this class is pretty much what you would expect.
The CAs in the class made it difficult to get help or ask more questions because they weren't present (except for the first two days) in class/office hours.
This class if fine. Nothing crazy exciting or interesting, but a lighter math course. Also, be prepared for how chaotic and unprepared your CAs and Tf will be.
This is a fun class to learn topics you might not encounter in other classes but are still interesting to learn and applicable!
Super fun class. Very little work or pressure. Take with some friends and you can just hang out working on little problems
This is a great way to engage casually with math!! This course exposed us to a lot of different types of math while giving space for genuinely fun collaboration. If you’re looking for a rigorous course that will challenge your math skills this is not it — but if you want the chance to exercise your math brain in a low stress environment this is perfect.
This was my favourite math course I've taken at Harvard. The problem sets were super interesting and I loved getting to work in groups on difficult but fun math problems. Would highly recommend.
