If you're doing stats / mathematical econ / etc., real analysis is just a must–take, and math 112 is a good, manageable way to cover Rudin. The content is not going to be riveting but you just have to sit through it. This semester math 112 was taught by Prof. Park Sunghyuk, who was a good lecturer. He basically taught from Rudin verbatim , but lectures were still worth going to because people asked a lot of clarifying questions and Prof. Park is open to fielding questions. He's also quite approachable.

Psets were from Rudin and very manageable, and exams could be quite difficult / a time–crunch but was remediated by the extremely generous amount of extra credit on psets throughout the semester.
It will take many hours per week to complete the psets, but since they are 70% of the grade, it is in line with my expectations.
