Comments
This course introduces literary methods that are common today, but also that many students don't need much coaching to be familiar with (ie reading texts with an eye to gender and sexuality, race, empire, etc.). Not a place for critical thought, but a place to make bland comments in agreeing with everything the texts say.
Professor Miller is an amazing professor, and so was the TF at the time (Celine Shanosky)–– take their classes!
I would tell them to do the reading!
This is a wonderful class. It is heavily focused on discussion, which I liked, and Professor Miller is really kind, knows a lot, and is happy to talk about whatever we are interested in talking about. There is lots of theory, which I found fascinating and the assignments are all good.
Unfortunately, this course was not one I enjoyed taking. The seminars were unproductive at best, boring at worst. The bent of the course seems to be quasi–anti–intellectual: the more general and imprecise your statements, the better. Discussions in class were sub–spark–notes–level. One only remembers that one is at Harvard upon leaving the room.
Professor Miller is so caring and I really enjoyed his style of teaching. The course itself is flawed, and you're undeniably going to come across things that you don't like, but ultimately it's a good course!
This is a great foundational course in the English department! I ended up deciding not to concentrate in English and I still feel like I got a lot out of this course. I've definitely come away from this class a better literary thinker. I feel more confident using the language of literary analysis.
