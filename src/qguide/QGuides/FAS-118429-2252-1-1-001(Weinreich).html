This rendition of MATH118r was a mixed bag. Max is a kind lecturer and works hard. Though, at times, the course quality can drop (whether in lecture or homework assignments). The course does well at being accessible to a large audience, including physicists etc., but if you are there for the mathematics then you might find rigor lacking.
The class was mathematically interesting and required engagement, but it was totally reasonable in the workload too. It was project–based (at least this year), which took some stress off during midterms and finals season. Overall, I would definitely take it again. My only real gripe was that the attendance policy was changed from "optional / not part of grade" to "mandatory / part of grade," with sign–ins and detractions for absences, several weeks into the course (Week 4 or something?). As someone who doesn't learn that well from lectures, I think it's kind of uncool to change such an important course policy partway through the semester. Otherwise, I really liked Max.
This is a great option for anyone looking to take a pure math course that isn't too challenging. Max was a fun and engaging lecturer, and none of the assignments were overly challenging. However, the content felt disorganized at times, and didn't always follow a clear direction. Take this course if you want to be introduced to a variety of topics at a surface level, not if you want to learn a single subject in–depth.
Max is a wonderful instructor. He constructs the course in a way such that everyone can enjoy the course regardless of their background.
It's so different depending on who is teaching it. The biggest thing I have learned from the math department: NEVER look at the Q guides for a class because they lack the ability to standardize curriculum and it singularly depends on the professor. Max is super nice, I didn't like the content and I'm not sure if that was his fault.
Very easy class. Spent too much time on discrete dynamics IMO since it was the instructor’s expertise. Learned interesting things but overall felt like we missed very important things, for example we never learned normal forms, continuous time bifurcations, limit cycles, and other fundamental concepts. As an R class, it felt more like we were covering random topics related to dynamics and less like a structured introduction to the topic. Although I really appreciated the low workload, the class was a little too easy to learn much from and the textbooks were also very easy, however for a low–workload senior class it worked nicely especially paired with reading Wiggins on my own time.
