What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

This is an amazing course; if you're on the fence about taking it, I would strongly advise doing so. This course provides the opportunity to engage with a wide variety of different forms of Russian art—short stories, novels, plays, operas, ballets, movies, and more. The entire syllabus is thoughtfully selected, and provides a great taste of fundamental Russian art pieces from the 19th century. Prof. Buckler is extremely knowledgable about all of the material, and does a fantastic job adapting the course throughout the semester to respond to student feedback. If you're curious about any specific aspects of the history, reception, criticism, etc. of the works that are covered, she also does a great job responding to individual interests. That said, this course is not at all intimidating, and is quite friendly to those without any prior exposure to Russian art / literary analysis (I've never taken a course like this, and was still able to confidently contribute to each discussion). Weekly readings are substantial but not overwhelming, and assignments are not stressful (the oral exam was slightly scary in its uniqueness, but I felt well prepared to take it). The final project is open–ended, so you have the opportunity to play to your strengths.
There will be a great amount of reading in some part of the class, but it will be worth it.
I recommend this class with enthusiasm! By no means do you have to be a Slavic concentrator/have a Russian literature background to take this class. I highly, highly recommend it for anyone who has an interest in genuinely good literature, plays, and other works of art. Professor Buckler is absolutely incredible, and I will be looking to take more of her classes in the future.
The crown jewel of the course is the week on Anna Karenina but the other authors (Gogol, Chekhov, Pushkin) are very nice too. You do need to keep up with reading for the class to remain interesting, but it is not a huge burden at all. Professor Buckler is lovely and the class is engaging. There are dry points as there are with every class but if you are looking for a fun elective that will broaden your literary palate without overly consuming your time, I would recommend this class.
This is a beautiful course that really provides a fantastic history of Russia and a complex understanding of literature through the golden age of Russia.
Take this class! I came into the course as a STEM student with no background and left with a deep appreciation and newfound interest in Russian literature/arts. Professor Buckler and Natasha are amazing instructors who share a lot of passion for the course material. They will make sure that you are caught up to the course material and will work with you in cases of illnesses/other emergencies. All the course contents are very interesting and touch different genres from authors like Pushkin, Gogol, Chekhov, and of course Tolstoy. I watched my first ballet and opera as part of the coursework even (as you can realize I had not been particularly inclined to the appreciation of art), and realized that I really enjoyed historical opera! The courseload is also very managable and targeted to make students enjoy the course material rather than focus on completeing assignments.
If you are looking for an accessible way to dip your toe into Russian literature, then this is the course for you! Professor Buckler is extremely engaging, and the syllabus is well–curated. You will be guided through the Russian greats with attention to the history as well as the culture. There was good variety too –– we watched an opera, a ballet, and several movies to complement the literature. The midterm oral exam was slightly stressful, but if you were reading along and paying attention in class, you would be able to study without much difficulty. I have taken a LOT of literature courses (senior, humanities major) and this one was excellent. It wasn't a gem, per se, but that meant that people were actually there because they were interested, which was well worth it. Give small departments a try!
This class is an incredible way to read the most famous works of Russian literature fro pleasure while also getting a chance to study them in an academic setting with Professor Buckler. Take this class, it is a very relaxed and enjoyable class.
I learned so much about Russian history, culture, literature, and how the world works in general. This course made me a more intelligent person!
Just take it!
TAKE THIS CLASS. Even if you have no experience in Russia or Slavic studies, this class is incredibly accessible and is a great way to fulfill the arts and humanities distributional requirement. The readings are super enjoyable and you will learn a lot. Professor Buckler is lovely and great at dividing the class between giving you information and discussion.
Take this class! Professor Buckler is an amazing lecturer, and you will learn so much about Russian literature and culture. All of the books are fascinating and enjoyable, and you will also learn about the performing arts, which is very fun.
Please take this class! I hadn't had the chance to read fiction in a class here before I took this course, and this was one of the most valuable experiences I've had here. The novels, short stories, and plays you'll read are coupled with a good deal of historical and cultural context, which helped me really immerse myself in the course. The structure of the course allows you to really spend time reading these great works, Anna Karenina on down, plus there aren't any tedious essays, just short discussion responses, and you can really do whatever you want on the final project. Prof. Buckler is a fantastic lecturer, and her enthusiasm for the course content really shines through. If you want to enjoy reading again and/or explore the rich literary culture of this period, definitely take this course!
