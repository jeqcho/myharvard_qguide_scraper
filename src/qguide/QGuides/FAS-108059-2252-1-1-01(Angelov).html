HIST 1035 is an all–around solid elective course, and a great way for more modern history focused students to fulfill the pre–1750 history requirement. Great lectures, solid course content, a well thought out syllabus, a great TF. Prof. Angelov himself is a wealth of knowledge — I can't recall any question that stumped him, and he's a great resource. For those interested in pre–1750 history, this is an even better time. You'll learn LOADS about everything to do with the Byzantine Empire from the 300s all the way to 1453. This kind of survey course is perhaps the kind of course the history department should be offering more of.
If you are interested in medeivel history, this course is a must. The Byzantine Empire was in the middle of Russian, Islamic, and European history with the history of Christianity always in the background. Prof Angelov does an excellent job of taking a very complex history and making it manageable. His lectures are informative, he is very engaging, and the TF Andy is an excellent teacher as well. They are both dedicated to ensuring that you learn Byzantine history. They go out of their way to clear up any confusion between OH, review sessions, and section.
Great survey course of Byzantine history – professor is passionate and students are interested in the material, making for great class discussions.
fantastic course, I went in with zero background on medieval history, let alone Byzantine history, and was thoroughly pleased with the experience of both the professor and the TA, the lectures are generally very digestible and walk you through a clear timeline of the Byzantine Empire over the course of the semester, just make sure you take notes during lecture so that you get all the context of the slides
This is an excellent history class. The readings are reasonable and well–chosen, the lectures are engaging, and the assignments are targeted and brief. Understanding Byzantine history helps students to better understand the interaction of Roman law, Greek culture, and Christianity in Western culture.
This class is a really well structured history class that helps you understand how to use sources, and how to study an empire that is fell so many centuries ago. The content is fascinating to learn since the Byzantine Empire is often overlooked relative to other Empires. Prof. Angelov and Andy were also very involved and helpful throughout the course and provides support to those who might not have a background in history or in the time period.
Tbh didn't have time to do many of the readings, just crammed before the midterm and final... Wish I had more time to do them, though they seemed very interesting and well–picked.

For the response papers, GO TO OFFICE HOURSE BEFOREHAND. sometimes the section leader can be a bit more stringent on formatting, it's good to check in before to see what they want.

Overall solid class, wouldn't say gem (if you put in the work), but if you've got a busy schedule it's still a doable class. Prof Angelov is very funny sometimes and Andy is wonderful!
Hello history concentrators looking for a pre–1750 requirement! This is a pretty good option – Prof. Angelov is such a sweet professor, I honestly would take it again just for him, because it was a great time. Andy is actually an amazing TF to get to know, so also would highly recommend just for him. To be honest, I don't know if Byzantium is my thing after this class, HOWEVER I honestly had a great time. The assignments aren't too difficult and neither is the midterm or final. They are very generous and are not trying to trip you up – just know the material and what they expect of you.
Definitely take this class! Professor Angelov really makes it shine, the material is interesting, and it's a great introduction to medieval history that isn't too difficult.
