What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an awesome class! The Korean department is so nice and friendly. The class does move pretty fast, but you'll learn a lot and have support from the teachers.
If you are planning on having some important application of Korean in the future (family, friends, job), this class is an excellent introduction. It is not a class to take lightly – you shouldn't come waltzing in with the hope of picking up a few words and phrases – you should be committed to learn the language in its entirety, with weekly writing and speaking evaluations. You will get the most out of what you put in.
Park SSN is the GOAT! She's the only reason I took this class even though it was really difficult and I wanted to drop. This course is pretty difficult if you don't have any Korean background. The majority of your grade in this class is made of weekly quizzes, which I bombed because I didn't study. But you should study!
The professors are amazing and looking to help whenever you need it. Take the class if you are willing to work and want to learn Korean. It is definitely a challenging course if you don't have any Korean background but the professors structure it for you to succeed.
You might be afraid to take this course because it meets every day of the week, but the lectures and sections are taught so well and have so much camaraderie that it really doesn't feel excessive at all! In fact, the daily practice really helps sharpen your language skills; you'll learn so much in just one semester in a very well–structured and well–taught environment.
Best class.
Korean is definitely a language worth taking. By the end of the semester, it's really nice to realize how much you've learned and be able to use your Korean in basic conversation with your classmates or friends who also know any amount of Korean.
There are a lot more Korean–American students in this class than you might expect. Great way for an intro to Korean, and the professors sometimes bring food :)
Be prepared to sometimes have to neglect other classes/activities to stay on top of the workload.
