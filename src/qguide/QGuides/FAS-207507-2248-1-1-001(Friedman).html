Prof Friedman is the best! The readings and discussions themselves can be quite long, but the subject itself is very interesting and applicable to the outside world. You do not have to do the readings for the discussions nor assignments (which are very chill and ungraded), but you take more out of the class if you do. Overall, quite enjoyable and a very solid first–year seminar.
This is an amazing course if you're interested in Economics, Computer Science, or the future of AI. You get to talk to a renowned professor and read top academics' opinions on where AI will go in the future. The class is discussion based, so you can speak freely about your ideas. Although the class has group debates toward the end, it's not a very social class, and your experience may depend on who is in the seminar. However, the content and professor are great, and listening to your peers' ideas is very enlightening.
This is an excellent seminar if you are interested in learning more deeply about how AI impacts our world from historical, ethical, and current day viewpoints. The class is really what you make of it, so please take if you have interest on tech and how it impacts us as the more you put into it the more you learn; applicable to every field AI touches so basically every field
Take this class. Coming from Sri Lanka, I was unfamiliar with US economic policy, but that did not matter, as the assigned readings teach you everything you need to know. Professor Friedman is exceptionally accommodating. He takes great effort to get to know all his students on a more personal level and is happy to help with all matters both inside and outside the classroom. It also counts as Economics elective credit, which is the cherry on top!
This class is so much fun. The professor is geunienly passionate about this topic and is interested in learning from the students just as much as he is in teaching the students. The discussions are relevant and fascinating
