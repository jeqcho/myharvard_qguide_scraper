What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Please take this course if you have any interest in any field involving research or data science. The R that you will be taught in this seminar will be greatly beneficial to you regardless of what your concentration is, R is the language of research and data analysis. Being able to code in R is a must–have for concentrations like psychology, statistics, economics, and computer science which all have you handle data like you will learn how to do in this seminar. Knowing how to code in R will put you ahead of everyone else in other classes that require the use of R, the statistics portion of the class is informative although not a very in–depth introduction to statistics. The best part of this course, in my opinion, is learning R and learning how to create graphs, the feeling of successfully creating a graph and it coming out perfectly is an amazing feeling and the feeling that you have created something is unmatched. Absolutely take this course, Andrea is an amazing professor who gives great feedback and is incredibly kind. – MN
Strongly recommend this seminar!! Dr. Foulkes is such a sweet and kind professor and she is so dedicated to making the course a good experience for students. The material/readings are really interesting and you also learn a lot of R!
This is a good course that allows students to have fun playing around with different types of data. There is also an introduction into R, which, although not super thorough, is engaging and solid on a practical level. Andrea also does a good job of adapting the course to what the students would like to do more of; in my class the consensus was wanting to work more in R, so she shifted the last few weeks of the class to focus on that.
More coding than expected
This class is a really great class that covers interesting content. As it is a Freshman Seminar, it is also relatively easy. You get what you put into this class, and it is quite easy to not put a lot in and still make it through the class. In this way, the course does teach R, however, because of the nature of the course it is quite difficult to facilitate the learning of new coding language without more of a rigorous structure. That being said, if you did the work outside of class to study what we were learning in class, you probably could leave with a good understanding of the language.
