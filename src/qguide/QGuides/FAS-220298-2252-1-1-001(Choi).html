As an Asian American female, this course allowed me to reflect a lot on my and others' experiences growing up. I learned a lot about the context/history of the times that the writers we read were in. There was a good variety of readings that were interesting and spanned across centuries from across East Asia. It was good to discuss and analyze these texts in a space made up of mostly other Asians / Asian Americans. Professor Choi is very very kind and reasonable, but stresses out a lot about things that don't need to be worried about. She is chill most of the time, but has a lot of things that she wants to be done a certain way and will not accept anything otherwise. She likes her classroom to be run a specific way and students to be very engaged. She also will always bring up something that you mentioned many weeks ago (i.e called a student out in the middle of class for not eating a snack she bought for the class because the student told her she liked that snack many weeks ago) or call someone out for something they said weeks ago or knows about them (i.e one student studied French previously and she always called that student out when there was a single French word or person mentioned and "quizzed" her or made her explain to the class even when she didn't know or seemed uncomfortable). Don't get me wrong, she is so so knowledgeable, passionate, and very sweet, but very "helicopter" mom–like in a way that is uncomfortable/cringe in certain situations. She also just makes us read off the lecture slides then we talk about the readings in pairs then as a class, same thing every class. She brings snacks, though! It's hard to explain to anyone that doesn't know her. I do recommend the class because the materials are fascinating, interesting, and relevant. Just be prepared to be cold–called constantly, no electronics, and all readings on paper.
please take this class if you get the chance to!! this was easily one of my top3 classes taken here. the readings can be a little weird sometimes but everything overall was super interesting and gives you insight into east asian women's experiences in modern and pre–modern times. everything was just so enjoyable, from the readings to classes
prof choi is one of the sweetest professors ive had the pleasure of taking a class with
Professor Choi is so wise, dedicated, suppotive, and enthusiastic. The texts we read are phenomenal and I would not have known about them if I had not taken this class and I thank her for introducing them to me. They gave me colour on my own history and was exactly what I was looking for to unearth something I am trying to learn more about but find difficult to study on my own. The class is well structured and very manageable and a community is fostered amongst your classmates.
I learned a lot about ancient Asian female writers–––I think I had no knowledge or background coming in and I had no expectations. I also feel that it humanizes a niche demographic in history that was not perceived as intellectual. The essays were very intellectually stimulating but not so difficult that I felt stress over completing them on time. Professor Choi definitely follows the syllabus, but she is also lenient as long as you communicate to her ahead of time that you might need an extension or extra help.
TAKE THIS CLASS! I have learned so much about myself. There were very intricate feelings that I did not know how to vocalize as an Asian American woman that this class has been able to validate and explain. The readings are fun to read and are well chosen. I also liked learning more about East Asian history, which is helpful to learn and know. I really gained so much valuable insight from this class.

Professor Choi is so sweet. She brings snacks every class, has guest writing speakers, and even a fun cooking workshop. She is flexible on deadlines if you talk to her.

The reading workload varies week to week. Some weeks are much heavier than others. Overall, it was a good change from my usual problem sets. Manage your time well because doing the readings allows you to connect with the in–class discussions much more deeply. The conversations are rich and leaves you thinking even outside of class.
Take the course! One thing to note, while it is implied, but not explicitly, stated the course is very East Asian centric. So, if you're looking for a course about the broader Asian female diaspora, this may not fit. Nonetheless the course was super interesting, extremely manageable, and Dr. Choi is a great professor to have. You'll have reasonable weekly readings, with a short written assignment (~2 pages) every few weeks or so, culminating in a creative final project that you can really make your own. I loved getting to meet the other students in such an intimate setting and listening to all of their thoughts and final projects at the end of the semester. Readings consist of many seminal works by traditional and modern Korean, Japanese, and Chinese women as well as thought–provoking films. Awesome class, would definitely take it again. I took it as a 5th class and it did not add much to my coursework, and Dr. Choi is always great at checking in to make sure everyone isn't overwhelmed and engaged. She does a great job at engaging even the quieter students and making sure everyone feels welcome and included. Loved this course.
Dr. Choi is a wonderful professor with a strong ability to elicit active in–class discussion and promote inclusion, though this is of course a multi–party effort. She has rather strict but clear expectations for students that, should a conflict cause you to fall short of meeting them, can at times be frustrating to make up for. Nonetheless, she brings out the best in students and I looked forward to this class every week. You don't really need any background in Asian–American history or literature to still get a lot out of this course. If you are looking for theory, you won't get very much of that through the syllabus, since the readings are all primary sources; but if you are looking to apply theory, this is a great class to do that in.
Highly recommend this class! It was an eye–opening experience into how women in East Asia and Asian American women have expressed themselves and their lives over history.
You will not regret taking a class with Professor Choi. She is warm, patient, and her genuine investment in the success of her students shines through. She makes every class feel
engaging and rewarding both on an intellectual and personal level. The course material is never dry nor boring, and she cultivates a classroom environment in which everyone is open to hearing and engaging with other perspectives.
