The GOV 97 curriculum is pretty reasonable, and covers the elements of writing a through research paper well (sans any data analysis). Writing said paper was not too difficult, but I would recommend choosing a topic you're genuinely interested in, as I delayed coming up with one until pretty late, and ended up writing about something fairly esoteric that I wasn't terribly excited about. I enjoyed Noah's "Political Geography" section, if it's still being offered in the future. He's a super chill guy who takes what you have to say seriously (and also is a pretty generous grader, based on what I expected to earn haha). His weekly assignments didn't really require much work, and were kind of abandoned half–way through the course anyway. Political geography ended up being a really interesting topic, as it kind of covers just about anything as long as you can geographically orient it. The readings were pretty varied but all interesting.
