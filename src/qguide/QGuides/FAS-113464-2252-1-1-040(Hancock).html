Math MB is great. The staff and CAs are fantastic. A lot of people complain about the Math department at Harvard because they teach things differently than most people are used to, but as someone who hadn't taken a Math class in like 8 years I thought it really allowed me to understand what I was doing rather than just following a set of rules to spit out correct answers. Like any other Math class it's 3 PSETs a week. Yeah it kind of sucks but most of the time they take like 1–2 hours and if you do it right after section it's not so bad. There's 3 skills checks and 2 mid–terms. None of them are particularly difficult as long as you've payed attention in class and done your PSETs. That's the really nice thing about Math here is there's no real way to fall behind as long as you're doing the bare minimum.

If you have already taken Math MA then you're probably choosing between MB and Math LS, Q, etc. If you plan on or are even considering concentrating in anything that requires a higher level math course (1b, 21a, etc.) take this course. If you're Econ or Pre–Med and you don't need more Math take Q or LS respectively. You learn a lot of the same concepts but they get applied to your actual field of study rather than being straight math classes.
Embrace the difficulty/newness of the course because it will begin to flow more if you aren't actively dreading the class.
