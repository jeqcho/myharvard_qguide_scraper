GEM GEM GEM GEM GEM GEM GEM. Learned so much valuable info about Harvard Forest, local environments, and global–change issues that apply to all of us. Dave is the goat. He presents information in a digestible manner and is overall very personable. All assignments are very doable, just put in effort. If you are passionate/interested in global change, and like being outside, this class is for you.
If you are interested in climate change, global change, ecology, forests, carbon, or the history of New England's landscape, TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!!

This is by far one of the best and most interesting classes I have ever taken! Besides the extremely interesting content of the class, the class itself was an incredible experience. You get to go to the forest, stay in a really nice house, and bond with 9 other students for a whole weekend, three times throughout the semester. These weekends served as extremely nice and relaxing breaks from the rest of the semester, and I still got a lot of work done for other classes at night while there. The group was great and had some of the nicest people I've met on campus, and since you will all share similar interests if you are in this class, there are endless things to talk about with the group. I personally found some great friends in the class, people who I will hopefully stay close with throughout the rest of my 4 years at Harvard.

Additionally, as for the class itself, it was the best learning environment I have ever seen in a class. It provided an extremely immersive learning environment where we were enabled not only to be much more interested in what we learned, but we could have a dialogue about each topic and ask further questions about things that were not necessarily pointed out to us, since we were in an immersive environment where our own observations were appreciated and adapted into the fluently shifting yet curriculum–based conversations. We got to see real, clear evidence of global change, learn why and how it happened, and how it can be mitigated or better understood through experiments that were occurring.

The content was super interesting and I had some great conversations with Dave and the rest of the class about climate change and just anything that came up in conversation. It really helped me to see that there absolutely is a lot we can do about climate change, and inspired me even more to pursue research in my future. I think I understand global change, forests, and ecology much more than ever before as a result of this class, as there is just so much content, and it is provided in a very digestible and engaging manner.

Dave is also great and such a knowledgeable guy, and is typically able to answer just about any question that the students ask him.

I truly encourage all to apply for and take this course, it was seriously the best class I had this semester and an incredible introduction to Harvard.
This course really gives you a chance to breathe during the overwhelming stressful time that is your freshman fall. Once a month, you have this amazing opportunity to go spend a weekend getaway in the forest with a bunch of amazing people. During your time there, you get to learn from AMAZING professor Dave, who is so down to earth and easy to talk to, then you get back to the house by 5pm, having dinner and reflecting over the day, then getting the rest of the night to work on homework from other classes, sleep, play board games with the others or go out stargazing! This course offers so much flexibility, as you only meet once a month which is during your time at the forest, and you learn such concrete information about ecology, environmental science, meteorology and so much more! You get to hear from experts in these fields and you do a lot of hands–on learning, lots of walking/hiking trails in these forests and minimal time sitting/taking notes as you would in a regular classroom. There are times where we had sat down in classrooms within Harvard's forest to hear from experts but these were rare and when we did it was just equally as engaging because they were sharing with us real concrete research about work they were doing and its impact. I had not been someone who had ever hiked or spent time in the forest prior to this course, having mainly done advocacy work related to environmental science. However this course is super friendly to everyone from many disciplines, some of us were Government concentrators, Astrophysics concentrators, Planetary Studies concentrators and so much more. There were such little assignments in this course compared to other seminars! After each weekend in the forest, we would have a week to write a 1–2 page reflection paper on what we learned while in the forest, main takeaways, etc., which was always super doable and manageable. While in the forest, there are no assignments of any kind, we would just take notes whether through our phones or on papers while walking, to collect all the information we were learning about. During our last weekend in November, Dave introduced the final assignment, which was a paper & presentation on any element related to global change which was a super fun/creative assignment that we had a lot of time to work on. Honestly, take take take this class!!!!!!!!! It’s so refreshing and filled with laughs and pure awe for the nature that surrounds us. I made some of my closest friends through this class and made some of my best freshman fall memories at the forest!
Take this course if you can. As a first year course, its different format was refreshing. The weekends away from campus really allow you to focus on the material as well as relax and get perspective on your Harvard experience. That being said, you'll be doing some walking in the forest, so if you're not mildly outdoorsy this might not be your thing.
This course is an absolutely brilliant experience! Both for someone interested in climate and sustainability and novice to the topic, I think it is an incredible opportunity to spend time in nature, take a break from the buzz of campus activity and learn a wealth of information about global change in the New England forests. Even if you don't consider yourself an outdoorsy person who loves spending time, hugging trees by the end of the seminar, you'll appreciate the natural world so much more – take it and love it!

The atmosphere is fun and friendly and the accomodations are so nice and very comfortable. The catered food is great and they buy us plenty of snacks. Berries!
