What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Whatever you do, don't take Math 1B in the Fall. Only take this class if it's absolutely necessary for a concentration requirement. Its unnecessarily difficult and overloaded with content that can be impossible to keep up with. The problem sets are time–consuming but only account for 25% of your grade. Youll likely feel like youre struggling the whole time. Calculus 2 shouldnt be this hard! Students who took it in the Spring or Summer definitely had a better experience. Make sure to go to office hours, go to MQC, and get a coach if you dont do well on any exams.
I definitely devoted more time to this class than my other classes (I took it first semester freshman year), but everyone's relationship with math is different. Problem sets took me a while, and I devoted a lot of time to studying for tests, redoing trouble problems from homework as well as making sure I fully understand every problem on every practice test. The tests themselves were usually more straightforward than the practice tests, so don't let it freak you out if you're studying and the questions on practice tests seem a lot more convoluted than you're used to. They're pretty nitpicky about grading homework which I wasn't used to, so definitely devote adequate effort to that. I would've saved myself a lot of confusion in class if I had watched pre–lecture videos consistently before class meetings, and there were also many instances when I wasted a lot of time trying to figure out a problem set question on my own just to go to office hours and have it explained to me in a minute. Some office hours are crowded and stressful, but it's still worth it to figure out which ones are helpful for you to go to because it definitely played a big role in helping me understand confusing topics and clarify some of my concerns.
This course is not difficult if you pay attention in class, do your pets, and attend office hours/mqc for any questions. Also, find a pset buddy/group it is really helpful. DO NOT BE AFRAID TO ASK QUESTIONS DURING SECTION. This is when 90% of learning is done so please take advantage of having your TF readily accessible and ask questions when you are unsure.
I wouldn't take this course unless it was required. Be prepared for a hefty workload; 3–psets a week gets pretty gnarly. My section had a pretty nice environment, though it was cramped (30+ people in one of the small Science Center rooms). Don't be afraid to take this course though. I went in with only having taken AP Calc AB and had middling algebra skills and I did fine.
If you are familiar with integration and differential equations, skip this course and maybe choose a higher level one.
There are 3 PSets a week and you have to be on top of them. Once you finish one, start on the next one. Also, if youre deciding to choose between this class or a higher class (particularly Math 21A), I recommend this one! I regret not having switched into this class earlier in the semester, but it still was the best decision I made. I would have spent much more time in the other class and would have not enjoyed it at all.
In my experience, it was easiest to just get the problem set done on the day you get it (if your schedule allows for it). This gave me more peace knowing it was done, and I was able to work on other class assignments. This class is three problem sets a week, so there is a big time commitment that you will need to account for. I found that every TF was extremely helpful in class or during office hours (go to office hours! It was so helpful). I especially found Jonier's office hours to be when I got my best work done.
