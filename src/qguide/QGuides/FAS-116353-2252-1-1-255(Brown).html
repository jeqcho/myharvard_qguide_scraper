What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
The material was genuinely engaging without being overwhelming, and you can clearly tell how passionate Dr. Brown is about the subject. If you’re interested in literature, you’ll definitely enjoy this course—but even if you’re not, the class had a majority of STEM and Economics students, which actually fostered unique and compelling discussions.
Don't dismiss this expos section simply because you have no interest in the topic! Brown is a great professor and makes sure to get to know each of his students individually, and is great to converse with.
I really enjoyed this expos, and I would recommend it! If you want an expos with a more literary focus, than this is probably a good option. A lot of the class is discussion on the books/movies/short stories we read, and my section had a really good environment. Dr. Brown really cares about his students and he’s very supportive and patient in the conferences. The homework was very manageable most weeks, but you should make sure you’re really using the assignments to stay on schedule with your essays.

I think I usually spent too much time stuck on what I wanted to say, so it made it stressful for me since I did a lot of writing last minute. I would definitely recommend trying to finish your expos essays early so you have plenty of time to revise and ask questions!
The subject matter and core texts are very interesting. I do feel, however, that the focus on trauma is very narrow and hard to apply to the papers that you will have the write, especially because Dr. Brown chooses such obscure and specific lenses. The instructions for the unit essays are also very ambiguous, and Dr. Brown's grading is not sympathetic in that respect. We had many short stories and films to watch, however classroom discussions on those works were very stagnant, and Dr. Brown was often the only voice in the classroom. I heard that his past EXPOS class, Wastelands, was much more enjoyable, but I would not strongly recommend this section.
