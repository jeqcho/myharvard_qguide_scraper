If you have a lot of experience in computer science and have learned about cyber security before, this course might be slightly too introductory. But for anyone who doesn't know much about cybersecurity and computer science and is at all interested in computers and the internet, take this seminar. The teachers are very interesting and we covered some very cool topics. And it's not hard—just do the readings (or skim if you don't have time) and be ready to have a good conversation in class.
This is a very interesting class but I would say that you will get a lot more out of it if you have some foundation in coding or understanding the foundations of computers and how the internet works. If you don't you will still learn things and it is a great class. You might just get a little lost in conversation sometimes. But it's a great time either way.
interesting topics and labs, honestly fun in person, profs were very clearly enthusiastic about the topics, and discussions were fun, HOWEVER reading took more time than I realized when signing up for the class… close to 100 pages each week, but you get out what you put in
AMAZING seminar; definitely recommend it!! The seminar is very beginner–friendly. Mike and Simson are amazing professors who will go in–depth about the topic. They will walk you through the concepts step by step to ensure you understand them. You'll learn that there is much more to cybersecurity than the "hacking" that we often see in the news. The mix of chapters from books and research papers gives you a good balance of subjective and objective insights into the field while the lab will give you some handy skills. They are flexible in shaping the discussion component around what you and the others want to discuss rather than sticking to a concrete plan. The seminar was very enjoyable and the little "back–and–forth" (in the most positive connotation as I cannot find a word to describe it right now) between the two professors is funny and makes it a lot of fun so definitely TAKE THIS SEMINAR if you can.
Though cliche, what you gain from this class is what you put in. The professors are extremely supportive and ready to answer/help answer any of the questions you might have about cybersecurity and more. This support is the ultimate resource of this seminar, and I would recommend taking advantage of the berth of expertise and passion available as much as possible by thoughtfully engaging with the material and discussion.
TAKE THIS COURSE!! The professors are great, with a (seemingly) unlimited knowledge base of experience and stories. The readings can be long or difficult, but the discussions in class clear everything up and are very conversation–like. The labs are hands–on and very informative, but fun at the same time. I had some reservations in the first few course dates, but by the end I can say this is much better than I could have expected.
Have an open mind and take this course even if you don't know much about CS
Super fun even if you have no background in cybersecurity. I would say you need a basic understanding of computer science to take this class (ie. knowing what a bit is, what a CPU is, etc.) but nothing too serious or technical. Mike and Simson are such engaging and expert teachers and I love the way they lead discussions. The lab section of class was fun too, and I enjoyed messing around in the terminal and trying different things in a low–stress environment. If you're interested in cybersecurity or tech history at all I would really recommend this seminar!
Take this class!!! Don't miss out on this opportunity!!! This course taught me sooo much about all the many ways that technology can be manipulated to distort, change or delete information. The readings are difficult but super interesting but otherwise this class is a GEM! The professors are super engaged and passionate about the materials!
