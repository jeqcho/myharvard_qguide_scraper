Professor Roxburgh is a wonderful and generous mentor. Take a course with him if you can during your time at Harvard.
Be ready to read (with attention to detail) a lot per week. However, it is worth it!
This is a very interesting class that demands an intensive amount of reading. I think it is a great class to take for someone deeply interested in Islamic arts. It is definitely grad student–heavy and can be reasonably overwhelming for an undergraduate student. However, Professor Roxburgh is very fun and engaging, along with deeply knowledgeable on the subject. It could not be taught by a better professor.
take this class!! professor roxburgh is wonderful, the material is interesting and the class covers a huge span of time and land while managing to study each site/city deeply. the class was mostly graduate students, which is a little intimidating as an undergraduate, but the vast majority of them were friendly and helpful. don't be afraid of prof. roxburgh either–he is intimidating but a GREAT professor and has some hilarious and bonkers stories. he will push back if you make a controversial or bold claim during discussion, but it's only to make your argument stronger and grow your ability to defend your claims. awesome class, awesome professor. suggestion if you're an undergraduate: take it with a friend! it makes the grad student cohort less scary
The course taught me about a subject I have rarely seen taught in architecture schools. The professor is enthusiastic and deeply cares for his students, so the classroom environment reflects this attitude and is a very welcoming space.
Participate in discussions, even the ones on subjects that don't quite capture your fancy. Also: get to class early to get a seat at the table.
Professor Roxburgh is just an amazing professor who is so knowledgable with what he teachers. Whether it be his soothing accent to purposeful methodology of framing class lectures, he is one of the best professors to take a class with. He is very willing to work with the students and doesn't shy away from their opinions... in fact he encourages it. While the readings may be difficult and overwhelming at times, the lectures bring the material together and is comprehendible.
