This is a great class! Donhee is very knowledgeable and willing to help. Assignments are fair and relatively easy to complete if you attend office hours. Overall, it was a pleasant experience.
Donhee is a great lecturer and this class is super well designed. Homework generally aligns well with what we are learning in lecture and is often a repeat of derivations or calculations covered in class—usually very approachable save a few questions here and there. Definitely recommend having a strong background in quantum mechanics. There’s a lot of mathematical tools and notations (Dirac notation, phasors, etc) that you are assumed to be familiar with.
The lectures are the strongest asset, and quite enjoyable
This is a really solid course and Donhee is a fantastic prof. It can feel easy to not take this class seriously, but you truly won't get anything out of it if you just slack off. The problem sets can be a little tough, especially the ones where you can't collaborate, but Donhee's office hours are very useful and his course notes are incredibly in depth. If you read the lecture notes carefully and attend lecture, you can get so much out of this class.
As my first physics course, this was an incredibly helpful introduction and the problem sets are involved enough to make sure that you understand the material. My only gripe with this class was that it was a bit slow. The first 5 weeks of the course were extremely redundant and could be condensed into 4 lectures.
