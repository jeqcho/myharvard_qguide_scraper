Do NOT take this class if you can avoid it. The three PSETS a week are a grind and often take away from the learning experience since youre more focused on completion rather than really learning the material and its applications. This class, because it is not lecture style, is super TF dependent. If you end up getting a TF whos not really great at explaining, BELIEVE ME, youre going to have a bad time (This is what happened to me)! So my piece of advice is that if you can take 22A instead it, you definitely should!!!! I understand the proofs are an added aspect to the course, but what you add to the rigor of the class, will be outweighed by the lack of structure, bad TF's, and the three PSETS a week which kills the learning process and makes this class an absolute nightmare. I am an engineering concentrator so I had the choice between 22A and 21B, but due to my plan of study timeline I had to take this course at the time I did, if I could have I would have avoided it.
This was genuinely a class that made me, a serious math lover, dislike Math a whole lot. The concepts are fine enough but the jump from learning them to applying them is really quick and not easily explained by TFs or CAs either. Don't take this class if you can avoid it.
Do not take this class unless you absolutely have to. There is far too much content and they teach by the guess–and–check method, which means that they do not actually teach and you are stuck teaching yourself everything. Go to office hours for the psets and advocate for yourself and ask for help whenever needed.
I think what makes Math 21b intimidating is the concentrated workload, from 3 psets a week to exams. My biggest tip is to always ask questions when you’re lost! Schedule a meeting with your TF, attend OH/MQC, and make review sheets (this was extremely helpful for last minute review before an exam). The earlier you start connecting concepts, the less studying (and hopefully less stressed) you’ll feel before the exam!
I think this was a great way to fulfill the math requirements because the content isn't really that challenging as long as you really pay attention during section and do the readings beforehand because some section explanation is a bit unclear without having seen how the problem is done. Psets could be very short or long so be prepared and get started early. The practice exams are all very representative of the midterms/final so just make sure to do those and understand the solutions. True/false is really just luck of the moment based on how good your intuition is.
Take this course !! You learn a great amount of math in an accessible manner. Takin in the Spring though, there are more office hours and such. Also, go to office hours !! Start your pset early because they are very long. I recommend also studying early and taking all the practice exams before exams. Great teaching staff and great support.
An amazing class that you should take if you want to get an intro to linear algebra and differential equations. My TF was great, meaning sections were super productive and the CAs made the experience very fun and enjoyable. There are three psets a week, which seems intimidating, but if you sit down and focus on them it is definitely doable even with a packed/busy schedule. Tests were reasonable throughout the semester, and I met some really close friends through the class. Would really recommend!!
