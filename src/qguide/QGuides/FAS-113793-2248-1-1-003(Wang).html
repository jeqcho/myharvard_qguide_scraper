What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is so encouraging and welcoming, regardless of your incoming skill. I am a senior that didn't take Chinese Ba/Bb, so the last time I took Chinese was my senior year of High School. I also already fulfilled my language requirement, so I was just taking this course to deepen my skills in Chinese, and it WASN'T BAD! Although it's a good amount of work, you will genuinely improve your Chinese so much in such a fun way. Probably the most organized department in the school by far. Everything connects from the textbook to the lectures to the homework. It's awesome how they introduce new words each week and then proceed to integrate them into the everyday lesson in conversation. It helps solidify the words as you get to here them in natural conversation and refresh your memory on their meanings.
Amazing class! The teaching team cares so much – they put significant effort into special activities, like a scavenger hunt, tea ceremony, sticker making competition, and weekly language dinners for first–years. The class culture is very supportive. I laughed so much every class and genuinely had a lot of fun. They do give a lot of work, but they grade pretty leniently.
Definitely alot of work, but if you're serious about learning Chinese this class improve your language skills incredibly. Teaching staff are all very enthusiastic and open to help. This isnt really the type of class to cram for, needs consistent studying and practice.
I have never met a more caring group of instructors. This course is difficult, because Chinese is a tough language, however the support from the 120a teaching staff is the best I've ever experienced
Great class! Super fun to continue Chinese and really encourage. Does get harder from BA/BB, more words and grammar each lesson, but dictations are easier.
Chinese 120a requires work and commitment for success, but it is so worth it! All of the teachers are incredibly passionate about teaching and it shows during each lecture/drill section. They also are always willing to support you, should you face any challenges along the way: I never took a formal Chinese course, other than a study abroad program, prior to 120a and felt that I could approach the instructors with any questions. Take Chinese 120 and don't look back!
Amazing class! Kindest, most effective teachers, best, most helpful textbook. Stop deciding whether to take Chinese or planning to take it sometime – just start fitting it into your schedule, you won't regret it!
This class is not easy, but it is very rewarding. I entered this course with a lower level of spoken fluency in Chinese (due to having less practice with this aspect of Chinese in high school), but the teaching staff was very helpful, and my fluency soon improved to an appropriate level. Homework assignments and tests are relatively easy with an understandable amount of studying. Make sure that you review the course content throughout the semester to prepare for the final exam, as it is definitely not an easy test. Overall, if you are recommended for this course, even if you feel that you have a slightly lower level of fluency, I would recommend that you enroll. The teaching staff is amazing and the class, while not explicitly easy, is not incredibly difficult.
This course demands a lot of commitment but it is so incredibly fun. If you're consistent about it and try, you'll be fine.
