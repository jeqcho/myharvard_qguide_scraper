If you are interested in pursuing any life science–related field, TAKE THIS CLASS! Whether you are pursuing medicine, ecology, forestry, or anything in between, this class has something for you. This class is perfectly tailored to serve as a terminal math class for students in the life sciences, as it teaches you select topics from multivariable calculus, linear algebra, and differential equations that can be best tailored to applications in the life sciences. Students are also presented with many examples of real–life experiments and modeling that use these types of math through discussions of academic articles. Students are also familiarized with the software Wolfram Mathematica and taught to mathematically model differential equations throughout the class and during the final project, which lets you look into any biological topic that is related to mathematical modeling and that interests you. Additionally, as long as the placement tests placed you into this class, and especially if you have taken calc BC, you are definitely prepared to take it. If not, I would advise against taking 19A.

Additionally, a quick disclaimer. Other reports may paint this class as extremely difficult, and that is because it takes a lot of work. The class does certainly require a lot of work, but everything difficult on the problem sets and the final project can be made much easier by simply going to office hours and asking Wes, the professor, your questions. He is super open to helping and very available, which is even better due to the small size of the class. Wes himself is one of the most effective lecturers that I have ever had and really does want his students to grow, which is especially clear during the final project period, as long as you take advantage of your resources and schedule conferences with him.

The small size and difficulty of this class is also great for community–building. Myself and many other students have built friend groups with fellow Math 19a students. My personal suggestion is to get lunch with the class after lecture if you can! It makes for a great debrief and brings you closer with the class, which is great because you will definitely need to check problem sets with your classmates if you want to get all of the points. Another great thing is that there is one, long pset each week, giving you ample time to fix your mistakes, thus letting you better learn the content and get more points than the Psets in Math 21a, which have 3 short problem sets per week.

However, there are two reasons against taking this class. firstly, if you do not want to put the work into this class, then it is not the class for you. You get what you put into it, which can be super beneficial, but only if you actually put the time in to learn the material. Secondly, if you are looking for a full multivariable curriculum, complete with triple integrals and all, then take 21A. But, if you are looking for one final math class that best teaches you about the life sciences, then take this class. You will learn just what you need for the field, eliminating the need to take future classes such as linear algebra and differential equations.

TLDR; TAKE THIS CLASS!!! It will be a lot of work, but if you are willing to put in the bit of extra work, you will get so much more out of it, in terms of friends, community, and applicable knowledge, than you will get from 21A.
