What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is an excellent course for those interested in surrendering to working practice that is likely less familiar than they expect. My experience was immensely rewarding, more than just academically. I received an introduction to photography as a medium of expression and representation without necessary utility (I'm struggling to word that exactly right). It felt in many ways like a language course, and I had to learn a new way of talking about images, one that is both more objective and more viewer–centric. It also serves as an excellent forum for discussing the place of art in general. The professor and TF are wonderful resources, and the class has a sort of alchemy – our timeslot became very close, with all of us celebrating our improvements in the class together.
This class is not a throwaway course or a schedule filler; it is a real time commitment for those who want to participate fully (which I recommend doing). It is also a serious course, despite its sometimes unorthodox structure. Take it!
We were consistently exposed to all the diverse areas of photography including shooting in film and playing around with digital camera's specifics to take more emotion–evoking pictures. The best thing about this course is the Patrice–Stephanie Duo. They are just great teachers and incredibly fun people to be around and learn from.
This is an incredible class and as hard as it might be for you, you will not regret taking it what so ever. This will likely forever stay my favorite class at Harvard, despite the fact that I'm not even an afvs concentrator (yet haha, this class might have changed me). In terms of timing, I would strongly advice you to take this class if your schedule isn't packed. I took it during my sophomore fall with 3 super hard econ classes and I really wish I had more time to contribute to this class.
I had a great experience with the Introduction to Still Photography course. My classmates were very welcoming and encouraging, the prospect of seeing them every week gave a warm feeling. You get to borrow a decent beginner DSLR camera with hard drive, SD card, photoshop equipped computers, and free premium printing for the entire semester, which is also a great privilege. Patrice and Stephanie were good instructors with kind hearts. Hope everyone stay close even after this course.
Take this class! You'll leave with a life–skill that is very useful and also a group of new friends. Patrice is absolutely amazing and quite possibly the best professor I have ever had at this school because they curate an environment of learning that also feels so safe and comfortable. You will grow as an artist (you definitely have to work for it though) and it will be so special to see your personal photography style develop.
The best course I have ever taken. Its a shame that Patrice wont be here next year.
