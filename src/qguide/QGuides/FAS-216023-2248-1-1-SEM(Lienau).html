Comments
I think this class is an excellent avenue for post–colonial studies. If you have ever been interested in decolonization, take this class. (I also think everyone in general should take this class.) While you could pick up the books on the syllabus and read it diligently, Professor Lineau's discussions and office hours will reveal ideas and connections in between the texts' lines. Take it
This course touches on critical subjects of counter–imperialism, especially within the context of African and Asian literatures which are often less commonly taught. This is a particularly unique course not only in subject, but in the types of conversations that are prompted by Professor Lienau. Professor Lienau does an excellent job of facilitating conversation, including in her introduction of the texts and building upon students' comments. She has a wealth of expertise in this area, and I would highly recommend taking advantage of the opportunity to learn from her. I recommend taking this class if you have any interest in the subject and/or regions.
This is hands–down one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard. Professor Lienau is extremely knowledgable about the course content and is an amazing facilitator of class discussions. The readings were all interesting and built beautifully upon and one–another throughout the semester. I especially appreciated the variety of assigned literature; we pulled from philosophical and political theory, poetry, and novels, alongside the occasional film or piece of music. Assignment load was relatively light, with 3 papers throughout the semester and 5 discussion posts alongside the weekly readings. It was sometimes difficult to get in touch with Prof Lienau outside of class as there were no regular office hour times, but she always scheduled ~15 min meetings with each student before each paper was due. Overall, though, would highly recommend any class with Prof Lienau!
