Comments
The details of this course will probably vary a lot depending on the professor teaching it. However, I imagine broad similarities will remain––namely, this course is going to be more difficult than 253a by a good margin, and you will understand less than you did in 253a by a good margin. However, I found that by the end of 253b, I definitely understood some of the material in 253a a lot better, so you get that I guess. We had biweekly problem sets this year, but don't let that fool you into thinking that this course is not much work––the problem sets were sometimes devilishly difficult, and took us countless hours to solve. The final presentations were fun and good way to finish this kind of course. If you are going into theory/want to develop a good understanding of QFT, you should definitely take this course, and there aren't many alternatives, but know what you're getting into when you enroll.
This course is very interesting and I think it's definitely worth taking, but be prepared to spend a lot of time reading outside of class and going to office hours. QFT is one of those courses where you need to engage with the material multiple times until you understand it.
