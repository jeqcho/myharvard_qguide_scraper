I thought this class was fine! Preface, I originally took this class because the requirements seemed more lax when I selected it in the spring for the upcoming fall, but over the summer the requirements and course components got more intense. All that to say, am not extremely passionate about folklore and or artfullness which might've contributed to my indifference about this course.

I do enjoy exploring sociology and culture, but some aspects of this class just seemed too loose and blurred for my understanding and appreciation of the topics. There weren't many concrete understandings, conclusions, or knowledge points; so much so that when it can time for the midterm, many were confused as to what we had learned up to that point. Section did help solidify this more. I think most of my learning and takeaways came from section as opposed to actual lecture.

At times there seems to be some disconnect between the TFs and Dr. Craycraft on course materials and expectations which causes confusion for students. And at times, despite her incredible zeal for folklore, Dr. C can get a bit flustered and lose her train of thought in lecture, which also can confuse students on what we are supposed to be learning.

However, the writing assignments and the end of the semester project (our final) was very fulfilling for me! These were most of our grade anyway, so it was great that the fun and gratifying assignments also carried us the farthest. In the end, this wasn't a bad class at all; perhaps I'd think differently if I truly needed 100% understanding of the concepts for my concentration, but I don't.
