This is a good introductory mechanics course. The demonstrations are fun and the support in section and office hours is great.
– Please take this class! The teaching team are all angels, and the material is so interesting. The pace is very reasonable, and it’s clear they want you to do well. If you are debating between 15 and 12, 15 is much more focused on intuition and challenging problem–solving.
Physics 15a is a good class overall, and it's evident that the professors and TFs genuinely care about the students. As someone who took AP Physics C, it was a bit boring, and I wish I had taken Physics 19 instead. Though on the flip side, I think that this course would be quite challenging if I hadn't taken physics in high school. Despite the strange dynamic of being too easy for those with a background in mechanics and too hard for those without, I did enjoy the demos and the special relativity unit that was new to me.
I will start with the bad and end with the good.
The bad...
The lectures were quite confusing, especially when Stefan took over lectures, and overall the formulas and main concepts were so scattered in the lecture notes that it was difficult to know how to approach PSET questions. If I could do it again, I would go back after each lecture to understand all the practice problems and make a running formula sheet to organize all the concepts. I also wish that we had spent more time doing easier problems in lecture to actually build a foundation for the concepts before we used them in PSETs. The lecture format of introducing a brand new physics concept and then asking us to solve a problem with it to experience the "struggle" was really discouraging and not helpful for me at all because I didn't even understand the basics of what I was supposed to be struggling with to even begin to approach a more advanced problem right away. At the end of this class, I still don't feel like I can look at a new physics problem and approach it in a meaningful (even if incorrect) way without an extensive cheat sheet. My peers who understood content quickly and were able to actively engage in lecture later told me that they had done AP Physics in high school or in private school and that almost the entire course was a repeat of content for them. I went to a large public school that did not offer advanced physics classes. Despite what the course catalogue says, this class is not accessible to those who have not seen physics before. The lab portion of the class was a grind and the computations we learned were never very clear. The last 2 psets of this class were so long and challenging that the CAs could not do some questions and they felt long and difficult for the sake of it and not because they were useful to our learning.
The good...
Physics night is so fun and makes doing the psets enjoyable. It's hard to fully crash out about angular momentum when you have apple cider in one hand and a cookie in the other. Professor Klales is SO kind and it is very easy to get a meeting with her if her regular office hours do not fit in your schedule. The teaching staff are also all incredible in office hours. Helena and Vincent carried me through the semester and were able to explain things clearly in the moment or took the time to think about the right way to help with a concept. I had Gia as my section TF and she is so amazing and so intentional. Her goal is to be a teacher so she always puts incredible effort into her explanations and being in her section was probably the highlight of 15a for me. Sometimes she would even bake sweet treats for us! She made physics very approachable and was able to balance her sections between revising lecture content and taking suggestions for what to review.
Make sure you work on the pset yourself before going to office hours/physics night. Although those times are incredibly helpful to collaborate with people and finish the psets, you want to make sure that you understand all of the content yourself.
Make good use of office hours, prepare early for exams, and come prepared for lectures to make the most of this course.
Dr. Klales is an amazing instructor and the section TF Sanjay and Gia are also very amazing. they care about how comfortable you feel with the classroom materials. They also want you to pass with a good grade so they will give you make ups if you miss something. However, sometimes the lectures become too hasty.
Good pace and level of material. Hard problems push you to know the content at a deep level. Lectures have very fun demos. Good fundamental introduction to the field of physics.
Start the psets early! Attend office hours.
This class is not super interesting. I didn't really get an intuition for why these physics concepts occur (which might be my fault, but still). If you are coming to this class out of a curiosity for physics, you should probably take physics 19.
Try not to start the psets the day they're due. It's not good for your mental health.
This class is doable if you're locked in. Go to office hours, get help from instructors, do all the practice for the exams, and you'll be fine!
I thought this was a really good class! I took it off cycle, and I’m glad I did because I liked having a smaller class. The office hours and section were really helpful, and I feel like that’s were I made the most progress towards understanding the material better.
TAKE IT! TAKE IT! TAKE IT!!!!!! This was the best STEM course I took at Harvard so far. I highly suggest taking 15a instead of 12a if you are truly interested in learning how Physics works. The material is awesome, the lectures are great and the labs are super manageable. I loved it!
