Comments
Take Chinese Ba! I came into the class with a few years of experience in Chinese under my belt (which you will find a good number of students have) and this class bridged the gap between my knowledge from middle school and high school to a higher level of proficiency. That being said, so many people who take Chinese Ba have no prior experience with the language and one of the strengths of the class is that it builds up your language skills from the ground up.

The teaching staff is incredible and made me excited to go to lecture, the projects and assignments are creatively designed and fun, and the class as a whole is one of my favorites I've taken at Harvard. Take this course!!!
It is very fun! Don’t let the 5–days a week schedule scare you, it is actually super helpful for content retention.
Fun class that will ACTUALLY teach and drill Chinese into you. Assignments are hard to get a perfect score, but also hard to get a low score. Tests are moderate, some are more difficult than others but if you are on top of it it's hard to fail a test. Two dictation quizzes a week can be annoying but in the end it is so so so rewarding because class is easy and fun after being on top of learning new words.
I loved this class, the professors are awesome. They are nice and helpful. If you are considering this course and legitimately have no experience, it is very difficult but worthwhile to try. Professors are more than willing to help you out.
Do not take this class unless you are willing to make a serious commitment to learning Chinese. Having some background in the language is obviously very helpful, but with or without that, this is an outstanding course if and only if you are willing to put in the time and energy. You'll be amazed at how much of the language you'll internalize after only one semester, which the daily meetings really help with. But if it's already going to be a brutal semester, don't bother — this is an all–or–nothing class.
