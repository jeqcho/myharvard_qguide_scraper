The class culture is a little bizarre in that since most are engineering/overzealous pre–med students there is an ambient feeling that unless you won a Nobel prize at like 11, you are behind your peers – please don't be unassuaged, they are either also struggling in the class or having to take it too. Especially with regards to lab components or in class discussion/activities. There's a few students that are going to try to be dick–riders, but literally ignore them.

Last, and probably the most important: Linsey and her teaching. This class was honestly a bag of mixed feelings. The purpose of the class is to be a brief intro to a lot of different systems of the body and give you a more general understanding of bioengineering in that sense. I think the content itself was not difficult, just the fact that we did not have access to answer sheets for the problems done in class, recordings, or annotated lecture slides really made things very annoying to self study. I personally could not concentrate during class because of Dr. Moyer's more monotone lecture style and my ADHD and would have loved to also have the annotated lecture slides and class recordings always available. So classes were hard to go to, despite the topic being interesting. I do think we skipped around a lot between systems and maybe cutting the systems in half and diving deeper into them would make more sense to feel that you actually understand them a bit better now.

The MATLAB portion was fine, you learned a lot of it using ChatGPT and the CAs, so that isn't much of a struggle. PSETs are long so you gotta start early but they're the only homework for this class. Labs are fun but I personally struggled with having to do them with other people because some just seemed less committed to perfection than others and this led to you dividing up the work and you going back to fix clear mistakes or just ignoring it and getting an overall bad grade. There's no point in asking for a regrade either because it doesn't work. In that sense, it's difficult to have your grade relying on other people and a lot of people had issues with the random groups where you lack that accountability and synergy between members. Exams were fair up until the last one where everyone stayed an extra 10 minutes, so study a lot for that one because it's extremely difficult and not like the other exams. The final project a bit stressful since its at the end of the year but it is super fun and you really do begin relating what you learned in class with their real world applications. Overall, you have to take this class anyways if you want to do BE or BME, so I'd be thinking about going to every class and office hours to self teach a lot of the stuff that is delivered in a monotone way, do every problem by yourself to make sure you know everything, take some time learning MATLAB because its a good skill to have, and begin studying for exams at least 3 days in advance and you should be fine (except the last one where I'd give it a week in advance). You learn a lot but not too in depth about anything. ALSO you do get the chance to meet a lot of the staff that is doing research in BME so that's super fun and the class is a very nice opportunity to meet Dr. Moyer and begin planning your future.
