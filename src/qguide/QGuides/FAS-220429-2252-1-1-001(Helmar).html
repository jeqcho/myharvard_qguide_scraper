This class is an excellent intro to digital photography. The best thing is that you get a camera for the whole semester and get to just go out in life and take amazing photos. It's truly such a wonderful freedom. However, this doesn't mean there aren't any parameters for the photos you take––you have to be intentional with what you make, and be prepared to get your ass kicked a little during critiques by peers and your prof. It can feel a bit harsh at times, but take it all in respectfully and attentively, as the feedback you receive is all super useful and does truly make you improve. Also be prepared to be snapping lots of photos a week (144 every two weeks), if that's your thing.
As far as I understand, Patrice Helmar is leaving ––– which is sad, because they were awesome. I am not sure how the course will change with a new instructor. Still, TAKE THIS CLASS if you have the opportunity. Photography is such a fun and useful activity; I have already helped multiple friends with headshots using the skills that I learned during this course. I went from having no clue what aperture was to being paid for my photography work. The course is that practical. At the same time, we touched on the history of photography and talked a bit about the spirit and morality of taking pictures of people. All around fun and productive.
This course is absolutely amazing. The culture is great, and the freedom of expression helps massively with artistic development. Also, budget time for this course. Good photos take time, and you have to put yourself in interesting situations to get the right moments. This course taught me more about myself than any other course I have taken –– I would highly recommend this course.
This class is awesome. It's not easy and is a pretty major time commitment, but the work is really fun and different from most other classes. Because of the small nature of the class, it also fosters an incredible community that made me look forward to our three hour 9 AM classes (which would be dreadful under most circumstances). I feel like I learned to think critically and creatively about my work, and have come away from the class with increased technical knowledge and an excitement to continue photography throughout my life.
