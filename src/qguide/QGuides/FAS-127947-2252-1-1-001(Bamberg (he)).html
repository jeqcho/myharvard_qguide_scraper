What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This was for me a good review of Stat 110 with a bit of measure theory thrown in, interesting application to option pricing, and very fair / generous grading. Unfortunately it won't be offered in the future as Paul will be retiring.
It's an interesting class that has some interesting content, but a lot of it is very similar to some other stat courses (110 and 210 mostly superset the material in the class, aside from the stuff about Black–Scholes). There are some special concepts like hedging that you won't see in the other stat classes, but they're pretty simple and you could probably understand some of them in about ten minutes—probably not worth taking an entire course for. It's also unclear if this class will be run in the future by a different professor, since I think Bamberg is retiring soon (?) so maybe it'll be less scuffed in the future.
