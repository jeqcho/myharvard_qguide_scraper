I loved the class itself! The preceptor was great and I loved the topic of the class (medical ethics). The preceptor is so fun and funny; she really made the class engaging. But, she can be a tough grader on the final essays, so be prepared for that. She won't give out an A easily– you will really have to work for it. All the other assignments are graded for completion though, so as long as you do them and put effort into them, you'll be fine. The readings are also not bad at all (I personally found them super interesting).

On a side note, if you took AP Seminar, that will help you a lot in this class.

But the reason why you should take this course is the professor – Dr. Katie Baca. This is your standard expos course but I think what made this course so strong was our professor, Dr. B. The strength of this course was definitely the professor.
TAKE THIS COURSE. Dr. B is UNDOUBTEDLY the BEST Expos professor you can have!! Okay I may be a little biased, but she is so so funny, open to new ideas, kind, and just overall a great professor. Her writing feedback is incredible and I genuinely don't regret taking Expos with Dr. B for a SECOND.

Now, aside from the Dr. B glazing, one thing you should know about Expos is that you should try to start the assignments EARLY. This class would keep me up until 2–4am (or even later) at times because I was a big procrastinator. Don't be like me. Start assignments early. Start your draft essays early. Start revising early. The more time you revise, the better your essays will be.

And lastly, enjoy this class and treat Dr. B nicely :)
