If you are looking for a really good Spanish history class through a literature lens, take this.
Take this class! Daniel is a great professor and you learn a lot about Spain and Spanish literature. I was nervous about not knowing enough Spanish but he meets you where you are and this is a great class to better your skills. The assignments may take you a while just due to the different language, but they are not super hard and you don't need to know every detail of the readings, just get the main idea and come to class with questions.
This is such a great class! The professor is super dedicated, and the topics are super interesting, especially if you’ve had past interest in politics and Spanish literature. The workload is not too bad, and the in–class essays are very fair.
Take this course if you're interested in Spain and Spanish history! The selected readings are honestly very interesting (although sometimes difficult). Daniel was a great lecturer – enthusiastic, funny, and explained things clearly. The in–person written exam format could be a bit stressful, especially if you don't like writing by hand, but it turned out fine.

Make sure to do your written reflection before class so that you can contribute to discussions. Overall, great class!
