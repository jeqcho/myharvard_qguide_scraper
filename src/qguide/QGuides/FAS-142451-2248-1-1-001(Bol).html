Very informative course, however it is a LOT of work. it's a flipped classroom format so you need to do several hours of module watching each week. It is very interesting and so worth it I learned so much!! Just be prepared for the time you will need to do the work––it is not a gem, do not take a heavy course load with this. Hands down the best part of this class was section. Aaron was a FANTASTIC tf. Sections were so so fun, he would put spongebob memes and make them so relaxed and funny––it makes it so easy and fun to participate in and we had so many great discussions. If he is tf–ing again then absolutely take this course and try to get his section. He really made this course so enjoyable and I learned so so much and looked forward to his section every Friday.
This is a heavy participation–based course (45% of final grade). They also don't let you take this gened pass–fail, so be cognizant of that. Since the class is flipped–classroom style, there's a decent amount of weekly work you have to put in, and you have to do the readings for section too since participation is required. Kirby and Bol are legends / experts on China so it's fascinating to hear their stories, but China's history is 5000 years and it's impossible to get a comprehensive understanding of it in 1 semester, much less in a gened. Overall, would probably recommend taking a different gened to fulfill HSI unless you're genuinely interested and are willing to put in the work.
This class is amazing. You will have a strong grasp of general Chinese history (very cool) and the case studies give a really good sense of business in China today. Prof. Bol and Kirby are incredible lecturers, but do note this gen ed certainly required time and work.
DO NOT TAKE THIS CLASS. They do not allow you to view your grades on canvas throughout the semester. They do not allow people to pass fail. They grade essays extremely harshly (and I am a social studies concentrator). They do not give clear rubrics or instructions. They do not allow you to rewrite papers. They do not offer extra credit opportunities. They force everyone to work in a group of four to write a final paper. They grade people in lecture by the number of times they speak in class, but didn't tell everyone. This is a gened, but it seems no one in this class is aware of that.

The class's content is amazing. The professors are amazing people that lecture extremely well. I could listen and learn from them all day. The content is extremely engaging and I started exploring it in my own free time. However, while I am in college to explore and learn, at the end of the day, I get a transcript. And my transcript was ruined by this class. I did not see it coming at all. If you care at all about your grades, or even just feeling like you got a grade that you deserve, don't take this class and find something else.
I believe this class is challenging for students with little to no background of World History. The modules can go over your head– especially when assignments from other classes all pile up, but I will say the teaching staff and material are both very engaging. I definitely learned a whole bunch and am continuing to keep up with current information surrounding China due to its relevancy with current world culture– something I probably wouldn't of have done if I have never taken this class.
This is a very interesting class that will give you a good foundational knowledge of Chinese history. However, do expect a high workload with significant readings each week.
Not a small amount of work with the weekly modules and readings, but really interesting and worth the time.
Not a gem. Requires you to do reading, with a real grading system.
The class is really 2 classes. The first is an intro to Chinese history with a top scholar (Peter Bol). The other is a bunch of histories of minor businesses (William Kirby). The former is very interesting; the latter, in spite of a personable teacher, is very boring.
This is the best class I have taken at Harvard, and I say that as someone with a STEM concentration. Professor Bol and Kirby are amazing professors and by the end they will both know your names and you will have a personal relationship with them. The assignments are high quality and the lectures are amazing. Be ready to participate a ton each class and if you can do that you will do great.
if Xiaorui is a TF, take it, and make sure you're in her section. Her section was probably the best section I've ever been a part of. She really cares about the subject, is lenient and understands when you need to skip section for other personal/academic matters, and is really good at choosing engaging topics that students can actually talk about.

I wasn't a fan of the teaching of the lectures by the professors. The style is based on cold–calling, which I'm not a fan of, but even putting that aside, the professors would interrupt students trying to answer questions. I don't think cold–calling and interrupting is a set–up that makes a lot of sense.

This class has a good deal of reading (as well as recorded lectures) and requires active participation (cold calling) but is well worth it.
