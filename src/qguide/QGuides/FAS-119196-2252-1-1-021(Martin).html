Stay curious and keep up with the problem setseverything clicks with practice.
I think the 21a structure is pretty good, and they have a lot of resources like office hours and MQC to help support you. I think the content was interesting, but most students take the class because it's a requirement, so the energy level can be a bit low. Probably shop both if you're deciding between 21a and 22a.

Try to stay a little ahead of the material. Watch the videos early, and start psets early because you learn much better when you're not scrambling to finish psets last minute. Office hours and MQC are helpful. Don't stress too much about getting everything right on every pset, but just make sure you understand the material. The exams are worth much more than the psets, and there are a lot of psets. The exams are fair, and they provide a lot of practice exams which was nice.

I was in Eva's section, and she was really nice!
It's worth it. Although it's going to challenge you with 3 psets a week, it's a good class. It has a great support system for students. Just make sure to find the right TF who you can easily understand.
good class overall. 3 psets/wk is annoying but class is taught well and you are given lots of resources
very well taught and practical!
if you have taken math 1b, math 21a will feel like a natural continuation. to me, it felt even easier, given that, by then, I was used to the way math is taught at Harvard. if you like rigor and proofs, don't take the class, you will find neither. but the class does well what it promises: a good overview of multi for stem (non pure math) concentrators. as you will know, the experience in the class will depend largely on your TF. Eva was great and made every single section very enjoyable and engaging. exams were super fair, definitely easier than practice tests.
