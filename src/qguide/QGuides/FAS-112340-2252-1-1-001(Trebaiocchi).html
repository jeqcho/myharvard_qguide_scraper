What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
While this course is a bit fast–paced, you have the ability to see rapid progress every week. You go from 0 to knowing the vast core of Italian grammar in one semester. With the skills you learn, you are able to carry on conversations, read books, and watch movies all in Italian. The assignments are lots of fun (cooking, watching music videos, planning a vacation to Italy, researching a topic of choice). Plus, the course staff / Italian faculty are very supportive and will help you every step of the way.
It's a lot of work (like a lot) but I'd say it's worth it if you want to get a good and fast start to a new language.
This is a great class to take, and I definitely recommend it, especially because Professor Linsky is always willing to help and provides strong support throughout the course. That said, it’s important to understand that ITAL 15 is truly intensive. On top of the regular assignments, be prepared to spend extra hours practicing, attending office hours, or seeking tutoring, particularly if you don’t have a background in Romance languages. Staying motivated every single day is crucial, because it’s very easy to fall behind, and once you do, it can be hard to catch up. Overall, the course is rewarding and well–structured, but it's a significant commitment. Make sure you have the time and energy to stay on top of it from start to finish.
