Really amazing class, highly recommend taking! Worth it to try and understand each reading assigned
I thoroughly enjoyed this class! I think that throughout, I was a little lost sometimes because I just wasn't that familiar with the vocab and concepts being referred to (I also took this class under the impression that it'd be like behavioral economics, but it certainly wasn't; it definitely is behavioral FINANCE). But, the lively discussions during lectures (where I want to say the split between Stein speaking and students engaging/speaking was 60/40 which is a great ratio) were so interesting to hear. I also liked the readings, especially the ones that had a greater overlap with psychology, and inspired on the readings that most intrigued us, we created a final research project of our own – and that was really cool to take ownership over a personal research study like that.
This is a great junior tutorial pick for those at all interested in the topic. Prof. Stein is a great lecturer who is super knowledgeable which comes through in this class. Highly recommend.
I highly recommend this course. If you have any interest in finance, psychology (how it interacts with finance), or finding interesting exogenous variables and natural experiments (somewhat in the style of Freakonomics), then I strongly recommend this course. I came into it with no finance experience or background and Professor Stein does a great job of explaining basic concepts while discussing important papers in the field. I had little to no trouble keeping up. Further, the final project is a great opportunity to test out if you'd like to write a thesis or if you enjoy economics/finance research; it is super free form and Professor Stein has no expectations for how it turns out, only that you work hard on it and demonstrate effort. The weekly discussion papers initially intimidated me since I have no experience reading academic papers, but after just 2–3 weeks I enjoyed reading the papers and writing my thoughts about them and could get it done in an hour or so. I think this course is perfect for anyone interested in finance, considering writing a thesis senior year, or interested in the intersection of psychology and economics. Truly cannot recommend it any more; certainly my favorite economics course I have taken so far at Harvard. P.S. Alex, the TF, is super accessible outside of class, is great to work with, and gives amazing feedback and resources for the final project.
Amazing class! Prof Stein is incredible and going to class each week was always really fun. Definitely take this class if you have any interest in financial markets or writing a thesis.
Take this class!! I cannot recommend it enough, it was probably one of my favorite experiences at Harvard so far! It is easy to tell that Professor Stein is very kind and smart and wants you to have the best experience possible. The weekly workload with readings and a short discussion note is very manageable and helpful to prepare for in class discussion, which was probably my favorite part. The discussion is lead in a very genuine way and Professor Stein makes it a point for students to be able to ask questions and let the discussion lead where it leads. All the different steps for the final paper make it feel very approachable but don't make the same mistake as me and wait to ask for help. Its super helpful to get started on your data analysis early so you don't get stuck! And again, take this class!!
TAKE THIS CLASS — Jeremy Stein is one of a kind and one of the sharpest professors I have ever taken a class with at Harvard. This class was great at providing a digestible overview of behavioral finance phenomena and market behavior.
This was one of the most intellectually rewarding classes I’ve taken at Harvard. Ec 980 challenged me in the best way possible—especially through the readings, which were dense but super insightful. I grew a lot over the semester, both in how I think about finance and in how I approach research. Professor Stein was deeply engaging; he didn’t just teach the material, he also brought it to life with thoughtful questions and an amazing ability to clarify complex ideas. Oftentimes, his own scrutiny of some of the readings and material is what made me think most critically about the topics. Loved it. My final project pushed me to apply what I had learned, specifically because I attempted to update and apply one of the readings' trading strategies to modern markets, and it ended up being the most fulfilling academic experience I’ve had in college. I cannot express more genuinely how much I loved this course. If you have even the slightest interest in finance, you should ABSOLUTELY take this course.
