One of the best tutorials that I've been in so far! Definitely choose a topic that you're very interested in, because the content could go very deep into the theory and past literature of that particular topic. The course mostly consisted of paper reading and discussions, with a STATA minicourse and psets on the side that was very useful for the final research project. Other assignments were quite qualitative and were structured well in helping break down how to write a full economics paper. Overall, amazing course! (but likely dependent on who is teaching your particular section)
Take this section!! As someone who went into the class with very little background in the environment, this course has been an eye–opening experience. The course is organized by having one week on some aspect of the political economy with the following week focusing on how that aspect of the political economy intersects with or helps explain some aspect of the environmental crisis. Magdalena made a really cool syllabus, covering topics like media bias and colonial legacies and weaving those topics seamlessly with discussions on the environment. I would strongly recommend this section!
This tutorial was excellent! The readings were thoughtfully chosen and very interesting, and the classroom atmosphere was warm and relaxed. Magdalena is incredibly supportive and offers valuable feedback on assignments. Grading is good, and assignments are reasonable and interesting. Would highly recommend!
Make sure you stay on top of the readings and start your research project as early as possible. It may be easier to research a question that has data already easily available than to try to find data to match a very specific question. Also make sure you use the library resources – they are amazing or this class.
