I seriously recommend this course with reservations. To start off with the good, I think that the material in 21b is extremely interesting, enjoyable, and applicable to real life. I think this course was even better than 21a, which I took in the fall. I also think that for the most part, this course was much easier than 21a, but this is a controversial opinion. 21a felt much more methodological while this is just brute force problem solving, which I personally enjoy. Additionally, this class is taught in small sections, so this more intimate setting is helpful for asking questions and getting to know your peers. I especially enjoyed working on the boards.

With that said, there were also a bunch of things I didn't like about this class. First, I feel that three PSets a week is so much, especially given how long most of them were. There were several PSets in which we had 4+ problems with parts A through E or F. This was extremely time consuming, and it almost felt counterproductive to my learning because sometimes I just needed to get the PSet done, and I couldn't take as much time as I wanted to truly understand the material since we were constantly moving. I would estimate that each PSet took between 2–6 hours, with the majority probably taking 3–4. I'd definitely consider how this much time out of class fits in with your other classes before adding this to your Crimson Cart.

Additionally, I was in the 3 PM section, and there were hardly ever office hours after 4:15 when class ended, which meant that I had to wait until MQC at 7:30 to get help since I had 4 classes Mondays, Wednesday, and Friday starting from 9:45 and stretching all the way until the end of math until 4:15. This would be even worse on Fridays because I would have to wait all the way until Sunday MQC to get any help, so this was incredibly frustrating since I also had PSets for two other classes due around the same time each week, and I needed to get support for those as well.

I think the exams were relatively fair, but there were 20 true–false on each exam and no partial credit was given. That felt a bit excessive.

As a student looking to study CS, I honestly wish I took 22a or a higher numbered course instead because I would've gained more mathematical maturity from taking one of those courses, which is integral to CS. But I still enjoyed this class, and I still think you would get a lot out of it. I would recommend only taking the 21 series if your intended concentration recommends it over any other level (I came in as engineering, where 21 is sufficient) .

However, I think that with the right people, the problem sets and the studying are very doable. I basically lived at MQC this semester, but I think I learned a lot from my peers while I was there and the CAs were also helpful.
