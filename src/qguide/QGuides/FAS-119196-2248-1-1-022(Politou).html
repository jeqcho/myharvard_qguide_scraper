Take it but expect to work hard
I loved this class so much. This class has students with a mix of different math backgrounds but most students had previously taken AP Calculus BC and this was a good transition. There is so much available help for this class that the only way you can do poorly is if you don't take advantage of all the help they offer. I didn't use the MQC or office hours but my friends who did found them very useful. Most people's experiences in this class depends on who their TF is but fortunately I had Eva who made me look forward to coming to class MWF.
If you are interested in multivariable/need it for what you are planning to study, this course does exactly what its intended to do: It gives you foundations of calculus in higher dimensions. The TFs are all ver nice as well. However, this class is quite frustrating at times. Missing a singular lessons will feel as if you are weeks behind due to how fast its moved. Furthermore, the nightly pset (including right after exams) is a lot of work and often does not feel particularly useful at all.
Take this math class!! It was challenging for me at first but overall a great learning experience. With a AP Calc BC background, the course was very manageable – my TF, Eva, explained concepts very clearly and there are plenty of opportunities for problem–solving with problem sets! The three psets a week were also very manageable, make sure to take advantage of MQC regularly to collaborate with our students and clarify concepts with CAs. You also have many resources available to prepare for exams with pset practice and the practice exams made available to ensure you are ready for exams!
This is a very solid option for multivariable calculus. The best part about it is the sheer amount of quality CAs that were available in section to help out with problems whenever you were stuck. Workshops are annoying but a comparatively easy way to hit the QRD requirement, and the AI tutor is a decent alternative if your workshop leader isn't good. 3 PSets/week is manageable but still a lot and I wouldn't recommend stacking more than one additional STEM class on top of this one.
Take Math 21a! It is definitely challenging but it is 100% worth it.
Eva is the most wonderful course head and helped me so much to improve after the first midterm; however, I still found this class pretty difficult and the 3 psets a week are not for the weak.
Visit MQC as much as possible! It really helps with problem sets.
Take this class seriously because it definitely isn't a joke. What I mean is to be attentive during lecture because it isn't like other recorded lectures. Take notes before, during class, and after if you can because the jump from the worksheets to problem sets and exams is a bit difficult. For people who have never taken multivariable calculus or are completely unfamiliar with it and vector calculus, I highly recommend brushing up on some concepts and basics before taking this class. It will make a world of difference.
People either love or hate this class. Its a lot of visualization, so if thats not your thing you might struggle, especially early on.
Your TFs and Instructor make/break the class – EVA IS THE BEST! Have fun in your section/workshop and the class won't feel like work. Also, work with others on your PSET and do not procrastinate them and you will have a 20x better experience. Tests aren't too hard.
This class holds you hand a lot, and you should work with friends on Psets if you get stuck. Practice exams are pretty different from the tests though, especially the final, so be careful. You want to really know your stuff for those, especially the final.
