If you like Jane Austen, TAKE THIS CLASS! Having a class where the homework (reading and thinking about Austen's novels) made me genuinely happy was such a beautiful part of my semester. Prof. Lynch is very engaging, and lectures were always interesting and informative. Sofia is also an awesome TF! The reading is absorbing and (maybe as a result) very manageable, especially if you break it up and do like half an hour a day. The essay prompts were all very interesting (there are 4 essays spaced throughout the semester), and writing them made me so much more engaged with the books and other readings. I learned a lot from this class: it made me see both Austen and fan culture in a completely new way. Highly recommend!!
This class is phenomenal! Professor Lynch is so passionate about the subject matter that she provides spectacular lectures!! More importantly she encourages students to be creative and have fun with course assignments and discussions. In fact, part of our participation credit was sending Jane Austen memes to the course slack channel!! If you are considering taking this course please do! It is reading intensive but the readings are rather enjoyable.
Definitely take the class if you have the chance! Professor Lynch is a really great lecturer (funny, compelling, makes interesting points) who cares a lot about the material and it shows. It moves at about a book every week and a half so make sure you set aside time to do that in your week because they're definitely longer than I thought before I took the class, but audiobooks of Austen books on YouTube are free and can honestly be really helpful. Section was very fun also, basically just a book club debrief with a few die–hard Austen crazies and people that had never read most of the books, which was really fun.
This is an AMAZING English class. Great literature, fascinating lectures, engaged professor, and fun class culture. Take it, take it, take it! Everyone in the class gets super into Jane Austen together. It's like talking about a romcom each class with an English twist. Prof. Lynch is simply awesome as well.
Amazing class! So glad I got the chance to read all of Austen's novels and learn about them from Professor Lynch, who is a scholar in this field and so so knowledgable! The assignments did pick up towards the second half of the course but Professor Lynch was very receptive to my request for an extension and I appreciated the opportunity to turn in work I felt I was truly proud of. The prompts for the evaluative papers were really interesting and I often had difficulty picking just one to write on. Going to the Houghton Museum for the first research paper was also a really unique experience and one I'm glad I had as I learned so much about fandom in the 19th–century and how people consumed and interacted with art. Class discussions were always engaging and even I wasn't up to participating, learning from others was still a highlight of class time. I'm not an English concentrator and was a bit hesitant about the course for that reason, and the standards for writing certainly aren't low in the class, but I felt like I could go to my TF with questions or discuss the feedback on my writing which was very helpful for future assignments. I really felt like I learned a lot and I'm quite glad I got the opportunity to take this class in my final year at Harvard. If this class is being offered, you should certainly take it! Don't miss out on such a wonderful experience to read some great novels and engage with them in a fun way.
TAKE THIS COURSE. This has been one of my absolute favourite courses at Harvard so far, Professor Lynch is so incredibly passionate about the materials and I have come away learning so much. Lecture is engaging and discussion encouraged, section is the same, and there is a very reasonable amount of work set which is all relevant to the course. Adam is by far the best TF I've had at college, facilitating sharing in section, giving great feedback, and caring about is students. I couldn't recommend a course more and if you have the chance to take it do it.

The lectures were so engaging, and Professor Lynch seems to have mastered the blend between lectures and audience participation. You will love it.
This course is absolutely incredible, and if you get the opportunity to take it you 100% should. Professor Lynch is brilliant and I feel so lucky to have been able to take a course with such a renowned scholar and professor. She is so kind and it was so special to get to know her through office hours. The section component of this course was my favorite hour of class each week, and I found myself absolutely enraptured during each lecture. The course significantly deepened my knowledge of and appreciation for Jane Austen, and the assignments were really fun and not too overwhelming–there is no long final paper and instead a creative option is available which I had so much fun with! Overall, this class is a must–take and has been one of the best classes I've taken at Harvard.

She is a very compassionate and engaging professor who wants to see her students grow into themselves as readers and thinkers and writers and her class is a formative step to developing that. I felt sad on the last day because there was so much to learn despite reading all of JA’s novels (only 6) — I think more than reading Austen, you learn a lot about yourself and your preferences when you are in a signle–author course like this.
