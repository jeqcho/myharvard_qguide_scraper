This is a really enjoyable GENED. Professor Hyman is an engaging lecturer who works to incorporate students into the lecture by asking open–ended questions and making students dive deeper into their answers. It can be hard to figure out what to take away from a given lecture, as the slides have a lot of information, and Professor Hyman tends to go on philosophical tangents, but ultimately the assignments rely much more on your ability to reason with ethical frameworks than your knowledge of specific facts. Sections are formatted as debates on a given fictional scenario, which provides a great opportunity to apply the ethical frameworks and psychology/neuroscience data discussed in class. With regard to assignments, both of the essays are short and graded fairly. The exams are fair in terms of content but require a lot of handwriting in a short period, which can be overwhelming if you are a slow writer. I ended up switching to pass/fail because this was my last GENED and I didn't feel confident in my ability to finish the exams, but I definitely could have received a good letter grade with a little more effort. Overall, I would definitely recommend.
