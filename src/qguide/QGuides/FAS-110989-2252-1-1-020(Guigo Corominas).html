Tough class but taught well and is a super important topic that is worth learning.
Review for those coming from 21A: Now that you have experienced a higher level of abstraction, it is time to take another step in that direction with this course. I think you are more than ready, but be prepared to wrestle with these concepts. Ultimately, I would recommend that you create a schedule to frequent office hours; this not only helps you with your pset grades, but also ensures that you ask questions to the teaching team that aid in your conceptual understanding of the material, as having agency over it will be valuable in courses that use linear algebra. In that schedule, I would also suggest reading the prescribed textbook sections before section and after, and reviewing the worksheet solutions. THEN work on the PSet during office hours. This will likely ensure a close to 100% uptake of the material with lasting and intuitive understanding.

Review for incoming first–years: Depending on the high school you are coming from, this may be vastly different or similar to what you are used to, so give yourself some time to try out different study techniques, especially for this course, as the three psets a week can really grind your gears. If you have a decent understanding of multi, then you are more than ready for this course. It will refer to multi concepts a few times for specific lessons but some review should suffice. I mainly want you to be informed of the abstractness of the material and the amount of time this will take out of your week (10–16 hours outside of class). Good luck!
Keep up with studying well before exams
Here is the difference between 21a and 21b. The material in 21a is more difficult to understand. However, the midterms and finals are exactly like the practice exams, so if you do and understand the practice exams, you can get A. For 21b, it is much easier to understand the content, but the exams are made up of questions you have never see before, so you have to expand on your understanding. As a result, getting an A is much more difficult.

Overall, in 21b, you learn a lot. You will work hard, but in the end, it will be worth it.
