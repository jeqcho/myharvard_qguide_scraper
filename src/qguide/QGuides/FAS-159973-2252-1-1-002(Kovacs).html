What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class is an absolute GEM!!!! Take it, Hajnalka Ji handles this subject with so much depth and care, and you're in good hands with her. She cares a lot about the subject as well as the health of her students, and isn't too strict. This class builds a lot on 101A, so the grammar and logic based approach to the Hindi/Urdu language she begins there is supremely helpful (not that you can even recieve credit for this course without 101A). In terms of coursework, its workbook exercises, one weekly assignment, and some Urdu practice. Easily manageable, and you should basically treat this class like a PSET–based one, because that's basically what it is. Not to say that the class is hard: the grading is SUPER lenient, and you have as much time to finish classwork as you need, although you really should try to get it in weekly. There's only one final, but it's only 30% of the grade and you'll get plenty of time to practice and learn the language. The only bad part of this class is having to do the conversations in the workbook (1 per unit), but that's tedious, not hard. Other than that, the class environment is super welcoming and great. You'll get plenty of time to talk about what interests you, and you're allowed to bring food to class as long as you are clean about it. Hajnalka is super understanding of student's circumstances as well, she's really knowledgeable and smart too.
Hajnalka Ji is really kind and a great teacher. She is flexible with homework assignments, gives good feedback, and adjusts flexibly to the level of the learners in the class
Hindi–Urdu 101b is a good course just like its a counterpart, but definitely harder. Learning the Urdu script takes time, and the grammar concepts this semester are difficult (even for native speakers). However, the difficulty makes it more rewarding.
