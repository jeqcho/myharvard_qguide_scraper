Comments
I don't feel like most of the content is too difficult but the 3 psets a week, CA's/TF's grading extremely harshly and taking off unnecessary points on psets and exams, and the lack of teaching from the TF as its focused more on teaching yourself and going up to the board is what makes it difficult. its possible to do well if you put in a lot of work on your own to fully understand the content and perform well on the assignments, which may be difficult depending on how much time you have
Make sure to do all of the problem sets and sections. I missed a few and that definitely made it hard to follow class and catch up, as the classes are so frequent.
Review the worksheet solutions after every class. There were times when there would be something that we had to include in the answer to receive full credit that wasn't mentioned in section at all and was only included in the worksheet solutions. On the first exam I got an insane amount of points off for not fully explaining my answers even though they were correct because I answer the questions the way that we did in class without looking at the worksheet solutions.
I understand if you need to take this class, but whatever you've heard about Math 1b, it's all true. Go to MQC and Office Hours early if you have to take this class. The lack of curve may kill your GPA
Do–able as long as you don't let yourself get confused and fall behind –> ask questions.
This class is not easy but definitely manageable. Using MQC and office hours and study groups to really max out the points on PSETs is definitely a must. Do the extra credit when it's available; it can help push your grade up that extra 0.5–1%. Practice materials for exams are plentiful, so if you actually start studying with time before the exams, they are definitely manageable.
Take this class when you are able to dedicate a lot of time to it. You can be successful if you have time to focus on the PSETs and study for the exams but it is hard if you get behind
