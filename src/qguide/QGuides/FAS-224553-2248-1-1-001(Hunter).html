What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I strongly recommend if this course if you are very interested in biology, would like to learn how to read and evaluate scientific papers, and if you are willing to put in a good amount of work that is not normally associated with first–year seminars. The readings can feel very challenging to understand at times but that is expected and a normal part of the process. I thought I understood very little of what had been covered throughout the seminar until I gave my final presentation at the end of the semester and realised I understood much more than I thought I did and could speak confidently on advanced biological ideas.
It's a lot of fun and you will learn a ton about how to read scientific articles.
Do the readings every week or you will get nothing out of each class. Also, re–read the papers multiple times, ask questions, go to office hours, do the summaries with other people!
IF YOU FEAR FOR AN INCOMING RIGOROUS SEMESTER, TAKE THIS COURSE!

The workload for this seminar was very fairly manageable, as you only need to devote some hours once per week. Aside from the final presentation, your only duties are to read one or two research articles per week and have question ready about them for Prof. Hunter. For the final presentation, you will choose a research article, a bit more complex than those discussed in class, and you will cover it in class for around 20 minutes, a really manageable assignment.

–––

tl;dr: low stakes course, take an already heavy semester ahead.
This is a really great course. C. elegans start to appear in your dreams so there's that. Give yourself enough time for the readings. They are readings you have to read and read again. Craig is super nice and has the best office hours.
