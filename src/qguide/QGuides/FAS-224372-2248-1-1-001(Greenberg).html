Comments
This class is an amazing opportunity to learn about religion and state from someone with both advocacy and academic experience. Dr, Greenberg is so generous with her time in office hours, so take advantage of that. You can also write your paper about whatever is exciting to you, so it's super flexible, and the course readings are interesting, if a bit long. Dr. Greenberg can be a bit strict and serious, and is careful about a professor–student boundary (she's one of the only people in SocStd who goes by "Professor" or "Dr"), but she's seriously so nice and SO smart – you'll be lucky to learn from her.
This is a great tutorial for anyone interested in religion and social life. Dr. Greenberg is great, and super helpful for thesis thinking. Would highly recommend.
doooo the readings !! it's worth it and you get much more out of the class discussions if you know what's roughly going on
Professor Greenberg is a fantastic tutorial leader who sets high expectations but is deeply invested in your learning and development. This is not a course to take if you're looking for an easy junior tutorial. Instead, its an active and rigorous learning experience that will transform the way you approach social studies and build on the foundational material from 10a and 10b.

If you enjoy theory and appreciated how each week of 10a and 10b challenged your thinking and broadened your perspective, youll have a similar, mind–expanding experience in this class. Its an intellectually rewarding extension of the core material that pushes you to think critically and engage deeply with complex ideas.
I think this class is well done but perhaps less engaging than other social studies junior tutorials. If you are interested in religion, I think this class is a great opportunity. It also doesn't require a background knowledge in religion, but it is helpful for many of the discussions.
