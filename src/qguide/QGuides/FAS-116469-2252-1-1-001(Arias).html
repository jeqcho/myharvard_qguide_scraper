What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I think for students, they should really consider whether you have the time commitment to take this course, because it is very time consuming and requires a lot of studying and practice if you don’t have any Spanish experience. Although, I would say that it is a very collaborative course, and it was very fun to come to class everyday. The instructor was very very helpful and gave assistance whenever possible. Everything in this course was very organized and there is a lot of assistance.
I really loved Spanish 15! It's a little intensive in terms of time, but most of the time class was a joy and I loved the environment we had. It was very supportive and we got to talk about some really interesting topics — learning about Latinx and Hispanic culture, getting to know more about Boston and immigration across the world. It was enriching and I always left class with a smile on my face!

9am everyday sounds hard but it's really not, and helps you get a good sleep schedule. I think the immersion made it way easier for me to learn Spanish because I would be using it practically everyday, and when we had breaks, e.g. Spring break I could feel the struggle a little to get back into the rhythm. Profesor Israel, and our TF Ceci were just wonderful! They brought so much energy everyday to class and were so supportive and wholesome :) we really got to know them – and each other in class – and so it felt a lot less formal, and it was never intimidating to speak in class. I had a great set of classmates and honestly learned so much from them — even if we were speaking broken Spanish.

Be prepared to do lots of daily readings on Canvas, but they're short and the quizzes are manageable. The class is definitely a commitment, but it's really worth it! The assignments don't take all that much time if you're engaged in class and help reiterate and store knowledge. Before every exam there's generally a study guide and so you'll be super prepared and have good expectations for what's to come.
If you are willing to wake up at 9am 5 days a week, take this course. Honestly, that was the only drawback of the course. In general, it teaches you a lot about Spanish, you learn a lot of time tenses, and words – you start speaking somewhat confidently from the 2nd month. It was very impressive how fast you can start speaking in basic sentences. The instructor is very nice and the TF –– Ceci –– was very very helpful and the nicest person ever. Israel is also incredibly helpful, understanding and willing to meet outside of class. The class is very interactive and you become close with your classmates. It is not easy to do well in class in terms of grades –– there are lots of assignments but you really can and should stay on top of your assignments because the cutoff for A is a 95.
TAKE THIS!! I have taken another language courses here (in a different language) but this one absolutely blows everything out of the water. I had a lot of incentives to learn Spanish and was shocked at how much progress I was able to make in just 12 weeks. I went from no Spanish at all (Duolingo level 1) to being able to hold a (albeit very basic) conversation with a Spanish–speaker and to understand conversations. The instructors (Israel and Ceci) were incredibly wonderful, always helping me improve and encouraging me. It was absolutely worth the daily 9ams.
I took this course as a graduate student in an intensive STEM program. Yet I managed to attend the classes every day in the morning and achieve a good level.
