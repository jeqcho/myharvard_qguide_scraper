Do your readings and if you can, write some of your thoughts down before class so that these notes help you better engage with what other people talk about! The weekly critical papers help a lot.
This is a great course to take about disability, especially for anyone who has not had experience with disability studies or a focus within disability in general. I think that the readings are very heavy and will take many hours to complete and read to the level which is expected, as well as to sufficiently participate in discussions. Definitely take a few anthropology courses before taking this course. Otherwise, the professor and TA are incredibly supportive, and I really appreciated their assistance and guidance throughout the course of the semester.
Take it! If you are interested in disability studies at all this course is extremely helpful in terms of introducing you to the field. The class is very discussion based, and readings are exciting to talk about. The course staff are also extremely helpful! Overall I had a great time!
This course was a great introduction to disability studies and anthropology of the body. Through the readings, you will learn different theoretical frameworks surrounding disability as well as lived experiences. Discussions were very thought–provoking and helped me think about disability through various perspectives. Dr. Az really cares about her students, is highly receptive to student feedback, and provides useful support and feedback. I would highly recommend this class!
I really enjoyed this class. I think it is a very difficult topic to grapple with, but the discussions each week always left me with something to discuss with others and a new perspective. In terms of workload, the readings are not difficult and the discussion posts (which are once a week) are truly not extremely difficult, but it does take a lot of time to formulate arguments and perspectives for discussions because a lot of the readings are very nuanced. Overall, liked the class and would recommend!
This course was excellent and thought provoking. Each class raised very interesting and relatable topics on disability. I highly recommend taking this course!!
Solid elective. Manageable assignments and great reading selections. Lecturer is a little intense, but with a good class, the discussion will be bountiful.
Had a very interdisciplinary group of students, and people outside of anthro still thrived. Do not let that stop you. Dr. Az is incredibly smart about this field, and it is difficult to find this topic at Harvard. Content–wise, this class is incredible for learning more and changing your understanding of disability. Would recommend! I will say it felt a little disorganized as the inaugural teaching of this class, but Dr. Az was receptive to feedback, and I'm confident it will be better in the future. Take!! Am biased bc I'm writing my thesis on disability though.
