Please take this course! It's the most enjoyable course I've taken in all four years of college. There is no performance pressure at all, just pure curiosity and deep discussion. The topics are the most important topics of our times, and it's crucial to understand theoretical perspectives on politics and economics if we are to change our broken political and economic systems today. Every student should take this as part of their education, regardless of their interests.
This course EATSSS. as someone who literally hated theory (and like lowkey still is annoyed by it/doesn't care for it) Professor Ecker does such an amazing job of making the content interesting and digestible. He is amazing at facilitating discussion and guiding you through writing papers and the class through talking. He is honestly the reason I ended up loving this class despite having a predetermined disdain for the content. And also he is so good with grading and feedback!! Literally such a goated professor.
This is a very rare opportunity to have this material taught this enthusiastically and so collaboratively.
If you have any interest in political economy, economics, and/or the ideas behind them, you should take this class! The readings can be difficult at times, but Jordan does a great job of extracting all the key points while also allowing people the freedom to talk about what interests them most. The papers also require you to do a good deal of thinking and review, but I do think they are manageable if you've been keeping up with the readings and the course themes.
I think that this class is my favorite class I have taken in the gov department, I am not ever someone who would think I am good at theory, I am intimidated by it, I loved this class so much the, I highly recommend it, like with enthusiasm.
If youre a gov/ec concentrator/secondary, this course is perfect for you. Ecker is fantastic at moderating conversation and clarifying when necessary. I went in with no knowledge about the economy, but found relevant ways to participate. Genuinely enjoyed my time in class.
This was a terrific class. Dr. Ecker does a great job breaking down readings each week and facilitating good discussions. This helped me understand economic thinking and theory in an in–depth and substantial way. Readings are manageable at about 100 pages each week. Assignments are doable (reflection, essay, final essay).
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Jordan's wonderful, and the course material is incredibly fascinating — a great way to fulfill your theory requirement. You cover many of the foundational thinkers in political economy (e.g., Smith, Marx, Keynes, Jevons, Weber, etc), which is useful for future coursework. Jordan has this amazing ability to break down and explain dense texts; bless his heart for trying to make Keynes understandable. You don't need a background in economics or anything like that for this class. I really looked forward to class every week and could not recommend this more.
