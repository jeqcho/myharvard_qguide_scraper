Take Math 21a! If you're unsure whether you'd like to pursue a more quantitative field and have a decent math background coming from high school, 21a is perfect. The support system is incredible with MQC run by CAs to office hours held by all TFs. Stay on top of the 3 psets per week by starting on the day that they're assigned and going to MQC if you have questions about any of the problems. The exams are relatively straightforward and focus exclusively on the content covered in class. I recommend making a quick summary of each worksheet in a notebook after each class to simplify studying for the midterms and final. Do the extra credit opportunity if you score below 90 on any exam. Also do the skill check and submit the exam practice to earn pset drops. It's kind of annoying that there are no extensions on psets, so make sure to capitalize on opportunities to earn extra pset drops if you know that you're going to have busy weeks.
This class is a lot of work. You meet 4 times a week if you include the additional required math workshop, and you have 3 psets a week which honestly are not short. Exams are definitely VERY fair, but psets and practice exams are generally more difficult. DO EVERY PSET AT MQC AND UNDERSTAND EVERY QUESTION!!! Or you likely will NOT succeed in this class

If you need to take multivariable calculus as a concentration requirement, or just want to explore more math classes, I would definitely recommend Math 21a. This course has one of the most dedicated, consistent, and organized teaching teams Ive experienced here at Harvarddefinitely a breath of fresh air compared to some other courses of similar size. I had Nathan as a TF, and he was absolutely amazing. Paying attention in section and fully understanding pset material is key to doing well in this course. Math 21A is a very well–structured class that will guide you through the material in a methodical way. If you are proactive in using the resources (MQC, OH, pset groups), you will get a lot out of the class. The 3 psets a week allow the work to be broken down in a very reasonable way and will help you not get overwhelmed. The class also has a lot of ways you can learn the material (worksheets, videos, textbook) so if one way doesn't work for you, you can always try another. Grading is generally very fair.
