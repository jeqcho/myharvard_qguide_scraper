The course was a bit disorganized this time around (lectures were a bit hard to follow, assignments shifted a lot, etc) because it was the first time it was taught, but I am sure it will improve immensely. The material was so fascinating, and most of the lectures are just watching and talking about movie clips. The sections were super interesting, the assignments are pretty straightforward and accessible for non–music people, the weekly screenings were so fun, and the guest lectures were super cool. Take it take it take it !!!!

Seriously, you learned something new and totally unexpected from each of them. Otherwise, this class is for ANYONE, we ALL watch movies.

Let me preface this by saying that I'm a film music fanatic I love film scores and how they function. If you feel similarly, you'll have some engaging discussions during the course, but you will not learn all that much from lectures. My main gripes:

1. The course is pretty haphazardly put together. Some weeks' themes blended together (e.g. horror/anempathy and classical music in film were two separate weeks of content, but overlapped almost entirely); film choices didn't always make sense; and the assignments were mostly reflections on guest lectures. You won't become a better writer, composer, or thinker by virtue of taking the class. The readings and guest speakers are perhaps the highlight, but you can watch the guest speakers even if you aren't in the course, and you can also read the readings...

2. If you are interested in film MUSIC and how it functions as a score, this is NOT the course for you! There is no music theory in the course not a whit. You will not learn how to compose film music, nor analyze a film–music score. If you are an AFVS concentrator (specifically, a film concentrator) looking to learn more about film music and film sound, this class might work for you.
