Unfortunately, previous year reviews of MCB 175 are correct – while the Canvas page for the course states that they give A’s for effort, the A’s come at the cost of interest and engagement. Although the course is very minimal in terms of assignments, the topics themselves are extremely boring and have nothing to do with my passions within the molecular biology field. I have never found myself to be excited about this class, but instead sit through lecture just with the hopes of earning an A. If that’s what you’re looking for, MCB 175 is the perfect fit! However, don’t get your hopes up in terms of the content, as the topics truly put me to sleep.

Very manageable workload, but be careful those reading quizzes can be tricky. The research proposal is a great exercise if you're actively involved in research.

The teaching fellows were also very kind and available to answer any questions or discuss the course material.

This class is very manageable in terms of courseload. I love how the class is more focused on learning and collaboration, rather than tedious assignments or psets. The weekly quizzes are also very manageable and there's support in office hours if you ever need help.
Great course would highly recommend!!
Take this class! It is a very interesting class if you want to learn more about ion channels and their biological and physiological applications in humans and other organisms. It also involves weekly quizzes where you read a paper and answer a couple quiz questions, 2 take home exams (one of them is questions on the first few lectures and the second one is on literature review), group presentation, and final research proposal. Different assignments enable you to further your understanding on this specific subject and teach you to think and communicate your ideas scientifically and effectively. The professor and the teaching team are very supportive and kind. I recommend people to take the class!
