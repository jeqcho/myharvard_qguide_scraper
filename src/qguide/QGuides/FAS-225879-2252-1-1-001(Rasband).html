What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I hope the government department can expand the curriculum catalog regarding migration and keep this type of content available for students, even though its a controversial topic for many students.
The GOV Department doesn't have courses on immigration policy and the ones that exist outside of the department are largely U.S.–focused. I'd recommend this course because of its deep dive into assessing immigration policy and its impacts on multiple dimensions, while using a comparative perspective that looks at the application of immigration policy globally. You will learn a lot about the ways immigration is talked about and governed across the globe. While there is a Western lean, I think what makes this course stand out is its engagement with scholarship from the Global South which unfortunately isn't common enough in the GOV Department. If you're interested in immigration policy enough to write a robust final paper on it, take this course.
