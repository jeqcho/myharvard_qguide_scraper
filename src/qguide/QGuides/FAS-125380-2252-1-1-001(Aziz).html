Take this course if you're interested about energy technology and want to build a basis for a discussion instead of just copying what the news may say about energy
Would really recommend this class! The class is a bit of work, but definitely worth it as you learn so much about energy technology. The professor is very knowledgeable and the class is very rewarding!
This course is a must–take for anyone interested in the energy/electricity sectors – it offers a very thorough treatment of virtually all energy–related technologies, spanning both fossil fuels and renewables, as well as other relevant topics like energy economics and power grids. I feel like this is the one class in particular I've taken at Harvard that has best informed/reshaped my understanding of the broad landscape of energy and power.
Regarding work, the textbook is the linchpin of the course and strikes an excellent balance between breadth and technical detail; the lectures largely follow the same structure as the book. As such, my main advice is: the more time you put into reading the textbook before each class, the more you will get out of this course. It's a lot to take in, but you won't regret putting in the legwork here. This also goes for the final project: start early and pursue something interesting, and it will be a highly rewarding experience. Finally, definitely go to Prof. Aziz's office hours or otherwise discuss the content/projects with him, and also definitely try to make all of the guest lectures!
A very interesting survey of practically every technology for generating, storing, and converting energy. I loved the exposure to a variety of different technologies and also some of the more rigorous engineering sections, e.g. on thermodynamics. The problem sets are not too much work (~10 hours, every 2 weeks) but sometimes feel tedious. There is a really wide range of backgrounds and a lot of undergrads in the class which sometimes limits how technical the discussion is – which I personally felt like was a drawback but I understand allows the class to be accessible to a wider audience. Prof Aziz typically goes through students' annotations/comments on the online textbook reading before every lecture which is a level of dedication I have not seen from any other professor before.
The course is approachable and useful, it doesn't matter what your background is, you will take something useful from this. If you have any interest in energy science, this will give you the opportunity to explore the topic in great detail while simultaneously fulfilling academic requirements. Highly recommend.
Prof. Aziz is very knowledgable, but this course is not structured well. Each lecture focuses on a specific energy technology, and while the course covers a wide variety of technologies, it seems very disorganized. The content is based on Aziz's textbook with pre–readings required for each lecture. I did not get much out of lecture, however the textbook was well–written. The assignments were relatively vague with little guidance. I was excited to take this course, as someone interested in the energy sector, however, I am skeptical to recommend this class.
Typically, you'd either get a surface level overview of many, or a highly focused zoom on one specific, energy technology. This courses balances both – covering all areas at large, and at the same time giving you the keys to explore any specific element at depth. Can be intense but rewarding.
