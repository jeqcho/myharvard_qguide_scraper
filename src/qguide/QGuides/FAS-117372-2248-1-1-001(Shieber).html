This class had pretty bad Q reviews last year so I was hesitant to take it. Really, I think the organization was probably better this year than last year but there is still a lot of room for improvement. It's true that the labs are better than psets, which makes this class "easier" than 120 or 124 or the like, but it was frustrating to feel like I never quite knew what they wanted from the labs and final project segments. I was always getting docked points for little things like not commenting code (didn't know that was part of the rubric) or my short answer questions in the labs (there wasn't much feedback so idk what the issue was). I think maybe I just also didn't have the math/stat background despite taking Stat 110 and Math 21a/b, so a lot of the time I would just be staring at the lab question and not really know where to start. OH weren't super helpful since TFs didn't always have full solutions so they had to manually debug code which is just hard! I've TFed other classes and would never want to be in that position. I actually have no idea what grade I got, like not even a ballpark estimate...

Overall, if you want to take this class, I would recommend 1) learning more about pytorch 2) previewing the textbook ahead of time 3) attempting to figure out what the rubric will be in advance (but this might not be possible tbh) and 4) STARTING ON THE LAST FINAL PROJECT SEGMENT EARLY BECAUSE IT'S 10x AS HARD AS THE OTHERS
Labs were kinda difficult and got harder as the semester went on, so you need to go to office hours. I feel like I have an ok understanding of NLP after this course – very applicable to ChatGPT type of stuff – if that is what your goal is. Many assignment grades have not been released and we have no idea what our grades mean so it's hard to gauge how well you are doing in the course. I think it is more organized than last year though. That being said, I thought having two labs a week and a larger problem set every other week was very manageable and not too much work.
I would like to tell future students to TAKE this course. Prof Shieber is great, and you will see a unique perspective on one of the defining technologies of our time, LLMs.
Take this course if you are interested in NLPs but don't expect to learn much about the latest developments. A lot of this is linguistics and you don't receive feedback most of the time. Decent general intro though.
If you are at all interested in machine learning and NLP, take this class. It's a little rough around the edges; questions go unanswered, assignments go ungraded, and no one ever really knows what's going on. With that said, however, professor Shieber is an EXCELLENT source of knowledge and is always more than happy to discuss his work and philosophy. There really is a lot to learn from this class. For those completely new to ML, you'll walk away with a pretty strong grasp of PyTorch and the resources and skills to start building and training your own models. For those interested in NLP, you'll touch on every single part of the language model pipeline and walk away implementing your very own sequence to sequence model from scratch. Additionally, you'll learn a lot about formal language theory. While this language is less broadly applicable to ML, it is still interesting stuff especially if you're into linguistics at all.

This is one of those classes where you get out what you put in. If you're willing to spend the time and work past this courses flaws, you'll find this class really is wonderful. On top of that, work outside of class is definitely a lot less than a standard upper level CS class, which is very nice.
I took this class hoping the feedback from previous years might finally be incorporated to provide a better learning experience. I was mistaken. This is one of the most poorly structured courses I have taken. Was it the worse thing ever? No, but it was not particularly enjoyable either. Assignments were okay, but for many of them there is a lack of guidance, and you need to heavily rely on office hours, which are few and far between, and frequently are moved or canceled. Feedback is nearly non–existent, and we have still not received feedback from assignments due in early–mid October. A number of the concepts are a bit outdated. If you're just looking for a class to fulfill the AI tag and don't want to take 181, like I was, perhaps you can take this, but definitely make sure you take it with friends, as it will make the experience more bearable. However, if 182 is offered, or if in future years there are more options to fulfill the requirement, I advise taking one of those instead.
