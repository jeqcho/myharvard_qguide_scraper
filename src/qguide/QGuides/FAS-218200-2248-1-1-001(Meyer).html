Super engaging class!! Rachel is an amazing lecturer incredibly knowledgeable, and genuinely concerned for the well–being/learning of the students. I'd highly recommend this course if you're even slightly interested in corporate culture, labor movements, emotional labor, flexible jobs, etc.
This is a great course, with an enthusiastic and knowledgeable professor, a very important topic, interesting readings, and critical insights about organizing in 21st century America, and sources of working class alienation and right wing populism.
That said, as a social sciences concentrator and senior, it often felt overly simplistic Important theoretical topics felt dumbed down, essay prompts were overly prescriptive, and theoretical/quantitative readings were eschewed for simpler qualitative readings.
Take this course – especially if you're thinking about entering corporate america (consulting, banking, SWE). The content is very applicable to our day to day and will be very informative as you navigate recruiting at Harvard. Grading wise, Rachel and Minjin are very accessible and kind. They understand not everyone who takes the course is deep into sociology and writing and will help you out no matter where your writing skills are. That being said, I recommend taking EXPOS before this class – I know people who succeeded and didn't but I find it makes the essay planning and writing process much easier.
Take this course if you want a chill Sociology elective! Each week basically looks at a different group of workers/culture of workers, whether it's investment bankers or truck drivers. You talk about the context and actual worker experiences outlined in the readings during lecture (lecture is very interactive and discussion–based) and then your main assignments are to complete 3 papers based on the 3 units of the class. I would say that I was often not able to bring the things I learned from lecture into the actual essays I wrote because lecture was more about context or history, but they also provide a ton of office hours to make sure you have a good grasp of the material and are able to make a case about it. I personally enjoyed the format of this class and would recommend it to others.
Everyone should take this course especially if you plan to follow one of the typical Harvard trajectories of law, tech or finance etc. This class will open your eyes to the way the world works and give you the tools to operate within that world effectively. It is fascinating. Even if you do not plan to follow one of the Harvard trajectories this class is universally relevant to anyone who plans to work or who can make the choice to not work.
This is a great deep dive into "work" and "culture" from a sociological perspective. Interesting and engaging if you are looking to learn more about these topics, but this is not a great class if you're looking to simply explore sociology as a whole because it is so focused on this specific topic. If you stay on top of the readings and pay attention to class discussions, this course becomes quite enjoyable. Only thing is the paper topics are confusing, but the instructors will take the time to answer your questions and offer support.
Super interesting and applicable. Rachel is awesome and it's not a time–intensive class.
Rachel and Minjin were SO passionate about the topic and it was great to have a very discussion based section AND lecture.
Extremely relevant class that offers a low barrier to entry. This was my first and only sociology class, but I learned a lot relatively quickly on a topic that impacts everyone. Not too difficult – eye–opening readings were useful and not too long.
Take this class!!! Even if you have no interest in sociology, this class teaches you about the workforce. Rachel is an amazing lecturer and you will learn a lot, trust.
I especially recommend this class to non–sociology concentrators who are interested in learning more about their relationship to work as they prepare to enter the workforce. The lecture format can sometimes be a bit frustrating, as it oscillates between lecturing and small group discussion and individual participation in a way that can lack focus. Ultimately, if you are interested in work and enjoy thinking about how Harvard changes your relationship to different aspects of life, you'll enjoy this class.
Top 3 favorite course at Harvard. Professor Meyer is amazing at generating enthusiasm for the material and leads very good discussions during class time. Super appreciative that the readings are short (<50–65 pgs a week) and targeted.
This course was THE TRANSFORMATIVE EXPERIENCE of Harvard for me! Take it!
they are on your team!!
