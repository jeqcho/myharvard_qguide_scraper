An amazing introductory course into HEB. Genuinely learned so much through sitting in lecture and completing the weekly assignments (which probably took 1–2 hours max). Random but I feel like ratio of the amount that I took away from this course versus the amount of work was very high. Not having section was also nice. Would highly recommend to any person interested in evolutionary bio, archaeology, behavior, and human prehistory. Also, Abby and Ellie are absolutely amazing
Take this class!! As a humanities major, having this science class in the mix with my English and writing classes was a great way to give my brain a break from writing and reading and analyzing. I found the workload super manageable but didn't feel like an easy pass – grading is not harsh but learning from feedback feels super necessary to advance. Readings are really interesting – if you have the time, DO ALL THE READINGS!! They make a world of difference when you're listening to lecture, but also for material understanding overall. The readings Desmond picks are always super interesting and if you're interested in a specific topic, make sure to focus on the readings. The lectures are great but sometimes found myself drifting off. If you miss a lecture, you're not completely cooked but I would recommend going to all the lectures! It's new material every week and can be hard to catch up if you miss something, especially if you miss two in a row. The lecture slides are great to refer to, but Desmond says a lot of stuff that doesn't make it onto the slides in lecture. Assignments are great and can be procrastinated if you're a procrastinator. Super doable in the night they're due, but actually really helpful. I felt like the writing I did every week helped me to study for the final! And my writing improved so so much from TF feedback. She's super empathetic and doesn't punish you for the learning curve in academic writing in the beginning of the class. I felt like I was seeing my work improve with her help.
Great class!
Very low stakes class. Take it if you want an easy A. The material is not the most interesting in the world if youre not an HEB concentrator, but its your usual Evolutionary course. There are weekly assignments which take about half an hour to do max. Make sure you pay attention in class and you will be fine. The midterm is a take home answering a couple of questions and the final is seated.
Abby is great and so is the class. Super interesting topics, take it even if you don't want to concentrate in HEB. Highly recommend.
I learned so much about the evolution of our species. I think there is a general narrative that most people believe about the evolution of our species, why we are the only hominins left, and the superiority of our species. I think this course really opens eyes to a more accurate portrayal of this evolution and in doing so provides a lot of insight into who we are and why. It will also improve your academic writing!
Lectures are super interesting!
Take the class! The professor is an amazing lecturer and she's super nice and chill. The class is also really easy with a lot of chances to do well.
Take this course if you can! This course was my favorite out of every class I took this semester. Dr. Desmond ensures that assignments and exams are fair to a variety of students. The midterm is a written essay while the final is a sit–down exam– ensuring the course supports students with different strengths. Not to mention, the readings and lecture slides are relevant to main themes of the course. As an HEB concentrator, this course is extremely engaging and the material covers the content from the human and chimp last common ancestor to modern humans. Although there is so much to learn, the support of course staff ensures that the course does not feel overwhelming and you have everything you need to be successful.
Take this class! If the syllabus sounds even a little interesting, you should take this! The grading across assignments (essays, midterm, final) is more than fair, and the teaching staff limit the scope of these assessments to the content emphasized in lecture. This was by far the best class I've taken at Harvard so far. You will learn so much about human and cognitive evolution, and the lectures are very interesting.
Take this class! Lots of information but super rewarding in the end!
