Make sure to really try to understand the homeworks – there is a lot of information and moving parts packed into every question, and things can get very complicated.
This class is really difficult, but since it is a requirement for AM/GOV, there is no way around it. Go to OH and section as much as possible. The midterm and final makes up 80% of your grade, so make sure to study a lot for them. No matter how hard you think the exams are, they will be harder.
Great instructor who is invested in students' learning outcomes and is skilled at teaching. Recommend to those who are interested in learning about the concepts behind formal models.
This is a difficult class, but the section component was very helpful in understanding the material. Jerry is the best!
To PhD students: I would recommend taking Formal I at the beginning of coursework because 1) problem sets take time, 2) this is an introduction, so if you want to expand, then you definitely want to take formal II and then have the opportunity to take an econ political economy type class in the 2nd year. So I think that it the way to go. That being said, this is an N = 1, and there may definitely be a good rational to take it later.
If you are not really interested / experienced with game theory, or don't have to take this course, I would not recommend it. The exams are not well–leveled to the material learned / taught. The course is taught using a lot of jargon and notation, which is not helpful and just get's confusing. I'd avoid this course if you can
take this class if you want a comprehensive intro to formal theory at the grad level, but be prepared to devote time to it. Peter's great at explaining concepts and the class materials are amazing. It's also going to help you be a better consumer of formal methods but will not help at all in learning to produce your own models (not from this course alone at least).
This is an amazing class but be prepared to be challenged by the concepts at a first glance sometimes. Peter is such a nice professor and very available outside class and I really learned a lot in this class. Be ready to put some work in the assignments since its only 3 questions (on one PSET) per week but you will definitely need the week to work on them so start early. Also, go to office hours and section, however optional they may seem.
