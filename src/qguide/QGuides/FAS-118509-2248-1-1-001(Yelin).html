What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
It's a ton of time but is eventually very rewarding. Wouldn't recommend taking more than 1 easy class in parallel with this one.
The psets are hard!! Split them up within a study group and then discuss the problems together.
Start homework early and spend time studying the material outside of class. Use the notes!
Tbh I think you should only take this course if you already have some background in AMO physics (especially a theoretical background) and 100% know you will do research in this area. If you're a QSE student who doesn't do AMO, you will unfortunately have to suffer (unless they drop the requirement). The course goes way too fast. Lectures cover a ton of material and gloss over a lot of the mathematical details, which is unfortunate because so much of the PSETs are just extremely tedious and unintuitive math. Prof. Yelin and the TFs are very nice and generous with grading, and the final project is okay, but I think the class is just inherently too ambitious and torturing with the content it tries to cover. I didn't really learn anything except that I am so glad I don't do AMO physics. If you take this class, my advice is to know when to give up on the PSETs, because if you try to understand every little detail, you will end up wasting so much of your time.
