Overview

CHEM 27 is a continuation of CHEM 17, but with a strong focus on biological applications—think enzymes, pH effects, and biochemical pathways. The content is more intensive in both breadth and depth, so expect a faster pace and more complexity.

Workload & Preparation

Start reviewing early—ideally 1–2 weeks before the midterm. The material builds quickly, so staying on top of it is key.

Pros:

Tons of workshops and practice problems
Helpful info sheets with key cofactors, structures, and pathways
Many CHEM 17 mechanisms reappear—now with helpful abbreviations that make drawing easier
Annotated slides with speaker notes (a great alternative to rewatching lectures)

Cons:

No cheat sheets allowed (same as CHEM 17)
Don’t rely on memorizing! Practice questions are rarely reused or slightly modified—they’re usually conceptually new
Tips for Success
Focus on understanding the logic behind mechanisms and concepts (e.g., effects of a residue mutation on binding affinity)
Practice redrawing mechanisms from memory—on paper or iPad—without notes or lectures
Redo all PSETs and PollEverywhere questions
Review Gradescope feedback carefully; track your mistakes and learn from them
If you struggle with a PollEv question, rewatch that part of the Panopto lecture and listen to how the instructors explain it

Final Thoughts
This course can seem overwhelming at first, but it’s incredibly rewarding. It’s eye–opening to see how organic chemistry comes alive in biological systems!
