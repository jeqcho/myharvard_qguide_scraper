It's pretty good. not a life changing class or anything, but you'll learn 2–3 bulletpoints worth of ideas by the end of it. You don't get any feedback on papers, and in general not too much structure. 20% A, 70% A–, 10% B+ curve, so free A– but you have to participate every day and write good essays + good final project for A. If you are working on a startup, this might just be a good space to meet new people. You can have office hours with HBS professors and Kent who's a partner at Bessemer. They also bring in pretty cool guest lecturers from startups, so that gives you an opportunity to meet them too. I feel that the learning opportunities are moreso with interacting with the very knowledgeable staff than the cases and stuff, though those are cool and fun to read too. Overall I'd recommend it.
