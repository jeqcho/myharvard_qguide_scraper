Take this class! The content is so cool and the discussions are really fun. Professor Biel is great!
One of my favorite classes at Harvard and I am a CS major who took this as an elective. The film choices are excellent and found myself loving almost all of the films we watched. Even the couple of films that may have been on the slower side or weren't as good as some of the classics we watched, had very rich themes that led to great class discussions. This is a class where you get as much as what you put into it. The readings, while some weeks were a bit long, were very interesting and insightful, especially about such a rich period of American history that was the 70s. We read about the Vietnam War, New Hollywood, 70s disillusionment, crime, violence, family, feminism, and so much more. Professor Biel is also amazing. He facilitates really fun and interesting discussions, where you do a deep dive into the films you watch, and gives incredible feedback on your short essays. I couldn't recommend this class more and if you like watching film, then this class never feels like work
MY FAVORITE CLASS AT HARVARD. You get to watch movies by legends Francis Ford Coppola, Martin Scorsese, and more. Each movie had great analytical value and with the readings you could really see how it ties into themes of the 70s. Also coming from the AP system in the states where we sort of stop learning history around the 70s, this class was a super fun way to learn more about the context of the Nixon era, Vietnam, etc.
Workload wise it's super light: one movie every week with skimmable readings to complement them. He assigns a 500 word short response for every movie so there were around 10 total, but you only had to do 5 of them. So for half the semester you only have to watch a movie every week for preparation. I'd highly highly recommend this class.
