Professor Jenks is amazing and although the class is long, she does a great job of splitting it up between lecture, small group discussions, and the guest speakers. If you're thinking about law school in the future, this class gives you a great understanding of how to analyze statues and read court opinions.

Again, I cannot recommend this class enough – it was the highlight of my semester.
I learned a lot in this class and found it to be very informative of future career and academic interests. The class does a great job of going over a variety of topics, while also allowing you to go deeper into specific interests. As someone who is considering law school and a career in environmental regulations, Professor Jenks does a great job of explaining the different roles of professionals and how policies are made. I would recommend this class to anyone who is curious about the legislative processes of environmental laws and enjoys going over the details in how policies are made.
This is a FANTASTIC course! It gave me a much more realistic understanding of how industries, governments, and public stakeholders collaborate to pass environmental policy. We discussed very practical scenarios, like how long it takes to pass an environmental regulation, the legal uncertainty with what has been almost inevitable litigation on any policy, and the strategies to success. We also had really amazing guest speakers who talked about their real life experiences working on the topic at hand in each lecture. Although each lecture is technically 2 hours and 45 minutes, it always felt like it went by pretty fast. The professor is captivating and the TF Loann is also a really great person to talk to in Office Hours with useful tips on environmental careers. They are also kind in their flexibility for assignment deadlines. Students were regularly engaged, leading to fruitful discussions. Overall, I would say definitely take this class, but only if you are for sure interested in the nitty gritty of regulations/statues/court opinions, and more – otherwise it may be a bit too in the weeds.

I think this course is really helpful in helping students dive in the policy aspects of Environmental policy, before this class I have never written a single policy memo, and I think in that aspect it provides a helpful foundation, and the professor really walks the process through with a lot of assistance. However, I do think that it may be a bit different from what people expected, because when I first entered the course, I expected something more technical and science, but the whole course as the name states focuses on environmental policy; I think that it could focus more on policymaking, and the challenges that legislators have. I think we barely spoke on that. In addition, I also think that although we do speak on the interactions between the act of legislating and implementation, it was not as in depth.

This course is perfect for people who wants to learn about the fundamental legislation in areas such as emissions or other more specific policies, but I think if you are looking for a more technical or macro picture, it may not be the best course.

However, I do think that this course is very phenomenal in giving students assistance, and it is a good course to take if students are unsure if they want to pursue ESPP, it gives a lot of assistance and provides students with a great foundation.

I HIGHLY recommend this course to anyone who has any interest in government structures, climate mitigation/adaptation, and the environment. Professor Jenks, though very thorough in communicating the various obstacles and challenges of environmental + public health protection, maintains an optimistic, forward–thinking outlook that's incredibly refreshing compared to the doom and gloom that sometimes pervades environmental science courses.

The once–a–week section allows you to engage deeply with the material and spend more time thinking about issues and solutions rather than completing assignments as a means to an end. Additionally, the different perspectives of weekly guest speakers offer a view into the practical applications of everything you learn about. The assignments are challenging, but feedback is very helpful and grading is fair. I walk away from this course not only feeling more confident about my ability to engage with big questions and complex solutions but also more prepared to engage with challenging source materials and write policy memorandums. Take this course if you want to grow in your understanding, to grow as a writer, and to grow as an aspiring agent of change!
