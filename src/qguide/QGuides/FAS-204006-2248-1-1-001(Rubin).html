This course is challenging, they will grade assignments hard. However, if you have a passion for this area of science that will allow you to succeed and you will LOVE this course. One of my favorites, Professor Rubin is the best!
This class taught me a lot about how to think as a scientist and look at scientific literature with a critical eye. It provides a very translational and practical view of disease therapeutics that's insightful both for those with experience in academia and industry. Lectures both from Dr. Rubin and the guests lecturers are incredibly informative. The group project final is difficult and you should be working on it throughout the term while actively seeking feedback during the process. You will be expected to understand the field you cover with more depth than other courses may expect, but the professor and TAs are willing to help. As long as you can stomach the group project and challenging final, I would reccomend this course.
Enrolling in this class should be a must!! One of the greatest classes I have attended.
This is a decent SCRB class. basically no work for most weeks, which is nice when you are taking hard classes at the same time. there is a 20 page final paper that you work on throughout the semester with a partner, which is good if your partner is good and personable and prioritizes the class, which mine did not unfortunately. the lectures are super difficult to understand, with a lot of guest lecturers who are insanely smart and have trouble making that knowledge accesible to students. thankfully, you do not need to know the details of the lectures, just the main ideas.
Pros:
– the material is so interesting!
– awesome guest speakers!
– prof rubin is brilliant
– cookies every class!
– you will likely become friends with your partner for the final project

Cons:
– the quizzes are HARD
– the grading is tough and so confusing
– the final project expectations are really unclear. we got contradictory feedback constantly about what we should include/not include in our paper and i have no idea what grade i'm going to get on this paper. i also honestly have no idea what the teaching team wants me to write.
– the TFs are not really helpful

tldr I'd say take it because it's a great class and you'll learn a lot but also idk what grade you will get
This entire course is project based along with a few quizzes sprinkled in, so it can feel difficult at times to have your grade depend on just the project really. I would definitely recommend speaking a lot with the course staff about your project idea so you can be sure you are going in a direction that they want to see. Other than that, Lee really enjoys what he is teaching about and he brings in relevant and interesting guest lecturers.
I LOVED this class! But it was by no means easy, and you definitely get out what you put in. The 20 page final paper may sound a bit daunting but its completed with a partner and half is submitted as the midterm, so you only really have to write two separate five page chunks. For me, the quizzes were considerably more challenging than the paper and definitely required great note taking in class and careful reading of all the papers.

All that said, the difficulty of the course was well worth it, and I learned SO much. Incredible teaching staff who were all highly supportive with the final project and other components of the course. Professor Rubin is particularly amazing and cares so much for all his students (brought us cookies every day!). The course content is super relevant and current and I frequently used information from this class in my lab and in other classes. 10/10 would recommend for anyone willing to put in the required effort.
Interesting course! This is one of the most business focussed courses in the SCRB department, and you will definitely learn more about drug development and drugs on the market more than other courses. Professor Rubin and the teaching team are very passionate and very encouraging of participation! The project can be a bit daunting and if you get a not so collaborative partner, then it can be a bit of a nightmare. Additionally, the quizzes can be a little tough and random and definitely require a fair bit of thinking.
Dr. Rubin is an amazing teacher and the class itself is unlike most other STEM classes out there as it encourages questions and really teaches you the intricacies of ESC cells from start to finish. The teaching staff are thoughtful with feedback and there are lots of opportunities to engage more deeply in the course material.
