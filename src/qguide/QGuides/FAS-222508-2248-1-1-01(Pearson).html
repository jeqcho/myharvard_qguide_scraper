THE BEST SEMINAR! Anne is such an amazing teacher, super caring, supportive and passionate about teaching this course. The readings are interesting, and the assignments are very straight forward. Going into this class my family has a little garden on the side of our house and learning how to properly maintain a balanced system was super interesting and applicational. I would definitely say that an interest in the ecosystem, farming, or the environment will make this class so much better and way more interesting and collaborative. I loved the people in the seminar making the discussions fun and interactive. I would basically sum this class up as foodie readings, foodie talks, foodie movies, vineyard making, and snack time!
Definitely take this class if you are interested in learning more about eco–friendly farming practices! I found the weekly readings to be genuinely interesting, and the snacks to go with them were always delicious. Even as someone who isn't interested in concentrating in environmental science, I found this course to be a blast.
Really fun and engaging class! Very accessible to people without a strong science background. Interesting connections to history, government, anthropology, etc. Opened my mind to new ways of approaching agriculture. Snacks every week!!
Definitely take this class. It is a great overview of biogeochemistry and sustainable agriculture practices, and helps you learn so much more about where your food comes from. Ann, the professor, is fantastic, and always makes class fun and interesting. Snack time is also so fun, and the food every week is always delicious.
Take this seminar!! Ann is the best professor, so approachable and easy to talk to, and you get amazing, homemade snacks every week! The small community really helps you build stronger connections with your peers compared to a larger class, and its a very relevant topic with ties to so many different fields of studywhether youre a life sciences, social sciences, or humanities major, theres something here for you!!
Just enjoy the class. Go in with an open mind to learn, don't stress, and you will have a great time.
