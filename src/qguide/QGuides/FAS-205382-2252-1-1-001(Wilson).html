What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this, especially if you want to think critically.
Prof. Wilson is phenomenal and considerate—if you are interested in the relationship between government and the university, the role of professional science in war (cold or otherwise), or twentieth–century American foreign policy... take this class!
This class is incredible and I’m so glad I took it! The material covers a thorough range of Cold War topics from all sorts of interesting perspectives. The readings always surprised me, and I learned new, mind–blowing things every week. It made me draw connections between many ideas I’d encountered in science, economics, and daily life that I would never have thought about otherwise.

Prof. Wilson is a really excellent professor. He is skilled at guiding discussions towards interesting questions without controlling the conversation. He challenged and elaborated on students’ points in a productive way that made me think more critically, all in a welcoming and encouraging environment. His feedback throughout the final paper process was super helpful, and I appreciated that he was always happy to talk more about anything vaguely history of science or Cold War related.

The course was well–structured and the readings were interesting. There was a reasonable workload of weekly readings and discussion posts, which made me feel prepared for class discussion without feeling over–burdened. The class discussions leaned on the variety of expertise in the room from everyone’s different backgrounds, and this made the conversations really insightful.

I left each class more curious than I was before, in a way that I have not experienced in other courses. That was a genuine highlight of my semester. This is one of the best courses I’ve ever taken at Harvard, and I would highly recommend it!!!
This is a fantastic course, and Professor Wilson is one of the best in the History of Science department. Even if you don't think you're interested in Cold War science, chances are that there will be something that catches your eye over the course of the semester. I genuinely didn't realize how much the Cold War impacted many scientific narratives I had previously learned about within different frameworks.
If you have any interest, however remote, in the Cold War, take this course.
