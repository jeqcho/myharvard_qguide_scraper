If you take this class seriously it will leave you with a great understanding of the immune system. Because of how content heavy it is, it requires you to do a lot of work on your own, a lot of which for me piled up just before the exams. Prior to taking this course, I looked at the immune system as one big complicated black box but now I understand its complexity much better and how everything works together. It is truly remarkable.
169 is a great course to take as a "from the ground up" building of your immunology knowledge. The lectures are interesting and there are some guest speakers with current topics in cancer, HIV, etc. research. The workload is fairly light, but the more you put into reading the textbook or discussion papers, the more you get out of it.
A great introduction to immunology. The class covers a broad range of topics, exploring many aspects of the field.
it's a great start for learning immunology :)
It's a good course covering several important but also interesting concepts in immunology. The workload is light as there is no weekly psets (only 3 in a semester) and just reading a paper every week or so for paper presentation in section (you only have to present once).
I think that this is a super interesting course, providing a great introduction to immunology. I really enjoyed the course content and lectures. I also loved the HMX course offered (make sure to get started on that early)!.
You learn a lot, but you have to stay on top of the material and it's definitely one of the more conceptually challenging classes in regards to memorization of a lot of content. Shiv is great, but his lectures are sometimes hard to understand with a lot of tangents so you end up doing most of your learning in section. The midterm is one question about one topic, so it can be challenging if you haven't studied everything thoroughly. The final also felt high stakes given that it's one of minimal graded assignments throughout the semester.
Your mileage may vary with your TF. Discussion sections are so important for this class because this is where you review content from the lecture and clear any confusion you may have. This will depend on your TF and how they choose to use section time since there is also a paper discussion component to sections. The grading scheme is very generous and a majority of your grade will come from participation during section which makes the rest of the course a bit less stressful. Shiv can be a great lecturer depending on your learning style as he doesn't have very many slides and usually draws things on the board. As a result, this class might be difficult to catch up in if you miss too many lectures. Overall, this course is good for learning your basic immunology as you will find a lot of what you learn in class crossing over into papers you read/your research.
Great class. Shiv is an awesome professor and his lectures are engaging. Workload is not bad at all, but you get more out of the class the more effort you put in. Would strongly recommend attending lecture, and if you have time reading the book.
the TA's have interesting grading strategies
Definitely take this class. Shiv is super kind and the teaching staff are super supportive. The lecture was sometimes a bit confusing to follow, but section cleared everything up. The textbook goes into far more detail than what's necessary. The in–class psets were a bit funky and were super short along with the midterm so if you got a question wrong, there was nothing really you could do. However, most of the grade is the final and participation, so you learn a lot in a fairly low stress environment.
If you are interested or want to learn more about immunology, this is the right choice! It provides a solid introduction to the field with one of the best professors. Each class presents a new immunological concept, covers its historical background, and ends with a creative summary in verse. Shiv creates an interactive environment, frequently pausing to engage students and encourages participation. Additionally, the workload is manageable (3 PSETs, one midterm, and one final) allowing students to enjoy the course without the pressure of excessive assignments.
I would highly recommend this class to all students who are curious to learn about the diverse facets of immunology. Any student interested in medicine or the biological sciences would definitely benefit from this class.
Take this class! And go to lecture, Prof Pillai gives the most awesome immunology poems at the end of every lecture.
This class will not demand much from you over the semester (PSETs in class open note, 20 minute midterm, online HMX course and easy and helpful and took about 1 hour per week for me). However, do not let that fool you into slacking off: I took pretty good notes and attended every lecture and still had a lot of studying to do for the midterm and final. Do not underestimate how much memorization you will have to do. I think immunology is a super interesting field and this class provides a great overview. The lectures were not the most helpful to me specifically because they often lack slides and rely on vague drawings – so if you get distracted at all, it is hard to catch up. There are no study guides or anything to review topics you are expected to know.
Great class! Pay attention in class, and review material over the semester (don’t try to cram) because a lot of it is memory / recall. The lecturers are great, except the cancer immunology lecturer could be quite confusing at times.
MCB 169 is a pretty cool class, and definitely something worth taking if you like to learn more about immunology. Professor Pillai gives quite fun lectures, although you have to be very attentive at all times to grasp the content. I love the end–of–class poems! Please go to section (they are mandatory anyways) – the wonderful TF team (shoutout to TF Jaclyn Walsh!!) are so supportive and do a great job at explaining the content and what we need to understand from lectures. Don't forget to do HMX, they really help with content review and are explained in a very concise and straightforward manner.
This is an extremely difficult course to take as an undergraduate as most of the people are grad students or PhD students interested in specific specialties. The grading will kick your but they demand specifics on everything and you will lose tons of points no matter what. You cannot just know the concepts you have to know the nitty gritty details. The terminology is extremely hard to understand if you have no knowledge of immunology and are completely new to these molecules. However, it does get better during the semester and you will get used to it. There is an absurd amount of memorization of specific molecules and process as well. You will have to learn a lot and be very self–motivated because there is barely any work but you need to take the initiative to go over lectures and reading the textbook or watching HMX videos. The HMX videos were the best part of the class as it really made understanding the material much easier and simpler. Overall the teaching staff is incredibly kind and great people overall always willing to help so that was reassuring. Shiv and Jackie were great people with great advice and insights.
Good class if you are into immunology.

Additionally, the inclusion of review sessions and discussions is incredibly helpful for reinforcing the material and ensuring a deeper understanding of the content. The course is a rewarding learning experience.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Although the information is ver complex, I believe it is worth it! The staff will do everything to help you succeed and the sections were a life safer. The content was so interesting and I learned so much more about the immune system.
This is a course on comprehensive immune topics and provide great basic knowledge for students interested in conducting immunology research.
