What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a great class to take. Afro–China is a topic consistently misunderstood in the media and even in a lot of the scholarship. This is a course that will truly update your view on the topic. The course is a survey of Afro–China literature, and you really do get a great sense with critical and nuanced perspectives.
Dr. Tamburo is a great instructor. I'm not entirely sure if she will be here again, but if you ever encounter her, you should take a class with her or just chat. She is very well–versed in the topic of China and Afro–China, with a particular focus on real estate, urban space, construction, and infrastructure. She structures the classroom so that students can speak more without losing sight of what's important in the discussions. She makes herself available to students, even though she's incredibly busy with her own writing. I wish we had met more times to chat.
The book review is nothing complicated and straightforward. Dr. Tamburo guides you through how to write a proper book review. The final paper is a little tricky, since it's an anthropological paper that you need to write without going to the field, quite counter–intuitive at times because you'd usually make arguments based on ethnographic evidence. For people who are using this paper as a pre–fieldwork paper, it might be a bit of a struggle if the topic of your research is not a heavily researched topic. I'd have really appreciated it if we could have the choice between a precis+lit review and an argumentative paper. All being said, Dr. Tamburo is avail herself to chat about the paper – you should definitely go chat with her if you're feeling confused or stuck about it. She is a great source of help.
I hesitate to recommend this to undergrads unless it will directly relate to a requirement/thesis. Tamburo is such a passionate professor and very clearly values the topics we are discussing. Her selection of materials and ensuring that every individual says something during the seminar is incredibly inclusive and valuable. As one of two undergrads in this class; however, I would hesitate to recommend it, given that it's dominated by grad students who often talk about theories, speak with jargon, and abstract a lot of the concepts we are talking about. One concern I had was the way in which discussion was directed. I definitely felt as though it would derail pretty often and end up in a completely different space/intention than the beginning of seminar.
TAKE THIS CLASS!
Take this class! I learned a lot about anthropology as well as the subject matter of China in Africa. Dr. Tamburo runs a fantastic discussion and clearly thinks deeply about how best to present the topics discussed.
