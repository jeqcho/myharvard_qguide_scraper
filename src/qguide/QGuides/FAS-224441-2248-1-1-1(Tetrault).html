TAKE THIS COURSE!! Pascale is an incredible lecturer who makes certain that every student understands the coding and circuitry concepts, which is very important as the course progresses and builds on itself. Pascale also sets aside many office hours to work through any questions together and is very dedicated to supporting student progress. You don't need a background in coding / circuitry / technology. Everything will be taught step by step, and all class resources are made available outside of class so you can keep working through coding exercises and reference concepts later on. The assignments are fun and set you up very well for the final project, where you have freedom to incorporate digital interactivity / technology however you'd like. Pascale gives very extensive feedback throughout and overall, is one of the most supportive and committed lecturers I have ever had. If this course is offered again, take it! You will learn so much in a small scale environment that allows for exploration.
Taking risks is as encouraged as it gets in this class. Like most AFVS studio courses, a lot of work is going to be required leading up to open studios and always budget enough time for that towards the end of the semester. Otherwise, you really have so much creative freedom which is what I enjoy most about this class.
Take this course! This is easily one of the best courses I have taken while in college. Pascale is a FANTASTIC teacher and an incredibly kind, supportive person who really wants to see you succeed and achieve your own goals. She was organized, provided thorough feedback, and made herself available to help.

This class teaches you real skills that can be incorporated into many different spheres. It was really empowering to learn things that I can actually use (in my art practice and regular life) and to build things with my own hands. Pascale did an amazing job of demystifying tools and methods that can seem inaccessible and intimidating.

The assignments are structured enough to give you an idea of what is expected, but open enough to allow you plenty of artistic freedom. The assignments could take me a long time, but I often didn't even notice because they were so much fun to work on. The final project can be demanding, but was really enjoyable and rewarding!

I appreciated that this class is accessible to anyone (coding experience in the class ranged from none to very experienced/skilled). It was great that we had the flexibility to push ourselves, and that Pascale seemed able to understand how much to push each person based on their previous experience.
remember this is an art class where assignments take into account the art itself; be ready to put in time for this, especially the final project – many project proposals are very lofty and you have to be ready to tone it down but also make it work and have an artistic vision in mind – this was a rewarding class and learned a lot, honestly liked it a lot even though it took several all nighters towards the end
This course changed the way I understand and appreciate art. Pascale is the kindest and most patient instructor I've ever had. This course beautifully encourages a new perspective on the digital world and the future. The assignments were manageable and fun, and I never felt left behind in the class.
I think this course is definitely beginner friendly. I would say it’s a 8/10. I love it for the support and the expertise of the professor and also the environment the students created. Although we are not close, being able to listen to their ideas is very mind–blowing and I personally feel that they are very open–minded artists that I would love to keep in contact in the future! The materials are more than sufficient for you to freely experiment with the capacities this course can give you. This class is definitely not a gem considering that I still have low grades from it but I really love its contents I cannot say otherwise. Besides, I still feel that the class becomes boring if you don’t really care about electronics/arts that much but I enjoyed what I accomplished through it so…!
