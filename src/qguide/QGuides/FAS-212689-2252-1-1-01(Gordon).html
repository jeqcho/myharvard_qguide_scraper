This course is awesome and I'd recommend to anyone interested in the ideas at all – really regardless of where you stand politically. Peter Gordon has fantastic lectures. TFs are lively and engaged. Essays will help you improve as a thinker. Really couldn't think of a reason *not* to take this course. No better way to tackle Hegel than something like this!
Great lectures. Definitely pay attention to the lecture handouts, they are super useful.
Amazing course: Professor Gordon takes challenging topics and explains them in an amazingly simple way!
This course is highly insightful, and the lectures are fantastic. Would strongly recommend to anyone interested in these thinkers or continental philosophy more generally.
Take this class if you are an undergraduate! I think it gives you a good grounding on two seminal thinkers in modern European social theory. For graduate students, take this class with a friend with whom you can regularly discuss the reading. There is rather little attention given to graduate students, but if you come in with an awareness that this is likely to be the experience, it can be very rewarding.
This is an absolute must–take at Harvard. Every lecture had me reeling, and the students in this class are some of the most intelligent, engaged, and sharpest people I've met at Harvard. Reading Hegel and Marx is something worth doing at least once in your life, and Professor Gordon's lectures were incredible — he is so articulate and can break down even the most complex concepts in a digestable way. Every lecture builds on the last, so there is a flow to things, and it felt like the class never lost momentum. I was just as excited about it from the first lecture as I was at the last lecture. This was one of the more challenging classes I've taken as a humanities concentrator, mostly because I had no social theory background going in. But the readings are doable, and if you put in the work, it's so incredibly worth it.
This is a truly fantastic course! It really increased my appreciation towards the more “continental” kind of philosophy and combined philosophical issues with historical ones in a very successful way.
Peter Gordon is insane. This class was really incredible and the philosophy was genuinely transformative –– Hegel maybe even moreso than Marx, to my surprise. Professor Gordon's lectures tell a really clear and deep story, tracking complex throughlines from Hegel to Marx with lots of textual analysis. That being said, he also provides a lot of his own interpretation within his teaching (as you would have to do to create the sort of narrative arc he does), so I would really strongly recommend doing the readings on your own and coming to your own interpretation before listening to Professor Gordon give his. They do hold you to a really high standard in grading for the course, but that should never be a reason not to engage with transformative philosophy.
Fantastic class! I highly recommend this class if you are interested in getting an understadning of philosophy and history and having an approachable way to read difficult texts like Hegel and Marx. Great teaching team, especially Professor Gordon's lecture which really clarify readings!
Really great lectures. Hegel and Marx are such foundational thinkers and this course is an excellent way to get a handle on their works and prepares you well for understanding their downstream influences.
If you are in the Social Sciences/Humanities – You NEED to read Hegel and you SHOULD read Marx.

Prof. Gordon's knowledge is unmatched, he is a human encyclopedia.

Reading assignments are super manageable. Hegel can be difficult at times but lecture clears up each reading before going into section.

Only three essays that were graded SUPER fairly.

Section with Kellyn was unmatched – The best section leader that I have every had at Harvard.
Professor Gordon is a phenomenal(ogy of spirit) lecturer and makes even the densest sections of the Philosophy of Right come alive. It's also an intellectually serious course, though, which in practice partially means that you have to spend a lot of time wading through boring stuff like sense–certainty and Marxist economics so that you can enjoy fun stuff like Hegel's philosophy of history and Marx's theodicy. I enjoyed hearing about different readers of Hegel and Marx and about the generous ways to read older thinkers who aren't straight–up analytic philosophers, and also about a thinker's responsibility to they way their ideas get used. I will say that, especially in the beginning sections of this course, I felt like we were just throwing around these big opaque concepts like "dialectic" and breaking them down into component parts without really making clear what they were. Like I still don't see how the three paradigms illuminate what the dialectic really is. Also, as many people have commented, you either are taking this course for Hegel or you're taking it for Marx. A fun game is guessing which of your classmates fall into which bucket.
Take this class!
This class is super interesting! If you already know a fair amount about Hegel or Marx, this might feel repetitive/redundant at times. As someone who is relatively familiar with Marx's writings, I began to lose interest around then, but the lectures on Hegel were incredibly helpful towards understanding his theories and place in the academic canon.
Lecture and readings are awesome. And so so helpful going forward. As a social studies concentrator, actually reading Hegel (spending more than just the week we had in 10A) really allows you to appreciate Beauvoir, Fanon, etc. And ofc Marx is great. I think my writing improved, too! Your section experience is really going to depend on who your section leader is.
Be prepared to do a lot of reading.
