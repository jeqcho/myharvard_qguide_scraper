This class is more intensive than it may initially seem. There are several written assignments which can be very time–consuming, and the grading was often strict and unclear in our section. The students in this class really made the discussions lively and beneficial, and yes, you'll definitely gain practice with the process of writing psychology research papers. Take this to fulfill a concentration requirement, but I wouldn't necessarily recommend it as an elective.

I would caution future students to be prepared for a challenging experience—not necessarily because of the academic rigor, but because of the lack of support and clarity throughout the course. The teaching fellow was often dismissive and unapproachable, feedback was minimal and harsh, and grading felt inconsistent and unfair. Rubrics were unclear, and readings were rarely discussed, which made it difficult to fully engage with the material or understand expectations. If you take this class, be proactive in seeking out your own learning resources and don’t be discouraged if your ideas are not immediately validated. Most importantly, trust in your own academic voice and don’t let one negative experience define your confidence as a student.
Do not take this course. It made me want to not concentrate in CNEP.

First, the instructor is very hostile. She would constantly target one student and would argue with a student in front of the rest of the class until she got it her way. She also would refuse to change her very unfair grading, never communicate assignment expectations beforehand, was very unapproachable, etc. She used AI to generate feedback on assignments and made it very difficult to ask for help. The class discussions were fun because of the other students. However, she would cut off discussions and not let students ask questions whenever she felt like it. However, she would waste time on things that were so minute just because she didn't want to "lose the argument."

Second, the grading is incredibly harsh. We had rubrics for assignments, but they were so broad. She also refused to give us an annotated rubric that marked where we got points taken off, which made it so difficult to know what to improve on. We also never received grades for the majority of the assignments we submitted, so you will never know where you stand grade–wise as she never returned anything. The feedback on assignments was basically the bare minimum, and for the final essay, she gave us our feedback a couple days before the deadline. When we had asked for our feedback on our drafts before it was due, she said, "I don't know if I can get it back to you before the final draft deadline, but I guess I can try. I will let you know," which is just ridiculous.

Third, you will never feel that you are improving because (1) you don't get grades back on 75% of your work, (2) you barely get any feedback, (3) her grading (if we do get it back) is unnecessarily harsh, (4) she barely tells you what you are doing wrong, (4) she plays favorites, etc. The instructor truly does not care about the students in the course, and you can tell.

All in all, I dreaded coming to this class and it made me rethink my concentration. I realize that it is all due to the instructor and not the subject itself, so I stuck with it. But you shouldn't have to feel this way so please, for your mental health and GPA, do not take this course. The instructor will make you feel stupid, attempt to embarrass you in front of your peers, make you cry over the lack of feedback, etc. Run away.

If you really want to be a psych concentrator, take 971 and not 975. I heard it's nice over there because the grading is reasonable, you actually learn, and the instructors don't make you feel like dropping out.
