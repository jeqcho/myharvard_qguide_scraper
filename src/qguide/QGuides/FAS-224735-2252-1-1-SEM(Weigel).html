This is a really unique and rewarding class that brings together film, media, and technology in a way that feels fresh and relevant. You get to look at global cinema alongside current issues like AI and digital infrastructure, which makes for a super interesting mix. The instructor is incredibly thoughtful and engaged, and the way the course is structured makes the material feel both challenging and approachable. The workload is very manageable, and the assignments give you room to think creatively and critically. If you're curious about how media works across borders or how it connects to bigger global systems, this is a great class to take.
The professor is awesome, very chill.
Take this course! Professor Weigel is an amazing professor, and she teaches the subject in a way that makes it easy for anyone to understand and learn. The class is very reading heavy, so make sure to allot time each week for that. Assignments aren't super time consuming, and I loved the discussion based class.
Please take this class with you are interested in media at all! Professor Weigel is kind and happy to share the field with students.
*You must take this course* if you are interested in anything humanities or social sciences in general. This course is extremely well structured, guiding you step by step without being didactic. In the end, you find yourself gaining a holistic knowledge about global media in general, and hopefully, your particular focus as well. It's not a difficult class if you just listen in class and at least read most of the material. There really aren't that many things to read, and it's not difficult at all. If you want the full experience, you should just read everything.
The person who makes all these great learning experiences possible, Professor Weigel, is a fantastic professor and, all in all, just a wonderful person. She is very knowledgeable about what she is teaching and beyond. She is able to weave everyone's comments together to produce a very generative conversation. She is great at facilitating discussions using concrete questions and ad–lib comments that are somehow just as insightful. On top of that, she runs her class with a great structure/scaffold without being rigid at all. She provides a recapitulation of the previous classes, and these summaries help everyone to wake up and weave things together. She is overall a very organized person – her course website is so easy to navigate. She makes herself available to students despite her own responsibilities – I don't know how she does it!
Go to her office hours: she will surprise you with a constellation of knowledge, and at the same time, remain humble and never pushy. She really wants you to explore your own interests, and she's there to provide detailed, concrete, and theoretical feedback wherever you need, without trying to enforce her opinions on you. She is also very good with both humanities research and social science research!
Take this class! The readings are very chill but super interesting, Moira is an amazing lecturer, and the assessments are not stressful, but also make you think deeply about the course material. In an age where media technologies are becoming increasingly prominent and complex and society has become more global and connected than ever, I really appreciated the opportunity to take a non–STEM class that synthesized this information from a humanist perspective and applied it to my daily life. I don't think I will approach things like Amazon Stores, AI Chatbots, or new forms of social media the same after this course.
Great class, really interesting movies and readings which fit well with the theoretical components, great course staff and lots of contemporary social science readings
