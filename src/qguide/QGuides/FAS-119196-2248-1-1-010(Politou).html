Class basic overview:
Three 1hr 15min small classes per week (10–15 people, mandatory attendance), three PSETs per week (2–3 hrs per PSET), one short section per week, two midterm exams, one final exam.

My background:
Took BC in 11th grade, a high school multivariable course in 12th grade fall (didn't pay attention at all), and a linear algebra course in 12th grade spring. Took the course as a college freshman.

My recommendation:
Take this class if you are willing to put in 2–3 hrs of math every other day outside of class and are comfortable evaluating single variable integrals in word problems. Among my peers, those who paid attention during class and did their work throughout the term had a relatively easy time on the final. Those 2–3 hrs on PSETs are not spent trying to solve the problems, but rather transcribing your work for submission (for example, a lot of 3D graphing that takes a while).

If you have any previous multivariable calculus experience, this course will likely be easy/medium difficulty for you. I remembered nothing concrete from my high school multi class, but concepts quickly came back to me as I went through this course. Having vector experience also makes a fifth of it trivially simple (because they teach basic vector concepts in this class), but it is not necessary to do well. I would say that Math 21A is an easier multivariable calculus class than standard ones you find online.
Take 22b. 3 psets a week sucks and you I don't feel as though I have learned multivariable very rigorously (although this is partially my fault for choosing 21a over 22b for scheduling issues).
I think this is a solid and enjoyable math class, and a great way to learn multivariable calc which has so many interesting applications! The 3 psets a week might sound like a lot, but they are shorter than psets for other classes and very reasonable based on what we do in class. Also, there is so much support in this class if you ever have a question about homework or class content or just want to check your answers, so you can do it! Go to office hours MQC, especially MQC!
This was the first math class I've taken in 3–4 years, and I was really nervous going into it that I'd fail miserably. I've never been that comfortable with math. However, I think reading the course evals mentally prepared me for what was to come, and I tried to overprepare in the beginning to control my success as much as possible. For example, I did the Review2Preview module before the class started and wrote down all my questions about things (even simple stuff like how limits/Reimann sums worked). That module helped prepare me a LOT. I'd read the textbook chapters and watch the Panopto videos before class (try to ALWAYS watch the videos!! they are so useful!). I also went to MQCs and office hours when I could, checking my work with other students. Office hours + MQCs gave me the confidence I needed to know I could do well in the class, as everyone was so encouraging.

The first unit was the hardest for me, for sure. After midterm 1, I developed a better gauge for what to expect out of the class. They emphasized that "struggling is learning" and active learning is your path to success in this class. Make group chats with other students, work on homework with them, use your resources. My best studying tools for midterms were: (1) review notes, (2) write down ANY questions you have and ask your TF after class or in MQC/office hours/workshops, (3) make Quizlets to memorize rules/theorems/equations, (4) do ALL of the practice exams and write down questions/flag problems you aren't sure of the solution for and bring those to TF/MQC/office hours. The practice exams are absolutely the key to doing well on the exam.

Finally, spread the Psets out over two days or "sessions." Doing it all at once can be overwhelming. If I can succeed in this class, I think pretty much anyone can, you just have to believe in your ability to do it.
