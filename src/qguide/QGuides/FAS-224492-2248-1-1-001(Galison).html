Professor Galison is amazing and incredibly knowledgeable. He’s supportive and also very chill about finishing assignments and readings. Take this class if you’re actually interested in black holes and scientific concepts, though it’s definitely a little lighter on the humanities side than it advertises so I would recommend with reservation for those interested in that. Doing the readings will really give you an awarding experience in class and learning the material. Also the structure is a little less dialogue from students and a lot of listening / watching films. Classes tended to be not very discussion oriented and could often feel long because it was a lot of lecturing without a lot of structure, but it’s much more interesting if you’re actually really curious about learning the material.
It’s a great class. I learned so much. Professor Galison is very generous with his time and resources, and if you want to learn about this stuff I’d take this class. If you don’t want to do the reading or really engage at all, that is very sad for a class that has only a few kids so maybe steer clear.
I LOVE Professor Galison! He is a wonderful lecturer, so smart, and genuinely wants the best for the students in the class. It was a great first–year seminar –– if you are genuinely already interested in the topic. Know that there is a big emphasis on BOTH the history and science aspect of Einstein, but I have no regrets with taking this class! I learned so much and enjoyed all the conversations with the people in the course. You do actually have to do the readings in order to participate in the conversations, but if you miss a week, it's okay. We also got to see really random, cool experiments and scientific items. Galison is so smart!
Professor Galison is simply a genius, and being able to spend time learning from a genius about exactly what excites them most is a rare experience. Take the class! The paper is a bit long, but not too bad as it's spread out over the course of the semester.
I would recommend it to everyone and encourage them to work closely to the professor because he is very helpful.
If you put in effort week in week out, you will surely learn a lot
Take this course if you have an interest in physics, even if you have absolutely no experience with physics. There is a lot offered here, and there is something for everyone, not just STEM majors. Half of the class was non–stem. Professor Galison is also fantastic. He is very insightful and you will enjoy some of his stories and lectures. I will say that some classes felt like they dragged on a bit, but for the most part, there was never a dull moment during classes. Readings are pretty light and fun to go through, and you'll even get to watch some movies for some assignments. Also, there is a final paper, but it's very much a low–stress assignment that you have basically the entire semester to work on.
Prof Galison is amazing! Highly recommend
