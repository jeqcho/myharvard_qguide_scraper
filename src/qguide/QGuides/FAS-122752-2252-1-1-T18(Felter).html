Prof. Felter is such a sweetheart –– please arrange office hours with him sooner rather than later, especially to discuss your final paper, and closely follow his recommendations/edits! I'd say he's a tough but fair grader, and he leaves lots of detailed feedback that explains exactly how you can/should improve. Especially for your final paper, directly address all of his feedback on your proposal, and you should be in good shape for your rough + final draft. A demanding but rewarding tutorial option, especially if you're interested in law!
If you have an interest in the law this is a perfect course for you. Prof. Felter is a retired attorney with countless years of experience, often recalling very interesting stories of his during appropriate times throughout the course. the written assignsments are all interesting as well, and Prof. Felter provides tons of valuable feedback on all of these assignments which provoke you to think deeper about the topics and strengthen your writing. The coding assignments can be tedious, but aren't too challneging.
PLEASE DO NOT TAKE THIS COURSE. I am warning you and trying to save you a semester of pain and suffering. Professor Felter and grading of this course was very subjective and frustrating. After meeting with the professor multiple times, working everyday in Lamont for long hours, getting support from additional staff members (Ec970 office hours, librarians, writing center, etc.), I received the same bad grade and extremely discouraging comments every single time. Myself and others felt we could never improve, no matter what we tried. Professor Felter chooses who he likes in the beginning of the year and sticks with it. Some people may have loved this class, but they were probably his two favorites, and the majority of the class holds the same opinion as myself. I also felt shut down at times as Professor Felter is not the easiest to talk to, and towards the end of semester was very booked for his office hours. Furthermore, the bar and workload of the class was way higher than any other Econ Tutorial that I know of. We had papers and PSETs due on the same day multiple times, and readings were extremely lengthy and boring. All our draft papers were letter graded as short essays, whereas I know other classes were graded as completion. Lastly, the class discussion was very mundane and you could tell no one in the class cared or was participating. As someone interested in law, this class actively disuaded me against it. I would not recommend this class to anyone. The amount of work is insurmountable, and I had nothing to show for it but a bad grade and comments that made me feel very poorly about my academic abilities. In the end, this course discouraged me immensely and was my worst class of the semester and professor I liked the least.
This class is a big time commitment with many long readings each class and frequent papers but sooo worth it and rewarding. Prof Felter is absolutely amazing and so committed to each of his students individually. He also knows about literally anything law–related, making this class an amazing intro to studying law. It's really cool to see how economic concepts can be applied to law, and this class really forces you to think in a new way that combines logical reasoning, economic intuition, and the way lawyers think.
Horrible class. Do not take it. This is coming from a student who wants to go to law school and maybe get an econ PhD.
Mr. Felter is a great instructor. Lots of readings but manageable. Good discussions in class. Provides good feedback on assignments and grading is fair. Like most classes at Harvard, you get what you put in—and with this class there is a lot you can get out of it (particularly if you haven't studied any law before).
