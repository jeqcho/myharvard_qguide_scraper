This is a great course to take, especially during an election year or if you are a gov concentrator. Taking a class taught by Mark Penn is also such a cool experience, being that he is so accomplished. TAKE!
The topic of the class is very interesting and Prof. Ansolabehere and Mark are very knowledgeable and fairly engaging. If you are interested in polling, public opinion, and political campaigns, it can be a great experience. The class can be chaotic at times but if you are not a very ‘structured’ student it’s perfectly fine. The professors were always welcoming discussion and happy to help.
Definitely take it if you have the chance. The lectures are all engaging and relevant and both lecturers are approachable and have a lot of expertise in their fields.
This course is an excellent choice for understanding how public opinion shapes politics and policy. Stay engaged with the readings and participate actively in discussions to get the most out of the class. Be prepared to think critically and analyze polling data—it’s challenging but highly rewarding!
Fantastic and eye opening course for an election year. Great insights, learned a lot about polling. However, structure was pretty nonexistent, kind of a go–with–the–flow class. Almost the entire grade is on the presentation and final paper (mostly) – other assignments do well to build up to that.
This course gets you out of the academic bubble. Mark Penn has some hot takes on polling that may not be the most beneficial for modern public opinion research, but it's good to hear them to see the ideas that have driven the polling industry for the past few decades. All in all, it's a fun class with great experience in fielding your own poll and conducting analysis on it. For good analysis, prepare to use R or Stata.
Best class at Harvard.
Enjoy the opportunity. TAKE IT. Genuinely I would take this course every semester if I could. Hoping I can take some of their other courses in the future!
This course will help you both learn about political polling and how political elections work overall. The two professors teaching this course are both incredible and knowledgeable in their respective sides of the election polling world, and do a great job of working together to teach a cohesive and engaging class. This course is incredibly interesting and engaging. Highly recommend!
I highly recommend this class, especially if you are interested in American politics and/or public opinion. The professors are extremely knowledgable and it is worth taking the class just to learn from them. The assignments are pretty relaxed, the main thing is the final policy memo you write. Overall, this is a great class!
The content of this course is interesting and you get to field a real–world poll and analyze the results, which is so cool. That said, don't take this class if you need structure because it is very lacking and you will not get much feedback over the course of the semester. The professors are knowledgeable and lectures were interesting.
TAKE THIS CLASS! Truly one of the most valuable classes I have taken at Harvard. Stephen and Mark are incredible, no one better to learn this topic from. You will truly enjoy your time spent in the class and working on assignments as long as you have any personal interest in the topic. Just take it!!!!!! (Best QRD option around*).
Take it! Especially in an election year, it allows you to be tapped in and truly understand how the American people believe the issues of our time.
This class is really interesting but totally unorganized. There are no clear metrics for grading and no feedback on assignments so you will not have much indication what your grade will be. Even assignment deadlines can be confusing –– you need to go to class just to make sure you don't miss any changes in terms of assignment due dates or instructions since nothing is on the canvas site. The content is really interesting and both Profs are really engaging and fun to talk to, but just be aware that if you need structure, this may not be the class for you.
Taking this class is such a privilege. Steve and Mark are wonderful lecturers who are so open to sharing their knowledge with their students, and they have so much to share. They're experts in polling, surveys, and understanding public opinion, so seeing them twice a week on the brink of a presidential election was truly a treat. I highly recommend this course to anyone interested in American politics, even if you haven't taken a gov class before. It's a very accessible course to anyone and there is so much support provided for students throughout the course.
I really learned a lot about polling, and the political climate of the U.S. The assignments were very informative, and this class is great for anyone who wants to go into politics or government. It is a very lively, discursive environment with professors eager to help students.
If you can get past the lack of organization/feedback, it's a pretty chill and interesting class. You get to field your own survey questions and analyze them!
