Background: {Math 22A, GenEd, Expos 20, Language}, {Math 22B, FYSeminar, Language, CS51} {STAT 110, COMPSCI 1090A, GenEd, COMPSCI 1200}

Semester schedule: Sophomore spring – {COMPSCI 1240, COMPSCI 1810, Language, ES50}


Hours Breakdown:
There were 7 problem sets. Each took around 8 hours to complete, including typing it up in LaTeX. It’s the type of problem set that were a bit more puzzle oriented. You had to figure out some certain thing and then type it up in a somewhat formal way. I’ve never really gotten used to formal proofs. The level of formality was definitely undefined and I think I’d prefer a standardized level of formality, at least so that people know what’s expected and where to focus efforts on.

There were 3 programming sets. You could do this with a partner and you could do it solo. These also took around 8 to 9 hours


Grade Performance:
Problem sets and programming sets are out of points ranging between 90 and 110. For these, it’s important to just maintain a healthy margin above median. A little effort to make sure all parts of the question have been answered and you should be good. Getting a perfect score is definitely doable. I found the final psets on the more challenging end so if you spend time trying to understand them you’ll be more prepared for the final. Median for midterm was 33/75 and the median for the final was a 79/180.

Best part about the course:
A large portion of the course is interesting material. There is great support and sufficient office hours for the course which I appreciated. Madhu and Sitan are great lecturers who understand the content and they know how to answer the content. This course changed how I think, it taught me not to memorize things word for word, but it made sure I was able to apply different styles of thinking to different scenarios.



Worst part about the course:
Exams are quite difficult. It’s either you figure out the “puzzle” in a short amount of time, or you’re screwed. Apart from the 3 programming sets, not a lot of practical aspects have been incorporated into it. They should add more practical bits. Lectures were a bit of a slog sometimes, lecturers could have injected a bit more motivation/excitement for the content

TLDR:
It’s a hard class. Good support. Put your time and effort into it. Will be your hardest class of the semester. If you’re a CS major, just do it.

I'm writing this in the point of view of a non–CS, science major. This class is hard, but it is not impossible. Moving onto PSETs: these are so brutally difficult, and there's no good reason they should be. They can take up to 20 hours to complete. The only advice I have for these are to have a good friend group and have everyone go to office hours to get answers. The ProgSETs are actually not that bad. But because everyone goes to office hours to have the psets and progsets checked, basically everyone gets a 90+ (the median on the last few psets was literally a 100).

This means that exams will make (or break) your grade. I cannot understate just how crucial it is you are prepared for these, as in previous years the cutoff for an A has been around top 20% of the class. Do as much practice as possible for these, as if you look long enough you'll start to see the same flavors of problems. Also never leave a problem blank, just write something and write some runtime for every problem, since they are very generous with partial credit (I got half credit on one algorithm that I wrote two sentences of random stuff for).

The lectures are totally useless to your learning and are presented very poorly. I would just read the lecture notes or maybe go to section, but I just did the former and did fine.

Unfortunately, unlike other "hard" classes here, I don't think you can get an A in this class by putting in lots of effort; you really need just strong mathematical intuition and ingenuity that is hard to teach in one semester. That's to say: if you don't need to take this class, I would be very hesitant to take it. I definitely learned a lot in this class, and it certainly challenged me with novel ways of thinking, but I don't think the stressful days before the exams and long nights of PSETting made it worth it. Proceed with strong caution about adding this class to your schedule.
