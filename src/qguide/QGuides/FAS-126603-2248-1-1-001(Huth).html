Super chill course, Professor Huth is really passionate about the material so it’s fun to listen to him. The assignments are not very time consuming, but they’re not like any other class – I had to spend a day measuring the angle of the sun at 1 hour intervals. Makes you think about stuff that no other class does
This is a great class to satisfy the gened science requirement. The material is not something easily learned in another science class on campus (other than celestial navigation). Though lecture is sometimes boring, the assignments are fun and force you to get outside. Exams and assignments are easy to do well on. If you are interested in navigation at all, this class is a great choice too. It forces you to think about where you are in your surroundings. Would definitely recommend this class, even though it isn't mind blowing.
FANTASTIC course that everyone should take. Going into the class, I was surprised with how much new information I was learning. Navigation is an art and this class explores it fully. The assignments were so so informative, engaging, and fun! Lectures were super interesting and captivating. We got to do so much hands–on activity, which I adored. I cannot express how much I loved this course and I recommend it so much. I have developed a newfound love and appreciation for navigation and now try to stargaze nightly.
I loved this course!!! For your science gened or any elective or class, I would strongly recommend this one! I had a lot of fun and learned a lot, and the professor and TF are very passionate! The professor is incredibly passionate about everything we learned about in this course and his passion extends into the lectures and course content and any time you ask him a question. He truly loves the material and wants students to learn as much as they can about it. The expectations for assignments, exams, and final project is very clear with lectures, section, and the section handouts. A lot of the assignments usually required some sort of hands–on aspect which I loved since it often incorporated something that was not on a screen or could be translated into nature. I had a lot of fun taking this class and truly learned a lot!
Go and learn something you can use in real life!
I don't know if this course would be considered a gem but it is definitely one of the more interesting gened courses, especially if you are interested in the outdoors, backpacking, etc. It gives a basic understanding of wayfinding and pathfinding through tools that you can bring with you on your trips (i.e., compass and map). Many of the assignments use the skills you learned and then apply them to the outdoors so you can actually see your understanding of the topic come to fruition. The course derails sometimes into an environmental science and history course, but I personally enjoyed that, and it was fun to learn what Dr. Huth had to offer. Overall, Dr. Huth is a great professor, and his gened course was fun. Even if you have no interest in wayfinding, you will still come out of the class with useful skills.
Take it! It'll change who you are for the better and create an unforgettable experience. If you think you're a little interested in the subject matter, that'll burst into extreme interest just because it's such a great class.
Take this course with the knowledge that some assignments may take longer than others. There are two assignments that require you to work on them daily, which can be time consuming with other classes.
This gened is AMAZING, easily my favorite gened I've taken. Everything about this class is great. As a CS major who doesn't touch grass much, this course was a nice way for me to learn about nature and how the world works. We covered so much content, from how to navigate yourself if you're lost, to learning about the different star constellations, and finding longitude and latitude using the sun. Professor Huth is the kindest soul ever, he cares so much about the class and it's so nice hearing his anecdotes about everything. Val is also an amazing TF, she was so helpful with all the assignments and I really looked forward to her sections every week. Assignments were fun, and I enjoyed them because they were so different to my pets in other classes. The workload is a manageable, but you should really make sure to stay on top of things, especially the final project. All in all, if you're looking for a science gened, this is the one to take!
I really enjoyed this class and the lectures were fascinating for me. One nice thing about this course is that the minimum required work takes little time, so on busy weeks I could spend less time on the class, but for weeks where I was more free I could really interact with the assignments and have fun with the content.
Very interesting and rewarding class. Professor Huth is genuinely interested in your learning and has a lot of cool life experience to share.
One of the most unique classes I’ve taken at Harvard. Super helpful for developing life skills especially if you are interested in the outdoors. Definitely take this class!
