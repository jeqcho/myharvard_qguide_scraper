Do the pre–class assignment one day before the course to be consistently exposed to the content, which helps with long–term memory.
I really liked this course! I found the material challenging but not overly difficult. There are a lot of real–world applications for the course material. All of the problem sets are manageable and there is leninecy given on problem sets due the week we have Quizzes. Obviously, the course was not light work –– but it was super manageable. There is consistent work throughout the semester but it is paced really well. I would recommend this class to fulfill the DiffEq applied math requirement!
While I really enjoyed the material in this course, I would just say that if you are someone who tends to work slower or spend more time working through assignments on your own without OH, plan to allocate a lot of time for this course because assignments are dense. The material can get a bit jumbled as the semester goes on because its all related but terms with similar names have very different meanings so its important to just keep your notes and understandings organized! My biggest thing would just be that I spent more time on this course than any other that I've taken (psets, corrections, pre–class assignments, etc) so just beware that if you're schedule is busy at all, this class might be overwhelming.
This was such an enjoyable course. Not stressful, quite manageable with problem sets on the shorter side and lenient late policies, plus concepts were so cool and fun. The instructor Dr. Sarah Iams is fantastic.
It is a difficult but manageable course. Lots of work so be prepared but you get what you put in and learn a lot.
This class is one of the best applied math courses I have taken. The material is manageable, the lectures are interesting, and the in class work is fun. Lots of real world examples. Youll start seeing dynamical systems everywhere!
This course is quite fascinating in terms of content, but stay on top of the workload, especially near the end. Also, to the best of your ability, choose a project topic that is personally interesting to you, as you will put a lot of work into it.
This class is good and you should take it for your continuous requirement (if you're an AM concentrator). Sarah is fantastic and will work with you on any questions/misunderstandings you have in the course so long as you put in the time and effort––This class demands a fair amount of it, so I wouldn't load the schedule with too many more math classes beyond this one (especially if they keep it MWF), but overall I'd recommend you to take it and the grading and evaluation is very fair
This course is a lot of work. Meeting 3 times a week plus the flipped classroom means before class you have to watch an hour worth of videos so it feels like the class meets 5 times a week. Then after watching the videos you have quizzes on the videos, in addition to a problem set every week, multiple exams, and a final project. Taking AM105 is prob a better alternative. I don't like flipped classroom so I found this class pretty annoying but if you like flipped classroom you might like it. There are probably better ways to fulfill your AM requirements. I don't really recommend this course.
Excellent course. Make sure to stay on top of revisions, it's easy to slip out of one of the grade buckets by even one or two points on a quiz! The videos are quite helpful for every pre–class and help when doing the problems in class.
