I highly recommend this course to anyone interested in Pre–Columbian art. The lectures are engaging and insightful, and the professor's expertise truly enhances the learning experience. Be sure to keep up with the assigned readingsthey're incredibly helpful for understanding the material and are essential for writing strong papers.
Take this class: Professor Cummins is awesome!
The material for this class is actually kind of interesting. Lectures aren't really, but they are also on zoom and the recording is sent so you can watch them or not it won't really affect your performance I don't think. Your grade is 30% midterm exam (which is a take home essay), 30% final exam (which is also a take home essay), and 40% final paper. The midterm is done some time in november, and the final exam and final paper are due on the same week at the end of finals period. So, I would recommend starting the final paper (and maybe finishing the final paper) before the final exam prompt comes out just to make your finals week a little easier. Overall, the class is definitley a gem, and professor Cummins seems like a pretty chill guy in general. Would definitley recommend if somebody is looking for an easy 4 credits.
Take this class if you are interested in Pre–Columbian Art at all! Professor Cummins is an absolute sweetheart! He is so down to earth and extremely knowledgable about the subject. I felt excited to learn from him every week.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! Learning about pre–columbian art and how to understand it is so important for anyone who is interested in Latin America in any field, specially history (all of them), literature, and anthropology. Learning how to understand the material culture allows to to delve into these worlds that are so different from the western traditions. AND Prof. Cummins is just incredible. His classes are very entertaining and he is a walking encyclopedia.
