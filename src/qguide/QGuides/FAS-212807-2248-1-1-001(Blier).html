Take this class! It was so interesting and informative. Professor Blier did a great job bringing in speakers, and weaving together the story of Cambridge, all while serving as a resource to answer our questions. Get started on the assignments early, and ask questions. The class mainly concisted of weekly blog posts, a main final paper, and a requirement to attend a local cambridge meeting. My advice is to get started on the latter two earlier in the semester then I did! The class was one of the highlights of my week, and I'd really encourage you to take it!

The basis of this class is to be vague. That being said, the weekly assignments are okay, but the final projects are pretty up to you. I now know more about Cambridge, and it's a lot more social engagement–based than history–based.
REALLY INCREDIBLE COURSE!!! Please take this course if you get it. Seriously one of the most fun courses I've ever taken at Harvard. It's become a running joke with my friends that I always bring up something I learned in "History of Harvard Square," and honestly it's justified. We spend so much time walking around the square, and most of us never pause to think about the rich history under our feet. This course exposes you to the worlds and lives lived in our community over the past 400 years, and it's been really an eye–opening experience. You learn so much about the local politics of Cambridge as well. This isn't just a history course it's as much a current events course. This course has been top 5 of all time at Harvard (coming from someone who just finished junior fall).
TAKE IT!!!!! Professor Blier is kind, eloquent, hilarious, warm, and so knowledgeable / intellectually curious. You will consistently be amazed by just how much she knows and by the breadth of her research interests. Each class was a treat (especially the tour we did of the Square). She will inspire you to participate in local government, no matter where you go after graduation. I loved, too, the guest speakers every lecture that covered a wide swath of disciplines. And she had phenomenal suggestions for my final paper research topic; I had a great deal of fun learning and writing about my topic. Overall, I'm incredibly grateful and happy I decided to take it my senior fall. :)
