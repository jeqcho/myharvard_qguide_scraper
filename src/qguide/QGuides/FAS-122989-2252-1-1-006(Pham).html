What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Pretty good course, but you definitely have to do the readings to be engaged
This was my favorite class this semester! We essentially used the theme of dialogues as an excuse to dip our toes into a variety of subfields in philosophy. Moreover, Quyen Pham was one of the best philosophy section leaders I've ever had. She created a very friendly atmosphere, and I always looked forward to class.
this course will help you learn how to identify and reconstruct arguments. however, if you have already taken some philosophy courses (especially any advanced ones), this course probably won't add much to the skills you already developed in previous courses. the content can be interesting sometimes, but it's not ideal if you're looking for more ethics–oriented or highly applicable philosophical ideas – sometimes it gets kind of abstract and you might wonder what's the point of discussing that. with that said, Philosophy Through Dialogue goes through philosophers of different epochs and that are quite foundational to continue studying philosophy if you're concentrating in it (basically, helped you get broader foundations in philosophy).
in terms of assignments, they're doable and not a ton of effort, but can be a little annoying doing 5 response papers + final paper, especially because it's all the same: identify argument, reconstruct argument.
TheThe course was a mixed bag.
Readings were mostly good, although some weeks hundreds of pages of 18th Century deist metaphysics would be assigned, stuff that no one in their right mind would find particularly interesting.
Students were all uninterested. In a discussion class like this, that is a big problem. This is, I think, because the class had no topic of discussion, simply moving between topics each week. The vagaries of the class make it hard to learn very much about the topics, let alone to build up opinions and attitudes about the material.
