This is a solid course to take to complete your math requirement at Harvard. If math does not come easy to you, I would recommend spending time in class or right after to right out a clear method of how to approach the problems. MQCs are great! Use them! The CAs will check your answers. The three psets a week is intense, but it means you solidify your knowledge constantly.
This is not an easy course, but somehow everything makes sense at the end. Take advantage of working on the worksheets together during class and the multitude of office hours they offer. Consistently review the content throughout the semester, since it will all build up to the end.
this was a really enjoyable class in that the content was interesting to learn about. AI assignments were the worst and we should advocate against them. you do not at ALL need 1b or 1a to take this course, just know how to derive and integrate literally the basics. if you are a first year just go right into 21a for your requirements.
I would tell them not to be afraid to participate or ask questions in class. That is was section is all about. Also use the mqc to your advantage.
If you take this course, go to MQC and Office Hours – even if you understand the content. These are great resources to discuss broader course concepts and to get exam advice from CAs and TFs. This is a difficult course, but it is completely manageable. The 3 PSets a week are difficult (especially if you are taking other PSet classes), but they are good prep for exams. Put forth your best effort, and you will do well in this course.
