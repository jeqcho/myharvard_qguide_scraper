I put this class as my first choice and do not regret it at all. Dr. Quigley is an amazing instructor, sections are always interesting, and everyone in the class was super nice. I really appreciate that Dr. Quigley takes a more STEM–like approach to writing, which made the writing process way more enjoyable as a STEM concentrator. I feel that my writing has definitely improved since the beginning of the semester. She's also such a great person and super transparent and funny. Would definitely recommend this Expos section.
This is an AMAZING CLASS. Dr. Quigley skillfully facilitates class discussions to all perspectives. She's truly amazing. I've learned so much about mental health and its intersection with gender. The readings were very manageable and the various response papers were useful for the essay writing process. Barely any "homework". The only qualm that I have with this class is that her grading is a bit ambiguous. This is moreso a problem with the EXPOS curriculum as a whole, but this is one of those "there's no such thing as a perfect paper" classes so take that as you will.
Dr. Q is great, assignments are manageable, feedback is great. Great intro to a very intersectional topic
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! This is one of the best classes to discuss core theories as they apply to contemporary debates on gender and mental health and personal stories. You will learn to question what it means to be sick, what it means to receive a diagnosis, and what it means to develop a disorder.
I really loved Gender and Mental Health. While it's likely not being offered in this fall, take this course if you have the opportunity. Dr. Quigley is incredibly kind and down to earth and wants to see you succeed. She provides excellent feedback and she is there to help you every step of the way. She was always a pleasure to talk to and made fulfilling the Expos requirement nothing less than a great experience. Make sure to go to office hours not to set you up for a good grade on an essay, but to help you build your skills as a writer because you'll derive a lot of satisfaction from doing so.
I am not a psych major or anything related to psych. If you're also not into psych maybe you want to take another expos, as the gender component is not as heavy as I thought. Professor Quigley is quite demanding, and it might feel frustrating to see other peers getting better grades in other expos sections, but honestly and comparing myself with my friends, I think I learned more on how to write academic papers. Professor Quigley is really knowledgeable and she is always there to help you.
