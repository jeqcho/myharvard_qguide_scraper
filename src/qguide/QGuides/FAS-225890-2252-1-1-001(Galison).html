Comments
Prof. Galison follows his informative syllabus, so check that. Be prepared to read 1–2 longer books each week. As a seminar leader, he is very relaxed. He is an amazing scholar with deep knowledge across physics, history and philosophy of science, film, etc. We watched a lot of films in class and it was great getting to see his thoughts as a film maker. One of the best parts of this seminar was the mix of students: grads and undergrads, scientists and humanists, visiting students from MIT, Princeton, and China.
Great course! Highly recommend! Very interesting content and very manageable workload. Don't worry about how long the readings are, most of us don't get to all of the readings every single week, but sometimes we just do a good chunk of the reading enough to engage in solid discussions with the group. Start the collaborative essay early.
Galison is a fantastic professor and wonderful to learn from. Genuinely a wonderful class that taught me a lot about the scientific process, human collaborations, and the meaning of what we do in science. Plus fantastic documentaries!
This class brought together students from different disciplines but to me proved that interdisciplinarity is not necessarily an end in itself. Even though a lot of the texts were densely theoretical, many in the class had never read things of the sort in the past, so there was a limit to the common language we had during class discussions, which I wish had engaged more deeply with the material. The group paper was 50% of the course grade, in the spirit of collaboration, but could be quite frustrating at times due to aforementioned problems with common language and experience with researching and writing papers adjacent to the history of science and the kind of original work that was expected.

Still, Prof. Galison put together a very interesting syllabus and navigated as deftly as he could the various disciplinary backgrounds present in the class. I learned a lot from people who studied everything from literature to biology to physics that I certainly wouldn't have learned if not this course and the conversations it facilitated. Galison is also just such an interesting scholar, and this class is a great excuse to read some of his work and get to talk to him about it.
This was my favorite course of the semester, simply because the professor is excellent. Galison is so knowledgeable and remarkably available given his super busy schedule. Such a great opportunity to learn from him and my classmates.
This is a really interesting course with an incredibly knowledgeable professor, Peter Galison. Highly recommend if you're interested in the subject! Readings can be a lot at times (sometimes more than 1 book per week). Discussions are fun and interesting though.
