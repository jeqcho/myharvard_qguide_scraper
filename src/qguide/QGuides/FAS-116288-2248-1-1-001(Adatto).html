What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
if you're a social studies concentrator interested in systems of power, culture, art, or how any of those concepts affect mundane life, take this course! I can't recommend it enough. Kiku fosters a wonderful discussion, and the readings are super applicable to anything you'll do in your academic career moving forward (many of the people in the grade above me said that their analytical frameworks for their theses came from this course, and I couldn't agree more). One note is definitely make sure to do the readings: Kiku does sometimes cold call.
Take this class! Professor Adatto is fantastic, and if you're even remotely interested in Art, Culture, or Politics, this is the class for you!
I would tell future students that if they are really interested in learning about the intersection of art, politics, and civic life they should either find a specific tutorial that can teach them about their theory interests and perhaps an HAA–listed course to better understand their art interests or they should look for another course, outside of the tutorial framework, to interrogate that intersection and take a tutorial that might teach them an element of theory that they otherwise wouldn't know. The critical framework is not particularly robust, but if they are interested in thinking through popular culture and media in the United States, its criticism, its history, and acquiring a theoretical framework for doing so across a small group discussion seminar then they'll enjoy this class. The professor is quite accessible and loves discussing with students their work and providing input.
This was my favorite seminar I've taken so far at Harvard! Discussions were great, I learned so much, and Professor Adatto is a wonderful teacher and resource. Highly recommend
I would tell future students to 100% take this course. If you are remotely interested in the topics of the course it is definitely worth while to take. It will transform the way you think about and interact with art. Further, I found every week of discussion incredibly intriguing and the time always flew by!
