What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is really interesting if you have an interest in US foreign policy strategy, specifically oriented towards Russia (or historically the USSR) or China, beyond that there isn't too much other regional focus, although there is room to find that in your final essay. You also have to be ready to be super self–driven, as all feedback on assignments or participation is given if you go to office hours only, and that is also how you get feedback on preparation for the final essay. The class is very discussion based, although the standards for that are somewhat opaque. Be ready to participate and prep highly for your in–class presentations. Like other seminars, this class really requires you to do a lot of the heavy lifting from the materials, and a background in US–oriented IR and IR theory (as opposed to foreign region–IR classes) is super helpful, otherwise you very easily can feel out of your depth.
Take this class!!! Professor Imparato is a wonderful professor and does a wonderful job of introducing different theoretical perspectives on foreign policy as well as creating an exciting and dynamic course on grand strategy. I learned so much in this course and it was not difficult to keep up with the readings and relevant coursework!!
This class goes over one of the most interesting topics, at least to me, in the world. In addition, it is taught by one of the greatest and kindest professors Harvard has to offer. If you're willing to put in the work for the readings, you'll really learn a lot from this class and engage with American policy very differently than when you started.
Dr. Imparato is an incredible professor. Not only does he take a keen interest in the lives of his students inside and outside of the classroom, but he operates with a compassion in his teaching that would help anyone feel like they could learn. This course covers a fascinating array of topics within American foreign policy, and Dr. Imparato presents them all eloquently.
Gov94s are the most rewarding and interesting courses in the Gov department, but this one may be a bit inaccessible to people without prior background in IR. It may have just been my section, but discussions often became dominated by students with significant background in national security policy or US–Russo or US–China relations.
Well run course, definitely worth takingeven for those with little interest/background in grand strategy/USFP. Imparato is incredible as expected. True teacher deeply invested in the growth and success of his students.
