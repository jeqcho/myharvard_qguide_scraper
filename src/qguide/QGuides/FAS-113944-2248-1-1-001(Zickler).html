this course was kind of a train wreck for me. the exam is right at the end of the semester and everyone I know completely bombed it. the weekly psets are also stressful and there are other grad courses with less demanding schedules than that. nothing against prof. Zickler – he is great. its just a really hard course and is not always the most interesting (most of it is projective geometry and linear algebra so it feels dry).
This class was phenomenal. It is so interesting and the coding assignments are so fun and very very good exercises that really help you grasp the material. With that said, they are very difficult, but very rewarding. The class will also solidify your linear algebra and ability to use it as a tool when solving problems. It is such an interesting and fun class. The last few weeks are especially interesting as you begin to discuss some AI and neural net and transformer architectures which are so cool.
Zickler is certainly one of if not the best lecturers I’ve had in SEAS (although profs with an EE background just seem to be better than CS profs as a CS concentrator). Good lectures, excited about the content, extremely knowledgeable, and very available to students given the small class size. A couple of the assignments were pretty intense for one–week timelines, but overall fair including the midterm. Definitely take if interested in CV.
Very interesting topics in computer vision. The problem sets can be very very scary at first but start to feel easier someway through the class. The lecture content and problem sets can also feel detached, but at the end of the day they are rewarding once you get the hang of them, because you produce visually interesting results, which feel like they could be applied. The professor is a lovely person, extremely helpful, and very approachable. Don't expect to absorb everything in every lecture but focus on understanding rather than the details (except for the midterm).
Start the psets early, they're nontrivial and you get the most learning out of them by starting early. Really make sure you grasp the topics enough to explain them so that when it comes to the midterm, you can create a good cheat sheet and use it well. Think about the final project deeply and ask for advice and limitations in office hours. Todd is a super chill and genius guy, go to his office hours.
This is the best class I've taken at Harvard. Professor Zickler is an excellent teacher. Workload is high, but the assignments were useful and fun.
This class is a great all–around introduction to computer vision, but if you're interested in CNN methods only then a different class is probably more suitable. Zickler does cover the topic but at the end and your only real opportunity to do something related to it is in the final project–– this makes sense since he doesn't really study CNN computer vision in his research.

There were long WEEKLY psets for this class that would take 20 + hours which honestly became difficult to handle. I took this class along side CS61 and another pset class so it honestly was a very difficult semester for me. Moreover, the prof is very strict on deadlines and there are only 3 1–day extensions per semester. What I will say is that having gone through the trenches of each long Jupyter notebook after another I have come away with a lot of knowledge. Tough but worth it.

If you decide to take this class, start on the project early!
If you are looking for a good balance of theory and practical applications with rigorous practice in both, take this course!!! You will learn so much!!
This is a fun but quite challenging course. There's a big emphasis on psets and coding rather than reading papers or understanding current research. Psets are mostly numpy, implementing really cool illusions/CV techniques. Debugging can take a long time but as long as you ensure you actually understand the math you should be OK. Midterm is very math–focused. There is a final project –– I recommend being extremely proactive in asking for feedback and staying on top of the work. The pace of this course is quite fast and there is quite a bit of work –– moreso than other grad CS classes I took, where psets are much more nominal.

The math in this course is amazingly cool and the techniques we learn are foundational and interesting. However, there is little on newer CV techniques (ML, diffusion, transformers). I do wish there was a bit more emphasis on the newer techniques used in industry/research lab.
Take this course! It is amazing in every dimension you are thinking of, and Todd is the best!
This class is rather difficult and work–intensive, however it is a very fair class and Todd is a great lecturer, so you get out what you put in. The weekly psets can take a long time, but I found them to be quite fun for the most part. This class covers a wide variety of topics, from linear algebra basics to projective geometry to signal processing and so forth, but it by no means requires you to be an expert in any of them. Overall, if you're looking for a low–work gem, don't take this class, but if you're looking to learn a lot about computer vision (a REALLY interesting topic) from a variety of perspectives, I couldn't recommend it enough!
