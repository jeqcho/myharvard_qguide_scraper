This class is weirdly structured. That's not to say that it's not organized (which I think is one of the greatest strengths of this class), but the workload is much heavier at the beginning of the semester. The quizzes in weeks 2–7 require you to do all of the readings (which there are A LOT), however after the midterm in week 6, the readings become unimportant (they are talked about in lecture and section, though you are not assessed on them). Attendance is taken via a Slack post, which requires you to pay attention in lecture (which can be a challenge for people who expected an apocalypse–focused class and found out it leaned more into religion). Make sure to attend section – the makeup assignments for being absent are far more tiring than showing up for an hour. The final project allows for a lot of creativity, which I enjoyed. Overall, workload isn't terrible, and I'd recommend this class if you need to satisfy the GenEd requirement (but not for any other reason).

This is a fairly standard Gen Ed in terms of getting out of it what you put into it. Professor Bazzana is great and gives very interesting lectures and he also invites a diverse slate of guest lecturers throughout the semester. I also found the art component of the class quite interesting— you get to look at a lot of different works from different periods and styles and one week of section is a visit to the Harvard Art Museum.

The first six weeks of the class try to build a foundational understanding of apocalyptic texts from across various cultures and then the second half of the class applies that framework to various historical issues and modern cultural phenomena. In the first half, you have a reading quiz each week, which is not particularly difficult if you have done the readings and held me accountable to actually do them. Because of that, the midterm felt quite manageable and reasonable. Without the reading quizzes in the second half, I sometimes found it hard to motivate myself to keep up with the course content, especially on weeks when I had a lot of other things going on. Overall, the other assignments (a very short paper and a creative final project) were very manageable and there was a lot of support in section for the final project.
