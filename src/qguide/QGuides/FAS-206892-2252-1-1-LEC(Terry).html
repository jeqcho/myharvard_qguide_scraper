What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I wouldn't consider this class to be easy necessarily, because the hard assignments get really hard. Yosvany is really chill, and so is Fabian, the section leader. They are both understanding, lenient, and more than willing to elaborate on things.
This course provided an excellent overview of Cuban music from the classical era to the 1960s, and I greatly appreciate the specificity in which all the genres were discussed. The presentations from guest lecturers on danzón and mambo allowed for lively discussion among the students in the class, and listening exercises during lecture provided each student with an opportunity to explain key elements in the musical atmosphere of each song. The composition–based assignments were truly outstanding, and I was able to grasp the rhythmic and harmonic foundations of Cuban music through these assignments. This course changed me by exposing me to such diverse genres of Cuban music and giving me the opportunity to perform pieces associated with these various styles. What a wonderful, enlightening course!
