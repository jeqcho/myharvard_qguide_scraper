This was my FAVORITE class I have taken at Harvard so far (other than Gov 1290)! Professor Kennedy is truly amazing. I have learned so much from her, and I hope to stay in contact with her in the future. The class subject was super interesting–negotiation and the art of negotiation is something I didn't know I would want to learn about, but I am walking away from this class having learned a lot. There isn't a lot of work overall, but I would say when she does assign a case to read, you should read it because it makes class much more engaging. Also, the readings are pretty short and like once every other week, so there is literally no reason not to do them. The papers are not bad either because her feedback is extremely specific and helpful on drafts, so unless you don't take her feedback, it is pretty hard not to do well. Overall, I learned a lot because I loved the course material, but she is also the sweetest woman, and she helped my writing so much. TAKE HER CLASS! Not because it's easy, but because she is the GOAT and I learned a remarkable amount about negotiations worlwide.
Negotiation skills are very applicable to many facets of life and in many unexpected ways, definitely pay attention to the lessons taught in this class because they will change how you view even the smallest of exchanges.
This is an excellent class. Dr. Kennedy is engaging and passionate about her students, writing, and negotiation. The class feels incredibly relevant and useful. Dr. Kennedy is welcoming to all perspectives and actively brings all students in to the conversation.

She provides wonderful feedback on the drafts and revisions. Take full advantage of office hours and conferences to get her perspective on your papers.

The readings are typically condensed to specific weeks and are case–based, so they were usually fairly interesting and engaging. It is not an incredibly heavy workload, and nothing seems like busywork.
This class is excellent! It teaches you how to think critically about your writing decisions and pushes you to improve your writing. After submitting the essay drafts, the comments might seem a little bit intimidating, however, if you follow them and listen to the professor's feedback you will receive a good grade in your revisions as long as you are putting the effort.
You should definitely take this class. Professor Kennedy really cares about all her students and puts lots of effort into supporting and caring for all her students. You will have a wonderful EXPOS experience.
It's an amazing class that is super super interesting (even for a bio major like myself). You can truly see how the principles of negotiation that you learn affect the politicians we hear about every day, and the policies that are created.
The instructor is awesome – she will work with you as much as you need and genuinely wants to see you succeed. Take this class if you want to become a better writer while enjoying your expos requirement. The course material could be more interesting imo, but check it out yourself (syllabus) and make a decision based on your interests. It wasn't super interesting to me in and of itself, but our study/analysis of it built skills and made it worth it.

Perhaps the best part of this class was the environment that Prof. Kennedy helped form: out of the 15 people in my class, I plan on saying hi to every one of them when I see them around campus. Even if you don't like writing, you'll enjoy your time in Expos with Dr. Kennedy, and that can't be said of every (or even many) expos sections.
I really recommend this class for anyone interested in learning about negotiation and looking to improve their writing. In this class, like many EXPOS classes, you will get as much as you put in. Dr. Kennedy is a fantastic teacher and a really great lecturer. She is very accommodating and is willing to help you throughout the course. However, I do think the structure of the course is a little one–dimensional sometimes, but I am less sure if this is a reflection of the class or more so a reflection of EXPOS writing courses.
This is an awesome class and Professor Kennedy is a fantastic teacher. Very interesting content and highly recommend you choose this course.
