If you plan to take this course, make sure to stay on top of each topic, as getting lost/falling behind can be very easy. I took this course simultaneously with some other challenging classes, and while it is doable, you need a large support system, whether that's finding time for office hours or having a Pset group who can help you clear up confusions early on before exams. After finishing these class, it felt divided into 3 distinct sections: 1. the basics 2. using these basics to solve problems (matrices for systems, etc.) 3. differential equations, so taking the time to connect these concepts and link them together either through formulating a "how to" list for the topics as you go or before the midterms were the most fruitful in my opinion. The 3 psets a week can be exhausting and tedious, but if you put in the work, you will be fine (Highly recommend watching the review videos before exams to solidify what was taught during section). Good luck, you got this!
