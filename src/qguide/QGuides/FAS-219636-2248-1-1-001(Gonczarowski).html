Comments
Yannai and Jack were a great teaching team! They're serious about their work and their research, and it's not a class where you can BS the output you produce. It's not too much work on a weekly basis (3 psets), but the content is also definitely advanced––for undergrads, CS 136 is necessary.
Great class, workload is chill (only three PSets) but you learn a lot; more guidance/checkpoints on final projects would've been helpful
Would so highly recommend this class! Yannai is a great lecturer who really cares about his students and is very supportive—it's clear that he wants everyone to succeed and welcomes questions, always. He's bad with names but makes an effort to learn yours :^) Super reasonable workload (3 psets and a final project) that is intellectually fulfilling and fun, and quite low stress for the majority of the semester. One of my favorite grad classes!
Take this course if you are seriously interested in theoretical econ/CS, extra emphasis on the theoretical component. Course can be a bit dry at times with the theoretical lecture component, but Yannai is generally a solid lecturer. Definitely would recommend coming to class each time as there isn't really a good alternative, and there is some informal emphasis on attendance. Would say in general the staff is friendly and Jack is a great TF. It's also pretty hard to pursue this class without knowing at least another person in the class to do a project with, since that project can be pretty complex and it is much better to be working on a team.
