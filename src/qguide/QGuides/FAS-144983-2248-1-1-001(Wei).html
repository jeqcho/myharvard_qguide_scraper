This class was not a good experience. Do keep in mind that this course probably won't be with Chris and Gu again in the near future, so my words probably don't have much bearing on what your experience will be like. But yeah, this was a huge slog, and we did not learn very much at all. My chief complaint about this class is that we did not use any of the skills we have built up in our engineering classes to complete this project. I know there aren't many serious problems you could solve using two years of college education, but really, I believe the only requirement for this class is Expos 20. You did not need to have taken a single engineering class to do this project. Chris and Gu were very upfront early on about how an actual physical product would likely be outside the scope of the class they designed, like that was not a serious problem with the class! On our very first day, they showed us some of the past projects that previous years have worked on, to help generate excitement. It's ironic that all of these past examples were cool robots solving a smaller scale problem. Chris and Gu know that this is what we are here to do! Please make sure that we can!

Anyway, you have to take this class, so I should offer some advice rather than just complain. If/when you get the opportunity to speak to experts in the field you are working on, take every one that you get. It will be the most helpful thing for your project by a mile. Aggressively hunt for interviews early on, these will give you real direction for your project, and offer some perspective that you can't just get on google scholar. Honestly, I would be saying much better things about my time in this class if I had done more of this early on – for future instructors, please note that this is the absolute most helpful thing for the students, and you should ensure that at least 3 expert interviews are conducted by each team. I would also avoid being in a group with your closest friends – you will work a lot harder that way.
This class will be as interesting as you are willing to make it. If you invest effort your work can feel real and tangible.
You have to take it for an SB. Choose a good group to work with. It's annoying but it'll be over soon
It won't be your favourite class and you don't really know what's happening most of the time, but it's a requirement and it's not a bad class so....enjoy!
This is probably already a requirement for you, but don't be scared! This class can be challenging at times, since it is so open–ended. But the instructors are usually very receptive and provide guidance. You will get through it, and you will most likely be proud of what you end up making at the end!
I will start with the positives: It was very cool to take an engineering class where you have to deliver to an actual client. In this case, our client was the GSA and we had to deliver sustainable plans to data centers. I think this will prepare us for the real world of engineering consulting, but is not preparation at all for a senior thesis project which is what the class is supposedly designed to do. This class is good in that there is virtually no work, but it is a huge waste of time. I am very disappointed in how this class was taught. It could have been a really cool class where we developed a meaningful solution for our client, but instead we spent way too much time discussing problem statements that we nearly immediately abandoned when we needed to come up with a solution. Taking 6–7 weeks to discuss a problem is far too long; it should only take 2 weeks. This would give us more time to create a more developed solution instead of a half thought–out plan. Overall, this class was a waste of time. Chris Lombardo and Gu need to have more command of the classroom. Almost all of us were on our laptops doing other work the whole time because it was pointless to pay attention. If we actually did meaningful work and research, instead of useless class activities like the snowflake throwing, it would have been a much more productive time.
