Do the readings!
This tutorial's topic is fascinating. Most of the readings were super interesting, and we overall had some nice discussions in class. Shiv is a great instructor and always managed to keep the class's energy up! Highly recommend this tutorial. It can be more work than expected, but our syllabus was organized in a way that made everything manageable. Start your final project as soon as possible!
This is an excellent tutorial. The content is fascinating, you get to read a wide range of papers regarding development, discussions each class were super interesting. Shiv is an excellent teacher and does a great job leading the discussions. I think out of all the Ec 970 sections, this is definitely one of the most interesting ones. I feel like I learned so much over the course of the semester. Highly highly highly recommend this one! The readings might seem a bit much at first, but they're honestly really interesting and you get the hang of reading it.
This is a requirement as an Economics Concentrator, but it should not be a dreaded one. If you choose a subject you are somewhat interested in, the readings can be very engaging. Even if it is not your top choice, there is something to be said about learning to read the type of economics papers you will encounter. There is a ton of reading assigned, especially for someone like me that is a math and social sciences guy. But as my TA said this is a lesson in learning how to skim as much as it is how to read economics papers. A 60 page research paper, in my opinion, has about 30 important pages and a few sentences that are relevant on the other ones. So not that bad. And the paper, if you actually start early, is genuinely fun. Meet with your TA about the code and get all your regressions and results coded out early. That way whenever you sit down to write its not actually an awful time crunch but more doable. And its like working out: hard, but feels awesome after your done. Take it with Shivram Viswanathan, if you can!
I was originally the least excited about this course of all of the classes I had lined up for the semester, but it quickly became my favorite. Mostly because of my incredible TF Shiv (I think your experience in this course is highly TF–dependent), who curated an incredible reading list and a semester of incredibly reflective and enlightening conversations about the nature of global economic development. The final project was also an awesome opportunity to mini–thesis on something that interests you, which was awesome and I learned a ton through that experience.

The problem sets are annoying and pretty disconnected from the rest of the course, but a good reminder to practice R/Stata throughout the semester before you need to be quite fluent in it for your final project.
