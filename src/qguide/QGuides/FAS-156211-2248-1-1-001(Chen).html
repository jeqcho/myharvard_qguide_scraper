The most comprehensive ml theory course. I recommend taking it, but also be prepared, it is not an easy course.
I took this course as a sophomore; arguably, the greatest course of my academic career. The problem sets are arduous, but so rewarding. This course covers a breadth of content without sacrificing technical details for proofs. Professor Chen is an amazing lecturer as well, though I wouldn't recommend live–texing since the comments btwn blackboard writing are at least as valuable as the writing itself. The class is well structured, with a lot of potential for self–guided exploration of supplementary material, and the final project exposes you to some really neat content. I can't understate my love for this course; if the psets are hard to fit in your schedule, audit it – this is too good to pass up.
This is an excellent course and if you pay attention in class you will get a lot out of lectures. He does a mix of lecturing with slides and with the blackboard which is really nice. The problem sets were no joke (4 total). My strategy as a busy PhD student was to do them in a single day (or night) and accept that I would have to skip many questions and earn a lower grade in the class.
This is a difficult course. Don't sign up for it lightly. This is also one of the best courses I've ever taken.

Basically if you think you have good intuition for stats and math and want to learn more in ML theory, I highly highly recommend. It will be an amazing experience.
This class covers a lot of content, but you don't get much time to really appreciate the deeper consequences of the material. The psets don't really help you learn.
