Comments
It's a great class to learn about Sicily's culture, history, and unique identity, primarily through an archaeological approach that's broken down into a well–structured class. The field trip is a great way of connecting with the topics explored in class while also extending interest in the field as it gives you a sense of "I was there" that can't be replicated by PowerPoint slides.
I LOVED the trip, but the rest of the class isn't great. The professor is a bit shy/antisocial and the class isn't organized super well. Definitely more interesting if you've taken an archeology class in the past, especially with the professor. She seemed to favor people who've had her before (yeah, I said it).
The trip makes this class worthwhile and brings the readings to life! The first half of the class was a bit dry, but after we returned from Sicily, our discussions became much richer, and students were more willing to participate (since we had gotten to know each other much better during spring break).
Readings are very archaeology heavy, which can be frustrating. (I would have appreciated more historical/literary sources.)
Overview of Sicilian history was a bit cursory, and class discussions were often repetitive. Professor sometimes seemed underprepared for lectures.
Still, the trip and the final paper gave me a solid understanding of Sicilian history and culture and motivation to continue learning.
112 is a required course, but it is not one of those courses that is meant to weed out the feint of heart. It is fun, engaging, and builds a deep knowledge base. Ive been looking forward to this course for a while, and it did not disappoint.
In order to get the most out of this class, so the readings, participate in class, and get to know your classmates. It makes the trip so much more enjoyable when you do the site report in advance. Also, find out what topic interests you the most to make the final paper an enjoyable assignment to complete.
If you get the chance to take it, go for it! Prof. Andrews is wonderfulfunny, insightful, and a great teacher. You'll learn a lot, and have a lot of fun in Sicily. Be careful when ordering limoncello spritzes in Marsalathey're basically 100% alcohol and sugar.
The most incredible class! I learned so much, not just about Sicily but how to ask questions that historians and archaeologists are constantly asking, and how to grapple with questions that are impossible to answer.
