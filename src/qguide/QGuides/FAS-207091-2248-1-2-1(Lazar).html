Take this class!! Take this class! Its such a wonderful opportunity to be able to learn from such an amazing Professor and to learn this rich material. It changes the way you think and approach life.
If you're quite interested in meditation and psychology, this is the course for you. It's taught by the first person to take an fMRI image of a monk's brain to show it was quantitatively different. She is a well of knowledge and you will be invested in meditation after this course!!
This is a nice neuro elective if you want to learn a lot about meditation though, as the title suggests, the course is more like a journal club where you examine neuroscience papers as opposed to get a deep understanding of the history and and different types of meditation. Professor Lazar is really nice and always available to meet outside of class and is just super passionate about the topic.
Probably the most life changing course I took at Harvard––meditation is a powerful tool and has already made my life so much better, and it was awesome to get to go through this great course alongside other students and watch ourselves deepen our understanding together. Highly recommend this course even if you struggle a bit with neuroscience papers like I do!
This is a great class. The structure of it is probably the best of any class I've been in. You post discussions on readings which can be tedious but ensures that everyone in the class can contribute to the discussion. Also, after the first couple of weeks, the class is just student–led discussions on papers that were assigned, which I thought was great (made everyone comfortable with presentations and kept discussion going every week). Also, meditation at the top of every class is great.
This course is wonderful!! Meditation is now part of my personal routine because of this course. Throughout the semester, you really gain an understanding of the neurological concepts involved in meditation and related topics. You also gain a basic understanding of Buddhist philosophy. There is quite a bit of complex reading for this class, so make sure you are ready for that. You will also be presenting one of the readings throughout semester, which I found to be extremely helpful. Definitely take this course if you are ready to fully engage with it–––however, this would be a very difficult course to only put half of your effort into.
