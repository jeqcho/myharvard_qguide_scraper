work hard and go to class! don't take shortcuts, and you will really enjoy it here and be glad you chose this course. everyone is here to help you, and if you feel you're struggling, more people than you know feel the same way. this is a bonding experience with your cohort, you will learn and grow with your friends if you take this course a lot more than other courses.
Highly recommend this class! Even if I have no plans of ever working with some of these model organisms, having this class as background is really helpful when it comes to understanding how other papers and fields ask and answer biological questions. While the class was challenging, I feel like it was time well spent.
This is a challenging class but well worth your time and effort. The teaching faculty wants everyone to do well, and they offer lots of resources for students who need a little extra help. You will get out what you put in, so go to lecture, work through the problem sets, and study for the exams.
This course is difficult, but will learn alot.
I highly recommend this class if you are interested in Genetics or want to deepen your understanding of the basic model organisms used in biology. Fred and Max are amazing course directors and put a ton of effort into this class. Your experience will be partially dependent on your TF who helps with application of class material. It's a difficult class, so having taken genetics classes in undergrad is very helpful, or having work experience in a genetics lab.

Try not to be dissuaded by the challenging reputation. This is a great class. The content is slightly challenging but in a good way (just enough to get you thinking deeply about the material). I initially had no interest or background in genetics but found the psets and exams fair and relatively enjoyable to complete. If you put your best foot forward you will learn a lot and should not have too much difficulty passing the course.
I would tell future students that doing the problem sets alone and then discussing it with others (in that order) is very important for learning and will help in exams and in your future graduate career.
You probably have to take it, but you'll have fun! It's challenging but not something to be stressed about.

First of all, I would like to say Fred Winston is an amazing lecturer (and I truly appreciate his sense of humor). I have a strong background in genetics, but regardless, I learned a lot in this class because: 1) it gives a great broad review of genetics , 2) touches upon contemporary experimental approaches, and 3) gives you a great introduction to model organisms and principles in genetics.

The only downside of this class was the population genetics module, because I think it just wasn't organized too well...
