PLEASE TAKE THIS COURSE. Matt is literally incredible and probably one of the most helpful members of the social studies department. He is so kind and understanding of your life as a student, but at the same time, very thourough in evaluating your work and providing QUALITY feedback. Super grateful for all the help he has provided over the months with my thesis and would 100% retake a course with him given the chance.
If you take this class in the fall and you already know what your thesis topic might shake out to look like, the work in this class can be preliminary work toward your thesis that you will use, and the class will prepare you for funding opportunities and for planning your research. If you take this class in the fall and don't already know your topic, this class will prepare you to start thinking about your thesis, and a lot of the work you produce might not end up being usable in your actual thesis but it will be helpful for you to explore topics with less pressure. If you take this class in the spring, be prepared for the pressure of funding deadlines and department deadlines as you are developing work toward your thesis. This can be very stressful if you still don't know your full topic, if you do this work will feel timely and urgent and will likely shape up to be work you use as you develop your thesis.
If you are a social studies student, this class will really help frame your thesis in a very compelling way. I would recommend strongly taking this your junior year.
