There are definitely easier Econ electives out there, but I personally found this class to be very interesting! You learn some of the most foundational models in urban economics (even if they appear very stylized), and just hear about a host of issues facing cities today — as well the successes of cities. Both professors bring a wealth of experience, and really made the lectures very engaging. For grading purposes, you can probably get by just by understanding the models (which the sections really help for) and nothing else, but the interesting content is all in lecture so make sure to go to lecture!
Glaeser speaks really fast (this is the best part of the course). Material is kind of interesting, but also at times kind of obvious (e.g., rent is higher at the center of the city, wow!). Data problem sets can take a long time. Exams are pretty easy as long as you can do algebra quickly. A pretty decent way to fulfill writing requirement.
TAKE THIS CLASS. Best elective in Econ department. Glaeser and DiPasquale are amazing lecturers. requires active participation in lecture and section but you will get out a lot. Super interesting and relevant material
Pretty gemmy economics class with some interesting content.
This course is engaging and very interesting. It covers way more than just economics of cities and really feels like it teaches you about a bunch of different national problems. Even if you've never taken an econ course, take this one because its a great example of how economics shapes the real world
GREAT CLASS!!!! Glaeser and DiPasquale are amazing professors and it was an amazing experience to get to learn from them. Highly recommend this class!
Very different from other economics courses; really a great elective. Not too demanding week to week so important to stay on top of the material yourself. Also, I don't think there were too many prereqs, but I came in w/ a big econ background and definitely found that useful in understanding the theoretical models (which glaesers loves) and some of the data work.
Fascinating class that broadly describes what makes cities great (and sometimes not so great). Not an easy A, but psets don't require too much effort and exams are very manageable if you study the models. GO TO SECTION—lectures are not good at teaching the math!
the nice combination of economics, history, policy, and politic
This is a great econ elective that satisfies the writing requirement. Lectures are fun, there only 6 Psets that are mostly light, and the paper is pretty short. The paper is very open in terms of topics and the class provides so many topics to write about, or connect to what you want to write about. I really enjoyed this class. I would also appreciate more feedback on Psets and on the final paper – unless that feedback is still to get to me.
This class is pretty great, especially if you're an urbanite or if you want some exposure to real estate topics.
super interesting class and relevant to take, models covered in the class are pretty simple and not super applicable but a good thought exercise. manageable workload
Midterms are not a joke – understand the intuition of models instead of learning the equations. Really interesting class overall. Make use of the TFs!!
Although the exams and PSets were quite challenging and sometimes felt not very relevant to the course, this is a great class for anyone interested in learning about how cities work. Both professors are incredibly knowledgeable about urban economics, and all lectures were insightful. Professor DiPasquale in particular clearly cares about the students and is always happy to help.
take this course!!
– Show up to lecture – bro if you're taking this class and not pulling up what are you doing
– The readings you should actually do are Prof Dipasquale's textbook – working through the models one step at a time are CRUCIAL to success in the finals.
– The psets are jokes (graded check–, check, check+ where both check and check+ are 10/10 and no late submissions) the exams are not – you will need to haul ass to prepare and the best way is to GO TO SECTION and rederive the formulas
– The policy paper and other parts of the grade are very fair.
– Definitely one of the most interesting courses I've taken at Harvard.
This is a great econ elective that was chill and also very interesting. Prof Glaeser is a very entertaining lecturer. Prof DiPasquale is a very insightful lecturer. The psets were doable and there were only a few of them.
Cannot stress how great this class is. Professors Glaeser and DiPasquale are both amazing lecturers and very accesible and great to talk to. The class covers very interesting topics and you will get out what you put into, so make sure to enjoy the pre–lecture videos. The psets make a big bulk of your final grade, but they're graded pass/fail, so you should do all of them!
Professors Glaeser and DiPasquale are great and I learned a lot both about the economics of cities and the history of cities. They made an effort to get to know every student in the class through pizza lunches which I appreciated and made the class feel more personal. Psets are fair but exams are hard
Take it if you're interested in urban design, transportation, building communities, or if you're just a senior looking for a fun class so you don't leave Harvard regretting not taking anything cool! Ed Glaeser is an incredible economist with amazing stories to tell, and probably one of the coolest people you will meet at Harvard. Ed and Denise both really make an effort to get to know you, and it's so cool to hear what they have to talk about! You'll look at cities in a new way after the class, like why cities grow at the rates and dimensions they do and what causes some cities to be more successful than others. Plus it's not too difficult mathematically, so don't be deterred by Ed's ec 1011A reputation. Just take it!
