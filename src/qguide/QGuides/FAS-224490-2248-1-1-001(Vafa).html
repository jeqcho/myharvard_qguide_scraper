Prof. Vafa is funny and very excited about this topic, so it is great to even just listen to him speaking. The puzzles for homework are interesting, sometimes hard, but he only grades on whether you've attempted them or not. If you want a preview/overview of some topics in physics or math through the lens of solving puzzles, this is a good class! It provides a very unique perspective on all these topics, and I had a lot of fun.
Do take this course. Little to no workload. Professor Vafa is the coolest person and so kind too (he arranges 10 minute break with snacks for recharging). Final assignment is just the presentation so the course is pretty chill.
Great first–year seminar with a great professor! Vafa is very passionate and enthusiastic and is a pleasure to be around with. Very light workload which helps if you are taking an intense math or physics class. If you are considering this seminar, my piece of advice to you would be to actually commit to doing the puzzle assignments properly. Yes, they are pass–fail, but if you take them seriously, you can get a lot out of them. I got into the habit of doing them last minute and relying on other people to carry me, and in hindsight I feel like I did not gain as much out of the course as I would have, had I taken the mini–PSETs more seriously.
Definitely take this seminar if you enjoy physics and are ok with not understanding everything in absolute depth but simply enjoy being introduced to complicated ideas and delving deeper into the world of physics. If you already and incredibly confident and talented physicist and mathematician, definitely take this seminar as you will still be intrigued and challenged by the puzzels
Vafa's the goat. Easiest seminar ever. Fun puzzles
This is a fun class whose concepts take on a large range, from simple coin puzzles to beautiful topological and physical solutions to real problems. Its topics are elegant and compose a demonstration of some of the beauties of physics. The earlier problems sets can be quite difficult, but gets easier later on. Overall I enjoyed it, it was inspiring and fun.
