Professor Given gives really thorough feedback and cares a lot about his students!
I TOTALLY recommend and LOVED this course. This class will challenge the way you think about history, ownership, and the stories we choose to preserve. You’ll dive deep into questions that don’t have easy answers—like who has the right to possess cultural objects, and how institutions like museums shape our understanding of identity and memory.

What makes this class special is how it blends intense academic writing and concepts with real–world current ethical dilemmas. You won’t just be writing to meet a requirement—you’ll be exploring questions that I believe genuinely matter and can help you intellectually across disciplines. The discussions are engaging, the readings are rich and provocative, and Professor Given creates a space where your voice and perspective are truly valued.

Come ready to challenge your assumptions, engage with complex topics, and grow as a thinker and writer. This class stays with you long after it ends!!!
I HIGHLY recommend this course, especially if you have any interest in art history! Not only do you improve your writing, but you also get to visit a lot of the Harvard museums and learn about the history of Harvard objects, which is truly such an unique experience. Additionally, Professor Given is very kind and makes himself very available to meet for any questions about the papers or your writing in general.
This is a great expos to take! Given provides very helpful feedback and guides you through the elements of the class well.

Your writing WILL improve if you take this class.
