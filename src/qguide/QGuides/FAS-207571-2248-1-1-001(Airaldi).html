So this was a special year for TDM 109, since Remo took over for Marcus without much warning, but god it was phenomenal. Take a class with Remo, whatever class you can. I've been doing theatre for almost a decade now, and I still learned so much about acting as well as directing (learning from Remo with how he gives acting notes)!! The class is what you make of it, so really put time into your monologues/scenes. The two scenes that I got to do in this class were two of my favorite acting experiences ever! Remo also does such a great job of tailoring his feedback to the experience levels of each performer, so if you're more experienced, you still learn so much from his more nit–picky specific feedback. This has been one of my favorite classes of all time at Harvard, and I'm so grateful to Remo and Alex for this experience!!
This class was absolutely phenomenal. If you see Remo teaching a course, you should absolutely 100% take it. He's one of the best professors I've had in my entire time at college. He makes class unbelievably fun and engaging, and everyone is always enjoying, laughing, and energizing one another. Alex was also absolutely amazing too, and he could have taught a course like this superbly; look out for him as a TF or as a teacher in the future as well!
I can't speak highly enough about the teaching team and about the class. If you come in with an open mind and with enthusiasm and willing to be vulnerable and fail, you will grow tremendously. Everyone became so much more confident, composed, playful and just much better actors by the end of the class. The workload is really manageable and also fun; this is one of the only classes I've felt like I really enjoyed reading other students' weekly blog posts and then replying and interacting with them, and everyone did too based on the dynamic that emerged in the forums!
There's really not much else to say: Best class at Harvard, Remo is best teacher, Alex is best TF. Take this class!
genuinely one of the most fun and fruitful classes that i have ever taken at this school. i learned so much about myself and emotions and the people around me
This course is EXCELLENT! Remo and Alex are gems. Your peers who take this class will be a joy to get to know as well –– I met and made great friends.

There will be a couple of weeks where you put in around 4–5 hours of work (preparation for monologues and scenes). Otherwise, the workload will be minimal, at most an hour per week. This was undoubtedly my least work–intensive class (I was in two 8–10 hr/wk courses and one other 2 hr/wk course). Looked forward to this class as a fun break of my schedule rather than another academic commitment.

I hope this class maintains the current structure of whole class acting activities + monologue and scene work, as it is more engaging than only monologue and scene work.

Overall, I took so much out of this course. The course taught me how to get into the flow of acting, structure scenes, and monologues, and improve in acting.

This course is for you if you want to learn how to be an actor in a fun way.
