What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you have any interest in migration, history, discrimination, or law, you must take this course. Professors Herzog and Hoffnung–Garskof are some of the best out there, and the selection of reading for this course was truly unmatched though heavy at times. We got to speak with authors of works we read, as well as visit the Boston Immigration Court for our final project. Definitely one of my favorites I've taken at Harvard.
If you are interested in the story of migration and how it connects to the law, take this course! Professors Hoffnung–Garskof and Herzog are incredible scholars that are leaders in their field. Just being a part of the weekly discussions with them is enough of a reason to take the class. Do be prepared for intensive readings (a consistent 100 pages minimum, with some weeks including full books, 200 to 300 pages, per week) and writing assignments though.
I would say take this course if you are interested in issues of migration and rights and law but know that it is a lot of work and reading. You will learn a lot and become much more informed but have to be willing to put in the time.
If you have interest in the topic and the regions, and particularly the time periods covered, I would look into taking this course. The two professors build off one other in class conversation and very clearly have a wealth of knowledge in their respective fields. However, I would be aware of the workload going into the class because it is a significant time commitment. I had the heaviest workload in this class compared to my other classes this semester. The readings each week can be extensive (some weeks there's 1 book and there's sometimes multiple long texts) and then a ~3 page essay analyzing the readings that's due Sunday morning. We receive mid–semester feedback, but beyond that, it is also difficult to know where you stand in the class since the grade is composed of 1 reflection paper and participation. I enjoyed the conversations in class, but the grading can make it feel difficult to know if your work and effort has been acknowledged. I would like to have received feedback on overall class performance more frequently, in addition to the mid–semester comment.
