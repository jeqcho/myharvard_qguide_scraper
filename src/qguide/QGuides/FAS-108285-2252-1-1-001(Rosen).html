Per spring 2025, I do not believe this is ACTUALLY a methods course. We read different thinks on various topics related to theory, sure, but, this course taught me nothing about how to think through political theory, approach researching/writing, or developing an argument. If you're hoping to take this to prep for writing as thesis (as the course intends to be), I'd suggest just taking a different political theory course. Again, this course simply taught me 0 about political theory methods. Highly disappointing.
This course is structured as a six–week boot camp on key topics in political theory, followed by five weeks of student presentations on papers which are relevant to their term papers. I enjoyed the seminar discussions. Prof. Rosen is an excellent teacher and facilitator with witty comebacks and anecdotes. I would recommend this course to those with a strong interest in political theory (and willing to do all the readings!).
There's cake at the end and Professor Rosen is amazing!
Only three assignments. A group presentation, your presentation on your final paper, and the final paper. 50% on the final paper, and 50% on participation. Section discussions can easily be taken over by "section kids" and if you didn't do the readings, it is difficult to make meaningful contributions.
I would really advise looking at political theory courses in the philosophy department instead. They often have much richer discussions, and the Professors are really invested in your growth. This course is a great way to learn what the foundational texts of the course are. But the Professor doesn't really give a lot of feedback on the midterm assignment, so you don't know how you can substantially improve until post finals. Moreover, there aren't any reviews over your drafts of the final paper. It just isn't the best course if you want to learn how to improve your writing and argumentation.
Take this class if you plan to write a senior thesis in political theory. It will teach you the methods you need to succeed. Most students are either juniors or seniors, but first–year and second–year students can join, the same as students in other concentrations. This course allows you to explore a topic (you choose it) under the political theory umbrella while applying political theory methods. In short, this course will show you "how to write a research paper in political theory."
This class is a great way to accentuate your political philosophy. Professor Rosen is exceptionally brilliant and picks up on the most important points that I think valuablly help my understanding of the course.
I would recommend some background, at least a class covering political philosophy at large, but it is doable if you have no background. Professor Rosen is more than willing to walk you through the canon.
Professor Rosen's jokes may be the best part of the class. He may recycle a few of them, but you will nonetheless laugh at both instances.
This was a difficult class, but a good one. Professor Rosen is clearly very brilliant and so helps to understand concepts in political theory. Doing readings each week is key, but they are oftentimes a bit long. I think this class is most helpful if you are preparing to write a political theory thesis.
