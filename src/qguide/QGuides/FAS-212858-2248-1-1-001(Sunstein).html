If you're interested in really making change, by which I mean dismantling the systems that oppress us, then this class is not designed to help you with that. While a lot of the behavioral science content is important and useful (if you apply it in directly the opposite way that Sunstein does), the basic premise of the class is that governments and big corporations have the best interest of the people at heart, so we should encourage and enable them to use their massive control over our lives for good—make people choose to eat healthier, choose to quit smoking, change their mistaken beliefs... it's always at the individual level and never addressing anything resembling systemic evil. I'm left wondering what one is supposed to do when the government and/or corporations do not have one's best interest at heart, or indeed are the perpetrators of the crimes one is fighting to end.
Please, please don’t take this course. Take my advice and don’t do this to yourself or your GPA.
Take this course –– lecture is very interesting!
I was surprised by how much I actually enjoyed this course. I'm an applied math concentrator, so I was not expecting a course on the law, politics, and policy of social change to be fun. However, I ended up finding the content to be quite interesting. I even enjoyed section, despite the fact that we had to talk about some extremely controversial issues. I will say that even though Prof. sunstein and the TFs did their best to be objective throughout the course, there was still a distinct left–wing bias present. Of course, it is extremely hard not to be biased when commenting on political/social movements, but I noticed that if we were talking about a right–leaning social movement, such as the gun rights movement, the readings would usually consist of liberal scholars or journalists commenting on conservative thought, rather than conservatives speaking for themselves. I would have appreciated more readings from a conservative perspective to balance things out a bit. Still, I think that Prof. Sunstein did a good job of trying to be fair to all sides of an issue while still having a clear left–wing bias, and my section TF did not try to push us toward one side of the political spectrum or the other at all, merely facilitating our discussions. The workload was also very manageable; there were several readings, but nothing compared to what I've seen some humanities classes give, and we only had to do four discussion posts, a midterm, and a final throughout the semester, which was reasonable in context of the class. Overall, I enjoyed this course, and I would definitely recommend it as a good way to fulfill the ethics and civics requirement.

I really enjoyed this course! Professor Sunstein is a truly remarkable person with a remarkable career and life experiences – it was really cool to have him as a lecturer. He is genuinely invested in the students and the content he is teaching. I think everyone should take this course because it really opens your mind up about how people think – both the logistics and practical nuances as well as more philosophical and personal nuances – which is really important in today's polarized political environment. I genuinely learnt quite a lot about topics and issues I thought I had a good grasp of but that I actually had a pretty one–dimensional view of. The readings were interesting but I highly encourage going to lecture (which is just once a week) and actually paying attention to Professor Sunstein, rather than being on your laptop or doing other work, because his lectures give you a much better understanding of topics and also helps you remember things that you might need to know for the final/midterm. Other than the content though, the actual workload is extremely easy to work with – only 4 discussion posts throughout the entire semester – so you essentially don't really have much work to do during the semester, which I think is great, because then you feel better about setting aside the 2 hours to go to lecture and really focus on the content there because that's where I believe most of the learning lies. The only times you really have to put your head down and work is for the midterm and final (also because they constitute a large part of your grade) but the work for this is also doable. Take this class it's an easy workload but you get a lot out of it in terms of learning!
