The ONLY negative is the final 7–page essay and slide presentation you have to make but even then the benefits of "field trips", labs, interesting lectures, and an easy workload make me want to recommend this class to anyone.

The class is very enjoyable. The professor is super funny and knowledgeable and as a class, we had good discussions. However, the workload is a bit on the heavier side for a first–year seminar and can be time–consuming at times. But this class teaches you valuable skills like reading and understanding research papers and most importantly, how important microbes are!
You will read research papers galore.

It is true that this class is a lot of work for a seminar. It's something I really did not really comprehend that well coming in, but one thing to note about seminars is that you really should take them only if your already interested in the subject or have an interest in learning more about it/discovering it. I think, knowing that, it can make your experience with this work–heavy seminar more enjoyable. In the end however, all seminars are pass fail so don't stress yourself out. It is a lot of work but a workload that comes from a passion to learn and complete it not to retain a perfect GPA.

All in all, this seminar is great for just exploring and knowing more about microbiology and symbiosis, a topic that doesn't get a lot of focus seemingly. Every class we'd go over the chapter of the microbiology book we read and completed a short 200 written assignment on. After that, kids would usually give out presentations concerning whatever independent research review on the newest microbiology discoveries were (usually had a written assignment reading like 2 to 3 research papers to gain background info). Then class would end with some food or like a trip to the lab to dissect. Seminar filled with fun field trips and amazing people. Prof Cavanaugh is so funny, not in a haha way but in like a conversational mishap way. You will be called out if your not paying attention/didn't do your work though.

Overall, would really recommend. Lots of work for high reward. The microbiology I learned in this class was literally useful in my other biology courses and something I'll take with me throughout my career and how I think about health and biology.

We read the book I Contain Multitudes by Ed Yong and it was really interesting. I learned a lot about microbes, and I feel more comfortable reading scientific papers now. This class has really cool field trips too, and I especially liked the trip to the aquarium and the tour of the Bellono lab!
Professor Cavanaugh is extremely knowledgable. It's directly reflected in how informative each lesson is even with only one class per week. She's also so sweet and really works hard to give her students the best experience. She also brings in food every class :D

There does appear to be more work for this class than other first–year seminars, but the knowledge you gain is worth it!
