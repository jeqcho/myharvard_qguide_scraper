Of all the courses at Harvard, this has been the most enjoyable and educational without causing a great deal of stress. Truly a masterclass in how STEM classes should be operated at Harvard and don't think you can come across a better ran/more engaging upper level bio elective at this school

Also, the final project is an opportunity to be creative which is not often seen in science courses. Have fun with it!!

the teaching team is awesome and really care about the students and the course. It more of an in depth version of SCRB 10 with less memorization and mechanisms and more examples and oerall understanding. Loved it!
Honestly this is a great SCRB class and you’re guaranteed to do well in general. I definitely recommend taking it if you want to learn a lot and don’t want to stress too much. To get a higher grade though, it helps to pay attention and take notes in lecture. It’ll make studying for exams much smoother since it would be recall rather than trying to learn everything from scratch and not knowing what to focus on (it was very clear from lecture what was important versus what was not). Like SCRB10 this is very focused on experimental techniques and application, NOT memorization. The especially helpful part is that the exams are open note (as you’d expect in real life research) and you can create your own notes to use during exams which make a huge difference. Make note of the experiments and results they review during lecture
Discussions are fun and the reflections are graded based on completion. Make sure to read the papers (at least one) so you’re not lost when discussing them in class with your group. Also make the final project fun and don’t stress about it! Especially make sure to stick to the time limit. You never know where you stand in the class (I had no idea what grade I had at any point) but honestly I wouldn’t stress because they want you to succeed so there’s chances to do well all throughout the semester
Great class! Sweetest teaching team ever. They explain the material in a way that is super easy to digest, and the paper discussions are actually super interesting. This is probably one of the best SCRB upper levels to take if you are an HDRB student. For work, it is pretty much participation, couple paper reading assignments, a group project, a midterm and a final. The exams are open note and super manageable if you study. **
