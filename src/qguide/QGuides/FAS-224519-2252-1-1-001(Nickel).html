What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course fundamentally changed me as a student. My writing is a lot more precise and crip than it was before. Moreover, the practice of writing a term–paper is something that every student should experience. With this in mind, I truly think that every philosophy concentrator should dabble in philosophy of language at least once during their time at Harvard. To be clear, this course is not easy. The readings are not the most intuitive but make sense with time and many office–hour conversations. So, you absolutely need to advocate for yourself when you are confused and need clarification.

Fortunately, Professor Nickel is one of the most kind and dedicated professors that I know. That being said, he is ALWAYS happy to touch base and guide you towards where you need to be. He makes it clear through his actions and words that he wants every single student to succeed. Plus, he's super funny and approachable.

TL; DR: TAKE THIS COURSE. This is one of the few seminars that is going to stick with me for years to come.
I would absolutely recommend this course if you have any interest in the content area or in contemporary theoretical philosophy more broadly. Bernhard is a fantastic instructor. He makes the course content accessible and is very supportive of students' ideas. The course is relatively demanding for two reasons. First, the research paper at the end can take up quite a bit of your time if you let it. Bernhard has a bunch of really helpful scaffolding assignments to help you structure the research process, and they're extremely useful, but that does mean that you can dump a ton of time into your paper over the course of the semester. Second, Bernhard expects really tight argumentative writing (in the small assignments as well as the research paper), and it can take a good bit of time to develop the skills to do that successfully. Those two things are definitely not bad (they were my favorite aspects of this course), but I note them because it might be difficult to really give this course your all in a really busy semester. Overall, I'm really glad I took this, and I've learned a ton from watching Bernhard teach and do philosophy with his students.
This is a pretty difficult course! However, you take out what you put in. The lectures are great, discussions often go very deep, and Bernhard is very very responsive and open to help.
This is the class where I attended the most office hours — so you really need to put the work in.
