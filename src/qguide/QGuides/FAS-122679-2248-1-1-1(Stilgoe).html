Professor Stilgoe is a visionary. He made me excited to learn and come to class this semestera rarity in this day and age.
The bandit dictator strikes again. Stilgoe's class is not for everyone. He's brash, brazen, and bold. He will say things about dating, politics, and life that might make you wince. At the same time, he will say things that make you remember why you wanted to come to Harvard in the first place. As someone who is trying to figure out political life, Stilgoe provides an old–guard perspective of Harvard, having taught here for 50+ years. To that end, the class is old school. You show up, there is no syllabus, and each week he talks about something "random" from which you are expected to make sense of the material. I really like this format. He also welcomes questions on any topic at any point, which I really like. Stilgoe, above all, encourages the pursuit of intellectual liberty and common sense. Don't embrace media narratives. Look at source material yourself. Don't fall into buckets of thought. Several students did complain about the class, saying his tangents on dating were a waste of time. I say, you get what you choose to take from the class. There is a lot to take. There is some to ignore. But John Stilgoe is one of the most genuine professors I have come across during my time at Harvard. He really cares about students, holding consistent office hours, getting into our interests, and really wanting our personal growth. He also does not pretend to be any less human than us. He talks about his former marriages; his mistakes in life; his own questions and inquiries into the world. I get so sick of the performativity of academia at Harvard. Stilgoe was a breath of fresh air away from the norm. If you're interested in this course, shop the first two classes to get a better vibe. I personally got a lot out of the class. Not everyone did. Life is what you make of it. Lend a close eyes to your world and enjoy the ride. – JPM
honestly, i was disappointed by this course. the q guide said it would be absolutely life changing–––my opinion is that if you have ever taken a walk or did arts and crafts in your life, you don't need to take this course, because you already know what it's going to teach you.

this is what the class is like: first, professor stilgoe hands out about 5 lbs of paper handouts at the beginning of each lecture about an incredibly diverse range of topics, none of which will be relevant in the slightest to that day's lecture/any future exam or paper or assignment, many of which contain outdated/sexist views. he proceeds to talk for an hour, about 50% fun facts about the day's topic, and about 50% personal anecdotes, mostly about his ex–wives. much bitterness against women as well as blatant misogyny that ought not to be written off; i was often surprised by the sheer ignorance said with immense confidence that prof. stilgoe possessed on topics that he was unfamiliar with. he seems to consider himself the expert on women, and made many comments that made me feel uncomfortable–––but what made me the most uncomfortable was that it seemed like many of the men in the room were following along or agreeing.

the sexism was not so bad as to make me want to leave the class; some readings were interesting; i appreciated having a class in my schedule where i could slow down and practice evaluating my own life in context. but this course did feel a bit like a waste of an elective and a harvard class–––even rereading my notes, i am not sure that i learned anything of substance. don't take this class if you want your life changed (which i was promised in last year's q guide).
This course allowed me to be a more insightful, introspective, and observant person. Professor Stilgoe is an amazing lecturer who cultivates an excellent community for curiosity.
I'm sure if you're reading this you already know about Professor Stilgoe and the reputation of this class. I came in without any AFVS background, but I had heard that this course would change the way you look at the world. And it definitely did! It is truly one of the most unique and interesting classes I've taken here at Harvard, and it makes you appreciate the importance of a "visual" education. It's very easy to take courses that teaches typical Harvard stuff (CS, math etc.) but Professor Stilgoe's discussions are of a Harvard that has rapidly faded in the previous decades. You will learn so much from knowing about the old Harvard (for instance, don't worry at all about the midterm). His lectures are all based on images, and the readings are long but really insightful. If you want a class that will make you *think* about your life, the world around you, and the stuff you see on a daily basis, you MUST take this class. While some of his remarks may seem out of place (I know I don't usually agree with his political leanings but it's always nice to hear a different perspective, which is something that is sorely needed in today's Harvard), that shouldn't detract you from appreciating his vast knowledge of the American landscape and its history. This isn't a course solely about the built environment: it's about the people who inhabit it. If you're interested in people, this is the course for you.
Everyone knows this, but Stilgoe is incredible. I feel extremely fortunate to have taken this course during my time at Harvard.

The professor is extremely engaging and gives great lectures. I am so glad that I took this course before I graduated – a true gem at Harvard University.
Take this class without a doubt. Professor Stilgoe is my favorite professor at Harvard so far and I have learned so much and enjoyed every aspect of this course. He is a traditional Harvard teacher who cares about his students and it shows!
Harvard needs more Professors like Stilgoe who actually pay attention. There is so much that has gone wrong with higher education. We forget that we still have our wise elders around to guide us. As Stilgoe nears the end of his career, I pray that we listen to him. I am forever inspired by his lectures, his photographs, his handouts, his feedback, his office hours, his alumni, and my peers. I would not trade this course experience for anything else.
