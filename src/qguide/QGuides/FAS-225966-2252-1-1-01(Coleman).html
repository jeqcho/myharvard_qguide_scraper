Take this course, you’ll have a great time! Lively conversations, awesome guest speakers, and interesting reading material. You really take a deep dive into the world of neoliberalism. One of my favorite courses in all my four years at Harvard so it’s definitely worth it.
Professor Coleman is great, the readings are well chosen, the individual weeks are well structured, and overall it is an excellent course if you want to get a better sense of what the major buzzword of "neoliberalism" is actually referring to
*** This class is AMAZING. Highly recommend to all. Danny is an amazing teacher, one of the best I have had during my time at Harvard. He really engaged the class and encouraged all to speak their mind. Kept all conversations and classes relevant and value–add. Really really really enjoyed this class.
This was a fantastic course, and Dr. Coleman is certainly one of the best teachers (and people I've had as a teacher) of my Harvard experience, so even if he isn't teaching this exact course in the future, I would recommend betting on him anyways. The content of this course was more difficult than most history classes I have taken since it deals with the history of an idea rather than focusing primarily on events, which causes one to think more critically. I enjoyed that aspect of the course, so if you are looking to push yourself, this course gives you that opportunity. Lastly, I'd add that this course is one of the few in which I have walked away feeling like it genuinely expanded my understanding of the world around me in profound ways, so I think the effort required for the course is more than worth it.
This seminar was one of the best I've taken at Harvard, hands down. Neoliberalism is a pretty unwieldy and multi–pronged concept but each week, the readings built upon each other and you'll definitely walk away having learned something new. The readings are dense but the assignment load isn't too heavy and Danny was an excellent instructor and conversation facilitator. Take this class if you're lucky enough to!
Take this class! One of the best courses I've taken in terms of putting our current world in context, and Danny is an incredible professor. I was actively excited to come to class every week.
Though I would say an interest in intellectual history is key (this course is not just a history of conservatism, it very much is a course about the history neoliberalism as an idea), I think taking the course for the sole reason of the professor is valid. Super interesting topic combined with exceptionally well–read, approachable, and just overall really cool professor.
The way Dr Coleman led this seminar was incredible. He arranged for two guest speakers to come and speak to the class, organized the readings in a way that they would not be too onerous or numerous and facilitated really important discussions throughout the twelve or so weeks of the course. It was the first time ever that I felt so comfortable in expressing my views in a Harvard classroom. Would really recommend the course.
