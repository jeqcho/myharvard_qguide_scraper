English 20 with Professor Whittington and Victoria has been one of my favorite experiences at Harvard thus far; it is genuinely such a good iteration of the course. There is a good mix of creative and analytical assignments. If you like writing poetry, you'll especially enjoy this course. Regardless, the best thing about this course is how thoughtful, genuine, supportive, and intentional the course staff are. They genuinely want to get to know you and care about your success. In office hours, I'd talk with them about everything from course assignments, concentration advising, to opportunities for publishing poetry. I feel so grateful and honored that such intelligent people cared that much about me.

Also, the beautiful thing about this course is that it feels so personal and genuine to your own goals and thought processes. For example, one of the assignments is a commonplace book where you write down your favorite lines from course readings –– and over time, you'll end up writing a cento poem using those very lines. In that way, the course is so informative but also so fulfilling and enriching. I learned so much and got to know so many amazing people through this course. I seriously cannot recommend enough!

Very much what I pictured a college English class to be.
I loved this course. Professor Whittington is a such great lecturer—she smiles throughout her lectures, and she's one of the friendliest professors I've had so far. The course was well–structured, with six main assignments that were evenly spaced throughout the year. In my opinion, the assignments became slightly harder over time, which felt like a natural progression. I really, really enjoyed the assignments, especially the first few creative ones. I had fun writing the poems and the transformation, which doesn't usually happen with bigger assignments. Lectures were engaging, interesting, and sometimes a lot of fun, and Victoria was great at leading sections, although sometimes they didn't feel completely necessary. I learned a lot about literary forms and the history of literature over time. Before taking this course, I didn't know how different forms of literature changed and modified themselves over time, and I feel now that I have a comprehensive understanding of the history of written forms.
It's a required course for concentrators, but for anyone who isn't studying English looking for a course to take, I would highly recommend this one!!

Honestly, my advice is to really dive deeply into the texts that interest you, and skim the rest, but pay attention in lecture and give each book/poem/epic/drama a chance. You will get so much more out of the class if you orient yourself toward what you really love and do your assignments around those works of literature and just don't stress about the rest. Let yourself have fun with the writing assignments. My writing and analytical skills genuinely improved because of this class. You get out of it what you put in, and the workload is a LOT more flexible than it might appear at first.
