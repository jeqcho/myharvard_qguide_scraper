Math 21b is a very rigorous but very rewarding course. I would recommend to take this course because I feel like I learned a lot of mathematical skills that will be helpful for other STEM courses. However, when the course description says it is fast–paced, it's telling the truth. If you're taking this class it will be your main class for the semester, and you will be booked 7:30–10:30pm Sunday–Thursday (MQC). The bright side is that you make a lot of friends trauma–bonding through MQC as you struggle to make Mathematica work so you can finish your PSet (only to have another one assigned the very next day). Three PSets a week is no joke. Yes, this class is very hard, but if I were to go back to course registration, I would still choose to take it because of how much I got out of it. The material is very challenging, but the teaching staff is amazing, and the small–section teaching model makes it a lot easier to learn the content. There are also so many opportunities to get support when it is needed. If you are debating taking the class, I would urge you to, just be mindful about the time commitment when choosing your other classes.
The 3 Psets a week are definitely brutal. The class is hard, excruciating, and deadly. Go to MQC, you'll make friends and understand PSets. Exams were hard, but many people who had already taken this course beforehand did much better. If you're reading this, you probably need this class for some requirement – good luck.
You learn a lot and linear algebra is so applicable to everything,.

If you put the effort in you will be fine. Reach out to you TF if you need to meet and discuss the material as they are always welcoming to help you learn and understand the content.
