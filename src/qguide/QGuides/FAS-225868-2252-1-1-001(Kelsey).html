If you see a class with Professor Kelsey, take it!! You may not get an A, but you will come out a more precise speaker, writer, and thinker. Don't let your GPA stop you from taking this!
This is a must–take HAA course if it is offered. Professor Kelsey is one of the most stimulating, kind, and interesting professors to learn from. The course went in a direction that I did not expect, but the consistent hands–on learning at HAM was extremely captivating. The final project allows you to work on something you find passion in throughout the semester. This is overall an extremely fun and low–stress class. Readings can be challenging but Kelsey embraces our discomfort with unfamiliar topics and seamlessly introduces foreign photography terms to us in a way that we could understand and remember them easily. This was a highlight for me in the HAA department.
Take it! Kelsey is incredible. The material is incredible. I could not possibly recommend this course more strongly. There is lots of looking at photos in the art museum. The readings are all super useful for understanding the context of the material, and the only assignment outside the main paper is a weekly discussion post.
Professor Kelsey is lovely and cares so deeply about what he teaches. This course offered a really rich introduction to the history of photography.
Professor Kelsey is incredible. He runs the class so smoothly and truly cares about his students. It was such a joy to take this course. The two classes in the Fine Arts Library and the interactive art were some of my favorites.
Professor Kelsey is such a wonderful professor and he is incredibly knowledgeable about the history of photography. I loved how he ran this seminar, and I thought it was a very organized and focused mix of art study center visits and class discussion on the week's assigned readings. He is also really accessible outside the classroom in office hours, and I feel like I learned a lot about this topic that I'll carry with me into future courses. This has definitely been a highlight amongst the courses I've taken in the HAA department. The community of the course was also quite wonderful, and class discussions were always fun and engaging.
Prof. Kelsey is great and this course is super interesting if you're interested in photography and art history. I would definitely recommend taking this class, especially to expand your concept of art history and modern art. Some of the readings and photography analysis in the modern era requires keeping an open mind to what photography should/can look like, so I would especially recommend if you're interested in modern art and how photography can move into the present day.
