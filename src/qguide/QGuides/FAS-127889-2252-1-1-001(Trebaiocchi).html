This was my favorite Italian class at Harvard!! It was super engaging and informative, and I feel that I made so many friends as well!
TAKE THIS CLASS!!!!! If you already have some experience with Italian and are looking to polish your skills while learning about social justice topics that are extremely important and relevant today, this is the perfect class to take. Dr. Trebaiocchi is truly one of the best professors I’ve had here at Harvard, and her teaching style has truly made Italian my favorite course to attend every week. The course topics are extremely rich, so there is truly something that will interest everybody, We also had many opportunities to engage with guest speakers, from podcast editors, to researchers, it was truly such a unique and valuable opportunity. The final project in this course — to create a group podcast — was honestly one of the most fun projects I’ve completed at this school. We had the chance to visit the learning lab and learn how to record and edit our podcasts in person, so I left this class not only being an advanced italian speaker, but also with some experience in making podcasts. Also don’t stress too much about the writing exams, they’re very reasonable, and you’ll be surprised to see how much your writing has improved naturally through assignments and other course work! Seriously such an awesome experience and easily one of my favorite courses at Harvard — I cant stress it enough to take this class!!!!
Take this course! All of the modules are super compelling and I learned a lot of new information. I feel as though my speaking, writing, and listening abilities have really been strengthened through this course and I would highly recommend (especially if Dr. Trebaiocchi is teaching).
This is a really worthwhile class if you’re looking to improve your Italian and talk about meaningful topics. You’ll do a lot of speaking and discussion, which can feel intimidating at first, but it really helps your confidence. The topics are super relevant (things like feminism, social justice, Italian culture today, etc etc) so it’s not just textbook stuff.

Professoressa Chiara Trebaiocchi is great. She’s really understanding and supportive, but she also expects you to put in the effort and will definitely push you to get better. The workload can be a lot at times, so just stay on top of things and don’t leave stuff for the last minute. If you’re willing to participate and put in the work, you’ll get a lot out of this course.
