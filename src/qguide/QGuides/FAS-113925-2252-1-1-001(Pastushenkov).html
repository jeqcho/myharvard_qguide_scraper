What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class will bring you from zero to a solid foundation of Elementary Russian. You will start in the first class with learning the Cyrillic alphabet; within the first weeks you can say some basic sentences; and if you are fine with the awkwardness of talking about pre–defined topics, you can use the small group sections to start experimenting with the language. By the latter half of the course, you can write about your day, your aspirations, etc. It is remarkable how much we all progressed!
Some people in the course had some prior knowledge or background, but most of us started fresh. Russian has a lot of grammar, so some familiarity with Latin or languages with a case system will make the beginning easier.

However!!! A prerequisite is that you HAVE to do the daily homework, ON TIME. If you fall behind more than one or two days, it becomes a huge burden to catch up, and it's much less effective if you are just cramming material into your brain and regurgitating it all out at the weekly vocab and unit tests. Note that even though most core concepts are covered in class, there are some specific phrases or constructions that are introduced via homework only, so if you don't do that, you'd be missing out on some. Technically it's a flipped classroom, so you are supposed to read the material beforehand including the daily homework, I found it fine to just follow along the course, and do the homework the same evening (when I was not behind).

Overall: This is an 8 unit class not without a reason. But if you commit to it and the strict schedule of doing the work that comes along with it, you'll make so much progress in a single term!
Dmitrii Yur'evich is an excellent language teacher and will ensure that you have a fun time learning Russian. He is incredibly understanding and has one of the most structured courses I've ever taken at Harvard. I can't recommend this course enough if you have the time to commit to it.
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! If you are at all interested in Russian, take this class absolutely!! Pastushenkov is such an amazing teacher and makes it so worth it to get up every day. Don't feel horrified by the M–F 9am schedule – it gets you into a great routine and gets your day started early. I made such strong relationships with all the other kids in my class – by the end of the semester, we were doing study sessions together in the library and hanging out outside of class. I learned more Russian than I thought was possible in a few months, and I am already enrolled in the Intermediate for next semester. This is an incredibly engaging and rewarding class. Make sure you have the time to commit to the courseload, though – they don't call it an intensive for nothing. It is the effort and time of two classes, that's why it's 8 credits. Make sure you are committed to spending the time to doing homeworks and studying for the tests, because it doesn't just pay off in grades, it pays off in how much of the language you actually end up learning.
This class is no joke. You will fully commit to this class with every minute of your free time. With that, this was by far my favorite class thus far. Do not take lightly, and make sure you do your homework on time.
It is VERY intense, but if you do your homework, show up to class, and buy in a little bit, you should be fine.
The teaching staff are wonderful. Just make sure you're committed to learning the language and spending multiple hours a night on homework and studying, but if you are, you will get so much out of it. Don't skip assignments and don't get behind. Study for the vocab quizzes every night!
It's not a joke. This class will consume your life. I am talking 15 hours a week outside of class. It is hard because it is a non–stop uphill battle that only slows down long enough to trick you into thinking you're not completely overwhelmed. You will have fun, but it will also be the only thing you have time to do outside of classes.
Took this without taking econ before and it was fine. Super useful for S&T
Take this course if you want to learn a new language and be challenged. The courseload is approximately 20 hours a week (I only took three classes this semester, which is why it was manageable for me,) but the content is very thorough and all the effort feels worthwhile in the end as you learn so much. By the end of the course, I found myself being able to understand small and simple conversations and even short (4–page+) stories, which was extremely satisfying. You also form genuine bonds with other students, so by the end it feels like a small family.
