This course is excellent. Whether it is your first time encountering Chinese history, or you are familiar with its 20th century, you will learn a lot from the readings and perspectives outlined, as well as dozens of random facts about the country. I recommend it 100%.
Truly an amazing course. There's so much that has happened to China in the 20th century and Professor Ghosh does a wonderful job guiding you through it. Everything from the class lectures, to the reading material, to the section component was so interesting. There truly feels like there's no busy work or fluff in this class, you will get alot out of it. Was my favorite class of the semester
Taking this course will not only offer you a valuable perspective on China and the key events of its twentieth century history, but it will also uniquely offer a complex vision of it. You will not just be studying the machinations of court/party politics, but will get the perspective of rural people, people in the western provinces, and the common folk. Additionally, Professor Ghosh is an amazing lecturer who offers nuanced and valuable insights on the big events of the twentieth century.
Very comprehensive and holistic view of modern China! Prof. Ghosh knows his stuff
Take this class! It's very much a traditional lecture course, but Professor Ghosh is a wonderful lecturer, and you'll come away having retained a vast trove of information /facts and analytical frameworks to work with moving forward. If Xiaorui is still the Teaching Fellow, you'll have wonderful sections as well — she is super smart, organized, and perceptive, and she provides really strong feedback.
This is a very nice example of a typical history survey course. If you're curious about Chinese history but know absolutely nothing about it, this is a great way to pique your interest and make you want to learn about earlier eras.
Fantastic course with engaging lectures from Professor Ghosh and a phenomenal job by the TF, Xiaorui. This course helped me learn most of what I know about China, offering a complete sense of the country's history from the end of the dynastic period through today's governance under Xi Jinping.

Took as an elective (considering a history secondary because of its light requirements) as a government concentrator.
Good class on Modern China, a lot packed into a semester (so some areas are glazed over); primary sources/photos/videos were great to see; research project was interesting experience (as a non–history/social science concentrator); very fair exams
This class gives a great sweep through modern Chinese history! At first, it was a bit overwhelming, coming from a background of little Chinese history, but by a couple of weeks in I was able to more clearly navigate lectures and readings to advance my knowledge. One of the highlights of the class, for me, was the Survey of China Mainland Press Project, where we got to look through the different books and do group research on a topic. Xiaorui was a WONDERFUL TF and went out of her way to answer questions, offer guidance, and make section as fun as possible. Besides being brilliant, she is always superbly kind and welcoming, without a doubt adding to the course with her intense knowledge. The review guides/outlines were immensely helpful in preparing for the exams and consolidating the topics covered.
