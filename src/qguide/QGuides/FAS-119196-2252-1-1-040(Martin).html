WARNING ! This class is SO HARD. Especially if you do not come from a math–intensive background. A lot of people in these QReports will say that it is fine and that the class was manageable, but these are people who have already taken multivariable calculus in the past. There are two clear distinctions of people in this class: (1) those who are doing well and do not study much for the exams and still ace them vs. (2) those who have not taken multivariable calculus before and are struggling.

My biggest advice is to find friends and understand that you will struggle. You will feel lost but push through it because I am assuming you don't plan on taking this class just for fun but you need it for your major. Talk to your TF (and make sure you like them and they like you) and ask your friends to study with you so you can all solve problems together.

The exams can also be extremely difficult. Midterm 2 for us was so bad. Most of me friends and I got horrible grades (aka < 60%) but it's consistently this trend of people who already know the material getting A's and us who studied for weeks (genuinely) and getting screwed over with the unrealistic expectations. I am writing this at a time where I haven't taken the final yet, but this class has been MISERABLE and it will be MISERABLE if you are like me, coming from a non–math extensive background and a high school with limited resources. Know you are not struggling alone and take up all the resources there are. Goodbye MATH 21A, I hated you.
