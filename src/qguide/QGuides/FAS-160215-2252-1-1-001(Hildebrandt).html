Comments
This is a wonderful seminar to take. Sabine Hildebrandt is a wonderful seminar leader, and is just the most knowledgeable person out there in regards to this subject. You will constantly be amazed by the stories she tells (and then she'll proceed to tell you that she is the one who first learned about it in an interview or in her research!). Take this if you are interested in the subject matter–– the content can be incredibly heavy sometimes, but that should be expected.

If you take this course, you MUST participate. Dr. Hildebrandt would often ask questions to no response. Please do the prep work (it took maybe an hour per week, if that) and please participate in class. She loves hearing what people have to say, and will sometimes even follow up after class to hear more about your thoughts. I've never met a professor so interested in what her students thought.

This course isn't just for pre–meds! No matter what you plan on studying, I would still recommend this seminar. The content, though sometimes heavy, will teach you so much.
If you have the opportunity to take this class, please do. Dr. Hildebrandt is extremely knowledgeable and cares a lot about the content. The content itself, while sometimes difficult to hear, is extremely interesting. The only work required for this class is weekly readings and a final paper on any topic related to medical ethics. This class was very eye opening and I would 100% recommend it to any student, pre–med or not.
This class is eye opening and deeply emotional and disturbing. Dr. Hildebrandt has such a unique perspective and is incredibly knowledgeable. This is is a very important class to take.
This course is really good if you need an easy class to balance out other challenging courses your taken in the same semester. There is barely any work. You just have to read a couple of short articles and maybe watch a video and prepare to talk about it in the next class. The engagement of this class is kind of what you and your classmates make of it. It can get boring if no one participates.
TAKE IT
