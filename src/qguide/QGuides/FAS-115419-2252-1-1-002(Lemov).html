Great class! Can sometimes be hard to sit through 2 hour plenary sessions, but Les and Rebecca make it fun, plus the content is fascinating. The assignments are manageable and fair, just BE ON TOP OF YOUR TIME (esp with archival research). Would recommend this class, an amazing intro to histsci!
This course is a tough one to figure out. The structure is a little strange, with overlapping assignments, but fully manageable. I highly recommend taking this course before HS100 if you like learning in lecture form, as a lot of the material overlaps. If you like being the section kid, then post–HS100 for sure. I think the problem with this course is that it accomplishes some goals and fails at others. The in the boxes project sets you up nicely for archival research, and I think it really changed the way that I approach archives at Harvard. But, the lectures are long and don't actually have that much content in them. It is a 2 hour plenary block where a class full of students pretends to have done a reading we didn't really do. The readings are easy to fake having done, so very few put in that extra effort. Grading wise, it is super fair, and the course staff is incredibly kind. The professors and TFs are AWESOME, and I really loved the final presentation aspect. It is definitely a low pressure environment in which to test research ideas and thoughts on your Junior paper topic.
I LOVE THIS CLASS!!! TAKE THIS CLASS! Les and Rebecca are the best and are genuinely so kind. I decided to take this class to "test the waters" on whether I wanted to be HistSci or not, but I definitely had a great time and the department felt like a cute little family. The assignments are very chill, just make sure to do them ahead of time (i.e. don't procrastinate). 10/10 would do again!
Prepare yourself for long lecture classes! Go to section and engage with the readings; they're very insightful.
