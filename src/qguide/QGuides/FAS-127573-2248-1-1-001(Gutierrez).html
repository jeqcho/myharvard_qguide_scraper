Take this class! I learned so much about the several injustices that businesses (predominantly American ones) do in LATAM while improving my Spanish.
Great class – a lot of discussion and reading but interesting material
This class was a joy to take, and I loved the readings even though they were sometimes long. You do readings twice a week and come to class to discuss them. Sometimes you will discuss the readings in small groups and then come together as a class to share what you talked about, and Profe Gutierrez would cover and misunderstandings and explain where necessary. You also have Talleres de escritura, which are basically a short 2–3 page essay where you cover what you learned in the past two weeks or so of reading and discussion done in class. I recommend you do the readings because it makes this process much easier. There is also the business project, which is a slideshow of research you do about a business of your choice. One more thing, there are speaking examenes in class, which are just 15 minute general yaps with a couple of your classmates and Profe Gutierrez about the readings you've done. I think she is very well spoken, and she speaks Mexican Spanish and she goes a little slower compared to some other Spanish speaking countries, which is great if you are unsure of your listening skills. As a freshman in this class with only juniors and seniors, it felt a little lonely, and I didn't make any lifelong friends, but everyone was a pleasure to talk with and have fun with in class. The class felt somewhat like a family because it was small, and everyone go to know each other from the beginning when we practiced saying everyone's names, and Profe Gutierrez genuinely cares about us as students and getting to know you.
This is a great course that builds on a lot of the themes from Spanish 40 and 50. It covers the history behind current events in Latin America very well, and its assignments strike a great balance between developing writing, grammatical, vocabulary, and speaking skills.
This is a great class to take to expand your vocabulary in spanish and learn about the history of latin america.
61N (business) is a fantastic class. The readings are on average really engaging and the class topics progressively get easier. The year starts out a lot more discussion based, which will take up the whole class, but towards the end there is a presentation basically every class, making the actual amount of work you do less. The presentations are also very engaging and interesting, and overall is a super great class
This course was great!! You learn a lot about the different human rights violations that multinational companies commit in Latin America. Truly very eye opening. In terms of assignments, the class does have a decent amount of work. You will have to do readings for each class so you can participate in the discussion, but they are usually very interesting. There are many assignments (writing workshops, oral tests, presentations) but this means each one lower–stakes and all the assignments are very doable. Assignments also usually incorporate a creative component which is nice. Overall, I thought the class was structured well and covered interesting material! I definitely recommend it!
This is a great class. Although it says ethics of business, and it is true that there is a disproportionate amount of time spent criticizing anything that makes money, you are presented with the history that underpins the events of today and come to understand many of the peculiarities to doing business in Latinoamerica. The professor (Gutierrez) is wonderful and you initiative to speak and think and learn is rewarded. Great class.
