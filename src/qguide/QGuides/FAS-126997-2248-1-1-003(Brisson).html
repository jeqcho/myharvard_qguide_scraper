Take French 40! This class is focused purely on movies!! There is a greater emphasis on communication and writing skills as you’ll have 3 presentations to complete and 3 compositions. But for the final project, you’ll get to film a short movie and have a movie screening on the last day of class. I genuinely enjoyed the writing process and being exposed to contemporary French films for the first time. My only complaint is that the Cahier is not updated nor presents relevant material. All the cahiers were updated up until unit 4. Because of that, I studied for the exams through using PowerPoints and the practice tests.
Great way to engage with francophone culture in a low–stress environment while improving writing, speaking, and listening skills
–pay attention to the grammar points in class (that's basically what the exams are on) and understand the film through the class discussions if the actual film made little–to–no–sense (unlikely, but it can happen because they're in french––the film's aren't too bad)
take it take it! Easy, fun, sometimes might be a little boring, but it's a good class to take for pleasure
French 30 was okay. I would recommend studying abroad instead if you can. This level of French seems less about learning new grammar and vocabulary, and more about putting it into practice speaking and listening.
