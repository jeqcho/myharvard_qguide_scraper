TLDR: If you're looking for a course to deeply explore cancer biology, especially from a molecular and mechanistic standpoint, this class may not meet your expectations. The material remains fairly general, and the lectures can be difficult to follow. However, if you're seeking a lighter intermediate–level biology course that’s less demanding in terms of content depth, this class might be a suitable option. Just be aware that the structure and clarity of the course may not be as strong as in other MCB classes.

The course was unstructured and lacked rigor, particularly when it comes to teaching the molecular mechanisms underlying cancer biology. Currently, the content tends to remain at a very surface level—for example, pathways are often described in vague terms like “promotes growth” without deeper explanation of the molecular players involved or how these pathways are regulated.

I found it difficult to follow the material during class, often leaving lectures more confused than informed. One recurring issue was a lack of preparation; lectures frequently felt like loosely organized commentary on the slides rather than a guided explanation of complex processes. While Professor Kunes is clearly a kind and well–meaning instructor, he did not seem fully confident in presenting up–to–date cancer biology content, and sometimes responded to student questions with “I don’t know” or misunderstood the question entirely. This undermined the learning experience.

The Wednesday workshop sessions also felt less effective than intended. Although the goal of incorporating active learning is appreciated, the execution could be improved. Many discussions were repetitive and often lacked clear direction, and the practice of having each group present the same concepts led to redundancy rather than reinforcement. A more focused, instructor–led review or structured literature discussion might be more valuable in that time.

Finally, several students experienced unnecessary stress due to grading inconsistencies or errors.

The course material itself was very interesting, and the papers are very enjoyable. The structure is awesome, and Sam is brilliant and beyond kind, baking us banana bread and coffee every Monday. He was extremely responsive to emails and uber helpful.

That said, I would strongly advise going to office hours to ensure that you are wording the assignments in the exact manner they expect you to do so. For the first part of the semester, the teaching staff was very pedantic about language, and if we failed to include a concept that seemed to be outside of the scope of the question, despite understanding the material, we would experience a significant point deduction (e.g., we had to know how to read a question and cover every single follow–up question they could ask about our answer that was not explicitly posed in the original phrasing). It was frustrating to really enjoy and understand the material but to be deducted for more than 50% of the points for describing a concept accurately without using a specific word they expected us to know to use. It felt more like we were being graded on rhetorical skills, rather than knowledge understanding.

Halfway through the course, they shifted gears, however, and adjusted the grading going forward, which was really great. They also regraded many of the assignments. Many of my classmates had major issues on how the midterm was graded, with massive point deductions for a single, specific word that was omitted, where a concept was phrased differently, for example. I will say that the professor singlehandedly regraded each exam after we all went to the staff and was very understanding about the way it was graded, giving us points back where it was otherwise unreasonable. It seemed there was an initial discrepancy between how harshly the TFs graded and what the professors wanted us to take away. For that reason, I would be a bit cautious, but the teaching staff really was receptive to feedback and cared about the class. They're very kind people. I believe in the next iteration it will be calibrated!
