Ana and Reese and David great. Prepare well for the presentations and you should be fine
Very chill and enjoyable courseProf. Liu is a great lecturer and you learn a lot about gene editing from someone who pioneered a lot of the modern technologies for it. It's pretty compelling through the first few weeks (you can essentially trace the history of the field and see the innovation build) and the final project is a fun chance to see what's currently going on/what might be built in the future.
this is a good class and it is interesting, but if your finals period is rough, or if you are busy for the weeks surrounding your assigned week, it is tough to meet with the TFs beforehand, which has an impact on the grading. There are only three assignments (assigned paper, then final presentation/paper), the material is genuinely fascinating, and the guest lectures are fantastic; you just have to do really well on the three assignments for you to have a good grade in the class.
This course is unique in the sense that it is a relatively low–effort (unless your paper presentation is coming up soon) and high–reward class. Although three hours on a Friday evening isn't the most ideal, this is one of the first courses I've taken in the sciences in which I just came to class to truly sit and listen. David is an great lecturer who has incredible experience in the field, and it's a great opportunity to learn about gene editing from it's start to modern day.
Awesome class, learned a lot and not at all stressful. If you get in, definitely take it! Final project allows for a lot of creativity and opportunity to delve further into an area of interest, and the TFs are incredibly helpful and engaged. Professor Liu is also much more accessible and friendly than I originally expectedhes great and genuinely cares a lot about his students despite probably having way bigger fish to fry, so that was really nice. He provided individual commentary on everyones presentations and gave excellent lectures. 10/10 would highly recommend to anyone in the gene editing space or interested in learning more about it.
This course is AMAZNG. David Liu is an incredible scientist and lecturer, and the course requires minimal work on a week to week basis (the only weeks you have to do work are the week you present your paper and then preparing for the final project). The TAs are also really great. This is the best class I've taken at Harvard, and if you're at all interested in chemical biology, gene editing, or science broadly, I highly recommend this class!
David is a great lecturer, and it was great to learn about the field (and his impact on the field!) from his perspective. Truly a privilege to take this course with him!
If you don't have an extremely robust chem background, you will be very confused and bored, but if you do, you'll adore this class.
