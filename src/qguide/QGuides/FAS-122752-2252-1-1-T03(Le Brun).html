What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This is a required class for the Economics Concentration, so you don't really have a choice whether to take it or not. But there are many options of topics, so you should be able to find one that is of interest to you. Besides for the final paper, the other papers are relatively short and don't take too much time. There are readings at least twice a week, but often times you can get the relevant information from the abstract/by skimming the results/conclusion sections. The final paper is definitely an intense assignment, but if you take the prospectus seriously, you will have at least a good outline going into reading week. It's a small class (no more than 8–12 people) so you can get a lot of support from your professor if you need it.
Unfortunately, this course is a requirement. The class meetings were a waste of time we just went over readings that were already assigned, but evidently half the class didn't read or understand. The assignments were condescending. The good news is that despite the excessive busy work, the course assignments can be completed relatively quickly.
