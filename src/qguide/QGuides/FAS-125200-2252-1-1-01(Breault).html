What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This class takes an amazing approach to studying cell therapies – you get both the expert science and patient perspectives which makes for a highly engaging and impactful experience
This is a great class to learn about super interesting research from leaders in the field, and the patient perspectives are amazing! Because there are so many topics covered (it was generally divided by organs/diseases, like brain, cardiovascular, diabetes, embryos, etc) , it means there's something for everyone. The paper also talks about some social/public policy concerns that have arisen with stem cell therapy, like equitable access, non–FDA approved therapies in the Caribbean, 'my sister's keeper'–esque family planning with IVF, and more, so it's not just hard science 24/7 which I appreciated. The weekly paper write–ups are graded semi–harshly with the goal of helping you refine your analysis of papers—I actually feel like I improved a lot in this aspect over the course of the semester and my grade was generally fine. The professors are both very well–connected in the medical/research/pharma world so the guest speakers are highly credentialed and super interesting. The con of this is that the professors are really busy and your actual learning (weekly paper roundtables, advice on your proposal) are all directed by a TF. It's harder to build a relationship with the professors. The patient stories are incredibly moving and diverse, and something that you'll really only otherwise get in medical school 'patient as professor' sessions. Take this class for a great survey in stem cell research!
Definitely take the course. The readings are kinda difficult but the patient experience is worth it. Hearing from them really challenges your perspective on life in a good and productive way.
This class is awesome! Definitely take if you are pre–med because you get to learn about the whole disease. The doctors that talk are super insightful and all the patients were awesome!
Definitely take this class if interested in medicine at all! It can have tough assignments and papers but overall so worth it and the TFs are there to help you!
This class will require work and effort to do well, but it is truly an incredible course. I loved how the class focuses on a different stem cell system or condition each class, and learning from the expertise of guest doctors/researchers was amazing. What is most special about this class is the conversations you will have with patients that live with the conditions we learn about – hearing the patient perspective is one of my most meaningful learning experiences in college. If you are premed or interested in stem cell research, you should definitely take this class. The assignments are graded somewhat harshly but it is manageable if you make an effort to meet with your TF. Some background experience with stem cell research is helpful.
START THE MIDTERM AND FINAL EARLIER THAN YOU THINK YOU NEED TIME FOR!!! And email your TFs any time you have a question!
TAKE THIS CLASS! I truly cannot recommend another class with such great enthusiasm. If you are involved in biological research, this course will show you the impact and historical challenges of what you are doing. If you would like to be a doctor, this course will teach you the power of medicine to change lives (not just fix medical issues!). This is one of the most incredible classes I have taken at Harvard.
Every week we talk about a different disease with a guest speaker expert to come in to lecture then they bring in a patient. So so interesting, relevant, and unique. Very med–school like and keeps the weeks interesting. You just read one paper that relates to the week's topic, then journal club in section to talk about the paper and techniques. The assignment is basically one "design your own" experiments paper like a research grant. Definitely need SCRB 10/50 for this, it would be pretty difficult otherwise, unless you have significant research experience. The TFs give very good and personalized feedback.
