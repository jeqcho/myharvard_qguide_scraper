I absolutely LOVED this course, as someone with no prior knowledge in finance and just wanted to learn about how it's done. Here's some feedback for you all prospective students! :)

[Lecture]:
It's nearly 3 hours, so it can be tiresome. Fortunately there is a break in the middle.
– Ask questions! I love the Professor's teaching style. He gave us pop tarts and you can tell he has such a love for what he does.

[Assignments]:
One assignment per week due before class, graded on completion and is relevant to what you learn during class.

[Exams]:
The course heavily leans on their exams. As long as you go through past practice exams, look through the assignments, you should be fine.

[Topics Covered]:
Financial Statements: balance sheets, income statements, cash flow from assets
Financial Ratios: short–term/long–term solvency ratios, turnover ratios, growth ratios, and more
Types of Capital: Bonds with coupons, Annuities, Perpetuities, and Stocks with dividends
How to determine if a project you're going to invest in will be profitable
How to determine/predict future cash flows for a project using pro forma statements
Risk and return: beta volatility in assets, debt, and equity
Financial Policies for companies: leverage policy, dividend payout policy
Entrepreneurial funding
