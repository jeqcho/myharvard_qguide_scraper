What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Make SURE to do every pset on time and to study for tests in advance!
This class is designed for you to succeed so all that you have to do is do what they tell you to – go to MQC and office hours and most importantly, talk to other people in the class and make a set group to study and work with!
Definitely keep up with the psets, since a lot of the material is accumulative. Attend MQC and ask questions after class.
If you don't like a lecture style class this is perfect although lowkey 3 psets a week can be a lot and visualizing graphs is not fun but if you have to take multi this is a good option.
I originally took Multivariable Calculus at my local community college in high school and found the course here to be significantly better. Taking Math 21a allowed me to have a solid understanding of the content, and I appreciate the amount of effort put into teaching the material. It meets 3 times a week in small class sections, which is by far the best part of the course. Your TF will lecture, and you solve problems with others at your tables or on the chalkboards in small groups – repeating this with the next iteration of new content multiple times in each lecture. This makes it helpful to follow and understand the material.

I think you only need AP Calculus AB (or Math 1a) and some self–studying of integration by parts to take this course. It is not hard as long as you take the time to understand the material before the next lecture. The most important piece of advice I have is to not fall behind!

The midterm exams were very fair. I never left feeling like I had not studied enough. However, the PSETS and AI Assignments, which I believe were new this semester, were my least favorite part of the course. I am very detail–oriented and try to understand everything for an easier study period when exams come up. As a consequence for me, I spent 4–5 hours on average for most of the PSETS in this course. Some people I know were able to get them done faster. The AI assignments were problematic because the AI tutor simply refuses to tell you if you are on the right track with your answers.

Overall, this course is great!
Take it but keep in mind the amount of psets per week and sections.
