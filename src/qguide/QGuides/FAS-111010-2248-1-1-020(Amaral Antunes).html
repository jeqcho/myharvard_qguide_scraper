Strongly recommend taking this course! MQC is a very helpful tool to be successful with the course and collaborate with other students on PSETs. Reasonable work load. You are given all the tools you need to be successful in the class!
If you put the time in and concentrate and go to office hours it is not that difficult if you have been placed to the class. The math placement tests seem to be very accurate. Don't leave the psets till the last minute and find friends to study with as you are allowed to collaborate to a reasonable point.
This is a course for those with a background in AP Calculus AB or BC. You should undoubtedly go to MQC every time a PSet is assigned. MQC is the best resource for this course and is a fantastic aid for PSets. The course material is occasionally abstract, but in the end, you will have a deep knowledge of integration, series, and differential equations. Stick with it and you will do well. Study "Nathan's Notes" before exams.
I would say that it is very important to pay attention in class. Give yourself ample time to do the PSETs, and if you're struggling in the course, MCQ and office hours can be helpful. When reviewing for midterms/final, give yourself plenty of time to review and grade them. As long as you understand the basic methodology of solving a certain type of problem, you will likely be able to solve anything you encounter on a test.
The class can get really annoying at times, but just do the PSETs early and go to office hours.
