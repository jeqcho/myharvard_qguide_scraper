There could truly not be a better decision in your course selection than taking any class with Bohdan––literally any class he teaches will be the most amazing class you have ever taken. I have never had a teacher who cares more deeply for his students. Almost all of the students were from non–humanities majors and yet he generated so much enthusiasm and excitement that made the material equally fascinating for engineers and math students, as for humanities majors. every class the students would stay an extra 30 minutes voluntarily PLUS time for questions at the end. That is how amazing Bohdan is as a lecturer. It is mesmerizing to watch how he weaves so many decades, cultures, and interests into his lectures while incorporating a vibrant discussion component and our own interests. If you dont take this class, at least meet with Bohdan because in that meeting you will learn so much and feel motivated to be in college again! Classes like this are why we are at Harvard!
Bohdan’s course gives an excellent overview of the development of Ukrainian literature and provides important context for the Russo–Ukrainian war. Although there is quite a bit of historical–literary ground to cover in 12 weeks, Bohdan makes the material interesting and accessible for newcomers to Slavic/Ukrainian Studies as well as for those who have studied the region for a while. Overall, Bohdan is a brilliant professor who genuinely cares about his students, and I highly recommend taking the course. Sign up to read works of literature that never made it into the canon of “great East European literature” because they were suppressed!!
This is a great class, especially if you're interested in Ukrainian culture and haven't had a chance to explore it yet. The best part is definitely Prof. Tokarsky's endless enthusiasm and support.
TAKE ANYTHING THAT BOHDAN TEACHES. Yep, just go ahead and click enroll. You won't regret it, I promise. (Even if it's your 5th course) And if there is a time conflict this semester, make it a priority to take his course next semester (or, better yet, make the conflict go away). Taking a course with Bohdan is truly a bucket list item. Genuinely the best professor ever. He brings such enthusiasm and a wealth of knowledge to every class that it's impossible to leave each class not suddenly finding a new interest you never knew you had. I'm not even a slavic major and I took this course on a complete whim after having a random conversation with him –– best. decision. ever.
He really meets you where you are (our class had two engineering majors and a chem major but then also a Slavic grad student and a Ukrainian student and he really leveled the playing field for everyone). This course was genuinely the highlight of every week and literally my only complaint is that it didn't meet more because we always had more to talk about. Like he would dismiss class, and one person who had somewhere to go would leave, but everyone else would stay back and chat for half an hour every week.
He is so accessible in office hours if you want more feedback or just want to chat. He knows so much about Ukrainian literature and history –– he personally translated much of what we read. And on the off chance he doesn't know the answer to one of your questions, he writes it down and comes to you next week with the answer. He brought one of the authors we read (who is also one of the most famous living Ukrainian writers and performed at the Grammys) to Cambridge and invited us all to dinner with her.
He gives super detailed feedback and works with you every step of the way on your final paper. He also asks every week what the rest of your workload looks like and he makes sure to adjust the work so that he's not assigning a lot during those couple weeks in the semester when everyone else is going crazy. I would take this course again in a heartbeat if I could. He opened my eyes to a whole new world. Bohdan proves that you can be both an expert in your field and a great professor. He is what I want my tuition going to and Harvard needs to hurry up and give him tenure ASAP before someone else poaches him. [goes to thesaurus.com to find more positive adjectives] Life–changing. Transcendent. Perfection. [For context: I'm not a Slavic major, and my Q responses are usually somewhat critical].
Seriously, what are you still doing reading this review? Just close this tab and go enroll in his course.
