This class is very teacher–dependent. Depending on who leads your section, the difficulty of the class and grading changes. I did not have a great experience in this course, but others I know did.
Great class to fulfill the language requirement. Not too difficult at all if you took Spanish 10.
This class is great if you've taken Spanish in high school or Spanish 10, but make sure to refresh your Spanish before you take it because they don't go over simple phrases, numbers, colors, clothing etc. that is all expected for you to know. The tests are pretty challenging, especially the writing portions. The projects are great to help your grade and engage with the material. Talk Abroad is also great for practicing your Spanish with a native speaker, and you only have to submit a report about the conversation you had. Class three times a week is a lot, but it's definitely necessary because you need to be consistently conversing in Spanish to really get good at it. Overall, be prepared to put in the work, but your Spanish will improve a lot.
Its a good beginners Spanish class that helps you learn more about the language and culture. However, the tests can be a bit difficult and the projects and talk abroads at some points were overwhelming considering the timeline.
Have fun on the projects in this class and be creative and interact with your classmate and the class will be and enjoyable experience
