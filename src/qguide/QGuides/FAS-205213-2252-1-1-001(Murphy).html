What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I know of two ways people did this class:

1) Studying, doing the paper readings every week, thinking hard about the weekly quiz questions, locking in when need be

2) ChatGPTing the quiz questions every week, doing basically nothing, freaking out come time for the final project, and absolutely grinding as much RL as possible in order to have something good for the poster presentation.

I took the second approach, though I'm very aware of people who did the first. Main takeaway: FOR BOTH APPROACHES, YOU WILL LEARN A LOT OF RL IN THIS CLASS.

I went back and looked through the papers I was supposed to have read a couple months ago, and I understood them! The RL made sense! I know how the various algorithms work, and why some might be preferable over others for different tasks! It all worked!

Morel of the story: Take this class! You will learn RL one way or another
Not a bad option for a graduate statistics class, although I would recommend starting the project earlier, as it can be more time consuming than you would expect. RL background/interest would be helpful for understanding concepts better though, as I would've found the class much more confusing without taking CS 184 first.
I recommend you take this course if you are interested in reinforcement learning. The lectures cover complicated content, but Professor Murphy is enthusiastic and helpful, and the course does not have that much homework, beyond daily class quizzes (less work than they sound) and a final project. Holistically, if you are a grad student and reinforcement learning is even remotely relevant to you, I strongly recommend you take this course. You will learn a lot and still have time for research.
Start the project as early as possible. I was still generating results the day the paper was due and that could have been avoided.
This is a chill course, as it only has 2 weekly quizzes and no assignments. The quizzes are usually not too bad, although sometimes it can be a bit unclear what they are looking for. Most of the work I did for the course was for the final project. I didn't really learn much prior to the project because you can do fine on the quizzes with only a high level knowledge of what's going on. The project was fun and a good learning experience. Grading also seemed chill for it. Overall would recommend this course if you want to do an RL project!
