What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class!! It is so so worth it. I was a bit apprehensive before I took it because I wasn’t sure if I remembered enough theory, but the teaching staff does a great job engaging everyone and making sure everyone is on board. We cover so much music and spend most of the time listening, writing, and analyzing music. I looked forward to class every week and absolutely loved the lectures and sections. The final composition was incredibly rewarding and it was amazing to hear our own pieces performed by the Parker Quartet.
This is your chance to compose a string quartet and have it performed live! As a person who does have a music theory background but haven't looked back at it for years, this course was so much fun! I was able to solidify the knowledge on music theory already had and successfully compose a decent string quartet. This course is totally worth it – this is your sign to take it! Plus you don't have to be a music major to take it! (I am an Economics/English concentrator) If you enjoy listening to classical music, this is also the course for you!
Take this class! Professor Rehding is an engaging and dynamic teacher who is always super kind and funny in lecture, and the sections are so much fun working on music with a small group of people. I was nervous about my music background going into it but I didn't need to be, the TFs are super supportive throughout. If you are a music concentrator or secondary it is a must, and if you just enjoy music I would recommend taking it as an elective too!
THIS WAS A PHENOMENAL MUSIC THEORY COURSE!!! If you need to fulfill ANY music theory requirement for the music concentration / secondary, or even if you're just curious about taking music theory as an elective, I strongly strongly recommend this course. I do NOT think that 51a is actually necessary as a prerequisite as long as you're a little bit familiar with roman numeral analysis and basic voice leading rules, and willing to work quickly to catch up during the accelerated review of 51a material at the beginning of the class. In fact, if I could go back and do things over, I would have taken this class without taking 51a first.

Professor Rehding is an outstanding lecturer and you can tell he loves to teach this class. I found myself being able to put words to patterns I'd noticed in classical music (especially classical era classical music, but even other eras) that I'd never understood were formally analyzed. I have a newfound appreciation for and genuinely enjoy listening to classical music, string quartets, piano sonatas, and concertos. The curriculum is structured very logically and each week builds very naturally on what came before it. Lecture felt a bit overwhelming at times, but having the two small sections each week to dive into examples with the TFs (who were also wonderful) really helped to reinforce the lecture material. And, of course, the opportunity to have your final composition project performed by the Parker String Quartet is unique and incredible.

10/10, cannot recommend strongly enough to anyone interested in a classical music theory course.
