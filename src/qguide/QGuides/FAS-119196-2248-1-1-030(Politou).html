This course is not bad at all if you make sure you do not fall behind on the materials. If you need extra help, MQC and Office Hours should be enough to catch you up. The practice tests are harder than the actual exams so don't stress if you do poorly on the practices.
A lot of material is covered in this class. Make sure to pay attention to section and to learn the concepts to be able to apply them to things.
Hello future Math 21a students! If you are thinking of taking Math 21a, but are unsure of whether you're a good "fit" for the course, ask yourself this: Are you willing to learn and work hard? Do you want to gain a glimpse at what Applied Math might look like? Do you want to see cool 3D shapes (and find the volume or surface area of such shapes)? If the answer to these questions is yes, then I would recommend that you give this course a shot! The first few PSets will allow you to adjust to college math (if you're a first year), and with MQC and your TF/CAs at your side, there's nothing stopping you from succeeding in this class. :)

Mine was absolutely amazing and class felt like a social each time. Overall great class.
