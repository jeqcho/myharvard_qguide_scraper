Outstanding class. Professor loves the material and is very knowledgable
Take this class! Dr. Hersch is a really engaging and extremely knowledgeable lecturer, and the essays were fun to write.
If you’re considering this course to learn about American technology and warfare, be prepared for significant disappointments. While the topic is fascinating and has great potential, the course falls far short of delivering a meaningful or rigorous learning experience due to politically charged lectures, poorly constructed course materials, and insufficient feedback on assignments.

The lectures, while occasionally interesting, frequently strayed far from the course’s stated focus. Topics like Donald Trump, the January 6th riots, the Second Amendment, gun violence, and who should or shouldn’t have access to guns were brought up more than once, reflecting the professor’s personal political opinions rather than offering balanced or relevant historical analysis. The professor consistently got sidetracked, focusing more on conveying political views than sticking to the course material. A particularly egregious example was the final lecture, which was partially devoted to critiquing Richard Nixon before devolving into a highly partisan critique of Donald Trump. If you’re someone who dislikes Trump and want to hear a professor ridicule him for an entire lecture, then this class is for you.

You should also be prepared for some of the worst and most hypocritical course materials. The professor relied on their own draft textbook as the primary source for the course. Unfortunately, this draft was not peer–reviewed, lacked citations entirely, and contained clear political biases and subjective opinions about history. As students, we are expected to provide properly sourced and balanced arguments in our work, yet the course materials written by the professor failed to meet those same basic academic standards. If you want to be graded by a professor who disregards academic integrity and standards while holding you accountable to them, then this is the class for you.

The assignments, while engaging in topic, were undermined by poor feedback. Papers often took an unreasonably long time to be returned, and when feedback did arrive, it was minimal and unhelpful. The professor was entirely absent from this process, leaving the teaching fellow as the sole source of comments. If you’re not concerned about improving your writing or receiving constructive criticism, then this is the class for you.

While this course is easy to get through and covers some interesting topics, don’t expect to gain much in terms of meaningful knowledge or skills. This class felt more like a cleverly disguised platform for the professor to vent political rhetoric to a captive audience unlikely to challenge him. If you enjoy being talked at for two hours and forty–five minutes a week, much of which is historically skewed and slanted towards anti–American ideologies, then you’ve found the perfect class. For anyone else, avoid this course. You deserve better.
Professor Hersch is a fascinating lecturer – he is deeply knowledgeable and passionate about the material and brings it to life each class, including with hands–on activities and great anecdotes. He balances this with primary source films too, so it is not only a three hour lecture. In addition, Professor Hersch maintained an awareness of the difficulty of this material and its place in society, and encouraged us to think in the same way. I strongly recommend this course.
TAKE THIS CLASS!! It is so interesting and Dr. Hersch is a great lecturer. I left each lecture somehow energized at the end of a long day.
If you are a person that enjoys military history in passing, I would recommend this course – you can definitely learn a lot! However, if you are an avid military history enjoyer, you might consider other classes. You might find certain talking points interesting, but you will largely know the material already.
The material was accessible and fascinating. Lectures were deeply engaging and involved. The professor frequently brought weapon replicas to aid in discussions and taught us some obscure combat technique that may prove useful in the case of an apocalypse. There was a palpable leftward slant, and little effort was made to include any material that would contradict the narrative being taught.
tl;dr highly recommend this course. Really I'd recommend any course Hersch teaches, if you're looking for something to check out in the HistSci department.
