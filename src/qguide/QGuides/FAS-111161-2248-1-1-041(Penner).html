What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Although the class is intro–level it is by no means easy. Was a difficult class that required a lot of time commitment and work outside of class. PSets take a long time, exams are very hard, and workshop is an extra burden. Ming Li was a super–good TF and explained concepts super well. Just be prepared for the amount of time this class takes.
I would say that it is tough and if you do not like math and doing it almost everyday, do not take this course.
Start your psets early!! Also go to office hours/MQC for help the day that the pset is given out if possible. Additionally, the practice tests and quizzes are always harder than the test itself so take a deep breath and know it will be okay.
This class is so much work compared to every other class you will likely take fall of your freshman year. Check if your intended concentration requires a math class. If not, definitely do not take this class. If it does require math, this class is managable and you can get and A if you really put in the work.
I strongly recommend you take this course if you want a solid introduction to math. The pace is wonderful–– not too fast or slow, the TFs explain why the math you do is the way it is and where it comes from, not just how to do it or why. The classroom is also smaller and more interactive, so you have more of a hand in your learning. For someone who gets sleepy easily in class and is over lecture–style classes, this course was a breath of fresh air. Yes, it's super time–consuming, but very worth your while. Other random thing: MQC on top! Very helpful and great space for pset help (you can finish your whole pset there in one sitting). TLDR: Math MA is great and you should take it–– you will *not* regret it!
