This class made me love math. I thought math was my least favorite subject and was dreading taking this class, but I genuinely enjoyed this course so much that I enrolled in two more math classes next semester. If you are scared to take it because you feel that your calculus skills aren't good enough, they are more than likely sufficient. I took Calc BC in my senior year of high school, and took this course my sophomore spring as my first math class at Harvard. I was terrified and was confident that I would need to drop down to Math 1b; however, the content is taught so incredibly that I did really well in the class. I am not amazing at math, and, honestly, I am probably a little bit worse than the average Harvard student, but I am finishing the class with a good grade and a new interest in math. I can't recommend this course enough.
Three problem sets a week + three mandatory 75–min sections per week + one weekly AI assignment is a lot to keep track of and solve. It can feel quite hand–holdy at times, especially in section. The teaching team is very supportive and the class is not very difficult to do well in.
Do all the psets, and don't be discouraged by bad grades. School is about learning, not numbers (even in math class) :)
Math 21a is a wonderful course, and Amadeus was one of the best instructors I've had at Harvard so far! The interactive and clear teaching style made taking Math 21a a much nicer experience than Math 22a (linear algebra with proofs), which I ended up almost entirely self–studying due to the ineffective lectures. (That said, if you're considering 22a, I've heard Dr. Wood has since gotten better, and I did appreciate the experience with proofs.) The 3–4 psets a week can get tiring, but they are quite reasonable and only took me about an hour to complete. Overall, this course will give you a strong foundation in multi–variable calculus.
I cannot tell you how much I love this course. It is so well structured! If you are in a section with Amadeus, that's a +++ bonus. He is the GOAT. People will say that 3 psets a week is a bit too much, but honestly, they are not very long, and they help you use the materials you learned in the class immediately. Due to this, I never really felt lost in the class. If you are taking 25 or 55, go for it. But, take 21 if you are thinking of 22. 22 is not it, very bad class.
Make sure you genuinely put time into this class because if you don't, you will be cooked. I have trouble thinking in 3D and got a little traumatized by this class. I also come from a weak math background and so many kids in the class were already familiar with the content so I felt really behind all the time on top of the fact that it moves fast with 3 psets/week, but you will be okay.
TAKE THIS COURSE! I was uncertain "multivariable calculus" coming in, but this class hugely increased by interest in math. It's definitely challenging with three p–sets per week, but I see it as the "perfect Harvard course" that will challenge you but be extremely rewarding.
Take this class! It's taught very well (depending on your TF). Amadeus is really great, and I think the content is actually really interesting and fun to learn. Sections were super effective and although it's 3 psets a week, you can finish one in less than 2 hours. If you want to learn multivariable calculus, this is such a great class. Definitely take advantage of all the practice materials when exams come around. The first midterm was pretty chill and the second one was slightly harder. Start studying relatively early and take all the practice exams, and you will be fine.
Take this course! Genuinely a great intro to multi, and if you already took multi, it is still definitely worth taking. I took multi in high school at a community college, and I can say with confidence that I have zero regrets revisiting concepts with this course. The theoretical/derivation approach is a incredible experience that adds so much to understanding the material.
If you are going to take this test and you are put in Amadeus' section you will probably cook on the exams as long as you really try and pay attention. Lowkey I cooked on the second exam only because I had been really been paying attention and class and I had actively tried to understand where each concept came from and what ideas it was building off of. Also find a p–set buddy because those 3 p–sets a week ain't no joke.
If you can, take it with Amadeus!
