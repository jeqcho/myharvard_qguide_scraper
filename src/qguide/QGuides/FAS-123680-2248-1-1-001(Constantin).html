It's a good foundational calculus class.
Look into other options. Three psets a week is too many if you're not passionate about math. You will seldom know peace. If you have no calculus background, take MA. This course moves way too fast for someone without some calculus background to keep up. Most people in my section had some calculus background and agreed with me on this point. The Math Question Center, helpful in theory, is crowded and frustrating in practice, so don't expect it to always come in clutch for you. Expect exams beyond the difficulty the course demands, and limited instruction from actual people (teaching yourself with premade worksheets is better, we seem to have decided?)
Even if you took calculus in high school and did very well in it, listen to me: this class will suck up your time. Prepare to spend a solid few hours on the psets. Math at Harvard is incredible if you're obsessed with math—otherwise, trust me, your energy is better placed elsewhere. If this is a concentration requirement for you, you'll be just fine, but if it's not, I'd highly recommend looking elsewhere.
Really great class in terms of structure and organization. Alex, Yana and Sina are all amazing instructors and helped tons during Office Hours! There is a mini–exam and 2 midterms which seems like a lot, but really gets you prepared for the final which is cumulative of the whole course content. The 3 psets a week is really the only downside to this class. You will be putting in a lot of time, especially if you want to do well on your psets, but they'll start to take less and less time as you go through the semester. GO TO OFFICE HOURS AND MQC if you have questions/are unsure about something. Get started on your psets as soon as you can so you're not going to the MQC at 10pm. Make sure to review the worksheets you do in class + the practice midterms/finals, I found those to be the most useful preparation.
Use office hours and MQC sessions as much as possible. Do not go to get the answer but to confirm your answer or get help if you're stuck.
If you're debating between 1a and MA, I think you should take this class if you have already had a year of calc in high school because they will cover topics very quickly and it can be tough to newly learn all those topics in semester rather than a year with the MA/B set up.

But even though the course covers topics quickly, you will still have a deep understanding of them by the end of this course.
The course is pretty fast paced, and I think I would have been confused without having taken calculus in high school. That said, I don't have a super strong math background and didn't find anything too difficult. Three psets a week can be challenging to adjust to at first, but start them early and you'll be fine! Don't hesitate to use Office Hours and the MQC – just make sure you learn from them instead of just copying down answers.
I would tell future students to make sure they feel comfortable with everything in the class if they want to take it. Before the time to drop a class or switch sections is over, make sure you feel comfortable with the material and learning style.
Not really an intro to calc class. More of a refresher and extension
I used to love math. Now, I don't. Is this correlation or causation? I'll tell you next semester after I take stat. Really, this course is fine. You MUST have a calculus background to succeed in this course. If you don't, you'll leave every class feeling confused. The math department does a great job teaching effective reasoning, but it doesn't do a great job at teaching math. I spent a large amount of time on Psets and at the math question center, and I still felt quite confused after most classes. Much of the material ends up being self–taught, so prepare yourself for that. Take this course if you need a math requirement, but prepare to hate math in a few months.
If you’re considering this class, make sure you have a very strong pre–calc background, as the course assumes you already know topics like logarithms, trigonometry, and lots of algebra. If you’re not confident in these areas, you might want to take Math Ma. You’ll have 3 problem sets per week, each taking 2–4 hours to complete, and you don't have access to the grading rubrics, so to get full marks, you’ll likely need to use the MQC, where the CAs can review your work and point out any issues (no matter how confident you are of your own work, there are always small details that you will miss).

The exams are fair in terms of difficulty, but the time is very tight. You need to be well–prepared, focus carefully, and avoid mistakes because there’s usually no time to redo questions. However, they do accept partial work on exams, so write EVERYTHING you know about a problem, even if you can’t solve it completely. Finally, be ready to self–learn a lot, as the course requires a good deal of independent study.
I am so happy that I chose to take this course! I was placed in Math MA for both the online placement and the skill check but I decided to trust my gut and take this course and it was the best decision. For context, I took AP Calc AB and I got a 3 on the exam. If you have any prior calc experience, you should be completely fine to take this class instead of Math M. They will try to scare you into switching into MA at the beginning of the course but don't be deterred.
Speaking of the beginning of the course, the first three weeks are absolutely the roughest. Three psets a week is exhausting but you will get used to it! Also, they take the most points off of the psets in the beginning so don't let that discourage you either. Focus on learning how your specific CAs grade and what they take points off for. The key to getting full points on the psets is either going to the mqc (ideally on the night that the pset is assigned, not when its due) or having a really solid pset group.
Exams aren't terrible, the questions are always easier versions of pset problems. Your biggest enemy will be time. The group portions of the midterms were awful though but you can consult with other groups so you should be able to get full points no matter what.
Overall, only take this class if you have to because it is a large time commitment. There are less time consuming ways to fulfill your distributions. But if you have to take it for your concentration, you can do it don't be discouraged!!
