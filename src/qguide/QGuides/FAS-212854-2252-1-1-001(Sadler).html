This is a fun course. It will not be a gem gened because it requires a good amount of work. Sometimes you have to do the experiments outside of class, which requires some time, and a bunch of readings. However, if you actually engage it will be a fun class and you will learn a lot. As a science concentrator I enjoyed being able to see science from a more simple perspective, but also one that felt more profound, as we took the time to learn the history and the why behind the history of science and all the experiments we did. It's a fun clas, you should take it, but be prepared to engage. Phil is a really sweet professor and he likes to have students be engaged.
The class is great. However, too much repetition. Topic would be introduced on Tuesday, experiment for it on Thursday, section discussion, PSET, and then Tuesday again recapping the topic. it made the class at some points a bit boring because we were hearing the same information a lot of times. Sections should be taken out of the course, I didn't find them too engaging. However, the teaching team is AMAZING!!
This is a great course, but honestly a decent amount of work for a GENED. Each PSET is probably minimum 2–3 hours + experiment, and there's a final project and final exam, which is pretty overkill in my opinion. Staff is easy on grading, but the work itself is enough to second–guess whether it is a gem or not.
this class is pretty fun and chill, but you won't actually learn that much (besides a few pretty cool units). it's honestly just like a high school class. the pset each week takes a few hours and boils down to some reading questions and some experimenting with toys. the course staff are also very chill (i lowkey was late to every class), and the latework policy is almost absent. i especially recommend this class if you want to get ed secondary credits for your gened, but there's not a big reason to take it if your science gened is already fulfilled.
A good class for those with an interest in the history of science. Lots of hands–on projects which were the biggest highlight of the course (build a motor, make a microscope, etc.). After having spent a couple semesters in heavy science classes and research labs, this course definitely helped rekindle my love for science and how it can be taught in an accessible, interactive way for people from all backgrounds (STEM and non–STEM). However, psets definitely took some time to do (~1–2 hours), and readings were a little heavy at times. The final project was interesting and relatively fun, but did take up a significant amount of time and could have been run a lot smoother with better support from the staff. That being said, staff were always very friendly and supportive, and have a genuine interest in the material and your questions. If you are willing to put in the work on the weekly psets and the final project, and enjoy hands–on activities, this is a good gened for the science requirement.
Okay, many reports say this class is a g*m. While this may be true, it is only true in the sense that if you do the work, you will receive a good grade. I feel that many people I spoke to believed that g*m meant no work=good grades. This is untrue. You must do the work. Phil is quite sweet. He has fun stories and is very knowledgeable.

I also distinctly remember that he said he was one of the only, if not only, faculty members in the FAS that had a doctorate in education. I very much appreciated this because I believe it shows he truly enjoys teaching rather than being at Harvard for research alone.
This GenEd is momentarily very hard but besides those weekly nights, it’s a very manageable class. My peers were awesome and I constantly learned from their comments and Phil’s BBQ at the end of the year was so good
I personally didn't love quite how hands on the homeworks were but I think it is always good to get out of your comfort zone and approach learning with an open mind. Phil and Max are the best, if nothing else it was so worthwhile to meet the two of them and to take a course with them. Few people will be so happy to teach and so happy to discuss the course material.

it is an amazing course please do take it
Great experiments is AWESOME. Phil (the course head) is great. And all the other TFs are also super enthusiastic during class and lab. this course is an absolute gem. Seriously, take it. It is 1 "pset" a week which is basically answering questions based on a reading about an experiment. And then there was a final project which involved making a poster board on a topic of your choosing and a take home final. I put in about 2–2.5 hours a week on the pset and received very high grades. If you put in moderate effort, you should expect to do very well in this course. I took this with 3 other very heavy stem courses this semester, and this class was just what I needed. Section and lab 2x a week is so chill and enjoyable. You just sit and do fun experiments that are high key really cool.
TAKE THIS COURSE if you have the chance! I absolutely loved it, what an incredible teaching staff – Phil + Max are the absolute best. It genuinely rekindled the curiosity I had for science and experimentation as a kid. Some of experiments were just pure fun, and you end up filling your entire room with all of the materials. As a bonus, there's a BBQ at the end of the semester after final project presentations. Taking any other Gened STS would be a mistake...this is it.
