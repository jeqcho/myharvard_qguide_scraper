This class is incredible and I highly recommend. I took this class for fun and could not be happier with that decision. Professor Rhyder teaches with such passion for the topic and is an incredible professor. I really enjoyed getting to know Professor Rhyder. Additionally, there is little work (only 2–3 page reading response once a week) and then the final can be a bit of memorization. This class is fun and engaging, with a cooking class, a trip to the MFA, and many other fun class activities. Cannot recommend this course enough.
**Incredible class!** Both professor Rhyder and TF Andrew Hile are incredibly passionate about the subject matter and I was able to learn an incredible amount of new information, as well as new perspectives to analyze ancient texts through. The mix of theological, anthropological, archeological, and historical sources made the course more fluid which allowed for more vibrant discussions. Anyone with an interest in Food History or Abrahamic religions would be missing out if they skipped this course.
The content is really interesting, but it may be boring at the start. It's not the easiest course I've ever taken (you have to put in some work for the tutor bots and show up to class having done the readings), but it is easy enough that you can get by with minimal effort.
I loved this course!!! So interesting and manageable. Assignments are always given at same time and the course is super organised. We got to visit museums and it was far more interactive than any other course I have taken. Julia was a wonderful professor and was so interesting to listen to and I really hope that Andrew stays on as a TF because he was great!!!!
This class is great for anyone who wants a broad and hands on experience about food in the ancient Near East.
Take the class if you are genuinely interested in the topics presented. If you are not interested, the class will feel long and draining. Professor Rhyder and Andrew are both awesome instructors.
I HIGHLY recommend this class to all students, even those not particularly interested in a Near Eastern Civilization concentration or secondary. This class was outstanding. Professor Rhyder is a great professor and explains course content very well. The readings and lectures are very interesting and section is amazing, short, and draws on course concepts in an interactive way. The assignments are not too challenging which makes them exciting. This class is amazing and if you put in the work, you will get a good grade.
This course was great but the title is a bit misleading. I assumed that we would be learning about food in the entire bible. Instead, we only focused on the Hebrew Bible.
