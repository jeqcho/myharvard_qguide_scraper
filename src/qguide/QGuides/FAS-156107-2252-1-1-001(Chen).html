What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I enjoyed this class! I thought it was a cool, niche area to explore as an extra class during my final semester. I appreciated the balance of technical content–it wasn’t too overwhelming, which made the course accessible to students from a wide range of academic backgrounds. We really covered a broad spectrum of hazards and methods to analyze them. My main piece of feedback is to consider reducing the number of research papers assigned, as reading and writing reflections every week started to become tedious. Instead, I would strongly recommend incorporating discussions on current events. There were so many instances throughout the semester when I saw hurricanes, wildfires, policy changes, etc. in the news that were directly relevant to this class. I think class participation and engagement would greatly increase since current events are more relatable than research papers about GIS methods, and it would make the course material feel more timely and impactful.
This is a beneficial class for mny students going into place0based design, social impact, etc.
Take this class if you are interested in GIS. My only reservation about this class is the discussion component. It is very difficult to have discussions on the readings we read for class.
This course is great! One of the few courses where you can learn such a significant tangible skill (that helps with both research skills and employment prospects) with such straightforward work. The teaching in this course and the materials that the teaching team puts together are truly top notch, and as long as you have at least a basic understanding of ArcGIS Pro and can navigate basic technology, you will definitely learn a lot in this course. Professor Chen is also an amazing educator. She is not only extremely capable in her field, but also very skilled at transferring her expertise to students who wish to learn. This is also a cool course to meet students from all of the schools at Harvard: there are students from the college, GSD, HKS, public health school, phd students, and more. Definitely recommend this course! Start thinking about final projects early too ––– it's possible to tie in almost any interest, but the final project goes better if you can work out where to get data early because the poster presentation comes up quickly and the deadline for that is very firm.
Take this class! The instructor delivers effective, straightforward lectures and is genuinely interested in student's learning.
