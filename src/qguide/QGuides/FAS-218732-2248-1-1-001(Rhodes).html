Honestly, I wish I could recommend this class, but the discussion is so strained. The Prof interjects too much into the conversation, and there is not enough to debate or discuss. The class generally had a unitary perspective on the subject of the week, and we just went over points in the readings.
This course is amazing and will broaden your understanding of Africa. You will complete a separate research project on an African nation of your choice, and then relate it to what the other students are studying. I learned a lot and the discussions were lively every day. Come prepared to class.
Great course overall. There were 3 short papers and a final paper, which helps space out your work, and this makes it better than over GOV94s. However, Professor Rhodes is often slow to give feedback, so your grade hangs in the balance. The topics covered each week were interesting and diverse, but I felt as though too many countries were covered. I never learned deeply about any other country except for the case study I wrote all four papers on.
This was a really cool class! Professor Rhodes is just such a chill guy and it is a pleasure going to lecture but you also learn a lot. I personally did not know a ton about Africa before, and now I feel like I understand a lot more and could make some reasonable assessments of political developments on the continent. Do not expect that will know everything, because for that the continent is simply too diverse, but you get a very good intro to many concepts across all relevant topics. Take it!
