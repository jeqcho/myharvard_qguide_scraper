What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This course is excellent. Mr. Bafford is an amazing preceptor who you will honestly begin to look forward to talking to in class. Make sure to take advantage of his office hours to discuss papers or any other topics from class. He really is a wealth of knowledge and incredibly kind. The only judgement that I will pass on his grading (what everyone seems to care about) is that it is fair. You won't receive a paper back and be confused about why you got the grade you got. You need three things for this class: Don't procrastinate. Engage with Mr. Bafford. And bring the right mindset. Take it and you will have fun.
When I read the summary for this course it was a lot different than I expected. Honestly, after the first few weeks I didn't really love it, but over time the class became better and I am so thankful I took it. I learned how to read long pieces of literature, and how to open myself in discussion time which I have never been great at. I won't say its not hard, and I highly suggest you prepare for a lot of reading that you actually have to read very fast. Moreover, make sure to check in a lot with the preceptor to make sure tat the way you are writing it aligns with what is expected. It involves a lot of Freud...so be prepared for that.
I would tell them that this is an excellent class with an excellent instructor who values each individual student. I would recommend setting up many conference meeting with the instructor to ensure that the writing meets the right standards.
Very challenging class in terms of reading and writing expectations–but Prof. Bafford is amazing in his discussions and is a wealth of both writing and academic knowledge.
