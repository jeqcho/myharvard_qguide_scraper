there is a lot of work and make sure to read the textbook to stay on top of the content.
This class is harder than Math 21a, but it's still very doable. Pay attention in class. Start studying early for the midterms. Go to the quiz sessions and utilize the MQC if you find a pset particularly challenging. With the proper time management, this course is not that bad. However, the problem sets do take longer than Math 21a problem sets!
21b is a solid class overall. Will teach you a lot about linear algebra and specific parts of diff–eq. Tests are relatively fair and there are true false questions which take some practice to get used to. If you are deciding between 21 and 22 especially with regards to proofing/3 psets a week, Math21b has great support lots of office hours and MQC from 7:30–10:30 Sunday–Thursday. MQC is amazing resource! HOmework involves mathematica which can be a bit finicky at times but it isn't too much. There are sometimes extra credit problems on the homework, do them!
Go to the review sessions that 21b holds, they are so helpful and great practice. Practice practice practice
Stay on top of psets.
I would advise against taking this course unless it's a concentration requirement as the current structure didn't feel very helpful towards learning. That being said, I would recommend finding a pset group or making friends in section to get through the class.
The learning curve is steep at first but the concepts were built on each other very nicely. The support system is very strong, with a lot of materials and office hours/mock sessions to help you understand the material and prepare for exams. Also, the practice exams are a lot harder than the actual exams. This might depend on the course head, but a lot of math puns which lightened up the mood!! This course lowkey made me find my love for math again. The struggle was real, but linear algebra is so cool. Daniel Hu is a great TF (such a cool and relatable person)!! He explains things step–by–step, such as how the previous concepts lead to the current one, and the different chart organizations to help remember them.
I enjoyed 21B a lot more than 21A. I thought the teaching staff was better, nicer grading, lots of extra credit opportunity on PSETs, and we got review days before exams (without psets due.) This class is a grind though with 3 psets a week that take around 1–3 hours each, but just one of those classes you need to get through for concentration.
