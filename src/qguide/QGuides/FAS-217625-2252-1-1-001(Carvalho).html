What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class!!!! It is so enriching and enjoyable. You read incredible works of literature and analyze them from a very interesting lens (what they tell us about urban history). Professors Carvalho and Chalhoub care deeply about their students and are both very interested in the material. Everyone in the class has different and interesting perspectives, which helps you learn a lot about the texts.
TAKE THIS CLASS. If truly has the most interesting readings I have ever read for any class. If you're even remotely interested in Brazilian history, urban history, urban literature, or 19th–century literature, take this class. The weekly discussion posts are super chill. The midterm paper had very interesting prompts to choose from and you receive solid feedback. Final paper also gives you ample space to branch out a bit. Cant stress enough how amazing the readings are and discussions in class! The profs are amazing.
You should take this class!
Bruno and Sidney are two of the most caring professors I have ever had the pleasure of taking a course with at Harvard. They foster such a supportive and enthusiastic classroom setting, and the way they bounce ideas off each other to lead class discussion is refreshing and enlightening. I highly recommend this course to anyone who is interested in reading stories based in the urban experience.
Take this course if you are coming from history, historical knowledge is a tacit of the course–– it builds from there through fictions. If you are on the literature side with less historical knowledge, expect to work speculatively. Building assumptions backward through depictions of the cities in fiction.
