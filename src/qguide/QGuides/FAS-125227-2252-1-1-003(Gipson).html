things feel awkward at first, but the only way you'll learn is by stepping outside of your comfort zone. The faster you do, the more you'll grow as a speaker
This class was such a memorable experience!!! I had Terry as a section leader, and I think his teaching style and philosophy was what totally made the class unforgettable. From what I've heard, he's probably the most different from other section leaders from the way he designs his syllabus. There were virtually no readings and all of our major speeches were significantly shorter than other classes, which gave us the opportunity to give more impromptu and unique speeches. Getting more reps speaking to audience definitely helped me be more comfortable. A lot of the exercises we did were theater–flavored, which was really exciting since most of us in class had little to no theater background. I was trying so many new things, and everyone else was too, which helped build a strong class environment.

I would highly recommend you try to be in Terry's section if you can! You get much more practice actually speaking, and spend less time having to read and do extraneous assignments outside of the classroom. Regardless of who you get as your section leader, Expos 40 is such a quintessential Harvard experience and provides you with so many useful tools!
Take this class! It is a great opportunity to develop a skill that you will use for the rest of your life. Public speaking will always be necessary regardless of what career you go into, and this class is a great way to improve through practice and feedback. Terry was fantastic and super helpful, but probably a harder section that other Expos 40 sections and we had many more speeches assigned than other sections. Because of this I think we all got much better, and I really loved being in Terry's section!
Expos 40 should be a mandatory course. You will love it and leave a much more confident speaker. The situations you learn in can be applied properly to post graduation.
Don't be afraid to put yourself out there! The more you are open to feedback, the better a public speaker you will become. This has been such a great class that almost felt like my old theatre class. You will become a better public speaker if you actively participate and prepare well for your speeches!
This course is an excellent way to improve your public speaking skills. Terry structures his section to maximize the number of opportunities you have to practice speaking and makes sure you get lots of personalized feedback. Its both fun (we do improv exercises) and you come out of it a significantly more engaging speaker.
