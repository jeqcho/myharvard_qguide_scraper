Comments
This is a great continuation of Hindi–Urdu 101! It is a bit more difficult, the assignments are longer, and you are expected to have a pretty solid understanding of the Devanagari and Nastaliq scripts, but it is still very enjoyable and such a fun class! I feel like I learned a lot that may be practically important as a Hindi–Urdu speaker, and have expanded my foundations in the language.
Richard Ji is understanding and engaging, and he knows a lot about the cultural and historical context of the language. However, you should either keep up with memorizing vocabulary and understanding the readings on your own or be prepared to cram a lot for the final, which might be during reading period.
If you have a hunger to learn, the professor Richard–ji will satiate you. Second year is all about reinforcing all the grammar rules, Hindi and Urdu spelling, and getting acclimated to South Asian culture via an awesome movie and more eloquent poetry/stories. If you don’t feel you are making progress on your own learning, it’s because, to get the most out of this class, you have to spend time writing sentences on your own, learning the words in the stories, and even talking to native speakers or Richard–ji during OH.
This class is definitely more difficult than 101, as the assignments are very long and you are expected to be able to speak in Hindi easily. I am not the best at the language, but knew it would be a fun class that forces me to understand it better, and that's exactly what it was. Richard Ji is amazing as always. But be prepared to go to office hours if you miss a day, and you will have to actually pay attention in class.
If you know Richard Ji or have taken a class by him before you know how amazing of a teacher he is. This class is an extension of Hindi 101A/B. We dive much more into reading more complex stories and pieces in Hindi and Urdu and become more fluent in our writing and speaking. Homework is given once per week and summarizes all that is worked on during class that week. Overall this class has really pushed my Hindi/Urdu skills much farther both in writing and speaking but especially in being closer to being receptively fluent.
