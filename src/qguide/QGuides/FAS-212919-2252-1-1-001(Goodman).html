I loved this gened. Genuinely a very very very good class, especially for not very sciency people who would still like to learn more about science (btw, not about a specific discipline) without worrying about the grade aspect of it. It is definitely a class where the professor and teaching team do care about the students, with a lot of attention spent on making a long (it does feel long) class more entertaining. We shot arrows around the rooms, navigated the yard without phones and comparing them with coordinates, looked in the telescopes, and much more. We also had snacks!!! And a small break. Like you can basically see in these details that the teaching staff is very–very into making this class as fun as possible. Loved it.

I cannot stress enough how much you should take this class. If you are looking for a 5th class, that's it—you've found it.
This class feels a lot like a middle school course in terms of the activities and its pretty scattered. That being said its a relatively easy course and the work is pretty easy to do.
This class teaches you things you wouldn't otherwise think you would learn in a science gened; over the course of the semester I learned about prediction from Roman augury to AI, and it was taught in an engaging/fun way. A large part of the course is very history of science–coded, which, as a humanities student, I appreciated. There are also tons of fun activities: going outside to navigate harvard yard, shooting nerf arrows, reading tarot...this was a very good class to take senior spring.
This is genuinely the best option for fulfilling your science Gened requirement. You only need to attend class once a week (although it is 2.5 hours) and then one 1 hour section. The assignments are very reasonable (one single page prediction journal and a module/set of videos to watch per week). I genuinely think this class could be of interest to anyone because the topic is very open ended. Just be aware that there is a high chance that they will implement a no computers in class policy next semester (they tried to start at the end of this semester). Overall, highly highly recommend!! *******
