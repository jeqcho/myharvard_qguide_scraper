I loved this class. If you are interested in gov, you must take a Levitsky course, he is a PHENOMENAL lecturer––and I think this is the one to take. As a Gov concentrator, I am SO happy to have taken this class as my entryway into Comparative Politics. It is a very well curated course that truly helps you to survey and get to know a vast array of cases by giving sufficient time to each region, and exploring cases in the contexts of different themes (e.g. democratization, democratic backsliding, etc.). Levitsky is also a wonderful lecturer — that goes without saying — and conversations with him are also just candid yet insightful in general. Great sense of humor, but also deep passion, humility, and curiosity. Go for his Office Hours, speak with him about his thoughts! That's half of what you're in this course for.
That said, the coursework itself is no easy task and there is going to be a LOT of readings that seem impossible to get through during tougher weeks. Sections were really helpful for that — Lainey was a great facilitator and also did a wonderful job consolidating theories and cases during section that were super helpful even during weeks when I did have the luxury to actually go through all the readings. The graded case memos every other week are also helpful to consolidate your understanding about theories and cases, but can definitely take quite some time so plan your time well from the start to complete them on time. Work together with your friends to go through readings, summarize them, and discuss them — consistency is going to be key for this class, so that you truly internalize the significance of terms and cases all the way until the final exam. It will be impossible to run through all 300 terms and remember the necessary case evidence without active recall across the semester.
Definitely recommend for Gov concentrators, but be sure to engage with the material consistently and do your due diligence weekly!
