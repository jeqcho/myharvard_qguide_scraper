This is a great Expos course. You will learn a lot about cults and new religious movements. Choose a group you're interested in for the final project, work with the writing center on your drafts, and you should be fine.
Dr. Musselman is a great professor and I truly enjoyed the course, even as a STEM concentrator. The course far more than a view of cults. Rather, it is an exploration of what makes a group a cult, how people come to join cults, and how cults affect their members.
Dr. Musselman is an amazing preceptor! Her course is well designed, interesting and great for improving your writing. Compared to what I heard from peers about their Expos courses, this class is not only great at spreading out the readings and 3 essays in a way that gives you plenty of time to work on them, but it also is vastly more interesting in terms of its content. Who wouldn't be interested in learning about cults!? Here are my tips for how to do well in this class:

– Do all of the readings! It may seem like a lot, especially towards the beginning of the semester when in Unit 1, but the readings are fairly simple and should not take you a lot of time. Once having done them, be sure to write your discussion comments, these also should not take a lot of time to do.
– Participate in class discussions: I felt hesitant at the beginning of the semester, but Dr. Musselman does a great job at facilitating open and inclusive discussions. Your comments don't have to be profound or sound sophisticated– just say what you are thinking about the readings and likely many other students will agree with you or have their own ideas to add on.
– The most important components of each paper are: your analytical question and your thesis. SPEND A LOT OF TIME ON THIS PART!!! If you don't have a good thesis and idea of where you want your paper to go, you will find yourself lost throughout the process. Be sure to use the in–class opportunities Dr. Musselman provides such as thesis workshops, peer editing, etc. to really revise and settle on something you are happy with.
– Take advantage of the conferences and office hours. Have a rough draft prepared before you go into them. This helps you and the instructor better work through the improvements that can be made and where the paper can go. More importantly, don't be scared if you have to change entire paragraphs or even your whole paper. There is a lot of time built into the essay writing process, enough for you to be able to do either of those things without needing to panic.
– The final research paper is the most hefty one. It also makes up the largest portion of your grade. PICK A SUBJECT YOU ARE ACTUALLY INTERESTED IN!!! Don't just pick a subject that has the most research or sources, genuinely go with something that you can see yourself researching and writing about for a month.
– Utilize the Academic Writing Center, especially for the first or second unit. They offer a lot of support, and it's better if you go at the start of the writing process, rather than later.

This course is not easy, but Expos is not meant to be that. What differentiates this class is that you have a lot of opportunities to ask for help, you get fantastic detailed feedback on almost everything you turn in, and your writing will truly improve by the end. Good luck learning about cults!!!
It is a cannon event that you are probably going to have to scrap whole paragraphs of your paper, but don't be discouraged! Just work through things one step at a time. Please take advantage of the writing center! Also, Dr. Musselman is very accessible, so don't hesitate to reach out to her at any step of the writing process to ask for help!
