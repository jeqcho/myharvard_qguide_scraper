What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I had previously come to Harvard with many reservations not thinking that my math skills were good enough to handle the rigor, but Math 1a was a really good course to show that the knowledge does transfer over while also introducing and building on my Calculus knowledge even more. It was tough at times but overall was not that difficult to handle. The psets are a bit much but with pset partners and office hours you should do fine!
Be sure to find a good study group to make psets more manageable. If you were placed into Math 1a or Math MA just take the course, I found that it was a very useful review that made me more confident in the material from calculus that I learned my junior year of high school. Also, Mario is amazing and truly wants to help and be availiable for all his students. If you attend class and actually try on all your psets this isn't a hard class.
Do your psets by yourself. It’s the best way to learn the material. Nobody goes to office hours, but it’s SO helpful because there are little exceptions to every topic that you will only learn by asking questions.
It is best to have some knowledge about calculus prior to this course, but if not that's okay too. The class moves at a relatively fast pace so just make sure to pay attention in class and it will be fine and very manageable.
Put the work in and you can succeed!
There are geniuses who find this class easy and manageable. But it's hard. At least in my experience, workload can take up to 10 hours per week, concepts are not actually taught in class so you have to figure it out on your own as you're doing a pset. The course is designed in a way so you learn math in depth, but I'd rather not. They want you to rediscover/figure out concepts on your own, but I just wanted them to give me that formula. Regret taking this class. But students are nice, teachers and CA are very fun as well. Met amazing people, and the help provided by office hours and Mac is useful
Success in this course is dependent on the work that you put in. MQC is your friend!! Even if you feel like you understand the questions MQC allows you to get a more conceptual understanding which is helpful when taking exams. If your TF is Mario, you're in great hands!
If you feel like you are still struggling with pre–calculus concepts or trigonometry, I would take Math MA. This course was significantly harder than my calculus class in high school. Math MA would give you a better foundation for Math 1B
The math department offers a lot of resources! The psets are confusing and picky at times, so the best thing to do is just go to MQC or office hours, where you can essentially find the right way to solve the problem you're stuck on. Besides that, just get ready to dedicate the necessary time for assignments + studying for exams.
Mario is great. Make sure to go to office hours and MQC because sometimes the p–sets stray from what we learned in class.
If you're ready for a challenge and want to take your calculus skills to the next level, I highly recommend this class.
As someone who hasn't taken calculus before, the class was a bit of a struggle. It's marketed as an "intro" class, but a lot of material is glossed over, making homework and exams difficult. Keep in mind that most people in the class have taken calculus before, and that's what the teaching team really caters to. If you choose to take it with no calculus knowledge, don't be discouraged if you seem a little behind, it's just that most other students have already seen the material ! :)
Don’t underestimate this class even if you have a solid precalc background. This class will challenge you in the best of ways, encouraging you to approach familiar problems in a novel manner. On the flip side, earning good marks comes naturally if you stay engaged in class and treat problem sets like your study time. Do the smart thing and make the class/pset time count, so that you internalize the concepts on the first go–around as opposed to spending extra time trying to self–study later!
