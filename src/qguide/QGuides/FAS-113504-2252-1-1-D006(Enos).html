Tutorial is obviously hard because of the complex assignments. But I will say, it enlightens you and makes you think in a way you never did before. Brian's section in particular had me not being able to sleep because the questions were so fascinating and important. I think overall no matter how much this course challenges you, you learn so much and engage in deep thought that you never expected. I fell in love with government all over again through this course.
There is a lot of work to put in for the sophomore tutorial so take a topic that is interesting to you.
Place, Race, and Power, my specific gov 97 section, won't be offered again, so I'll opine on the class as a whole. Gov 97 is in theory a great course to introduce you to the gov seminar style and a great opportunity to take a gov seminar with someone knowledgeable in the field. That being said, the assignments felt disorganized at times; I was never super clear on exactly what we were supposed to submit and how we were going to be graded. We didn't get our first grade back until the second to last week of classes, which made things feel so uncertain. Overall, it's not a bad requirement and has a manageable workload but the organization could definitely be improved.
One of the best classes I've taken at Harvard. Incredible readings and class structure.
This course was a difficult experience for me. The topics covered in the course were interesting but at times felt disconnected and random. The course was quite disorganized and we often did not get instructions on completing assignments until days before they were do. Directions were vague and I found it hard to figure out what exactly was being expected of me from most assignments. If you have some interest in local government the course topics are interesting, but the organization of the course made it quite difficult to follow.
