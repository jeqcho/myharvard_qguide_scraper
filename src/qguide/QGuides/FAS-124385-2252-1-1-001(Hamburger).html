What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

TAKE THIS COURSE! This is one of the best courses the department has to offer HAA concentrators. It was such a treat to be able to study in situ, abroad in Europe with two faculty members and a group of graduate students. We had a myriad of productive discussions both during the trip and in the classroom, and although the assignments (presentations, final paper) felt rather low–stakes, the class provided a lot of opportunity to grow as a thinker and art historian. Also, you get to travel somewhere completely for free! I also met some of my closest friends on this trip so I would take this class over and over again if I could. I was hesitant to take this course at first since I have never traveled so far without family before, but I am so glad I stepped out of my comfort zone and decided to go through with it. I did not expect to love it as much as I did.
One of my favorite classes ever. But the enjoyability is largely dependent on the professors and how much engagement and effort you/the other students put in. The excursion portion will be joined by a couple phd students as well.
Even though you might not study the field, I’d still recommend taking HAA100r. It’s such a great opportunity to meet other concentrators and really get to know them well – I’ve become close friends with many of them since the excursion. It was also so nice to know the professors who are all really kind and approachable!
The excursion is fantastic and, although tiring at times, provides a wealth of knowledge from the professors that help to move you into the next level of thinking and writing art historically. The classes were lecture–y, but still very informative. All HAA concentrators should take this class.
This is a great class! One thing is reading and learning about objects and maybe seeing them at the Harvard Art Museum but it is another experience traveling to the site. It was a lot of fun applying what we learned in class in another continent. Through this class you also get to know your fellow concentrators better.
100% worth taking the class, no matter where they are going! Despite haven taken some art history courses, whenever I travel, I have yet to have been able to fully understand the art that I am seeing. This course truly taught me the importance of seeing the objects I am studying in person. The course was dedicated to exploring the materials and sites we visited in Germany, and the two components (in class and in–situ) complemented with each other to create a holistic experience. Professor Hamburger, Professor Pereda, and Professor Estrin were all incredibly knowledgeable and wonderful, and truly brought the materials to life. Definitely recommend this course!
