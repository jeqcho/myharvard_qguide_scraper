Give yourself time to do the readings! Some of the text can be a little difficult to wade through, so ask good questions in discussion (participating is sooo key in this class).
Also be creative with the assignments, it will work to your benefit.
it's a really great course and the reading is interesting and worthwhile. expectations and grading are very fair.
This course throughout the semester was not that bad, however if you are not interested in medieval history it can be extremely boring. During class you just do the readings, which are a lengthy and then just discuss it, and take notes. There are four blog issues you have to do and you edit some and write the other half. You have to write a 10 page paper for the final and then make a 20 minute presentation based on your paper. What I did not like was how late feedback was given to students. You give each other feedback, but the professor would send feedback two days before you present and I had to change my paper so man times, after I have already finished. I did not like the final component at all, and then you had to present at a conference and it took all day. I believe it should be broken up, because it felt like a whole day wasted when it was reading period and I had other things to work on.
Take this class! Super fun and engaging and the professor makes it really fun. Def lots of reading and the assignments can be tedious but overall interesting to do
The conference at the end of the course where you present a paper sounds terrifying — it is not really that serious. You work on the paper for several weeks and have a lot of feedback and support from the professor and your peers. The conference itself is really just an opportunity for the class to gather for one last day together — it ends up being quite fun, and not stressful even for someone who hates public speaking like myself. Overall the other aspects of the class are fantastic. Couldn't recommend it enough.
This is a great course. The professor is engaging, passionate, and makes the content come to life. This course is very enjoyable and a great chance to learn about the important women that history often forgets. This course is very beginner friendly for non–humanities concentrators and has a manageable workload.
take this class!!! it’s super interesting, not heavy workload and the professor is amazing!!
This class covers interesting materials, but the course itself is not designed well. It is not designed to learn content as much as it is designed to learn a professor's opinion. The course is supposed to be a discussion, but it is not treated as such, and it feels like the smallest lecture you've ever had.
