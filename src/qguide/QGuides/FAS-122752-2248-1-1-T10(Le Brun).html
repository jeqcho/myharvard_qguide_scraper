This is a wonderful course! It has been my favorite class I've taken at Harvard so far, and you learn so much about both economics and law. You get to think about economics and psychology, while improving your empirical analysis skills through Stata and the final research project as well. The course material is super engaging, and Professor Felter does an amazing job facilitating discussions in class where you can express your opinions on the reading, and he pushes back on your understanding, making you think in different ways. He also provides such comprehensive feedback on the writing assignments. Overall, I would definitely recommend taking this class and getting to know Professor Felter since he and this course have truly made my college experience so much better.
If you are considering law school at all, PLEASE take this tutorial! This was probably the best class I've taken at Harvard so far. Professor Felter is an incredible discussion facilitator, and the content is very well curated (we spent each week of class talking about a different type of law e.g. patent or criminal). There is a lot of work (around 70 pages of very dense readings per week on top of biweekly STATA assignments and response papers), but if you are interested in the content you will truly learn so much from this course. Professor Felter is so knowledgeable and very accessible outside of class. He is a fair grader, though this class is not an easy A. If you are not too interested in law, consider carefully if you want to take this course as it will demand a decent amount of time outside of class to prepare for discussions and will be very tedious if you're uninterested. That being said, I know that a lot of the class came into the course feeling ambiguous/not knowing much about the law and have come out of it feeling much more interested. Start your final paper early.
Felter is awesome and really cares. You will learn a lot. The class can be a chase sometimes with grades as "Excellent work" will usually mean A– on a paper.
The economics and law section with Professor Felter is terrific. This course is a lot of work, but he is very accessible and provides good feedback. Staying on top of the reading is important, and getting help on the problem sets is essential because they are self guided. Find the topic you're interested in and try and do as much of the readings as possible, it will really help on assignments and discussion. But it is a lot of work.
A great class for learning a lot on a more specific topic. Gets much more practical than Econ 1010a/b, although with that comes a greater workload. If you have to take this class, there is a fairly high workload.
This class is extremely hard and is graded relatively harshly. The workload is very high with some weeks at the end of the semester requiring nearly 30 hours of work. However, average was more like 15–20 hours outside of class. The professor is fantastic and has a great background and breadth/depth of knowledge to learn from. He is great at teaching as well. Good class but expect to work very hard.
Even though this class was extremely time–consuming (even more so than my 3 pset/week math class) and the papers were oftentimes a huge time sink, I would still highly recommend this class! I found the topics to be quite interesting, and while the readings may appear scarily long at first glance, you'll find a lot of interrelated and applicable ideas throughout them. Professor Felter is an INCREDIBLE tutorial leader, and his feedback is some of the most thorough I have ever seen in my schooling; not to mention, he is always open to meeting with you if you ever need help. Based on previous students' experiences, this tutorial is on more–intense side — but if the topic interests you, you won't find a better class in the Economics department!
The writing standards of this course are definitely challenging, especially as Professor Felter is (I think fairly) a scrutinizing grader. However, I enjoyed section a lot and the topics are very interesting, even though I knew nothing about economics and law before!
