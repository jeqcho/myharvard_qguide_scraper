If you lottery into this course, you should definitely take it. The class is very collaborative and the professors are great.
WOW! I wish more classes at Harvard were taught like this. Fernanda and Martin are passionate, attentive, and genuinely care about you as a student. They push you to think critically and creatively about data and its storytelling potential.

As an international student, I felt especially lucky to learn not just about data but also about other students' passions. Many chose datasets from where they are from, creating an opportunity to explore global perspectives through data.

If you’re lucky enough to get into this class, take it.

The only thing I would say is that the assignments can be time–consuming, so start early. For the final project, I strongly recommend choosing a clean dataset that doesn’t require extensive cleaning. This was my mistake, and I ended up spending way too much time on the wrong parts of the project.
NOT A GEM. Listen carefully, my friends. This class is NOT about code, data, and art. In fact, this class should be renamed to "How to make a NYT–level data visualization in 4 days," because that is what you will be doing the whole time. You have weekly assignments (brutal) given every Thursday and due on Monday at 10 pm. Not a lot of turnaround. You are required to be at the SEC two times a week (also not fun). Realistically, this class is a data journalism class due to how open–ended the assignments are. Every week, you are given a vague assignment, such as "Create a visualization with an emotional impact." Then, FROM SCRATCH, you have to find an intriguing dataset, find a story, clean the data, and create a compelling visualization in p5.js. The thing is that due to Chat GPT, people just sweat time into this class and churn out high–quality stuff, so you can't slack. I think the professors are amazing, the teaching staff was super kind, and there is a lot of good stuff to learn in this class. I actually enjoyed it. But I ended up spending just as much time on this class than CS61, and that is insane. This class should just have two major projects instead of weekly assignments. Some of my weekly projects were more in–depth than my final project, also. The project is given over Thanksgiving break, which I cannot stand. I barely had any time to work on it, as I was not sacrificing time with my family for this class. Anyways, I like the material and profs, but I would recommend with reservations due to the true nature of this class. Take if you want, but NOT a gem.

The professors are incredible at teaching and know so much about art and data, and are a great resource for questions and curiosities. As someone who hates coding, I loved this class and it is far beyond a "CS" class— anyone who loves art would enjoy taking this class and may even benefit from learning about data viz in the process of exploring digital art in this class. It takes a decent, deserved amount of time per week but is rich in content and enjoyment.

Expect to do a LOT of work within tight turnarounds. They largely assume you are fluent with coding. The class is very generative, the feedback sections were really great. I would take it again.

IF YOU HAVE THE CHANCE TAKE THIS CLASS!!! I think this is the best class I've taken in Harvard as it perfectly merges art with technology, teaching great lengths about principles of design and animation like color, typography, and movement, and how these can be implemented in code (learning p5.js in open processing) to visualize data in mind–blowing arrangements that transcend bar graphs. Having little experience with computer science (only CS50) sometimes working on the assignments was a bit difficult to transfer my ideas onto code but fortunately, the class is mostly graded on your aesthetic vision AND you get incredible amounts of feedback and support to grow. This class was soo good it made me decide to do a secondary in CS or continue pursuing data visualization. I cannot emphasize how much this class altered my brain chemistry as I looked forward to lecture and critique every week. APPLY TO TAKE IT!
This class was amazing in that every week you actually produce something, which is far different from most of the other CS classes offered at Harvard! It's a great opportunity to walk away with a portfolio of tangible data visualization pieces. The lectures are also amazing and filled with interesting concepts that cover a broad interdisciplinary set of topics. The professors are also extremely attentive and very willing to help on any assignment. However, this class is definitely a heavier workload than you might expect and the lack of clarity on grading can sometimes make pieces take a long time. The amount of coding is typically not difficult, but the creative ideation process might be difficult especially if you come from a more typical CS background (and the expectations for creativity are quite high). Overall, would highly recommend this class for anyone interested in data viz, graphic design, or art +cs!
TAKE THIS COURSE if you are at all interested in art as a CS major or in CS as an artist/designer. It was a very fun and fulfilling experience. Prof. Viegas and Wattenberg are leading figures in their field and it was SO cool seeing them talk about their own work in lecture as well as give us many many examples of cool projects from famous artists. Every week we had a group critique based on our homework assignments. Though this is a CS class it is essentially a studio art class. I left the class with many new portfolio pieces and a passion for data visualization that I will pursue in the future maybe as a career.
If you are considering taking this class, I assume you are interested in data visualization or art or design. If this is you, and you are not a total newbie to coding, please take this class, I beg of you. I feel like for the past year, I've been feeling just–ok about everything that I've been studying, but this class sparked so much joy + added so much excitement and joy and wonder to my semester.

The lectures are actually mind–blowing. Fernanda and Martin have such a gift of choosing the absolute best examples on a given topic, and fostering discussion within the class. Each lecture was interesting and surprising, and I found myself sharing snippets of what I learned with my roommates and friends throughout the semester.

I will say though that this class can be a GRIND. The turnaround time for the sketches is Thursday to Monday and you will want to make yours good (both from the intrinsic motivation to produce fun, quality work but also because of peer pressure since everyone else is so amazing). This takes time, both in terms of coding and implementing everything, as well as thinking about novel ideas. For me, this was a rewarding process, but I think I didn't fully understand how large of a time commitment this would be, so I was sometimes stressed and really had to dedicate my Sundays / Mondays to this class. The exact amount of time probably varies per person but I'd say 10 hours per week outside of class. But the cool part is you get a lot of very intriguing projects out of it! I would love sending my work to my roommates and friends because I was often very proud of what I made!! If you are doing technical recruiting, you could honestly also showcase some of these projects there. Critique is also amazing in this class. You need to know how to take criticism, but as long as you see it as a well–intentioned thing, your skills and work can get so much better.

All in all, please take if you have the bandwidth! I promise you won't regret!
This is the best class ever: a MUST TAKE if you're interested in data visualization or interactive design! Fernanda and Martin are both SO experienced, knowledgable, and kind, and were always willing to provide feedback or chat during office hours. Their skillsets and temperaments complement each other perfectly and it is so clear why they are teaching this class together. The course overall is structured extremely well, with lecture slides presenting material in an easily digestible way with lots of super cool examples. I thought that the weekly assignments were well designed and a lot of fun: some prompts are vaguer than others, so it's good to get an early start. You really get what you put in: if you don't want to spend a lot of time on an assignment, you can get by, but if you become engrossed like I did, there's always room to explore and improve. I thought the critique element of the course was fun and a good way to get feedback, and I loved getting to see the people in my class grow over the course of the semester. Genuinely this class changed me and made me want to do data viz all the time outside of class: could not recommend more!
I enjoyed the class and the level of work I was able to produce. I also learned skills that will benefit me in the future, and am grateful to have taken it. That said, please think about taking this course before you decide to do so. Sure, is it better than other CS courses, maybe. However, be prepared to have this class take a significant amount of time if you are not an expert coder in JavaScript who can whip together a visualization and not break a sweat (there are those individuals that exist, but us non–magicians need a bit more time with the code). Very little taught in terms of actual coding. They do not well–explain the concepts they want to see in the context of the designs that students do, and rather just commend the students that do well, and tell others they are on the wrong track with very little explanation as to how to get on the right track. That being said, instructors are more than willing to meet to provide guidance, but students need to make the effort. Assignments are given on Thursdays and due Monday evenings, so expect to have no weekends. I both enjoyed the course, and also found it a huge growth experience that was painful at times, but one I would repeat again if I had the choice.
