This is a research methods class, not the most immediately catchy, but I really appreciated the way it was taught. There is a lot of flexibility built into it by having online lectures, but also there is structure for the assignments. This is the one where you really know what it means to carry out an experiment. It’s organized well, and for a required class, I was really grateful for my experience. It's an important subject and it exceeded my expectation. You are doing actual work, but Dr. Cikara does not leave you to sort it out. She knows you are an undergraduate, and allowed us to do the whole thing (design, carry out, report, present for an academic audience/lay) in our own Harvard backyard. Dr Cikara is very knowledgeable on the subject, and the larger implications in the field. She also is clearly interested in student feedback and learning, and is intentional with assignments. Psychology sometimes gets the reputation of being pseudo or easy, but here you really confront some of the approaches, limitations, and harder realities of a science with the goal of understanding people.

If you're taking this course, I assume it's because it's required for a concentration requirement. I would strongly advise against taking it otherwise. In my opinion, it's unnecessary to have both this course and PSY 1900, as the topics covered overlap significantly. The main difference is that the final project here requires you to conduct a research project, while PSY 1900 asks you to analyze data from a previous study. Additionally, the content of PSY 971 also overlaps heavily, as it requires students to design a study and write a research paper on it. Since many other psychology department classes already require students to conduct research and write research papers, and many students pursue research opportunities independently, it seems redundant to require this class when our time could be better spent taking other psychology courses that genuinely interest us.

The sections were not very helpful and also seemed like a waste of time. We learned very little about R or how to choose and use methods of analysis. Prior to the midterm exam (which is very tricky—definitely study for it!), we covered minimal review material in section. I wish we had gone over sample problems that we might expect to see on the midterm exam during section; that would have better prepared students for the exam.

Overall, I think PSY 1901 requires significant changes and should perhaps be removed as a psychology concentration requirement entirely. Every single topic we covered in PSY 1901 was already addressed in other required psychology classes, and it felt like a huge waste of time.

This class was much better than I expected honestly.
Lectures were very interesting and Prof Cikara was super super knowledgeable on everything she spoke about, she made the class feel engaging, and she always helped us feel like we were doing well.
Lab (section) was extremely helpful because we were able to work on our final projects during that time which saved our group time outside of regular class hours. Also, having the TF answer our questions immediately during lab helped save time with troubleshooting. We also learned how to use Qualtrics and reviewed R basics which definitely assisted with the final project.
Readings were interesting but honestly should be skimmed because we only really use them during lecture and for the Slack discussion posts. Also, during lectures, Prof. Cikara always mentions the key takeaways and quotes from each reading so that’s useful.
The final project was so fun if you have a good group. Make sure to have fun with your topic. As someone who did a survey instead of in–person trials, it is much easier to set up in the long run. Make sure to test your qualtrics survey MULTIPLE times before publishing it. Also, communicate well with your group and don’t let anyone take control.
The assignments were really chill since they were graded based on completion, but make sure to do them to your best extent because I got all my feedback early on and by the time my draft was due, I didn’t need any more revisions before the final project. It saves you time during reading period and finals to avoid having to write most of the paper if you have it done already.
Lastly, the exam was good if you payed attention to the prerecorded videos or during in person lecture. I took notes during lecture in person and didn’t watch the videos and got a good score. I wasn’t stressing like the people that skipped lecture most days
This class was fun and Dr. Cikara is very passionate about psychology and the world around her! Lots of class discussion and in lecture participation. Section is important and gives your group a lot of time to work on your final project.
Overall, it was a decent class, though it’s required for psych concentrators, so there wasn’t much choice. The course was generally straightforward and not too difficult. I really enjoyed the component where we created and ran an experiment. Professor Cikara’s discussions were engaging and informative. Where the class fell short was during the section. It felt disorganized and somewhat unnecessary. I would have preferred more opportunities in section to review class material rather than just working with our project groups. Additionally, when we requested a midterm review well in advance, we were immediately told that we needed the time to work on our projects. However, that wasn’t really the case, and I think most of us would have preferred the review instead. The feedback from the TFs was sometimes conflicting or not particularly helpful, which added some confusion. Despite these issues, I do think the actual class content was valuable, especially for psych concentrators planning to continue in research. It definitely introduced or helped clarify many important concepts for me.
