What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is a hands–on meat grinder that teaches students highly applicable and relevant lab skills. If you are looking to hit the ground running as an undergrad with limited/no previous experience, this course will whip your ass into shape quicker than greased lightning. The lab staff is supportive and available quite often, and TFs are highly invested in the course material, which helps to somewhat ease the demanding nature of the course. Admittedly, this course demands your time, but as an undergrad, the only way to improve your technique is to get reps at the bench, which takes time. This course is highly rewarding, and I personally feel as though I am walking away from this course with more takeaways than a majority of the other bio–related courses I have taken. As for Professor Parker, he is a somewhat polarizing man. He speaks with authority, and far more directly than other professors I have encountered. For some, this is a huge turn–off, especially if you are used to sugarcoated feedback and calm lectures that bore you to death. For others, myself included, direct constructive feedback is probably the most helpful thing I could ask for as a learning student. Bottom line: if you are doing something stupid, Parker will say "hey, stop doing that stupid thing" (sometimes in a more colorful manner).
This course requires a significant time commitment, making it challenging to balance with other academic responsibilities. If you’re planning to take it, consider treating it as the equivalent of 2.5 courses in terms of workload. If you have prior experience with cell culture and a background in cell biology, the course will be pretty easy– but regardless of your experience level, you will spend a lot of time on this course. That said, you’ll gain a deep understanding of cell biology, and the course is both engaging and enjoyable!
