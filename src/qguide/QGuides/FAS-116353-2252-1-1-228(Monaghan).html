What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
I loved this class! Readings were super cool – we read about everything from cyber warfare to Orwell getting shot to Marguerite Duras tricking a member of the Nazi secret police. We also got to go to the Harvard Art Museum to look at German prints from WWI. The final project also introduced me to Hollis and I went into the stacks for the first time, which was super fun. Dr. Monaghan has an amazing amount of knowledge about pretty much every war that has ever happened.

Definitely make sure you meet with her for conferences and office hours in order to succeed on essays. Try to get started on essays early – the deadlines come really quickly. Though I've heard this is one of the harder Expos offerings, it was very worth it for me and I highly recommend it!
This is an objectively very good class. Monaghan teaches very well and assignments shouldn't be a heavy lift if you actually pay attention and put a little effort into the writing. Super interesting topic as well.
This class is a lot of work, but if you have that in your schedule, this class will be worthwhile. If you do not think you will have excess time in the semester, this course will put you in a chokehold and you will think "where do I even find the time."
This class can be hard, and it will certainly push you out of your comfort zone, but lean into that. You may not get an A, but that's not the purpose of Expos. The purpose is to grow as a writer, so don't be afraid to make mistakes during drafts and always make an effort to ask questions. Be aware that Professor Monaghan is a tough grader, but she does it because she wants to see improvement and effort. She will push you and expect the best you can offer every time.
