Firstly, I want to say that Jason is a phenomenal scientist and a great person. All of the lectures are super interesting, and I was glad to learn some of the most recent theories and cool papers in epigenetics (I feel like most bio classes at Harvard are quite repetitive and explain information that is relatively basic/well known). The final project was super fun and overall the class was fairly low stress but high reward. Definitely take this class for your upper level reqs if you're an HDRB concentrator.
Choose any class but this one. Does start off interesting and simple enough (I genuinely think the material is so interesting and relevant) but the course work will get extremely overwhelming a little before spring break. There was only one office hours and the grading was extremely rough, with the TF answering my question one way just to say the opposite thing in the next class. The psets are hard and the practicals are also hard if you do not know R, although I was able to get by without knowing R. For the level of harsh grading, there needs to be more TFs and office hours but even when I asked to meet with the TF she would say no. Not to mention the project is very hard and I spent 2 full days just looking for a data set that worked. There is almost no support and the professor is never available to support this level of work either. I do not even know why this class has a final, but at least the final is basically a pset. Way too much work, cried too much over the class.
