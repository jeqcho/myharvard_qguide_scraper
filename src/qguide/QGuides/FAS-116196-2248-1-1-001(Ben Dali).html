Interesting introduction to combo! Ranges over a lot of different topics, so much more of a survey course rather than an introduction to key ideas in the field. Can sometimes move quickly, especially with respect to concepts relying on linear algebra/group theory that are technically not pre–reqs, but really are.
I've heard the math 155r experience is quite variable instructors, but under professor Ben Dali it was a chill but rewarding class. His teaching is great!
The course assignments were all interesting and very reasonable. Houcine is really open to feedback and his lectures improved over the course of the semester. Overall, I'd definitely recommend it if he's teaching again!
Solid class and Houcine is a good lecturer and clearly very knowledgeable. However, psets were returned very late (i.e. none until last couple weeks), although Houcine himself returned our papers very quickly and with good detail (and he even started grading the psets which the CA had not). The class also needs some more structured material – i.e. there were no lecture notes, no recordings of lectures, and the content was drawn from 3 800+ page textbooks without so clear reference as to which chapters would be drawn from. That said, Houcine is a new lecturer and will probably adapt quickly, so there's much promise, I'm sure.
It's a great introduction to combinatorics. Topics may vary, but the it's good to explore and get a feel for the field. Ben Dali was a great lecturer and instructor and was super generous with his time and help outside of class time. I wish we had our problem sets graded in a more timely manner, this would have been more useful for studying for the in–class midterm.
