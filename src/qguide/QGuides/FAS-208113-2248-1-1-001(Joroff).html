This course is an amazing addition to your education if you are interested in climate change, but even if that's not the case, it is a really interesting course for anyone hoping to go into law, business, or public service. It teaches so much about how policymaking, execution, and legal challenges to that policy work. It is, in my opinion, essential knowledge for a successful career anywhere, but especially anywhere in public service.
This course is amazing!! Please please please take it. Super informative and helpful to ESPP concentrators to frame environmental thinking in terms of policy to understand praticality. Aladdine is a fantastic professor and always brings a sweet treat for class!
Look. I think this is generally a good course, and you SHOULD take it (esp. ESPP concentrators looking to satisfy advanced course requirements). BUT you should know the following before taking this course: (1) there are a LOT of readings per week, but I don't think you need to do them to participate (although I did complete all of them because they were pretty interesting); (2) readings/assignments are posted/returned pretty late (this was super frustrating and stressful... for example, Prof. posted an assignment worth 15% of our grade late and gave us 72 hours to complete which is crazy since Profs. don't usually meet on the weekends and we kept reminding her to post them ––– hopefully the Prof. learns about schedule sending/posting because that would improve student experience a TON); (3) the assignments/grading need more structure, details, and transparency –– very hard to know when you'll do good or bad. That said, I did learn a lot and the content is interesting; the Prof is super sweet, brings snacks, and does amazing work outside of teaching for the State.
Take this class!!! I absolutely loved this course and learned so much. Definitely one of my fav course I have taken at Harvard!
This is a fantastic class. Joroff is an amazing and very friendly professor, and brings snacks to each class. The meeting time is later at night, which is a bit challenging morale–wise, but the class itself is so interesting and well–taught that it makes up for the late hour. You build a really great community with the other students, and the conversations and assignments are fascinating and stimulating. Joroff is always willing to meet and answer your questions, and is super accessible. Only thing to keep in mind– this class is definitely technical, and as someone with no law or government background, there was a bit of a learning curve. Also, the assignments were graded a bit harshly, and it was difficult to improve for the subsequent assignment, since each paper tests a different legal skill and doesn't build off of the previous. Definitely rewarding, but more time–consuming and a little more challenging than expected.
