TAKE THIS COURSE!! It was a great introduction to many philosophical questions surrounding technology and AI. The writing prompts are also very fun in that you get to apply philosophical topics to real–world topics and issues while incorporating examples. You also get at least 2 weeks notice regarding all written essay assignments, and these assignments are maximum 3 pages, so the assessments are very reasonable and engaging. Professor Risse is also very insightful and lively lecturer. His lectures summarize all of the key points from the readings, so don't worry about having to do all of the heavy lifting in understanding the class's core content. Lastly, sections are a fun time to chat regarding the topics. If you are interested in tech and/or public policy, definitely try out this class.
This is a philosophy course disguised as a Gened. I will say the subject matter makes you critically think about how technology affects your life. The work commitment is manageable. Just don’t take it if you can’t stand philosophy.
Very chill class. Readings can be interesting. Tashrima was an awesome TF, though she's leaving next semester.
Amazing class. Very interesting material that is very applicable to the world and to the future. Prof is great, assignments on a week–to–week basis are very manageable. Readings can be quite dense. Definitely would take this and I recommend it.

very hard philosophy reading at the start (heidegger, and other incomprehensible philosophers). but over time the readings become less classical philosopy and more contemporary topics (AI, climate change, transhumanism) so the material becomes more fun. three (secretly four iykyk) essays is all the work along with 12 reading reflections (in hindsight i should've done these for the later classes where the readings are much easier). for the essays what is required is the standard "argumentative essay" style of writing (expos–esque, building up to a thesis, arguing for it, addressing counterarguments, etc.) so be prepared to essentially write four expos essays for this class (though they're all quite short, about 1000 words).
Wonderful class, especially the discussion sections which are really the heart of the course. Also, fair warning that the class spends the *overwhelming* majority of time discussing various philosphers of technology, and only at the very very end (like literally the last 3 weeks out of 14) do we actually talk about AI, biohacking, and geoengineering
If you are interested in the topics of technology and its effect on the world, take this class. It is super interesting, straightforward assignments, and it really might change how you see the world. Great prof and tf group, interesting readings, and engaging lectures and sections. Would recommend.
