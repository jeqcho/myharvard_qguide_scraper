Comments
As this course continues to be offered in future years, it will definitely become more organized and fluid. This course was my first taste of the French curriculum at Harvard, and I definitely feel that it has inspired me to take more classes in the department. The actual time commitment is not much, it is just essential that you engage with the works and in discussion with peers. Professor Rungoo and Emile are incredibly insightful and approachable.
I would highly highly recommend this course! Class and sections were engaging, the graphic novels and films in the course were fantastic, and I felt like I really learned a lot. I do think that one of the things that made this class so great was that everyone was engaged in the class, so if you're not willing to fully engage with the course this might not be the best course for you. Other than that, this is a fantastic course that introduced me to many new perspectives!
Take this class if you're interested in the subjects and themes explored but don't need frequent class meetings to practice/improve your French oral skills. I enjoyed the material, but my main goal was to improve my fluency, and the class size and low frequency of meetings weren't very conducive. Prof. Rungo and Emile were great teachers
Decent class with a repetitive schedule of one reading/discussion post/section/seminar week. LEarned a lot about the francophone world, but the class size was a little bit large for the discussion–based focus of the class and some readings were unhelpful.
I loved the curriculum! It was very diverse, and I learned about places and history I had never heard of. Take this class, Usha and Emile were incredible instructors and such kind people.
