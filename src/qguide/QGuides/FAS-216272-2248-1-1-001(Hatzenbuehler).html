The lectures and sections are both fantastic, the staff is fantastic, and the open discussions we have are, again, some of the best ever. Everyone chooses to focus on a different stigma/stigmatized group, so you'll be learning from each other throughout the semester, too. This class is really perspective–opening and encourages you to have compassion toward diverse individuals and diverse struggles. Such a good and important class. Can apply to everyone and everything – medical professionals, nonprofits, law, leadership, everyday interactions... You can and should apply the learnings of this class to your daily life and whatever career you may be pursuing.

This was, with certainty, the best class that I've taken at Harvard. I admit that it is a subject matter that I was already very passionate about, but I think that there's something that everyone can take away from the course. In fact, many of my classmates were not Psychology concentrators; this topic surely attracts students of a variety of disciplines because it's not only widely applicable but also deeply relevant to our lives and society as a whole. There wasn't a single week that was boring or uninteresting, and it was evident that the readings were thoughtfully selected. The assignments were definitely manageable, too, though I would recommend that you spend a lot of time and effort on the term paper. This is one of the BEST CLASSES I've ever taken at Harvard, as a senior. The topic of stigma is so important and applicable to our daily lives. The class taught me so much on how to understand stigma and use different frameworks to combat the consequences of stigma, both in my own life and for my future career in working with vulnerable populations. I HIGHLY RECOMMEND this class to literally anyone, whether you wanna learn more about stigma and how to address it in your own life, you wanna work as human rights, public health, or policy work, you wanna be a clinician or doctor, or you wanna better help others.

This is also THE MOST ORGANIZED AND STRUCTURED class I've ever taken at Harvard. I felt like every assignment and reading made sense and added value to my learning. Every week's lecture connected so well to the previous and following weeks'. My favorite part were the weekly application tasks and time in section hearing from classmates about their tasks. Each student chooses a stigma topic to study throughout the semester, and I got to learn about how stigma affect so many different groups. The biggest takeaway is that stigma is present everywhere. For example, there is stigma against people with tattoos or piercings, people who work certain jobs, people who are ethnic, gender and/or sexual minorities., etc.

Lastly, the teaching staff, both Professor H and Nathan, are so understanding and helpful!
