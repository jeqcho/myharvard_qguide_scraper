IF YOU CAN’T DECIDE BETWEEN LPSA AND LS1A, CHOOSE LPSA!!!

I had this very dilemma during orientation week. For context: I passed both AP Biology and AP Chemistry. To decide, I had a conversation with Prof. Amanda Spiewak, of the Chemistry and Chemical Biology Department. She was incredibly helpful in helping me make a decision. In short, she told me that LPSA would help me cover the bases of chemistry and biology all over again, to have those foundations strong for the rest of my Harvard undergraduate career. In contrast, LS1A would not so be an introductory course, but an introductory applications course, as this class covers not the theoretical aspects of biology and general chemistry, but their applications in introductory lights.

–––

tl;dr: Both are really manageable if you have a background similar to mine, but I would recommend that, if you seek re–learning the foundations from a Harvard perspective, take LPSA! If you feel already confident and would like an introduction much more application–based, do LS1A.
