What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Definitely take this class!
If you're newish to statistics, need a refresher, need to understand linear models, mixed effects models, bayesian vs. frequentist approaches, or how to model your data when it's not normally distributed – TAKE THIS COURSE. It is a fantastic one and you will not be disappointed. Patrick is an awesome instructor and Will is an awesome TF. I simply cannot give higher praise to them both.
1. The material is covered fairly quickly in lecture because there is so much to get through so take advantage of re–watching the lecture recordings
2. Making an index–style note of what specific packages/commands are covered in each lecture/code file can help when you want to refer back to it or use it for a midterm/final (i.e. brm syntax is covered in unit 17 code file)
It’s helpful to have a basic understanding of applied statistics (such as types of variables, regression, and linear models) and some familiarity with R, though it’s not strictly necessary. The class doesn’t teach R directly, but it provides access to DataCamp for learning the basics. Even without prior knowledge, I believe it’d be manageable to follow along. I really enjoyed this class and would highly recommend it to anyone who has the opportunity to take it. For the midterm and final projects, you get to choose your own dataset and apply the methods you’ve learned, which makes the assignments both practical and engaging. Make sure to give yourself at least three days to work on them!
I would highly recommend that you take this class! It has bolstered my understanding of statistical concepts, coding and modeling in R, and learning the best way to manage my data.
This class is a no brainer for anyone working with data or doing experimental research. Patrick is amazing, and you'll be surprised at how much progress you make by the end of the class!
