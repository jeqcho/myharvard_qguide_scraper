***THIS ONE*** IS THE EXPOS 20 YOU WANT TO CHOOSE!!

Our classroom was composed of students that, although none of us stated nor were asked to state, came from uberwealthy and underserved backgrounds, the harmony of educated discussions reigned supreme all throughout the semester. Prof. Jim Herron did a fantastic performance as mediator and initiator of all this divergence and convergence of thought, of which I derive some of my most fond memories in exploration of who I am and my place in Harvard.

As for formal preparation, I would recommend prospective students to have extensive experience in academic expository writing, perhaps some two or three years of college–level prose. As for thematic preparation, I believe that any student candidly curious to learn about interclass relations in the United States and the philosophical foundations of a ruling class thereof would thrive in this course.

I came to Harvard with a reasonably developed political ideology based on Christian Mexican conservatism. From this course, I have learned about the origin of power, mechanisms of legitimacy, and the exertion of rule, from Quixotic ideals to a Machiavellian philosophy of state. I have acquired a more rationally progressive perspective upon my traditionalist view of elitism, and, most relevant to myself, I have learned what it means to be privileged. Through this course, I have come to understand what constitutes privilege, especially educational privilege, and my place in it.

This is a course I am candidly glad I took. Prof. Jim Herron is one of the most passionate professors I have met here, and I remain thankful for the vast wealth of resources provided to us in the form of academic aid. (And do not worry, extensions were provided as well, but use them wisely.)

FANTASTIC COURSE WITH FANTASTIC INSTRUCTOR
TAKE THIS CLASS!!! Would 10/10 take a bullet for this professor (if thats not saying anything, idk how to say that this course and professor are without a doubt life–changing). Before starting classes, I was told by almost everybody that expos was going to be the worst requirement that I would need to fulfill. Professor Herron is the absolute goat. He is always available, ready to answer any questions, and more than likely will accommodate anything that comes up. During midterms when we had a paper due, he was extremely lenient and handed out extensions. All of my peers have had horrible times with expos, but I can confidently say that this was not only bearable, but was the BEST BEST BEST class of my freshman fall. The content is also super interesting; coming into Harvard as a middle class student, I was pretty critical of my affluent peers. However, after taking this class, I learned how to navigate discussions about class, how it affects every single aspect of life, and how different classes deal with their handling of wealth. The research paper (the largest assignment of the year) seemed extremely daunting, but during the interview and research process, it brought about an EXTREME amount of insight into life at Harvard and why we are all here.
To future students of this class, please engage with the readings, professor, and discussion to the fullest. This class genuinely altered the way I see culture, inequality, and many more subjects that relate to larger conversations in the social sciences.
