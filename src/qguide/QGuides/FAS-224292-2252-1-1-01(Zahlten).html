What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Fantastic course with really fascinating readings. I recommend to anyone interested in history, media, or literature. Media Studies provides helpful frameworks for all of those disciplines. You can definitely take this course even if you are an undergrad or not an area specialist. Professor Zahlten is very flexible and kind with expectations, so don't let that hold you back.
This was a great course, though sometimes it felt that other people in the class dragged the conversation. As it is a seminar, one or two student speaking too frequently and too long can interrupt the flow of the open ended conversation. Overall, however, I did learn quite a bit, especially from our many visiting guests.
This is a great class. Its content is invigorating and a fantastic overview of the field of media studies and area studies, with the most recent/important texts. It's very refreshing to see media studies taught NOT from an Euro–American centric model.
Prof. Zahlten is extremely nice and approachable. He will make extra effort to meet with you outside of class, regardless of how busy his schedule is. He is very knowledgeable about the field without being pretentious at all. A fantastic teacher to have. One thing to notice is that he is very keen on the open–ended student–led discussion, which at times might leave the class in slight confusion. I do really wish there were slightly more lecturing/introduction from him, since he's actually a fantastic teacher.
I would recommend taking this version of the course with Professor Zahlten. There is a fair bit of reading, but the content is thought–provoking.
