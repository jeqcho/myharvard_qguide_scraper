What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
I would highly recommend to work on writing skills before taking this course as there is a higher focus on writing and grammar than on your thoughts and observations.
Although your experience surely depends on your TF, your experiences with faculty and the constant practice that you get in how to think about art history really does help you grow.
This course exposes you to a lot of different specialties with some really important art historians. It's a good amount of reading and writing, but doable. The lectures from each visiting professor are very insightful, and I'm glad to have taken this class.
This class is life changing for HAA majors! I went in intimidated by everyone's level of thought and writing (HAA concentrators know their stuff) but I came out feeling proud of myself for improving so much with writing and putting myself out there in seminar discussions. This class is very discussion based so be prepared to speak up. The museum sessions with HAA professors were a great way to "tour" the department's many courses and decide what you would like to explore more.
Very helpful but tough course. Every week, the visiting professors are highly engaging and introduces new, comprehensive fields of art history. Coming in with experience in art history, the assignments for this course are still quite difficult. Expectations are very high and grading is very strict. However, the feedback is helpful for future writing (e.g. grammar, style, etc), although not really applicable for any real assignment, as the papers are only 400 words long.
I gained a much higher appreciation in the art of observation and analysis. Because we were documenting objects (paintings, sculptures, scrolls, daguerreotypes, etc.) so intimately, I've learned what it's like to really be an art historian. Understanding every inch on an object and being able to read about it and interact with it is really special, and HAA97R really allows you to do that. It's tough and heavy on writing and reading, but it really prepares you for your senior year/thesis.
