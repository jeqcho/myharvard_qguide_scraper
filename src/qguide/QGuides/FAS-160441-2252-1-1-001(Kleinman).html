What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Take this class! Every week there is a different speaker (all highly regarded and super interesting and insightful) who is the author of the book/reading set for that week. The seminar/discussion style makes it very interactive and engaging. The speakers also represent a range of different topics within the field. Readings are quite a bit but genuinely interesting. One thing is you do not get much guidance for the assignments...
Be ready for a lot of reading. But if you actually do the readings and engage with the guest speaker by asking questions you can learn so much from this class! The assignments during the semester are very manageable. The final paper is long but the proposal sets you up well to have a topic/plan early. Thank you Dr. Kleinman!!
This was a wonderful survey course in medical anthropology with incredible, renowned faculty and guests.
The best course I have taken at Harvard! Fascinating discussions and incredible guest speakers! An experience truly like no other. Dr. Kleinman is a giant and my TF, Emma, was truly incredible and one of the kindest people I have ever met.
Though this past year was Kleinman's last, this course will change your life. I have benefitted immensely from the impact that the ethnographic literature has provided in my career, and whether or not you find yourself interested in anthropology, you will meet students from diverse concentration backgrounds that will broaden your horizons on the field overall.
Readings are really informative, I would recommend that you do them. Lectures can be repetitive at times but you really do get to meet some of the leaders of the field of Medical Anthropology
Arthur Kleinman's last class at Harvard was truly a memorable one for me. The people he brought in every week to talk about their experience with medical anthropology were all extremely interesting, and he was an engaging interviewer and occasional lecturer. The readings were all great, and they all had nuanced and interesting views on various topics in and out of the field of medical anthropology. The section component was so interesting and facilitated some interesting discussion in the class, and I met some amazing people through this class!
