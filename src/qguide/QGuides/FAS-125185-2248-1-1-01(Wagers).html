Amy is a great lecturer! She's super engaging and approachable if you have any questions. Psets are manageable and the fact that this class meets twice a week with no section was also pretty nice. However, I'm not a scrb concentrator and even though I've taken other STEM courses, the midterms and finals were difficult for me. They were heavily focused on designing experiments and critically assessing clinical trials/scientific papers (which I've heard is very scrb, but as a psych/ib person this was way different from what I was used to). The class was rewarding but the midterms and final took a lot of time to study for and I don't know my final grade yet.
This class is a must take if you're remotely interested in aging or aging–related research. The course is a nice balance between fundamentals and current therapeutic options. It's also a very reasonable course with no section and nothing intensive outside class. I adore Amy and the teaching fellows. They are super approachable and helpful
TAKE THIS CLASS. this is the best scrb class i have every taken. honestly, it is the best bio class i have ever taken. the topic of aging is so interesting and applicable to life and general conversation. you sound super smart when you talk about what you learn from class. in terms of how the course is organized, the workload is so manageable. there is no busy work. the weekly psets are short, on canvas, and right to the point. if you pay attention in lecture, they are very easy to do. the paper discussions are interesting and group based. the final project is challenging but super interesting, you choose a clinical trial in early phases and answer questions about it. take this freaking class.
This is probably one of my favorite classes I've taken at Harvard! The course was so organized, approaching aging from various perspectives (biological, evolutionary, sociological, etc). It was the first SCRB elective offered at Harvard and that is definitely apparent from the way the course is structured to the variety of knowledgeable speakers to the in–depth and incredibly current course content. I really enjoyed Prof. Wager's lecturing style and found the course to be a great experience. It is a fair amount of work (weekly PSETs, 2 midterms, and a final paper), but overall it felt manageable. I would highly recommend taking this course (there's snacks!)!
This class is really interesting and unique from other SCRB classes that I've taken here –– it delves into many different aspects of biology, ie different organ systems, and relates them to aging. You learn about clinical trial design and therapeutics as well, which I found very interesting. I think the assignments were very fair and there is a lot of support. Prof. Wagers is really knowledgeable and a great person to learn from. Overall, I would highly recommend this course for anyone interesting in the aging field and the therapeutic space.
