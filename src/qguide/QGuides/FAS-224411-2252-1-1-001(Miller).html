I would tell future students to do their best to do all the reading, especially the Ibsen and Chekhov plays. Professor Miller has really incredible insights about the plays we read, so you want to make sure to be able to engage in the discussion and pick his brains about them.
Definitely take this course!!! I have always wanted to learn about these writers and it is really interesting to see how their work was influenced by those before them and how they are influencing current styles in theater and film/tv today. Professor Miller is extremely knowledgable and sets up lively discussion each week!
TAKE IT! I'm so serious, you won't find a better teacher at Harvard. Prof. Miller really reads your discussion posts and actually replies to them, sometimes with thoughtful refutes. He finds ways to work what you wrote in your posts into discussion and credits your ideas. He conducts seminar with meaningful discussions using the readings. All his course material is carefully selected to contribute to your positive learning experience. He has so many different types of assignments to choose from, which encourages students to try new writing skills. He's available to answer questions. He truly cares about your education. You will be so lucky to take any course he teaches.
Interested in dramatic literature? Take this course! Prof. Miller's enthusiasm for the material is infectious, and his discussions are as fun as they are historically, politically, and culturally informative.
A fantastic class—Professor Miller is funny, knowledgeable, and engaging. About 150–200 pages of reading a week (2–3 full plays), but it goes by fast. Would definitely recommend taking a class with Professor Miller!
This is an amazing class, and I highly recommend anyone take this (or any seminar with Professor Miller) if you are at all theater interested. He is an amazing professor, and is so knowledgable about these playwrights. Could not recommend enough!!!! 11/10 class.
