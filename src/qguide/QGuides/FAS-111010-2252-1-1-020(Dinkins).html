Good class, does cover too much material
Take your time with questions. Go to MQC and office hours. Ask questions.
I would only take this course if you actually need it. This class is a bit of a hurdle to get over, and talking to other students, it seems to be one of the more difficult math course. Something to be mindful of is that this class is weighted heavily on exams, which can really affect you at the end. This course wasn't something that I was worried about in the first semester, and turned up to be the class I worried about the most ending off the semester. I by far spent over 15 hours on this class doing problem sets, learning the material, studying, and still wasn't able to succeed in the course. I'm not sure what background you need, but the exams aren't fully representative of the materials taught in class. With the problem sets, you're expected to learn new topics on your own.
Completing three psets a week is definitely time–consuming, so expect this course to require a significant time commitment. My best advice is to do your psets at the MQCthe CAs are super helpful, especially if you are stuck/don't know what the question is asking. Putting a decent effort into the psets will also make studying for exams much easier.
This class can be hard, but make sure to do the psets and it will help a lot.
If you have to take this course, there is no getting around it. I personally preferred it to 1A and I know it will be very helpful going forward.
1B is not organized well and it feels like a patchwork of various topics that are pre–requisite to going further in math (namely the 21/22 series). It's not a difficult class by any stretch of the imagination, but it's tedious, often boring, and not very enjoyable overall. You're probably taking this because you have to, so make sure to MQC and get your notation right!!!
