What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
This is a pretty fast course with 4 exams containing essay components and 2 presentations. Definitely not a cake walk for the language requirement, but a little I'd say 2–3 years of some form of experience would be helpful –– Spanish 11 can serve as a good introduction for those without much. The readings can be pretty long at times, but just make sure you understand the main ideas of them before the next class. I found a lot more success when I developed some sort of routine for practicing the language, combining course materials and assignments with sources outside of the scope of the class to practice vocabulary or different grammatical structures. Duolingo is free, I'd definitely try it out! As far as the class itself, it's solid but can be a lot of work at times.
Take this class!I improved a lot on my Spanish speaking skills, and I enjoyed learning about Latin American History
Adan was such a great instructor. He was so kind and wanted us to do our best. He said he was a more lenient grader, but I found most of his grading to be just fair. I definitely encourage taking paper notes. Adan enforced this early on, but it makes a world of difference to write things down and not use a translator when you are attempting to learn the material.
