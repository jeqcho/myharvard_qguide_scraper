Comments
The sections in this class can be long and boring, as you typically do assignments that resemble high school–level science homework. The lectures are also not as exciting, but you do gain a pretty broad understanding of science and astro concepts.
The first half of the class (before midterm) gets lost in all the equations and basics of biochemistry/geology/astronomy, but the second half is a lot more interesting. The lecture is not straightforward, and you have to listen for key information that might be on the exam. However, you will also learn a lot of fascinating facts. The in–class experiments are also really cool when we have them, but you will rely mostly on a section to learn the material, and the worksheets are sometimes very confusing (but Mifrah is a very chill and relatable TF). Overall, cool class but requires a lot of patience with the disorganization.
Very hard class to get an A, the final exam is very difficult, and the midterm is hard to get an A as well. Would recommend taking P/F
There's a lot to remember!! The homeworks are decent (although you do have to look up a lot of stuff b/c it's not all covered in class). Biochemistry and Physics in one class; it can be stressful. Don't take this class thinking it will be a breeze, but it's not too terrible. It's manageable, and you'll do good if you just show up, honestly.
Very disorganized class. There were times where I showed up to a section discussion and no TF even showed up or times where in the discussion we did not have the materials to complete worksheets. I also found that barely any of the material that was taught in lectures had anything to do with what we were being tested on.
Most cheese science gened you will find. Easy high school physics with some basic chem/bio knowledge. Never went to class. Section is just a packet you work on and leave when it's done. Chill TFs/prof.
Pretty chill course! Interesting material and live experiments in lecture are fun. Professor Sasselov is great.
