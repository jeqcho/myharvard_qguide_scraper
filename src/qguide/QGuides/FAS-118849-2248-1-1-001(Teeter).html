What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Being an undergrad in this class was a bit overwhelming, as it is mostly HDS students who all seem to know each other. However, I thought it was a great learning experience and I know now a lot more about the content and structure of the Hebrew Bible. The assignments are not crazy (a weekly reading response or page of notes, no essays) and the exams were very reasonable. Professor Teeter is a very enthusiastic lecturer, though I feel he views the Bible through a fairly specific approach (looking at parallels and overall text structure), which could be good or bad depending on what you want to get out of the class. I learned a lot and enjoyed having this class in my schedule!
Take this course!!! It will change the way you read the Bible. Perfect for whether you are a person of faith (as I am) or simply interested in understanding a text that underpins western civilization
This course allows you to gain greater knowledge about the OT, but it is very disorganized and presupposes previous biblical knowledge and experience writing humanities papers that many undergrads do not have. This is definitely not an ideal class for an undergrad, but a graduate student in the divinity school would be more prepared. Professor is very passionate and well–versed in OT studies, but the lectures are usually not the easiest to follow, take notes for, or understand since they can be all over the place. The assignments are not clearly described on the syllabus and are graded extremely harshly by the TF with unclear instructions. You learn a lot from this course, but it is probably not worth all the trouble and stress for most undergrads. A strength of the class is that study guides are provided for exams and TF tries his best to make sure that you do well with resubmissions of assignments and extra–credit assignments.
