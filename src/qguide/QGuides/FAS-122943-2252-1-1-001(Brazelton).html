I don't know who is going to be teaching next semester but Brazelton was a very good/passionate teacher. Definitely take this class if you are interested in pure math. Start your psets early and go to office hours. I wish I had.
Math 101 was hard for me. I think it could have been taught a bit better. But overall it was really fun to take the class.
The best thing about this course is that it is successfully an "introduction" to sets, groups, and geometry. If you have written 0–10 proofs in your life, this course is definitely for you. It walks you through everything you need to know in logical steps and it was very easy to keep up with. Psets were often more difficult than the class material but the CAs were phenomenal for this class. Professor Brazelton was an amazing professor and there was a lot of time spent on actual problem solving each lecture. I would highly recommend this course to anyone in the 21 sequence trying to decide if they want to study math or enjoy proof–based mathematics.
Fantastic course. I really recommend taking it. Be aware, though, that the course suddenly jumps in its level of difficulty midway, so don't be fooled by the easy initial psets!
Loved this class – Professor Brazelton is the goat. This is definitely, by far, the best class structure I have had at Harvard so far. Math classes here are usually very annoying: the teaching component feels unstructured, most of the time is spent working in groups, and it almost feels unclear as to what you should know. In this class with Prof. Brazelton, the class is very clearly structured. The notes on the board are very clear, there are specific definitions, helpful examples, and the class notes that he publishes are exactly the material you learn in class. We then have time to work in groups/partners on a worksheet, which solidifies the material. PSETS are not time consuming at all and don't make you do anything ridiculous just for the sake of it; they cover the material and make it clear as to what you need to know for the class. He teaches the material very well, and I just loved how this class was taught. 100% recommend. teaches you very well how to do proofs, as someone who has never taken a proof based class before. Midterms/exams are also super reasonable; they can have tough problems, but it is very much the material that you are expected to know and learned in class.
Show up prepared and have fun.
I loved the class. Thomas was a great teacher, as well as a terrific lecturer. if you have never taken a proof/abstract math class before and are considering concentrating in math without being a math 55 kid, absolutely take the class. that was indeed my case: I was coming from a very poor high school math background and wasn't sure abstract math was the right fit for me. if you are in the same situation, please take the class as soon as possible, possibly I your freshman year. this class gave me a lot of confidence that I can actually study more abstract math. finally, this was just a fun class! after the first unit of intro to abstract math/proofs and the second unit on group theory, we studied some applications of group theory on geometry of 3D shapes. Thomas made us actually cut from paper and glue solids in class and study their symmetries. note: the third unit of the course is used to changing semester after semester. make sure to check out what your syllabus says!
As a humanities concentrator, I think it's worth taking 101 if you're at all interested in pure math or abstract logic. It turns out I don't enjoy these things as much as I expected, but there was nevertheless something beautiful about it all.
This course could be so interesting if it was well taught. Unfortunately, that was not the case this year. If Brazelton teaches, I would highly NOT recommend this course. The course content is so interesting and you really learn a lot, but that's in spite of the fact that the teaching staff is basically incompetent. Some of the TFs were amazing, but Brazelton was unorganized, unempathetic, and just seemed to really not care about his students' learning experience. The homework problems came out of left field and we were NOT equipped with the tools to solve problems, leaving us to go to OH to solve it. That would be fine if the OH weren't being cancelled every single week, or Brazelton answered his email within the day. Unfortunately, this class was horribly taught. I would recommend you take it only if it's taught by someone else. Shoutout Victor on the TF team because he is the goat and would hold extra review sessions and OH just to make sure we understood the material.
I took it as a freshman along with 21b and I don't know if that was a smart move. Since it's offered both semesters, I would take it a semester where your other pset classes aren't heavy
This is a great class. I took it as an introduction to proofs because there are some more advanced cs classes I wanted to take that required a proof knowledge but I really enjoyed it. Prof Brazelton was really understanding and en effective lecturer, really helpful when you needed help without being overbearing when you didn't need help. The smaller size was really great for engaging with the material and asking questions. Overall psets were not bad at all. The first half of the semester was really really easy, got more difficult toward the end but still definitely manageable. A really great introduction to pure math, I had never taken a pure math course before and was really intrigued by the different way of thinking. Definitely recommend!
The concepts are pretty approachable, though the first few psets are deceptively simple. At some point of the class, be prepared to be blindsided by time sink psets. Exams are reasonable.
