What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
Great course if you're in EPS or ESE. Tackles almost every aspect there is to environmental science, with (in my opinion) a heavier focus on water (but overall very broad). If you're considering taking this course, definitely check if you have a background in chemistry (at least PS11). Also some coding experience is very helpful. Assignments were relevant and Bryan does and assigns everything with intention. He's enthusiastic about what he does and teaches, so lectures are never boring. Be prepared to write technical papers for lab reports! The final project itself was also an experience in itself. Very applied, extremely fun, especially if you decide design it to what you're interested (that's the whole point). Bryan and Nick (head lab TF) are generally available and will aid you throughout the process. Overall, wonderful course and definitely would advise taking if you're wanting a deeper but somewhat general dive into environmental science.
I would 100% recommend this course with enthusiasm. I loved my experience in it, it was one of my favorite classes I've taken due to the small size making it easier to learn and the professor's ability to teach to all of us on a more individual level. The labs were also fun and I learned a lot of new skills. This class gives you great perspective of the complexity of nature and why it is challenging to come up with good solutions and the thought that needs to go into it. It was also a great review of topics I had learned earlier in my time at Harvard!
This class was very enjoyable, and I would recommend taking it!
All ESE, ESPP, and EPS students should have to take this course. If you are interested in environmental sciences at all, you should take this course. It provides an essential and in depth background on ecosystems and nature, the content is the backbone of everything that is environmental sciences. Bryan is very passionate about the course content and teaches it in a very engaging way. The labs and field trip were fantastic ways to apply our learnings in lecture. The final project was super fun and really pushed our learning and skills and you get to experiment, build, or create anything you want!
