Comments
Great class, sometimes dives into the more philosophical side of things. Garcia is a solid instructor and gives clear feedback, and is truly willing to accomodate his students. Not a whole ton of work outside of the three required essays (including 8–10 page final), but deadlines can sneak up on you. The first unit is much more boring than the second and third.
NOT A GEM. Someone told me this and I was lied to. We write a close reading essay, a lens essay, and then a research essay. Before most classes, there were either readings or response exercises, often both, and these were often tedious to keep up with. Very standard expos class. Not easy but definitely not one of the hardest ones. Usually very lenient about grading but it is very hard to get an A. If you are thinking of this Expos because conspiracy theories interest you, know that the first one is niche, historic, and gory and the second is niche, irrelevant to the world at large, and literally a Netflix episode (which was fun to watch). You will not be theorizing about JFK or uncovering hidden modern truths unless you choose to do so in the third essay where we had free rein over the topic. Often, feedback was vague and we received very little guidance on the second essay. The Writing Center was a must and I wish I could have gone more often. Garcia is a very nice instructor and usually accessible by email and would often end class 15 minutes early (which was so nice!). TLDR: If you need a light Expos then this is not the one for you but if you just need to get through it and are able to put in the time, you'll be fine.
Take it! Prof. Garcia is really great and definitely provokes some good thoughts. I'd argue this is one of the best Expos classes not only because of how it is structured but also because of the conspiracies. Though, beware the class really focuses on a few theories so do not expect to come to class and learn about a new theory every day. However, for the last essay you get to choose your own theory to explore which I found to be really interesting (which is great because this is the longest essay).
