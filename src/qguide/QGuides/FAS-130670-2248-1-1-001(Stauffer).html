This was a great class! We cover a large breadth of rhetoric and literature and learn a lot about the history of these two talented speakers. I had not read much of either of them prior to the start of the class and I'm so glad that I have now. Some weeks had more readings than others, but it was never unmanageable. Go to Professor Stauffer's office hours even just to chat, he's great! I'm not an English student and was pretty intimidated going into the course, but Professor Stauffer was so encouraging.
I would tell future students to do the readings carefully and bring questions/ points to discuss to class that may spark interesting discussions.
This course was AWESOME. Professor Stauffer is undoubtedly one of the most fun and passionate Professors that I have met at my time at Harvard. This was my first English class at Harvard (took it as a distributional requirement during my Senior Fall) and I loved it so much, I'm going to take another one in the Spring! Professor Stauffer's feedback was so encouraging and helpful. He is so knowledgeable, it's inspiring. And, he is accessible and always willing to help / brainstorm essay ideas. The readings were fascinating and not too long. Sometimes the classes felt like they were a little too long + there were definitely some section kid commentary. But, I learned so much about these two figures and felt intellectually stimulated throughout the semester. I cannot recommend this course enough!
This course was incredible. Professor Stauffer is so dedicated to the class and to his students and you can tell he truly loves talking about the course material. Class discussions were engaging and vibrant and this class changed the way I think about American history and oratory. Couldn't recommend more!
Take this course!
Great class! Professor Stauffer is an amazing professor who seems to have genuine interest in the topic. Go to his office hours, he always will engage in interesting conversation. Don't be afraid to do the political commentaries, they are a safe space to improve in your writing.
Great class with an incredible professor. Workload chill and interesting. Would be a mistake not to take.
An interesting course if you're curious about Frederick Douglass and Abraham Lincoln. The course feels more like a Gov/History course than an English one. Assignments are relatively light.
TAKE THIS! Unique view of US history in the 1800s through the lens of Lincoln and Douglass's speeches. Significantly changed my understanding of Lincoln and made for some great discussions. Stauffer is the best.
TAKE THIS CLASS. GREAT PROFESSOR, GREAT READINGS, GREAT DISCUSSION. MY FAVORITE CLASS AT HARVARD BY FAR. JOHN STAUFFER IS THE GOAT.
This class is likely the best class I have taken during my time at Harvard. I have never seen a professor with the same level of enthusiasm for the material as Professor Stauffer. Even if you have never studied either of the titular figures of the class or the Civil War, you will leave it feeling deeply passionate about them and searching for further study on the subject. Take it.
Take this class –– there's literally no reason not to. It's fun, it's fascinating — I think learning about individual, extremely impactful figures in history can often be the best way to learn about an era –– and you might just figure something out about yourself. Also, Stauffer is awesome and so chill. Go hang out with him at office hours and just listen to him talk. It's fun. At least some of your classmates (if they're anything like mine) will also be super bought in and remind you that there are some really smart, kind, and genuinely thoughtful people at Harvard.
This class is a testament that the free–exchange of ideas, conversation and debate is still very much alive at Harvard. It is extraordinarily rare to be able to learn about a subject directly from the person widely considered as its foremost expert, especially a topic both set in a controversial historical era and steeped with modern implications. Professor Stauffer truly works magic in this course: everything about this course, from its student–led discussions to the daguerreotypes brought to class, has made this among the excitingly unorthodox class at the college. Classes like these only exist in movies. Take it.
