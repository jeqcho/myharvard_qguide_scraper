If you are interested in climate science or the content outlined in the syllabus, definitely take this class. All the material is made to be approachable and is widely applicable in the real world. This course is engaging and rewarding, I would definitely recommend.
This is an excellent intro course to the field of geochemistry. The whole semester builds on fundamental geochemical principles which are applied to modern and geological environmental systems, with an emphasis on climate.
3 words: TAKE. THIS. COURSE. With an awesome instructor, engaging material, and confidence–building coursework, what more could you ask for! The workload is more than manageable as long as you pay attention in class, which really isn’t difficult with how enthusiastic Dave is. If you’re even remotely interested in the subject matter, EPS 53 is well worth taking.
One of the best classes that I have taken! It's interesting to learn about what is going on with the planet from a factual standpoint and it is a great course if you are just interested in understanding carbon and climate in general. Dave is an amazing professor, cannot speak more highly of him. He truly cares about his students and making sure that they understand what is happening within the course. If there is anything that you are interested in learning or want to talk more about, Dave will make sure that topic gets addressed which is very unique to this course specifically. Additionally, Dave and Ruth are super easy to talk to and so supportive, they really foster an amazing learning environment.
I cannot stress how amazing this class is! I had so much fun and miss it already. Dave is an incredible prof (and Ruth was a wonderful TA)!! This course is one of the few classes I’ve taken at Harvard where I truly felt like I was learning for the sake of learning without the normal stress and anxiety. It’s the perfect mix of good, interesting content while not involving undue stress. I didn’t have any experience in chemistry other than LPSA and very little math going into this course, and found it to be a joy through and through! And I know so much more about the ocean now! Yay!
