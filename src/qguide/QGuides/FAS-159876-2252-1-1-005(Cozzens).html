AB is time consuming and a lot of work but it is so worth it. My Arabic improved significantly and I do not regret taking this course.
Solid class — you’ll learn a lot, and each individual assessment is fair in its own right. The grading is fair and the feedback is helpful. Be warned, this class is going to be more annoying than you think. It’s not the assignments are hard per se, but you will regularly have 3–4 separate homework assignments every single day. That might not sound like much, but believe me it’s annoying.

Don’t be scared off from taking the class because of all of the busy work. But it is a lot.
Incredible class, especially if you have Ustaaz Cozzens. The in–class activities actively pushed us to speak and think, and I was able to build my confidence a lot through both drills and informal conversations. They also do a good job to make sure native speakers and first–time learners alike got to be challenged in their own way, and we were all able to learn from each other and hear different dialects. The assignments all felt meaningful, and was never superfluous grunt work. I didn’t just learn Arabic—I learned how to learn Arabic.

For those on the fence about whether or not to take it, you should! It won’t feel like too much time. Took this as an elective—couldn’t recommend it more.
If you are interested and passionate about learning arabic take this course. However, be prepared to put in the work. Learning a language isn't easy, especially one like Arabic that uses completely different characters than English. If you work hard, ask questions, enjoy the struggle, and learn from your mistakes you will learn a lot and will do well in this course.
