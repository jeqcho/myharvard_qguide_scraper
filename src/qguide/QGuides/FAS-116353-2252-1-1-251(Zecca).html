What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
Dr. Zecca is great! She is really sweet and chill. This class is not a gem (grading wise), but it is pretty relaxed, and we had great discussions. I am not interested in craft at all, but I managed to get a lot out of the course.
The entire grade only depends on basically three essays and the syllabus doesn’t have much info. Throughout the entire semester, I had no idea what my grade in the class was and what counted towards the final grade.

There are also very long readings for the two sections per week (sometimes up to 50 pgs) so be prepared.

Before unit essays, there are conferences to review your drafts and oftentimes feedback that you receive doesn’t reflect the grade that you receive on the essay (ex: positive feedback but unsatisfactory grade) so make sure to double check and ask a lot of questions.
This course is interesting and definitely not a bad option for expos. Dr Zecca is lovely and really enthusiastic about the course content. Her feedback is somewhat unclear and does change between the conference and final draft (something that was great in conference will not be explained enough in the final). You really have to buy into the topic, even if talking about craft for a semester does get a bit difficult. The capstone is nice.
At least skim the readings, you will have to discuss them in class so you should at least know the concepts broadly, but most of them are actually very interesting so I would recommend doing them.
