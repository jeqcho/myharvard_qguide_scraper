What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course gives students the opportunity to learn about histories and conflicts that are not discussed in traditional classrooms (sometimes intentionally so). It was honestly an incredible refreshing and eye–opening experience, and I am so sad that I only got to see a course like this during my very last semester at Harvard. Our exploration of marginalized histories—particularly those of Indigenous, Black, and diasporic communities— allows us to understand the transnational dimensions of resistance and oppression, challenging the narrow, often sanitized narratives of U.S. history. Ultimately, the course offers a rare and vital intellectual space where students can grapple honestly with histories of violence, erasure, and resilience, all within a classroom environment that feels safe and deeply affirming.
This class was one of my favorites at Harvard, and I definitely learned a lot from this class as a STEM student. The course discussions were extremely interesting and Soham is a great facilitator!
I loved this class. I loved my classmates, I loved Soham as a professor, I loved the EMR department. This course is a great space for leftist conversation on campus, especially as someone who has struggled to find those spaces.
This course will no longer be offered, but it was the best course I have taken.
Soham is a compassionate, down to earth scholar who has a lot to provide for you and your learning.
