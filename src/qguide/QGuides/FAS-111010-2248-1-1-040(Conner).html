Take this class if youre passionate about math or calculus, or if youre planning to pursue pure math. If math isnt your favorite subject, this will likely be the last math class you take, especially if you dont need anything more advanced. Its a solid, manageable course, but only worth taking if it aligns with your academic goals or interests.
I definitely would recommend this class, just be ready to do a lot of PSETs. However, these PSETs are definitely helpful with solidifying concepts, and can be fun to do especially with study groups or at office hours!
This class is great– 3 psets a week seems like a lot, but its really doable and makes studying for exams a lot easier because youve been studying the whole time. Some tfs struggle with time management, so make friends in other sections and go to MQC
3 psets is a lot!!! Stay on top of psets early because you can very quickly fall behind. Go to office hours – the TFs are super helpful! MQC is normally super crowded and you may have to wait a long time to see a CA. Also form a pset group early in the semester!
The course is difficult but manageable. Be ready to put in the hours on those psets. Go to mqc. And don't do the early section. Do 3pm they will probably run it regardless what the sectioning sheet says.
It is pretty difficult, but your grades reflect your effort
Math 1B is a fine class. 3 PSETs a week definitely gets annoying at times and can feel like a grind at some points. Go to MQC to check PSET answers and have some people you can consistently do them/review over them with. The exams were probably like a 6/10 in terms of difficulty, but if you study a decent amount and consistently listen and do the PSETS, you'll do fine in this course.
Psets take a long time but content is super manageable if you put in the work
I am a freshman coming from a very weak high school who took this class. Oftentimes it felt like I was the only one struggling with material. I didn't benefit from section much because you are asked to solve the problems before the concept is explained and the kids in my group would solve it but I didn't know how they arrived at the answer. That being said, the Math Question Center is an amazing resource. I did most of my learning there and felt very comfortable asking the course assistants my questions. I definitely can say I learned a lot in the class but PLEASE GO TO MQC IF YOUR BACKGROUND IS WEAK. Half the people in this class already took Calc BC so just another thing to be aware of as I had to work twice as hard to end up in the same place.
