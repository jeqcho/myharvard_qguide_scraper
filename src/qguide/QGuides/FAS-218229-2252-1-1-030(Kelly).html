What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
As someone who had no prior experience with data analysis, I was a little apprehensive about taking Math Q at first but I loved it! Granted, this was the first year that it was being taught in this format so some parts of the course were a bit disorganized but the course logistics will definitely smooth out over its next couple of iterations.

If you’re at all interested in data analysis or a future in finance then I would highly recommend taking this course; I found that it provided a pretty low stakes introduction to coding and working with spreadsheets. Like many of the other introductory math courses, there are ample resources available for Math Q. I spent a lot of time in MQC and office hours over the semester which paid off when we had to come up with projects based off the work that the we did in class and on PSETs. It’s definitely possible to skate by without using those resources but I think that if I didn’t understand the code or basic concepts that we learned in class, it would have been much harder to come up with a unique project.

Learning how to code in Google Colabs was definitely the most challenging part of the course. There were MANY problem sets with coding elements that I wouldn’t have been able to figure out on my own unless I went to MQC or office hours. That being said, we were allowed to use the AI helper in Google Colabs on tests and quizzes which definitely saved me multiple times during the semester.

The quizzes and final were extremely fair and, in my opinion, very manageable given the way that they’re structured (each quiz corresponds with a unit and the final was broken up into the four units).

Lastly, I just wanted to highlight that Matt and Brendan, the two course heads, are probably the most caring and genuine teachers that I’ve met at Harvard so far. They clearly want to see their students succeed (two examples: staying longer than their scheduled office hours and curving a quiz when nobody finished on time), are excited about the course material, and are extremely receptive to feedback. Although some parts of the course were challenging, I ended the semester with a newfound interest in data analysis and familiarity with basic coding and modeling in spreadsheets.

TL;DR: If you’re deciding between Math Mb and Math Q, take Math Q!!! ESPECIALLY if you already took calculus in high school.
Take this course if you are not interested in majoring in math and want to get ready for stat
It is a great course to learn real world mathematics, but the coding is difficult and the class is poorly organized.
