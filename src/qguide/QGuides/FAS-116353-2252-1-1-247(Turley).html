What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
First off, go to as many draft conferences as you can! This class is a great experience overall, and though it won't exactly make you funnier, it ends up being quite interesting to read about the different theories of humor. You might be wondering what you'll gain out of this class if it doesn't actually make you funnier, so just know that what you read about in this class actually makes for pretty good conversation with friends or your curious parents. Prof. Turley is a great guy and really does care about his students improving over the course of the semester. His comments are very helpful and he encourages everyone to contribute to class discussions. Grading is decently rigorous and I wouldn't call the class a "gem", but I do feel that Prof. Turley makes expectations for papers pretty clear. Generally, I'd recommend the class given that Prof. Turley is truly an expert in humor studies who evidently enjoys teaching , there are two major papers as opposed to three (plus a capstone, but I'd say it's better than a third major paper), and that the readings (of which there will be a decent chunk in any expos class) actually happen to be enjoyable.
This class is very fair compared to other Expos classes. The grades given out are almost always very fair typically high B's to A's, and the feedback on the papers are extensive and extremely personalized. The work is pretty light throughout the whole term, except deadlines for papers creep up fast which unfortunately is the main component of the grading in this class. Also, not related to writing at all but this class is very male dominated.
This is a great course if you have a lot of experience with structured, argumentative writing in high school.
Eliot is a fantastic instructor. He really cares about his students and helping you grow as a writer. He offers very thorough feedback and facilitates engaging class discussions. I really enjoyed our conversations based on readings and working with peers.
This class was a good class for the Expos writing review. He grades fairly and how you would expect. The readings were not too demanding and pretty interesting.
Take this class! You've probably seen this on multiple Q–reports but this is one that you really should think about. Great way to satisfy the requirement and learn at the same time with workload just the right amount. Not easy but not impossible!
