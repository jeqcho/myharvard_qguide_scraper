THIS CLASS IS AMAZING!!! TAKE IT IF YOU CAN! Actually one of the best classes I have taken at Harvard. You get to learn how fermentation works and then do so many different hands–on fermentations. So many fun projects, and we got to taste a lot of what we made. The self–directed projects were very cool as well, we all got to investigate things we were interested in. Pia and Alex are so wonderful and knowledgable!
Chances are you’ve been referred to this class by someone, and your eyes are wide open at the blueness of the Q Guide.

This class was an absolute privilege and a blast to take. Pia and Alex are incredibly talented, friendly, and passionate about what they do—they’re the kind of instructors I wish every science class had. If you’re looking to engage in profound yet low–stress science while also having fun and learning how to make (and understand) fermented food, this class is for you.

This is also very much an “only at Harvard” kind of class: the dedicated teaching space, overqualified and deeply engaged teaching staff, ample resources, exciting field trips, and guest lectures all come together to create a one–of–a–kind experience—an ideal bookend to your senior year.

My only regret was not having more time to devote to it. That’s actually the one piece of advice I really hope you take: try to keep your schedule clear before and/or after class. I didn’t do this, so it’s definitely not a requirement—but if you can, it makes the cooking sessions feel much more relaxed (since you’re not rushing off to your next class), and it gives you the chance to stick around afterward and talk with the teaching team. That extra time also helps with the final project, which requires some work outside of regular class hours. Regarding the project, make sure to plan ahead, coordinate well with your group, and give the it your full effort—it’ll make the experience that much more fun and rewarding.

Other than that, just come to class with energy and a willingness to get your hands dirty. If you do, this will be one of the highlights of your Harvard academic experience.
