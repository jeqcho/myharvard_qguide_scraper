TAKE THIS CLASS. I approached the course with a bit of hesitancy– were startups really that big of a deal? I came in more interested in the 'big fish', the companies we all know and see today, and I wanted to figure out how those operations work. But, as with every company, they must start from a founding idea, and seeing this transition from startups to business models to scale –– especially in a case study, where you put yourselves in the shoes of the founder, making the decisions and seeing how you do –– is unbelievably valuable.

[What I Learned– and you can too!]
– Funding sources beyond just traditional VC (angel, bootstrapping, moonlighting, accelerators, incubators, personal loans, lines of credit, etc.)
– Funding timeline (rounds, valuations, SAFE notes, convertible notes, debt vs equity funding)
– Operational decisions (inventory, business models, distribution models)
– Team synergy (complementary founders)
– Customers (beach–head customers, customer timeline graph)
– Scaling (KPIs, PMF)

[Time commitment]
– Each week you read 2 cases. Be prepared to discuss them. Participation is key and what I truly think helps you to get things out of the class!
