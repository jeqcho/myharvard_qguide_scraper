For students deciding between MA and 1a, choose 1a only if you have taken AP Calculus AB or above or AT LEAST a 5 on AP Precalculus. The skills required to succeed in 1a reconciles with AP Precalculus (especially unit 1 and 2). For those without Calculus background, expect to spend a range of 3 to 6 hours on each problem sets (3 sets per week). Coming in with only AP Precalculus background this class is the equivalency of having a part–time campus job. The class is designed to cover the entirely of AP Calculus AB before Thanksgiving Break.

In terms of class experience, you will find yourself stand out among the crowds of students who have taken AP Calculus AB, BC, and even MULTIVARIABLECalculus. Ia emphasizes hands–on collaboration but in reality it's kids who haven taken Calculus solve problems effortlessly on the board while Pre–Calculus kids question their intelligence competence and stand behind to desperately try to comprehend Calculus lecture notes they learned seconds ago.

For AP Precalculus kids!! Know your unit 1 and 2 like physics 1 formulas in head. Trigs and trigs identity are very frequently used (especially in derivatives and integration). If you follow through the AP Precal concepts you will be in good shape, but keep in mind and rest assure that you may be spending 5 hours on any given PSETS and that is TOTALLY normal! Do not compare yourself to friends who casually finish the same PSET in an hour just because they have taken Calculus AB/BC/Multi/IB HL.
3 psets a week is manageable, but please use Office Hours and the MQC. You will learn a lot and receive direct help.

No matter what your field is, having a very solid understanding of single–variable calculus will be very useful. Please use this opportunity, particularly in undergraduate studies, to become facile with math because there is a trend towards quantitative reasoning across so many fields in our day and age. In taking MATH1A you will be pushed hard (3 problem sets a week, none of which are trivial), and learning to enjoy the grind (to get 0.1% better every day) will leave you very confident to pursue higher level math in the future. The teachers are ALL fantastic – special shoutout to Mario (our section head) and our CAs Margaret, Anjeli, and Iliana for being so wonderful, kind, and encouraging to all of us!
