This was an interesting and enjoyable class, I thought the lectures were comprehensive and understandable –– Dr. Coughlin usually derives the equations during them, but he also makes sure to do quite a few examples to show how all of the concepts are applied. The problem sets were also doable and fair; they aligned well with what was taught in class, and they were a good amount of challenging without being too frustrating. I do wish that there was some more discussion about the relationships between different concepts and how to utilize them to inform them when designing (and not to just do calculations on a given design), so that we have more of an idea of how we would apply these in the future. The exams were also fair, and they were similar to the format of the homework questions, so it was not too stressful studying for the exams, although the two midterms were a time crunch.

I liked the bridge competition at the end of the semester, but it would have been nice to get more guidance on how we can make sure that we're optimizing the strength–to–weight ratio and design tricks/lessons for it. I also wish the labs were more hands–on, usually, we just watched the experiment being done and then just recorded the data. Also, the lab reports were sometimes tedious to do, but there are only two of them for the semester, so it's not too big of a deal. Overall though, ES 120 will teach you a lot, and the content will teach you a lot. Hopefully, Dr. Coughlin keeps teaching this course because he does a great job, and it's also clear that he cares about the students and wants to make sure that we understand the content.
