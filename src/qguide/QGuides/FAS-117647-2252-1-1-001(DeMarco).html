What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Comments
It was cool. Pacing was a little funky because I think we aimed to cover a lot more than we could, and so we ended up skipping over some heavy duty conceptual stuff that made the end of the course pretty challenging. Homework was really chill and so was the final paper
The quality of this course will depend on who is teaching it, but it's definitely a course worth shopping! If the teacher is good, then this class is a chill way to learn some niche math and knock out the junior paper.
If the 99r has an interesting topic, by all means take it! Our year there were completion–graded weekly PSets to help you along.
The paper isn't too stressful.

Another major benefit is that the grad students are very available outside of class, and are very kind and receptive to questions about anything math related!

(and Wyatt was amazing this year)
I was hoping this tutorial would be a chill introduction to category theory, sheaves and cohomology, with a problem set every two to three weeks and a short final paper, but I think it was overly ambitious to the extent that I didn't get much out of it. Each lecture felt like a rapid overview of many different topics, none of which were that well motivated or understandable given how quickly they were presented. There were also weekly problem sets, which, though completion based, was more than I was expecting to have to put into a tutorial, so I didn't feel satisfied with the amount of time I was able to dedicate to them. If you aren't taking anything else intense and you are very passionate about category theory to the extent that you would put in many hours learning it outside of class, then maybe this is for you, but it wasn't for me.
My one recommendation is don't just think 99r classes are going to be a joke. 99r are different every year but this iteration was a very serious math class covering content no other undergraduate course covers. We covered more material per lecture than any other class I had been in.
