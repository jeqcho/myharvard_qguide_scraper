TAKE THIS CLASS! I took another tutorial in the gov department during this semester and the two are incomparable. The history department is fantastic, the Professors are incredible, and your peers are warm and supportive. I seriously do not have a single negative thing to say about this class.
This is a tough class but it is still one of the best history tutorials. They are all structured similarly and the deadlines are pretty unreasonable imo. But Jasanoff is a genius and our tutorial leaders were great.
The history of empires is complex and well represented. It covers a tremendous span of human history, so finding a subject to focus in on is not difficult. Become familiar with research tools and the librarians who can assist you if researching difficult materials. The readings can be dense, but are interesting. This course has a lot of assignments, so it is time consuming. Be prepared for a lot of writing, and balance your courseload in preparation.
Take. This. Class. Professor Jasanoff is absolutely brilliant, the material and readings are really interesting, and you will emerge a better writer with a strong grasp of what history (as a concentration and a subject) really can be. The grading is very fair and the assignments can take a decent amount of time but are generally designed to push you forward as a writer. Hist 97 can be a grind but this class is absolutely worth it.
This is probably the most difficult class I have taken at Harvard for literally no reason. This class is supposed to teach you to become a better writer, but I think it actually made my writing quality decline. Teaching staff with give you Cs on your drafts for A–B quality writing just to humble you. Then, if you actually implement the feedback they give you, they will tell you that the argument doesn't make sense. They make it seem like you have a lot of flexibility to write on whatever topic you want but will tell you to change your topic 5 times (once 2 days before the draft of the 15–page final paper was due) even though they approved your topic the week before. Teaching staff and professor literally do not talk to each other, or at least that's what it seems like. The TF will tell you one thing, while the professor says another. For example, you will be told that an essay will be worth a participation grade ––– it will not be. The overly harsh grading and lack of communication made this class incredibly difficult and insecure about my writing when it is supposed to be teaching you to improve. In every other class I have ever taken at Harvard, my writing has never received below an A–, even in very difficult courses. Compared to other Hist 97 classes, this is the hardest. Other classes will grade you fairly (A–B on drafts) and will actually help you improve instead of trying to humble you for no reason.

Will single–handedly tank your GPA because the professor is on a power trip ––––––– do not take for your own mental health.
This course is an excellent opportunity to improve your writing through regular practice and feedback. Professor Jasanoff is very knowledgeable, available, and easy to speak with. Would recommend meeting with Prof. Jasanoff to discuss research and other questions.

I felt that class time was generally not spent well – 2 hours and 45 mins is a very long time, and many activities were unengaging to me (e.g. we spent a very long time talking about how to cite sources in Chicago, which is perhaps useful to some people in the class but if you've taken any history courses before, you will have presumably had to learn this). One exception – the map collections field trip was fantastic.
