What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
If you enjoyed 112A, this is a worthwhile continuation. You read a good amount of Greek. Professor Greenwood is one of the best professors you will have the good fortune to take a class with. She is astoundingly erudite and clearly puts in a tremendous amount of effort into the seminars.
Though the texts and content of this course are wonderful, Prof. Greenwood's teaching was by far the best part. Greenwod prepared comprehensive handouts for each class which guided class discussion and served as great resources for revising the content. Greenwood's expectations of us were reasonable, and, I believe, gave ample opportunity for success in all aspects of the course. For example, passages for the midterm and final were taken from a set of passages from the term rather than the whole reading list (as in 112a). These are just a few things of many more, but taking this class under Greenwood is an absolute delight and by far the most stimulating and productive intellectual experience I have had thus far at Harvard
Very time–consuming, since for each class, there is probably a good amount of Greek and 1–3 papers to read on average, as well as quite a few written assignments. Like 112A, it feels very rewarding to read through lots of Greek each week and actually get a good sense of what different texts are like. And Prof. Greenwood is an amazing lecturer!
If you want to study Greek, you've got to take this class. It's hard work, but certainly worth it. I learned a lot and my Greek is vastly improved. Also, it's really interesting subject matter and great fun to read.
