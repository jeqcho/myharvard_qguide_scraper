This is a lovely class that I could easily recommend to anyone. Meg brings a great attitude to lecture, and the content is really great knowledge to pick up while you're at Harvard. This class's biggest strength is its essays. You're required to write three 1000–1500 word essays (one can be a short story) throughout the semester. The topics force you to engage with the course in a way that isn't a slog. I never felt like I didn't care about what I was writing, which was really nice. The class is pretty easy, but not because it doesn't assign enough work – its assignments are just very realistic and manageable. The course staff are also reasonably friendly with the grading – if you do what's asked of you, they're not afraid to give full marks. Just a great elective to fill out your timetable. If that's what you're looking for, then you've found it!
