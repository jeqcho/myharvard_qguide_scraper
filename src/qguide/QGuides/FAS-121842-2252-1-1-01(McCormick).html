What would you like to tell future students about this class? (Your response to this question may be published anonymously.)

Table for [QTitle].
Comments
This course is tough as an undergrad because it is dominated by PhD students, who made up about 3/4 of the class. They had extensive specific background knowledge that made some conversations difficult to follow. That aspect of the course is not ideal, but Professor McCormick is awesome and does her best to make discussions accessible to all backgrounds. She clearly cares about her students and wants everyone to succeed, and that is why the class is enjoyable.
This is a very interesting class. Though it may be daunting with the course material and grad students, Professor McCormick really made sure it’s approachable to undergrads! I really loved our readings and weekly topics, even though I’ve struggled with the theoretical pieces, we unpacked them together in class and they turned out to be so useful for my own studies even beyond this course!
I feel like if you have any interest in Japanese art or feminist art historical methods, you should take this class. It functions almost like a survey class but the students and professor all know about each topic in depth so you learn more than if it was just a lecture.
